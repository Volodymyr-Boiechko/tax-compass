## Executive Summary

**Scale and scope:** This dataset covers all 147 full-chapter jurisdictions in the EY Worldwide Personal Tax and Immigration Guide 2025-26, representing the most comprehensive personal tax comparison available from a single authoritative source. Combined with targeted web searches for special regimes not detailed in EY, the dataset captures effective tax rates for both employment and self-employment at standardized income points (€30k, €60k, €100k).

**Biggest surprises:**
- **Gulf + offshore dominance:** Twelve jurisdictions offer 0% personal income tax (UAE, Bahrain, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Oman, Bahamas, Bermuda, BVI, Cayman Islands, Brunei, Monaco) — covering ~8% of the world's jurisdictions. For IT professionals, these represent a near-total tax elimination, though immigration barriers and lifestyle factors vary significantly.
- **Georgia's exceptional SBS regime:** At 1% flat on revenue up to GEL 500k (~€170k), Georgia's Small Business Status is the lowest transparent, legally certain self-employment tax in any non-zero-tax country, yet it receives far less attention than UAE or Bahamas. Mandatory work permit requirements from March 2026 now add friction for foreign nationals.
- **Albania's time-limited window:** The Person Fizik 0% regime (until 31.12.2029) is extraordinary — only fixed SS costs apply — but expires in 4 years. IT professionals considering Albania should plan the post-2029 transition (reverts to 15%).
- **Eastern Europe remains the IT-freelancer sweet spot in Europe:** Ukraine ФОП (~7%), Bulgaria free professional (~9%), Kosovo (~8%), Czech Republic Paušální daň (~17%), and Slovenia Normiranec (~5% at €30k) all dramatically beat Western European rates.
- **Latin America outliers:** Paraguay (flat 8–10% IRP, territorial) and Guatemala (5–7% on gross) are exceptionally low for the region and largely overlooked by the digital nomad community. Mexico's RESICO (~2%) is similarly underappreciated.
- **Denmark now tops the global high-tax list** with a top marginal rate rising to ~61% in 2026 (new 5% surtax above DKK 2.5M) — overtaking Finland and Belgium. The bottom-10 high-tax countries are all in Western/Northern Europe.
- **Territorial systems as a category:** Panama, Paraguay, Honduras, Malaysia (FSI exemption to 2036), Singapore, and Uruguay (new resident exemption) all effectively exempt foreign-source income, making them natural "zero-tax" options for remote workers with non-local clients — without requiring the extreme lifestyle adjustments of Gulf residency.
- **Uzbekistan's 1% rate (new 2026):** One of the most significant 2026 changes globally — a country of 36M people now offers a 1% revenue tax for small entrepreneurs, placing it among the world's lowest-tax jurisdictions for freelancers, with much lower cost of living than Georgia or UAE.
