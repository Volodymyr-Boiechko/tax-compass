## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (IRS — Imposto sobre o Rendimento das Pessoas Singulares) for residents taxed on worldwide income. Tax year = calendar year. Filing period: 1 April – 30 June of the following year.

Business and professional income may be taxed under the Simplified Regime (if prior-year gross ≤ EUR 200,000 and taxpayer does not opt for organised bookkeeping) or the Organised Bookkeeping Regime. (EY p.1280)

**PIT brackets — 2025 (IRS rates)** (EY p.1290)

| Taxable income (EUR) | Tax on lower amount (EUR) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 8,059 | 0 | 12.50% |
| 8,059 – 12,160 | 1,007.37 | 16.00% |
| 12,160 – 17,233 | 1,663.49 | 21.50% |
| 17,233 – 22,306 | 2,754.18 | 24.40% |
| 22,306 – 28,400 | 3,992.10 | 31.40% |
| 28,400 – 41,629 | 5,905.50 | 34.90% |
| 41,629 – 44,987 | 10,522.56 | 43.10% |
| 44,987 – 83,696 | 11,969.69 | 44.60% |
| Over 83,696 | 29,234.18 | 48.00% |

**Solidarity surcharge (sobretaxa solidária)** (EY p.1290):
- Taxable income EUR 80,000 – EUR 250,000: additional 2.5%
- Taxable income over EUR 250,000: additional 5%

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Employee personal deduction: EUR 4,462.15 (for 2025); compulsory social security contributions in excess of this amount are deductible without limit (EY p.1288)
- Tax credits: EUR 600 per child (EUR 726 for first child under 3; EUR 900 for second child under 6) (EY p.1290–1291)
- General household expenses credit: 35% (45% for single-parent families), capped at EUR 250 per taxpayer (EY p.1291)
- Health expenses credit: capped at EUR 1,000 (EY p.1291)

**Investment income:** Withholding tax 28% on interest, dividends, bank deposits (final or elective inclusion in taxable base). (EY p.1280)

**Rental income:** Special rate 25% (residential) or 28% (commercial); reduced rates for long-term leases: 15% (5–10 years), 10% (10–20 years), 5% (20+ years). (EY p.1281)

**Capital gains:** Generally taxed at ordinary IRS rates; gains from securities taxed at 28%; 50% inclusion for real estate gains; long-term securities exclusions: 10% (2–5 years held), 20% (5–8 years), 30% (8+ years). (EY p.1282–1284)

**Notes:**
- Married couples may opt for joint taxation (income divided by two, then progressive scale applied)
- Loss carryforward from business/professional activities: up to 12 years (EY p.1292)
- Directors' fees taxed as employment income (EY p.1280)

## Social Security

Contributions payable on all salaries, wages, bonuses and other regular income (excluding lunch subsidies). No ceiling on the contribution base for employer/employee contributions. (EY p.1293)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (general) | 11.00% | 23.75% | 21.4% |
| Social security (specific situations) | — | — | 25.2% |
| **Total** | **11.00%** | **23.75%** | **21.4%–25.2%** |

**Self-employed contribution base:** 70% of total gross services provided (20% for production/sale of goods), with a monthly cap of EUR 6,270 for 2025 (= 12 × EUR 522.50). (EY p.1293)

**Client withholding obligation:** Entities contracting self-employed service providers pay an additional 10% (if >80% of services to same entity) or 7% (if 50–80% to same entity) social security levy on amounts paid. (EY p.1293)

**Management/governing bodies:** 11% (individual) + 23.75% (company) for management roles; 9.3% (individual) + 20.3% (company) for other governing bodies. (EY p.1294)

**Income ceiling:** None for employer/employee contributions; self-employed base capped at EUR 6,270/month (EUR 75,240/year).

**Notes:**
- Self-employed contributions first due from the 1st day of the 12th month after commencement of activity
- Quarterly returns filed in April, July, October and January
- Totalization agreements with 50+ jurisdictions including EU, US, Australia (EY p.1294)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Regime Simplificado (Simplified Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals (ENI — Empresário em Nome Individual or Categoria B professionals) whose prior-year gross business/professional income did not exceed EUR 200,000, and who do not opt for organised bookkeeping. (EY p.1280, 1288–1289)
- **Tax base:** Taxable income = gross revenue × applicable predefined coefficient:

  | Coefficient | Activity |
  |---|---|
  | 0.15 | Sales of goods, hotel/similar services, food & beverages, crypto assets (non-mining) |
  | **0.75** | **Professional services listed in Article 151 IRS Code (IT, consulting, most liberal professions)** |
  | 0.35 | Other service rendering activities |
  | 0.95 | Royalties, investment income from trade/business, rental income, net worth increases |
  | 0.30 | Non-operating subsidies / grants for equipment |
  | 0.10 | Operating subsidies (personnel training, etc.) |
  | 0.50 | Local accommodation (apartments/dwellings in restricted areas) |
  | 1.00 | Services to tax-transparency companies or companies where taxpayer holds ≥5% shares |

- **Rate / mechanism:** Taxable income is added to other income and taxed at standard progressive IRS rates (12.5%–48% + solidarity surcharge). Social security contributions (paid separately) are NOT deductible from the IRS base under the simplified regime.
- **Social security:** 21.4% × 70% of gross services = effective 14.98% of gross revenue (capped at EUR 75,240/year base).
- **VAT obligations:** VAT exemption (Article 53) for annual turnover below EUR 15,000; standard rate 23% (reduced rates 13%/6% for specific goods/services).
- **Key limits:** Revenue ceiling EUR 200,000 (prior year); regime ceases if exceeded two consecutive years or by >25% in a single year. Coefficient 0.75 for professional services is partially conditioned on proving expenses exceed 15% of gross income. (EY p.1289)
- **New business benefit:** In first and second years of activity, coefficients for services rendered and operating subsidies are reduced by 50% and 25% respectively (if requirements met and business not ceased in last 5 years). (EY p.1289)

### Option B — Regime de Contabilidade Organizada (Organised Bookkeeping)

- **Who qualifies:** Any self-employed individual; mandatory if prior-year revenue exceeds EUR 200,000.
- **Tax base:** Net profit = revenue minus allowable business expenses (using corporate tax rules). Own remuneration, per diem allowances and kilometre allowances are NOT deductible. (EY p.1289–1290)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net profit taxed at progressive IRS rates (12.5%–48%).
- **Social security:** Same as Option A (21.4% of 70% of gross receipts, capped).
- **VAT:** Standard VAT obligations apply; organised accounting records required.
- **Key limits:** None on revenue; preferable when business expenses are substantial (typically >25% of revenue).

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### IFICI — Incentivo Fiscal à Investigação Científica e Inovação (NHR 2.0)

- **Eligibility:** Individuals becoming tax residents of Portugal in 2024 or subsequent years under Portuguese domestic law who have NOT been resident in Portugal in any of the prior five years. Must perform activities in qualifying categories: higher education/scientific research; qualified jobs under Investment Tax Code (Chapter II); highly qualified professions in companies with significant investment applications or industrial/service companies exporting ≥50% of turnover; qualified jobs in AICEP/IAPMEI-recognized entities; R&D personnel; jobs in certified startups (Portuguese Start-Up Law); activities in Azores/Madeira by regional decree. (EY p.1286–1287)
- **Benefit:** 20% flat rate on net income from qualifying employment and self-employment activities. Foreign-source employment/business/professional income, investment income, rental income and capital gains are exempt (if from non-tax-haven countries and effectively taxed or subject to OECD MTC rules). Foreign pension income does NOT benefit from the exemption (taxed at 10% special rate). (EY p.1287)
- **Duration:** 10 consecutive years from the year of registration as tax resident in Portugal. Possibility to interrupt and resume under conditions similar to the old NHR. (EY p.1287)
- **Application deadline:** Must register as IFICI beneficiary with Portuguese tax authorities upon establishing residency.
- **Caveats:** Only income from qualifying activities benefits from the 20% flat rate; other income taxed at ordinary progressive rates. Not cumulative with the "Regime para Regressados." (EY p.1287)

### Regime Transitório NHR (Transitional Non-Habitual Resident Regime)

- **Eligibility:** Individuals already registered as NHR under pre-2024 rules, OR individuals who became tax residents by 31 December 2024 and met one of the qualifying conditions by 10 October 2023 (promissory employment agreement, lease agreement, property reservation contract, enrolled dependants at Portuguese educational institution, valid residence permit, or pending visa procedure). (EY p.1285–1286)
- **Benefit:** Full original NHR benefits: 20% flat rate on Portuguese-source qualifying income; exemption method on foreign-source qualifying income.
- **Duration:** Up to 10 years from initial registration.
- **Caveats:** Transitional regime will not apply to new registrations after qualifying deadline. Foreign pension income taxed at 10% special rate (since 2020 State Budget Law). (EY p.1285)

### Regime para Regressados (Favorable Regime for Returning Individuals)

- **Eligibility:** Individuals becoming tax residents of Portugal as from 2024 who were NOT tax residents in Portugal during the five years prior to return, but who were tax residents of Portugal in some earlier period. Must have tax affairs up to date. Applicable to those becoming tax residents through 2026. (EY p.1287)
- **Benefit:** 50% exclusion on employment and self-employment income, up to EUR 250,000.
- **Duration:** Year of return and four following years (5 years total).
- **Caveats:** Not cumulative with NHR/IFICI regime.

## 2026 Changes

- **IFICI (NHR 2.0) fully operational:** Primary special regime for new residents from 2024 onwards; the old NHR closed to new applications from 1 January 2024. IFICI applications open for qualifying professionals under new activity categories. (EY p.1286–1287)
- **Transitional NHR continues:** Existing NHR registrants and those who qualified under transitional rules continue to benefit under the old regime up to their 10-year period. (EY p.1285–1286)
- **Returning residents regime:** Applies to individuals becoming tax residents through 31 December 2026 (50% exclusion up to EUR 250,000, 5-year benefit). (EY p.1287)
- **Long-term rental reduced rates in force:** 15%/10%/5% flat rates for leases of 5–10/10–20/20+ years respectively. (EY p.1281)
- **No bracket changes announced:** PIT brackets shown are for 2025; 2026 brackets subject to annual State Budget (no changes announced as of EY content date).
- **Solidarity surcharge continues:** 2.5% on income EUR 80,000–250,000; 5% above EUR 250,000. (EY p.1290)
- **Residence permit rules tightened (Law 61/2025):** Job Seeker Visa now limited to highly qualified professionals; family reunification applications must prove adequate housing; applications generally only after 2 years of legal residence. (EY p.1297–1298)
