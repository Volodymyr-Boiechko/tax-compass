## DIGITAL NOMAD / NEW RESIDENT NOTE

> Uruguay is highly attractive for new tax residents:
> - **New residents:** Optional 5-year exemption on all foreign-source income (IRPF applies only to Uruguayan-source income for the first 5 years). Alternatively, may elect to pay a flat 7% on foreign income instead of the standard rates.
> - **IRPF Category II (labor income):** Progressive 10%–36% on net income.
> - **IRPF Category I (capital gains):** Flat 12%.
> - **Territorial element:** Foreign-source investment income (Category I) not taxed unless certain conditions are met. Foreign-source labor income: also subject to conditions.
> - **Considered digital nomad-friendly** due to the 5-year foreign income exemption and straightforward residency process.

## Personal Income Tax (IRPF — Impuesto a la Renta de las Personas Físicas)

Effective from 1 July 2007. Resident individuals subject to IRPF on Uruguayan-source income AND, under certain conditions, on foreign-source capital gains and work income. Income divided into two categories: (EY p.1687)

**Category I — Capital Income (Rentas de Capital):**
- Basic rate: **12%** on capital gains, investment income
- Includes: dividends from Uruguayan companies, interest, rental income, capital gains from sale of assets
- Foreign-source capital income: generally exempt unless "linked" to Uruguayan-source activities

**Category II — Labor Income (Rentas de Trabajo):**
- Progressive rates: **10% to 36%**
- Includes: employment income, pensions, self-employment income, directors' fees
- A specified amount of income (non-taxable minimum) is not subject to tax

**IRPF Category II progressive rates (labor income):** (EY p.1687 — rates confirmed 10%–36%; brackets from DGI supplemented)

| Annual taxable income (UYU) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ~192,480 (≈ 7 × BPC) | 0% (non-taxable minimum) |
| ~192,480 – ~342,240 | 10% |
| ~342,240 – ~513,360 | 15% |
| ~513,360 – ~760,800 | 24% |
| ~760,800 – ~1,521,600 | 25% |
| ~1,521,600 – ~2,282,400 | 27% |
| ~2,282,400 – ~3,043,200 | 31% |
| Over ~3,043,200 | 36% |

**Note:** Brackets above are approximate 2024 values based on BPC (Base de Prestaciones y Contribuciones). The BPC is updated annually. For 2025, BPC = approximately UYU 6,752 (verify with DGI/BPS). All bracket thresholds must be verified annually at dgi.gub.uy.

**Pension income:** Subject to IASS (Impuesto de Asistencia a la Seguridad Social — Tax of Assistance to Social Security), similar rates to IRPF Category II. (EY p.1687)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Non-taxable minimum: approximately UYU 192,480/year (7 BPC; verify for 2025/2026)
- Dependent children allowance
- Mortgage interest on primary residence
- Employee SS contributions (BPS contributions) are deductible from IRPF base
- FONASA health contributions: deductible
- Medical expenses: some deductibility

**Notes:**
- Nonresidents: flat 12% IRPF on Uruguayan-source income (EY p.1687)
- Technical services by nonresidents from abroad: also subject to 12% if certain conditions met
- Capital gains: 12% flat (Category I); no progressive rates
- Real estate transfer tax: 4% (2% seller + 2% buyer) (EY p.1687)
- Net worth tax: 0.1% on net assets for residents (2024); for nonresidents not subject to income tax: rates 0.7%–1.5%; tax-free allowance UYU 6,381,000 (2024; 2025 amount to be determined) (EY p.1687–1688)
- Inheritance/gifts: 4% transfer tax (3% for direct-line consanguinity) (EY p.1688)
- Tax authority: DGI (Dirección General Impositiva)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (IRPF Category II)

- **Who qualifies:** All resident individuals with income from independent professional or trade services.
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus deductible SS contributions (BPS); progressive IRPF rates on net income.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 10%–36% on annual net income. Monthly advance payments required.
- **Social security:** BPS contributions on notional base (30% of invoiced total); pension fund contributions vary by category.
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IVA (Uruguay VAT) at 22% standard rate (10% reduced rate for some goods); monthly filing.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; SS calculated on notional income rather than actual earnings.

### Option B — Monotax (Monotributo)

- **Who qualifies:** Small operators with very low annual revenue (below threshold set by DGI/BPS). Primarily for small traders, not professional services.
- **Tax base / Rate:** Single monthly payment covers both IRPF and SS obligations.
- **Key limits:** Revenue ceiling applies — most professionals exceed the threshold; primarily for micro-traders.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### 5-Year Foreign Income Exemption for New Residents
- **Eligibility:** Individuals who become Uruguayan tax residents for the first time (having not previously been residents).
- **Benefit:** For the first **5 years** of Uruguayan residency, foreign-source income is completely exempt from IRPF. Only Uruguayan-source income is taxed during this period.
- **Alternative:** Instead of the 5-year exemption, new residents may elect to pay a **flat 7%** IRPF on all foreign-source income (in exchange for being considered "fiscally domiciled" in Uruguay for all purposes, including treaty eligibility). This election is available from year 1.
- **Duration:** 5-year exemption period (from first year of residency). After 5 years, standard worldwide income tax applies.
- **Application:** Notify DGI of the election at time of filing.
- **Caveats:** Applies only to Category I (capital income) and Category II (labor income) from foreign sources. Uruguayan-source income always taxable. (EY p.1687)

### Digital Nomad Attractiveness
- No specific "digital nomad visa" law but the 5-year foreign income exemption combined with straightforward residency requirements makes Uruguay very attractive for remote workers earning from foreign clients.
- Residency: permanent visa application requires passport, health certificate, proof of financial means, criminal records, birth certificate, Uruguayan address.
- Processing time: 3–6 months.
- No work permit as such — foreign national may work under permanent residence visa.
- No limitations on foreign nationals starting businesses or heading subsidiaries. (EY p.1691)
