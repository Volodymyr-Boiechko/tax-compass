## Personal Income Tax

Schedular (flat-rate) system — no progressive brackets. Different income types attract different flat rates.
Tax year = calendar year. Most employment income withheld at source by the employer (tax agent).

**IMPORTANT:** Ukraine is under martial law. Tax legislation may change rapidly. All figures reflect laws in force as of 1 January 2026.

Exchange rate reference: UAH 42.0295 = USD 1 as of 1 January 2025 (EY p. 1580).

| Income type | PIT rate | Military levy (від 01.12.2024) | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employment wages — standard | 18% | 5% | **23%** |
| Employment / gig income — Diia City residents | 5% | 5% | **10%** |
| Dividends from Ukrainian resident companies | 5% | 5% | **10%** |
| Dividends from non-resident / collective-investment entities | 9% | 5% | **14%** |
| Royalties; interest from bank deposits; investment income | 18% | 5% | **23%** |
| Capital gains — investment assets (above UAH 4,240 threshold) | 18% | 5% | **23%** |
| Sale of real estate: held <3 yrs OR 2nd+ sale in reporting year | 5% | 5% | **10%** |
| Sale of real estate: held >3 yrs AND 1st sale in year | 0% | 0% | **0%** |
| Prizes / non-statutory lottery gains | 18% | 5% | **23%** |
| Gifts / inheritances from first-degree relatives | 0% | 0% | **0%** |
| Gifts / inheritances from non-relatives or non-residents | 18% | 5% | **23%** |
| Self-employment income — simplified-tax ФОП | PIT-exempt (single tax applies instead) | 1% on revenue (group 3) | see ФОП section |

(EY pp. 1584–1585)

**Military levy detail (EY p. 1585):**
- From 3 August 2014: 1.5% on wages and analogous compensation
- From **1 December 2024**: raised to **5%** on all personal income to which PIT applies (Law No. 4015-IX, 10 October 2024)
- Rate stays at 5% until 1 January of the year **following the end of martial law**
- **Exception for ФОП on single-tax system:** group 3 pays 1% of revenue as military levy (not 5%); groups 1 and 2 pay fixed monthly amounts (UAH 864.70/month in 2026 = 10% of minimum wage). This is a carve-out within the simplified-tax code.

**Deductions / social tax benefit (EY pp. 1583–1584):**
- Standard social tax benefit (соціальна пільга): reduces taxable salary by **0.5 × subsistence minimum = UAH 1,514/month** (subsistence min = UAH 3,028 as of 1 January 2025)
- Higher benefits (up to 2 × subsistence min) for single parents, disabled persons, Chernobyl victims, war veterans, etc.
- Applies **only** if gross monthly salary ≤ 2.5 × subsistence minimum = **UAH 7,570/month** (~UAH 91k/year ≈ €2k/year at UAH 44/EUR)
- At any professionally relevant salary this threshold is not met; benefit is irrelevant for scenarios below

**Annual tax discount (filed via return by 31 December following year):**
- Education expenses (self + immediate family)
- Mortgage interest (portion)
- Charitable donations ≤ 4% of annual taxable income
- Long-term life insurance premiums
- Housing costs for internally displaced persons (IDPs)
- Equipment to convert vehicles to biofuel
(EY pp. 1583–1584)

## Social Security

**Employment — Unified Social Tax (ЄСВ):**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Unified Social Tax (ЄСВ) | **0%** | **22%** |
| **Total** | **0%** | **22%** |

- Base cap: **15 × minimum monthly wage = 15 × UAH 8,647 = UAH 129,705/month** (2026; the 20× cap was enacted in the State Budget Law for 2026 but POSTPONED for 2026 — PwC other-taxes confirmed; EY file incorrectly stated 20×)
- Employer pays 22% on gross salary up to cap; above cap no ЄСВ
- **Employees pay no ЄСВ** — employer-only obligation
- Foreign employers domiciled outside Ukraine and paying salary from abroad do NOT pay Ukrainian ЄСВ (EY p. 1585–1586)

**ФОП (self-employed) ЄСВ — 2026 figures:**

| | Per month | Per quarter | Annual |
|---|---|---|---|
| Minimum ЄСВ | UAH 1,902.34 (= 22% × UAH 8,647) | UAH 5,707.02 | UAH 22,828 |
| Maximum ЄСВ | UAH 28,535.10 (= 22% × UAH 129,705) | UAH 85,605 | UAH 342,421 |

ФОП are required to pay at least the minimum ЄСВ regardless of whether they had any income.
In practice, the overwhelming majority of ФОП pay only the minimum.
(Sources: tax.gov.ua 2026 announcement; pfu.gov.ua 2026 ЄСВ notice)

**Note:** The 2025 minimum ЄСВ was ~UAH 1,760/month (22% × UAH 8,000 minimum wage). The 2026 increase to UAH 8,647 raised it to UAH 1,902.34.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — ФОП Group 3 (Спрощена система оподаткування — Єдиний податок)

- **Who qualifies:** Any natural person registered as a private entrepreneur (ФОП — фізична особа-підприємець) with annual gross revenue below **UAH 10,091,049** (= 1,167 × minimum monthly wage of UAH 8,647). No restriction on employee count for group 3.
- **Tax base:** **Gross revenue** received in cash or in kind (cash-receipts basis). No expense deductions.
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - **Єдиний податок (single tax):** **5%** of gross revenue — for non-VAT-registered ФОП
  - Alternatively **3%** of gross revenue for ФОП registered as VAT payers
  - **Військовий збір (military levy):** **1%** of gross revenue (special carve-out rate for group 3 single-tax payers; does NOT follow the 5% general levy)
  - Income under the simplified system is **fully exempt from PIT (18%)**
  - **Total effective tax on revenue (non-VAT):** 5% + 1% = **6%**
- **Social security:** ЄСВ minimum = UAH 1,902.34/month = **UAH 22,828/year** (fixed; independent of revenue; paid quarterly within 20 days after each quarter)
- **VAT obligations:**
  - Non-VAT option (5%): no VAT obligations; clients cannot reclaim VAT
  - VAT option (3%): must register for VAT; standard 20% VAT on services; input VAT recoverable
  - Mandatory VAT registration threshold is UAH 1,000,000/year (standard) — but group 3 ФОП can stay non-VAT up to the group limit
- **Reporting:** Quarterly declaration (within 40 days of quarter-end); single tax and military levy paid simultaneously
- **Prohibited activities:** Financial intermediation, insurance, gambling, certain extractive industries, law practice (notaries)
- **Key limits:**
  - Annual revenue cap: UAH 10,091,049 (~€229k at UAH 44/EUR) — excess is taxed at 15%
  - May not receive income from former employer under same activity category in same year (anti-avoidance)

(Sources: tax.gov.ua 2026; smartfin.ua 2026; dou.ua 2026 tax changes; 7eminar.ua; easyfop.com)

### Option B — Diia City Gig-Specialist (Гіг-Спеціаліст Резидента Дія.Сіті)

- **Who qualifies:** IT professionals working under gig contracts with companies holding **Diia City resident status** — a special regime for Ukraine's tech industry (administered by the Ministry of Digital Transformation).
- **Tax base:** Full remuneration received under the gig contract.
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - **PIT:** **5%** (vs. standard 18% for regular employees) — withheld at source by the Diia City company
  - **Military levy:** **5%** (increased from 1.5% as of 1 December 2024; Law No. 4015-IX)
  - **Total tax on remuneration: 10%** (5% PIT + 5% military levy)
- **Social security:** The Diia City company pays 22% ЄСВ on gig fees at employer's cost (up to the UAH 172,940/month cap); the gig-specialist does not pay employee-side ЄСВ. See Known Gaps — exact ЄСВ treatment for gig-specialists under Diia City needs verification.
- **VAT obligations:** None for the individual gig-specialist.
- **Key limits:**
  - No stated income ceiling for the 5% PIT rate
  - Company must maintain Diia City resident status (qualifying IT revenue ≥ 90%, minimum headcount, etc.)
  - Pre-December 2024, total burden was 5% + 1.5% = 6.5%; now 10%

(EY p. 1585; factor.academy; Law No. 4015-IX of 10 October 2024)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Diia City (for IT workers — residents and non-residents)

- **Eligibility:** Employees and gig-contractors of companies with active Diia City resident status. Available to both Ukrainian citizens and foreign nationals.
- **Benefit:** 5% PIT instead of 18% on wages/gig income. Foreign workers benefit equally.
- **Duration:** Ongoing while company holds Diia City status; regime's duration tied to Ukrainian legislation.
- **Caveats:** Military levy rose to 5% from December 2024; total is now 10%, not the original ~6.5%. No residency requirement for Diia City companies to hire foreigners, but immigration/work permit rules still apply.

### No Standard Newcomer/Expat Regime

Ukraine does not currently offer a general remittance-basis or territorial-tax regime for new residents. All Ukrainian tax residents pay full PIT on worldwide income.

## 2026 Changes

- **Military levy 5%** (from 1.5%) in effect from 1 December 2024 for all PIT-subject income; continues through 2026 (EY p. 1585; Law 4015-IX)
- **Minimum wage raised to UAH 8,647/month** from 1 January 2026 (from UAH 8,000 in 2025), triggering:
  - ЄСВ minimum for ФОП: raised to **UAH 1,902.34/month** (from ~UAH 1,760)
  - ЄСВ cap base: **UAH 129,705/month** (15× UAH 8,647; the 20× cap enacted in 2026 State Budget Law was POSTPONED — remains at 15× per PwC confirmed 2026-05-31; EY file stated 20× which was incorrect)
  - ФОП Group 3 income limit: raised to **UAH 10,091,049/year** (from ~UAH 9,336,000)
  - ФОП Groups 1 and 2 fixed military levy: **UAH 864.70/month** (= 10% × UAH 8,647)
- **ФОП group 3 military levy remains 1%** of revenue (special carve-out maintained in 2026)
- **EY martial law note:** "At the time of writing, martial law was in force in Ukraine. As such, Ukrainian tax legislation may change rapidly." (EY p. 1580)
- No announced PIT rate changes for 2026.

(Sources: tax.gov.ua; pfu.gov.ua; dou.ua; misto.media January 2026)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> **1 EUR = UAH 44** (approximate; EY gives UAH 42.03 = USD 1 on 1 Jan 2025; EUR/USD ~1.04 → UAH/EUR ~43.7).
> "Employment" = gross salary received (employee-side only; employer's 22% ЄСВ is an additional cost not shown).
> "ФОП Group 3" = gross revenue invoiced; minimum ЄСВ; non-VAT; 5% single tax + 1% military levy.
> Military levy: 5% for employment (from Dec 2024); 1% for ФОП group 3.
> Social tax benefit does not apply above UAH 7,570/month (~€2k/month).

### Working: UAH 1,320,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| PIT (18%) | 18% × 1,320,000 | UAH 237,600 |
| Military levy (5%) | 5% × 1,320,000 | UAH 66,000 |
| Employee ЄСВ | 0% | UAH 0 |
| **Total burden** | 237,600 + 66,000 | **UAH 303,600** |
| **Effective rate** | 303,600 / 1,320,000 | **23.0%** |

### Working: UAH 1,320,000 (≈ €30,000) ФОП Group 3

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Single tax (5%) | 5% × 1,320,000 | UAH 66,000 |
| Military levy (1%) | 1% × 1,320,000 | UAH 13,200 |
| ЄСВ minimum (annual) | UAH 1,902.34 × 12 | UAH 22,828 |
| **Total burden** | 66,000 + 13,200 + 22,828 | **UAH 102,028** |
| **Effective rate** | 102,028 / 1,320,000 | **7.7%** |

All ФОП Group 3 income points are within the UAH 10,091,049 annual limit (~€229k).

**Diia City gig-specialist (for reference, not separately modeled):**
- Total tax: 10% flat (5% PIT + 5% military levy)
- At €30k: effective 10%; at €60k: effective 10%; at €100k: effective 10%
- Employer pays 22% ЄСВ on gig fee additionally (employer-side cost, not deducted from fee)

**Key insight — ФОП vs employment gap:** Ukraine's ФОП group 3 offers one of the lowest effective self-employment tax rates in Europe (~7% at €30k, ~6.5% at €100k). Employment is a flat 23% at all income levels. The €30k crossover shows a ~15 percentage-point gap — entirely due to the 6% revenue tax replacing 23% income + levy.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/UAH = 44 (approximate mid-2025 rate; actual NBU rate fluctuates; use official rate for precision)
- Employment: gross salary basis; no social tax benefit (irrelevant above UAH 7,570/month)
- ФОП Group 3: non-VAT (5% single tax); minimum ЄСВ only; full 12-month ЄСВ (no exemptions)
- Employer 22% ЄСВ not shown in employment scenario — adds UAH 290,400 at €30k
- No tax discounts, no charitable deductions applied
- ФОП revenue treated as invoiced (not net of expenses — there are no deductible expenses under single tax)
