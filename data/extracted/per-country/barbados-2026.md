## Personal Income Tax

Residents domiciled in Barbados: taxed on worldwide income. Residents not domiciled in Barbados: taxed on Barbados-source income, income from a Barbados office, employment income, and income remitted to Barbados. Non-residents: Barbados-source income only. Tax year = calendar year (ending 31 December). Filing deadline: 30 April. (EY p.118)

Resident = present in Barbados > 182 days aggregate in calendar year, OR ordinarily resident (permanent home + intent to reside ≥ 2 consecutive years). (EY p.118)

**PIT rates (effective from 1 January 2024):**

| Taxable income (USD) | Tax rate | Tax due (USD) | Cumulative tax due (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 25,000 | **12.5%** | 3,125 | 3,125 |
| Over 25,000 | **28.5%** | — | — |

(EY p.121)

Non-residents taxed at the same progressive rates, except for domestic dividends (5%), interest (0%), royalties (0%), management fees (0%), and technical services fees (25% withholding). (EY pp.119–121)

**Investment income (resident individuals):**
- Domestic dividends: 15% final withholding tax (dividends from dividend reinvestment plans held ≥ 5 years exempt up to USD 3,750/year)
- Interest received from resident borrowers (non-pensioners): 15% final withholding
- Interest for pensioners (age 60+): exempt
- Rental income from residential property: **15% flat rate**
- Royalties: 50% exempt for resident "authors" under Copyright Act; remainder taxed at regular rates; all other royalties: full regular rates
- Capital gains: **not subject to tax** in Barbados; capital losses not deductible (EY p.120)

**Personal deductions and allowances (2025 rates):**

| Deduction | Maximum amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| Basic personal allowance | 12,500 |
| Basic allowance for pensioners (age 60+) | 20,000 |
| Dependent spouse allowance | 1,500 |
| Green energy audit (150% of cost) / retrofitting (150% of 50% of cost) | Up to 5,000/year over 5-year period |
| Charitable donations to exempt charities | Allowed in full |
| Donations to registered charities (≤ USD 500,000) | 10% of assessable income |
| Donations to registered charities (> USD 500,000) | 50% of assessable income |
| Medical expenses (persons over 40) | Up to 375 |

(EY p.120)

**International Business / Financial Services Sector — Special Exemption (qualifying individuals):**
Specially qualified individuals in international business and financial services with a valid work permit for ≥ 3 years may qualify for tax exemptions as a percentage of remuneration:

| Income level | Percentage exemption |
|---|---|
| Income not exceeding USD 75,000 | **35%** |
| Income USD 75,001 – USD 250,000 | **50%** |
| Income exceeding USD 250,000 | **60%** |

(EY p.120)

**Foreign currency earnings credit (effective 1 January 2025):**
Residents earning income from sources outside Barbados remitted through the banking system may claim a rebate of income tax on the foreign earnings:

| Foreign earnings as % of total earnings | Rebate of income tax (as % of tax on foreign earnings) |
|---|---|
| 20% and under | 24% |
| Over 20% but under 41% | 31% |
| At least 41% but under 61% | 45% |
| At least 61% but under 81% | 55% |
| 81% and over | 65% |

(EY p.121)

**Notes:**
- PAYE system for employees; quarterly prepayments for self-employed (based on prior year tax)
- Married couples taxed separately on all income types
- Stock option benefit: taxable at market price minus exercise price; 10% deduction of assessable income applies; recaptured if shares sold within 5 years (EY p.120)
- Business losses: carry forward up to 5 years (from 2025); offset limited to 50% of assessable income (from 2019 income year) (EY p.121)

## Social Security

National Insurance Scheme (NIS) — mandatory for employees, employers, and self-employed. (EY p.122)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| National Insurance | **11.1%** | **12.75%** | **17.1%** |

- Applied on maximum monthly insurable earnings of **USD 2,640/month** (USD 31,680/year) — current ceiling
- **2026 increase:** Monthly insurable earnings ceiling increases to **USD 2,680/month** in 2026 (EY p.122)
- Maximum employee annual contribution: ~USD 3,516 (11.1% × USD 31,680)
- Maximum self-employed annual contribution: ~USD 5,417 (17.1% × USD 31,680)

**Totalization agreements:** Canada, CARICOM, United Kingdom (EY p.122)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader / Self-Employed

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual conducting business in Barbados as a sole proprietor
- **Tax base:** Net business income (business profits per financial statements, subject to tax adjustments; all wholly and exclusively incurred expenses deductible)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT rates: 12.5% on first USD 25,000, 28.5% on excess; basic allowance of USD 12,500 deductible
- **Social security:** NIS at 17.1% on insurable earnings up to USD 2,640/month (USD 31,680/year)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual taxable supplies ≥ BBD 200,000 (~USD 100,000); VAT rate 17.5%
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling for standard regime; losses carry forward 5 years

(EY pp.118–121)

### Option B — Incorporated Entity (IBC / International Business Company)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals who incorporate and draw salary or dividends; International Business Companies have special tax treatment
- **Tax base:** Personal salary: standard PIT 12.5%/28.5%; Dividends: 15% WHT
- **Rate / mechanism:** Entity-level corporate tax + personal tax on distributions; qualifying IB sector workers may access exemption regime (35%–60%)
- **Social security:** NIS applies to salary drawn from company
- **Key limits:** IB sector exemption requires valid work permit for ≥ 3 years

(EY p.120)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Barbados 12-Month Welcome Stamp Visa

- **Eligibility:** Non-nationals employed in a country other than Barbados, working remotely for companies and individuals outside Barbados; income ≥ USD 50,000/year; medical insurance required
- **Benefit:** Work remotely from Barbados for 12 months; **not liable to pay Barbados income tax** while working for employers/clients outside Barbados; free movement in and out of Barbados during the 12-month period
- **Fees:** USD 2,000 (individual), USD 3,000 (family bundle); renewal: USD 1,500 / USD 2,250
- **Duration:** 12 months; multiple applications allowed
- **Application:** Decided within 5 working days; visa issued via email (EY pp.125–126)

### International Business / Financial Services Sector Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Specially qualified individuals holding valid work permits for ≥ 3 years in international business and financial services sector
- **Benefit:** 35%–60% income tax exemption depending on income level
- **Duration:** Ongoing with valid work permit
- **Caveats:** Must be in qualifying sector with valid work permit (EY p.120)

### Special Entry Permit for High Net-Worth Individuals

- **Eligibility:** High net-worth individuals; assets > USD 5 million (over 60) or > USD 300,000 (under 60); health insurance > USD 500,000 (over 50) or > USD 350,000 (under 50)
- **Benefit:** Residency in Barbados (leverages 0% capital gains, favorable income tax)
- **Fees:** USD 150 application + USD 3,500–5,000 approval fee (EY pp.126–127)

## 2026 Changes

- **NIS insurable earnings ceiling increase to USD 2,680/month in 2026** (from USD 2,640/month) — increases maximum NIS contributions (EY p.122)
- **Foreign currency earnings credit (from 1 January 2025):** New rebate mechanism for residents remitting foreign earnings through Barbados banking system (EY p.121)
- **Business loss carry-forward extended to 5 years from 2025** (EY p.121)
- **Free movement agreement (1 October 2025):** Barbados introduced free movement with Belize, Dominica, and St. Vincent and the Grenadines — nationals may live and work without visa or work permit (EY p.124)
- **Norway tax treaty terminated from 1 January 2024** (EY p.123)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (basic personal allowance USD 12,500).
> **1 EUR ≈ USD 1.08** (Barbados Dollar BBD pegged at 2:1 to USD; incomes stated in USD throughout EY).
> Employment scenario: progressive PIT; NIS 11.1% capped at USD 31,680/year base.
> B2B scenario: self-employed; NIS 17.1% capped; standard regime (12.5%/28.5%).
> Welcome Stamp visa holders working for foreign employers: 0% Barbados income tax.

### €30,000 Employment (≈ USD 32,400/year)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross income | USD 32,400 | |
| Basic allowance | USD 12,500 | |
| Taxable income | 32,400 − 12,500 | USD 19,900 |
| PIT: 12.5% on USD 19,900 | 12.5% × 19,900 | USD 2,488 |
| NIS (11.1%): 11.1% × 31,680 (cap) | (32,400 < cap; 11.1% × 32,400) | USD 3,596 |
| **Total burden** | 2,488 + 3,596 | **USD 6,084** |
| **Effective rate** | 6,084 / 32,400 | **18.8%** |
