## Personal Income Tax

Progressive income tax (*impôt sur le revenu des personnes physiques*). Tax year = calendar year. Annual return filed by 31 December of the following year (deadline cannot be extended). Employers withhold wage tax monthly. Three tax classes: Class 1 (single individuals), Class 1a (single with dependant child or aged 65+), Class 2 (married couples / registered partners — joint taxation). (EY pp. 895, 900, 902)

**Marginal rate threshold:** 45.78% marginal rate applies above EUR 234,870 (Class 1 single) and EUR 469,740 (Class 2 married couple jointly taxed) (EY p. 900)

**Unemployment fund contribution:** Maximum 9% surcharge included in the above rates; the fund contribution rate varies by income level and is incorporated into the effective rates shown.

**Investment income:**
- Dividends from qualifying Luxembourg/EU/treaty-country companies: 50% exemption; 15% withholding tax at source (offsettable or refundable) (EY p. 896)
- Interest from private assets: final 20% withholding tax option; annual tax-free allowance of EUR 250 per person per paying agent; or EUR 1,500 exemption (doubled for joint taxpayers) from progressive tax (EY p. 896)
- Royalties and real estate rental income: taxed at progressive rates (EY p. 897)

**Capital gains:**
- *Movable assets (substantial shareholdings >10%):* Fully taxable; 50% average rate reduction and EUR 50,000 relief (doubled for joint taxpayers) if held >6 months; dependence insurance 1.4% also applies (EY p. 898)
- *Non-substantial shareholdings and investment fund units (≤10%):* Tax-free if held >6 months (EY p. 898)
- *Real estate:* Gains on buildings/land sold within 2 years of purchase: taxable as ordinary income. Gains on real estate held >2 years: taxable at half the normal rate (maximum 21.4% or 21.8%), EUR 50,000 standard exemption (doubled for joint taxpayers), inflation adjustment (EY pp. 898–899)
- *Principal residence:* Exempt (EY p. 899)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY pp. 899–900)
- Standard employment expense deduction: EUR 540 (doubled for married couple where both earn employment income)
- Lump-sum deduction of EUR 25 for dividend/interest-related expenses (EUR 50 for joint taxpayers)
- Annual ceiling of EUR 672 per household person: voluntary life/accident/sickness/unemployment insurance, third-party auto insurance, consumer loan interest; can be increased to EUR 1,344 for subscribers aged 18–40 for home-saving contributions
- Old-age providence premiums: deductible up to EUR 3,200/year (if conditions met)
- Mortgage interest: fully deductible for recent purchases (ceiling decreases with years of occupation; full deduction after 31 December 2022 occupancy)
- Social security contributions (except care insurance): deductible from taxable income
- Extraordinary expenses (hospitalization, close relative maintenance, handicapped persons, childcare, domestic staff): deductible if specific conditions met (EY pp. 899–900)

**Additional dependence insurance:**
- Employee rate: **1.4%** on total gross income (no ceiling) (EY p. 902)
- Applicable after annual deduction of a quarter of minimum salary for unskilled workers (EUR 7,913.40, applicable May 2025)
- Employers NOT subject to dependence insurance contribution

**Income ceiling (pension, illness, accident, mutual insurance):** EUR 160,905.28 annual (ceiling may increase automatically per salary increase mechanism) (EY p. 902)

**Self-employed:**
- Must register for social security
- Applicable rates are those for employers and employees combined (EY p. 902)
- Total approximate: pension (16%) + illness (6.1%) + dependence (1.4%) = ~23.5% before other contributions

**Totalization agreements:** EU Regulation 883/2004; bilateral agreements with numerous countries including Argentina, Brazil, Canada, Cape Verde, Chile, China Mainland, India, Japan, Korea (South), Moldova, Montenegro, Morocco, Philippines, Quebec, Serbia, Tunisia, Türkiye, United States, Uruguay, Yugoslavia (former) (EY p. 902)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed Individual (Net Business Income)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals carrying on a business or profession independently in Luxembourg
- **Tax base:** Net business profit (all business expenses deductible: materials, staff, finance charges, self-employed social contributions, depreciation, provisions, loss carryforwards within limits)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive income tax rates identical to employment (maximum marginal 45.78%); municipal business tax (taxe commerciale) also applies on trade profit with EUR 40,000 standard exemption, approximately 7.5% (6.75% for Luxembourg City) (EY p. 900)
- **Social security:** Self-employed pay combined employer + employee rates; approximately: pension 16% + illness 6.1% + dependence 1.4% + other = ~23.5%
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above EUR 35,000 annual turnover (standard rate 17%); reduced rates 3%, 8% for certain goods/services
- **Key limits:** Losses can be carried forward without time limit (for losses incurred in or after 1991); losses from after 31 December 2016 limited to 17-year carryforward; no carryback (EY p. 901)
- **Quarterly tax prepayments** required, based on most recent final assessment (EY p. 903)

### Option B — Expatriate Tax Regime (Highly Skilled Employees)

- **Who qualifies:** Employees temporarily assigned intragroup or directly recruited from abroad by a Luxembourg company; minimum base salary EUR 75,000; not resident in Luxembourg or its border area (150km rule) in the 5 years before employment; at least 75% working time in Luxembourg
- **Benefit (from 1 January 2025 — revised regime):** Flat 50% exemption on gross annual remuneration (excluding benefits in kind and other tax-exempt income); maximum exempt remuneration: EUR 400,000 gross (i.e., up to EUR 200,000 exempted)
- **Duration:** Throughout assignment/employment and until end of eighth tax year in Luxembourg
- **Notes:** Previous regime (specific expense exemptions) continues for employees already benefiting unless they irrevocably opt into the new regime. New regime introduced simplified approach replacing the prior itemised expense exemption method. (EY p. 897)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Highly Skilled Employee Regime

- **Eligibility:** Intragroup assignees or direct hires from abroad; base salary ≥ EUR 75,000; non-resident for 5 years prior in Luxembourg or 150km border area; ≥75% working time in Luxembourg
- **Benefit:** 50% flat exemption on gross remuneration up to EUR 400,000 (from 1 January 2025 revised rules)
- **Duration:** Until end of eighth tax year in Luxembourg
- **Application deadline:** Formal notification to tax authorities required
- **Caveats:** Only available to employees (not self-employed); prior regime available for those already enrolled (EY pp. 897–898)

### Frontier Workers

- Luxembourg has Memoranda of Understanding with Belgium and Germany; comprehensive new double tax treaty with France
- **34-day teleworking rule:** Non-residents can work up to 34 days per calendar year outside Luxembourg without triggering taxation in their country of residency
- France: effective as of 1 January 2023 (retroactively); Belgium: as of 1 January 2022; Germany: as of 1 January 2024 (EY pp. 903–904)

### Joint Taxation (Class 2)

- Married individuals and registered partners (Luxembourg or foreign law registration): eligible for joint taxation (Class 2); significant tax benefit as it effectively splits income between spouses, dramatically reducing effective rates (see rate table above)
- Joint return eligibility: partners must share common domicile/residence; partnership must exist for entire fiscal year (EY p. 895)

## 2026 Changes

- **No specific 2026 legislative changes** noted in EY guide for Luxembourg
- **Expatriate regime:** Already revised effective 1 January 2025 (new simplified 50% exemption); employees on prior regime may continue or opt into new regime
- **Frontier worker telework rules:** Germany–Luxembourg 34-day rule applicable from 1 January 2024 (already in force)
- **Social security ceiling:** EUR 160,905.28 (subject to automatic salary increase mechanism); rate unchanged from 2025

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Full progressive tax bracket schedule:** EY provides effective rates but not the marginal bracket table (thresholds in EUR at each rate). Full bracket schedule available from Luxembourg Tax Administration (ACD — Administration des contributions directes).
- [ ] **Unemployment fund contribution rate:** EY includes maximum 9% in the 45.78% marginal rate but does not publish the exact income-graduated schedule for the unemployment fund contribution. Affects effective rates at different income levels.
- [ ] **Municipal business tax exact rate:** Approximately 7.5% (6.75% for Luxembourg City) but varies by municipality; added cost for self-employed not included in effective rate table.
- [ ] **Dependence insurance exact floor deduction:** EUR 7,913.40 deduction applicable May 2025; subject to minimum salary increases.
- [ ] **Self-employed social security exact total:** EY states combined employer + employee rates apply; exact total depends on whether accident and mutual insurance rates (variable) apply to self-employed.
- [ ] **Class 2 joint taxation effective rates:** Not calculated above; married couples benefit significantly from income splitting — effective rates dramatically lower (see EY table on p. 900).
