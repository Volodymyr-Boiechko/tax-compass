## Personal Income Tax

> Luxembourg has a progressive income tax with a solidarity surcharge (9% of tax for most taxpayers, integrated into the published maximum rate of 45.78%). Tax year = calendar year. Tax classes: Class 1 (single), Class 1a (single with dependent children or age в‰Ґ64), Class 2 (married/registered partners jointly taxed). **Tax class thresholds (2025, including 9% surcharge):**
- Tax Class 1 (single): marginal rate 45.78% at income > EUR 234,870

- Personal deductions/allowances (each item separately, annual ceilings per person in household): EUR 672 each for:

  - Consumer loan interest

- Old-age provision premiums: up to EUR 3,200/year
- Mortgage interest on owner-occupied residence: full deduction for loans after 31 December 2022; EUR 4,000 ceiling for loans between 31 December 2018 and 1 January 2023 (EY p. 899)
- Extraordinary expenses: hospitalization not covered by insurer, maintenance of close relatives, disability-related expenses, childcare, domestic staff (EY p. 900)

**Notes:**
- No net wealth tax for individuals (EY p. 901)
- Inheritance tax: 0% direct line (children, grandchildren, parents); spouse exempt; other rates 2%вЂ“48% (EY p. 901)
- Dividends from qualifying sources: 50% exemption (Luxembourg resident company fully taxable; EU/treaty-country companies also eligible) (EY p. 896)

- Capital gains on substantial holdings (>10%): taxable at half average rate; EUR 50,000 relief for single (EUR 100,000 married)
- Capital gains on real estate sold >2 years after purchase: half normal rate; EUR 50,000 allowance (renewed every 10 years)
- Frontier worker rules: 34 telework days allowed in residence country without triggering taxation there (Belgium and Germany); similar rule with France (EY p. 904)

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals and sole traders in Luxembourg are taxed on net business income at progressive PIT rates. They must register for social security (employer + employee combined rates).

### Option A вЂ” Sole Trader / Individual Business (CommerГ§ant / IndГ©pendant)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying out commercial, artisanal, or professional activity in Luxembourg
- **Tax base:** Net business income (revenue minus all business expenses, depreciation); same progressive rates as employment income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT rates (max 45.78% including unemployment surcharge); quarterly prepayments based on prior year assessment
- **Social security:** Self-employed pay both employer and employee sides: ~12.45% (employee) + ~11.26% (employer) = ~23.71% total on income up to ceiling EUR 160,905; dependence insurance 1.4% on all income
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above EUR 35,000 annual turnover; standard VAT rate 17% (one of Europe's lowest); reduced rates 8%, 3%, and super-reduced 3%
- **Key limits:** Municipal business tax: ~7.5% (Luxembourg City: 6.75%) on trade profit above EUR 40,000 exemption; applies in addition to PIT; losses may be carried forward indefinitely (post-2016: limited to 17 years); no carryback (EY p. 901)

### Option B вЂ” Incorporation (SARL)
- **Who qualifies:** Any entrepreneur
- **Tax base:** Company profits at 17% CIT + ~7.5% municipal business tax в‰€ 24.94% effective; director salary deductible
- **Rate / mechanism:** Director draws salary (progressive PIT rates) + dividends (50% exempt if qualifying); overall effective rate may be optimized
- **Social security:** Director/manager: social security at combined rates on salary
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT 17%
- **Key limits:** Minimum share capital EUR 12,000 for SARL; annual filing and accounts required

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Tax Regime (Highly Skilled Employees вЂ” RГ©gime impatriГ©s)
- **Eligibility:** Employees temporarily assigned to Luxembourg between intragroup entities OR directly recruited abroad by Luxembourg company; minimum annual salary EUR 75,000; must not have been Luxembourg resident or resident within 150km of Luxembourg border during 5 years preceding assignment; at least 75% of working time must be spent in Luxembourg
- **Benefit (from 1 January 2025 вЂ” new simplified regime):** Flat 50% exemption of gross annual remuneration (capped at total remuneration of EUR 400,000); exemption amount applied regardless of actual relocation expenses; excludes benefits in kind and other tax-exempt income. Previous regime: specific itemized expense reimbursements (housing, travel, school fees)
- **Duration:** Applicable throughout the assignment or employment duration, until end of 8th tax year in Luxembourg
- **Application deadline:** Must apply upon commencement; existing beneficiaries may continue old regime unless they irrevocably switch to new regime (EY p. 897)
- **Caveats:** Employees already on old regime will continue on it by default; new regime significantly more straightforward; Luxembourg social security still applies in full; effective rate substantially reduced for qualifying employees

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 895вЂ“909)

- **Expatriate regime significantly revised from 1 January 2025** (new 50% flat exemption on gross remuneration up to EUR 400,000) вЂ” first full year effect in 2026 for employees who commenced in 2025
- Frontier worker telework rules extended and updated: 34 telework days applicable with Belgium (from 2022) and Germany (from 2024); agreement with France (retroactive to 1 January 2023)
- Annual income tax brackets indexed per automatic adjustment (AICM); 2026 thresholds not yet published at EY review
- Social security ceiling: EUR 160,905.28 (May 2025); automatic increases may apply
