## CRITICAL DISCLAIMER — VENEZUELA

> **Venezuela's tax system operates under conditions of extreme economic instability. The following caveats are essential:**
>
> 1. **Tax Units (UT/TU — Unidad Tributaria):** All thresholds and brackets are expressed in Tax Units (TU), not in nominal Bolívares (VES). The TU value is set by SENIAT (the tax authority) and adjusted periodically, often with long delays vs. actual inflation. For the **2025 tax year, 1 TU = VES 42** (EY p.1700). For **2024**, 1 TU = VES 9. This means the TU value increased approximately 4.7× in one year.
> 2. **Exchange rates:** Venezuela has multiple exchange rates. The "official" rate published by the Banco Central de Venezuela (BCV) has historically differed enormously from parallel/informal market rates. As of EY data date (October 2025), the official rate must be verified at bcv.org.ve. EUR/VES conversion is extremely unreliable.
> 3. **Hyperinflation history:** Venezuela experienced hyperinflation exceeding 1,000,000% annually in 2018–2019. While inflation has moderated since dollarization became widespread (2019+), the VES remains highly volatile.
> 4. **USD dollarization:** A large portion of Venezuela's economy operates in USD. Many salaries and transactions are denominated in USD, not VES. Tax obligations are in VES but employers often pay salaries in USD.
> 5. **All figures in this file should be treated as highly approximate.** The effective rates shown are estimates under normal operating assumptions and may not reflect the reality of Venezuelan economic conditions.

## Personal Income Tax

Worldwide income for residents. Nonresidents taxed on Venezuelan-source income at a flat 34% rate. Residency: > 183 days in current or immediately preceding calendar year. Additionally, an individual who has acquired tax residency in a jurisdiction with which Venezuela has a valid double tax treaty is protected under the independent or dependent personal services clause. Tax year: calendar year. Returns filed by 31 March. Tax liability paid in three portions: first when return is filed; second within 20 days of filing; third within 40 days. (EY pp.1700–1701, 1704)

**Resident PIT triggered when:** Annual gross income exceeds **1,500 TU** (approx. 2025: 1,500 × VES 42 = VES 63,000) OR annual net taxable income exceeds **1,000 TU** (approx. 2025: VES 42,000). (EY p.1700)

**PIT rates — Tariff No. 1 (resident individuals — Tabla 1):** (EY p.1702)

| Taxable income — Exceeding (TU) | Not exceeding (TU) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 1,000 | 6% |
| 1,000 | 1,500 | 9% |
| 1,500 | 2,000 | 12% |
| 2,000 | 2,500 | 16% |
| 2,500 | 3,000 | 20% |
| 3,000 | 4,000 | 24% |
| 4,000 | 6,000 | 29% |
| 6,000 | — | 34% |

**2025 TU-to-VES conversion:** 1 TU = VES 42 (2025 tax year). (EY p.1700)

**Bracket thresholds in VES (2025):**

| Taxable income (VES, 2025) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 42,000 | 6% |
| 42,000 – 63,000 | 9% |
| 63,000 – 84,000 | 12% |
| 84,000 – 105,000 | 16% |
| 105,000 – 126,000 | 20% |
| 126,000 – 168,000 | 24% |
| 168,000 – 252,000 | 29% |
| Over 252,000 | 34% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY pp.1701–1702)
- Mortgage interest: up to 1,000 TU (VES 42,000 in 2025)
- Rent for primary dwelling: up to 800 TU (VES 33,600 in 2025)
- Education expenses (taxpayer, children < 25 years): for Venezuelans or foreigners in Venezuela
- Medical/health/surgery/hospitalization insurance premiums (from Venezuelan domiciled companies): unlimited
- Medical, dental and hospitalization expenses: unlimited (for taxpayer, spouse, ascendants, descendants)
- Personal annual rebate: 10 TU (VES 420 in 2025) — for all resident individuals
- Family rebate: 10 TU (VES 420 in 2025) per qualifying family member (spouse, ascendants, descendants under legal age and unable to work or studying and < 25 years old who lives in Venezuela)
- Standard deduction alternative: 774 TU (VES 32,508 in 2025) — no documentation required; replaces all itemized deductions listed above

**Exempt income:** (EY p.1701)
- Severance indemnities received by employees
- Travel-expense reimbursements for rendering personal services

**Notes:**
- Nonresidents: flat 34% withholding on Venezuelan-source income (EY p.1700)
- Dividends: 34% withholding (effective from January 2001) to the extent income before taxes exceeds net taxable income; recipients taxed at same rate on dividends from non-Venezuelan companies, less foreign taxes paid (EY p.1701)
- Capital gains: taxed with other income at Tariff No. 1 progressive rates (EY p.1701)
- Interest from Venezuelan banks and financial institutions: **tax-exempt** for residents and nonresidents (EY p.1701)
- Directors' fees: employees taxed at regular rates; others taxed as self-employment income (EY p.1701)
- Loss carryforward for self-employed: 3 years; limited to 25% of income each year; no carryback (EY p.1702)
- Equity Tax: 0.25% on net worth of "Special Taxpayers" (large enterprises, determined by SENIAT) — min 0.25%, max 1.5% (EY pp.1702–1703)
- Tax authority: SENIAT (Servicio Nacional Integrado de Administración Aduanera y Tributaria)
- Double tax treaties: Austria, Barbados, Belarus, Belgium, Brazil, Canada, China, Cuba, Czech Republic, Denmark, France, Germany, Indonesia, Iran, Italy, Korea (South), Kuwait, Malaysia, Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Qatar, Russian Federation, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Trinidad and Tobago, UAE, UK, USA, Vietnam (EY pp.1704–1705)

## Social Security (IVSS — Instituto Venezolano de los Seguros Sociales)

Multiple separate contribution categories, each with a ceiling of 5 or 10 minimum salaries. (EY pp.1703–1704)

| Type of contribution | Employee % | Employer % | Cap |
|---|---|---|---|
| IVSS (social security) | 4% | 9% / 10% / 11% (a) | 5 minimum salaries/month |
| Unemployment insurance | 0.5% | 2% | 5 minimum salaries/month |
| Housing (BANAVIH) | 1% | 2% | 10 minimum salaries/month |
| INCES (training) | 0.5% on profit-sharing | 2% on total employee remuneration | — |
| Special SS Contribution | — | 9% | On total employee remuneration (incl. non-salary) |
| **Total employee (approx.)** | **~5.5%** | **~24%–31%** | — |

(a) Employer IVSS: 9% (regular), 10% (medium risk), 11% (high risk) — based on business activity.

**SS Caps:**
- IVSS and unemployment: 5 minimum salaries/month
- Housing: 10 minimum salaries/month
- **Minimum salary:** Updated by government decree; subject to extreme inflation — verify current rate at bcv.org.ve

**Notes:**
- SS contributions are based on a percentage of monthly salary up to the ceiling in minimum salaries
- Special SS Contribution (9% employer): levied on total employee remuneration including non-salary benefits
- Totalization agreements: Chile, Ecuador, Greece, Italy, Portugal, Spain, Uruguay (EY p.1704)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All resident individuals with income from independent personal services or trade/business activities.
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus deductible business expenses (all expenses essential to produce self-employment or business income). Net income taxed at progressive rates once above 1,000 TU threshold.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive Tariff No. 1 rates (6%–34%) on annual net taxable income in TU. SS contributions based on percentage of income (nonresident guidelines apply for certain calculations).
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IVA (Venezuela VAT) at 16% standard rate; monthly filing.
- **Key limits:** Loss carryforward 3 years, limited to 25% of income per year.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Specific Digital Nomad Visa
- Venezuela does not have a digital nomad visa or preferential tax regime for new residents as of EY data date.
- The economic situation and practical difficulties of obtaining Venezuelan residency make Venezuela unattractive for digital nomads.

### Work Visa (TR-L)
- Foreign nationals intending to provide services in Venezuela for more than 90 days must obtain a TR-L (Working Transient Visa) and labor permit.
- Valid for 1 year; renewable.
- For services lasting less than 90 days, no labor permit or visa required. (EY p.1705)

## 2026 Changes

- **TU value 2025:** VES 42 per TU (vs. VES 9 in 2024 — a 4.7× increase). The 2026 TU value will be set by SENIAT; likely to increase significantly again. **All VES amounts in this file will be outdated by the time the 2026 TU is set.**
- **Dollarization trend:** Growing proportion of Venezuela's economy operates in USD. PIT compliance in VES terms is complicated by this.
- **Equity Tax:** 0.25%–1.5% on net worth of Special Taxpayers — continues in 2026.
- **No structural PIT changes announced** but TU adjustment is effectively equivalent to a structural change each year.

## Effective Rate Examples

> **EXTREME CAUTION:** All calculations below are based on the 2025 TU value of VES 42. Given Venezuela's inflation trajectory, these figures may be entirely obsolete by 2026. The EUR equivalents are particularly unreliable.
>
> Exchange rate: 1 USD ≈ VES 51 (approximate BCV official rate, October 2025). 1 EUR ≈ VES 56 (approximate; 1 EUR ≈ 1.10 USD). Parallel/informal market rates may differ substantially.
>
> €30,000 ≈ USD 33,000 ≈ VES 1,683,000  
> €60,000 ≈ USD 66,000 ≈ VES 3,366,000  
> €100,000 ≈ USD 110,000 ≈ VES 5,610,000
>
> In TU terms (at VES 42/TU):  
> VES 1,683,000 ≈ 40,071 TU (≈ €30,000)  
> VES 3,366,000 ≈ 80,143 TU (≈ €60,000)  
> VES 5,610,000 ≈ 133,571 TU (≈ €100,000)
>
> These are extremely high TU amounts — at 40,000+ TU, the 34% top rate applies to almost the entire income above 6,000 TU. **The effective rate approaches 34% for all meaningful income levels in EUR terms.** The low TU thresholds mean that in practice any professional income expressed in USD/EUR will be in the top bracket almost entirely.

### Working: ~40,071 TU/year (≈ €30,000) — Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross income (TU) | ~40,071 TU | — |
| IVSS employee (~4% + 0.5% + 1% = 5.5%; capped at 5 min. salaries/month; effectively very low vs. gross) | Estimated max SS (small absolute amount due to low minimum salary cap) | ~VES 5,000 (est.) |
| Personal rebate | 10 TU = VES 420 | — |
| Standard deduction | 774 TU = VES 32,508 | — |
| Taxable income (TU) | 40,071 − 10 − 774 (or equivalent in TU) ≈ 39,287 TU | — |
| PIT at 34% top rate on virtually all income above 6,000 TU: tax on 6,000 TU = 6%×1000+9%×500+12%×500+16%×500+20%×500+24%×1000+29%×2000 = 60+45+60+80+100+240+580 = VES 1,165×42 wait; calculate in TU directly: 6%×1000=60+9%×500=45+12%×500=60+16%×500=80+20%×500=100+24%×1000=240+29%×2000=580 = 1,165 TU on first 6,000 TU; then 34% × (39,287−6,000) = 34%×33,287 = 11,318 TU | 1,165 + 11,318 | ~12,483 TU = VES ~524,286 |
| **Effective rate (PIT only)** | **12,483 / 40,071** | **~31.1%** |

### Summary (approximate)

| Gross income | EUR equiv | Scenario | PIT effective rate | SS employee | Total effective rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~40,000 TU | ~€30,000 | Employment | ~31% | ~1% (capped SS) | **~32%** |
| ~40,000 TU | ~€30,000 | B2B | ~31% | ~1% | **~32%** |
| ~80,000 TU | ~€60,000 | Employment | ~33% | ~<1% | **~33%** |
| ~133,000 TU | ~€100,000 | Employment | ~33.5% | ~<1% | **~34%** |

**Key observation:** At any income level expressed in EUR/USD terms, the Venezuelan PIT is effectively close to the **34% top rate** because the TU thresholds (6,000 TU = approx. VES 252,000 = approx. USD 4,941 = approx. €4,492) are extremely low relative to any professional income. The SS burden is minimal in EUR terms because the ceiling (5 minimum salaries) is very low.

**Assumptions:**
- 1 TU = VES 42 (2025); will change substantially for 2026
- 1 USD ≈ VES 51 (BCV official, October 2025); highly volatile
- 1 EUR ≈ VES 56 (approximate May 2026)
- SS caps at 5 minimum salaries (minimum salary in VES not specified; caps assumed very small relative to professional income)
- Standard deduction of 774 TU applied
- Personal rebate 10 TU applied
- All figures approximate given extreme economic conditions

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **TU value 2026:** SENIAT will set the 2026 TU value; all VES amounts change with it. Verify at seniat.gob.ve.
- [ ] **Exchange rate:** Official BCV rate vs. parallel market rates may diverge significantly. All EUR/USD conversions are highly unreliable.
- [ ] **Minimum salary:** Used for SS caps; updated by government decree; not specified in EY source. Verify at bcv.org.ve.
- [ ] **IVA rate:** Venezuela's IVA rate has fluctuated; currently 16% standard. Self-employed must register and file monthly.
- [ ] **Practical enforceability:** SENIAT's capacity to enforce PIT compliance for individuals in the context of widespread dollarization and economic crisis is uncertain. Many employers and employees transact in USD but nominal compliance in VES is required.
- [ ] **Special SS Contribution (9% employer):** This appears to be a very significant employer-side burden but calculation base (total remuneration including non-salary) makes it complex.
- [ ] **Equity Tax (0.25%–1.5%):** Applicable to "Special Taxpayers" with gross income or sales above specific thresholds (set in multiples of the exchange rate of the highest-value currency). Not modeled.
- [ ] **Double taxation risk:** Venezuela has treaties with many countries, but the practical application given Venezuela's economic situation and SENIAT administrative capacity may be unreliable.
- [ ] **IGTF (Large Financial Transactions Tax):** Venezuela introduced a tax on large financial transactions in foreign currency and cryptocurrencies; rate varies. Not modeled — verify with SENIAT.
