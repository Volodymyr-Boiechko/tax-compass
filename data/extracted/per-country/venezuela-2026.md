## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system using "tax units" (Unidades Tributarias — UT) as the unit of measurement. All brackets expressed in UT; the UT value is set by SENIAT (Servicio Nacional Integrado de Administración Aduanera y Tributaria) and adjusted at least annually.

**UT values (EY p. 1700):**
- 2025 tax year: **VES 42 per UT**
- 2024 tax year: **VES 9 per UT**
- The bolivar-to-UT ratio can be modified at least once per year (National Assembly approval)

**CRITICAL WARNING:** Venezuela has experienced hyperinflation for years. The VES (bolívar soberano/digital) has been devalued repeatedly. Any EUR/VES conversion is highly approximate and may change dramatically. The official exchange rate and a parallel (black market) rate coexist; tax obligations are calculated using the official BCV (Banco Central de Venezuela) rate.

**Residency:** Physically present in Venezuela for more than 183 days in the current or immediately preceding calendar year. Individuals with tax residency in a country with which Venezuela has a double tax treaty are protected under personal services clauses.

**Filing threshold:** Must file if annual worldwide gross income exceeds 1,500 UT OR annual worldwide net taxable income exceeds 1,000 UT (EY p. 1700)

**Non-residents:** 34% flat rate on Venezuelan-source income (EY p. 1700 and p. 1701)

**Standard deductions / allowances (EY p. 1701–1702):**
- Standard deduction (no documentation required): **774 UT** per year (= VES 32,508 at 2025 UT value = ~USD 893 at official rate)
- Personal rebate (annual): **10 UT** per taxpayer
- Family rebate: **10 UT** per family member living in Venezuela (spouse, ascendants, descendants not of legal age or handicapped/studying and < 25 years old)
- Mortgage interest: deductible up to 1,000 UT; rent payments up to 800 UT
- Educational expenses for taxpayer and children under 25: deductible (no limit stated)
- Surgery, hospitalization, maternity insurance premiums: deductible (no limit)
- Medical, dental, hospitalization expenses for taxpayer, spouse, ascendants, descendants: deductible (no limit)

**Notes:**
- Tax nonresidents: may NOT use any of the above deductions (EY p. 1702)
- Dividends: withheld at 34% to the extent pre-tax income exceeds net taxable income for years beginning on/after 1 January 2001 (EY p. 1701)
- Interest from Venezuelan savings instruments/banks: tax-exempt
- Capital gains: taxed as ordinary income at progressive rates
- Business losses (self-employed): may be carried forward 3 years; cannot exceed 25% of income in each carry-forward year; no carrybacks

## Social Security

Venezuela has a multi-contribution social security system (EY pp. 1703–1704):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Cap |
|---|---|---|---|
| IVSS (Social Security) | **4%** | **9%/10%/11%** | 5× minimum salaries/month |
| Unemployment Insurance (INCE-related) | **0.5%** | **2%** | 5× minimum salaries/month |
| INCES (National Training) | **0.5%** (on year-end profit-sharing) | **2%** (on total remuneration; employers with ≥5 employees) | — |
| Housing Fund (FAOV) | **1%** | **2%** | 10× minimum salaries/month |
| Special Social Security Contribution | — | **9%** | On total remuneration (salary + non-salary benefits) |
| **Total (employee approx.)** | **~5.5%** | **~24–25%** | |

**Notes (EY pp. 1703–1704):**
- IVSS employer rate: 9%, 10%, or 11% depending on risk classification
- All contributions capped at multiples of minimum salary (5× or 10× depending on the type)
- INCES: employer 2% on total employee remuneration (if ≥ 5 employees); employee 0.5% on profit-sharing received at year-end
- Housing Fund (FAOV): employer 2%, employee 1%; ceiling 10× minimum monthly salaries
- Special Social Security Contribution: 9% employer-only on total employee remuneration including non-salary benefits
- Social security totalization treaties with Chile, Ecuador, Greece, Italy, Portugal, Spain, Uruguay (EY p. 1704)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Independent Business Income (General)

- **Who qualifies:** Resident individuals earning self-employment or business income
- **Tax base:** Gross income less all expenses necessary to produce income; standard deduction (774 UT) or itemized deductions
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive Tariff No. 1 rates (6%–34%), same as employment
- **Social security:** Subject to social security contributions on directors' fees (percentage of monthly salary — EY p. 1701); pure self-employed contribution structure not fully specified
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to VAT (IVA) on taxable sales/services; standard rate 16%
- **Key limits:** Quarterly advance payments; annual return by 31 March; business losses carried forward 3 years (max 25%/year)

### Option B — Simplified / Forfait Regime

- No general simplified tax regime described in EY for Venezuela; all individuals subject to Tariff No. 1 progressive rates or 34% non-resident flat rate

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No General Newcomer Exemption

Venezuela does not offer a territorial or exemption-based regime for new residents. Residents pay tax on worldwide income from the moment they meet the residency threshold.

### Non-Resident Treatment

- 34% flat rate on Venezuelan-source income
- Must file if deriving Venezuelan-source income
- No deductions available to non-residents

## 2026 Changes

- **UT value 2025 = VES 42** (vs VES 9 for 2024 — a ~4.7× increase, EY p. 1700); 2026 UT value will be set by SENIAT; expected to increase further given ongoing inflation
- **Equity Tax:** Applies to "Special Taxpayers" exceeding threshold of 7,500× exchange rate of highest value foreign currency in gross annual income; 0.25%–1.5% on net worth (EY p. 1703)
- **Money-transfer charge / exchange controls:** Ongoing; official BCV rate used for tax purposes
- No announced structural changes to PIT brackets (in UT terms) at EY data date

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **UT value 2026:** Will be updated by SENIAT; 2025 value (VES 42) used; 2026 value likely significantly higher
- [ ] **Official VES exchange rate:** Highly volatile; EUR/VES ~39.7 is an approximation; all EUR equivalents should be recalculated with current BCV rate
- [ ] **Parallel exchange rate:** A significantly different parallel/black market rate exists; many transactions priced in USD; this adds major uncertainty to real-world effective rates
- [ ] **Minimum salary 2025/2026:** The minimum salary determines social security caps; exact current value not stated in EY; caps are very low in USD terms making employee SS contributions negligible at international income levels
- [ ] **Equity Tax applicability:** Threshold and rates for Special Taxpayers; may apply to high-income residents
- [ ] **Cryptocurrency / digital asset reporting:** Venezuela is a significant crypto-using economy; tax treatment of digital assets not detailed in EY
- [ ] **Practical tax collection:** Due to economic instability, enforcement and practical collection may differ significantly from statutory rates
