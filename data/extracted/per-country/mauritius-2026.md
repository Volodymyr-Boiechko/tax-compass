## Personal Income Tax

Mauritius levies income tax on residents' **worldwide income** (income derived from outside Mauritius taxed on a remittance basis). Nonresidents are taxed on Mauritian-source income only. Tax year runs from 1 July to 30 June. Annual return due by 15 October. PAYE withheld by employers on emoluments. Tax administered by the Mauritius Revenue Authority (MRA). (EY pp. 982, 990)

**Residency tests:** 183+ days in the tax year; OR 270+ days aggregate in current and two preceding years; OR domiciled in Mauritius (unless permanent abode outside). (EY p. 982)

### Income tax rates (from income year ending 30 June 2026)

| Chargeable income (MUR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| First MUR 500,000 | **0%** |
| Next MUR 500,000 (MUR 500,001–1,000,000) | **10%** |
| Remainder (above MUR 1,000,000) | **20%** |

(EY p. 987)

**Maximum marginal rate: 20%**

**Fair Share Contribution (FSC) — solidarity levy on high earners:**
- Applies to individuals whose **aggregate net income > MUR 12 million** for years ending **30 June 2026, 30 June 2027, and 30 June 2028**
- FSC rate: **15%** of the excess of leviable income over MUR 12 million
- Leviable income = aggregate of: chargeable income + Mauritius-source dividends + dividend income from any resident partnership or succession
- Excluded from leviable income: dividend income and distributions from Global Business Entities (GBLs); lump sums (pension commutation, death gratuity); excluded termination employment income (EY p. 984)

**Dividends paid by resident companies:** Exempt from tax in the hands of shareholders (EY p. 983)

**Capital gains:** **Generally not taxable** in Mauritius (EY p. 984)

**Investment income:**
- Interest from Mauritius banks: **exempt** for both residents and nonresidents
- Interest on government securities, stock exchange debentures, Bank of Mauritius Bills: **exempt**
- Multiple other interest exemptions (renewable energy bonds, sustainability bonds, peer-to-peer lending, public infrastructure bonds) (EY p. 983)
- Withholding tax of **10%** on payments by individuals to nonresident entertainers/sportspersons and management fees (EY p. 984)

**Self-employment income:** Business expenses deductible to the extent exclusively incurred to produce gross income (EY p. 983)

**Mauritius Diaspora Scheme:** Members of the Mauritius Diaspora returning to Mauritius receive a 10-year tax exemption on all Mauritian and foreign-source income from the income year of return (exemption limited to specific employment/business/trade/profession/investment of the individual) (EY p. 984)

**Premium visa holders (remote workers):** From 1 November 2020, income of premium visa holders working remotely from Mauritius is deemed to be Mauritian-source income only if remitted to Mauritius. If not remitted (spent via foreign credit/debit card), remittance basis does not apply. If deposited to Mauritius bank account, it is taxable unless tax paid in country of origin is declared. (EY p. 987)

**Negative income tax allowance:** Citizens of Mauritius with monthly earnings ≤ MUR 9,900 are entitled to a negative income tax allowance ranging from MUR 100 to MUR 1,000/month (subject to conditions including annual net income of individual and spouse) (EY p. 987)

**Personal reliefs and deductions (income year ending 30 June 2024):** (EY pp. 984–987)
- Income Exemption Threshold (IET) based on number of dependants:
  - 1 dependant: MUR 110,000
  - 2 dependants: MUR 90,000 (cumulative 200,000? — note: EY lists individual amounts per dependant count)
  - 3 dependants: MUR 275,000
  - 4 dependants: MUR 355,000
- Retired individual aged ≥60 with no taxable emoluments >MUR 50,000: additional MUR 50,000 deduction
- Medical/health insurance: up to MUR 25,000 (taxpayer) + MUR 20,000 each (2nd, 3rd, 4th dependants)
- University education: MUR 500,000 per dependent child (conditions)
- Private school fees: up to MUR 60,000 per dependent (from income year ending 30 June 2025)
- Caregiver wages: up to MUR 30,000
- Interest relief on housing loans (conditions apply)
- Solar energy/rainwater harvesting/EV fast charger: investment allowances
- Charitable donations: up to MUR 50,000

## Social Security

**Contribution Sociale Généralisée (CSG) — replacing National Pension Fund from September 2020:** (EY pp. 988–989)

| Category | Employee/participant | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Private sector, monthly remuneration ≤ MUR 50,000 | **1.5%** | **3%** |
| Private sector, monthly remuneration > MUR 50,000 | **3%** | **6%** |
| Public sector, monthly remuneration ≤ MUR 50,000 | **4.5%** | (payable by employer) |
| Public sector, monthly remuneration > MUR 50,000 | **9%** | (payable by employer) |
| Domestic service worker, monthly rem. < MUR 3,000 | **3%** | — |
| Self-employed, monthly net income < MUR 10,000 | MUR 150/month (fixed) | — |
| Self-employed, monthly net income MUR 10,000–50,000 | min. 1.5% of 90% of net income | — |
| Self-employed, monthly net income > MUR 50,000 | min. 3% of 90% of net income | — |

**National Solidarity Fund:** Employer: 2.5% (max MUR 714.25/month); Employee: 1% (max MUR 285.70/month) (EY p. 988)

**Employer levy:** 1.5% of total salary (EY p. 988)

**Social security ceiling (CSG):** No ceiling stated for CSG itself (rate changes at MUR 50,000/month threshold); National Solidarity Fund has monthly caps.

**Foreign nationals:** Subject to social security obligations regardless of length of stay (from January 2014), except those in export manufacturing enterprises (subject after 2 years of continuous residence) and certain other exemptions. (EY p. 988)

**Totalization agreements:** None beyond the CSG framework; EU Regulation does not apply (Mauritius is not EU). (No specific totalization agreements listed in EY for social security)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Business / Profession)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals carrying on trade, business, or profession in Mauritius
- **Tax base:** Business profits less exclusively incurred business expenses
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive PIT rates as employment (0%/10%/20%); FSC if total income >MUR 12M
- **Social security (CSG):** Based on monthly net income:
  - <MUR 10,000/month: MUR 150/month fixed
  - MUR 10,000–50,000: minimum 1.5% of 90% of net income
  - >MUR 50,000: minimum 3% of 90% of net income
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds MUR 6 million; standard rate 15%
- **Loss treatment:** Losses may be offset against any source except employment income; carryforward 5 years; capital expenditure allowances carryforward indefinitely

### Option B — Presumptive Tax (Agriculture/Fishing/Manufacturing only)

- **Who qualifies:** Small enterprises in agriculture, forestry, fishing, or manufacturing only; yearly gross taxable income ≤ MUR 10 million; taxable income from other sources ≤ MUR 400,000
- **Rate:** **5%** on gross income (irrevocable election)
- **Excluded activities:** Restaurants, retailing and wholesaling (EY pp. 987–988)

### Option C — Occupation Permit (Self-employed foreign nationals)

- **Who qualifies:** Non-citizen self-employed individuals via EDB occupation permit
- **Requirements:** Initial investment USD 50,000 equivalent; bank statement proof; minimum 3 letters of intent (2 from local clients); minimum business income MUR 750,000 from year 1; cumulative MUR 6 million by year 5
- **Permit validity:** 1 year; renewable (EY p. 995)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Occupation Permit (Work and Self-Employment)

**Professional (ProPass):** Monthly basic salary ≥ MUR 30,000 (EY p. 995)
**Expert Pass:** Highly qualified professionals; monthly basic salary ≥ MUR 250,000 (EY p. 995)
**Investor:** Initial investment USD 50,000–USD 100,000 depending on track; minimum turnover MUR 1.5M–MUR 1M from year 1; various cumulative turnover targets by year 5 (EY pp. 994–995)

**Permanent Residence (20 years):** Available to investors with aggregate company turnover MUR 75M over preceding 5 years; self-employed with annual income ≥ MUR 3M for 5 years; professionals with monthly salary ≥ MUR 400,000 for 5 consecutive years immediately preceding application (EY p. 996)

**Retired persons:** Residence permit requiring transfer of at least USD 24,000/year or guaranteed income of USD 2,000/month (EY p. 997)

### Mauritius Diaspora Scheme

- **Eligibility:** Mauritians returning from abroad
- **Benefit:** 10-year exemption from Mauritius and foreign-source income taxes from year of return
- **Scope:** Limited to specific employment, business, trade, profession, or investment activity of the individual (EY p. 984)

### Premium Visa (Remote Workers)

- Allows foreign nationals to work remotely from Mauritius
- Income of premium visa holders is deemed Mauritian-source only if **remitted** to Mauritius; non-remitted income (spent via foreign card) is not taxable in Mauritius
- Remittance basis: if deposited in Mauritius bank account, taxable unless tax paid abroad is declared (EY p. 987)

### Global Business Company (GBC) Framework

- GBC1 (now GBC) licenced by Financial Services Commission: taxed at standard corporate rate of 15%; previously received partial exemption; FSC (solidarity levy) excluded on GBC dividend distributions for individual shareholders
- No capital gains tax on investments through GBCs
- Extensive DTT network used for international tax planning (EY p. 991)

## 2026 Changes

- **New income tax rate structure (EY p. 987):** From the income year ending **30 June 2026**, the previous flat 15% rate is replaced by a three-bracket system: 0% (first MUR 500,000), 10% (next MUR 500,000), 20% (above MUR 1,000,000). This is a significant change from the prior flat 15% regime.
- **Fair Share Contribution (FSC) (EY p. 984):** FSC at 15% on aggregate net income over MUR 12 million applies for years ending 30 June **2026, 2027, and 2028** — three-year window confirmed.
- **Angel investor allowance (EY p. 988):** The angel investor allowance (deduction for investing in qualifying startup SMEs) **no longer applies as of the year ending 30 June 2026**.
- **Animal adoption deduction (EY p. 987):** MUR 10,000/animal deduction through approved NGOs **no longer applies as of year ending 30 June 2026**.
- **Household employee deduction (EY p. 987):** The deduction for household employees no longer applies as of the year ending 30 June 2026.
- **Voluntary disclosure scheme (EY p. 990):** Full waiver of interest and penalties for assessments issued on or after 5 June 2025 if tax paid by 31 March 2026 and application made by 31 December 2025.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Mauritius uses MUR (Mauritian Rupee). **1 EUR ≈ MUR 50** (approximate; verify at bom.mu).
> Tax year 1 July–30 June; 2026 rates applied (from income year ending 30 June 2026).
> Employee CSG: 1.5% (salary ≤ MUR 50,000/month) or 3% (>MUR 50,000/month).
> No capital gains tax. Dividends from resident companies exempt.

### €30,000 Employment
- MUR equivalent: EUR 30,000 × 50 = MUR 1,500,000/year
- Income tax:
  - 0% on MUR 500,000 = MUR 0
  - 10% on MUR 500,000 = MUR 50,000
  - 20% on MUR 500,000 (1,000,001–1,500,000) = MUR 100,000
  - Total PIT: MUR 150,000
- CSG: MUR 1,500,000/year = MUR 125,000/month average > MUR 50,000/month → 3% rate
  - Actually MUR 1,500,000 / 12 = MUR 125,000/month → 3% employee rate
  - Annual CSG: 3% × MUR 1,500,000 = MUR 45,000
- Total burden: MUR 150,000 + MUR 45,000 = MUR 195,000
- Effective rate: 195,000 / 1,500,000 = **13.0%**

### €60,000 Employment
- MUR: EUR 60,000 × 50 = MUR 3,000,000/year
- Income tax:
  - 0% on MUR 500,000 = 0
  - 10% on MUR 500,000 = MUR 50,000
  - 20% on MUR 2,000,000 = MUR 400,000
  - Total PIT: MUR 450,000
- CSG: MUR 3,000,000/12 = MUR 250,000/month > MUR 50,000 → 3%
  - Annual CSG: 3% × MUR 3,000,000 = MUR 90,000
- Total: MUR 540,000; Effective rate: 540,000 / 3,000,000 = **18.0%**

### €100,000 Employment
- MUR: EUR 100,000 × 50 = MUR 5,000,000/year
- Income tax:
  - 0% on MUR 500,000 = 0; 10% on MUR 500,000 = MUR 50,000; 20% on MUR 4,000,000 = MUR 800,000
  - Total PIT: MUR 850,000
- CSG: 3% × MUR 5,000,000 = MUR 150,000
- Total: MUR 1,000,000; Effective rate: 1,000,000 / 5,000,000 = **20.0%**

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **IET (Income Exemption Threshold) for single no-dependant:** EY lists IET amounts only for 1, 2, 3, 4 dependants; the base IET for a single person with no dependants is not explicitly stated in the EY table on p. 985. Verify at MRA (mra.mu).
- [ ] **EUR/MUR exchange rate:** MUR/EUR fluctuates; 50 is approximate. Verify at Bank of Mauritius (bom.mu).
- [ ] **CSG monthly ceiling:** EY does not state an annual ceiling for CSG; the rate change at MUR 50,000/month applies both above and below. National Solidarity Fund monthly caps are MUR 714.25 (employer) and MUR 285.70 (employee).
- [ ] **2026 income year end date:** Mauritius tax year ends 30 June; the "income year ending 30 June 2026" rates are those that apply during the period 1 July 2025–30 June 2026.
- [ ] **Negative income tax allowance:** Applies to Mauritius citizens earning ≤ MUR 9,900/month; conditions for entitlement (joint household income) not fully specified — amounts from MUR 100–MUR 1,000/month.
- [ ] **Premium visa tax position:** Tax treatment for remote workers on premium visa using non-remittance arrangements depends on individual facts; professional advice essential.
