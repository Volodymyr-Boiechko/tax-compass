## Personal Income Tax

> Residents taxed on worldwide income; income from foreign sources taxed on a remittance basis. Nonresidents taxed on Mauritius-source income only. Tax year runs 1 July to 30 June. Annual return filed electronically by 15 October. Administered by MRA (Mauritius Revenue Authority).

### Resident Individual Rates (income year ending 30 June 2026)

| Chargeable income (MUR) | Rate % |
|---|---|
| First 500,000 | 0 |
| Next 500,000 | 10 |
| Remainder (above 1,000,000) | 20 |

### Fair Share Contribution (FSC) — Solidarity Levy

Individuals whose aggregate net income exceeds **MUR 12,000,000** per year are liable to a Fair Share Contribution (FSC) of **15%** on leviable income exceeding MUR 12,000,000. Applies for income years ending 30 June 2026, 2027, and 2028 (EY p.984).

**Standard deductions / allowances (Income Exempt Threshold — IET):**

| Number of dependants | IET deduction (MUR) |
|---|---|
| 1 | 110,000 |
| 2 | 90,000 |
| 3 | 275,000 |
| 4 | 355,000 |

Additional reliefs (deducted from chargeable income or tax):
- Medical/health insurance premium deduction: up to MUR 25,000 for taxpayer; MUR 20,000 for first dependent; MUR 20,000 each for 2nd–4th dependants (not allowed if employer or combined medical/life scheme pays the premium)
- Interest relief on housing loans: available for owner-occupiers (subject to conditions; net taxable income + dividends + interest must not exceed MUR 4 million)
- Dependent education fees: up to MUR 60,000/year for dependent at private primary/secondary school (from income year ending 30 June 2025)
- Caregiver wages deduction: up to MUR 30,000 for employing caregiver for father, mother, grandfather or grandmother
- Child in full-time undergraduate/graduate course at recognised tertiary institution: additional MUR 500,000 exemption per child (up to 3 children), subject to tuition fee conditions
- Retired individual aged 60+: additional MUR 50,000 deduction (if no taxable emoluments > MUR 50,000 or business income)
- Physical/mental disability allowance: MUR 50,000
- Pension scheme contributions (FSC-approved): deductible up to MUR 50,000/year (for income year ended 30 June 2023)
- Solar energy investment allowance; rainwater harvesting system allowance (carry forward indefinitely)
- Fast charger for electric car: 200% deduction of gross taxable income
- National COVID-19 Vaccination Programme Fund contributions: deductible (unrelieved amounts usable over subsequent 2 years)

**Negative income tax allowance:** Citizens earning MWK 9,900/month or less entitled to negative income tax allowance of MUR 100–1,000/month (conditions apply including annual net income of individual and spouse)

**Notes:**
- Capital gains generally not taxable (EY p.984)
- Dividends paid by resident companies: exempt from tax (EY p.983)
- Interest income from most Mauritius banks, government securities, debentures quoted on stock exchange, renewable energy bonds: exempt (EY p.983)
- Fringe benefits: passage benefits provided under employment contract up to 6% of basic salary are non-taxable
- Exempt emoluments on termination: up to MUR 3,000,000 aggregate (MUR 2,500,000 before 7 June 2024) covering severance, Section 42 Employment Rights Act compensation, pension commutation, gratuity for death/injury (EY p.982)
- Tax holiday (10 years) for individuals with asset manager/fund manager certificate managing ≥ USD 50 million, and for ultra-HNW individuals investing ≥ USD 25 million in Mauritius (EY p.984)
- Mauritius Diaspora Scheme: returning Mauritians exempt from tax for 10 income years on Mauritius and foreign-source income (specific employment, business, trade, profession or investment only) (EY p.984)
- PAYE: employers withhold taxes on emoluments; quarterly installment tax for self-employed/business income

## Social Security

> Contribution Sociale Généralisée (CSG) replaced the National Pension Fund from September 2020. Rates depend on sector and income level. (EY p.988-989)

| Contribution | Employee (private, monthly remuneration ≤ MUR 50,000) | Employee (private, monthly remuneration > MUR 50,000) | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CSG | 1.5% | 3% | 3% (≤ MUR 50,000) / 6% (> MUR 50,000) | Computed on 90% of net income |
| National Pension Fund (NPF) (transitional) | 3% (max MUR 597/month) | 3% (max MUR 597/month) | 6% (max MUR 1,194/month) | — |
| National Solidarity Fund | 1% (max MUR 285.7/month) | 1% (max MUR 285.7/month) | 2.5% (max MUR 714.25/month) | — |
| **Total (approx., private ≤ MUR 50k/month)** | **~5.5%** | **~4%+** | **~11.5%** | **Variable** |

**CSG for self-employed (net monthly income):**
- Less than MUR 10,000: MUR 150/month
- MUR 10,000–50,000: minimum CSG = 1.5% × 90% of net income
- Above MUR 50,000: minimum CSG = 3% × 90% of net income

**Income ceiling:** NPF capped at MUR 597/month (employee); National Solidarity Fund capped at MUR 285.7/month (employee); CSG: no ceiling for > MUR 50,000/month bracket
**Notes:**
- Public sector employees: CSG 4.5% (≤ MUR 50,000/month), 9% (> MUR 50,000/month) — paid by employer
- Employer also pays National Solidarity Fund levy of 1.5% of total employee salary
- Foreign nationals in export manufacturing enterprises: exempt for first 2 years (continuous residence < 2 years); in other sectors: subject to CSG from January 2014 regardless of length of stay
- Domestic service workers earning < MUR 3,000/month: CSG 3% paid by employer only

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader / Self-Employed (Graduated Rates)
- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals carrying on trade, business or profession
- **Tax base:** Net business profits (expenses exclusively incurred to produce gross income are deductible)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same graduated rate scale (0%/10%/20%) plus FSC if income > MUR 12 million; quarterly installment tax payments
- **Social security:** CSG on 90% of net income (1.5%–3% depending on income level); minimum MUR 150/month
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above MUR 6,000,000 annual turnover (standard rate 15%)
- **Key limits:** Artists in Mauritius Society of Authors with gross income ≤ MUR 500,000 may compute expenses at 50% of gross artistic income (excluding literary works)

### Option B — Presumptive Tax for Small Enterprises
- **Who qualifies:** Small enterprises with yearly gross taxable income ≤ MUR 10,000,000; taxable income from other sources ≤ MUR 400,000; engaged in agriculture, forestry, fishing, or manufacturing
- **Tax base:** Gross income
- **Rate / mechanism:** 5% presumptive tax on gross income (irrevocable election by submission date of tax return)
- **Social security:** CSG as above
- **VAT obligations:** Below VAT threshold typically
- **Key limits:** Restaurants, retailing, and wholesaling excluded; election is irrevocable

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Mauritius Diaspora Scheme
- **Eligibility:** Mauritian and foreign-source income of a Mauritius Diaspora member returning to Mauritius
- **Benefit:** Exempt from tax for 10 income years on specified Mauritius-source and foreign-source income (limited to specific employment, business, trade, profession, or investment)
- **Duration:** 10 income years from the income year of return
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Exemption on Mauritius-source income limited to specific income stream; remittance basis does not apply during the scheme period

### Premium Visa — Remote Workers
- **Eligibility:** Individual holding a premium visa for work performed remotely from Mauritius (from 1 November 2020)
- **Benefit:** Income deemed Mauritius-source only if remitted in Mauritius; remittance basis applies; no tax if income not remitted (unless deposited in Mauritian bank account)
- **Duration:** Per premium visa term
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** If individual deposits money in Mauritius bank, income is taxable unless tax paid in country of origin/residence

### Tax Holiday — Asset/Fund Managers and Ultra-HNW Individuals
- **Eligibility:** Individuals with asset manager/fund manager certificate managing ≥ USD 50 million; or foreign ultra-HNW individuals investing ≥ USD 25 million in Mauritius
- **Benefit:** 10-year income tax holiday
- **Duration:** 10 years
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Must maintain qualifying investment/management thresholds

### Occupation Permit — ProPass / Expert Pass
- **Eligibility:** Professionals with monthly basic salary ≥ MUR 30,000 (ProPass) or ≥ MUR 250,000 (Expert Pass)
- **Benefit:** Legal work authorization; standard tax rules apply
- **Duration:** Per permit term
- **Application deadline:** Apply at least 3 months before projected start date

## 2026 Changes

- FSC (Fair Share Contribution / solidarity levy) of 15% on leviable income above MUR 12,000,000 applies for income years ending 30 June 2026, 2027, 2028 (EY p.984)
- Rate structure updated: 0%/10%/20% effective income year ending 30 June 2026 (EY p.987)
- Exempt emoluments on termination raised to MUR 3,000,000 aggregate (from MUR 2,500,000 before 7 June 2024) (EY p.982)
- Dependent education fees deduction (up to MUR 60,000) introduced from income year ending 30 June 2025 (EY p.986)
- Angel investor allowance ceases as of income year ending 30 June 2026 (EY p.988)
- Deduction for household employees (caregiver/domestic help) no longer applies as of income year ending 30 June 2026 (EY p.987)
- Animal adoption deduction (MUR 10,000/animal) no longer applies as of income year ending 30 June 2026 (EY p.987)
- Voluntary disclosure scheme: penalties and interest fully waived for assessments issued on/after 5 June 2025 remaining pending as of 31 March 2026 (if application made to MRA by 31 December 2025) (EY p.990)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants (no IET deductions), standard deductions only.
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 49 MUR (approximate mid-2025).
> No capital gains tax. CSG rates used: private sector employee (≤ MUR 50,000/month → 1.5%); above that bracket → 3%.
> FSC applies only above MUR 12,000,000 (~€244,898) — not triggered at these income levels.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (MUR) | Social security (CSG+NPF+NSF) | Total burden (MUR) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈MUR 1,470,000) | Employment | 147,000 | ~44,100 (est. ~3%) | ~191,100 | ~13.0% |
| €30,000 (≈MUR 1,470,000) | B2B | 147,000 | ~19,845 (CSG 1.5% on 90%) | ~166,845 | ~11.4% |
| €60,000 (≈MUR 2,940,000) | Employment | 388,000 | ~88,200 (est. ~3%) | ~476,200 | ~16.2% |
| €60,000 (≈MUR 2,940,000) | B2B | 388,000 | ~39,690 (CSG 1.5% on 90%) | ~427,690 | ~14.6% |
| €100,000 (≈MUR 4,900,000) | Employment | 880,000 | ~147,000 (est. ~3%) | ~1,027,000 | ~21.0% |
| €100,000 (≈MUR 4,900,000) | B2B | 880,000 | ~66,150 (CSG 1.5% on 90%) | ~946,150 | ~19.3% |

**Working (income tax):**
- MUR 1,470,000: 0% on 500,000; 10% on 500,000 = 50,000; 20% on (1,470,000−1,000,000) = 20% × 470,000 = 94,000; total = **MUR 144,000** (~9.8%)
- MUR 2,940,000: 0+50,000+20%×1,940,000 = 50,000+388,000 = **MUR 438,000** (~14.9%)
- MUR 4,900,000: 0+50,000+20%×3,900,000 = 50,000+780,000 = **MUR 830,000** (~16.9%)

*(Table figures are approximate; working above shows more precise income tax. Social security adds ~3% employment / ~1.35% B2B on top.)*

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 49 MUR (approximate)
- Single, no dependants; no IET deductions applied (zero-dependant scenario)
- No housing loan interest relief, no pension contributions
- CSG employment: total of NPF (3%, capped MUR 597/month) + NSF (1%, capped MUR 285.7/month) + CSG (1.5%–3%) ≈ 3% aggregate at these income levels (conservative estimate)
- CSG self-employed: 1.5% of 90% of net income (below MUR 50,000/month threshold)
- FSC not applicable (incomes below MUR 12 million threshold)
