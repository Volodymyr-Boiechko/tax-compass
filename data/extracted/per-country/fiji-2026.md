## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for residents on worldwide income; nonresidents on Fiji-source income only. Tax year = calendar year. Returns filed by 31 March. Employment income subject to PAYE withholding (final tax). (EY p.411, p.414)

**Residency:** A person who resides in Fiji meets any of: (a) domicile in Fiji; (b) present in Fiji continuously or intermittently during more than half the income year (unless usual abode is outside Fiji and no intent to take up residence); (c) government employee posted abroad. (EY p.411)

**Threshold:** Income tax threshold increased to FJD 30,000 for residents only. (EY p.412)

**Progressive PIT — Resident rates** (EY p.412–413)

| Chargeable income (FJD) | Income tax | Social responsibility tax |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 30,000 | 0 | 0 |
| 30,001 – 50,000 | 18% of excess over FJD 30,000 | 0 |
| 50,001 – 270,000 | FJD 3,600 + 20% of excess over FJD 50,000 | 0 |
| 270,001 – 300,000 | FJD 47,600 + 33% of excess over FJD 270,000 | 0 |
| 300,001 – 350,000 | FJD 57,500 + 34% of excess over FJD 300,000 | 0 |
| 350,001 – 400,000 | FJD 74,500 + 35% of excess over FJD 350,000 | 0 |
| 400,001 – 450,000 | FJD 92,000 + 36% of excess over FJD 400,000 | 0 |
| 450,001 – 500,000 | FJD 110,000 + 37% of excess over FJD 450,000 | 0 |
| 500,001 – 1,000,000 | FJD 128,500 + 38% of excess over FJD 500,000 | 0 |
| Above 1,000,000 | FJD 318,500 + 39% of excess over FJD 1,000,000 | 0 |

**Progressive PIT — Nonresident rates** (EY p.413)

| Chargeable income (FJD) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 30,000 | 20% |
| 30,001 – 50,000 | FJD 6,000 + 20% of excess over FJD 30,000 |
| 50,001 – 270,000 | FJD 10,000 + 20% of excess over FJD 50,000 |
| 270,001 – 300,000 | FJD 54,000 + 33% of excess over FJD 270,000 |
| Above 300,000 | Progressive 34%–39% |

**Other income types:**
- Dividends: dividend tax no longer imposed (effective 1 July 2017) except for 1% Dividend Transitional Tax on pre-2014 undistributed profits and 2014–2015 undistributed profits. (EY p.413)
- Capital gains: 10% capital gains tax on all capital gains, except exempt gains. Exempt: gains < FJD 30,000 by resident individual/Fiji citizen; gains from sale of principal place of residence (if it was individual's principal abode); gains from sale of shares listed on South Pacific Stock Exchange; gains from transfer of assets between spouses through "love & affection"; gains from sale of capital assets used solely to earn exempt income. (EY p.412)
- Interest, royalties, know-how fees paid to nonresidents: final withholding taxes per treaty table (10–15% depending on treaty country; 15% for non-treaty countries). (EY p.415)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Entertainment allowance: taxable to the extent not fully justified with documentation of business entertainment. (EY p.412)
- Business deductions: all expenses incurred in producing taxable income deductible (except personal, private or domestic expenses). Depreciation allowed at rates set by tax authorities. (EY p.412)

**Notes:**
- All stamp duty abolished from 1 August 2020. (EY p.414)
- No property tax, net worth tax, inheritance or gift tax in Fiji. (EY p.414)
- Employment income subject to PAYE (final tax); employees not required to file returns. (EY p.414)
- Self-employed: provisional tax in three installments (April, August, November); final assessment when return filed. (EY p.414)
- Expatriate employees resident in Fiji under service contracts of up to 3 years taxed only on income earned in Fiji. (EY p.415)

## Social Security (FNPF — Fiji National Provident Fund)

Although Fiji imposes no social security taxes, effective from 1 January 2024, all employers must contribute at least 10% of gross earnings of all regular employees to the FNPF. Total contributions must equal a minimum of 18% (10% employer + 8% employee). An employee need not contribute, or may contribute a smaller amount, if the employer contributes the difference on the employee's behalf. Contributions up to 30% allowed; amounts in excess of 10% employee contribution are taxable to the employee. (EY p.414)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| FNPF (provident fund) | min 8% | min 10% | — |
| **Total minimum** | **8%** | **10%** | — |

**Income ceiling:** Not specified in EY source.

**Notes:**
- On retirement: lump-sum equal to total contributions plus accrued interest, OR pension based on total contributions plus accrued interest. (EY p.414)
- No traditional social security taxes (unemployment insurance, health insurance) imposed through government scheme.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment / Business Income
- **Who qualifies:** All resident individuals deriving income from business activities (sole traders, active members of partnerships).
- **Tax base:** Taxable income = accounting profit from annual financial statements, adjusted for taxable and non-taxable items. All expenses incurred in producing taxable income deductible (excluding personal/private/domestic expenses). Depreciation allowed at tax authority rates. (EY p.411–412)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive resident rates 0%–39% (same scale as employment, with FJD 30,000 threshold).
- **Social security:** FNPF: employer contributes ≥10% + employee contributes ≥8% for regular employees; self-employed regime not separately specified.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT applies; standard rate 15%.
- **Key limits:** Business losses may be offset against other income (except employment income) and carried forward for 4 years (8 years for losses incurred on/after 1 July 2019). (EY p.413–414)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Short-Term Service Contract Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Expatriate employees who are resident in Fiji as a result of employment under a service contract of up to 3 years.
- **Benefit:** Taxed only on income earned in Fiji (not worldwide income, despite being a resident).
- **Duration:** Up to 3 years.
- **Caveats:** Only applies to expatriates under service contracts of ≤3 years duration. (EY p.415)

## 2026 Changes

- **FNPF mandatory employer contribution introduced from 1 January 2024:** All employers must contribute ≥10% of gross earnings of regular employees; total minimum 18% (employee 8% + employer 10%). This applies from 2024 onward into 2026.
- **No announced PIT rate changes for 2026** as of EY content date (October 2025).
- **Capital gains tax (10%):** In force; no changes announced.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> EUR/FJD rate used: 1 EUR ≈ FJD 2.45 (approximate 2025 average; FJD/USD ≈ 0.44; EUR/USD ≈ 1.08 → 1 EUR ≈ 2.45 FJD).
> "Employment" = gross annual salary; FNPF employee contribution 8% applied; FJD 30,000 zero-rate threshold applied.
> "B2B" = business net income at progressive rates; FNPF not mandatory for self-employed.

**EUR to FJD conversions:**
- €30,000 = FJD 73,500
- €60,000 = FJD 147,000
- €100,000 = FJD 245,000

### Working: FJD 73,500 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| FNPF employee (8%) | 8% × 73,500 | FJD 5,880 |
| Band 1: FJD 0–30,000 at 0% | 0% | FJD 0 |
| Band 2: FJD 30,001–50,000 at 18% | 18% × 20,000 | FJD 3,600 |
| Band 3: FJD 50,001–73,500 at 20% | 20% × (73,500 − 50,000) | FJD 4,700 |
| Total PIT | 3,600 + 4,700 | FJD 8,300 |
| **Total burden** | **5,880 + 8,300** | **FJD 14,180** |
| **Effective rate** | **14,180 / 73,500** | **~19.3%** |

- FNPF: 8% × 147,000 = FJD 11,760
- Tax: 0 + 3,600 + 20% × (147,000 − 50,000) = 3,600 + 20% × 97,000 = 3,600 + 19,400 = FJD 23,000
- Wait: FJD 147k < FJD 270k, so all in 20% band above FJD 50k: 3,600 + 20% × 97,000 = FJD 23,000 (corrected)

**Working — FJD 245,000 Employment:**
- FNPF: 8% × 245,000 = FJD 19,600
- Tax: 0 + 3,600 + 20% × (245,000 − 50,000) = 3,600 + 39,000 = FJD 42,600 (still below 270k threshold)
- Revised total: 19,600 + 42,600 = FJD 62,200 → ~25.4%

- EUR/FJD ≈ 2.45 (approximate; floating rate)
- FJD 30,000 zero-rate threshold applied
- FNPF: employee minimum 8%; not mandatory for self-employed (assumed 0 for B2B)
- PAYE applies to employment income (final tax); no additional deductions modeled
