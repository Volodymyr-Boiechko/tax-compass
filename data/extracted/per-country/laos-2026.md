## Personal Income Tax

Laos imposes income tax under Income Tax Law No. 67/NA (18 June 2019). The following persons must pay income tax: persons generating income in Laos; individuals resident in Laos working in a foreign country (unless a DTT applies); Lao employees in embassies/consulates earning income in Laos; foreigners working in Laos receiving salary in Laos; foreigners working in Laos receiving salary abroad if resident ≥183 consecutive or non-consecutive days in the year (unless DTT applies). Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Payroll taxes deducted at source and paid to authorities by the 20th of the following month. (EY pp. 838–841)

(EY p. 839)

**Exempt income:**
- Salary up to LAK1,300,000/month (zero-rate band)
- Profit from sale of shares on the Lao Securities Exchange
- Allowances for spouse/child up to age 18; maternity, sickness, occupational accident allowances
- One-time allowances, pensions, per diems for students
- Additional work and overtime income of persons earning basic income not more than LAK2 million
- Dividends paid by shareholders/partners of companies listed on the Lao Securities Exchange
- Interest on deposits and on government bonds/debentures
- Individual/corporate life insurance and property insurance payments

**Personal deductions:**
- Pension fund contributions and certain welfare fund amounts withheld are deductible (EY p. 839)

| **Maximum monthly contribution (employee)** | LAK247,500/month | — | |

**Coverage:**
- State organizations
- Public and social organizations
- Enterprises and other business entities employing labor and paying salary
- Individual freelancers and volunteers
- **Foreign employees** working for a labor unit in Laos and receiving salary or wages

**No SS ceiling separate regime for self-employed:** Individual freelancers are covered under social security (unusual for the region).

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Micro Enterprise Profit Tax (Turnover ≤ LAK400,000,000)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals engaging in business activities with annual turnover ≤ LAK400,000,000 (~USD18,500)
- **Tax base / Rate:** Based on annual gross turnover:
  - Turnover ≤ LAK50,000,000: **exempt** (0%)
  - LAK50,000,001–LAK400,000,000: **1%** (agriculture/industry), **2%** (trade), **3%** (services)
- **Social security:** 5.5% employee side + 6% employer side; individual freelancers are covered
- **VAT obligations:** VAT at 10%; registration required for businesses above VAT threshold
- **Key limits:** Gross turnover ≤ LAK400,000,000 for micro/small regime; above this, standard business profit tax applies

### Option B — Employment Income Brackets for Professional Services

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed professionals whose income is classified as employment income rather than business income (e.g., consultants, advisors with employment-type engagements)
- **Tax base:** Same progressive brackets as employment income above (0%/5%/10%/15%/20%/25%)
- **Deductions:** Pension and welfare fund contributions deductible

### Option C — Withholding on Consulting/Service Fees (5%)

- **Mechanism:** Broker fees, consulting fees, and fees for "other services" are subject to a **5% flat withholding tax** at source — applies when a Lao entity pays a foreign or domestic individual/company for services
- **Rate:** 5% of gross fee; payer must withhold and remit within 15 working days
- This is effectively a final tax for nonresidents on service income

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Dedicated Newcomer Tax Regime

Laos does not have a remittance basis, territorial tax, or new-resident personal income tax exemption. Standard progressive rates apply from day one of qualifying residency.

### Long-Term Visa with Bank Deposit

- **Long-term (I-B3) visa:** Available to foreigners intending to stay in Laos for a long period who deposit at least USD20,000 in a Lao bank if staying 6 months, or USD40,000 if staying a year (EY p. 842)
- **Tax implication:** Long-term residents are subject to Lao PIT on Laos-source income

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Micro business 3% rate for services ≤ LAK400M:** At €30k equivalent (LAK645M), gross revenue is already above the LAK400M micro business threshold, so the 3% rate does not apply. At very low income levels (below LAK400M ≈ €18,600), the 3% rate on gross is more favorable than progressive rates. This inflection point is important for freelancers.
- [ ] **Consulting WHT (5%) vs progressive rates:** A 5% flat withholding tax applies to consulting/broker/service fees when paid by a Lao entity. For foreign individuals receiving service income from Lao entities, this 5% may be the final tax. For resident individuals, the progressive rate typically applies — verify with local counsel whether 5% WHT satisfies the PIT obligation for residents.
- [ ] **SS for individual freelancers:** EY explicitly includes "individual freelancers and volunteers" in SS coverage — unusual in Southeast Asia. Self-employed pay both employer (6%) and employee (5.5%) sides? Or just one side? Clarify with local SS authority.
- [ ] **LAK exchange rate volatility:** The Lao kip (LAK) has experienced significant depreciation (>50% vs USD in 2022–2023). The EUR/LAK rate above is approximate; verify current official rate before using for planning purposes.
- [ ] **VAT threshold:** VAT at 10%; registration threshold not specified in EY extract. Verify applicable threshold from Lao Revenue Department; at €30k equivalent gross, VAT registration may or may not be required.
- [ ] **Pension/welfare deduction:** EY states amounts withheld for pension and welfare funds are deductible; no specific amounts given. Assumed not modeled above; if deductible, taxable income and effective rate would be slightly lower.
