## Personal Income Tax

> Progressive PAYE system administered by MRA (Malawi Revenue Authority). Tax year runs 1 April to 30 March (changed from 1 July–30 June effective 1 April 2022). Resident individuals taxed on Malawi-source income; foreign-source income is exempt. Nonresidents taxed at 15% flat rate on Malawi-source income.

### Resident Individual Rates (Employment — PAYE, monthly)

| Monthly income (MWK) | Pay-As-You-Earn rate % |
|---|---|
| First 170,000 | 0 |
| Next 1,400,000 | 30 |
| Next 8,430,000 | 35 |
| Above 10,000,000 | 40 |

**Nonresidents:** flat 15% on Malawi-source income (EY p.918)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Tax-free monthly threshold: MWK 170,000 (built into rate table)
- Redundancy pay up to MWK 50,000 exempt
- All income derived from pension funds (lump sum or annuity): exempt
- War disability or war widows pensions: exempt
- Foreign-source income: exempt
- Donations to approved/gazetted charitable organisations deductible (individual donations in excess of MWK 5 million to approved welfare/civic/educational nonprofits not allowed)
- Farming-related deductions: stumping, levelling, clearing of land; soil erosion prevention; boreholes; wells; aerial/geophysical surveys; specified water control work

**Notes:**
- Employment income: withheld by employer under PAYE; remitted to MRA by 14th of following month; employer must pay PAYE for payroll exceeding MWK 10,800,000 per year per employee
- Self-employed / business income: provisional tax paid quarterly within 25 days after each quarter-end; total installments ≥ 90% of actual liability
- Annual return filed within 180 days after year of assessment end
- Dividends: 10% final withholding tax (resident and nonresident individuals)
- Interest and rent: included in assessable income
- Capital gains: included in assessable income at normal progressive rates; exempt gains: principal residence disposal, spousal transfers, personal/domestic assets not used in trade
- Realized foreign-exchange gains: assessable; unrealized gains: not taxable
- Losses from trading: carry forward up to 6 years; loss carryback not allowed
- Married women may file their own returns; earned income of wife not aggregated with husband

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Business / Self-Employment (Graduated Rates)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on self-employment or business activities in Malawi
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross income less deductible expenses that are wholly, exclusively and necessarily incurred in production of income, and not capital in nature)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive monthly rate scale as employment; annual assessment; provisional tax quarterly
- **Social security:** None
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above threshold; standard VAT rate 16.5%
- **Key limits:** Capital expenses not deductible; losses carry forward 6 years

### Option B — Presumptive Tax (Small Business)
- **Who qualifies:** Small businesses with annual turnover not exceeding MWK 12,500,000
- **Tax base:** Gross turnover (quarterly payments)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed MWK amounts per Eleventh Schedule: 0 for turnover ≤ MWK 4,000,000; MWK 110,000 for MWK 4,000,001–7,000,000; MWK 170,000 for MWK 7,000,001–10,000,000; MWK 225,000 for MWK 10,000,001–12,500,000
- **Social security:** None
- **VAT obligations:** Below VAT threshold typically
- **Key limits:** Enforced via block management system in cities/towns; commercial passenger vehicles have separate rates (MWK 15,000–20,000/year)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

> No specific preferential tax regime for new tax residents identified in EY source. Foreign-source income is exempt for all residents.

### Source-Based Exemption
- **Eligibility:** All resident individuals
- **Benefit:** Foreign-source income exempt from Malawi income tax
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Nonresidents taxed at flat 15% on Malawi-source income

> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 1,900 MWK (approximate mid-2025; MWK highly volatile — Medium confidence).
> Monthly rates applied to annual income. No social security.

**Working (annual income tax — monthly bands × 12):**
Annual band thresholds: 0% on first MWK 2,040,000 (170,000×12); 30% on next MWK 16,800,000 (1,400,000×12); 35% on next MWK 101,160,000 (8,430,000×12); 40% above MWK 120,000,000

- MWK 57,000,000: 0% on 2,040,000; 30% × 16,800,000 = 5,040,000; 35% × (57,000,000−18,840,000) = 35% × 38,160,000 = 13,356,000; total = **18,396,000** (~32.3%)
- MWK 114,000,000: 0+5,040,000+35%×101,160,000 = 5,040,000+35,406,000 = 40,446,000; all within 35% band; total = **40,446,000** (~35.5%)
- MWK 190,000,000: tax through 35% band = 5,040,000+35,406,000 = 40,446,000; 40% × (190,000,000−120,000,000) = 40% × 70,000,000 = 28,000,000; total = **68,446,000** (~36.0%)

*(Table above uses approximate figures; see working for precise calculations)*

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 1,900 MWK (approximate; MWK volatile)
- Single, no dependants
- No deductions other than zero-rate band
- Employment and B2B rates identical; no social security either way
