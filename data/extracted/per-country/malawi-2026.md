## Personal Income Tax

Malawi levies income tax on residents' worldwide income from Malawi sources (foreign-source income is exempt). Nonresidents are subject to income tax at a flat 15% on Malawi-source income. Tax residency: physically present in Malawi for aggregate 183 days in any 12-month period. Tax year runs 1 April to 30 March (changed effective 1 April 2022). Annual return due within 180 days after year-end. PAYE withheld monthly by employers. Tax administered by the Malawi Revenue Authority (MRA). (EY pp. 918–924)

### Employment income tax rates (PAYE — monthly income)

| Monthly income (MWK) | Pay-As-You-Earn rate |
|---|---|
| First 170,000 | **0%** |
| Next 1,400,000 | **30%** |
| Next 8,430,000 | **35%** |
| Above 10,000,000 | **40%** |

Maximum marginal rate: **40%** (EY p. 920)

**Note:** The tax-free threshold is MWK 170,000/month (MWK 2,040,000/year). Annual income thresholds:
- 0%: up to MWK 2,040,000/year
- 30%: MWK 2,040,001–MWK 18,840,000/year
- 35%: MWK 18,840,001–MWK 119,160,000/year
- 40%: above MWK 120,000,000/year

**Nonresidents:** Flat 15% on Malawi-source income (EY p. 918)

**Dividends:** 10% final withholding tax on dividends paid to both resident and nonresident individuals (EY p. 918)

**Capital gains:**
- Capital gains derived by individuals are included in assessable income and taxed at normal progressive rates
- **Exempt capital gains:** disposal of principal residence; transfers between spouses/former spouses or from estate of deceased spouse; disposals of personal and domestic assets not used in connection with a trade (EY p. 919)
- Capital losses on non-qualifying assets: only offset against capital gains; may be set off against other income in year of taxpayer death or business cessation
- Loss carryforward: assessed losses may be carried forward for 6 years; no carryback (EY p. 921)

**Exempt income:**
- Foreign-source income
- Redundancy pay up to MWK 50,000
- All income derived from pension funds (lump sum or annuity)
- War disability or war widows pensions (EY p. 919)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Expenses wholly, exclusively and necessarily incurred for business/trade purposes are deductible
- **Non-deductible:** recoverable insurance losses, income tax itself, expenses for non-taxable income, subsidised expenses, rent on non-trade premises, personal living costs, commuting costs, fines (EY pp. 919–920)
- Donations to approved charitable organisations: deductible; individual donations >MWK 5 million to approved non-profits not deductible (EY p. 920)
- Farming expenses: specific deductions for land clearing, soil erosion prevention, boreholes, wells, water control works (EY p. 920)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment / Business Income

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on self-employment or business activities in Malawi
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross income minus deductible business expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates as employment income (0%–40%)
- **Social security:** None required
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds MWK 25,000,000; standard rate 16.5%
- **Key limits:** Business losses carry forward 6 years; no carryback
- **Filing:** Annual return within 180 days of year-end; quarterly provisional tax payments required

### Option B — Presumptive Tax (Small Businesses)

- **Who qualifies:** Small businesses with income not exceeding MWK 12,500,000/year
- **Tax base / Rate:** Fixed amounts based on annual turnover bands (EY p. 922):

| Annual turnover | Presumptive tax (MWK/year) |
|---|---|
| Up to MWK 4,000,000 | 0 |
| MWK 4,000,001 – MWK 7,000,000 | MWK 110,000 |
| MWK 7,000,001 – MWK 10,000,000 | MWK 170,000 |
| MWK 10,000,001 – MWK 12,500,000 | MWK 225,000 |

- **Mechanism:** Payable quarterly via block management system; enforced through traders in cities and towns
- **Social security:** None
- **Key limits:** Maximum turnover MWK 12,500,000/year for presumptive regime

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Business Residence Permit

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals establishing/running a business or engaging in self-employment in Malawi; minimum investment of USD 50,000 (MWK 20 million) from outside Malawi required
- **Duration:** 5 years, renewable
- **Allowed sectors:** Mining, trading, manufacturing, agriculture, self-employment, consultancy; NOT education, energy, tourism, health sectors
- **Requirements:** Company profile, business plan, police clearance, bank statement showing USD 50,000 brought into Malawi
- **Cost:** Application USD 100; approval fee USD 2,000–USD 3,500 depending on type (EY pp. 925–926)

### Temporary Employment Permit

- **Duration:** 6 months; renewable for one additional 6-month period; total maximum 2 years
- **Requirements:** Employer applies; must evidence local recruitment attempt; police clearance from country of origin; medical report; USD 100 processing fee + USD 500 approval fee (EY p. 925)

### Permanent Residence Permit

- **Eligibility:** Holders of temporary employment or business residence permit who have stayed in Malawi for at least 5 years
- **Cost:** USD 100 processing + USD 2,500 approval fee (EY p. 926)

### Double Tax Treaties

Malawi has DTTs with: France, Norway, South Africa, Sweden, Switzerland, United Kingdom (EY p. 924)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Malawi uses MWK (Malawian Kwacha). **1 EUR ≈ MWK 1,730** (approximate; MWK has depreciated significantly; verify at rbm.mw).
> No social security contributions. Simple progressive structure.
> Annual thresholds used: 0% up to MWK 2,040,000; 30% on MWK 2,040,001–18,840,000; 35% on MWK 18,840,001–119,160,000; 40% above MWK 120,000,000.

### €30,000 Employment
- MWK equivalent: EUR 30,000 × 1,730 = MWK 51,900,000/year
- Band 1: MWK 2,040,000 × 0% = MWK 0
- Band 2: (MWK 18,840,000 − MWK 2,040,000) × 30% = MWK 16,800,000 × 30% = MWK 5,040,000
- Band 3: (MWK 51,900,000 − MWK 18,840,000) × 35% = MWK 33,060,000 × 35% = MWK 11,571,000
- Total tax: MWK 16,611,000
- Effective rate: MWK 16,611,000 / MWK 51,900,000 = **32.0%**

### €60,000 Employment
- MWK: EUR 60,000 × 1,730 = MWK 103,800,000/year
- Band 1: 0
- Band 2: MWK 16,800,000 × 30% = MWK 5,040,000
- Band 3: (MWK 103,800,000 − MWK 18,840,000) × 35% = MWK 84,960,000 × 35% = MWK 29,736,000
- Total tax: MWK 34,776,000
- Effective rate: 34,776,000 / 103,800,000 = **33.5%**

### €100,000 Employment
- MWK: EUR 100,000 × 1,730 = MWK 173,000,000/year
- Band 1: 0
- Band 2: MWK 16,800,000 × 30% = MWK 5,040,000
- Band 3: (MWK 119,160,000 − MWK 18,840,000) × 35% = MWK 100,320,000 × 35% = MWK 35,112,000
- Band 4: (MWK 173,000,000 − MWK 120,000,000) × 40% = MWK 53,000,000 × 40% = MWK 21,200,000
- Total tax: MWK 61,352,000
- Effective rate: 61,352,000 / 173,000,000 = **35.5%**

- [ ] **EUR/MWK exchange rate:** MWK has experienced significant volatility and devaluation; EUR conversion is highly approximate. Verify current rate at Reserve Bank of Malawi (rbm.mw).
- [ ] **Annual income tax bracket thresholds:** EY states monthly PAYE thresholds; conversion to annual thresholds is straightforward (× 12) but verify the exact annual bracket limits with MRA.

- [ ] **Withholding taxes on self-employment payments:** Many payments (fees, rents, royalties, services) are subject to withholding tax (20% in most cases); these are advance tax payments credited against final liability.
