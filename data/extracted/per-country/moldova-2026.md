## Personal Income Tax

Moldova levies income tax (impozit pe venit) on residents' worldwide income (including income from overseas financial and investment operations). Nonresidents are taxed on income earned in Moldova and on income earned overseas for their work in Moldova (except for financial and investment income from outside Moldova). Residency: permanent domicile in Moldova OR 183+ days in any fiscal year. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return due by 30 April of the following year. Employers withhold income tax monthly. (EY pp. 1012–1016)

**Moldova does not apply different tax rates based on territoriality.** (EY p. 1012)

### Income tax rate

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Standard income (employment, business, self-employment) | **12%** flat |
| Farm income | **7%** flat |
| Interest on deposits with Moldovan banks | **6%** withholding (final) |
| Capital gains (taxable portion) | **12%** (on 50% of gains) |

(EY p. 1015)

**The standard personal income tax rate in Moldova is 12%.** (EY p. 1015)

**Capital gains treatment:**
- Capital assets include: shares, bonds, debentures, business ownership interests, private property not used for business purposes, land, options
- Tax base = **50%** of any capital gains earned during the fiscal year
- Capital gains deductible only against capital losses; no offset against ordinary income (EY p. 1014)
- Capital gains on sale of basic housing (principal residence) or car owned for ≥3 years: **exempt** (EY p. 1013)
- Capital gains as interest on state securities and/or local government bonds: **exempt** (EY p. 1013)

**Income NOT subject to tax (key exempt items):** (EY pp. 1012–1013)
- Compensation from budget for illness
- Damages from third parties for accidents/permanent disability
- Alimony and child allowances
- Inheritances and donations (with certain exceptions)
- Per diem within government limits
- Scholarships
- Government and charitable welfare
- Income from business patent activity
- Secondary raw materials from agricultural goods produced by individuals
- Recycling income
- Compensation for moral damages
- Meal tickets within legal limits
- Gains from sale of principal residence and car held ≥3 years
- In-kind gifts from employers not exceeding 10% of average monthly government forecast salary
- Sports/physical education facility subscriptions up to 50% of average monthly forecast salary
- Medical services up to one average monthly forecast salary
- Voluntary healthcare insurance premiums up to one average monthly forecast salary
- MDL 2,500/month per child under 3 (childcare cost compensation from employer)

**Deductions (individuals):** (EY pp. 1014–1015)
- Business expenses (entrepreneurial activities)
- Capital losses (to extent of capital gains)
- Compulsory health insurance premiums
- Compulsory social insurance contributions
- Voluntary health insurance premiums and medical services contracting (within limits)
- Life, health, fire, natural disaster insurance premiums (within limits)
- Interest on loans for first home purchase (within limits)
- Education expenses for dependent children (within limits — capped at one average monthly forecast salary per child, cumulative)
- All limits above tied to "one average monthly salary forecasted per economy per year" (amount varies annually)

**Personal deductions and allowances:** (EY p. 1015)
- Personal deduction: **MDL 29,700/year** for taxpayers with annual taxable income not exceeding **MDL 360,000** (EY p. 1015)
- Enhanced personal deduction: **MDL 34,620/year** for disabled veterans, parents and spouses of war veterans, and individuals disabled in childhood
- Additional deduction: **MDL 21,780/year** if spouse is a disabled war veteran, has disability from war/childhood, and spouse does not benefit from personal deduction
- Per-dependent deduction: **MDL 9,900/year** per dependant
- Permanent disability deduction: **MDL 21,780/year** for individuals with permanent disability

**Wealth tax:**
- **0.8%** on residential immovable property (except land) owned by individuals when: total estimated value ≥ 200 average monthly salaries AND total surface ≥ 120 sqm. Payable by 25 December each year. (EY p. 1015)

**IT Park special regime (EY p. 1016):**
- Employees of companies that are residents of information technology parks are subject to a **unique 7% tax** calculated from the employer's sales revenue (inclusive of: income tax, social security contributions, and medical insurance contributions due from both employer and employees)
- This is a comprehensive flat rate replacing individual components

## Social Security

**Employer social insurance contributions:** (EY p. 1016)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social insurance contributions | 0% (employee does not pay) | **24%** of gross payroll | varies |
| Compulsory health insurance | **9%** of salary | 0% (employer does not pay) | varies |
| **Total (standard employee side)** | **9%** | **24%** | — |

**Notes:**
- Employer social insurance rate: 24% of gross payroll of all individuals (EY p. 1016)
- Employee mandatory medical insurance contribution: **9%** on salary and other labour remuneration (EY p. 1016)
- Social insurance and health insurance contributions are **deductible** for income tax purposes (EY p. 1014)
- IT Park employees: covered by the single 7% tax on employer sales revenue (replacing all contributions)

**Self-employed:**
- Self-employed individuals (sole ownerships, limited partnerships, general partnerships, farms) pay income tax at the standard 12% (7% for farms) rate
- Social insurance and medical insurance contributions apply at specified self-employment rates (not explicitly stated in EY; standard rates broadly apply)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Entrepreneur / Sole Ownership

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered as a sole trader, including members of individual enterprises, limited partnerships, general partnerships, and farms
- **Tax base:** Net income after deductible business expenses; personal deduction (MDL 29,700) available if income ≤ MDL 360,000
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard 12% flat PIT (7% for farms)
- **Social security:** Employer-side 24% social insurance on gross payroll (self-employed pay as "employer"); 9% medical insurance as employee equivalent
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds MDL 1,200,000; standard rate 20%; reduced rates 8%, 12%
- **Loss treatment:** Business losses may be carried forward for 5 years (EY p. 1015)
- **Capital gains:** 50% of gains included in taxable income; taxed at 12%

### Option B — IT Park Resident Company Employee

- **Who qualifies:** Employees of IT park resident companies (defined by Law on IT Parks of Moldova)
- **Tax base / Rate:** **7% single tax** on employer's sales revenue replaces all individual income tax, social security, and medical insurance for both employer and employee
- **Benefits:** Dramatically reduced total tax burden compared to standard regime
- **Social security:** Included in the 7% comprehensive rate — no separate contributions
- **Key limits:** Only available to employees of accredited IT park resident companies; IT activities must qualify

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Work Permits

- Foreign nationals may work in Moldova based on a special decision from Moldovan immigration authorities
- Exception: Ukrainian citizens may work during a State of Emergency without a work permit (EY p. 1017)
- Temporary residence permit: issued for 1-year periods; extendable for consecutive 1-year periods
- Extended residence permits: available for foreign investors making substantial investments or creating significant workplaces (ranging from 30–4,000+ average monthly salaries; permit validity 2–8 years depending on investment scale) (EY pp. 1017–1018)

### Double Tax Treaties

Moldova has DTTs with: Albania, Armenia, Austria, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Canada, China Mainland, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Estonia, Finland, France, Georgia, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Japan, Kazakhstan, Kuwait, Kyrgyzstan, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Montenegro, Netherlands, North Macedonia, Oman, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Russian Federation, Serbia, Slovak Republic, Slovenia, Spain, Switzerland, Tajikistan, Türkiye, Turkmenistan, Ukraine, United Arab Emirates, United Kingdom, Uzbekistan (EY p. 1016). The treaties generally provide a residency test of 183 days in a fiscal year.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Moldova uses MDL (Moldovan Leu). **1 EUR ≈ MDL 19.5** (approximate; MDL floats; verify at bnm.md).
> Personal deduction MDL 29,700 applies only if annual taxable income ≤ MDL 360,000.
> Employee medical insurance: 9% of salary. Employer social insurance: 24% (not employee burden, not shown).
> Self-employed: 12% PIT + 24% social insurance (employer-side) + 9% medical = total self-employed burden shown.

### €30,000 Employment
- MDL equivalent: EUR 30,000 × 19.5 = MDL 585,000/year
- Annual income MDL 585,000 > MDL 360,000 → personal deduction NOT available
- PIT: 12% × MDL 585,000 = MDL 70,200
- Medical insurance (employee): 9% × MDL 585,000 = MDL 52,650
- Total employee burden: MDL 122,850
- Effective rate: 122,850 / 585,000 = **21.0%**

### €30,000 Self-employed
- MDL 585,000; income > MDL 360,000 → no personal deduction
- PIT: 12% × MDL 585,000 = MDL 70,200
- Medical insurance: 9% × MDL 585,000 = MDL 52,650
- Social insurance (employer-side, self-pays): 24% × MDL 585,000 = MDL 140,400
- Total: MDL 263,250; Effective rate: 263,250 / 585,000 = **45.0%**
- Note: Social insurance is an additional cost on top of gross; total burden as % of MDL 585,000 is high for self-employed

### €60,000 Employment
- MDL: EUR 60,000 × 19.5 = MDL 1,170,000
- PIT: 12% × MDL 1,170,000 = MDL 140,400
- Medical: 9% × MDL 1,170,000 = MDL 105,300
- Total: MDL 245,700; Effective rate: 245,700 / 1,170,000 = **21.0%**

### €100,000 Employment
- MDL: MDL 1,950,000; PIT: 12% × MDL 1,950,000 = MDL 234,000
- Medical: 9% × MDL 1,950,000 = MDL 175,500
- Total: MDL 409,500; Effective rate: 409,500 / 1,950,000 = **21.0%**

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Personal deduction threshold (MDL 360,000):** Effectively irrelevant for the three income levels modelled (€30k, €60k, €100k all exceed MDL 360,000 at current rates); relevant for lower-income workers.
- [ ] **Self-employed social insurance exact rates:** EY states employer rate is 24%; self-employed bear this themselves. The exact rate for self-employed individuals (as distinct from employed persons) should be confirmed with the Moldovan National Social Insurance House (CNAS).
- [ ] **EUR/MDL exchange rate:** MDL floats; 19.5 is approximate. Verify at National Bank of Moldova (bnm.md).
- [ ] **Average monthly salary 2026:** Used as reference for various deduction limits; EY notes 2026 figure not yet approved. This affects medical service deductions, education deductions, and salary forecast-linked exemptions.
- [ ] **IT Park eligibility:** The 7% single rate is highly attractive but limited to employees of accredited IT park resident companies. Full list of qualifying activities and accreditation requirements at the Moldova IT Parks authority.
- [ ] **Capital gains on 50% of gains:** The 50% inclusion rule means effective capital gains rate is 6% (12% × 50%); applies to shares, bonds, business interests, non-business property, land, options.
