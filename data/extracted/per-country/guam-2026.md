## Personal Income Tax

Guam uses the US Internal Revenue Code in "mirror-image" fashion — the word "Guam" is substituted for "United States." Residents are subject to tax on all income regardless of source; nonresidents on Guam-source income only. Tax year = calendar year. Returns filed with the Government of Guam (not the IRS) by 15 April. (EY p.534–535)

**Residency:** Guam residents include US citizens and permanent residents (green card holders) who are bona fide residents of Guam (present ≥183 days in the tax year AND no tax home outside Guam and no closer connection to the US or a foreign country). Foreign nationals who are not lawful US permanent residents are Guam residents if present ≥31 days in the current year AND deemed present ≥183 days over three consecutive years (applying the formula: current year 100%, first preceding year 33.33%, second preceding year 16.67%). (EY p.534)

**Important note:** US citizens and permanent residents with Guam income file both a Guam return AND a US return; income taxes withheld on Guam wages offset Guam income reported on the US return. (EY p.543)

**Gross Receipts Tax (GRT):** A 5% gross receipts tax applies to all income earned by an individual in connection with a business in Guam (dropping to 4.5% effective 1 October 2025, and 4% effective 1 October 2026); certain exceptions apply (wholesale sales, real property sales, export sales). Directors' fees are also subject to GRT. (EY p.536)

**Capital gains tax — 2025 (long-term, single filer):** (EY p.537)
- 0%: income up to USD 48,350
- 15%: income USD 48,350–USD 533,400
- 20%: income above USD 533,400

**Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT):**
- 26% on alternative minimum taxable income up to USD 239,100.
- 28% on AMT above USD 239,100. (EY p.541)

**Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT):**
- 3.8% on net investment income for higher income taxpayers (>USD 250,000 married joint / >USD 200,000 single). (EY p.541)

**Standard deductions — 2025:** (EY p.538–539)
- Single individual: USD 15,750
- Married filing jointly: USD 31,500
- Head of household: USD 23,625
- Married filing separately: USD 15,750

**Key itemized deductions:**
- Unreimbursed medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of AGI.
- State and local income, general sales, and property taxes (limited to USD 40,000 total).
- Home mortgage interest and investment interest expenses.
- Charitable contributions to qualified charities.
- Casualty losses from specified natural disasters.

**Business deductions (self-employed):**
- Same deductions as employees plus ordinary and necessary business expenses.
- 20% qualified business income deduction (complex limits; income below USD 197,300 for single in 2025). (EY p.539)

**Tax credits:**
- Adoption expenses: up to USD 17,280.
- Child tax credit for dependants under 17 with SSN: USD 2,200.
- Dependent care credit: up to USD 500 for certain other dependants.
- Education credits (American Opportunity, Lifetime Learning): USD 2,000–USD 2,500. (EY p.541)

**Notes:**
- No separate Guam estate or gift tax; US estate and gift tax applies to US citizens and certain others on assets located in the US. Guam residents generally not subject to US estate tax on Guam assets. (EY p.542)
- Losses: passive losses only offset passive income; capital losses deductible against capital gains + up to USD 3,000 other income/year; carried forward indefinitely. (EY p.541)
- AMT designed to prevent high-income individuals from eliminating tax liability through deductions/credits. (EY p.541)

## Social Security (FICA)

Guam is covered under the US social security system via FICA. (EY p.542)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| OASDI (pension/disability) | 6.2% (up to USD 176,100) | 6.2% (up to USD 176,100) | 12.4% (up to USD 176,100) |
| Medicare (hospital) | 1.45% (no limit) | 1.45% (no limit) | 2.9% (no limit) |
| Additional Medicare | 0.9% (income >USD 200,000 single) | — | 0.9% (income >threshold) |
| **Total employee** | **~7.65%** | **~7.65%** | **~15.3%** |

**OASDI wage ceiling 2025:** USD 176,100. (EY p.542)

**Self-employment tax:** Self-employed pay the full 15.3% (OASDI 12.4% + Medicare 2.9%) on net self-employment income, but may deduct 50% as a trade/business expense. No SE tax if net earnings < USD 400. (EY p.543)

**Notes:**
- Nonresident aliens are subject to FICA tax unless exempt (foreign government employees, J-visa exchange visitors, F/M/Q-visa students, totalization agreement individuals). (EY p.542)
- GRT (Gross Receipts Tax 5%/4.5%/4%) is a business tax, not social security, but applies to business income.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Schedule C equivalent)
- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals and sole traders.
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus ordinary and necessary business expenses; 50% of SE tax is deductible. 20% qualified business income deduction (limits apply).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive US-mirror rates 10%–37% + 5% GRT on gross receipts (dropping to 4% by 2026).
- **Social security:** 15.3% SE tax (OASDI 12.4% up to USD 176,100 + Medicare 2.9%); 50% deductible.
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT; GRT of 5% (reducing to 4% by 1 October 2026) on gross receipts.
- **Key limits:** AMT may apply; complex passive activity loss rules.

---

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No preferential income tax regime for new residents/expats in Guam. Same US-mirror rules apply to all residents. Nonresidents subject to 30% flat withholding on Guam-source investment income. (EY p.535)

---

## 2026 Changes

- **GRT reduction from 1 October 2026:** Gross receipts tax on business income decreases from 4.5% (effective 1 October 2025) to 4% (effective 1 October 2026). (EY p.536)
- **US-mirror tax brackets:** 2025 brackets indexed for inflation; 2026 amounts will be slightly higher (inflation adjustment). Specific 2026 brackets not yet published at EY content date.
- **OASDI wage ceiling:** USD 176,100 for 2025; adjusted annually for wage inflation.
- **Stock option tax point (from 1 January 2026):** For awards made from 1 January 2026, the tax point will be the date of vesting, exercise, or seven years from grant (whichever is earlier). (EY p.553 — Guernsey note; Guam follows US rules).

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deduction USD 15,750; no itemized deductions modeled.
> USD currency — no conversion needed.
> "Employment" = gross salary; FICA 7.65% (OASDI 6.2% up to USD 176,100 + Medicare 1.45%); standard deduction applied.
> "B2B" = self-employment income; SE tax 15.3% (50% deductible); standard deduction applied; 20% QBI deduction applied (income below USD 197,300 threshold); GRT 5% on gross receipts.

### Working: USD 30,000 Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| FICA (7.65%) | 7.65% × 30,000 | USD 2,295 |
| AGI | 30,000 | USD 30,000 |
| Standard deduction | USD 15,750 | USD 15,750 |
| Taxable income | 30,000 − 15,750 | USD 14,250 |
| Tax: first USD 11,925 at 10% | 10% × 11,925 | USD 1,193 |
| Tax: USD 11,925–14,250 at 12% | 12% × 2,325 | USD 279 |
| Total income tax | 1,193 + 279 | USD 1,472 |
| **Total burden** | **2,295 + 1,472** | **USD 3,767** |
| **Effective rate** | **3,767 / 30,000** | **~12.6%** |

### Working: USD 30,000 B2B Self-Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| GRT (5% on gross) | 5% × 30,000 | USD 1,500 |
| SE tax (15.3% × 92.35% of net) | 15.3% × 27,705 | USD 4,239 |
| Deduct 50% of SE tax from AGI | 50% × 4,239 | USD 2,120 |
| AGI | 30,000 − 2,120 | USD 27,880 |
| 20% QBI deduction | 20% × 27,880 | USD 5,576 |
| Standard deduction | USD 15,750 | USD 15,750 |
| Taxable income | 27,880 − 5,576 − 15,750 | USD 6,554 |
| Income tax: 6,554 at 10% | | USD 655 |
| **Total burden** | **1,500 + 4,239 + 655** | **USD 6,394** |
| **Effective rate** | **6,394 / 30,000** | **~21.3%** |

**Working — USD 60,000 Employment:**
- FICA: 7.65% × 60,000 = USD 4,590
- Taxable: 60,000 − 15,750 = USD 44,250
- Tax: 1,193 + 12% × (44,250 − 11,925) = 1,193 + 12% × 32,325 = 1,193 + 3,879 = USD 5,072 (revised)

**Working — USD 100,000 Employment:**
- FICA: 7.65% × 100,000 = USD 7,650
- Taxable: 100,000 − 15,750 = USD 84,250
- Tax: 1,193 + 3,879 (12% × [48,475−11,925]) + 22% × (84,250 − 48,475) = 1,193 + 4,386 + 7,871 = USD 13,450 (revised)

**Assumptions:**
- USD currency; no conversion needed
- Single filer; standard deduction USD 15,750 (2025)
- Employment: FICA 7.65% (OASDI 6.2% up to USD 176,100 + Medicare 1.45%)
- B2B: SE tax 15.3% (applied to 92.35% of net); 50% of SE tax deductible from AGI; 20% QBI deduction; GRT 5% on gross
- No itemized deductions, no credits modeled
- GRT is effectively a gross revenue tax on business income (not a net income tax)
