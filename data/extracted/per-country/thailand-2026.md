## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for tax residents. Tax year = calendar year. Returns due by **31 March (paper)** or **8 April (e-filing)** of the following year. Employers withhold PIT monthly. (EY p.1531)

**Tax residency:** Resident if present in Thailand for ≥ **180 days** in the calendar year. Residents pay PIT on Thai-source income and on foreign-source income remitted to Thailand (see foreign income rules below). Non-residents pay PIT on Thai-source income only. (EY p.1527)

**Foreign-source income — key rule change:**
- **Pre-2024 rule:** Foreign income was taxable in Thailand only if remitted to Thailand **in the same calendar year** it was earned (remittance in a later year was untaxed).
- **From 1 January 2024 (Departmental Instruction No. Por.161/2566):** All foreign-source income remitted to Thailand by a **tax resident** is subject to Thai PIT **regardless of which year the income was earned**. This closed the one-year deferral loophole.
- **Protected period:** Foreign income earned **before 1 January 2024** is not affected — the rule is not retroactive.
- **Proposed 2025 amendment (not yet enacted as of May 2026):** The Thai Revenue Department has drafted a proposal to restore a partial exemption — allowing foreign income remitted within 2 years (the year earned or the following year) to be exempt. As of May 2026, this amendment has not been approved by the Cabinet; the strict 2024 rule remains in force.
- **LTR visa holders:** Certain LTR categories are explicitly exempt from Thai PIT on foreign-source income (see Special Regimes below).

(Sources: EY p.1527; Nishimura & Asahi — Thai Revenue Dept proposal June 2025; expattaxthailand.com; wiseequitylegal.com)

### Resident tax rates

| Net assessable income (THB) | Tax rate (%) |
|---|---|
| 0 – 150,000 | 0 |
| 150,001 – 300,000 | 5 |
| 300,001 – 500,000 | 10 |
| 500,001 – 750,000 | 15 |
| 750,001 – 1,000,000 | 20 |
| 1,000,001 – 2,000,000 | 25 |
| 2,000,001 – 5,000,000 | 30 |
| Above 5,000,000 | 35 |

(EY p.1530)

**Non-residents:** Flat rates apply; employment income subject to withholding.

**Inheritance tax:** Cumulative value above **THB 100 million per benefactor** is taxed at 5% for descendants/parents; 10% for all others. Filing within 150 days of receipt. (EY p.1530)

**Gift tax:** Generally 5% flat on gifts. Exempt: gifts from parent/child/spouse up to THB 20M/year; gifts in accordance with custom/tradition up to THB 10M/year. (EY p.1530)

## Social Security

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security Fund (SSF) | 5% | 5% | N/A (not covered) |
| Employee Welfare Fund (EWF, from Oct 2026) | 0.25% (from Oct 2026) | 0.25% (from Oct 2026) | N/A |
| **Total (2025–2026)** | **5%** | **5%** | — |

**Social Security income cap:** THB 15,000/month (2025). Maximum contribution: **THB 750/month (THB 9,000/year)** per employee and per employer. (EY p.1530)

**2026 SSF cap increase:** The capped remuneration is expected to increase from THB 15,000/month to **THB 17,500/month** from 2026 to 2028, while the 5% contribution rate stays the same. Maximum annual contribution per side would rise to **THB 10,500/year**. (EY p.1531 — note: EY states this update has not been officially announced by the Thai Revenue Department as of October 2025)

**Employee Welfare Fund (EWF):** Mandatory for businesses with ≥10 employees (unless already providing equivalent provident fund benefits). Contribution rate: **0.25%** starting **1 October 2026** for both employer and employee, increasing to 0.50% from October 2031. Further implementation details pending official notification. (EY p.1531)

**Self-employed:** Not covered by Thai social security. Pay PIT on business income directly.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader (Individual business income)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a trade, business or profession in Thailand.
- **Tax base:** Net business income (assessable income less allowable deductions). For service/work-contract income, standard deduction of 50% (capped at THB 100,000) or 60% applies depending on category; or actual expenses if higher.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT rates 0%–35% on net assessable income.
- **Social security:** Not covered by the SSF (Social Security Fund covers only employees). No mandatory social security contribution for self-employed.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration mandatory if annual taxable revenue exceeds **THB 1,800,000** (standard rate 7%; reduced from 10%).
- **Key limits:** Self-employment income from sources outside Thailand remitted to Thailand is taxable from 2024 (see foreign income rules above).

### Option B — Thai Limited Company (บริษัทจำกัด)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual who incorporates a Thai limited company and contracts through it.
- **Tax base:** Corporate Income Tax (CIT) at **20%** standard rate. SME rate: 15% on first THB 300,000, 20% on next THB 3.7M and above (if paid-up capital ≤ THB 5M, revenue ≤ THB 30M).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Salary drawn subject to PIT. Dividends: 10% withholding tax.
- **Social security:** If the shareholder-director draws a salary, both employer and employee SSF (5%) apply on salary up to THB 15,000/month cap.
- **Key limits:** Must have minimum 3 Thai shareholders; 49% foreign shareholding max without BOI approval or Foreign Business Licence. Higher compliance costs.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### LTR Visa (Long-Term Resident Visa)

Thailand's LTR visa program provides a 10-year stay (5+5 years) for four categories of high-potential foreigners. Some categories include significant tax privileges. (EY p.1532)

**Category 1 — Wealthy Global Citizen:**
- **Eligibility (revised February 2025):** Minimum global assets of USD 1,000,000; minimum USD 500,000 deployed in Thai government bonds, BOI-promoted investments, or Thai real estate; **income requirement removed** as of February 2025.
- **Tax benefit:** Foreign-source income remitted to Thailand is **exempt from Thai PIT** under the LTR privilege.
- **Duration:** 10 years.

**Category 2 — Wealthy Pensioner:**
- **Eligibility:** Age 50+; passive income ≥ USD 80,000/year OR personal assets ≥ USD 1,000,000 + passive income ≥ USD 40,000/year; health insurance required.
- **Tax benefit:** Foreign-source income remitted to Thailand is **exempt from Thai PIT**.
- **Duration:** 10 years.

**Category 3 — Work-from-Thailand Professional:**
- **Eligibility:** Employed by a foreign company for ≥2 years; annual income ≥ USD 80,000; work experience ≥5 years; health insurance.
- **Tax benefit:** **Flat 17% PIT** on Thai-source employment income (instead of progressive 0%–35%). Foreign-source employment income from the foreign employer is taxable if remitted to Thailand but benefits from reduced rate.
- **Duration:** 10 years.

**Category 4 — Highly Skilled Professional:**
- **Eligibility:** Working for a Thai entity in a targeted industry; specific qualifications; minimum salary THB 100,000/month or equivalent.
- **Tax benefit:** **Flat 17% PIT** on Thai-source employment income.
- **Duration:** 10 years.

(Sources: EY p.1532; goldenvisamap.com; hlbthai.com — LTR Visa 2026 Guide)

### DTV (Destination Thailand Visa)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals wishing to work remotely from Thailand. Minimum income/savings: USD 500 cash access per month. No employer or income requirements beyond showing sufficient funds.
- **Tax benefit:** No specific PIT exemption — standard Thai PIT rules apply. DTV holders who stay ≥180 days become Thai tax residents and are subject to PIT on Thai-source income and foreign-source income remitted (from 2024 rule).
- **Duration:** 5 years; up to 180 days per visit. (EY p.1532)

### Non-Immigrant B Visa + Work Permit (standard employment route)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals employed by a Thai entity.
- **Tax benefit:** None — standard progressive PIT 0%–35% applies.
- **Duration:** 1 year; renewable.
- **Caveats:** Employer must obtain work permit from Employment Department. (EY pp.1532–1533)

## 2026 Changes

- **Foreign income remittance rule (effective 1 January 2024):** All foreign income remitted to Thailand by tax residents is now taxable regardless of when earned (closes the prior-year deferral loophole). This change is in force as of 2026 despite a pending amendment proposal. (Departmental Instruction No. Por.161/2566)
- **Proposed 2025 amendment (not enacted as of May 2026):** Thai Revenue Department has drafted rules that would restore a 2-year remittance window exemption; draft requires Cabinet and Council of State approval; expected to apply from January–March 2026 filing period if enacted — monitor official announcements. (Nishimura & Asahi, June 2025)
- **Thailand e-Donation System (from 1 January 2026):** Donations to eligible organizations must be made through the Revenue Department's e-Donation system to qualify for PIT deductions; paper receipts no longer accepted. (EY p.1529)
- **Social Security cap increase (expected 2026–2028):** Monthly wage cap expected to rise from THB 15,000 to THB 17,500, increasing max annual SSF contribution per side from THB 9,000 to THB 10,500. Not yet officially confirmed as of EY publication date. (EY p.1531)
- **Employee Welfare Fund (from 1 October 2026):** 0.25% contribution for both employer and employee for businesses with ≥10 employees; implementation details still pending. (EY p.1531)
- **LTR Wealthy Global Citizen (February 2025):** Income requirement removed; now purely asset-based (USD 1M global assets + USD 500K Thai investment). (goldenvisamap.com; hlbthai.com)
- **Inheritance / gift tax:** No changes announced for 2026. Inheritance >THB 100M per benefactor: 5% for descendants/parents, 10% for others. (EY p.1530)
