## Personal Income Tax

**There is no personal income tax in the British Virgin Islands.** Under the Payroll Taxes Act 2004, corporate tax, PAYE income tax, and any other income tax payable under the Income Tax Ordinance are applied at a **0% rate**. (EY p.192)

Individuals are also **not subject to tax on investment income** in the BVI. (EY p.192)

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employment income | **0%** |
| Business / self-employment income | **0%** |
| Capital gains | **0%** (no capital gains tax) |
| Investment income (dividends, interest) | **0%** |

## Payroll Tax

Although income tax is 0%, the BVI imposes a **payroll tax** on employers. The payroll tax rate depends on whether the employer is Class I or Class II.

**Class I employers** (including self-employed persons meeting ALL conditions):
- Annual payroll ≤ USD 150,000
- Annual turnover/gross receipts ≤ USD 300,000
- Total employees/deemed employees ≤ 7

| Payer | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employee contribution | **8%** |
| Employer contribution | **2%** |
| **Total Class I payroll tax** | **10%** |

**Class II employers** (those NOT meeting all Class I conditions):

| Payer | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employee contribution | **8%** |
| Employer contribution | **6%** |
| **Total Class II payroll tax** | **14%** |

**Key rules:**
- Employers must pay payroll tax regardless of whether they withhold from employee remuneration
- Employers may withhold 8% from employee remuneration and pay that + their contribution (2% or 6%)
- First **USD 10,000 of earnings** per employee per tax year: **exempt from payroll tax** (employers may not withhold from first USD 10,000; exemption granted only once per person)
- Persons receiving income from a second employment are not entitled to the USD 10,000 exemption on that second income
- Tax year = calendar year; payroll tax due within 21 days after each month end

(EY pp.192–193)

## Social Security and Health Insurance

**National Insurance Scheme:**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| National Insurance | **4%** | **4.5%** | **8.5%** |

- Effective 1 January 2025: based on weekly/monthly wages up to **USD 980.77/week** or **USD 4,250/month** (USD 51,000/year ceiling)
- Maximum annual contribution: USD 2,040 (employee); USD 2,295 (employer)

**National Health Insurance (NHI):**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| NHI | **3.75%** | **3.75%** |

- Effective 1 January 2025: premiums assessed on monthly income up to **2× the upper wage limit for social security** = USD 8,500/month maximum
- Maximum monthly NHI contribution: **USD 318.75** each for employee and employer
- Employees must also contribute an additional 3.75% on behalf of an unemployed spouse

(EY p.193)

**Summary — maximum annual employee burden (social insurance + NHI):**
- National Insurance (employee): USD 2,040/year (capped)
- NHI (employee): USD 318.75/month × 12 = USD 3,825/year (capped at USD 8,500/month income)
- Total employee SS + NHI max: USD 5,865/year

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed (Class I)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed persons with gross receipts ≤ USD 300,000/year and ≤ 7 employees
- **Tax base:** N/A — no income tax on self-employment income
- **Payroll tax:** 10% total (8% employee + 2% employer — both paid by self-employed person); first USD 10,000 exempt
- **Social security:** National Insurance 8.5% total on income up to USD 51,000/year; NHI 7.5% total up to USD 8,500/month
- **Key limits:** USD 300,000 gross receipts ceiling for Class I status

### Option B — Self-Employed (Class II)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed persons with gross receipts > USD 300,000/year or > 7 employees
- **Tax base:** N/A — no income tax
- **Payroll tax:** 14% total (8% employee + 6% employer); first USD 10,000 exempt
- **Social security:** Same as Class I
- **Key limits:** No upper limit

(EY pp.192–193)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No income tax means there is no preferential tax regime needed. The BVI imposes no income tax on any individual. The BVI is widely used as an offshore financial centre — individuals must obtain work permits to work in the BVI.

**Note:** The BVI is primarily known as an offshore company jurisdiction. Individuals residing and working in the BVI are subject to payroll tax (not income tax) and social insurance contributions.

## 2026 Changes

- No income tax — no PIT changes.
- **National Insurance ceiling increased (from 1 January 2025):** Maximum weekly wages for NI purposes: USD 980.77/week (USD 4,250/month, USD 51,000/year); maximum annual employee contribution USD 2,040; employer USD 2,295. (EY p.193)
- **NHI ceiling (from 1 January 2025):** Maximum assessed income doubled to USD 8,500/month (2× NI upper wage limit); maximum monthly NHI: USD 318.75 each for employee and employer. (EY p.193)

## Effective Rate Examples

> The BVI has NO income tax. The individual burden is: (1) payroll tax employee portion (8%), (2) National Insurance (4%), and (3) NHI (3.75%).
> **1 EUR ≈ USD 1.08** (BVI uses USD as official currency).
> Class I scenario assumed (small employer/self-employed); USD 10,000 annual exemption applied.

### €30,000 Employment — Class I employer (≈ USD 32,400/year)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross annual remuneration | USD 32,400 | |
| Payroll tax exempt (first USD 10,000) | USD 10,000 | |
| Payroll taxable base | 32,400 − 10,000 | USD 22,400 |
| Employee payroll tax (8%) | 8% × 22,400 | USD 1,792 |
| National Insurance (4% × min(32,400; 51,000)) | 4% × 32,400 | USD 1,296 |
| NHI (3.75% × min(32,400/12; 8,500) × 12) | 3.75% × 32,400 | USD 1,215 |
| **Total burden** | 1,792 + 1,296 + 1,215 | **USD 4,303** |
| **Effective rate** | 4,303 / 32,400 | **13.3%** |

### Summary table

| Gross income (USD) | EUR equiv | Scenario | Payroll tax (employee) | NI + NHI (employee) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| USD 32,400 | €30,000 | Employment Class I | USD 1,792 | USD 2,511 | USD 4,303 | **13.3%** |
| USD 32,400 | €30,000 | B2B self-employed Class I | USD 1,792 | USD 2,511 | USD 4,303 | **13.3%** |
| USD 64,800 | €60,000 | Employment Class I | USD 4,384 | USD 5,865 (capped) | USD 10,249 | **15.8%** |
| USD 64,800 | €60,000 | B2B self-employed Class II (>USD 300k threshold not met) | USD 4,384 | USD 5,865 | USD 10,249 | **15.8%** |
| USD 108,000 | €100,000 | Employment Class II (large employer) | USD 7,904 | USD 5,865 (capped) | USD 13,769 | **12.7%** |
| USD 108,000 | €100,000 | B2B self-employed Class II | USD 7,904 | USD 5,865 (capped) | USD 13,769 | **12.7%** |

**Employee payroll tax calculation for €60k (USD 64,800):**
- Exempt: first USD 10,000
- Taxable: USD 54,800
- 8% × 54,800 = USD 4,384

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/USD = 1.08 (BVI uses USD; approximate EUR/USD rate)
- USD 10,000 annual payroll tax exemption applied per employee (first employment only)
- NI capped at USD 51,000/year base: employee 4% × min(salary, 51,000)
- NHI capped at USD 8,500/month base: 3.75% × min(monthly salary, 8,500) × 12
- Class I employer assumed for €30k and €60k scenarios (gross receipts ≤ USD 300,000)
- Self-employed pays both employee (8%) and employer (2% Class I / 6% Class II) portions; table shows employee portion only for comparability — self-employed total payroll tax = 10%–14%

## Known Gaps

- [ ] Self-employed: for comparability the table shows the "employee portion" (8%) only; self-employed pay full 10%–14% payroll tax (both employee and employer portions); total self-employed burden is higher than shown
- [ ] NHI additional contribution for unemployed spouse (3.75%): not modelled — applies to employed individuals with unemployed spouses
- [ ] Capital transfer/stamp duty on real estate (4% general; 12% to non-BVI Islanders): relevant for property transactions, not income
- [ ] Double tax treaty: BVI has only one treaty (with UK); very limited treaty protection for other jurisdictions
- [ ] Work permit requirements for foreign nationals working in BVI: not detailed in EY; required for employment by foreign nationals
- [ ] BVI is a British Overseas Territory — UK has oversight but BVI has full fiscal autonomy; 0% income tax confirmed and stable
