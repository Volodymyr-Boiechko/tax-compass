## Personal Income Tax

Flat-rate PIT system. Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents taxed on Uzbekistan-source income only. Administered by the State Tax Committee (Soliq).

**Residency:** Physically present for 183+ days in any 12-month period beginning or ending in the calendar year for which residency is determined. Individuals present for less than 183 days in Uzbekistan but more than in any other state are also considered residents.

### Tax Rates (EY p. 1695)

| Taxpayer type | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Resident individuals — general income | **12%** | Flat rate on all income (employment, self-employment, royalties, etc.) |
| Non-resident individuals — general income | **12%** | Flat rate; except: |
| Non-resident — dividends | **5%** | Withholding (final) |
| Non-resident — interest and freight income | **10%** | Withholding (final) |

**Investment income (residents) — EY p. 1694:**
- Dividends from Uzbek companies: **exempt** (from 1 April 2022 to 31 December 2028; previously 5% for residents)
- Interest on bonds of enterprises: **exempt** (from 1 April 2022 to 31 December 2028; previously 5% for residents)
- Interest on bank deposits and government securities: **exempt** (EY p. 1695)
- Royalties and other investment income: taxed at general 12% rate

**Capital gains:**
- Capital gains from disposal of private non-business property: generally exempt
- Capital gains from sale of residential premises held > 36 months: exempt
- Other capital gains: taxed at 12% flat rate
- Capital losses: not deductible (EY p. 1695)

**Exempt income includes (EY p. 1693):**
- Alimony
- State pension income
- Gifts received from other individuals (except immovable property, motor vehicles, securities received from non-close relatives; and employer gifts > 2.11× minimum wage per year ~USD 220/year)
- Funds of employees directed to ESOP (up to monthly salary equivalent)

**Deductions:**
- No significant tax deductions for individuals (except private entrepreneurs applying individual income tax who may deduct documented business expenses — EY p. 1695)

## Social Security

Employers make mandatory contributions to individual accumulative pension accounts and pay social tax (EY p. 1695):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Individual Pension Accumulation (INPS) | **4%** | — | Deducted from employee salary; subtracted from accrued individual income tax |
| Social Tax (employer) | — | **12%** | On gross payroll (25% for state organizations) |
| **Total** | **4%** | **12%** | |

**Notes:**
- Employee INPS contribution (4%): subtracted from accrued individual income tax (i.e., acts as a tax credit against the 12% PIT, not an additional charge on top)
- Employer social tax 12% is an additional employer-side cost on gross payroll (25% for state/government organizations)
- Uzbek law does not contain provisions allowing foreign citizens to be self-employed in Uzbekistan (EY p. 1698); foreigners must work through an Uzbek employer

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Private Entrepreneur (Yakka Tartibli Tadbirkor) — General Regime

- **Who qualifies:** Uzbek citizens registered as private entrepreneurs (individual businesspersons)
- **Tax base:** Gross income less documented, allowable business expenses
- **Rate / mechanism:** **12% flat** individual income tax on net income; annual individual income tax declaration filed by 1 April of following year; additional tax due by 1 June
- **Social security:** Social tax 12% of payroll if employees are engaged; INPS 4% for self
- **VAT obligations:** Register for VAT if annual taxable turnover ≥ UZS 1 billion (~USD 82,176); standard VAT rate 12%
- **Key limits:** Losses cannot be carried forward or back (EY p. 1695); foreign citizens cannot be self-employed in Uzbekistan

### Option B — Small Business Simplified Tax (Revenue Tax) — effective 1 January 2026

- **Who qualifies:** **Private entrepreneurs and self-employed individuals** with annual turnover **up to UZS 1 billion (~USD 82,176)**; effective from 1 January 2026 (EY p. 1693)
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue (turnover)
- **Rate / mechanism:** **1% of revenue** (EY p. 1693)
- **Social security:** Social security contributions still apply separately
- **VAT obligations:** Exempt from VAT if below UZS 1 billion threshold
- **Key limits:** Annual revenue ceiling UZS 1 billion; above this threshold, subject to general tax regime (corporate income tax, VAT, and other taxes applicable to legal entities); **this is a major new regime for small IT freelancers and service providers in Uzbekistan**

**Note for IT sector:** Uzbekistan has a well-developed IT Park regime for tech companies (IT Park residents get special tax treatment including 0% CIT and reduced payroll taxes). Individual IT specialists employed by IT Park residents benefit from reduced payroll tax rates at the company level. The 1% simplified revenue tax is relevant for very small individual service providers.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### IT Visa and Investment Visa (EY p. 1697)

- **IT visa:** Long-term multiple-entry visa for IT specialists and IT Park founders; valid for up to 3 years; renewable without leaving Uzbekistan; no standard work permit required
- **Investment visa:** For foreign nationals investing ≥ 8,500× the basic calculation value; valid for up to 3 years; multiple-entry
- **Tax treatment:** IT visa holders working for an Uzbek IT Park resident company are subject to standard Uzbek employment taxation (12% PIT flat; employer 12% social tax); IT Park companies themselves have special tax treatment reducing the effective burden

### No General Newcomer PIT Exemption

Uzbekistan does not offer a general territorial or exemption-based regime for new tax residents. All residents pay 12% flat on worldwide income.

### Foreign Nationals — Work Restriction

- Uzbek law does **not allow foreign citizens to be self-employed** in Uzbekistan (EY p. 1698)
- Foreign nationals must work through an Uzbek employer or representative office of a foreign company
- An accreditation card is required for representatives of foreign companies, issued for one year

## 2026 Changes

- **NEW from 1 January 2026 — Revenue Tax 1% for small private entrepreneurs:** Private entrepreneurs with annual turnover ≤ UZS 1 billion (~USD 82,176) subject to 1% revenue tax instead of standard 12% income tax (EY p. 1693) — **major change**
- Dividend/interest exemption for securities: continues through 31 December 2028 (EY p. 1694)
- Annual PIT declaration deadline: 1 April of year following reporting year; payment by 1 June
- No announced changes to 12% flat PIT rate or employer social tax rate (12%)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants; no significant deductions allowed.
> Exchange rate: **1 EUR = UZS 13,500** (approximate 2025; USD/UZS ~12,500, EUR/USD ~1.08).
> "Employment" = gross salary; INPS 4% acts as a credit against PIT, not an additional charge.
> "B2B" = private entrepreneur under the NEW 1% simplified revenue tax (from 1 January 2026) for turnover ≤ USD 82,176.
> Employer social tax 12% is an additional employer-side cost (not deducted from employee).

### INPS Credit Mechanism Explanation

The 4% INPS contribution is **deducted from accrued PIT** — meaning:
- Gross salary: UZS 100,000
- PIT (12%): UZS 12,000
- INPS (4%): UZS 4,000 — subtracted from the UZS 12,000 PIT due
- Net PIT paid to state: UZS 8,000
- INPS paid to pension account: UZS 4,000
- **Total deducted from employee: 12% gross** (same as flat PIT rate — the 4% INPS credit is included within the 12%, not on top)

Effective employee-side deduction = **12% of gross salary** (12% PIT, of which 4 percentage points go to pension account, 8 percentage points go to state).

| Step | Calculation | Result |

| **INPS to pension account** | 4% × 405,000,000 | **UZS 16,200,000** |

**Key insight:** Uzbekistan offers one of the lowest effective tax rates in the world for small-scale private entrepreneurs from 2026 — **1% revenue tax** on turnover below UZS 1 billion (~EUR 74k). For employment, the flat 12% rate is also low by international standards. Above the UZS 1 billion threshold, the general regime (12% income tax + VAT) applies.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/UZS = 13,500 (approximate 2025)
- Employment: 12% flat PIT; 4% INPS included within 12% (not additional); employer social tax 12% not shown (employer-side cost)
- B2B (2026): 1% revenue tax for turnover ≤ UZS 1B; no social security separately modeled for self-employed (see gaps)
- Above UZS 1B (~EUR 74k): general regime 12% income tax applies to net income
- No dividends, no foreign income

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Self-employed social security for private entrepreneurs:** EY does not specify INPS/social tax obligations for solo private entrepreneurs without employees; needs verification with Soliq/INPS
- [ ] **1% revenue tax launch date:** EY states effective 1 January 2026; as of EY data date, enabling regulations may not be fully published; verify with Soliq
- [ ] **IT Park company-level benefits:** The IT Park regime (0% CIT, reduced payroll taxes) applies to resident companies, not directly to individual employees; individual IT specialists benefit indirectly; details need IT Park Uzbekistan verification
- [ ] **Foreign citizens self-employment ban:** Confirmed by EY; the 1% simplified regime and general private entrepreneur status are available only to Uzbek citizens (and potentially permanent residents); foreigners must use employer route
- [ ] **VAT registration threshold:** UZS 1 billion (same as revenue tax threshold); above this, full VAT and general tax regime apply
- [ ] **INPS credit mechanism:** EY p. 1695 states contributions "subtracted from accrued individual income tax" — treatment confirmed but nuances for high earners with significant foreign income may differ
