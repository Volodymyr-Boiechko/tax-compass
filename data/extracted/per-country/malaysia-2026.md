## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for tax residents. Tax year = calendar year. Self-assessment system; employees subject to Monthly Tax Deduction Scheme (MTDS). Returns due by 30 April (employment income only) or 30 June (business income) of the following year.

**Residency:** An individual is resident if physically present ≥ 182 days in the calendar year (or meets alternative multi-year tests). Non-residents pay a flat 30% withholding on most income (employment income: 30% on cash remuneration). (EY pp.927–928)

**Foreign-sourced income (FSI) — current rule:**
- From **1 January 2022**, the historic blanket territorial exemption for FSI was removed.
- However, a conditional **tax exemption** applies to FSI received in Malaysia from **1 January 2022 to 31 December 2036** for tax residents, **excluding** income from a partnership business in Malaysia, provided the FSI has been **subject to tax in the country of origin** (income tax or withholding tax paid or payable). (EY p.927; extended via Malaysia Budget 2026)
- FSI kept offshore is **not** treated as received in Malaysia.
- Resident individuals must declare qualifying FSI in their return and retain supporting documentation.
- **DE Rantau (digital nomad) visa holders:** Foreign-sourced income is **0% taxable** — nomad visa holders are not tax residents and only Malaysian-source income (if any) would be taxed. (EY p.943 — DE Rantau Pass)

(Sources: EY p.927; KPMG Flash Alert 2022-010; Budget 2026 extension)

**Notes:**
- Malaysia does not impose estate, gift or net worth taxes. (EY p.935)
- Interest income from approved financial institutions is **exempt** from income tax. (EY p.929)
- Dividends: generally exempt under single-tier system. Dividends from Malaysian companies exceeding MYR 100,000/year subject to **2% tax** from YA 2025. (EY p.930)
- Capital gains on real property: Real Property Gains Tax (RPGT) applies. For Malaysian citizens/PRs: 30% (held ≤ 3 years), 20% (3–4 years), 15% (4–5 years), 0% (>5 years). For non-citizens: 30% (≤5 years), 10% (>5 years). (EY pp.930–931)
- Capital gains from disposal of chargeable assets (shares in closely held companies with substantial real property) effective 1 January 2022 also subject to RPGT. (EY p.930)

## Social Security

### EPF (Employees Provident Fund)

EPF is a mandatory statutory savings scheme for Malaysian citizens and permanent residents' old-age retirement. **From 1 October 2025, EPF contributions also apply to non-Malaysian citizen employees** (excluding domestic workers) with valid work passes — at a fixed 2% rate for both employer and employee. (EY p.936)

| Contribution | Employee (%) | Employer (%) | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| EPF — Malaysian citizens/PRs, salary > MYR 5,000/month | 11 | 12 | Voluntary; up to MYR 100,000/year |
| EPF — Malaysian citizens/PRs, salary ≤ MYR 5,000/month | 11 | 13 | — |
| EPF — Malaysian citizens/PRs, age 60+ | 11 | 4 | — |
| EPF — Non-Malaysian citizens (non-PRs), from 1 Oct 2025 | 2 | 2 | N/A |
| **Total (citizen, salary > MYR 5,000)** | **11** | **12** | — |

**Notes:**
- No ceiling on wages subject to EPF contributions (unlike many countries). (EY p.936)
- EPF Account 3 (flexible account) introduced 11 May 2024: 10% of all new contributions go to Account 3, withdrawable at any time.
- EPF contributions and interest credited are **not subject to Malaysian income tax** on withdrawal (at age 55 or on permanent departure).
- Employers may increase contributions up to 19% without restriction by tax authorities, still deductible for corporate tax.
- Prior to 1 October 2025, foreign employees were **not required** to contribute to EPF. The new 2% rate applies to wages from October 2025 onward.

### SOCSO (Social Security Organisation)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Employment Injury Scheme | Nil | Varies |
| Invalidity Scheme | Varies | Varies |
| **For foreign employees (from 1 July 2024)** | **Included (prescribed schedule)** | **1.25%, capped at MYR 74.40 for wages > MYR 6,000** |

- Malaysian citizens: SOCSO contributions mandatory under the Employees' Social Security Act 1969.
- **Foreign employees:** From 1 January 2019, employers must register and contribute to the Employment Injury Scheme. From **1 July 2024**, both employer and employee portions are required for foreign employees (including expatriates and foreign domestic workers), per a prescribed schedule. (EY p.936)
- SOCSO contributions are deductible by employee (up to MYR 350 relief). (EY p.932)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Partnership (standard)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a business, profession or trade in Malaysia.
- **Tax base:** All profits from business; assessed under self-assessment. Business income taxed at progressive PIT rates 0%–30%.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT rates per the resident tax table above.
- **Social security:** EPF not mandatory for self-employed (voluntary up to MYR 100,000/year). SOCSO not applicable to self-employed.
- **VAT (SST) obligations:** Sales and Service Tax (SST) — service tax at **8%** (from 1 March 2024; previously 6%) applies to prescribed taxable services; registration threshold is MYR 500,000/year of taxable services.
- **Key limits:** Business losses may be carried forward for 10 years (from 2019 YA). Standard deductions (9,000 self relief, etc.) apply to reduce business income.

### Option B — Sdn. Bhd. (Private Limited Company)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual incorporating a Malaysian private limited company.
- **Tax base:** Corporate income taxed at **24%** (standard rate). SME rate: **17%** on first MYR 600,000 of chargeable income if paid-up ordinary share capital ≤ MYR 2.5M and not controlled by a company with >MYR 2.5M paid-up capital.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Salary drawn by director is subject to PIT. Dividends are tax-exempt under single-tier system (above MYR 100,000/year subject to 2% tax from YA 2025).
- **Social security:** Malaysian citizen/PR employees trigger mandatory EPF (11%/12%). Foreign director drawing salary: 2%/2% EPF from Oct 2025.
- **Key limits:** Higher compliance costs; annual statutory audit required for companies with revenue > MYR 2M.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### MM2H (Malaysia My Second Home Programme)
- **Eligibility:** Applicants ≥35 years old, show liquid assets ≥ MYR 1,500,000, offshore income ≥ MYR 40,000/month, fixed deposit in Malaysia ≥ MYR 1,000,000 (additional MYR 50,000 for dependants aged 35–49).
- **Benefit:** Long-Term Social Visit Pass (LTSVP) valid 10 years (renewable); live in Malaysia long-term. MM2H holders may own a business but cannot be involved in day-to-day operations. **Tax treatment:** MM2H holders are typically tax residents if present ≥182 days — they pay standard PIT on Malaysian-source income. FSI exempt (subject to "subjected to tax in source country" condition) under the FSI exemption to 2036.
- **Duration:** 10 years (renewable). Open to all nationalities.
- **Application deadline:** Ongoing.
- **Caveats:** Not a permanent residence permit. Cannot work/be employed in Malaysia. Medical insurance required. (EY pp.944–945)

### PViP (Premium Visa Programme)
- **Eligibility:** Offshore income ≥ MYR 40,000/month (MYR 480,000/year) OR fixed deposit ≥ MYR 1,000,000; participation fee MYR 200,000 (principal) + MYR 100,000 (each dependant).
- **Benefit:** LTSVP up to 20 years; unlike MM2H, PViP participants **can run businesses, find employment, invest and study** in Malaysia.
- **Duration:** Up to 20 years.
- **Caveats:** Applied through approved PViP agency. (EY p.945)

### DE Rantau Nomad Pass
- **Eligibility:** Digital freelancers/contractors (active project contracts > 3 months with ≥1 Malaysian or non-Malaysian client) OR remote workers (employed by non-Malaysian company for > 3 months). Minimum annual income: USD 24,000. Sectors: IT, digital marketing, digital creative content, digital content development.
- **Benefit:** Work permit (PVP variant) valid up to **2 years**, sponsoring of qualifying dependants. Foreign-sourced income effectively **0% taxable** — DE Rantau holders are present in Malaysia but income comes from foreign employers/clients; as non-tax residents (if <182 days) or with no Malaysian-source income, standard PIT does not apply.
- **Duration:** Up to 2 years.
- **Caveats:** Holder must not be employed by a Malaysian company. Does not automatically confer tax residency. (EY pp.942–943)

### Residence Pass-Talent (RP-T)
- **Eligibility:** Highly qualified expatriates with ≥3 years continuous living and working in Malaysia, minimum basic monthly salary MYR 15,000.
- **Benefit:** Live and work in Malaysia for up to **10 years**; change employers without new work permit; spouse can work (RP-T issued to spouse); children can study.
- **Duration:** 10 years; renewable.
- **Caveats:** Must be approved by RP-T panel. (EY p.943)

### Employment Pass (EP)
- **Eligibility:** Any non-Malaysian employed by a Malaysian company. Minimum contract salary MYR 5,000/month. Issued for up to 5 years, subject to MID approval.
- **Benefit:** Work authorization. Standard PIT applies (no special rate unless qualifying for JS-SEZ, REP or other incentive programmes).
- **Duration:** 1–60 months depending on role; renewable.
- **Caveats:** Prior DOL approval required; Labor Market Testing (LMT) required for most roles in West Malaysia. (EY pp.941–942)

## 2026 Changes

- **FSI exemption extended to 31 December 2036:** Malaysia Budget 2026 extended the conditional FSI tax exemption for resident individuals (all classes of income except partnership income) to 2036 — original transitional period was 2022–2026. FSI still must have been subjected to tax in the source country. (Source: Malaysia Budget 2026; CACORP; KPMG)
- **EPF for non-Malaysian employees (from 1 October 2025):** 2% employer + 2% employee contribution now mandatory for all non-Malaysian citizen employees with valid immigration passes (excluding domestic workers). This is a significant change for expatriates. (EY p.936)
- **SOCSO for foreign workers (from 1 July 2024):** Both employer and employee portions of SOCSO now mandatory for all foreign employees including expatriates. (EY p.936)
- **Dividend tax (from YA 2025):** Dividends received by individuals from Malaysian companies exceeding MYR 100,000/year are subject to a 2% tax. (EY p.930)
- **EPF Account 3 (from 11 May 2024):** 10% of all new EPF contributions allocated to flexible Account 3, withdrawable anytime. (EY p.937)
- **JS-SEZ knowledge workers:** 15% flat PIT available for qualifying workers in Johor-Singapore Special Economic Zone; applications open from 1 January 2025 to 31 December 2034. (EY p.934)

The top PIT rate of 30% (above MYR 2M) is high but the effective rate at €100k (~21–32%) is competitive within Southeast Asia.

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **EPF for foreign employees (2% from Oct 2025):** The employment effective rate table uses the 11% Malaysian citizen EPF rate; for foreign EP holders, EPF was 0% before Oct 2025 and 2% from Oct 2025 — re-run calculations with 2% EPF for foreigner employment scenario
- [ ] **SOCSO exact rates for foreign employees:** Prescribed schedule from Jul 2024 not fully extracted; amount is small but should be verified via SOCSO official schedule
- [ ] **Dividend tax (2% above MYR 100,000):** Not modeled in the scenarios — affects high-income sole proprietors who pay themselves dividends via Sdn. Bhd.
- [ ] **FSI "subjected to tax" condition detail:** If income earned in a zero-tax country (e.g., UAE, Gulf states), it may not qualify for the FSI exemption — verify LHDN guidance on this specific scenario
- [ ] **DE Rantau tax residency edge case:** If a DE Rantau holder stays >182 days in Malaysia, they become a tax resident; income from foreign clients could then become taxable if not covered by the FSI exemption; needs monitoring
- [ ] **SST (service tax) threshold:** MYR 500,000 for taxable services — not modeled; relevant for higher-income sole traders in prescribed service sectors
- [ ] **RPGT (real property gains tax):** Not modeled — relevant for property investors; rates vary by nationality and holding period
