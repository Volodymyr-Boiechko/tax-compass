## Personal Income Tax

Morocco levies income tax (*Impôt sur le Revenu* — IR) on residents' worldwide income; individuals resident in Morocco must pay tax on their employment income regardless of where services are performed or employer located. Nonresidents: taxed on Moroccan-source income only. Residency: maintain home OR center of activities (vital interests) in Morocco OR present 183+ days in any 365-day period. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return due by 1 March (fixed-tax regime / professional income), 1 May (RNR/RNS regimes), or 1 April (dividends/interest/capital gains from securities). (EY pp. 1038, 1045–1046)

**Note:** EY chapter explicitly states it has been updated based on the 2026 Finance Law. (EY p. 1038)

### Progressive income tax rates (employees and standard regime)

| Taxable income (MAD) | Tax rate | Tax due (MAD) | Cumulative tax due (MAD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 40,000 | **0%** | 0 | 0 |
| Next 20,000 (40,001–60,000) | **10%** | 2,000 | 2,000 |
| Next 20,000 (60,001–80,000) | **20%** | 4,000 | 6,000 |
| Next 20,000 (80,001–100,000) | **30%** | 6,000 | 12,000 |
| Next 80,000 (100,001–180,000) | **34%** | 27,200 | 39,200 |
| Above 180,000 | **37%** | — | — |

Maximum marginal rate: **37%** (EY p. 1044)

**Professional expense deduction (employment):** (EY p. 1043)
- 25% for gross annual taxable income > MAD 78,000 (capped at MAD 35,000 deduction)
- 35% for gross annual taxable income ≤ MAD 78,000

**Social security contributions deductible** from gross employment income for PIT purposes. (EY p. 1043)

**Additional personal deductions/credits:** (EY p. 1044)
- MAD 600 tax credit per dependant (up to maximum 6 dependants)
- 80% of income tax on foreign-source retirement pensions received in non-convertible dirhams

**Exempt income (key items):** (EY pp. 1038–1039)
- Family allowances
- Workers' compensation for industrial accidents or death
- Specific professional expense allowances (if corresponding to actual expenses beyond flat-rate deduction)
- Voluntary departure pay and severance within Labor Law limits (up to MAD 1 million)
- Alimony
- Supplementary pension (minimum 8-year contract, contributions not deductible)
- Compensation for pregnancy leave
- Literary/art awards up to MAD 100,000/year
- Tourism vouchers within limits
- Invalidity pensions paid to service persons from January 2020
- Trainee allowances up to MAD 6,000/month for 12 months
- Primary pension scheme pensions and retirement revenues

**New hire exemption:** Gross salaries of newly hired employees (first recruitment) up to MAD 10,000/month exempt for 24 months from hiring date; employee age ≤ 35; must be hired on open-ended contract; extended until 31 December 2026 by the 2023 Finance Law (extended to 2026 in EY). (EY p. 1039)

**Rental income:** Gross property income ≥ MAD 120,000 subject to IR at 15% (no filing exemption); from 2025, option to tax at 20% regardless of amount (EY p. 1043)

**Capital gains on real estate:** 20% rate; minimum tax 3% of transfer price; gains ≤ MAD 140,000/year: exempt (EY p. 1042)

**Capital gains on listed shares:** 15%; unlisted: 20% (EY p. 1042)

**Dividends withholding (as updated):** (EY p. 1041)
- 12.50% for benefits distributed from fiscal years beginning on/after 1 January 2025
- 11.25% for fiscal years beginning on/after 1 January 2026
- 10% for fiscal years beginning on/after 1 January 2027

**Interest:** 30% final withholding tax for resident individuals; excluded from RNS/RNR base. (EY p. 1041)

## Social Security

**Employer contributions (monthly, on gross compensation including benefits):** (EY p. 1045)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Monthly ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| Family allowances | 0% | **6.4%** | No ceiling |
| Illness/disability/maternity/death pensions | **4.29%** | **8.6%** | MAD 6,000 |
| "Loss of employment indemnity" | **0.19%** | **0.38%** | MAD 6,000 |
| Staff training (employer only) | 0% | **1.6%** | No ceiling |
| Mandatory medical insurance (AMO) | **2.26%** | **4.11%** | No ceiling |
| **Total (approx.)** | **~6.74%** | **~21.09%** | — |

**Notes:**
- Monthly ceiling for illness/disability pensions and loss-of-employment: MAD 6,000 (= MAD 72,000/year cap on these contributions)
- Social security contributions are **deductible** from employment gross income for PIT purposes (EY p. 1043)
- Companies set up before 2005 and already contributing to private medical insurance: 1.85% AMO rate instead of 4.11% (exempted from standard AMO) (EY p. 1045)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Unique Professional Contribution (CPU)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed service providers and commercial/industrial/artisan activities with annual turnover ≤ MAD 2 million (commercial/industrial/artisan) or ≤ MAD 500,000 (services)
- **Tax base:** Annual (or quarterly) realized turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:** **10%** applied to realized turnover; replaces the old fixed-tax regime (in force from 2021)
- **Complementary taxes:** Additional quarterly/annual surplus payments based on the income tax amount:

| Annual income tax (MAD) | Annual surplus of tax (MAD) |
|---|---|
| < 500 | MAD 1,200 |
| 500–1,000 | MAD 1,560 |
| 1,001–2,500 | MAD 2,280 |
| 2,501–5,000 | MAD 2,880 |
| 5,001–10,000 | MAD 4,200 |
| 10,001–25,000 | MAD 6,000 |
| 25,001–50,000 | MAD 9,000 |
| Above 50,000 | MAD 14,400 |

- **Capital gains rate under CPU:** 20% (EY p. 1040)
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory VAT above MAD 500,000 (services) or MAD 2M (commercial); standard rate 20%

### Option B — Auto-Entrepreneur Regime

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals with annual turnover ≤ MAD 500,000 (commercial/industrial/crafts) or ≤ MAD 200,000 (service providers); must register with Morocco social security (CNSS)
- **Tax base:** Annual turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - **0.5%** on turnover not exceeding MAD 500,000 for commercial, industrial, and crafts activities
  - **1%** on turnover not exceeding MAD 200,000 for service providers
- **Social security:** Mandatory registration with CNSS required
- **VAT:** Exempt (turnover below VAT threshold)
- **Bookkeeping:** Exempt from bookkeeping, inventory, computerised invoicing, invoice layout, and electronic address requirements (from 2020 Finance Law) (EY p. 1040)
- **Best for:** Low-revenue service providers — extremely competitive effective rate

### Option C — Actual Net Income (RNR) or Simplified Net Income (RNS) Regime

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals with professional/commercial income exceeding CPU/auto-entrepreneur thresholds
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus all business expenses); RNR = actual accounts; RNS = simplified bookkeeping
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT rates (0%–37%)
- **Business losses:** Carryforward 4 years; capital allowance depreciation carryforward indefinitely (EY p. 1044)
- **Filing:** Annual return by 1 May (30 April at latest)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### New Hire Tax Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Newly hired employees on their first recruitment; age ≤ 35 years; open-ended contract
- **Benefit:** Gross salary up to MAD 10,000/month exempt for first 24 months from hiring date
- **Duration:** 24 months; extended by 2023 Finance Law until 31 December 2026 (EY p. 1039)

### Local Employment Contract (Expatriate)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign national with local employment contract with a Moroccan company or branch; prior ANAPEC approval and Ministry of Employment approval required (EY p. 1047)

### Secondment from Foreign Parent Company

- Approval from Ministry of Employment required (EY p. 1048)

### Social Contribution to Solidarity (from 2022)

- Applies to individuals with **net income ≥ MAD 1 million/year** (EY p. 1044–1045):

| Net income subject (MAD) | Contribution rate |
|---|---|
| MAD 1,000,000 – MAD 5,000,000 | **1.5%** |
| MAD 5,000,000 – MAD 10,000,000 | **2.5%** |
| MAD 10,000,000 – MAD 40,000,000 | **3.5%** |
| Above MAD 40,000,000 | **5%** |

- Applicable for 2022–2025 fiscal years; **2025 Finance Law extended to 2026, 2027 and 2028 fiscal years** (EY p. 1045)
- Must file declaration by 1 June each year

## 2026 Changes

- **EY chapter updated for 2026 Finance Law** (EY p. 1038)
- **Dividend withholding rate reduction (EY p. 1041):** From fiscal years beginning on/after 1 January 2026, dividend withholding rate reduces to **11.25%** (from 12.50%); further reduction to 10% from January 2027.
- **New hire salary exemption extended (EY p. 1039):** The 36-month exemption for newly hired employees was extended until 31 December 2026 by the 2023 Finance Law.
- **Social Contribution to Solidarity extended (EY p. 1045):** Extended from 2025 to cover fiscal years 2026, 2027, and 2028 by the 2025 Finance Law.
- **Interprofessional Pension Fund exemption (EY p. 1043):** The 2026 Finance Law grants exemption for retirement pensions and life annuities paid to private-sector retirees under group supplementary retirement insurance contracts by the Moroccan Interprofessional Pension Fund (*Caisse Interprofessionnelle Marocaine de Retraite*).
- **Online gaming (EY p. 1043):** The 2025 Finance Law provides that foreign online game of chance gains are subject to 30% withholding tax.
- **Rental income (EY p. 1043):** From 2025, option to tax gross property income of MAD 120,000+ at 20% (previously always 15%).

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Morocco uses MAD (Moroccan Dirham). **1 EUR ≈ MAD 10.8** (approximate; verify at bkam.ma).
> Employment: 35% professional expense deduction applied (income ≤ MAD 78,000 at €30k level; 25% at higher levels); social security deducted from gross before PIT calculation.
> Employee social security: illness/disability 4.29% (capped MAD 6,000/month) + loss-of-employment 0.19% (capped) + AMO 2.26% = ~6.74%.

### €30,000 Employment
- Gross MAD: EUR 30,000 × 10.8 = MAD 324,000
- Social security (employee): illness/disability 4.29% capped at MAD 6,000/month = MAD 72,000/year cap; at MAD 324,000 annual: 4.29% × 324,000 = MAD 13,900 (below cap); loss-of-employment: 0.19% × 72,000 = MAD 137 (capped at MAD 6,000/month); AMO: 2.26% × 324,000 = MAD 7,322; Total social = ~MAD 21,359
- Gross after social security: MAD 324,000 − MAD 21,359 = MAD 302,641
- Professional expense deduction: 25% of MAD 302,641 = MAD 75,660 (capped at MAD 35,000); apply cap → deduct MAD 35,000
- Taxable income: MAD 302,641 − MAD 35,000 = MAD 267,641
- PIT:
  - 0% on MAD 40,000 = 0
  - 10% on MAD 20,000 = MAD 2,000
  - 20% on MAD 20,000 = MAD 4,000
  - 30% on MAD 20,000 = MAD 6,000
  - 34% on MAD 80,000 = MAD 27,200
  - 37% on MAD 87,641 = MAD 32,427
  - Total PIT: MAD 71,627
- Total burden: MAD 71,627 + MAD 21,359 = MAD 92,986
- Effective rate: 92,986 / 324,000 = **28.7%**

Note: At €30,000 the professional expense deduction cap of MAD 35,000 applies (25% of taxable after social sec.). The 35% deduction applies to income ≤ MAD 78,000 gross annual, but threshold applies to gross taxable income, not net after social sec. — EY text is somewhat ambiguous on exact interaction.

- At €30,000 (MAD 324,000 services revenue): exceeds MAD 200,000 limit → auto-entrepreneur NOT available; CPU regime or RNS/RNR applies
- Corrected: At €30,000, use CPU (10%): MAD 324,000 × 10% = MAD 32,400 + complementary tax
- Best self-employment scenario at €30k: CPU at 10% on turnover ≈ MAD 32,400 + ~MAD 15,000 complementary = ~MAD 47,400 → effective rate ~14.6%

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/MAD = 10.8 (approximate; verify at bkam.ma)
- Social security deductible from gross income for PIT
- Professional expense deduction applied (25%, capped MAD 35,000)
- Auto-entrepreneur regime limited to MAD 200,000 services turnover / MAD 500,000 commercial
- CPU 10% on gross turnover (no expense deduction); complementary taxes added
- Social Contribution to Solidarity not applicable below MAD 1 million net income

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Professional expense deduction exact interaction:** EY states 25% deduction for income > MAD 78,000 (max MAD 35,000) and 35% for income ≤ MAD 78,000. Interaction with post-social-security income (whether the MAD 78,000 threshold applies to gross salary or net after social security deductions) needs clarification.
- [ ] **CPU complementary tax exact amounts:** The EY table shows quarterly and annual surplus tax amounts based on income tax due; total tax under CPU = 10% × turnover + complementary surplus.
- [ ] **Auto-entrepreneur CNSS contribution rates:** EY states auto-entrepreneurs must register with CNSS but does not specify the contribution rate or fixed amount. Verify with CNSS.
- [ ] **EUR/MAD exchange rate:** MAD loosely pegged to a EUR/USD basket; verify current rate at Bank Al-Maghrib (bkam.ma).
- [ ] **Social Contribution to Solidarity 2026–2028:** Extended by 2025 Finance Law; applies to net income ≥ MAD 1 million. Affects high-income individuals.
- [ ] **Rental income option (20%):** From 2025, rental income of any amount may optionally be taxed at 20%; details of election procedure not specified.
