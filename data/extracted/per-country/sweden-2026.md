## Personal Income Tax

> Sweden levies both local (municipal/county) income tax and a national (statlig) income tax. Municipal tax is a flat percentage varying by municipality (29%–36%); national tax is 20% on income above a threshold. Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income; married persons assessed separately.

**Local (municipal) income tax (2025):** Flat rate 29%–36% varying by municipality; average approximately 32.37%

**National income tax (2025):** 20% flat rate on employment income exceeding SEK 643,100 (before personal deduction) per year

**Effective combined rates (2025):**
- Income up to SEK 643,100: local tax only (~32.37% average)
- Income above SEK 643,100: local tax + 20% national = ~52.37% marginal rate at average municipal rate

**Personal deduction (grundavdrag, 2025):** SEK 17,300 – SEK 45,300 range; for income up to SEK 24,800 no tax payable (full deduction); deduction phases in and then phases out at higher income

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal deduction (grundavdrag): SEK 17,300–45,300 (income-dependent); above SEK 24,800, deduction starts; phases out at higher incomes
- Interest expense deduction: 30% of interest expenses up to SEK 100,000 (21% above SEK 100,000); from 2025 only 50% of interest expenses not connected to a specific security deductible; **from 2026: only interest paid on loans connected to a specific security (e.g., mortgage) is deductible** (EY p. 1471)
- Travel deduction (home-work): excess above SEK 11,000 deductible; SEK 2.50/km for private car or public transport costs
- Household/housekeeping services (RUT): tax credit 50% of actual costs, max SEK 75,000/year per person (combined RUT + ROT max SEK 75,000)
- Maintenance/repair on primary/secondary residence (ROT): tax credit 30% of actual labor costs, max SEK 50,000/year; temporarily 50% from 12 May 2025–31 December 2025 (EY p. 1472)
- Green technology installation: tax credit 15% (solar panels) or 50% (storage/EV charging) of labor and materials, max SEK 50,000/year

**Investment income:** Capital income (dividends, interest, capital gains) taxed at flat 30%; real estate capital gains: 22% (22/30 × 30%); unlisted shares: 25% (25/30 × 30%)

**Notes:**
- Social security taxes paid by employer are not included in employee's taxable income
- A "pension contribution" minor amount passes through employee's tax return but is fully credited against tax paid
- Wealth tax: abolished from 1 January 2007 (EY p. 1473)
- Inheritance and gift taxes: abolished from December 2004 (EY p. 1473)
- Real estate fee: 0.75% of assessed value, capped at SEK 10,074/property (EY p. 1473)
- Nonresidents: flat 25% SINK (Särskild inkomstskatt för utomlands bosatta) withholding on employment income; no deductions; final tax

## Social Security

> Swedish employer social security contributions are paid exclusively by the employer at 31.42% (2025, general rate) on all salaries and benefits; employees pay nothing directly. Self-employed pay 28.97% on net taxable income from active business.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total (general, age <born 1959) | 0% | 31.42% | 28.97% (active business) |
| Total (born 1939–1958) | 0% | 10.21% | 10.21% |
| Total (born 1938 or earlier) | 0% | 0% | 0% |
| Passive business income | 0% | 0% | 24.26% |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling — contributions on all salary/net profit (EY p. 1473)
**Notes:**
- Employer pays 31.42% of gross salary (2025); no employee contribution required
- This means the true employer cost of a SEK 100 gross salary = SEK 131.42
- Self-employed: 28.97% on net taxable profit from active business (the taxable income itself, not gross revenue); lower rates for certain age groups; passive business income 24.26%
- Employee social security (pension contribution): small amount passed through employee's tax return but 100% credited against income tax — effectively zero net cost to employee
- Employer contributions deductible for CIT purposes

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals are taxed as "active business income" subject to local + national income tax and 28.97% self-employed social security contributions.

### Option A — Sole Trader (Enskild Firma)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on business activity in Sweden under their own name
- **Tax base:** Net business income (revenue minus all deductible business expenses); business income treated as "earned income" and taxed at local + national income tax rates; personal deduction applies
- **Rate / mechanism:** Local tax (average ~32.37%) + national tax 20% on income above SEK 643,100; self-employed SS 28.97% deductible in the same tax year
- **Social security:** 28.97% on net taxable business income; deductible against income tax base
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above SEK 30,000 annual turnover; standard VAT 25%; reduced rates 12% and 6%
- **Key limits:** Losses carried forward indefinitely; F-skatt (F-tax) registration required; quarterly preliminary tax payments; income splitting with spouse not permitted unless both work in the business

### Option B — Handelsbolag / Kommanditbolag (Partnership)
- **Who qualifies:** Two or more persons; income allocated to partners as active business income
- **Tax base:** Partner's share of partnership profit; taxed as earned income at progressive local + national rates; SS 28.97% on active share
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same as sole trader
- **Social security:** Each partner pays 28.97% SS on active income share
- **VAT obligations:** Standard 25%
- **Key limits:** Partners jointly and severally liable for partnership debts (HB); KS (limited partnership) provides limited liability for limited partners

### Option C — Limited Company (Aktiebolag / AB)
- **Who qualifies:** Any entrepreneur
- **Tax base:** Company profits at 20.6% CIT; salary to owner at progressive local + national income tax; dividends (from private company, 3:12 rules): 30% tax if below "dividend threshold" calculated from shares
- **Rate / mechanism:** 3:12 rules for closely held companies: annual dividend threshold (schablonbelopp) = SEK 209,550 (2025) or asset-based formula; dividends within threshold taxed at 20% (flat special tax rate for private company dividends); excess dividends taxed as employment income (~52%)
- **Social security:** Employer SS 31.42% on salary; owner pays from company
- **VAT obligations:** Standard 25%
- **Key limits:** Minimum share capital SEK 25,000 for private AB; 3:12 rules complex; professional advice needed for dividend planning

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expert Tax Regime (Expertskatten / Utlandsstationering)
- **Eligibility (2025 rules):** Foreign experts, scientists, executives, or specialists assigned to work in Sweden; must not have been resident in Sweden during the 5 years preceding the calendar year the assignment starts; monthly remuneration ≥ SEK 88,201/month (2025, including monthly benefits) OR must qualify as an expert (skills scarce in Sweden); assignment period must not exceed 7 years; application to Expert Tax Board (Forskarskattenämnden) within 3 months of first day of assignment; remuneration must be paid by a Swedish company, branch, or permanent establishment
- **Benefit (2025):** 25% of gross salary and benefits exempt from Swedish income tax and Swedish social security contributions; also exempt: moving expenses, two annual return trips to home country, children's school fees; effective marginal rate significantly reduced
- **Duration:** Up to 7 years (tax relief granted only for qualifying years)
- **Application deadline:** Application to Expert Tax Board within 3 months of first working day in Sweden
- **2026 proposed change (subject to approval):** Increase exemption from 25% to 30%; extend application period from 3 to 6 months; expand eligibility criteria (EY p. 1469)
- **Caveats:** Must meet minimum salary OR expert qualification criteria; Swedish employer must apply; if assignment ends early, relief is lost from that date; 5-year non-residency condition strictly applied

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 1467–1479)

- **Interest deduction restricted from 2026:** From 1 January 2026, only interest on loans connected to a specific security (e.g., mortgage on real estate) is deductible; general interest on unsecured loans no longer deductible (EY p. 1471)
- **Expert tax regime proposal:** Exemption percentage expected to increase from 25% to 30%; application period to extend from 3 to 6 months; eligibility criteria to be widened — expected to be approved and effective from January 2026 (EY p. 1469)
- **ROT credit temporarily increased to 50%** (from 30%) from 12 May 2025 to 31 December 2025 — reverts to 30% from 1 January 2026
- **Work permit minimum salary**: SEK 29,680/month from June 2025 (80% of median salary); updated annually in June
- EU Blue Card: new rules from 1 January 2025 (lower salary requirement at 1.25× average)
- Municipal tax rates for 2026 not yet published — approximate average 32.37% (2025)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, Swedish resident, average municipal tax 32.37%.
> Currency: SEK. 1 EUR ≈ 11.3 SEK (approximate 2025 rate). EUR amounts are approximate conversions.

**Employment at €30,000 (≈ SEK 339,000):**
1. No employer SS in employee burden; employee SS = 0%
2. Personal deduction: SEK 339,000 < SEK 643,100 → no national tax; personal deduction ~SEK 36,000 (income-dependent; assumed mid-range)
3. Taxable = SEK 339,000 − SEK 36,000 = SEK 303,000
4. Local tax: SEK 303,000 × 32.37% = SEK 98,082 ≈ €8,679
5. Total burden = €8,679 (no employee SS)
6. Effective rate = €8,679 / €30,000 = **28.9%**

**Employment at €60,000 (≈ SEK 678,000):**
1. SEK 678,000 > SEK 643,100 by SEK 34,900 → national tax on excess: SEK 34,900 × 20% = SEK 6,980
2. Personal deduction ~SEK 17,300 (phases out at higher income; approx. lower end used)
3. Taxable for local = SEK 678,000 − SEK 17,300 = SEK 660,700
4. Local tax: SEK 660,700 × 32.37% = SEK 213,847 ≈ €18,925
5. National tax: SEK 6,980 ≈ €618
6. Total burden = €18,925 + €618 = €19,543
7. Effective rate = €19,543 / €60,000 = **32.6%**

**Employment at €100,000 (≈ SEK 1,130,000):**
1. National tax: (SEK 1,130,000 − SEK 643,100) × 20% = SEK 486,900 × 20% = SEK 97,380
2. Personal deduction ~SEK 17,300
3. Local taxable = SEK 1,130,000 − SEK 17,300 = SEK 1,112,700
4. Local tax: SEK 1,112,700 × 32.37% = SEK 360,282 ≈ €31,883
5. National tax: SEK 97,380 ≈ €8,617
6. Total burden = €31,883 + €8,617 = €40,500
7. Effective rate = €40,500 / €100,000 = **40.5%**

**B2B (sole trader, enskild firma) at €60,000 (≈ SEK 678,000 revenue):**
1. Business costs ~15%: SEK 678,000 × 15% = SEK 101,700; net = SEK 576,300
2. Self-employed SS: SEK 576,300 × 28.97% = SEK 166,961 ≈ €14,775; deductible from income
3. Taxable = SEK 576,300 − SEK 166,961 − SEK 17,300 = SEK 392,039
4. Local tax: SEK 392,039 × 32.37% = SEK 126,883 ≈ €11,228 (below national tax threshold)
5. Total burden = €11,228 + €14,775 = €26,003
6. Effective rate = €26,003 / €60,000 = **43.3%**

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (self) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €8,679 | €0 | €8,679 | **28.9%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€3,800 | ~€5,800 | ~€9,600 | **~32.0%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €19,543 | €0 | €19,543 | **32.6%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | ~€11,228 | ~€14,775 | ~€26,003 | **~43.3%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €40,500 | €0 | €40,500 | **40.5%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€28,000 | ~€24,000 | ~€52,000 | **~52.0%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants, Swedish resident
- Average municipal tax 32.37% (varies 29%–36% by municipality)
- Employee SS: 0% (paid entirely by employer at 31.42%, not included in employee burden above)
- National tax: 20% on employment income exceeding SEK 643,100
- Personal deduction applied (SEK 17,300–45,300 depending on income)
- B2B: sole trader; 28.97% self-employed SS (deductible); 15% business costs assumed
- From 2026: interest deduction restricted to mortgage interest only — affects homeowners
- EUR/SEK ≈ 11.3 (approximate; SEK not EUR-pegged)
- Expert tax regime not modelled (standard taxation assumed)
