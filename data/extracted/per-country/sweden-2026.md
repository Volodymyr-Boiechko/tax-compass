## Personal Income Tax

Residents are subject to Swedish taxes on their worldwide income. Nonresident individuals are taxed on salary earned from work performed in Sweden, on certain pensions, and on other income sourced in Sweden.

**Residency:** Individuals present in Sweden for six months or more who regularly stay overnight are generally considered resident for tax purposes. (EY p.1467)

Tax year = calendar year. Annual tax returns filed by 2 May of the following year. Married persons are taxed separately on all types of income. (EY p.1474)

### PIT Structure (2025)

Sweden has two separate layers of income tax:

**1. Municipal income tax (kommunalskatt):** Levied on employment income at rates ranging from 29% to 36% depending on municipality. The average rate is approximately 32%.

**2. National income tax (statlig inkomstskatt):** A flat rate of 20% levied on employment income (and self-employment income) above SEK643,100 (before the personal deduction). Applied in addition to local tax.

**Effective combined top marginal rate:**
- Employment income below SEK643,100: local tax only (~29–36%, averaging ~32%)
- Employment income above SEK643,100: local tax (~32%) + national tax (20%) = ~52%

(EY pp.1471–1472)

**Personal deduction (grundavdrag, 2025):** Basic deduction ranges from SEK17,300 to SEK45,300. Below income level SEK24,800, no tax is payable (effectively zero for very low earners). The deduction is subject to proration for part-year residents. (EY p.1471)

### Employment Income Tax Rate Structure (2025, approximate)

| Annual employment income (SEK) | Combined rate (local + national) |
|---|---|
| Up to ~SEK24,800 | 0% (personal deduction; no tax payable) |
| ~SEK24,800 – SEK643,100 | ~29–36% (local tax only; ~32% average) |
| Above SEK643,100 | ~49–56% (local + 20% national; ~52% average) |

### Capital Gains and Investment Income (Flat 30%)

Dividend income from listed Swedish and foreign shares, net interest income, and income from rental activities are taxed as income from capital at a flat 30% rate. (EY p.1469)

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (listed shares) | 30% |
| Interest income | 30% |
| Rental income | 30% |
| Capital gains (listed shares) | 30% |
| Capital gains (unlisted shares) | 25% |
| Capital gains (primary residence disposal — 22/30 rule) | 22% (= 2/3 of 30%) |
| Dividends from unlisted companies to controlling shareholders (FINA rules) | Progressive rates may apply |

Capital losses on listed shares: offsettable against capital gains. Up to 30% of excess capital losses may be credited against taxes on employment income. (EY p.1470)

### Deductions (2025)

| Deduction | Amount/Condition |
|---|---|
| Basic (personal) deduction | SEK17,300–SEK45,300 |
| Interest expenses (on specific securities/home) | 30% tax credit up to SEK100,000; 21% above; from 2026: only interest on loans connected to a specific security (home/securities) deductible |
| Travel between home and work exceeding SEK11,000 | Deductible; SEK2.50/km by private car; SEK2.50/km for employer's business |
| Alimony to former spouse | Deductible |
| Social security contributions in other EU/EEA/Switzerland | Deductible |
| Household and housekeeping services (RUT credit) | Tax credit = 50% of actual costs; max SEK75,000/year per person (credit, not deduction) |
| Maintenance/repair of own home (ROT credit) | Tax credit = 30% (temporarily 50% from 12 May – 31 Dec 2025); max SEK50,000; both RUT+ROT combined max SEK75,000 |
| Green technology installations (solar, EV chargers) | Tax credit = 15–50% of labor+materials; max SEK50,000 |

Business deductions: All expenses included in financial accounts and incurred to obtain, secure, and maintain business income are deductible (with exceptions for penalties, fines, art, entertainment, wine/liquor). (EY p.1471)

**Self-employed social security deductions:** Social security taxes for self-employed individuals are deductible in the same year they accrue (at 28.97% for active business income; 24.26% for passive business income). (EY p.1471)

## Social Security

### Employer Contributions (2025)

Social security taxes are levied on salaries, wages, and assessed value of benefits in kind; paid primarily by the employer. Administered by a single authority. (EY p.1473)

| Employer rate | Applies to |
|---|---|
| **31.42%** | Standard rate for most employers (2025) |
| 10.21% | Individuals born 1939–1958 (both years inclusive) |
| 0% | Individuals born 1937 or earlier |

### Employee Contributions

Social security is NOT payable by employees in Sweden — employer pays all social security contributions. The employee pays a minor pension insurance contribution through the tax return, but this is normally fully creditable against the tax paid. (EY p.1473)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| All social security programs | ~0% (minor pension credited back) | **31.42%** | **28.97%** (active) |
| — passive business | — | — | **24.26%** |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling specified for social security contributions; apply on full salary/profit.

### Self-Employed Social Security (Egenavgifter)

Self-employed individuals pay egenavgifter (self-employed social security contributions) on their net taxable profit. (EY p.1473)

- **Active business income:** 28.97% for 2025 (individuals born 1939 or later)
- **Passive business income:** 24.26% (regardless of age)
- Reduced rates: 10.21% for those born 1939–1958; 0% for those born 1937 or earlier
- SS contributions are fully deductible from taxable income in the same year they accrue

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Sole Trader / Enskild Firma)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registering as a sole trader (enskild firma) in Sweden
- **Tax base:** Net business income = gross revenue minus business expenses; taxable income computed under sound accounting rules
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive municipal tax (~32%) + national tax 20% on income above SEK643,100; same rates as employment income
- **Social security:** Egenavgifter at 28.97% on net profit (active); these are deductible, reducing taxable income — effective rate on gross lower than headline rate
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration mandatory when turnover exceeds SEK120,000/year (threshold as of 2024); standard VAT rate 25%; reduced rates 12% and 6%
- **Key limits:** Losses carried forward indefinitely; no carryback for earned income losses

### Option B — Aktiebolag (AB — Limited Company)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals incorporating a Swedish limited company (AB); minimum share capital SEK25,000 (private AB)
- **Tax base / rate:** Company pays corporate income tax at 20.6% on net profit; owner pays wages (subject to PIT) and/or dividends (subject to 30% capital tax on qualified dividends under 3:12 rules)
- **3:12 rules (fåmansbolag):** For owners of closely held companies: a portion of dividends taxed at a reduced 20% rate (flat income from capital) up to the annual threshold (2025: ~SEK209,550); excess dividends taxed as employment income at progressive rates
- **Self-employed (AB) SS:** Owner draws salary subject to employer SS (31.42%); dividends within 3:12 threshold subject only to 30% capital tax (no SS); significant planning opportunity
- **Key limits:** AB requires annual accounting; 3:12 rules are complex; recommend Swedish tax advisor

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expert Tax Regime (Expertskatt / Foreign Key Personnel — SINK Variant)

- **Eligibility (current rules):**
  - Foreign experts, scientists, or senior executives with knowledge and skills scarce in Sweden
  - Assignment period must not exceed seven years
  - Individual must NOT have resided in Sweden at any time during the five years preceding the calendar year when the assignment starts
  - Monthly remuneration ≥ SEK88,201 (including monthly benefits) for 2025 calendar year, OR qualified as an expert
  - Remuneration must be paid by a Swedish company, branch, or permanent establishment
  - Application filed with Expert Tax Board (Forskarskattenämnden) within 3 months of beginning of assignment
- **Benefit:** 25% of gross salary and benefits is exempt from Swedish income tax and social security contributions (EY p.1469)
  - Moving expenses to/from Sweden: exempt
  - Travel expenses (2 return tickets to home country per year for individual and family): exempt
  - Children's school fees: exempt
- **Duration:** 7 years maximum
- **Proposed 2026 change:** A proposal (expected to be approved, effective January 2026) would:
  - Increase the exemption from 25% to 30% of gross salary
  - Extend the application period from 3 months to 6 months after start of assignment
  - Broaden eligibility criteria for who may qualify (EY p.1469)

### SINK (Special Income Tax for Non-Residents)

- **Eligibility:** Nonresident individuals receiving Swedish-source income
- **Rate:** 25% flat withholding tax on gross salary from Swedish-source employment (no deductions allowed)
- **Alternative:** Nonresident may elect to be taxed under ordinary progressive rules with deductions

## 2026 Changes

- **Expert tax regime proposed expansion (effective January 2026):** Exemption percentage increases from 25% to 30% of salary; application window extended from 3 to 6 months; broader eligibility (EY p.1469) — pending final government approval
- **Interest deductibility change (from 2026):** Only interest paid on loans connected to a specific security (home or securities) is deductible; general consumer loan interest will no longer qualify (EY p.1471)
- **Work permit minimum salary (June 2025):** SEK29,680/month as of June 2025; updated annually in June
- **EU Blue Card rules (1 January 2025):** Lower salary requirement (1.25× average Swedish salary); easier role changes for Blue Card holders
- No changes to PIT bracket structure or employer SS rates announced for 2026

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants. Personal deduction ~SEK17,300 at highest incomes (tapers down at lower incomes). 32% average municipal rate used.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employer SS 31.42% not shown in employee rate).
> "SE enskild firma" = gross revenue; 30% assumed business expenses; egenavgifter 28.97% on net profit deductible before PIT.
> Currency: SEK (Swedish Krona). 1 EUR ≈ SEK11.50 (approximate).

### Working calculations:

**€30,000 Employment (≈ SEK345,000):**
- All income below SEK643,100 → local tax only (~32%)
- Taxable income after personal deduction: 345,000 − 17,300 = SEK327,700
- PIT: 32% × 327,700 = SEK104,864
- Employee SS: ~0% (employer pays)
- Total: SEK104,864; effective = 30.4%

**€30,000 SE Enskild Firma (30% exp → net SEK241,500):**
- Egenavgifter: 28.97% × 241,500 = SEK69,962 (deductible)
- Taxable income: 241,500 − 69,962 − 17,300 = SEK154,238
- PIT: 32% × 154,238 = SEK49,356
- Total: 49,356 + 69,962 = SEK119,318; effective on gross = 119,318 / 345,000 = **34.6%**

**Note on SE vs. employment at €30k:** Self-employment carries higher burden at lower income due to egenavgifter. Employment is effectively ~30% (all municipal); self-employment ~35% (municipal + egenavgifter, even though SS deductible).

**€60,000 Employment (≈ SEK690,000):**
- Below threshold SEK643,100: 32% on 643,100−17,300 = SEK625,800 → 32% × 625,800 = SEK200,256
- Above threshold: 52% × (690,000−643,100) = 52% × 46,900 = SEK24,388
- Total PIT: SEK224,644; effective = 32.6%

**€60,000 SE Enskild Firma (30% exp → net SEK483,000):**
- Egenavgifter: 28.97% × 483,000 = SEK139,925 (deductible)
- Taxable: 483,000 − 139,925 − 17,300 = SEK325,775
- PIT: 32% × 325,775 = SEK104,248
- Total: 104,248 + 139,925 = SEK244,173; effective on gross = 35.4%

**€100,000 Employment (≈ SEK1,150,000):**
- Below threshold SEK643,100: 32% × (643,100−17,300) = 32% × 625,800 = SEK200,256
- Above threshold: 52% × (1,150,000−643,100) = 52% × 506,900 = SEK263,588
- Total PIT: SEK463,844; effective = 40.3%

**€100,000 SE Enskild Firma (30% exp → net SEK805,000):**
- Egenavgifter: 28.97% × 805,000 = SEK233,209 (deductible)
- Taxable: 805,000 − 233,209 − 17,300 = SEK554,491
- PIT (all below 643,100): 32% × 554,491 = SEK177,437
- Total: 177,437 + 233,209 = SEK410,646; effective on gross = 35.7%

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SEK345,000 (€30k) | Employment | SEK104,864 | ~0% (employer-side) | SEK104,864 | **30.4%** |
| SEK345,000 (€30k) | SE enskild firma (30% exp.) | SEK49,356 | SEK69,962 (egenv. 28.97%) | SEK119,318 | **34.6%** |
| SEK690,000 (€60k) | Employment | SEK224,644 | ~0% (employer-side) | SEK224,644 | **32.6%** |
| SEK690,000 (€60k) | SE enskild firma (30% exp.) | SEK104,248 | SEK139,925 (egenv. 28.97%) | SEK244,173 | **35.4%** |
| SEK1,150,000 (€100k) | Employment | SEK463,844 | ~0% (employer-side) | SEK463,844 | **40.3%** |
| SEK1,150,000 (€100k) | SE enskild firma (30% exp.) | SEK177,437 | SEK233,209 (egenv. 28.97%) | SEK410,646 | **35.7%** |

**Expert tax regime (indicative):** Under the expert tax regime, 25% of gross salary is exempt. For an employee at €100k (SEK1,150,000): taxable salary = 75% × 1,150,000 = SEK862,500. PIT below threshold: 32% × (643,100−17,300) = 200,256; above: 52% × (862,500−643,100) = 52% × 219,400 = 114,088; total PIT = SEK314,344; effective = 27.3%. Significant saving vs. standard employment (40.3%).

**Note on AB structure:** A sole owner running a Swedish AB company can draw salary within the 3:12 threshold and take the rest as dividends at 20% (reduced rate) rather than progressive rates. This often results in an effective total tax rate of 35–40% including corporate tax (20.6%) + dividend tax (20% on qualifying dividends), which is more favorable than high self-employment income. Requires professional Swedish tax planning.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/SEK = 11.50 (approximate)
- Municipal tax rate: 32% (average; ranges 29–36%)
- Personal deduction: SEK17,300 at higher incomes
- National tax: 20% on income above SEK643,100
- Employee SS: 0% for employee (employer pays 31.42%)
- Self-employed egenavgifter: 28.97% on net profit (active business); deductible
- B2B/SE: 30% assumed deductible expenses
- No RUT/ROT tax credits applied
- Expert tax regime not applied to base scenarios
