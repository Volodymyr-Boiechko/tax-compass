## Personal Income Tax

Residents are taxable on their worldwide income. Nonresidents are taxable only on income derived from certain Suriname sources and are generally subject to personal income tax from their first day in Suriname. Residence is determined based on applicable facts and circumstances: domicile (permanent home), physical presence, and location of vital personal and economic interests. (EY p.1460)

A resident individual who receives income from former or current employment is, in principle, subject to income tax in Suriname. Wage tax is withheld from employment earnings. For employees whose wage tax has been withheld by the employer, no personal income tax return is required unless the individual has other sources of income totaling more than SRD7,500/year in addition to the salary (or if receiving wages from more than one employer for periods longer than one month). (EY pp.1460–1461)

Tax year = calendar year. Preliminary income tax return filed by 15 April of current fiscal year or within 2.5 months after start. Tax payable in four quarterly installments due 15 April, 15 July, 15 October, and 31 December. Final return filed within four months after end of fiscal year. (EY pp.1464–1465)

**Income subject to tax:**
- Employment income (wages, director's fees, supervisory director's fees, benefits, allowances less standardized deductions)
- Self-employment and business income
- Income from immovable property (rental)
- Income from movable assets (dividends, interest)
- Periodic allowances (pensions, annuities, disability allowances, if payable for lifetime)

Note: Resident individuals benefit from the tax-free amount (SRD108,000/year); nonresidents do not. (EY p.1463)

### Capital Gains

Capital gains are generally exempt from tax. Capital losses are nondeductible. Exceptions — residents may be subject to income tax on capital gains in the following circumstances:
- Capital gains realized on disposal of business assets or other assets qualified as income from independently performed activities: up to 38%
- Capital gains on repurchase of company shares in excess of average paid-up capital: up to 38%
- Capital gains on liquidation of a business: up to 38% (but on request, 25% rate applies) (EY p.1462)

### Dividends

A 25% dividend withholding tax applies to dividends distributed by Suriname-established companies. (EY p.1462)

### Deductions (Personal)

| Deduction | Amount/Condition |
|---|---|
| Mortgage interest on primary residence | Up to SRD871,500 of principal debt; interest payments only |
| Exterior paintwork of dwelling | Deductible; once every three years |
| Pension, annuity, and periodic allowance contributions | Deductible under certain conditions |
| Life insurance premiums (annuity/pension type) | Up to 10% of income |
| Alimony | Deductible if meeting threshold |
| Medical expenses, educational expenses, support for relatives | Up to second-degree relatives; threshold applies |
| Disabled child allowance | Up to SRD8,000 |
| Acquisition costs (employment income) | 4% of wage; max SRD4,800; if specified expenses paid |
| Devaluation compensation allowance | Tax-free up to SRD800; salary increase condition applies (from 1 September 2021) |

**Business deductions:** In general, all business expenses incurred in accordance with sound business practices are fully deductible (with certain exceptions). (EY p.1462)

**Losses:** Carried forward for seven years; no carryback. Losses incurred during the first three years of a business may be carried forward indefinitely. (EY p.1463)

## Social Security

### Old-Age Insurance (AOV)

Annual old-age insurance contribution: 4% of employment income. (EY p.1464)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Old-age insurance (AOV) | 4% | — | 4% |
| Basic illness insurance (ZV) | 0.5% (employee min.) | Employer covers remaining ≥50% | — |
| Pension insurance | 0.5% (employee min.) | Employer covers remaining ≥50% | — |
| Parental Leave Fund | 0.5% (employee max.) | ≥0.5% (min. employer) | — |
| **Total AOV (minimum)** | **4%** | **~50%+ of ZV/pension premiums** | **4%** |

**Details:**
- **Old-age insurance (AOV):** 4% of employment income; paid by employee (EY p.1464)
- **Basic illness insurance law (ZV):** Took effect 9 October 2014; employer obligation to provide insurance coverage; employee contributes minimum 0.5%; employer covers at least 50% of premiums
- **Pension law:** Took effect 9 December 2014; employer must arrange pension scheme participation; employee contributes minimum 0.5%; employer covers at least 50% of premiums
- **Family Labor Protection Law (Parental Leave):** Monthly contribution of 1% of employee's gross salary to Parental Leave Fund; employee maximum 0.5%; employer minimum 0.5%

**Income ceiling:** Not specified for AOV.

**Wealth tax:** 3% on net wealth exceeding SRD100,000 (unmarried) or SRD120,000 (married). Applies to resident individuals; nonresidents subject to wealth tax on immovable property in Suriname, mortgaged debt obligations secured on Suriname property, and certain partnership interests. (EY pp.1463–1464)

**Notes:**
- The old-age insurance contribution is withheld by employer and declared in wage tax returns
- Self-employed individuals are subject to the AOV contribution; details on ZV/pension for self-employed not specified

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Net Income Basis)

- **Who qualifies:** Residents carrying on a trade, business, or profession in Suriname
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses in accordance with sound business practices)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 0–38% applied to net taxable income after tax-free amount (SRD108,000 for residents)
- **Social security:** AOV at 4% of income; ZV and pension: self-employed obligation unclear (likely voluntary or not covered)
- **VAT obligations:** Suriname has no VAT; turnover tax (OB — Omzetbelasting) may apply (not detailed in EY individual section)
- **Key limits:** Losses carried forward 7 years (indefinitely for first 3 years); business expenses must accord with sound business practices

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Dedicated Newcomer PIT Regime

Suriname does not have a remittance-basis or new-resident preferential PIT regime. Nonresidents are subject to personal income tax from their first day in Suriname on Suriname-sourced income.

### CARICOM Skilled Workers

Skilled nationals of CARICOM member states with a CARICOM Skilled Nationals Certificate are exempt from work permit requirements and have the right of free stay for up to six months. (EY p.1466)

### Double Tax Treaties

Suriname has tax treaties with Indonesia and the Netherlands. A new treaty with Curaçao was signed 1 July 2024; becomes effective once both countries complete ratification formalities. The Curaçao treaty will cover personal income tax, wage tax, dividend withholding tax, and wealth tax. (EY p.1465)

- PIT rates as of 1 January 2024 apply (no changes announced for 2026 as of October 2025)
- Tax-free amount: SRD108,000/year for residents; no changes announced

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, tax-free amount SRD108,000 applied.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side; employer costs not shown).
> "B2B" = gross revenue; 30% assumed business expenses.
> Currency: SRD (Surinamese Dollar). 1 EUR ≈ SRD39 (approximate; SRD is volatile — verify current rate).
> Employee SS: AOV 4% on gross + ZV/pension minimum 0.5% each = ~5% approximate.

### Working calculations:

**€30,000 Employment (≈ SRD1,170,000):**
- Taxable: 1,170,000 − 108,000 = SRD1,062,000
- PIT: 38% × (1,062,000 − 234,000) + (8%×42,000 + 18%×42,000 + 28%×42,000) = let's compute from brackets:
  - 0–108,000: 0 (covered by exemption; taxable starts at 108k)
  - 108,000–150,000 (42,000): 8% × 42,000 = 3,360
  - 150,000–192,000 (42,000): 18% × 42,000 = 7,560
  - 192,000–234,000 (42,000): 28% × 42,000 = 11,760
  - 234,000–1,170,000 (936,000): 38% × 936,000 = 355,680
  - Total PIT = 3,360 + 7,560 + 11,760 + 355,680 = SRD378,360
- SS: 5% × 1,170,000 = SRD58,500
- Total: 378,360 + 58,500 = SRD436,860; effective rate = 37.3%

**€30,000 B2B (30% expenses → net SRD819,000):**
- Taxable: 819,000 − 108,000 = SRD711,000
- PIT: 3,360 + 7,560 + 11,760 + 38% × (711,000 − 234,000) = 22,680 + 38% × 477,000 = 22,680 + 181,260 = SRD203,940
- SS: 4% × 819,000 = SRD32,760
- Total: 203,940 + 32,760 = SRD236,700; effective on gross = 236,700 / 1,170,000 = **20.2%**

**€60,000 Employment (≈ SRD2,340,000):**
- Taxable: 2,340,000 − 108,000 = SRD2,232,000
- PIT: 22,680 + 38% × (2,232,000 − 234,000) = 22,680 + 38% × 1,998,000 = 22,680 + 759,240 = SRD781,920
- SS: 5% × 2,340,000 = SRD117,000
- Total: 898,920; effective = 38.4%

**€60,000 B2B (30% expenses → net SRD1,638,000):**
- Taxable: 1,638,000 − 108,000 = SRD1,530,000
- PIT: 22,680 + 38% × (1,530,000 − 234,000) = 22,680 + 38% × 1,296,000 = 22,680 + 492,480 = SRD515,160
- SS: 4% × 1,638,000 = SRD65,520
- Total: SRD580,680; effective on gross = 24.8%

**€100,000 Employment (≈ SRD3,900,000):**
- Taxable: 3,900,000 − 108,000 = SRD3,792,000
- PIT: 22,680 + 38% × (3,792,000 − 234,000) = 22,680 + 38% × 3,558,000 = 22,680 + 1,352,040 = SRD1,374,720
- SS: 5% × 3,900,000 = SRD195,000
- Total: SRD1,569,720; effective = 40.2%

**€100,000 B2B (30% expenses → net SRD2,730,000):**
- Taxable: 2,730,000 − 108,000 = SRD2,622,000
- PIT: 22,680 + 38% × (2,622,000 − 234,000) = 22,680 + 38% × 2,388,000 = 22,680 + 907,440 = SRD930,120
- SS: 4% × 2,730,000 = SRD109,200
- Total: SRD1,039,320; effective on gross = 26.6%

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SRD1,170,000 (€30k) | Employment | SRD378,360 | SRD58,500 (~5%) | SRD436,860 | **37.3%** |
| SRD1,170,000 (€30k) | B2B (30% exp.) | SRD203,940 | SRD32,760 (AOV 4%) | SRD236,700 | **20.2%** |
| SRD2,340,000 (€60k) | Employment | SRD781,920 | SRD117,000 (~5%) | SRD898,920 | **38.4%** |
| SRD2,340,000 (€60k) | B2B (30% exp.) | SRD515,160 | SRD65,520 (AOV 4%) | SRD580,680 | **24.8%** |
| SRD3,900,000 (€100k) | Employment | SRD1,374,720 | SRD195,000 (~5%) | SRD1,569,720 | **40.2%** |

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/SRD = 39 (approximate; volatile — verify current rate; SRD has experienced significant devaluation)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Exchange rate volatility:** The SRD has experienced significant devaluation against the EUR/USD; any calculation is sensitive to the exchange rate — verify current rate at CBvS (Central Bank of Suriname)
- [ ] **Wealth tax practical application:** Wealth tax at 3% on net wealth > SRD100,000/120,000 applies in addition to income tax; for high-net-worth individuals this is significant; not modeled above
- [ ] **ZV and pension self-employed obligations:** EY specifies employer/employee obligations for ZV (illness) and pension insurance but does not clearly define self-employed contribution structure; verify at SVB (Sociale Verzekeringsbank Suriname)
- [ ] **Turnover tax (OB):** Suriname applies an Omzetbelasting (turnover/sales tax); rate and registration threshold not specified in EY individual section; verify at belastingdienst.sr
- [ ] **Dividend withholding interaction:** The 25% dividend withholding on company distributions affects business owners operating through a Suriname entity (NV/VBA); for IT entrepreneurs operating through a company structure, this is significant
- [ ] **Curaçao treaty ratification:** The July 2024 Curaçao-Suriname treaty pending ratification; once effective will cover Dutch Kingdom cross-border workers and affect Dutch nationals in Suriname
- [ ] **Currency denomination:** Tax brackets are in SRD; given high inflation in recent years, bracket values may be revised; verify current brackets at belastingdienst.sr
