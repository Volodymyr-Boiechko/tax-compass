## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PIT system. Tax year = calendar year. Employment income is withheld under PAYE within 7 days of month-end. Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents taxed on Tanzania-source income only at a flat 30% rate (previously 20%). Administered by the Tanzania Revenue Authority (TRA).

**Residency:** Present for 183+ days in the year; OR average 122+ days in current year and each of the two preceding years; OR permanent home in Tanzania and present for any time; OR government employee posted abroad.

Foreign non-citizens taxed on worldwide income after being residents for a total of two years during their lifetime.

### Resident Tax Rates — Monthly (EY p. 1521)

| Monthly taxable income (TZS) | Tax on lower amount (TZS) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 270,000 | 0 | 0% |
| 270,001 – 520,000 | 0 | 8% |
| 520,001 – 760,000 | 20,000 | 20% |
| 760,001 – 1,000,000 | 68,000 | 25% |
| Above 1,000,000 | 128,000 | 30% |

**Annual equivalents (multiply monthly brackets × 12):**

| Annual taxable income (TZS) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 3,240,000 | 0% |
| 3,240,001 – 6,240,000 | 8% |
| 6,240,001 – 9,120,000 | 20% |
| 9,120,001 – 12,000,000 | 25% |
| Above 12,000,000 | 30% |

**Non-resident rate:** 30% flat on all Tanzania-source income (EY p. 1521)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- No personal exemption announced (first TZS 270,000/month is a nil-rate band)
- Deductible expenses for employment income: only wholly and exclusively incurred in production of income (narrower standard than self-employment)
- Deductible expenses for self-employed: all expenses directly related to accrued business income, including cost of goods sold and administrative expenses

**Notes:**
- Non-residents taxed on employment income sourced in Tanzania at 15% withholding (EY p. 1519)
- Directors' fees (non-full-time directors): 15% withholding tax (final for both residents and non-residents)
- Self-employment and business income taxed at 30% (EY p. 1519)
- Capital gains from real property by individuals not engaged in business: 10% (residents), 30% (non-residents)
- Employer-provided housing: taxable at 15% of gross salary minus any rent paid by employee

## Social Security

Tanzania does not have a comprehensive social security system. Two main pension funds apply (EY p. 1522):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| NSSF (National Social Security Fund — private sector) | 10% | 10% | On monthly gross emoluments; no ceiling stated |
| PSSSF (Public Service Social Security Fund — public sector) | 5% | 15% | On basic salary |
| **Total (NSSF)** | **10%** | **10%** | |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling applies to social security contributions in Tanzania (EY p. 1522)

**Workers' Compensation Fund (WCF):** 0.5% employer-only, on basic salary plus fixed allowances (EY p. 1522)

**Skills Development Levy (SDL):** Employers with 10 or more employees pay 3.5% of gross emoluments, excluding benefits in kind (EY p. 1521). This is an employer-only obligation.

**Notes:**
- NSSF: Employee joins before age 40; membership ceases at age 60
- NSSF refund available if employee ceases contributions and meets conditions
- No employee-side unemployment insurance

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — General Self-Assessment (Business Income)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual earning self-employment or business income in Tanzania
- **Tax base:** Gross income less allowable business expenses (cost of goods sold, administrative expenses, depreciation at applicable rates)
- **Rate / mechanism:** 30% flat rate on net business income (EY p. 1519); same rate applies to all self-employed individuals
- **Social security:** NSSF contributions apply if employees are engaged; sole traders without employees may not be compulsorily enrolled
- **VAT obligations:** Register for VAT if annual turnover exceeds the VAT registration threshold; standard rate 18%
- **Key limits:** Annual turnover exceeding TZS 500 million requires financial statements certified by a CPA

### Option B — Presumptive Income Tax (Small Businesses)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with annual gross turnover up to TZS 100 million; excludes independent professionals, providers of technical, management, construction, and training services
- **Tax base:** Annual turnover
- **Rate / mechanism (EY p. 1521):**

  **Complete records:**
  | Annual turnover (TZS) | Tax |
  |---|---|
  | 0 – 4,000,000 | Nil |
  | 4,000,001 – 7,000,000 | 3% of turnover in excess of TZS 4,000,000 |
  | 7,000,001 – 11,000,000 | TZS 90,000 + 3% of turnover in excess of TZS 7,000,000 |
  | 11,000,001 – 100,000,000 | 3.5% of turnover |

  **Incomplete records:**
  | Annual turnover (TZS) | Tax |
  |---|---|
  | 0 – 4,000,000 | Nil |
  | 4,000,001 – 7,000,000 | TZS 100,000 |
  | 7,000,001 – 11,000,000 | TZS 250,000 |
  | 11,000,001 – 100,000,000 | 3.5% of turnover |

- **Social security:** Same as general; employer contributions apply if staff employed
- **VAT obligations:** Generally below VAT threshold; verify current threshold
- **Key limits:** Turnover ceiling TZS 100 million; professional service providers excluded

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No General Newcomer / Expat Exemption Regime

Tanzania does not offer a general territorial or remittance-basis regime for new residents. Foreign non-citizens are taxed on worldwide income after being residents for a cumulative total of two years during their lifetime (EY p. 1518). Prior to reaching the two-year cumulative threshold, foreign non-citizens are taxed only on Tanzania-source income.

### Non-Resident Employment Withholding

- Non-residents taxed on employment income sourced in Tanzania at **15% withholding** (EY p. 1519)
- Investment income (dividends, interest, royalties) withheld at rates shown in the withholding table (EY pp. 1519–1520)

## 2026 Changes

- No specific 2026 rate changes announced in EY guide; tax brackets and rates as stated above reflect the applicable law as of 1 October 2025
- Non-resident tax rate on business/investment/employment income: raised to **30%** (previously 20%) — this change is noted in the EY guide as current law (EY p. 1521)
- SDL rate: 3.5% (EY p. 1521); previously lower — confirm current year rate with TRA

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Exchange rate: **1 EUR = TZS 2,800** (approximate 2025 rate; USD/TZS ~2,570, EUR/USD ~1.09).
> "Employment" = gross salary (monthly PAYE). "B2B" = self-employed gross revenue; 30% flat tax on net income; assumes 20% business expenses.
> NSSF 10% employee contribution on gross (no ceiling).

### Working: TZS 84,000,000/year (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly gross | 84,000,000 / 12 | TZS 7,000,000 |
| PIT on TZS 7,000,000/month | 128,000 + (6,000,000 × 30%) | TZS 1,928,000/month |
| Annual PIT | 1,928,000 × 12 | **TZS 23,136,000** |

| **Effective rate** | 31,536,000 / 84,000,000 | **37.5%** |
