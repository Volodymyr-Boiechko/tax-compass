## Personal Income Tax

Flat-rate PIT system. Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents taxed on Macedonian-source income only. Tax year = calendar year. Employment income is subject to withholding at source (PAYE); self-employed and other income taxed via annual return. (EY p.1129)

**PIT rate — flat** (EY p.1129)

| Taxable income (MKD) | Rate |
|---|---|
| All income | 10% |

A second bracket applies to higher earners:

| Annual gross income (MKD) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to MKD 1,080,000 (approx. €17,500) | 10% |
| Over MKD 1,080,000 | 18% (on the excess) |

(The 18% bracket was introduced by the 2019 tax reform; EY p.1129)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal tax exemption: MKD 96,000/year (employees and self-employed) — effectively a tax-free amount
- Employee business expense deduction: 5% of gross salary (capped at MKD 24,000/year)
- For self-employed: actual business expenses deductible (or standard 25% lump-sum deduction of gross revenue)
- Pension contributions: deductible from gross income

**Notes:**
- Investment income (dividends): 15% flat withholding (final)
- Capital gains: 15% flat rate (included in annual return if not withheld at source)
- Interest income: 15% withholding (final for bank interest); other interest at 10%
- Rental income: 15% withholding or included in annual return
- No wealth tax, no inheritance tax between direct family members

## Social Security

Mandatory social contributions apply to employees, employers, and self-employed. (EY p.1130–1131)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension (PIO Fund) | 18.8% | — | 18.8% |
| Health insurance (FZOM) | 7.5% | — | 7.5% |
| Employment (AVRM) | 1.2% | — | 1.2% |
| Additional health (employer) | — | 0.5% | — |
| **Total** | **27.5%** | **0.5%** | **27.5%** |

North Macedonia uses a **gross salary** model where employee contributions are built into the gross; the headline "employer" cost is very low because contributions are largely employee-side in the calculation.

**Income ceiling:** MKD 12 × average gross salary per month = approximately MKD 660,000/year (for pension contributions). Health and employment contributions have no ceiling. (EY p.1131)

**Notes:**
- Contributions are calculated on gross salary before PIT
- Mandatory contributions are deductible from taxable income for PIT purposes
- Self-employed pay contributions based on a minimum base or actual income if higher
- Minimum contribution base for self-employed: 50% of average gross salary

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader (Trговец поединец / Trgovec poединec)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered as a sole trader under the Trade Companies Law. No revenue ceiling for standard regime.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (revenue minus allowable business expenses), or simplified: 25% lump-sum deduction from gross revenue as a standard expense.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 10% flat PIT (or 18% on income above MKD 1,080,000) on net profit after the personal exemption (MKD 96,000). Self-employed may elect to be taxed on gross revenue with 25% standard deduction.
- **Social security:** Same rates as employed (27.5%) but calculated on actual income (minimum base 50% of average gross salary).
- **VAT obligations:** Register for VAT (18% standard rate, 5% reduced) if turnover exceeds MKD 1,000,000/year. Monthly or quarterly filing.
- **Key limits:** Simple accounting maintained; annual PIT return due by 15 March of following year.

### Option B — Freelancer (Слободен уметник / other liberal professions)

- **Who qualifies:** Licensed professionals (lawyers, doctors, architects, consultants) registered in a professional chamber.
- **Tax base:** Same as Option A; may use lump-sum deduction.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same 10%/18% PIT rates.
- **Social security:** Same 27.5% on self-employment income.
- **VAT obligations:** Same MKD 1,000,000 threshold.
- **Key limits:** Professional registration required; income may be subject to withholding by corporate payers.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No formal preferential PIT regime for expats

North Macedonia does not have a specific reduced-rate regime or tax holiday for new residents. The flat 10% rate and relatively low cost of living make it attractive without special measures. (EY p.1129)

### Double Tax Treaties

North Macedonia has an extensive treaty network (~50 countries), including EU/OECD members. Treaty residence certificates and reduced WHT rates available. (EY p.1132)

## 2026 Changes

- No PIT rate changes announced for 2026 as of EY content date. The 10%/18% dual-rate system introduced in 2019 remains. (EY p.1129)
- Social contribution rates: no changes announced; 27.5% employee rate remains.
- VAT threshold: MKD 1,000,000 — monitor for potential increase.
- North Macedonia continues EU accession process; tax harmonization measures may be introduced.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants.
> MKD/EUR rate: 1 EUR = MKD 61.5 (MKD is pegged to EUR at ~61.5; ERM-like peg).
> "Employment": flat 10% PIT + employee SS 27.5%.
> "B2B Sole trader": flat 10% PIT on net revenue (25% standard deduction) + SS 27.5% on net.
> Personal exemption MKD 96,000 and 5% employee deduction applied for employment.

### Working: MKD 1,845,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (27.5%) | 27.5% × 1,845,000 | MKD 507,375 |
| 5% business expense deduction | 5% × 1,845,000 | MKD 92,250 (capped at 24,000) |
| Taxable income | 1,845,000 − 507,375 − 24,000 − 96,000 | MKD 1,217,625 |
| PIT: first 1,080,000 @ 10% | 10% × 1,080,000 | MKD 108,000 |
| PIT: excess 137,625 @ 18% | 18% × 137,625 | MKD 24,773 |
| Total PIT | | MKD 132,773 |
| **Total burden** | **132,773 + 507,375** | **MKD 640,148** |
| **Effective rate** | **640,148 / 1,845,000** | **34.7%** |

### Working: MKD 1,845,000 (≈ €30,000) Sole Trader B2B

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| SS on net income: net = 1,845,000 × 75% = 1,383,750 | 27.5% × 1,383,750 | MKD 380,531 |
| PIT base: 1,383,750 − 380,531 − 96,000 | | MKD 907,219 |
| PIT @ 10% | 10% × 907,219 | MKD 90,722 |
| **Total burden** | **90,722 + 380,531** | **MKD 471,253** |
| **Effective rate** | **471,253 / 1,845,000** | **25.5%** |

### Summary table

| Gross income | MKD equiv | Scenario | Income tax | SS (employee) | Total | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MKD 1,845,000 | €30,000 | Employment | MKD 132,773 | MKD 507,375 | MKD 640,148 | **34.7%** |
| MKD 1,845,000 | €30,000 | Sole trader B2B | MKD 90,722 | MKD 380,531 | MKD 471,253 | **25.5%** |
| MKD 3,690,000 | €60,000 | Employment | MKD 459,600 | MKD 1,014,750 | MKD 1,474,350 | **39.9%** |
| MKD 3,690,000 | €60,000 | Sole trader B2B | MKD 257,297 | MKD 761,063 | MKD 1,018,360 | **27.6%** |
| MKD 6,150,000 | €100,000 | Employment | MKD 877,400 | MKD 1,691,250 | MKD 2,568,650 | **41.8%** |
| MKD 6,150,000 | €100,000 | Sole trader B2B | MKD 427,247 | MKD 1,268,438 | MKD 1,695,685 | **27.6%** |

**Key insight:** Despite the flat 10% PIT (with 18% above MKD 1,080,000), employment effective rates are relatively high (35–42%) due to the 27.5% employee SS burden. Self-employed/sole trader enjoys significantly lower effective rates (25–28%) because SS is calculated on net income (after 25% expense deduction), and contributions are lower in absolute terms. This is one of the largest employment vs. B2B differentials in the Balkans.

**Assumptions:**
- MKD/EUR = 61.5 (pegged rate)
- Employment: 5% expense deduction capped at MKD 24,000; personal exemption MKD 96,000
- SS ceiling: contributions capped at MKD 660,000/year for pension (does not bind at €30k level but caps pension at €60k/€100k); health/employment uncapped
- Sole trader: 25% standard expense deduction on gross revenue; SS on net income at minimum or actual base (used actual here)
- No expense deductions beyond standard 25% for B2B scenarios
