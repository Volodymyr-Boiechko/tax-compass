## Personal Income Tax

> Malta has a progressive income tax system with separate rate tables for single, married, and parent filers. Tax year = calendar year (basis year); assessment year follows. Residents (ordinarily resident and domiciled) taxed on worldwide income. Residents not domiciled may use remittance basis (minimum tax EUR 5,000/year applies in certain cases). No capital gains tax on securities (with exceptions). Property transfer tax applies at 8% (final). Full imputation system for dividends.

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- No standard personal allowance (zero-rate band built into tax tables above)
- School fees (secondary/primary) for own children: max EUR 6,500/EUR 4,600 deductible
- Childcare fees: up to EUR 2,000 deductible
- Care fees for elderly family members in private home: up to EUR 2,500
- Sports activities fees for children <16: up to EUR 300/child
- Cultural activities fees for children <16: up to EUR 300/child
- Tertiary education fees: up to EUR 10,000 (excess carried forward)
- Part-time employment income (qualifying): 10% flat rate final tax (max EUR 10,000 income; EUR 12,000 for self-employment activity) (EY p. 967)
- Pension income: 60% exempt up to EUR 9,732 (2025); 80% exempt up to EUR 13,309 (2026) for pensioners active after retirement age from January 2022

**Notes:**
- Dividends: full imputation system; dividend plus underlying tax credit taxed at individual rates; excess credit refunded; 15% withholding on Untaxed Account dividends (EY p. 957)
- Capital gains: generally taxable if from immovable property (property transfer tax at 8%), securities of Maltese companies (with exceptions), business, goodwill, IPR
- Individuals not ordinarily resident in Malta or not domiciled: remittance basis; minimum tax EUR 5,000/year applies if ordinarily resident but not domiciled and meet certain conditions (EY p. 967)
- Part-time income (employment): optional 10% flat rate final tax on first EUR 10,000 earned
- Qualifying overtime: 10% flat rate for full-time employees
- Tax year (basis year) = calendar year; assessment by 30 June of following year

## Social Security

> Malta has a social security system under the Social Security Act. Employee and employer each contribute 10% of basic weekly wage. Self-employed (Class 2) contribute based on net profit from prior year.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed (Class 2) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security | 10% | 10% | 15% of annual net profits (or fixed amounts) |
| Min/week (born on or after 1 Jan 1962) | EUR 22.94 | EUR 22.94 | EUR 36.18 (if prior year income <EUR 12,543.72) |
| Max/week (born on or after 1 Jan 1962) | EUR 55.93 | EUR 55.93 | EUR 83.89 (if prior year income >EUR 29,083.35) |

**Income ceiling (annual equivalent):** Max EUR 55.93/week ├Ч 52 = EUR 2,908/year (employee); born before 1962: max EUR 49.04/week (EY p. 971)
**Notes:**
- Employee SS: 10% of basic wage; employer deducts employee's share before net salary payment; both employer and employee pay same minimum/maximum amounts (EY p. 971)
- Self-employed (Class 2): contribution based on previous year's net profit; annual maximum ~EUR 4,362/year (EUR 83.89 ├Ч 52); minimum ~EUR 1,881/year (EUR 36.18 ├Ч 52) if prior year income <EUR 12,543.72
- Social security contributions provide coverage for unemployment, illness, disability, retirement pension, maternity, and medical care
- Pensioners active after retirement do not pay SS (EY p. 971 тАФ implied)
- Totalization agreements: Australia, Canada, New Zealand, UK (EY p. 972)

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals are taxed at the same progressive rates as employed persons. Self-employment income is aggregated with other income. Class 2 social security applies.

### Option A тАФ Self-Employed Sole Trader
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual earning income from trade, business, profession, or vocation
- **Tax base:** Net accounting profits adjusted for tax purposes; all expenses wholly and exclusively incurred in production of income are deductible; capital allowances available
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT (single: 0/15/25/35%) on net taxable income; provisional tax paid in three installments (20%, 30%, 50%) on 30 April, 31 August, 21 December
- **Social security:** Class 2 contributions; based on prior year net profit; max ~EUR 83.89/week (~EUR 4,362/year) if income >EUR 29,083; minimum ~EUR 36.18/week (~EUR 1,881/year)
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above EUR 35,000 (services) or EUR 20,000 (goods) annual turnover; standard VAT rate 18%
- **Key limits:** Losses may be carried forward for offset against future income; losses may not be carried back; capital allowances (tax depreciation) available on business assets; 10% flat rate optional on first EUR 12,000 of self-employment income for qualifying part-time activities (EY p. 967)

### Option B тАФ Part-Time Self-Employment (10% flat rate)
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals who have employment as their primary occupation and carry out self-employment activities on a part-time basis
- **Tax base:** Gross self-employment income up to EUR 12,000
- **Rate / mechanism:** 10% final withholding tax тАФ no further Malta income tax; no deductions available against this income
- **Social security:** Class 2 rules apply if income exceeds EUR 910/year
- **VAT obligations:** Standard rules
- **Key limits:** Annual income cap EUR 12,000 for 10% rate; income above must be declared at progressive rates; cannot be used if individual is not employed full-time

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Tax Treatment of Highly Skilled Individuals Rules, 2026 (replacing earlier rules from 1 January 2026)
*This replaces the Highly Qualified Persons Rules, Qualifying Employment in Aviation, Innovation and Creativity, and Maritime/Offshore rules.*

**Highly Qualified Persons Rules (retained framework тАФ senior executives):**
- **Eligibility:** Highly skilled individuals receiving employment income (emoluments) from a qualifying contract; minimum income EUR 100,061 (2025 basis, adjusted by RPI annually); not domiciled in Malta; must have relevant qualifications and professional experience; must perform activities of an eligible office (listed roles in financial services, gaming, aviation, medical fields); specific conditions around residency and sickness insurance
- **Benefit:** Election to pay income tax at flat 15% on qualifying employment income; no further Malta income tax on income >EUR 5 million from a qualifying contract; no credits, deductions, reductions, or setoffs against this income
- **Duration:** 5 years for EEA/Swiss nationals; 4 years for third-country nationals; two extensions of 4тАУ5 years available on application (EY p. 953)
- **Application deadline:** Application required; conditions must be met continuously
- **Caveats:** Social security contributions still apply at normal rates; income from qualifying contract only; any other income taxed at normal rates; Maltese domicile disqualifies

**Qualifying Employment in Aviation (15% flat):**
- Minimum income EUR 45,000 from qualifying contract in aviation sector; same conditions as above; eligible offices in aviation listed (EY p. 953); 5 years for EEA/Swiss; 4 years for third-country nationals

**Qualifying Employment in Innovation and Creativity (15% flat):**
- Minimum income EUR 52,000 from qualifying contract in R&D/design/innovation/analytical activities; eligible offices as approved by Malta Enterprise (EY p. 954тАУ955)

**Qualifying Employment in Maritime and Offshore Oil & Gas (15% flat):**
- Minimum income EUR 65,000 from qualifying contract in maritime or offshore oil/gas industry; specific eligible offices (EY p. 955тАУ956)

### Malta Residence Programmes (EU/EEA/Swiss Nationals тАФ 15% on foreign income)
- **Eligibility:** EU/EEA/Swiss nationals who hold qualifying property (owned тЙеEUR 275,000 in Malta/EUR 220,000 in Gozo; rented тЙеEUR 9,600/year in Malta/EUR 8,750 in Gozo); not benefiting from any other scheme; not Maltese nationals; must have adequate financial resources, sickness insurance, communication in official Maltese language; fit and proper test
- **Benefit:** 15% tax on all foreign-source income received in Malta; minimum tax EUR 15,000/year
- **Duration:** Ongoing while conditions met
- **Application deadline:** Apply to Commissioner for Tax and Customs
- **Caveats:** Minimum EUR 15,000/year tax; local Malta-source income taxed at normal progressive rates; reporting obligations; scheme under review for consolidation (EY p. 958)

### Global Residence Programme (Non-EU/EEA/Non-Swiss Nationals)
- **Eligibility:** Same conditions as above but for non-EU/EEA/non-Swiss nationals; must hold qualifying property; minimum tax EUR 15,000/year
- **Benefit:** 15% tax on foreign-source income received in Malta
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Application deadline:** Apply to Commissioner
- **Caveats:** Same as EU/EEA programme; property thresholds slightly different

### Repatriation of Persons Established in a Field of Excellence (RPEFE)
- **Eligibility:** Maltese nationals who have lived outside Malta for тЙе20 years (not ordinarily resident for 10 consecutive preceding years); return to Malta as ordinary residents; receiving employment income from qualifying contract (approved by Malta Enterprise); minimum emoluments EUR 75,000
- **Benefit:** 15% flat rate on employment income from qualifying contract
- **Duration:** As specified by Malta Enterprise
- **Application deadline:** On return
- **Caveats:** Must work in manufacturing, R&D, or adjacent sectors; eligible employment contracts approved by Malta Enterprise (EY p. 960тАУ961)

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 950тАУ981)

- **New Tax Treatment of Highly Skilled Individuals Rules 2026** effective 1 January 2026, replacing the Highly Qualified Persons Rules, Qualifying Employment in Aviation, Innovation, Creativity, and Maritime/Offshore rules (EY p. 951)
- **New married tax rates from 1 January 2026** for families with one child (zero band EUR 17,500) and two or more children (zero band EUR 22,500) тАФ new rates apply to EU/EEA nationals or long-term residents whose child is born in Malta (EY p. 966)
- **New parent tax rates from 1 January 2026** for one child (zero band EUR 14,500) and two or more children (zero band EUR 18,500) under same nationality/residency conditions (EY p. 969)
- **Pension exemption increases to 80%** of pension income (up to EUR 13,309) from 1 January 2026 (was 60%/EUR 9,732 in 2025) for active pensioners from January 2022 onwards (EY p. 969)
- Malta tax authorities reviewing and proposing to consolidate residence programs тАФ legal provisions not yet announced (EY p. 959)
