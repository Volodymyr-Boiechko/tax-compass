## Personal Income Tax

Malta levies income tax (*taxxa fuq l-income*) under the Income Tax Act (ITA). Persons who are both **ordinarily resident and domiciled** in Malta are subject to tax on their worldwide income and chargeable capital gains. Persons who are **not ordinarily resident or not domiciled** in Malta are subject to tax only on Maltese-source income and on foreign income that is remitted to or received in Malta (**remittance basis**). Tax year = calendar year. Annual return due by 30 June of the following year. Employers operate the Final Settlement System (FSS) withholding on salaries. (EY pp. 950, 972)

**Remittance basis minimum tax liability:** Individuals applying the remittance basis who do not otherwise pay at least EUR 5,000 in Malta tax on their income must pay a minimum tax liability of **EUR 5,000/year** (may be capped lower in certain cases). (EY p. 967)

**Special Article 56(17) rate — foreign work duties:** Employment contract requiring performance of duties mainly outside Malta: income may be taxed at a flat **15%** at taxpayer's option (EY p. 951)

**Part-time employment income:** Full-time employees may elect to have part-time income taxed at flat **10%** (max EUR 10,000; EUR 12,000 for self-employment activity) instead of progressive rates (EY p. 967–968)

**Qualifying overtime:** Full-time employees in non-managerial posts may elect **10%** flat rate on qualifying overtime (subject to cap) (EY p. 968)

**Capital gains:**
- Chargeable capital gains (securities, goodwill, copyrights, etc.) taxed as ordinary income at progressive rates
- Capital losses may be carried forward (but not offset against trading profits)
- **Property transfer tax:** Transfer of immovable property taxed at **8%** of consideration/market value (final withholding tax; replaces progressive tax); lower rates: 5% (transferred within 5 years of acquisition, not part of project); 2% (sole ordinary residence transferred within 3 years); 12% (donation more than 5 years before transfer); 10% (Grade 1/2 scheduled property or urban conservation area) (EY pp. 963–964)
- **No capital gains tax** on gains from non-substantial shareholdings held >6 months (EY p. 963)
- Nonresidents: exempt from capital gains on Maltese company shares (except property companies) (EY p. 963)

**Standard deductions / allowances (employment):** (EY pp. 964–965)
- Alimony payments (court-ordered in EU/EEA/approved)
- School fees: up to EUR 6,500/child (secondary), EUR 4,600 (primary), EUR 9,320 (special needs facilitator)
- Childcare fees for children under 12: up to EUR 2,000
- Private home for elderly/disabled family members: up to EUR 2,500
- Sports/cultural activities for children under 16: up to EUR 300/child each
- Tertiary education fees (Malta or abroad): up to EUR 10,000 (excess carried forward)

**Business deductions (self-employed):** All expenses wholly and exclusively incurred in production of income, including capital allowances; EUR 935/child per employer for registered childcare (EY p. 965)

## Social Security

**Class 1 (employed persons):** (EY pp. 971–972)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Social security (basic wage-based) | **10%** | **10%** |
| Minimum weekly contribution | EUR 22.94 (born before 1962) / EUR 22.94 (born from 1962) | EUR 22.94 |
| Maximum weekly contribution | EUR 49.04 (born before 1962) or EUR 55.93 (born from 1 Jan 1962) | EUR 49.04 / EUR 55.93 |

- Employee weekly contributions: 10% of basic weekly wage; minimum EUR 22.94/week; maximum EUR 49.04/week (born before 1 Jan 1962) or EUR 55.93/week (born from 1 Jan 1962)
- Employees earning less than EUR 229.44/week pay EUR 22.94/week; may elect to pay 10% of weekly gross wage (with pro-rata contributory benefits)
- **Annual effective ceiling:** Maximum contribution = EUR 49.04 × 52 ≈ EUR 2,550/year (born before 1962) or EUR 55.93 × 52 ≈ EUR 2,908/year (born from 1962) — very low cap
- Social security contributions are deducted before net salary payment (EY p. 972)

**Class 2 (self-occupied and self-employed persons):**
- **Self-occupied:** Earn income >EUR 910/year from trade/business/profession/vocation
- **Self-employed:** Receive income from rents, investments, capital gains or any other income

Contribution rates based on prior year's annual net profit/income:
- Income in prior year < EUR 12,543.72: weekly contribution **EUR 36.18**
- Born before 1 Jan 1962, prior year income EUR 12,543.73–EUR 25,499.99: 15% of annual net earnings (up to max EUR 73.56/week)
- Born after 1 Jan 1962, prior year income EUR 12,543.73–EUR 29,083.35: 15% of annual net earnings (up to max EUR 83.89/week)
- Born before 1962, income >EUR 25,499.99: EUR 73.56/week
- Born from 1962, income >EUR 29,083.35: EUR 83.89/week
- Single self-employed (not self-occupied), prior year income EUR 1,006–EUR 11,082.72: EUR 31.97/week (EY pp. 971–972)

**Annual self-employed social security ceiling (approximate):**
- Maximum: EUR 83.89/week × 52 = **~EUR 4,362/year** (born from 1962, high income)

**Totalization agreements:** Australia, Canada, New Zealand, United Kingdom; EU Regulation 883/04 applies (EY p. 972)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a trade, business, profession or vocation in Malta
- **Tax base:** Accounting profits adjusted for disallowable expenses; all wholly/exclusively incurred business expenses deductible including capital allowances
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive income tax rates (single: 0%–35%); self-employment income aggregated with other income
- **Social security:** Class 2 contributions (maximum ~EUR 4,362/year for income >EUR 29,083)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds EUR 35,000 (goods) or EUR 24,000 (services); standard rate 18%; reduced rates 5%, 7%
- **Loss treatment:** Losses carried forward against future income; losses from trade/business may offset other income in same year; capital losses carried forward against capital gains only
- **Filing:** Provisional tax in three instalments (30 April 20%, 31 August 30%, 21 December 50%)

### Option B — Part-Time Self-Employment (10% flat rate)

- **Who qualifies:** Full-time employees earning additional self-employment income
- **Tax base:** Self-employment income only
- **Rate:** **10%** flat on first EUR 12,000 of self-employment income
- **Condition:** Must be a full-time employee; self-employment income cannot be the primary source
- **Social security:** Class 2 contributions may still apply (EY pp. 967–968)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Highly Qualified Persons Rules (HQP) — revised 2026

- **Eligibility (from 1 January 2026 — Tax Treatment of Highly Skilled Individuals Rules, 2026):** Replaces prior HQP rules; applies to: qualifying employment in aviation rules; qualifying employment in innovation and creativity rules; qualifying employment in maritime/offshore oil and gas rules. Minimum income: EUR 45,000–EUR 100,061 depending on sub-rule (EY pp. 951–957)
- **Benefit:** **15% flat rate** on qualifying employment income (no deductions, credits, or setoffs); income from qualifying employment contract exceeding EUR 5 million also at 15%
- **Duration:** 5 years (EEA/Swiss nationals); 4 years (third-country nationals); extendable by two periods of 4–5 years each
- **Caveats:** Not domiciled in Malta; must pass residency/sickness insurance/accommodation tests (EY pp. 951–957)

### Residence Programs for High-Net-Worth Individuals

**For EU/EEA/Swiss nationals — Special Tax Status (EY pp. 958–959):**
- **Benefit:** 15% tax rate on foreign-source income received in Malta; minimum tax EUR 15,000/year
- **Property requirement:** Own property ≥EUR 275,000 (EUR 220,000 in Gozo/south Malta), or rent ≥EUR 9,600/year (EUR 8,750 Gozo/south)
- **Conditions:** Not benefiting from any other scheme; not a Maltese national; not a third-country national; not Malta long-term resident
- **Caveats:** Cannot stay >183 days in any other jurisdiction per calendar year; must maintain qualifying property

**For non-EU/EEA/non-Swiss nationals — Global Residence Programme (EY p. 959):**
- **Benefit:** 15% tax rate on foreign-source income received in Malta; minimum tax EUR 15,000/year
- **Property requirement:** Purchase ≥EUR 375,000 (EUR 275,000 Gozo/south), or rent ≥EUR 14,000 (EUR 9,600 Gozo/south)
- **Government contribution:** EUR 37,000 (if purchasing or leasing property)
- **Conditions:** Similar to EU/EEA scheme; must hold assets ≥EUR 500,000 (minimum EUR 150,000 financial or EUR 650,000 with EUR 75,000 financial); not a Maltese/EU/EEA/Swiss national

**Malta Permanent Residence Programme (MPRP — EY pp. 959–960):**
- **Benefit:** Right to settle and reside permanently; Schengen area visa-free travel (90/180 days)
- **Requirements:** Third-country nationals; rent ≥EUR 14,000 or purchase ≥EUR 375,000; government contribution EUR 37,000; EUR 60,000 administrative fee; EUR 2,000 philanthropic donation; assets ≥EUR 500,000 (EUR 150,000 financial assets minimum); clean criminal record
- **Note:** MPRP itself does not confer a specific tax rate — participants use applicable Maltese resident or non-dom tax rules

**Repatriation of Persons Established in a Field of Excellence (RPEFE — EY pp. 960–961):**
- **Benefit:** 15% flat rate on Malta employment income from qualifying employment contract
- **Eligibility:** Individuals established in a field of excellence who return to Malta as ordinary residents; must have been ordinarily resident in Malta for ≥20 years but NOT ordinarily resident for the 10 consecutive years preceding return; minimum employment income EUR 75,000/year
- **Field of excellence:** Professional competence areas relevant to manufacturing, R&D, as defined by Malta Enterprise Corporation

**Non-dom / Remittance Basis (EY p. 950):**
- Individuals **not domiciled** in Malta (but resident): taxed only on Maltese-source income and **remitted** foreign income
- Foreign income **not remitted** to Malta is **not taxable**
- Minimum annual tax liability: **EUR 5,000** (applies where remittance basis results in less than EUR 5,000 tax)
- Cannot use remittance basis if: spouse/civil partner is ordinarily resident and domiciled; long-term resident status; permanent residence certificate (Free Movement EU Nationals rules)

## 2026 Changes

- **Tax Treatment of Highly Skilled Individuals Rules, 2026 (EY p. 951):** From 1 January 2026, the prior Highly Qualified Persons Rules are replaced by new rules covering: Qualifying Employment in Aviation Rules; Qualifying Employment in Innovation and Creativity Rules; Qualifying Employment in Maritime Activities and Offshore Oil and Gas Industry Activities Rules. The 15% flat rate benefit is retained under the new framework.
- **New married tax rates with children (EY pp. 965–966):** New progressive brackets for married couples with one child (0% on first EUR 17,500) and two or more children (0% on first EUR 22,500) come into effect from 1 January 2026.
- **Pension income exemption (EY p. 969):** 60% of pension income derived by pensioners who remain active after reaching retirement age on or after 1 January 2022 is exempt from tax — 60% exemption (capped EUR 9,732) for 2025; 80% exemption (capped EUR 13,309) for 2026.
- **Consolidation of residence programs under review (EY p. 959):** Malta tax authorities are reviewing and proposing to consolidate various residence programs into a unified tax framework with increased minimum tax and property value requirements. Legal provisions and transitional arrangements not yet announced.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person (standard single rates), no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Malta uses EUR. Resident, domiciled — worldwide income basis.
> Employee social security: 10% of weekly wage with annual maximum ~EUR 2,908/year (born from 1962).
> Self-employed (Class 2): maximum ~EUR 4,362/year (born from 1962, income >EUR 29,083).

### €30,000 Employment
- Income tax (single rates):
  - 0% on EUR 12,000 = EUR 0
  - 15% on EUR 4,000 = EUR 600
  - 25% on EUR 14,000 (16,001–30,000) = EUR 3,500
  - Total PIT: EUR 4,100
- Social security: EUR 22.94–EUR 55.93/week; at EUR 30,000 = ~EUR 576/week gross; 10% = EUR 57.6/week but capped at max EUR 55.93/week; annual = EUR 55.93 × 52 = EUR 2,908
- Total: EUR 4,100 + EUR 2,908 = EUR 7,008
- Effective rate: 7,008 / 30,000 = **23.4%**

### €60,000 Employment
- Income tax (single rates):
  - 0% on EUR 12,000 = 0
  - 15% on EUR 4,000 = EUR 600
  - 25% on EUR 44,000 = EUR 11,000
  - Total PIT: EUR 11,600
- Social security: capped at EUR 2,908/year
- Total: EUR 11,600 + EUR 2,908 = EUR 14,508
- Effective rate: 14,508 / 60,000 = **24.2%**

### €100,000 Employment
- Income tax (single rates):
  - 0% on EUR 12,000 = 0
  - 15% on EUR 4,000 = EUR 600
  - 25% on EUR 44,000 = EUR 11,000
  - 35% on EUR 40,000 (60,001–100,000) = EUR 14,000
  - Total PIT: EUR 25,600
- Social security: capped at EUR 2,908/year
- Total: EUR 25,600 + EUR 2,908 = EUR 28,508
- Effective rate: 28,508 / 100,000 = **28.5%**

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **New 2026 HQP framework full details:** The Tax Treatment of Highly Skilled Individuals Rules, 2026 replaced prior rules effective 1 January 2026; full details of all eligible roles under each sub-rule (aviation, innovation/creativity, maritime/offshore) not completely enumerated above.
- [ ] **Social security weekly contribution exact calculation for high earners:** The maximum weekly caps (EUR 49.04 or EUR 55.93 depending on birth year) result in a very low annual ceiling; the exact rate is 10% of basic wage but capped — for high salaries it becomes very low as a percentage.
- [ ] **Remittance basis minimum tax capping provisions:** EY notes minimum EUR 5,000 may be capped lower in certain cases; conditions for lower cap not specified.
- [ ] **Malta Enterprise Corporation guidelines for RPEFE:** Field of excellence definitions depend on Malta Enterprise Corporation guidelines; not specified in EY guide.
- [ ] **Residence program consolidation:** Malta authorities are reviewing consolidation of various HNW programmes into a unified framework — changes expected but timing and details not yet announced.
- [ ] **Part-time income cap amounts:** EUR 10,000 (employment) and EUR 12,000 (self-employment) at 10% flat rate; verify these figures with Commissioner for Tax and Customs as they may be adjusted.
