## Personal Income Tax

**There is no income tax in the Bahamas.** Income is not taxed. No capital gains tax. (EY p.110)

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employment income | **0%** |
| Business / self-employment income | **0%** |
| Capital gains | **0%** |
| Dividends | **0%** |
| Interest | **0%** |

---

## Other Taxes

**Stamp duty:**
- Sale of a business: 6% on the consideration attributable to property other than land
- Transfers of personal property: 2.5% (value ≤ BSD 100,000); 10% (value > BSD 100,000)
- Conversion of funds into foreign currency: 1.5%
- Transfer of funds out of the Bahamas: 1.5% (< BSD 500,000); 5% (≥ BSD 500,000)

(EY p.110)

**Value-added tax (VAT):**
- Standard VAT rate: **10%** on goods and services
- Real property transfers to companies/entities: **10%**
- Real property transfers to individuals (effective 1 July 2022):
  - 2.5% if value ≤ BSD 100,000
  - 4% if value BSD 100,001 – BSD 300,000
  - 6% if value BSD 300,001 – BSD 500,000
  - 8% if value BSD 500,001 – BSD 700,000
  - 9% if value BSD 700,001 – BSD 1,000,000
  - 10% if value > BSD 1,000,000
- VAT withholding (from 1 August 2022): certain government agencies withhold 40% of VAT due on payments above BSD 9,999.99 for qualifying government procurement, commercial leases, and equipment/goods purchases

(EY pp.110–111)

**Business license fee:**
- No fee for businesses with gross turnover < BSD 100,000
- For turnover ≥ BSD 100,000: 0.5%–2.25% of turnover (rate depends on nature of business)
- License year = calendar year; renewal application by 31 January each year; estimated payment by 31 March (or 4 equal instalments from 31 March)
- Occasional Business License: BSD 25 flat fee
- Temporary Business License: 1.5% of contract value

(EY p.111)

## Social Security

National Insurance scheme — mandatory for all employees and employers.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| National Insurance | **4.65%** | **6.65%** | **11.3%** |

- Applied to weekly/monthly wages
- **Income ceiling (effective 1 July 2024):** BSD 810/week, BSD 3,510/month, BSD 42,120/year
- Maximum employee contribution: ~BSD 1,960/year (4.65% × BSD 42,120)
- Maximum employer contribution: ~BSD 2,801/year (6.65% × BSD 42,120)

(EY p.111)

**Notes:**
- Social security contributions are the only mandatory payroll deductions for individuals in the Bahamas
- No health insurance mandate beyond the National Insurance scheme described above

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed / Sole Trader

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual conducting business in the Bahamas
- **Tax base:** N/A — no income tax
- **Rate / mechanism:** **0%** income tax on all business income
- **Social security:** National Insurance contributions apply to self-employed (rate and ceiling same as employees; employer and employee portions typically combined for self-employed)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover ≥ BSD 100,000; VAT rate 10%
- **Business license:** Required if annual turnover ≥ BSD 100,000 (0.5%–2.25% of turnover)
- **Key limits:** No income tax regardless of income level

(EY pp.110–111)

---

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No income tax means there is no preferential tax regime needed for newcomers — all residents and non-residents are subject to the same 0% income tax on Bahamian income. The Bahamas does not tax worldwide income of residents.

---

## 2026 Changes

- No income tax changes — the Bahamas does not impose personal income tax.
- Business license fee and VAT system remain in force as described.
- National Insurance ceiling increased to BSD 42,120/year effective 1 July 2024 (EY p.111).

## Effective Rate Examples

> The Bahamas has NO income tax. The only mandatory individual burden is National Insurance (social security).
> Currency: Bahamian Dollar (BSD) pegged 1:1 to USD. **1 EUR ≈ BSD 1.08** (approximate USD/EUR rate).
> B2B effective rate includes National Insurance + business license fee (for revenue ≥ BSD 100,000).

| Gross income (BSD) | EUR equiv | Scenario | Income tax | National Insurance (employee) | Business license | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| BSD 32,400 | €30,000 | Employment | BSD 0 | BSD 1,506 (4.65% × 32,400) | — | BSD 1,506 | **4.7%** |
| BSD 32,400 | €30,000 | B2B (< BSD 100k — no license fee) | BSD 0 | BSD 1,506 | BSD 0 | BSD 1,506 | **4.7%** |
| BSD 64,800 | €60,000 | Employment | BSD 0 | BSD 1,959 (capped at BSD 42,120 ceiling) | — | BSD 1,959 | **3.0%** |
| BSD 64,800 | €60,000 | B2B (license fee ~0.5% × 64,800) | BSD 0 | BSD 1,959 | BSD 324 | BSD 2,283 | **3.5%** |
| BSD 108,000 | €100,000 | Employment | BSD 0 | BSD 1,959 (capped) | — | BSD 1,959 | **1.8%** |
| BSD 108,000 | €100,000 | B2B (license fee ~0.5% × 108,000) | BSD 0 | BSD 1,959 | BSD 540 | BSD 2,499 | **2.3%** |

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/BSD = 1.08 (BSD pegged 1:1 to USD; approximate EUR/USD rate)
- National Insurance: 4.65% employee rate; capped at BSD 42,120/year base (max BSD 1,959/year)
- Business license fee: 0.5% rate used as minimum for businesses with turnover ≥ BSD 100,000; actual rate may be up to 2.25% depending on business type
- No VAT impact on effective income rate (VAT is a consumption tax passed to customers)
- Self-employed National Insurance modelled as employee portion only; employer portion may also apply to self-employed (combined ~11.3%)
