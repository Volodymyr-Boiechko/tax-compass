## Personal Income Tax

Schedular (cedular) system. Residents are taxed on Nicaraguan-source income; Nicaragua does not impose worldwide income taxation on residents. The tax year is the calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Tax is generally withheld at source by employers (PAYE-style withholding). Self-employed file annual returns. The main income tax law is the Ley de Concertación Tributaria (LCT).

**Progressive PIT brackets** (EY p.1113)

| Net taxable income (NIO) | Tax on lower amount (NIO) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 100,000 | 0 | 0% |
| 100,001 – 200,000 | 0 | 15% |
| 200,001 – 350,000 | 15,000 | 20% |
| 350,001 – 500,000 | 45,000 | 25% |
| Over 500,000 | 82,500 | 30% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal deduction: NIO 40,000/year (EY p.1113)
- Dependent deduction: NIO 25,000/year per dependent (spouse and children under 16, or disabled children)
- Social security contributions (employee share) are deductible
- Life insurance premiums up to NIO 10,000/year deductible

**Notes:**
- The first NIO 100,000 of net income is tax-free (0% bracket)
- Capital gains on shares/real estate: 15% final withholding tax (cedular, separate from employment income)
- Dividends paid by Nicaraguan companies: 15% withholding (final); to non-residents: 15%
- Interest income: generally 15% withholding (final)
- Rental income: 15% withholding (final) or included in annual return at progressive rates

## Social Security

Nicaragua's social security system (INSS — Instituto Nicaragüense de Seguridad Social) covers retirement, disability, health, and workplace risk. (EY p.1114)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension (IVM) | 4.25% | 12.5% | N/A (voluntary) |
| Health (Enfermedad y Maternidad) | 2.25% | 8.5% | N/A |
| Workplace risk (Riesgos Profesionales) | — | 1.5% | — |
| Victims Fund (INATEC) | — | 2.0% | — |
| **Total** | **6.5%** | **~24.5%** | **Voluntary** |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling on contributions (contributions apply on full salary). (EY p.1114)

**Notes:**
- INSS employer rate has fluctuated; the 2018 social security reform increased both rates; current rates confirmed as above per EY 2025-26
- Self-employed individuals are not mandatorily covered by INSS; voluntary affiliation available
- Medical care through INSS covers employees; private health insurance also common

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Cuota Fija (Simplified Fixed-Rate Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Small businesses and self-employed individuals with annual gross income not exceeding NIO 1,200,000 (approx. USD 33,000).
- **Tax base:** Flat monthly fee based on income bracket and business category; both income tax and VAT are combined in a single payment.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Monthly fixed payment ranging from NIO 200 to NIO 5,000 depending on income level and type of activity. Replaces both IR (income tax) and IVA (VAT) obligations.
- **Social security:** Not included; voluntary INSS affiliation.
- **VAT obligations:** Replaced by cuota fija; no separate VAT filing required.
- **Key limits:** Cannot exceed NIO 1,200,000/year in revenue; must not be engaged in activities excluded from regime (e.g., professional services with significant capital investment, certain commercial activities).

### Option B — Régimen General (Standard Self-Employed)

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals above the Cuota Fija threshold, or those who choose the general regime.
- **Tax base:** Net income (revenue minus deductible business expenses).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates 0%–30% as per the PIT brackets above.
- **Social security:** Voluntary INSS affiliation.
- **VAT obligations:** Must register for IVA (15%) if revenue exceeds the Cuota Fija threshold; file monthly returns.
- **Key limits:** Must maintain accounting records; subject to standard corporate compliance requirements.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No specific preferential PIT regime for new residents / expats

Nicaragua does not offer a formal tax holiday or exemption regime for new residents or inbound expatriates. The territorial income tax system (only Nicaraguan-source income taxed) is itself beneficial for foreigners with foreign-source income. (EY p.1113)

### Free Trade Zones (Zonas Francas)

- **Eligibility:** Companies operating in designated free trade zones; employees working in ZF-registered companies.
- **Benefit:** Significant tax incentives for companies; personal income of employees is subject to normal PIT.
- **Duration:** Per investment agreement.
- **Caveats:** Benefits apply to companies, not directly to individual PIT rates.

- No significant PIT rate changes announced for 2026 as of EY content date (October 2025). (EY p.1113)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants.
> NIO/EUR rate: 1 EUR = NIO 40 (approximate 2025 rate; NIO is pegged to USD with crawling peg, ~NIO 36/USD).
> "Employment": PAYE withheld + INSS employee 6.5%.
> "B2B": progressive PIT on net income; no mandatory INSS.
> Personal deduction NIO 40,000 applied.

### Working: NIO 1,200,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| INSS employee (6.5%) | 6.5% × 1,200,000 | NIO 78,000 |
| Gross taxable income | 1,200,000 | |
| Less: personal deduction | −40,000 | |
| Less: INSS employee | −78,000 | |
| Net taxable income | 1,200,000 − 40,000 − 78,000 | NIO 1,082,000 |
| PIT: 0–100,000 @ 0% | 0 | NIO 0 |
| PIT: 100,001–200,000 @ 15% | 15% × 100,000 | NIO 15,000 |
| PIT: 200,001–350,000 @ 20% | 20% × 150,000 | NIO 30,000 |
| PIT: 350,001–500,000 @ 25% | 25% × 150,000 | NIO 37,500 |
| PIT: 500,001–1,082,000 @ 30% | 30% × 582,000 | NIO 174,600 |
| Total PIT | | NIO 257,100 |
| **Total burden** | **257,100 + 78,000** | **NIO 335,100** |
| **Effective rate** | **335,100 / 1,200,000** | **27.9%** |
