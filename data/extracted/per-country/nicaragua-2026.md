## Personal Income Tax

Territorial system — resident and nonresident individuals taxed on income earned in Nicaragua and its links abroad as determined by law. Foreign-source income is NOT taxed. Residency: > 180 consecutive or noncontinuous days in calendar year OR center of economic interests in Nicaragua (unless can prove domicile abroad in a non-tax-haven country) OR Nicaraguan national on active diplomatic/consular service abroad. Tax year: 1 January to 31 December. (EY pp.1113–1114)

**Employment income — non-taxable threshold:** Annual employment income up to NIO 100,000 is exempt; income in excess of NIO 100,000 is taxable. (EY p.1113)

**Exempt employment income items:** (EY p.1113)
- 13th month salary (*aguinaldo*)
- Labor indemnification (up to 5 months' base salary)
- Indemnification up to NIO 500,000 received in addition to five months' salary
- Social security contributions

**Employment income annual PIT rates (resident individuals):** (EY p.1114)

| Annual taxable income — Exceeding (NIO) | Not exceeding (NIO) | Tax on lower amount (NIO) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 100,000 | 0 | 0% |
| 100,000 | 200,000 | 0 | 15% |
| 200,000 | 350,000 | 15,000 | 20% |
| 350,000 | 500,000 | 45,000 | 25% |
| 500,000 | — | 82,500 | 30% |

**Self-employment and business income annual PIT rates (residents with annual gross income ≤ NIO 12 million):** (EY p.1114)

| Annual taxable income — Exceeding (NIO) | Not exceeding (NIO) | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 100,000 | 10% |
| 100,000 | 200,000 | 15% |
| 200,000 | 350,000 | 20% |
| 350,000 | 500,000 | 25% |
| 500,000 | — | 30% |

**High-income self-employed:** If annual gross income exceeds NIO 12 million, a flat 30% rate applies to self-employment and business income. (EY p.1114)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Social security contributions of employed individuals: deductible (EY p.1114)
- Non-taxable threshold: first NIO 100,000 annual income exempt for employees

**Notes:**
- Dividends from local companies: 15% final withholding (residents and nonresidents) (EY p.1114)
- Per diem directors' fees: 25% final withholding (EY p.1114)
- Capital gains: 15% final withholding tax (EY p.1114)
- Property transfers (real estate and vehicles registered in Public Registry): progressive withholding 1%–7% of value (special rule) (EY p.1114)
- Nonresidents — employment income: 20% final withholding (EY p.1114)
- Nonresidents — professional services/technical advice: 20% final withholding (EY p.1114)
- Gifts and inheritances from Nicaraguan sources: 15% flat tax (EY p.1114)
- No tax treaties with any country (EY p.1115)
- Tax authority: DGI (Dirección General de Ingresos)

## Social Security (INSS — Instituto Nicaragüense de Seguridad Social)

Two schemes: **mandatory** (for employment relationships) and **optional** (for independent professionals who voluntarily register). (EY p.1115)

**Mandatory scheme (employment):**

| Contribution | Employee % | Employer % |
|---|---|---|
| INSS (mandatory) | 7% | 21.5% or 22.5% (a) |
| **Total** | **7%** | **21.5%/22.5%** |

(a) 22.5% for companies with more than 50 employees; 21.5% for companies with 50 or fewer employees.

**Optional scheme (independent professionals):**

| Contribution | Rate |
|---|---|
| INSS optional (full coverage) | 22.25% of income |

**Income ceiling:** Contributions calculated on gross salary with no stated ceiling.

**Notes:**
- Mandatory scheme applies from first day of employment for nationals and legal residents
- Independent professionals may voluntarily register for complete SS coverage at 22.25% of income
- No totalization agreements with any country (EY p.1115)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Progressive Rates)

- **Who qualifies:** All individuals with self-employment or business income, with annual gross income ≤ NIO 12 million.
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses). Self-employed pay installments of advance income tax.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates 10%–30% on annual net income (separate bracket table from employment income — bottom tier starts at 10%, not 0%). Monthly or quarterly advance tax installments required.
- **Social security:** Optional INSS scheme at 22.25% if registered; otherwise no mandatory SS.
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IVA (Nicaragua VAT) at 15% standard rate; monthly filing required. Must issue invoices.
- **Key limits:** Annual gross income ≤ NIO 12 million to use progressive rates; above NIO 12 million, flat 30% rate applies.

### Option B — High-Income Flat Rate

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed or business individuals with annual gross income > NIO 12 million.
- **Tax base:** Gross income (no deduction threshold as for lower earners; 30% flat on taxable income).
- **Rate / mechanism:** 30% flat on net taxable income.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Territorial Taxation (General)
- **Eligibility:** All individuals.
- **Benefit:** Only Nicaragua-source income taxed. Foreign-source income completely exempt.
- **Duration:** Permanent feature.
- **Caveats:** Residency threshold is 180 days (consecutive or non-consecutive). The "center of economic interests" test can catch individuals who have substantial ties to Nicaragua even if below 180 days.

### No Specific Digital Nomad Visa Confirmed
- Nicaragua does not have a specific digital nomad visa or preferential tax regime for remote workers as of EY data date. The territorial system means income from foreign clients for remote work is not subject to Nicaragua income tax.
