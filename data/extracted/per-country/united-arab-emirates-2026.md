## Personal Income Tax

**There is no personal income tax (PIT) in the United Arab Emirates.** Individuals pay 0% income tax on all personal income, regardless of source, amount or nationality. This applies to:
- Employment income (salaries, bonuses, benefits-in-kind)
- Self-employment / freelance income
- Investment income (dividends, interest, capital gains)
- Rental income
- Foreign-sourced income

(EY p.1592)

**No capital gains tax, no net worth tax, no inheritance/estate tax, no gift tax.** Capital gains are taxed only as part of regular business profits at the corporate level (if applicable). (EY p.1592–1593)

**Tax residency:** The UAE issued Cabinet Decision No. 85 of 2022 (effective 1 March 2023) on individual tax residency. An individual is a UAE tax resident if:
- Present in UAE for ≥ 183 days in any consecutive 12-month period; OR
- Present 90–182 days and is a GCC national or holds valid UAE residence permit AND has a permanent place of residence OR carries on employment/business in UAE; OR
- Present < 90 days but UAE is their primary/usual residence and center of financial/personal interests, meeting Ministry of Finance conditions.

A UAE Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) can be applied for to confirm UAE tax residency under domestic law and/or for double tax treaty purposes. (EY p.1592)

## Corporate Tax (for self-employed who incorporate)

While there is no PIT, self-employed individuals and entrepreneurs who conduct business activities in the UAE **may be subject to UAE Corporate Tax (CT)**, introduced by Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022, effective for financial years beginning on or after **1 June 2023**.

### CT Rates

| Taxable income (AED) | CT rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 375,000 | 0% |
| Above 375,000 | 9% |

**Free Zone Qualifying Income:** Entities in UAE free zones that earn "qualifying income" pay **0% CT**. Qualifying income includes income from transactions between free zone persons and foreign persons, and certain other qualifying activities. Non-qualifying income is subject to 9% CT.

### Small Business Relief (SBR)

- **Who qualifies:** UAE CT taxable persons (residents and non-residents with UAE business) whose total **revenue does not exceed AED 3,000,000** in the relevant tax period AND in all prior tax periods since 1 June 2023.
- **Benefit:** Elect to treat taxable income as **zero** (0% CT regardless of profit level).
- **Availability:** For tax periods **ending on or before 31 December 2026** only — SBR is a temporary provision and will **not be extended** beyond 2026 (no extension announced by UAE MoF or FTA as of May 2026).
- **Ineligibility:** If revenue exceeded AED 3M in any prior period since June 2023, SBR is permanently unavailable for that entity. MNE group members (consolidated global revenue > AED 3.15B) are not eligible.
- **Impact:** From 1 January 2027, businesses with revenue below AED 3M will be subject to standard 0%/9% CT rates — 9% on profits above AED 375,000.

(Sources: EY p.1592; tax.gov.ae — Small Business Relief; inchub.ae; dirhamwise.com)

### CT — Key Details

- **Tax period:** Financial year of the business; taxable income is accounting profit with adjustments.
- **Filing:** CT return must be filed within 9 months of end of financial year; CT paid on filing.
- **Transfer pricing:** Arm's length principle applies to transactions with related parties and connected persons.
- **Pillar Two (Global Minimum Tax):** Large multinationals (global revenue > EUR 750M) subject to Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT) at 15% from 1 January 2025.
- **Free zones:** Free zone companies can maintain 0% CT on qualifying income while being CT registered. They must file CT returns.

## Social Security

**UAE nationals** working in public and private sector entities are subject to the UAE social security system of the **Emirate** in which the employer is registered.

**Abu Dhabi (employer registered in Abu Dhabi):**

| Social insurance contributions | Rate (%) |
|---|---|
| Employer's contribution | 15 |
| Employee's contribution | 5 |
| Government's contribution* | 6 |

*Government contribution applies only if the individual entered the Abu Dhabi workforce before 1 December 2023; otherwise employee contribution is 11% and government contribution does not apply.

Salary ceiling in Abu Dhabi: **AED 100,000/month** (minimum: AED 6,000/month). (EY p.1593)

**Other Emirates (employer in Dubai, Sharjah, etc.):**

*Joined workforce before 1 October 2023:*

| Social insurance contributions | Rate (%) |
|---|---|
| Employer's contribution | 12.5 |
| Employee's contribution | 5 |
| Government's contribution | 2.5 |

Salary ceiling: **AED 50,000/month**.

*Joined workforce on or after 1 October 2023:*

| Social insurance contributions | Rate (%) |
|---|---|
| Employer's contribution | 15* |
| Employee's contribution | 11 |

*Government bears 2.5% of employer share for UAE nationals with remuneration < AED 20,000.

Salary ceiling: **AED 70,000/month**.

(EY p.1593)

**GCC (non-UAE) nationals:** Subject to their home country social security system while working in UAE (per GCC Unified Law of Insurance Protection Extension). Home country rates and caps apply. (EY p.1593)

**Non-GCC foreign nationals (expatriates):** **Not subject to UAE social security.** However, they are entitled to **end-of-service gratuity** upon termination of employment.

| Contribution | UAE nationals | GCC nationals | Foreign nationals |
|---|---|---|---|
| UAE Social Security | Yes (rates above) | Home country system | **No** |
| End-of-service gratuity | — | — | Yes (statutory) |

(EY p.1593)

### End-of-Service Gratuity (for expatriates)

Non-GCC foreign employees in the private sector are entitled to an **end-of-service benefit** upon completing more than 1 year of continuous employment:
- **First 5 years:** 21 days' base salary per year of service.
- **From the 6th year:** 30 days' base salary per year of service.

This is not a monthly contribution but an accrued liability paid on termination. The **Savings Scheme** (introduced November 2023) allows employers to opt for an investment-based alternative to the traditional gratuity.

**DIFC (Dubai International Financial Centre):** Employees of DIFC-registered employers are enrolled in the **DEWS Plan** (DIFC Employee Workplace Savings Plan) — a defined contribution scheme replacing the traditional gratuity for DIFC employers.

**ILOE (Involuntary Loss of Employment Insurance):** Private sector employees and federal government workers must subscribe to the ILOE scheme (unless working for DIFC/ADGM employers). Provides up to 3 months' compensation for qualifying unemployment. Registration within 4 months of entry permit or visa change. (EY p.1594)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Freelancer License / Individual Establishment

- **Who qualifies:** Individual freelancer working in specific professional activities in UAE free zones or on Dubai Department of Economy & Tourism (DET) freelance permit.
- **Tax base:** Business income subject to UAE CT (if total revenue > AED 3M, 9% CT on profits above AED 375,000; if ≤ AED 3M and SBR election, 0% CT through 2026; from 2027, standard CT rates apply).
- **Rate / mechanism:** 0% PIT always. CT at 0%/9% on business profits.
- **Social security:** Not applicable to foreign nationals.
- **VAT:** Registration mandatory if taxable supplies exceed **AED 375,000/year** (voluntary from AED 187,500). Standard rate 5%.
- **Key limits:** Each emirate and free zone has different freelancer permit requirements, costs, and eligible activity lists.

### Option B — UAE Mainland Company (LLC or Sole Establishment)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual incorporating a UAE mainland company.
- **Tax base:** CT at 0%/9% on company profits above AED 375,000. SBR available through 2026 (revenue ≤ AED 3M).
- **Rate / mechanism:** No PIT on salary drawn. CT on company profits: 0% (first AED 375,000) + 9% (above AED 375,000). Dividends from UAE company to individual shareholder: 0% tax (no dividend withholding tax for UAE individuals).
- **Social security:** Employer obligations for UAE national employees; none for foreign national shareholder-directors.
- **Key limits:** Foreign ownership now up to 100% for most activities (2021 Commercial Companies Law amendments). Emiratization quotas apply to companies with ≥50 employees.

### Option C — UAE Free Zone Company

- **Who qualifies:** Individual setting up company in a UAE free zone (DIFC, ADGM, JAFZA, Dubai Silicon Oasis, etc.).
- **Tax base:** **0% CT on qualifying income** from transactions with foreign persons or other free zone persons. 9% CT on non-qualifying (onshore UAE) income.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Effectively 0% CT for businesses transacting internationally. Must maintain substance in the free zone (office, employees, etc.).
- **Key limits:** Free zones have registration fees, annual renewal fees, and activity restrictions. To work in UAE mainland, a branch or commercial license may be needed.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Golden Visa (10-Year Residency)
- **Eligibility:** Multiple routes including:
  - Investors purchasing UAE property ≥ AED 2,000,000 (or AED 2M off-plan from approved developers).
  - Highly skilled professionals: monthly salary ≥ AED 30,000, bachelor's degree, executive/manager role or skilled profession.
  - Entrepreneurs: startup registered in UAE with enterprise value ≥ AED 500,000 (auditor-certified) OR from a government-recognized incubator.
  - Individuals with exceptional talent (arts, culture, sports, digital technology).
  - Scientists, researchers with PhD/Master's in STEM from top-100 global universities.
  - Outstanding students/graduates from UAE or top-100 universities globally.
  - Humanitarian pioneers.
- **Benefit:** Self-sponsored 10-year residency; no need for company/employer sponsor. Can stay outside UAE up to 1 year continuously (normal residency allows only 6 months). Can sponsor unlimited domestic helpers. Spouse and children (any age) and parents eligible for Golden Visa.
- **Tax benefit:** 0% PIT as with all UAE residents. Golden Visa + UAE TRC enables treaty-based tax residency certificates.
- **Duration:** 10 years; renewable.
- **Caveats:** Must notify MOM of annual earnings if holding ONE Pass equivalent; must maintain qualifying investment/employment conditions for renewal. (EY pp.1599–1600)

### Remote Working Visa (1-Year)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals working remotely for a company abroad (non-UAE employer). Minimum remuneration: **USD 3,500/month net**. Must maintain employment/contract arrangements abroad.
- **Benefit:** Live and work remotely from UAE for 1 year; sponsor dependants. **0% UAE PIT** on income.
- **Tax treatment:** Holder pays 0% UAE PIT. Foreign employer obligations remain in home country (depends on bilateral tax treaty and home country rules).
- **Duration:** 1 year; valid for sponsoring dependants.
- **Caveats:** Holder may not perform UAE-related work or services for UAE-based entities. All employment arrangements must remain abroad. Remote work must be performed from "neutral" locations (residence, hotel). (EY p.1597)

### Standard Employment Route (Employment Visa / Residence Permit)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals employed by a UAE company. Company must be eligible to sponsor; quota restrictions apply.
- **Benefit:** 0% PIT; UAE residence for the duration of employment.
- **Duration:** Work permit valid 1–5 years depending on role and jurisdiction (MoHRE or Free Zone authority).
- **Caveats:** Emiratization requirements apply to mainland companies (≥50 employees must meet UAE national hiring targets). Company must sponsor residence permit. (EY pp.1597–1598)

## 2026 Changes

- **Small Business Relief (SBR) — final year:** SBR available for tax periods ending on or before **31 December 2026**. No extension announced. From 1 January 2027, all businesses subject to standard CT rates (0%/9%) regardless of revenue. Revenue must not have exceeded AED 3M in any period since 1 June 2023 to still qualify. (tax.gov.ae; inchub.ae)
- **Emiratization targets:** UAE Mainland companies with 50+ employees must reach 10% UAE national employment of skilled roles by end of 2026. Semiannual monitoring; fines of AED 9,000/month per unfilled position (rising by AED 1,000 annually). Companies with 20–49 employees in 14 specified sectors have additional Emiratization targets (1% increase in 2026). (EY pp.1600–1601)
- **QDMTT (Pillar Two):** Large multinationals (global revenue > EUR 750M) subject to Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax at 15% — effective from 1 January 2025 for the first affected tax periods. (Not relevant to typical SME/freelancer scenario)
- **CT administrative updates:** UAE FTA continues issuing Ministerial Decisions and guidelines on CT implementation; rules on free zone qualifying income, transfer pricing documentation (Master File/Local File for certain groups), and related-party disclosures ongoing.
- **VAT:** Standard 5% rate unchanged; no changes announced for 2026.
- **Golden Visa:** Criteria periodically updated by the UAE government; no major announced changes for 2026.

## Effective Rate Examples

> **PIT is 0% in the UAE — all employment and self-employment income is free of personal income tax.**
> The effective rate examples focus on the **corporate tax cost** for self-employed persons who conduct business through a UAE entity, since CT is the primary tax consideration.
> **1 EUR = AED 3.9** (2025 average; AED is pegged to USD at AED 3.6725/USD; EUR/USD ~1.06).
> "Employment" = salary from UAE employer; 0% PIT; foreign national (no UAE social security).
> "Sole proprietor / freelancer via UAE entity" = gross revenue, 0% CT on first AED 375,000 of profit; 9% CT above AED 375,000. Through 2026 (SBR): 0% CT if revenue ≤ AED 3,000,000.

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Free zone qualifying income rules:** The 0% CT on qualifying free zone income has detailed conditions (substance requirements, eligible activities, non-qualifying income proportions) — not fully detailed here; consult UAE CT legislation and FTA guidelines for specific free zone eligibility
- [ ] **Pillar Two / QDMTT:** Not modeled — only relevant for MNE groups with global revenue > EUR 750M
- [ ] **VAT impact on self-employed:** 5% VAT on taxable supplies above AED 375,000 threshold adds compliance cost and cash flow timing impact — not modeled in the effective rate table
- [ ] **End-of-service gratuity quantification:** Accruing gratuity liability for employers is not a current cash tax but represents a significant total employment cost; not included in employee burden
- [ ] **Home country tax obligations:** UAE residents with home-country tax ties (e.g., US citizens, UK domiciled individuals) may owe tax in their home jurisdiction — the UAE 0% PIT does not eliminate home-country obligations
- [ ] **CT for unincorporated sole traders:** Whether an individual freelancer (without a company) is subject to CT as a "natural person" carrying on business is an area of some uncertainty in UAE CT practice — most freelancers operate through licensed entities (LLC, free zone company, sole establishment) which are clearly within CT scope
- [ ] **2027 SBR expiry planning:** From 2027, businesses previously relying on SBR will need to register for CT, file returns and potentially pay 9% on profits above AED 375,000 — timing of transition advice not included
- [ ] **Dubai/emirate-specific fees:** Various emirate-level fees (municipality levies, real estate fees, trade license renewal costs) are not income taxes but represent business costs; not modeled
