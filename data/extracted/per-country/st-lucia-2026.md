## Personal Income Tax

An individual resident in St. Lucia is subject to income tax on worldwide income regardless of whether it is remitted to St. Lucia. A resident but not ordinarily resident individual is subject to tax on income received in St. Lucia. Nonresidents are taxed on St. Lucia-source income only.

Residency: physically present ≥183 days in a calendar year, or ordinarily resident (permanent home in St. Lucia + present at any time during the income year). Also deemed resident if present for ≥183 consecutive days across two calendar years. Tax year = calendar year, ending 31 December. Filing deadline: 31 March following the income year. Married persons are taxed separately on all types of income. (EY pp.1339, 1342)

### PIT Brackets (2025 rates)

| Taxable income (XCD) | Tax rate (%) | Tax due at bracket top (XCD) | Cumulative tax due (XCD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Up to 15,000 | 15 | 2,250 | 2,250 |
| Next 15,000 (15,001–30,000) | 20 | 3,000 | 5,250 |
| Above 30,000 | 30 | — | — |

Nonresidents are taxed at the same rates as residents, except for interest, royalties, and management fees (which are subject to withholding taxes — see below). (EY p.1341)

**Capital gains:** Not subject to tax; capital losses are not deductible. (EY p.1340)

**Dividends and interest:** Dividends and interest on deposits in any bank in St. Lucia are not subject to tax on resident individuals. Other interest received by residents is aggregated with all other taxable income and taxed at PIT rates. Royalties are aggregated and taxed at PIT rates. (EY p.1340)

**Withholding on payments to nonresidents:**
- Domestic royalties, commissions, premiums, and management fees: 25% final withholding
- Domestic interest: 15% final withholding

Nonresidents may not claim the above deductions and allowances. (EY p.1341)

**Business deductions:** Any expenses incurred wholly and exclusively for the purpose of producing taxable business income are deductible. (EY p.1341)

**Notes:**
- The basic allowance of XCD30,000 is highly significant — it means income up to XCD30,000 has an effective PIT of 0% after deduction (though technically taxed at 15% and 20% on the portion up to XCD30,000, the basic allowance reduces taxable income to zero for incomes ≤ XCD30,000)
- Business losses may be carried forward six years; offset restricted to 50% of chargeable income; no carry-back permitted (EY p.1341)
- Employer-provided stock options taxed the same as other employment income (EY p.1340)

## Social Security

National Insurance Scheme contributions. Rates applied on maximum monthly insurable earnings of XCD5,000. (EY p.1342)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| National Insurance (all components) | 5% | 5% | 10% |
| **Total** | **5%** | **5%** | **10%** |

**Income ceiling:** Maximum monthly insurable earnings of XCD5,000 (= XCD60,000/year).

**Notes:**
- Self-employed persons contribute at 10% (employee + employer combined rate)
- Self-employed contributions are based on earnings agreed with the National Insurance Corporation (NIC), up to XCD5,000/month maximum insurable earnings
- Self-employed must file NIS returns regardless of income level (EY p.1342)
- St. Lucia has social security totalization agreements with Canada and CARICOM countries (EY p.1342)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Net Income Basis)

- **Who qualifies:** Any self-employed individual in St. Lucia
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus allowable business expenses); income tax imposed on net business income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 15/20/30% applied to net taxable income after basic allowance (XCD30,000)
- **Social security:** 10% NIS on earnings up to XCD5,000/month (= XCD60,000/year maximum contribution base); fixed maximum NIS = 10% × 60,000 = XCD6,000/year
- **VAT obligations:** St. Lucia VAT applies; standard rate 12.5% (note: EY does not detail VAT in this section; threshold and rate to be verified)
- **Key limits:** Business losses carried forward 6 years (50% offset limit); all business expenses must be wholly and exclusively for business purposes

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Dedicated Newcomer PIT Regime

St. Lucia does not have a remittance-basis or new-resident preferential PIT regime. Individuals establishing residency in St. Lucia become subject to standard PIT on worldwide income.

### Work Permit System

- Foreign nationals employed in St. Lucia must obtain work permits; evaluated case by case; no quota system. (EY p.1343)
- Work permit applicants may not work while application is being processed
- Permanent residence available after five years of continuous residence; permanent residents may work without a work permit

### CARICOM Free Movement

- St. Lucia is a CARICOM member; certain CARICOM nationals (skilled workers) have free movement rights for employment within the Community

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, basic allowance XCD30,000 applied.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side only; employer NIS 5% not shown).
> "B2B" = gross revenue, 30% assumed business expenses, net income subject to PIT.
> Currency: XCD (Eastern Caribbean Dollar; 1 EUR ≈ 2.94 XCD; 1 USD = 2.70 XCD fixed).
> NIS cap: XCD60,000/year → max NIS = XCD6,000/year for employee (5%) or self-employed (10%).

### Working calculations:

**€30,000 Employment (≈ XCD88,200):**
- Taxable income after basic allowance: 88,200 − 30,000 = XCD58,200
- PIT: 15%×15,000 + 20%×15,000 + 30%×(58,200−30,000) = 2,250 + 3,000 + 30%×28,200 = 2,250 + 3,000 + 8,460 = XCD13,710
- NIS (employee 5%, capped at XCD60,000): 5% × 60,000 = XCD3,000
- Total: XCD16,710; effective rate = 18.9%

**€60,000 Employment (≈ XCD176,400):**
- Taxable income: 176,400 − 30,000 = XCD146,400
- PIT: 2,250 + 3,000 + 30%×(146,400−30,000) = 5,250 + 30%×116,400 = 5,250 + 34,920 = XCD40,170
- NIS: XCD3,000 (capped)
- Total: XCD43,170; effective rate = 24.5%

**€100,000 Employment (≈ XCD294,000):**
- Taxable income: 294,000 − 30,000 = XCD264,000
- PIT: 5,250 + 30%×(264,000−30,000) = 5,250 + 30%×234,000 = 5,250 + 70,200 = XCD75,450
- NIS: XCD3,000 (capped)
- Total: XCD78,450; effective rate = 26.7%

**B2B (30% expenses assumed):**
- €30k → gross XCD88,200; net XCD61,740; taxable net − 30,000 = XCD31,740; PIT = 5,250 + 30%×1,740 = 5,250 + 522 = XCD5,772; NIS SE 10%×60,000 = XCD6,000; total = XCD11,772; eff. 13.3%
- €60k → gross XCD176,400; net XCD123,480; taxable − 30,000 = XCD93,480; PIT = 5,250 + 30%×63,480 = 5,250 + 19,044 = XCD24,294; NIS = XCD6,000; total = XCD30,294; eff. 17.2%
- €100k → gross XCD294,000; net XCD205,800; taxable − 30,000 = XCD175,800; PIT = 5,250 + 30%×145,800 = 5,250 + 43,740 = XCD48,990; NIS = XCD6,000; total = XCD54,990; eff. 18.7%

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| XCD88,200 (€30k) | Employment | XCD13,710 | XCD3,000 (NIS 5%, capped) | XCD16,710 | **18.9%** |
| XCD88,200 (€30k) | B2B (30% expenses) | XCD5,772 | XCD6,000 (NIS 10%, capped) | XCD11,772 | **13.3%** |
| XCD176,400 (€60k) | Employment | XCD40,170 | XCD3,000 (NIS 5%, capped) | XCD43,170 | **24.5%** |
| XCD176,400 (€60k) | B2B (30% expenses) | XCD24,294 | XCD6,000 (NIS 10%, capped) | XCD30,294 | **17.2%** |
| XCD294,000 (€100k) | Employment | XCD75,450 | XCD3,000 (NIS 5%, capped) | XCD78,450 | **26.7%** |
| XCD294,000 (€100k) | B2B (30% expenses) | XCD48,990 | XCD6,000 (NIS 10%, capped) | XCD54,990 | **18.7%** |

**Key insight:** B2B is significantly more favorable than employment in St. Lucia at all income levels, primarily because of the low NIS cap (XCD60,000/year = effective max NIS XCD6,000/year for self-employed) combined with deductible business expenses reducing the PIT base.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/XCD = 2.94 (approximate; USD/XCD = 2.70 fixed)
- Basic allowance XCD30,000 applied to all scenarios
- NIS: employee 5% capped at XCD60,000 → max XCD3,000/year; self-employed 10% capped → max XCD6,000/year
- B2B: 30% business expenses assumed
- No other deductions (retirement contributions, mortgage, etc.) included
- Employer-side NIS (5%) not shown
