## Personal Income Tax

Kazakhstan's **new Tax Code took effect 1 January 2026**. This chapter reflects the 2026 rules. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents taxed on Kazakhstan-source income only. Residency = present ≥183 days in any 12-month period ending in the current tax year, or center of vital interests in Kazakhstan. (EY pp. 761–768)

**2026 key figures:**
- Monthly calculation index (MCI): **KZT4,325**
- Minimum monthly salary (MMS): **KZT85,000**
- Average exchange rate 2025: KZT521.59 = USD1

### Personal Income Tax Rates (2026 — Resident Individuals)

The new Tax Code introduces a **progressive PIT scale** for residents (previously flat 10%): (EY p. 765)

| Taxable income | Taxable base | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| General income (employment, investment income excl. dividends, rental income, capital gains from property disposal, etc.) | Up to 8,500 MCI/year (= KZT36,762,500) | **10%** |
| General income | Portion exceeding 8,500 MCI/year | **15%** |
| Dividend income (received by residents, or by nonresidents owning ≥25% of paying company) | Up to 230,000 MCI/year (= KZT994,750,000) | **5%** |
| Dividend income | Portion exceeding 230,000 MCI/year | **15%** |
| Income of advocates and private notaries | No limit | **9%** |
| Individual entrepreneurship income | Up to 230,000 MCI/year | **10%** |
| Individual entrepreneurship income | Portion exceeding 230,000 MCI/year | **15%** |

**Threshold: 8,500 MCI = 8,500 × KZT4,325 = KZT36,762,500/year ≈ USD70,500/year ≈ €64,700/year**

**Nonresidents:**
- General income: same two-tier 10%/15% on Kazakhstan-source income
- Secondees: 10%/15% (same as residents)
- All other nonresident Kazakhstan-source income: **20%** flat
- Capital gains, dividends, interest for nonresidents: **15%** flat (EY p. 765)

### Basic Tax Deduction (2026)

- **30 MCI per month** = 30 × KZT4,325 = **KZT129,750/month** ≈ USD249/month deductible from taxable employment income
- **Annual basic deduction:** 360 MCI = KZT1,557,000 ≈ USD2,985
- If monthly taxable income is lower than the deduction (KZT129,750), unused portion may be carried forward within the same tax year (not across employer changes mid-year) (EY p. 764)

**Additional tax deductions:**
- Social deductions: for persons with disabilities, veterans, adoptive parents, other eligible categories
- Mandatory pension fund contributions (10% of gross): deductible for PIT
- Social health insurance contributions (employee 2%): deductible for PIT
- Social security contributions withheld under civil contracts: deductible
- Education fund contributions (for training/professional development under approved programs)

### Exempt Income (selected)

- Dividends and interest on KASE/AIX-listed securities (if securities were traded during the reporting year)
- Capital gains from open-bidding disposal of shares held >3 years (if the issuer is not a subsurface user and ≤50% of its assets are subsurface-related)
- Interest on deposits paid to resident individuals by licensed Kazakhstan banks
- Income from Kazakhstan state securities
- Business travel per diems, accommodation, and meal allowances within established norms

## Social Security

Kazakhstan has several mandatory payroll-related contributions. **Effective 2026** under the new Tax Code: (EY pp. 766–767)

### Mandatory Pension Fund Contributions (UAPF)

| Category | Rate | Ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| Employee (individual pension contribution) | **10%** of gross salary | Income up to 50 MMS/month = 50 × KZT85,000 = KZT4,250,000/month (≈USD8,150) |
| Employer (professional pension contribution) | **5%** for certain high-risk occupations (17 industry sectors: mining, oil/gas, pharma, consumer goods mfg) | No stated ceiling |

**Annual employee pension ceiling:** 50 × 85,000 × 12 = KZT51,000,000/year
**Deductibility:** Employee pension contributions are deductible for PIT and social tax purposes.

### Social Tax (Employer-Only)

- **Rate (from 2026):** Flat **6% of gross income** (employer-paid; reduced by allowable exemptions)
- **Minimum tax base:** 14 MCI/employee = 14 × KZT4,325 = KZT60,550/month
- Additional monthly: No longer reducible by mandatory social insurance contributions (from 2026 change)
- Social tax is NOT an employee deduction — it is an employer-side levy on payroll

### Mandatory Social Insurance Contributions

| Category | Rate | Ceiling (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Employer | **5%** of income paid to employee | Up to 7 × MMS/month = 7 × KZT85,000 = KZT595,000/month (≈USD1,140) |
| Employee (for civil contract income) | **5%** (withheld by payer) | Same ceiling |

**Note:** Social insurance is employer-only for employment income (5%); employee portions apply only for civil contract workers.

### Mandatory Social Medical Insurance (OSMS/FONMS)

| Category | Rate | Ceiling (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Employer | **3%** of income paid | Up to 40 × MMS/month = KZT3,400,000/month (≈USD6,500) |
| Employee | **2%** of income | Up to 20 × MMS/month = KZT1,700,000/month (≈USD3,260) |

**Employee health insurance ceiling (2026):** 20 × KZT85,000 × 12 = KZT20,400,000/year
**Deductibility:** Employee health insurance contributions deductible for PIT.

### Summary Table

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Entrepreneur (Standard Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Kazakhstan citizens engaged in individual entrepreneurship; must register with tax authorities
- **Tax base:** Annual gross business income (after adjustments and deductions) minus expenses incurred in generating income (when documented and permitted under the tax regime selected at registration)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 10% (up to 230,000 MCI/year) and 15% (above 230,000 MCI = KZT994,750,000/year) on net entrepreneurship income
- **Social obligations:** Social tax (employer equivalent), pension, health insurance, social insurance — as both employer and employee side
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if turnover exceeds KZT20,000 MCI/year (= KZT86,500,000 ≈ USD166,000); standard VAT rate 12%
- **Filing:** Must maintain books and submit annual declaration (Form 270.00)

### Option B — Individual Entrepreneur (Special Simplified Taxation Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Small individual entrepreneurs with annual income ≤ 24,038 MCI (= KZT103,964,350 ≈ USD200,000 for 2026); cannot have employees (or ≤3 employees)
- **Tax base:** Gross income (no expense deduction in simplified mode)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Special (reduced) tax rates set per regime type; historically simplified regimes used flat low rates on gross turnover
- **Note:** EY does not fully detail the 2026 simplified regime rates; Kazakhstan periodically adjusts; verify with local accountant

### Option C — Digital Nomad / Remote Worker Visas

- **Visa types available:** Digital Nomad visa (for in-demand IT professionals relocating to Kazakhstan with pathway to permanent residence); Neo-Nomad visa (for remote workers with foreign-source income)
- **Tax implication:** Remote workers with foreign-source income (Neo-Nomad) likely qualify as nonresidents if present < 183 days; foreign income may be exempt from Kazakhstan tax in that case; verify residency status annually (EY p. 773)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC) Investment Residency

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals who qualify as AIFC investment residents; must be present in Kazakhstan ≥ 90 days in any 12-month period
- **Benefit:** AIFC operates under English common law; separate tax regime and governance framework analogous to Dubai DIFC
- **Practical benefit for individuals:** AIFC investment residents have a clearly defined residency threshold; combined with the 10%/15% PIT rates and pension/health deductibility, effective tax burden can be competitive

### Foreign Nationals — Kazakhstan Tax Residency

- Foreign nationals who are tax residents (≥183 days) are taxed at resident rates (10%/15%)
- Foreign nationals who are not tax residents are taxed on Kazakhstan-source income at 10%/15% (for secondees) or 20% (other nonresidents)
- **Universal Declaration (UD) system:** From 1 January 2025, the majority of Kazakhstan's population entered the universal declaration requirement (Form 270.00) for assets and income (EY pp. 768–769)

## 2026 Changes

- **New Tax Code effective 1 January 2026:** Major overhaul; introduces progressive PIT rates (10%/15%) replacing flat 10%; new individual entrepreneurship rates; new social tax flat rate 6% (EY p. 761)
- **Progressive PIT (new):** 10% up to 8,500 MCI/year; 15% above; replaces the flat 10% that applied under the previous code (EY p. 765)
- **Basic deduction:** 30 MCI/month (KZT129,750) deductible from employment income; annual 360 MCI (KZT1,557,000) (EY p. 764)
- **Social tax flat rate 6% (from 2026):** No longer reducible by mandatory social insurance contributions; minimum tax base 14 MCI/employee (EY p. 766)
- **Health insurance ceilings updated:** Employee ceiling 20 × MMS = KZT1,700,000/month; employer ceiling 40 × MMS = KZT3,400,000/month (EY p. 767)
- **Pension ceiling:** 50 × MMS = KZT4,250,000/month (EY p. 766)
- **Social insurance ceiling:** 7 × MMS = KZT595,000/month (EY p. 767)
- **Universal Declaration (UD) expanded:** From 1 January 2025, broad population coverage; most individuals must report assets and income via Form 270.00 by 15 September following the tax year (EY pp. 768–770)
- **Capital gains new rules:** Broker commission now included in acquisition cost of shares; capital losses may now offset capital gains for listed shares (but no carryforward) (EY p. 764)
- **Digital Nomad and Neo-Nomad visas** now available (EY p. 773)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants; 2026 tax year.
> **Basic deduction: 360 MCI = KZT1,557,000/year**.
> **1 EUR ≈ KZT 565** (approximate May 2026; 2025 avg KZT521.59 = USD1; EUR/USD ≈ 1.08).
> Employment: progressive PIT (10%/15%) + mandatory pension (10%) + health insurance employee (2%).
> B2B (individual entrepreneur): same PIT rates on entrepreneurship income; pension 10% + health 2%.
> Pension and health insurance contributions are deductible from PIT taxable base.

### €30,000 Employment (KZT 16,950,000)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | €30,000 × KZT 565 | KZT 16,950,000 |
| Pension contribution (10%) | 10% × 16,950,000 (< KZT51M ceiling) | KZT 1,695,000 |
| Health insurance employee (2%) | 2% × 16,950,000 (< KZT20.4M ceiling) | KZT 339,000 |
| Basic deduction | 360 × KZT4,325 | KZT 1,557,000 |
| Total deductions | 1,695,000 + 339,000 + 1,557,000 | KZT 3,591,000 |
| Taxable income | 16,950,000 − 3,591,000 | KZT 13,359,000 |
| 8,500 MCI threshold | 8,500 × 4,325 | KZT 36,762,500 |
| Taxable income < 8,500 MCI → 10% flat | 10% × 13,359,000 | KZT 1,335,900 |
| **Total income tax** | | **KZT 1,335,900** |
| Pension (10%) | | KZT 1,695,000 |
| Health insurance (2%) | | KZT 339,000 |
| **Total burden** | 1,335,900 + 1,695,000 + 339,000 | **KZT 3,369,900** |
| **Effective rate** | 3,369,900 / 16,950,000 | **19.9%** |

### €30,000 B2B (KZT 16,950,000 gross revenue — individual entrepreneur)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue | | KZT 16,950,000 |
| Assumed business costs (20%) | | KZT 3,390,000 |

**Working for €60,000 (KZT33,900,000):**
- Deductions: pension 10% × 33,900,000 = 3,390,000; health 2% × 33,900,000 = 678,000; basic dedn 1,557,000; total = 5,625,000
- Taxable = 33,900,000 − 5,625,000 = 28,275,000 (< 8,500 MCI = 36,762,500)
- PIT: 10% × 28,275,000 = **2,827,500**; pension+health = 4,068,000; total = **6,895,500; effective 20.3%**

**Working for €100,000 (KZT56,500,000):**
- Pension: 10% × KZT51,000,000 (ceiling) = 5,100,000; health: 2% × KZT20,400,000 (ceiling) = 408,000
- Deductions: 5,100,000 + 408,000 + 1,557,000 = 7,065,000
- Taxable = 56,500,000 − 7,065,000 = 49,435,000
- PIT: 10% × 36,762,500 + 15% × (49,435,000 − 36,762,500) = 3,676,250 + 15% × 12,672,500 = 3,676,250 + 1,900,875 = **5,577,125**
- Pension + health = 5,508,000; total = **11,085,125; effective 19.6%**

The income tax itself ranges from ~8% to ~12% of gross due to deductibility of pension/health contributions and the basic deduction.

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **New Tax Code still evolving:** EY explicitly warns that Kazakhstan's tax legislation is in a state of development and subject to frequent and unpredictable changes. All rates and thresholds should be verified before making decisions.
- [ ] **Simplified regime for individual entrepreneurs:** EY does not fully detail the 2026 simplified taxation regime rates for small entrepreneurs. The standard progressive 10%/15% rates apply for entrepreneurs above the simplified threshold; verify simplified rates with local accountant.
- [ ] **Social tax base complexity:** The 6% social tax is employer-paid on gross income minus exemptions. The minimum tax base is 14 MCI/employee/month. Effective employer cost per employee is approximately (6% social tax + 5% social insurance + 3% health) = 14% of gross (up to respective ceilings), in addition to the employee salary.
- [ ] **Professional pension contributions (5%):** Employers in 17 high-risk industries (mining, oil/gas, pharma, consumer goods manufacturing) must make an additional 5% professional pension contribution. Not modeled above; applies to sector-specific employees only.
- [ ] **CFC rules:** Individual tax residents controlling foreign companies face PIT obligations on retained earnings of controlled foreign companies (CFC rules introduced under new Tax Code). Relevant for Kazakhstani entrepreneurs with offshore holding companies.
- [ ] **Universal declaration filing burden:** From 2025, most Kazakhstan residents must file Form 270.00 (declaration of income and property). This is a significant compliance change — assets held outside Kazakhstan must be reported annually.
- [ ] **Digital Nomad / Neo-Nomad visa tax treatment:** Foreign income for Neo-Nomad visa holders (remote workers with foreign-source income) is likely exempt from Kazakhstan PIT if the individual is a non-resident (< 183 days). If resident (≥183 days), worldwide income is taxable. Exact treatment requires confirmation from Kazakhstan tax authority.
