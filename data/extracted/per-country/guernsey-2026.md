## Personal Income Tax

> Guernsey has a flat personal income tax rate of 20% on income after personal allowances and deductions. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Residents taxed on worldwide income. No capital gains tax. No inheritance tax. No wealth tax. No VAT. Assessed individually from 1 January 2023.

| Taxable income (after allowances) | Rate |
|---|---|
| All taxable income | 20% |

**Personal allowance for 2026:** GBP 15,200 (2025: GBP 14,600) *(EY p. 554)*

**Tax caps (2026):**
- Worldwide income (including Guernsey source): GBP 320,000 maximum annual tax
- Non-Guernsey source income only: GBP 160,000 maximum annual tax
- New residents (open-market property, min document duty GBP 50,000): GBP 60,000 for up to 4 years

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal allowance: GBP 15,200 (2026); phased out at rate of GBP 1 per GBP 5 of assessable income above GBP 85,000 (2026)
- First GBP 2,500 of pension deductions not withdrawn (2026; 2025: GBP 2,500)
- Mortgage interest on principal private residence: deductible on interest on first GBP 400,000 of mortgage; subject to GBP 3,500 interest cap per borrower
- Pension contributions: deductible within limits
- First GBP 50 of bank/savings interest exempt

**Standard charge (2026):** GBP 50,000 (2025: GBP 40,000) — flat charge payable by individuals who are resident but not solely/principally resident; covers first GBP 250,000 of Guernsey-source income (2026)

**Notes:**
- No capital gains tax in Guernsey
- No inheritance or gift taxes
- No VAT (Guernsey does not have a VAT system)
- ITIP (Income Tax Instalment Payments) — employer deducts tax at source; similar to PAYE
- Effective 1 January 2026: repayment of shareholder loans treated as distributions (taxable at 20%) unless loan to trading company on commercial basis (EY p. 554)
- Covers Guernsey and Alderney; Sark has its own tax system

## Social Security

> Guernsey has a compulsory social security scheme with three classes: employed, self-employed, and non-employed.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security | 7.5% (2026) | 7.1% (2026) | 12.4% (2026) |
| Secondary pension (minimum) | 1.5% | 1.0% | N/A |

**Income ceiling (2026):**
- Monthly earnings limit: GBP 16,380 (GBP 196,560/year)
- Maximum annual employee contribution: GBP 14,742 (2026)
- Maximum annual employer contribution: GBP 13,956 (2026)
- Self-employed upper earnings limit: GBP 196,560/year (2026)

**Notes:**
- Employee rate: 7.5% for 2026 (was 7.4% in 2025) for persons under pension age (approx. 66)
- Employer rate: 7.1% for 2026 (was 7% in 2025)
- Self-employed rate: 12.4% for 2026 (was 12.2% in 2025)
- Non-employed rate: 11.8% for 2026 (was 11.6% in 2025) on income declared on tax return; non-employed allowance GBP 11,122 (2026)
- Persons over pension age: no employee social security contributions; health insurance contribution 3.8%
- Secondary pension: minimum total 2.5% from 1 January 2026; employer contributes at least 1% (EY p. 556–557)

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals in Guernsey pay income tax at the flat 20% rate and self-employed social security contributions at 12.4%.

### Option A — Self-Employed Sole Trader
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a trade, business, or profession in Guernsey (wholly or partly)
- **Tax base:** Net accounting profits (revenue minus business expenses); depreciation not deductible but capital allowances at 20% reducing balance on plant/machinery; buildings also eligible
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat 20% on net taxable income after personal allowance (GBP 15,200 for 2026)
- **Social security:** 12.4% on self-employment income; upper earnings limit GBP 196,560/year (max SS ~GBP 24,373/year)
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT in Guernsey
- **Key limits:** Business losses may be carried forward indefinitely; terminal losses (on cessation) may be carried back 2 years; quarterly advance payments on account; annual return due 30 November

### Option B — Standard Charge (Non-Principal Residents)
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals resident but not solely/principally resident in Guernsey
- **Tax base:** Pay standard charge GBP 50,000 (2026) — covers first GBP 250,000 of Guernsey-source income; worldwide income can then be remitted tax-free
- **Rate / mechanism:** GBP 50,000 flat payment; no personal allowances available
- **Social security:** Standard rules apply
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT
- **Key limits:** Standard charge increased significantly from GBP 40,000 to GBP 50,000 for 2026; useful for high-income individuals with primarily non-Guernsey income

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Open Market New Resident Tax Cap
- **Eligibility:** New residents of Guernsey purchasing open-market property and paying minimum GBP 50,000 in document duty
- **Benefit:** Maximum annual tax liability capped at GBP 60,000 for up to 4 years (from 2026 onwards)
- **Duration:** Up to 4 years from taking up Guernsey residence
- **Application deadline:** Cap available upon satisfying property purchase condition
- **Caveats:** Applies per person (not jointly) from January 2023; Guernsey-source land/property income excluded from cap; trivial amounts from Guernsey pension funds also excluded (EY p. 555)

### Worldwide Tax Cap
- **Eligibility:** All Guernsey tax residents
- **Benefit:** Maximum annual tax capped at GBP 320,000 on worldwide income (GBP 160,000 for non-Guernsey-source income only)
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Application deadline:** Elected annually on tax return
- **Caveats:** Guernsey-source land/property income and Guernsey pension income excluded from cap

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 552–559)

- **Personal allowance increased to GBP 15,200** (from GBP 14,600 in 2025)
- **Standard charge increased to GBP 50,000** (from GBP 40,000 in 2025) — covers GBP 250,000 of Guernsey-source income
- **Employer social security rate: 7.1%** (up from 7.0%); employee rate 7.5% (up from 7.4%)
- **Self-employed SS rate: 12.4%** (up from 12.2%)
- **Secondary pension minimum contribution: 2.5%** total from 1 January 2026 (employer at least 1%)
- **Open market new resident cap: GBP 60,000** (2026 — same as 2025)
- Shareholder loan repayments treated as taxable distributions from 1 January 2026
- Employer-provided stock options: tax point changes from 1 January 2026 to date of vesting/exercise/7 years from grant

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, solely resident in Guernsey, standard allowances only.
> Currency: GBP. 1 GBP ≈ 1.17 EUR (approximate 2025 rate). All calculations in GBP; EUR conversion approximate.
> "Employment" = gross salary. "B2B" = self-employed net revenue.

**Employment at €30,000 (≈ GBP 25,641):**
1. Less personal allowance GBP 15,200 → taxable = GBP 10,441
2. Tax = GBP 10,441 × 20% = GBP 2,088 ≈ €2,443
3. Social security: GBP 25,641 × 7.5% = GBP 1,923 ≈ €2,250
4. Total burden = €2,443 + €2,250 = €4,693
5. Effective rate = €4,693 / €30,000 = **15.6%**

**Employment at €60,000 (≈ GBP 51,282):**
1. Allowance GBP 15,200 (not yet phased out at this income level: phase-out starts GBP 85,000)
2. Taxable = GBP 51,282 − GBP 15,200 = GBP 36,082
3. Tax = GBP 36,082 × 20% = GBP 7,216 ≈ €8,443
4. SS: GBP 51,282 × 7.5% = GBP 3,846 ≈ €4,500
5. Total = €8,443 + €4,500 = €12,943
6. Effective rate = €12,943 / €60,000 = **21.6%**

**Employment at €100,000 (≈ GBP 85,470):**
1. Allowance: income = GBP 85,470 → phase-out: (GBP 85,470 − GBP 85,000) / GBP 5 × GBP 1 = GBP 94 reduction → effective allowance = GBP 15,106
2. Taxable = GBP 85,470 − GBP 15,106 = GBP 70,364
3. Tax = GBP 70,364 × 20% = GBP 14,073 ≈ €16,465
4. SS: GBP 85,470 × 7.5% = GBP 6,410 ≈ €7,500 (below ceiling)
5. Total = €16,465 + €7,500 = €23,965
6. Effective rate = €23,965 / €100,000 = **24.0%**

**B2B (self-employed) at €60,000 (≈ GBP 51,282):**
1. Business costs ~10% = GBP 5,128; net income = GBP 46,154
2. Self-employed SS: GBP 46,154 × 12.4% = GBP 5,723 ≈ €6,696
3. Taxable after allowance: GBP 46,154 − GBP 15,200 = GBP 30,954
4. Tax = GBP 30,954 × 20% = GBP 6,191 ≈ €7,243
5. Total = €7,243 + €6,696 = €13,939
6. Effective rate = €13,939 / €60,000 = **23.2%**

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €2,443 | €2,250 | €4,693 | **15.6%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€2,156 | ~€2,981 | ~€5,137 | **~17.1%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €8,443 | €4,500 | €12,943 | **21.6%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | €7,243 | €6,696 | €13,939 | **23.2%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €16,465 | €7,500 | €23,965 | **24.0%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€15,200 | ~€11,400 | ~€26,600 | **~26.6%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Solely resident in Guernsey; single, no dependants
- Personal allowance GBP 15,200 (2026); phase-out starts GBP 85,000 assessable income
- Employee SS: 7.5% (2026); employer pays additional 7.1% (not shown in employee burden)
- Self-employed SS: 12.4% (2026)
- B2B: 10% assumed business costs; SS paid on net self-employment income
- No VAT in Guernsey
- No capital gains tax, no inheritance tax
- GBP/EUR ≈ 1.17 (approximate)
- Tax cap (GBP 320,000 worldwide) not relevant at these income levels
