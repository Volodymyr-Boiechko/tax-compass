## Personal Income Tax

Flat 20% income tax for residents on worldwide income. No capital gains tax. No inheritance or gift tax. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Individuals separately assessed from 1 January 2023. (EY p.552, p.555–556)

**Residency:** Resident if: (a) spend ≥91 days in Guernsey in the fiscal year; OR (b) spend ≥35 days in Guernsey in the year and have spent ≥365 days in Guernsey in the preceding 4 years. "Solely resident" if resident in Guernsey and not resident elsewhere. "Principally resident" if: spend ≥182 days/year in Guernsey; OR spend ≥91 days/year AND spent ≥730 days in preceding 4 years; OR take up permanent residence in Guernsey. (EY p.552)

**Flat PIT rate:** 20% on all taxable income after deductions and allowances. (EY p.555)

**Savings income:** Investment income (dividends, interest, royalties, rental income) included in taxable income at 20%. First GBP 50 of bank savings interest exempt. (EY p.553)

**Capital gains:** NOT taxed in Guernsey. (EY p.556)

**Other income types:**
- Dividends from Guernsey companies: taxable at 20% if underlying profits were subject to Guernsey tax; tax credit given for tax suffered by distributing company.
- Non-taxable: dividends from companies not subject to Guernsey tax; savings income not subject to tax; capital gains from any source.
- Rental income from Guernsey property: taxable at 20%.
- Interest from non-Guernsey-resident payer: credit available for tax paid at source in another jurisdiction.
- Pension income from state pensions: not taxable.
- Pension income from approved schemes: 2.5% on pension payments.
- Income from employment exercised outside Guernsey by Guernsey-resident company employees: exempt if duties carried on outside Guernsey.

**Personal allowances — 2026:** (EY p.554–555)
- Personal allowance: **GBP 15,200** (2025: GBP 14,600).
- Withdrawal of allowances: GBP 1 withdrawn for every GBP 5 of assessable income above GBP 85,000 (2026) until fully withdrawn.
- First GBP 2,500 of pension deductions not withdrawn (even for higher earners).

**Other deductions and allowances — 2026:** (EY p.554)
- Mortgage interest on principal private residence: limited to interest on first GBP 400,000 of mortgage; interest cap per borrower GBP 3,500 (subject to withdrawal for higher earners).
- Pension contributions: up to 20% of earned income or GBP 35,000 (whichever is lower) for personal pension/retirement annuity contracts; first GBP 2,500 not withdrawn even for higher earners.
- Business losses: carried forward indefinitely for offset against income at same rate; terminal losses (at cessation) may be carried back two years. (EY p.555)
- Deductible business expenses: wholly, exclusively incurred for business. Depreciation not deductible; capital allowances at 20% reducing balance (buildings also eligible). (EY p.554–555)

**Tax caps — 2026:** (EY p.555)
- **Standard tax cap:** GBP 320,000 (2025: GBP 320,000) on worldwide income.
- **Non-Guernsey-source income cap:** GBP 160,000 (2026 and 2025) on non-Guernsey income + standard Guernsey income tax.
- **New open-market cap:** GBP 60,000 (2026 and 2025) for up to 4 years for new residents purchasing open-market property with minimum GBP 50,000 document duty. (EY p.555)
- **Alderney cap (from 1 January 2025 for new residents):** GBP 60,000 for new residents of Alderney from 1 January 2025 onward.
- These caps mean very high earners pay no more than GBP 320,000/year in Guernsey income tax.

**Standard charge — 2026:** GBP 50,000 (2025: GBP 40,000) for individuals resident but not solely/principally resident in Guernsey; pays tax on Guernsey-source income on first GBP 250,000 (2025: GBP 200,000). (EY p.555)

**Notes:**
- No capital gains tax, inheritance tax, gift tax, or estate duty in Guernsey. (EY p.556)
- From 1 January 2026: repayments of shareholder loans will be treated as distributions (taxable), unless loan is to a trading company on commercial basis. (EY p.554)
- Stock options: from 1 January 2026, tax point for awards is date of vesting, exercise, or 7 years from grant (whichever earlier); previously at grant. (EY p.553)

## Social Security

Guernsey has a compulsory social security scheme with three classes. (EY p.556)

**Employed — 2026 rates:**

| Contribution | Rate | Monthly earnings limit (GBP/month) | Annual cap (GBP/year) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employer | 7.1% (2025: 7%) | GBP 16,380 | GBP 13,956 |
| Employee (under pension age, ~66) | 7.5% (2025: 7.4%) | GBP 16,380 | GBP 14,742 |
| Employee (pension age and over) | — | — | — |

**Self-employed — 2026:**
- Rate: 12.4% (2025: 12.2%) on self-employment income.
- Upper earnings limit: GBP 196,560 (2025: GBP 188,640)/year.
- Under AB system allowance: GBP 432 deductible.

**Non-employed (including high earners over pension age) — 2026:**
- Rate: 11.8% (2025: 11.6%) on income declared on tax return.
- Non-employed allowance: GBP 11,122 (2026).
- For individuals over pension age: 3.8% (health insurance contribution) on taxable income minus non-employed allowance.
- Upper earnings limit: GBP 196,560 (2026).

**Secondary pension (from 1 January 2026):**
- All employers must auto-enroll employees; overall minimum contribution 2.5% (employer ≥1%, may contribute employee share of 1.5%). (EY p.556–557)

**Totalization agreements:** Austria, Barbados, Bermuda, Canada, Chile, Cyprus, France, Ireland, Isle of Man, Italy, Jamaica, Jersey, Korea (South), Latvia, Malta, Netherlands, New Zealand, Portugal, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, United Kingdom, United States. (EY p.556)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment
- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals carrying on a trade, business or profession in Guernsey or partly in Guernsey.
- **Tax base:** Taxable income = accounting profits, subject to adjustments. All expenses wholly, exclusively incurred for business deductible; capital allowances at 20% reducing balance. (EY p.553–554)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat 20% on net profit after personal allowance (GBP 15,200 for 2026).
- **Social security:** 12.4% on self-employment income up to GBP 196,560/year upper limit.
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT in Guernsey (Guernsey has its own Document Duty and IS a Crown Dependency not part of EU VAT area).
- **Key limits:** Business losses carried forward indefinitely; terminal losses carried back 2 years. Personal allowance withdrawal starts at GBP 85,000 assessable income.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Open-Market Tax Cap for New Residents
- **Eligibility:** New residents of Guernsey who purchase open-market property and pay minimum GBP 50,000 in document duty.
- **Benefit:** Tax payable capped at GBP 60,000/year (2026) for up to 4 years.
- **Duration:** Up to 4 years from becoming new resident.
- **Caveats:** Conditions must be met; income from Guernsey land/property excluded from cap (still taxable at 20%). (EY p.555)

### Standard Charge Option
- **Eligibility:** Individuals who are resident but not solely or principally resident in Guernsey.
- **Benefit:** Pay GBP 50,000 standard charge (2026); taxed on Guernsey-source income on first GBP 250,000 only; may then make tax-free remittances to Guernsey.
- **Duration:** Ongoing while conditions met.
- **Caveats:** Not entitled to personal allowances or reliefs; Guernsey-source bank interest deemed non-Guernsey source for this purpose. (EY p.555–556)

## 2026 Changes

- **Personal allowance increase for 2026:** GBP 15,200 (up from GBP 14,600 in 2025). (EY p.554)
- **Standard charge increase for 2026:** GBP 50,000 (up from GBP 40,000 in 2025). (EY p.555)
- **Worldwide income tax cap 2026:** GBP 320,000 (unchanged from 2025). (EY p.555)
- **Non-Guernsey income cap 2026:** GBP 160,000 (unchanged). (EY p.555)
- **Secondary pension auto-enrollment from 1 January 2026:** All employers must auto-enroll employees; minimum contribution 2.5%. (EY p.556–557)
- **Social security rates for 2026:** Employee 7.5% (up from 7.4%); employer 7.1% (up from 7%); self-employed 12.4% (up from 12.2%); non-employed 11.8% (up from 11.6%). (EY p.556)
- **Shareholder loan repayments (from 1 January 2026):** Treated as distributions unless to a trading company on commercial basis. (EY p.554)
- **Stock option tax point (from 1 January 2026):** Date of vesting/exercise/7 years from grant (earlier of). (EY p.553)
- **Alderney cap (new residents from 1 January 2025):** GBP 60,000 cap for new Alderney residents purchasing qualifying property. (EY p.555)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants; personal allowance GBP 15,200 (2026).
> GBP/EUR rate: 1 GBP ≈ EUR 1.163 (approximate 2025; 1 EUR ≈ GBP 0.86).
> "Employment" = gross salary; employee SS 7.5% (capped at GBP 16,380/month earnings limit = GBP 14,742/year max); 20% flat tax after personal allowance.
> "B2B" = self-employment net profit; self-employed SS 12.4% (capped at GBP 196,560 annual upper limit); 20% flat tax after personal allowance.

**EUR to GBP conversions:**
- €30,000 ≈ GBP 25,800
- €60,000 ≈ GBP 51,600
- €100,000 ≈ GBP 86,000

### Working: GBP 25,800 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (7.5%, cap GBP 14,742/yr) | 7.5% × 25,800 = 1,935 (< cap) | GBP 1,935 |
| PIT base | 25,800 − 15,200 | GBP 10,600 |
| Income tax (20%) | 20% × 10,600 | GBP 2,120 |
| **Total burden** | **1,935 + 2,120** | **GBP 4,055** |
| **Effective rate** | **4,055 / 25,800** | **~15.7%** |

### Working: GBP 25,800 (≈ €30,000) B2B Self-Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Self-employed SS (12.4%) | 12.4% × 25,800 | GBP 3,199 |
| PIT base | 25,800 − 15,200 | GBP 10,600 |
| Income tax (20%) | 20% × 10,600 | GBP 2,120 |
| **Total burden** | **3,199 + 2,120** | **GBP 5,319** |
| **Effective rate** | **5,319 / 25,800** | **~20.6%** |

### Summary Table

| Gross income | EUR equiv | Scenario | Income tax (20%) | Social security | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| GBP 25,800 | €30,000 | Employment | GBP 2,120 | GBP 1,935 | GBP 4,055 | **~15.7%** |
| GBP 25,800 | €30,000 | B2B self-employment | GBP 2,120 | GBP 3,199 | GBP 5,319 | **~20.6%** |
| GBP 51,600 | €60,000 | Employment | GBP 7,280 | GBP 3,870 (est.) | GBP 11,150 | **~21.6%** |
| GBP 51,600 | €60,000 | B2B self-employment | GBP 7,280 | GBP 6,398 | GBP 13,678 | **~26.5%** |
| GBP 86,000 | €100,000 | Employment | GBP 14,160 | GBP 6,434 (est.) | GBP 20,594 | **~23.9%** |
| GBP 86,000 | €100,000 | B2B self-employment | GBP 14,160 | GBP 10,664 | GBP 24,824 | **~28.9%** |

**Working — GBP 51,600 Employment:**
- SS: 7.5% × 51,600 = 3,870 (under annual max GBP 14,742; monthly earnings would be GBP 4,300 < GBP 16,380 monthly limit)
- PIT base: 51,600 − 15,200 = 36,400 (no allowance withdrawal below GBP 85,000)
- Tax: 20% × 36,400 = GBP 7,280

**Working — GBP 86,000 Employment:**
- SS: GBP 86,000 annual → monthly GBP 7,167 < GBP 16,380 cap → 7.5% × 86,000 = 6,450; but annual employee max is GBP 14,742 → not yet capped → GBP 6,450
- PIT base: 86,000 − 15,200 = 70,800 (income GBP 86,000 > GBP 85,000 withdrawal threshold → GBP 1 withdrawn per GBP 5 over threshold → (86,000−85,000)/5 = GBP 200 withdrawn → allowance = 15,200−200 = 15,000)
- Revised PIT base: 86,000 − 15,000 = 71,000 → Tax: 20% × 71,000 = GBP 14,200

**Key insight:** Guernsey's flat 20% rate combined with the GBP 15,200 personal allowance results in very competitive effective rates (15.7% employment at €30k; 23.9% employment at €100k). No capital gains tax, no inheritance tax, and the tax caps (GBP 320,000 maximum) make Guernsey extremely attractive for high earners. The open-market cap (GBP 60,000/year for 4 years) for new residents purchasing qualifying property provides further relief.

**Assumptions:**
- GBP/EUR ≈ 0.86 (approximate; floating rate)
- 2026 personal allowance GBP 15,200 applied
- Employment SS: 7.5% (2026 rate); no monthly earnings cap binding for these income levels (monthly earnings < GBP 16,380)
- B2B SS: 12.4% (2026 rate) on net self-employment income; upper limit GBP 196,560 not binding at these levels
- No tax caps modeled (caps primarily relevant for very high earners)
- Pension contributions, mortgage interest, and other deductions not modeled; would reduce effective rates
