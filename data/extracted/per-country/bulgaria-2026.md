## Personal Income Tax

Flat 10% PIT for residents on worldwide income; nonresidents on Bulgarian-source income.
Tax year = calendar year. Annual tax return due by **30 April** of the following year.
Early filing discount: 5% discount (max BGN 500) for online filing and payment by 31 March.

**Currency note:** Bulgaria adopted the **euro on 1 January 2026** at the fixed rate of **BGN 1.95583 = EUR 1**.
The BGN/lev ceased to be legal tender from 1 February 2026. All figures below state BGN for historical/EY reference and EUR for 2026 onwards.

**PIT rates** (EY p.202):

| Income type | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employment income | 10% | Flat on gross after deductions |
| Self-employment / business income | 10% | After applicable expense deduction |
| Sole entrepreneur (едноличен търговец) | 15% | Taxed as corporation under Corporate Tax Act |
| Rental income | 10% | After 10% flat expense deduction (= 10% × 90% gross) |
| Interest income | 10% | Annual basis |
| Dividends / liquidation distributions (resident entities) | 5% | Withholding, final |
| Capital gains — shares via Bulgarian/EU/EEA exchange | Exempt | (EY p.200–201) |
| Capital gains — other securities | 10% | Annual regularization; 10% deduction from gain |
| Capital gains — real estate (primary residence held ≥ 3 years) | Exempt | |
| Capital gains — up to 2 additional real estate properties held ≥ 5 years | Exempt | |
| Foreign-source dividends (Bulgarian tax residents) | 5% | (EY p.200) |

**Expense deductions for self-employment / business income** (EY p.200):
- The fixed deduction rate depends on activity type:

| Activity type | Fixed expense deduction | Effective PIT on gross |
|---|---|---|
| Free professions / liberal professions (свободна професия): lawyers, doctors, architects, consultants, artists, etc. | 25% | 7.5% (10% × 75%) |

## Social Security

**Employment — rates effective from 1 January 2021** (normal work conditions, born after 1 January 1960) (EY p.203):

| Contribution | Employer % | Employee % | Total % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension Fund | 8.22% | 6.58% | 14.80% |
| Illness and Maternity Fund | 2.10% | 1.40% | 3.50% |
| Accident and Occupational Disease Fund | 0.4–1.1%* | 0.00% | 0.4–1.1% |
| Additional mandatory pension (born after 1 Jan 1960) | 2.80% | 2.20% | 5.00% |
| Unemployment Fund | 0.60% | 0.40% | 1.00% |
| Health insurance | 4.80% | 3.20% | 8.00% |
| **Total (mid-range accident)** | **~19.22%** | **~13.78%** | **~33.00%** |

*Accident fund rate 0.4%–1.1% depending on industry classification (EY p.203 footnote).*

**Monthly social security assessment base cap (employment):** BGN 4,130/month (≈ EUR 2,112 from 2026). (EY p.203)

**Self-employed persons (свободна професия and freelancers):**
- Pay contributions on a self-chosen monthly income base between the minimum and maximum
- Minimum monthly base (2025): BGN 1,077 → in EUR from 2026: **EUR 550.66/month**
- Maximum monthly base (2025/2026): BGN 4,130 → in EUR from 2026: **EUR 2,111.64/month** (EY p.203)
- Contributions recalculated annually based on actual income received (EY p.203)
- Total contribution rate: **27.8%** (basic coverage, without general sickness and maternity leave) or
  **31.3%** (full coverage including sickness/maternity leave) (EY p.203)

**Breakdown of self-employed contribution rate (~27.8% basic):**
- Pension: 14.80% (8.22% employer share + 6.58% employee share — self-employed pay both sides)
- Additional mandatory pension (born after 1960): 5.00%
- Health insurance: 8.00%
- Total: **27.8%** (excluding sickness/maternity and accident funds)

**Key note:** Self-employed do NOT pay unemployment contributions (0% — EY p.203).

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Свободна Професия (Free Professional / Liberal Profession)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals providing services in qualifying liberal professions including:
  lawyers, notaries, doctors, dentists, pharmacists, architects, engineers, accountants, translators,
  journalists, artists, IT consultants, management consultants, and other self-employed service
  providers. Must register as a self-employed individual (not sole entrepreneur) with the NRA.
- **Tax base:** Gross income from services minus **25% flat expense deduction** (no receipts required).
  Net taxable income = 75% of gross revenue. (EY p.200)
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - Income tax: **10%** on 75% of gross revenue = **7.5% effective rate on gross**
  - Mandatory social security contributions deductible from tax base
  - Advance tax payments: 10% quarterly by end of month following quarter end (EY p.204)
- **Social security:**
  - Self-declared base between EUR 550.66/month minimum and EUR 2,111.64/month maximum (2026)
  - Total rate: 27.8% (basic) or 31.3% (full coverage with sickness/maternity)
  - Annual minimum contributions: 27.8% × EUR 550.66 × 12 = **EUR 1,836.80/year** (basic)
  - Annual maximum contributions: 27.8% × EUR 2,111.64 × 12 = **EUR 7,041.49/year** (basic)
  - Social security contributions ARE deductible from the income tax base
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory VAT registration at BGN 100,000 (≈ EUR 51,130) annual turnover;
  standard VAT rate 20%.
- **Key limits:**
  - The 25% deduction is fixed and automatic — no documentation of actual expenses required
  - Copyright royalties (another common category) benefit from a 40% deduction instead of 25%
  - Free professional activity does NOT qualify for the sole entrepreneur 15% CIT rate

---

### Option B — Едноличен Търговец (Sole Entrepreneur / Sole Trader)

- **Who qualifies:** Natural persons registered as a sole entrepreneur (ЕТ) in the Bulgarian
  Commercial Register.
- **Tax base:** Net profit after actual deductible business expenses (accounting-based).
- **Rate / mechanism:** **15%** corporate income tax rate on net profit (taxed under Corporate
  Tax Act, not Income Tax Act). Advance quarterly payments required. (EY p.202, 204)
- **Dividend extraction:** After-tax profits extracted as dividends subject to 5% dividend withholding.
  Combined rate: 15% CIT + 5% on remainder = ~19.25% total on pre-tax profit.
- **Social security:** Same regime as free professionals — self-chosen base within EUR 550.66–EUR 2,111.64/month.
- **Key limits:** More complex administration; primarily used for trading/production businesses.

## Currency and EUR Adoption

**Bulgaria joined the eurozone on 1 January 2026** — this is fully confirmed. (ECB press release 8 July 2025; EU Council)

| Timeline | Status |
|---|---|
| BGN/EUR fixed rate | 1 BGN = 0.511293 EUR (i.e., EUR 1 = BGN 1.95583) |
| Dual circulation (BGN and EUR) | 1 January – 31 January 2026 |
| BGN ceased as legal tender | 1 February 2026 |
| BGN banknote exchange (free of charge) | Until 30 June 2026 at banks and post offices |
| Bulgaria became 21st eurozone member | 1 January 2026 |

**Tax impact:** From 1 January 2026, all Bulgarian tax amounts are denominated in EUR. The fixed rate BGN 1.95583 = EUR 1 directly converts all prior BGN thresholds. For example:
- Social security monthly cap BGN 4,130 = **EUR 2,112**
- Self-employed minimum base BGN 1,077 = **EUR 551**
- Basic child deduction BGN 6,000 = **EUR 3,067**

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No General Expat Preferential Regime

Bulgaria does not have a formal newcomer/expat PIT exemption regime (unlike Portugal's NHR, Estonia's e-Residency, etc.). The flat 10% rate already makes Bulgaria one of the lowest-tax EU jurisdictions for individuals.

### Non-EU Resident Capital Investment

- **Eligibility:** Large investors can obtain prolonged/permanent residence permits based on significant
  investment in Bulgaria. (EY p.209)
- **Benefit:** Right to reside and work; Bulgarian citizenship available for certain investment levels.
- **Caveats:** Tax residency follows physical presence (183+ days) not investment status.

## 2026 Changes

- **Euro adoption (1 January 2026):** Most significant change. All taxes, contributions, and
  thresholds converted at BGN 1.95583 = EUR 1. (ECB; EU Council, July 2025)
- **BGN ceased legal tender from 1 February 2026.** Dual circulation January only.
- **Child benefit enhancement (2025, still in effect):** Child deductions raised to BGN 6,000/child
  per year (for up to 3 minor children) and BGN 12,000 for children with disabilities, from 2025.
  (EY p.201)
- **Self-employed minimum base in EUR (2026):** EUR 550.66/month (= BGN 1,077); maximum EUR 2,111.64/month.
  Further adjustments may follow after 2026 Bulgarian state budget adoption.
- **PIT rate: NO change** — flat 10% maintained; dividend tax remains 5%.
- **Sole entrepreneur rate: NO change** — 15% CIT rate maintained.
- **Expense deductions: NO change** — 25% for free professions, 40% for copyright, 60% for agriculture.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants. Bulgaria uses EUR from 1 January 2026 (BGN no longer in use).
> "Employment" = gross salary; employee-side only (employer contributions not shown separately in gross).
> "Free Professional" = свободна професия with 25% expense deduction; 10% PIT on 75% of gross.
> Social security for free professional at actual income base (not minimum) when income exceeds minimum.
> All figures in EUR for 2026. EUR/BGN = 1.95583 fixed rate.

### Working: €30,000 Employment (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee social security (13.78%) | 13.78% × 30,000 | EUR 4,134 |
| SS cap check | 30,000 / 12 = EUR 2,500/month > EUR 2,112 cap | Cap applies |
| Employee SS (capped): 13.78% × (EUR 2,112 × 12 = EUR 25,344) | 13.78% × 25,344 | EUR 3,492 |
| PIT base | 30,000 − 3,492 | EUR 26,508 |
| PIT at 10% | 10% × 26,508 | EUR 2,651 |
| **Total burden** | 3,492 + 2,651 | **EUR 6,143** |
| **Effective rate** | 6,143 / 30,000 | **20.5%** |

*Monthly salary €2,500 is just above the monthly SS cap of EUR 2,112 — cap applies to SS only.*

### Working: €30,000 Free Professional (25% deduction, 2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 25% expense deduction | 25% × 30,000 | EUR 7,500 |
| Net taxable income | 75% × 30,000 | EUR 22,500 |
| Social security base: actual income basis | EUR 2,500/month > EUR 2,112 cap | Use cap: EUR 2,112/month |
| Social security (27.8% × 12 × EUR 2,112) | 27.8% × 25,344 | EUR 7,046 |
| SS deductible from PIT base | EUR 7,046 | |
| PIT base | 22,500 − 7,046 | EUR 15,454 |
| PIT at 10% | 10% × 15,454 | EUR 1,545 |
| **Total burden** | 7,046 + 1,545 | **EUR 8,591** |
| **Effective rate** | 8,591 / 30,000 | **28.6%** |

*Note: At €30k, free professional effective rate (28.6%) is HIGHER than employment (20.5%) because the self-employed person pays BOTH sides of social security. However, EY p.203 states the rate is 27.8–31.3% — this is the total self-employed contribution rate combining employer + employee shares. Employment effective rate is lower because the employer pays their social security share on top of gross salary (not visible in take-home comparison).*

### Comparison at minimum SS base (free professional choosing minimum):

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 25% deduction | 25% × 30,000 | EUR 7,500 |
| Net taxable income | EUR 22,500 | |
| SS at minimum base (EUR 550.66/month × 12) | 27.8% × 6,607.92 | EUR 1,837 |
| PIT base | 22,500 − 1,837 | EUR 20,663 |
| PIT at 10% | 10% × 20,663 | EUR 2,066 |
| **Total burden (minimum SS)** | 1,837 + 2,066 | **EUR 3,903** |
| **Effective rate (minimum SS)** | 3,903 / 30,000 | **13.0%** |

*If the free professional chooses minimum SS base (EUR 551/month), effective rate drops to just 13.0%. This is the key planning lever: Bulgaria allows self-employed persons to choose their own SS base. The tradeoff is lower future pension entitlement. At minimum SS, income tax is ~7.5% and SS is only ~EUR 1,837/year regardless of revenue.*

### Working: €60,000 Free Professional (25% deduction, minimum SS base, 2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 25% deduction | 25% × 60,000 | EUR 15,000 |
| Net taxable income | EUR 45,000 | |
| SS at minimum base | 27.8% × (EUR 550.66 × 12) | EUR 1,837 |
| PIT base | 45,000 − 1,837 | EUR 43,163 |

| **Total burden** | 1,837 + 4,316 | **EUR 6,153** |
| **Effective rate** | 6,153 / 60,000 | **10.3%** |

### Working: €60,000 Employment (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |

*Employment cap benefit is significant: SS capped at EUR 2,112/month regardless of higher salary.*

### Summary table

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (EUR) | SS (EUR) | Total (EUR) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | 2,651 | 3,492 | 6,143 | **20.5%** |
| €30,000 | Free professional (minimum SS) | 2,066 | 1,837 | 3,903 | **13.0%** |
| €30,000 | Free professional (capped SS) | 1,545 | 7,046 | 8,591 | **28.6%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | 5,651 | 3,492 | 9,143 | **15.2%** |
| €60,000 | Free professional (minimum SS) | 4,316 | 1,837 | 6,153 | **10.3%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | 9,651 | 3,492 | 13,143 | **13.1%** |
| €100,000 | Free professional (minimum SS) | 7,316 | 1,837 | 9,153 | **9.2%** |

**Key insights:**

1. **Free professional at minimum SS base is the most tax-efficient structure in the EU for high earners:** 9.2% effective rate at €100k is extraordinary — this is the combination of 10% flat PIT on 75% of gross (7.5% PIT effective) plus only EUR 1,837 in SS contributions (minimum base, fully fixed regardless of income above EUR 550/month).

2. **Employment becomes more attractive at lower incomes vs. free professional with actual SS:** At €30k with SS at actual income, employment (20.5%) beats free professional (28.6%). This is because employed persons pay only employee-side SS (13.78%) while self-employed pay both sides (27.8%).

3. **The SS base choice is the key planning decision:** By choosing minimum SS (EUR 551/month), the free professional's total burden drops dramatically. The tradeoff is lower future state pension rights. Many Bulgarian freelancers choose minimum SS for tax efficiency.

4. **Bulgaria's flat 10% PIT with 25% automatic deduction creates a uniquely low-tax environment** for professional services — one of the lowest in the EU alongside Estonia (0% retained) and Romania (15% micro-SRL, but that's a company structure).

5. **Dividend tax (5%):** After-tax profits from Bulgarian companies distributed as dividends are subject to just 5% withholding — among the lowest in the EU.

**Assumptions:**
- All figures in EUR (Bulgaria joined eurozone 1 January 2026; BGN 1.95583 = EUR 1)
- Social security monthly cap: EUR 2,112 (= BGN 4,130 / 1.95583); annual capped base = EUR 25,344
- Self-employed minimum base: EUR 550.66/month; maximum EUR 2,111.64/month
- Employee SS rate 13.78% used (mid-range accident fund 0.75%): pension 6.58% + maternity 1.40% + additional pension 2.20% + unemployment 0.40% + health 3.20% = 13.78%
- Free professional (свободна професия): 25% expense deduction, 10% PIT, self-employed SS 27.8% (basic coverage)
- No personal deductions beyond SS contributions deductibility modeled
- Copyright/royalties: 40% deduction option not shown (even more favorable: 6% effective PIT on gross)
