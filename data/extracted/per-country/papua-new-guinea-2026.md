## Personal Income Tax

Salary and Wages Tax (SWT) system. Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents taxed on PNG-source income only. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). SWT withheld by employers is generally a final tax for employees with employment income only. Self-employed and individuals with other income must file an annual income tax return by 28 February of the following year. PNG Internal Revenue Commission (IRC) administers. (EY p.1204)

**Resident PIT brackets — effective 1 January 2025** (EY p.1206)

| Taxable income (PGK) | Tax on lower amount | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 20,000 | 0 | 0% |
| 20,001 – 33,000 | 0 | 30% |
| 33,001 – 70,000 | 3,900 | 35% |
| 70,001 – 250,000 | 16,850 | 40% |
| Over 250,000 | 88,850 | 42% |

**Non-resident PIT brackets — effective 1 January 2025** (EY p.1207)

| Taxable income (PGK) | Tax on lower amount | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 33,000 | 0 | 30% |
| 33,001 – 70,000 | 9,900 | 35% |
| 70,001 – 250,000 | 22,850 | 40% |
| Over 250,000 | 94,850 | 42% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- No general personal allowance for non-residents (first PGK 33,000 taxed at 30%)
- Residents: PGK 0–20,000 tax-free (0% bracket)
- Business expenses: deductible against business income (not employment income)
- Some employment-related expenses may be "rebated" through the expense rebate system
- No standard deduction for employment; work-related expenses may be rebatable

**Notes:**
- Capital gains: generally NOT subject to PNG tax; however, disposal of a capital asset as part of a profit-making scheme OR as part of the ordinary course of business IS taxable as ordinary income (EY p.1206)
- Dividends paid to residents/non-residents: 15% dividend withholding tax (final unless expressly exempt) (EY p.1205)
- Interest, royalties, rental: included in assessable income; taxed at progressive rates
- Non-cash benefits: taxed at concessional rates or exempt depending on nature (EY p.1205)
- Education levy: PNG imposes an education levy; the rate is typically 2% on salary/wages (verify current rate)
- Employer-provided housing: often provided tax-free or at concessional values for resources-sector employees

## Social Security

Papua New Guinea's Superannuation (General Provision) Act 2000 governs mandatory retirement contributions. (EY p.1207)

| Contribution | PNG Citizen Employee | PNG Citizen Employer | Non-citizen Employee | Non-citizen Employer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Superannuation (Authorised Superannuation Fund — ASF) | 6% of gross ordinary time earnings | 8.4% of gross ordinary time earnings | Optional | Optional |
| **Total** | **6%** | **8.4%** | **Optional** | **Optional** |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling on superannuation contributions (based on gross ordinary time earnings).

**Notes:**
- Compulsory only for PNG-citizen employees working for employers with 15+ employees who have employed the worker for 3+ months (EY p.1207)
- Non-citizen employees: contributions currently OPTIONAL (EY p.1207); however, HDMF or similar requirements may apply — verify current status
- The 6% employee contribution is deducted from post-tax salary and remitted by employer
- The 8.4% employer contribution is in addition to the gross salary (not deducted from employee)
- No contributions to non-resident superannuation funds are tax-deductible for employers
- No unemployment insurance; no government health insurance levy

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual / Self-Employed

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a business in PNG (either as a sole trader or in a partnership).
- **Tax base:** Net profit (assessable income minus deductible business expenses). Losses carried forward available (indefinitely for qualifying businesses).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive PIT brackets as employment income (0%–42% for residents; 30%–42% for non-residents). Must file annual income tax return by 28 February.
- **Social security:** No mandatory superannuation for self-employed; optional ASF participation.
- **VAT obligations:** Register for GST (10% standard rate) with the IRC if taxable turnover exceeds PGK 250,000/year. Quarterly or monthly filing.
- **Key limits:** Records must be maintained. Provisional tax (advance payments) required during the year based on prior year liability.

### Option B — Partnership

- **Who qualifies:** Two or more individuals in partnership.
- **Tax base:** Each partner taxed on their share of partnership income; partnership files informational return.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Partner's share included in individual income at progressive rates.
- **Social security:** Same as individual self-employed.
- **VAT obligations:** Same GST rules (PGK 250,000 threshold).
- **Key limits:** Partnership income fully flows to individual partners for tax purposes.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No formal preferential PIT regime for expats

PNG does not offer tax holidays or reduced rates for new residents or inbound expatriates. Expatriates are subject to the non-resident PIT brackets (no 0% band — 30% from first kina). (EY p.1204)

### Resources Sector Concessions

- Long-term assignments in mining, petroleum, and LNG sectors often involve employer-provided housing, flights, and living allowances — some exempt or concessionally taxed. Not a formal PIT regime but practically significant for expatriate packages.

### Double Tax Treaties

PNG has a limited treaty network: Australia, Canada, China, Fiji, Indonesia, Korea (South), Malaysia, New Zealand, Singapore, United Kingdom. (EY p.1208)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, resident taxpayer.
> PGK/EUR rate: 1 EUR = PGK 4.00 (approximate 2025; PGK ~3.70–4.00/USD × 1.08).
> Employment: progressive PIT only (SWT is final tax for employment-only earners).
> Self-employed: same progressive PIT + education levy ~2% (employment; may vary for SE).
> Superannuation 6% (employee): deducted but not tax-deductible against PIT base here.

### Working: PGK 120,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Superannuation (6%) | 6% × 120,000 (post-tax deduction; not PIT-deductible) | PGK 7,200 |
| Taxable income | 120,000 | |
| PIT: 0–20,000 @ 0% | | PGK 0 |
| PIT: 20,001–33,000 @ 30% | 30% × 13,000 | PGK 3,900 |
| PIT: 33,001–70,000 @ 35% | 35% × 37,000 | PGK 12,950 |
| PIT: 70,001–120,000 @ 40% | 40% × 50,000 | PGK 20,000 |
| Total PIT | | PGK 36,850 |
| **Total burden (PIT + super)** | **36,850 + 7,200** | **PGK 44,050** |
| **Effective rate (PIT only)** | **36,850 / 120,000** | **30.7%** |
| **Effective rate (all-in)** | **44,050 / 120,000** | **36.7%** |

| Gross income | PGK equiv | Scenario | Income tax | Superannuation (employee 6%) | Total | **Effective rate (all-in)** |

| PGK 400,000 | €100,000 | Self-employed | PGK 156,050 | PGK 0 | PGK 156,050 | **39.0%** |

**PIT working — PGK 240,000:**
- 0–20,000: 0; 20,001–33,000 @ 30% = 3,900; 33,001–70,000 @ 35% = 12,950; 70,001–240,000 @ 40% = 68,000
- Total = PGK 84,850 (not 88,850 — correction: 70,001–240,000 = 169,999 × 40% = 68,000; 3,900+12,950+68,000 = 84,850)

**PIT working — PGK 400,000:**
- 0–20,000: 0; 20,001–33,000 @ 30% = 3,900; 33,001–70,000 @ 35% = 12,950; 70,001–250,000 @ 40% = 72,000; 250,001–400,000 @ 42% = 63,000
- Total = PGK 151,850

**Key insight:** Papua New Guinea has one of the highest income tax burdens in the Pacific. The 42% top rate (above PGK 250,000 ≈ €62,500) combined with the 6% mandatory superannuation creates all-in effective rates of 37–45% at €30k–€100k. This is remarkably high for a developing economy and places PNG among the highest-burden jurisdictions in its region. The 0% band only covers PGK 20,000 (≈€5,000). Non-residents face an even harsher regime with 30% from the first kina.

**Assumptions:**
- PGK/EUR = 4.00 (approximate 2025)
- Resident taxpayer; full-year presence
- Superannuation 6%: shown as a burden (reduces take-home) but is paid to individual's retirement account
- No education levy modeled separately (often ~2% — would add to burden)
- No expense deductions for employment income
