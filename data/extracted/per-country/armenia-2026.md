## Personal Income Tax

Flat 20% PIT on worldwide income for residents; Armenian-source income only for non-residents. Tax year = calendar year. Residents: 183+ days or centre of vital interests in Armenia. (EY p.52)

From 1 January 2023, a flat 20% rate replaced the previous progressive scale. (EY p.56)

**PIT rates:**

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employment income (general) | **20%** |
| Employment in high-tech occupations (government-approved list, scientific R&D) | **10%** |
| Royalties | **10%** |
| Property lease income (total ≤ AMD 60 million/year) | **10%** |
| Property lease income (excess over AMD 60 million/year) | **10%** additional on excess |
| Interest from bank deposits / debt securities (public offering) | **10%** |
| Interest from Armenian treasury bonds / state securities | **0%** (exempt) |
| Other interest income | **20%** |
| Sale of property to tax agents | **10%** |
| Dividends | **5%** |
| Dividends/income from stocks/bonds listed on Armenian stock exchange | **0%** (exempt until 31 Dec 2027) |
| Income from alienation of building/apartments by real estate developers | **20%** |

(EY p.56)

**Social expenses tax credit:** Up to AMD 100,000/year tax credit for documented health, education, and housing expenses for the taxpayer and family members (spouse, parents, children). (EY p.57)

**Key exempt income items:**
- Voluntary accumulative pension contributions up to 5% of gross income
- State contributions to obligatory accumulative pension insurance
- Compensation within statutory norms (except unused leave on quitting)
- Inherited/gifted property and cash (except from individual real estate developers)
- Proceeds from sale of property to non-entrepreneur individuals (except from individual real estate developers)
- Crypto asset alienation income (except unique crypto assets and mining/validator rewards)
- Scholarships/stipends from state, NGOs, foreign states, intergovernmental organisations
- Income from sale of shares, treasury bonds, and other state securities
- Agricultural land alienation income (regardless of purpose)
- Insurance compensation (except voluntary accumulative pension system)
- Lottery winnings up to AMD 50,000 per winning; casino/game winnings; prize competition income up to AMD 50,000
- Employer health insurance contributions up to AMD 10,000/month per employee
- Agricultural income (with 10% threshold rule for mixed activities)
- State pensions (except voluntary accumulative pension system payouts)
- Alimony

(EY pp.53–54)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- AMD 100,000/year social expenses tax credit (health, education, housing)
- Individual entrepreneurs: all documented business expenses deductible (depreciation, lease, salaries, interest — subject to limits)
- Non-deductible: business-trip expenses above statutory norms; representative expenses > 0.5% of gross income (max AMD 5 million); fines/penalties; free-of-charge assets/services; interest on loans > 24%/year; management service expenses above statutory norms; social/cultural event expenses > 0.25% of pre-deduction taxable income

(EY pp.54–55)

**Notes:**
- Filing deadline: 2 March – 1 July of following year (for 2026 tax year); from 2026 reporting year, standard deadline applies (EY p.59–60)
- From 2025 tax year: all RA-citizen residents must file income tax returns (EY p.60)
- Capital gains on realised gains are subject to regular income tax; unrealised gains not taxed (EY p.54)
- Capital losses can be carried forward up to 5 years for individual entrepreneurs (EY p.57)

## Social Security

Armenia operates a mandatory funded (accumulative) pension system. There is no traditional social insurance contribution system — the accumulative pension is the primary mandatory contribution. (EY p.53)

**Mandatory accumulative pension (employees born after 1 January 1974):**

| Contributor | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee | **2.5%** of gross salary (up to AMD 500,000/month) | Mandatory |
| Employee | **5%** of gross salary (above AMD 500,000/month) | On the excess |
| State (co-contribution) | **2.5%** (up to AMD 500,000/month) | Matching state contribution |
| State (co-contribution) | **5%** (above AMD 500,000/month) | On the excess, capped |
| Employer | **0%** | No employer social contribution on top |

- The state co-contribution is capped: state contributes up to AMD 25,000/month (2.5% × AMD 500,000 + 5% × excess, subject to annual cap)
- Total employee + state: 5% (up to AMD 500,000) or up to 10% (above AMD 500,000)
- Contributions go into individual pension savings accounts (not pooled)
- Exempt income: state contributions to pension are excluded from gross income; voluntary contributions up to 5% of gross income are also exempt (EY p.53)

**Note on employer payroll taxes:** No traditional employer social insurance contribution is mentioned in EY for Armenia. The accumulative pension state co-contribution is funded by the state budget, not the employer.

**Income ceiling:** AMD 500,000/month triggers the higher 5% rate; no absolute contribution cap stated beyond the rate structure.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Entrepreneur (IE) — Standard Corporate Income Tax Regime

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered as an individual entrepreneur with the State Register; engages in business/commercial activities
- **Tax base:** Net income = gross income less documented business-related expenses (depreciation, lease, salaries, interest within limits); corporate income tax rules apply
- **Rate / mechanism:** **23%** corporate income tax on net profit (individual entrepreneurs calculate CIT at end of year at 23%; advance payments required quarterly at lower of 20% of prior year CIT or 2% of quarterly revenue) (EY p.60)
- **Social security:** Mandatory accumulative pension contributions (same structure as employees if born after 1974)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds AMD 115 million (~€105,000 at AMD 1,100/EUR); VAT rate 20%
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; losses carried forward up to 5 years

(EY pp.54–56, 60)

### Option B — Micro-Enterprise Status

- **Who qualifies:** Individual entrepreneurs or small companies meeting micro-enterprise criteria (turnover ≤ AMD 24 million/year ~€21,800); specific activity restrictions apply
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue
- **Rate / mechanism:** **0%** income tax — micro-enterprises are fully exempt from corporate income tax
- **Social security:** Accumulative pension contributions apply
- **VAT obligations:** Not VAT-registered (exempt below AMD 115 million threshold)
- **Key limits:** AMD 24 million/year (~€21,800) — below the income levels of primary interest; transitional stepping stone

(Note: Micro-enterprise regime exists under RA Tax Code; EY p.56 references individual entrepreneur CIT at 23% as the standard; micro regime confirmed via standard Armenian tax legislation)

### Option C — Turnover Tax Regime (for qualifying IE/small businesses)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual entrepreneurs and small companies with annual turnover ≤ AMD 115 million (~€105,000), in specified activity sectors
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue
- **Rate / mechanism:** **5%** turnover tax (replaces CIT and VAT for qualifying activities)
- **Social security:** Accumulative pension contributions apply
- **VAT obligations:** Turnover tax replaces VAT
- **Key limits:** AMD 115 million/year revenue ceiling; not available for all activities

(Standard Armenian tax regime; EY p.56 references IE CIT at 23% as primary rate — turnover tax is an alternative for qualifying small businesses)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### High-Tech / IT Sector Reduced Rate

- **Eligibility:** Employees in occupations on the government-approved list of professional activities in high-technology fields, involved in scientific research and experimental development work meeting government-set criteria, with positive opinion from the professional commission
- **Benefit:** 10% PIT rate instead of standard 20%
- **Duration:** Ongoing while employed in qualifying occupation with valid commission opinion
- **Caveats:** Activity must meet R&D criteria; positive opinion required from professional commission (EY p.56)

### Residence for Entrepreneurs

- Individuals engaged in entrepreneurial activities in Armenia qualify for temporary residence status without the need for a work permit (EY pp.64–65)
- Eurasian Economic Union (EEU) citizens can apply for Certificate of Legality of Stay when employed by an Armenian employer (EY p.65)

## 2026 Changes

- **Universal filing requirement (from 2025 tax year):** From 2025 onwards, all Armenian citizens who are RA residents must file personal income tax returns (EY p.60)
- **Filing deadlines for 2024–2025 transitional years:** For 2024 tax year: 1 Jan 2025 – 1 Nov 2025 (filing); 1 Nov 2025 (payment). For 2025 tax year: 2 March 2026 – 1 Nov 2026 (filing); 1 Nov 2026 (payment). From 2026 reporting year: standard 2 March – 1 July deadline applies (EY p.60)
- **AMD 382.52 = USD 1** as of 1 September 2025 (stated in EY p.52)
- Flat 20% PIT rate (in force since 1 January 2023) remains unchanged
- Individual entrepreneur CIT at 23% unchanged

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> **1 EUR ≈ AMD 420** (approximate; EY states AMD 382.52 = USD 1 as of Sep 2025; using AMD 420/EUR as conservative estimate — verify at cba.am).
> Employment scenario: flat 20% PIT; mandatory pension 2.5% (up to AMD 500,000/month) + 5% (above); state co-contributes matching amount (not an employee cost).
> B2B scenario: Individual entrepreneur turnover tax regime at 5% (if eligible) or CIT at 23% on net profit.

### €30,000 Employment (≈ AMD 12,600,000/year = AMD 1,050,000/month)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross monthly salary | AMD 1,050,000 | |
| PIT (20%) | 20% × 1,050,000 | AMD 210,000 |
| Pension: 2.5% on first 500,000 | 2.5% × 500,000 | AMD 12,500 |
| Pension: 5% on excess (550,000) | 5% × 550,000 | AMD 27,500 |
| Monthly pension contribution | | AMD 40,000 |
| Monthly total burden | 210,000 + 40,000 | AMD 250,000 |
| Annual total burden | × 12 | AMD 3,000,000 |
| **Effective rate** | 3,000,000 / 12,600,000 | **23.8%** |
