## Personal Income Tax

Resident individuals are subject to tax on Dominican Republic-source income AND foreign-source income from investments and financial gains. Income tax paid abroad on foreign-source income may be credited against DR tax liability (restricted to the DR tax allocated to that foreign income). Nonresidents are taxed on DR-source income only, and on technical assistance services income regardless of where provided. Residency: > 182 continuous or noncontinuous days in a tax year. (EY pp.365–366)

**New residents:** Individuals who become residents are subject to tax on foreign-source income after the **third year** of residency. A special retirement regime may exempt foreign-source income from tax if certain conditions are met. (EY p.365)

**Annual PIT brackets (resident individuals):** (EY p.367)

| Annual taxable income — Exceeding (DOP) | Not exceeding (DOP) | Tax on lower amount (DOP) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 416,220 | 0 | 0% |
| 416,220 | 624,329 | 0 | 15% |
| 624,329 | 867,123 | 31,216.35 | 20% |
| 867,123 | — | 79,775.15 | 25% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Employee SS contributions deductible for income tax purposes (EY p.366)
- Education expenses: deductible up to 10% of the individual's taxable income (not to exceed 25% of the minimum exempt amount under Section 296 of the Dominican Tax Code); not available to self-employed
- Business deductions: all costs and expenses necessary to generate, maintain and conserve taxable income (EY p.366)

**Notes:**
- Dividends from DR companies: 10% final withholding tax (residents and nonresidents) (EY p.366)
- Local interest payments to resident/domiciled individuals: 10% final withholding (EY p.366)
- Royalties and technical assistance to nonresidents: 27% withholding (EY p.366)
- Directors' fees (nonresident): 27% withholding (EY p.366)
- Capital gains on DR assets (resident/domiciled): taxed at progressive PIT rates 0%–25%; tax base is sale price minus historical cost adjusted for local inflation; nonresidents: flat 27% (EY p.366)
- Fringe benefits in kind: subject to 27% fringe benefits tax payable by the employer
- Nonresidents: flat 30% rate from DR sources
- Tax return deadline: 31 March of following year; employees with only employment income not required to file unless deducting education expenses
- Tax authority: DGII (Dirección General de Impuestos Internos)

**Confotur (tourism investment incentive):** Law 171-07 provides a special investment regime for qualifying tourism investors. Benefits may include residency permit and tax incentives. Foreign investors and retirees qualifying under Law 171-07 may apply for the Residence Permit Program for Investments in Dominican Republic. (EY p.369)

## Social Security

Three separate social security contributions levied on salaries. (EY p.367)

**Minimum wage:** DOP 17,250/month (legal minimum wage for SS cap purposes).

| Contribution | Employee % | Employer % | Cap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Retirement (AFP pension) | 2.87% | 7.10% | 20 legal minimum wages/month |
| Health (SFS) | 3.04% | 7.09% | 10 legal minimum wages/month |
| Labor risk (ARL) | — | 1%–3% | 10 legal minimum wages/month |
| INFOTEP (technical training) | 0.5% on profit-sharing (annual) | 1% of payroll | — |
| **Total (approx.)** | **~5.91%** | **~15.19%–17.19%** | — |

**SS Caps:**
- Pension (AFP): 20 × DOP 17,250 = DOP 345,000/month salary ceiling
- Health (SFS): 10 × DOP 17,250 = DOP 172,500/month salary ceiling
- Labor risk: 10 × DOP 17,250 = DOP 172,500/month ceiling

**Notes:**
- Employee contributions (pension 2.87% + health 3.04%) are deductible for income tax purposes
- Totalization agreement: Dominican Republic and Spain (EY p.367)
- Employer labor risk contribution varies 1%–3% based on activity classification

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Régimen Ordinario)

- **Who qualifies:** All individuals with self-employment or business income in the Dominican Republic.
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses). All costs and expenses necessary to generate taxable income are deductible.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT rates (0%–25%) on annual net taxable income. Annual return filed by 31 March.
- **Social security:** Self-employed are not explicitly covered by SS as employees; voluntary contributions possible — verify with TSS (Treasury of Social Security).
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to ITBIS (DR VAT) at 18% standard rate on most goods and services.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; full expense deductibility; capital gains taxed at progressive rates.

### Option B — Special Retirement Regime (Régimen Especial de Jubilados / Confotur)

- **Who qualifies:** New tax residents who meet conditions for the special retirement/investor regime; or qualifying investors under Law 171-07 (Confotur tourism investment incentive).
- **Benefit:** May exempt foreign-source income from income tax; provides other tax benefits for qualifying investments.
- **Caveats:** Conditions not fully detailed in EY source — verify with DGII.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Three-Year Foreign Income Exemption for New Residents
- **Eligibility:** Individuals who become tax residents of the Dominican Republic.
- **Benefit:** Foreign-source income is NOT subject to DR income tax until after the **third year** of residency. From year 4 onwards, foreign-source income is subject to DR tax.
- **Duration:** First 3 years of residency.
- **Caveats:** Applies only to foreign-source income; DR-source income is always taxable from day one. (EY p.365)

### Special Retirement Regime
- **Eligibility:** New residents who meet certain conditions (not fully specified in EY source).
- **Benefit:** May exempt foreign-source income from income tax and provide other tax benefits.
- **Caveats:** Conditions apply — verify with DGII. (EY p.365)

### Confotur Tourism Investment (Law 171-07)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign investors and retirees qualifying under Law 171-07.
- **Benefit:** Residence Permit Program for Investments; potential tax incentives on qualifying tourism investments.
- **Caveats:** Primarily an investment/residency incentive, not an income tax exemption for ordinary income. (EY p.369)
