## Personal Income Tax

Residents subject to tax on Dominican Republic-source income AND foreign-source income derived from investments and financial gains. Foreign income tax paid abroad may be credited against Dominican Republic tax liability (limited to the portion allocated to foreign-source income). Tax year = calendar year. Annual income tax return due by 31 March. (EY p.365)

**Residency:** Individuals spending more than 182 continuous or noncontinuous days in a tax year in the Dominican Republic are considered residents. (EY p.365)

**New residents:** Subject to tax on foreign-source income after the third year of residency. A special retirement regime may exempt foreign-source income from tax for qualifying new residents. (EY p.365)

**Employment income:** Annual employment income is taxable (salary, bonuses, premiums, commissions, allowances). Allowances paid in cash = taxable compensation. Benefits in kind = taxable fringe benefits subject to a 27% fringe benefits tax payable by the employer. (EY p.365)

**Progressive PIT brackets — Annual (2025)** (EY p.367)

| Annual taxable income (DOP) | Tax on lower amount (DOP) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 416,220 | 0 | 0% |
| 416,220 – 624,329 | 0 | 15% |
| 624,329 – 867,123 | 31,216.35 | 20% |
| Above 867,123 | 79,775.15 | 25% |

**Other income types:**
- Dividends paid/credited by local companies to residents and nonresidents: 10% withholding (final). (EY p.366)
- Interest paid to nonresidents from Dominican sources: 10% withholding (final). Local interest to residents: 10% withholding (final). Interest from abroad to residents: taxed at progressive rates. (EY p.366)
- Royalties, technical assistance payments, similar payments to nonresidents (Dominican-source): 27% withholding. (EY p.366)
- Directors' fees (nonresident/non-domiciled): 27% withholding. (EY p.366)
- Capital gains from Dominican Republic capital assets (shares, etc.) by residents: taxed at progressive rates (0%–25%). Tax base = transaction price minus historical cost adjusted by local inflationary rules. (EY p.366)
- Capital gains from Dominican Republic assets by nonresidents: flat 27%. (EY p.366)
- Donations: subject to 27% tax payable by recipient. (EY p.367)
- Real estate with value exceeding DOP 10,190,833: 1% annual tax on excess value. (EY p.367)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Employee contributions to social security: deductible for income tax purposes. (EY p.366)
- Education expenses (for individual and dependants): up to 10% of taxable income, but may not exceed 25% of the minimum exempt amount under Section 296 of the Dominican Tax Code. Does NOT apply to self-employed individuals. (EY p.366)
- Business deductions: all costs and expenses necessary to generate, maintain and conserve taxable income and protect investments. (EY p.366)

**Notes:**
- No estate or gift taxes specified (donations taxed at 27% on recipient).
- Tax treaties: Canada and Spain only. (EY p.368)
- Nonresidents: not required to file annual return if tax liability satisfied through withholding at source. (EY p.368)

## Social Security

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Retirement (AFP) | 2.87% | 7.10% | Cap: 20 legal minimum wages/month |
| Health (SFS) | 3.04% | 7.09% | Cap: 10 legal minimum wages/month |
| Labor risk (SRL) | 0% | 1–3% | Employer only; depends on activity |
| INFOTEP (technical training) | 0.5% (profit-sharing) | 1% (payroll) | Employee: on annual profit-sharing compensation |
| **Total (approx.)** | **~5.91%** | **~15.19%+** | |

**Monthly legal minimum wage:** DOP 17,250. (EY p.367)
**Retirement cap:** 20 × DOP 17,250 = DOP 345,000/month.
**Health cap:** 10 × DOP 17,250 = DOP 172,500/month.
**Labor risk cap:** 10 legal minimum wages (DOP 172,500/month). (EY p.367)

**Notes:**
- Employee contributions to social security ARE deductible for income tax purposes. (EY p.366)
- INFOTEP: employer contributes 1% of payroll; employees contribute 0.5% on annual profit-sharing compensation. (EY p.367)
- Cessantia/severance: not specified as mandatory contribution in EY source.
- Totalization agreement with Spain only. (EY p.367)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment
- **Who qualifies:** All individuals deriving income from self-employment or trade/business.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (gross income minus deductible business expenses: all costs necessary to generate, maintain and conserve taxable income). (EY p.366)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual rates 0%–25% (same scale as employment income).
- **Social security:** Self-employed SS regime not specified in EY source.
- **VAT obligations:** ITBIS (Impuesto a la Transferencia de Bienes Industrializados y Servicios); standard rate 18%. Applies to services and goods. (Not detailed in EY personal income section.)
- **Key limits:** Education expense deduction not available for self-employed. (EY p.366)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Special Retirement Regime for New Residents
- **Eligibility:** Individuals who become new residents of the Dominican Republic and meet certain conditions.
- **Benefit:** May exempt foreign-source income from income tax and provide other tax benefits.
- **Duration:** Available from the start of residency (exemption from foreign-source income tax during first 3 years is standard; regime provides additional benefits thereafter).
- **Caveats:** EY source indicates the regime "may exempt foreign-source income from tax" if certain conditions are met; full conditions not detailed in EY source. (EY p.365)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> EUR/DOP rate used: 1 EUR ≈ DOP 62 (approximate 2025 average; DOP/USD ~59, EUR/USD ~1.05).
> "Employment" = gross annual salary; employee SS 5.91% deducted (and deductible from PIT base); zero-rate band (DOP 416,220) applied.
> "B2B" = self-employment net profit at same progressive rates; SS not specified for self-employed.

**EUR to DOP conversions:**
- €30,000 = DOP 1,860,000
- €60,000 = DOP 3,720,000
- €100,000 = DOP 6,200,000

### Working: DOP 1,860,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (5.91%) | 5.91% × 1,860,000 | DOP 109,926 |
| PIT base | 1,860,000 − 109,926 | DOP 1,750,074 |
| Band 1: DOP 0–416,220 at 0% | 0% | DOP 0 |

| Band 3: DOP 624,329–867,123 at 20% | 20% × 242,794 | DOP 48,559 |

| Total PIT | 31,216 + 48,559 + 220,738 | DOP 300,513 |
