## Personal Income Tax

> Angola uses a schedular system (Groups A, B, C) under the Personal Income Tax Code (Law No. 18/14, amended by Law No. 28/20). Angola applies pure source-based taxation. Tax year is the calendar year. Group A income (employment) is withheld via PAYE; Groups B and C file returns in March following the tax year.

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Tax-free threshold: AOA 70,000
- Social security contributions excluded from taxable income
- Family allowances not included in taxable income
- Daily meal allowances up to AOA 30,000 excluded
- Daily transport allowances up to AOA 30,000 excluded
- Shortfall allowances (per diem, representation, travel, displacement) within civil servant limits excluded
- Salaries up to AOA 100,000 for national seasonal agricultural employees and Angolan domestic employees excluded

**Notes:**
- Group A (employment): PAYE withholding; employer files return by end of February
- Group B (self-employment): taxed on 100% of income without organized accounting; with organized accounting, up to 30% cost deduction allowed; WHT at 6.5% if payer is corporate/organized — creditable against final liability; otherwise 25% flat rate
- Group C (industrial/commercial): corporate income tax rules if organized accounting; 25% flat if no accounting
- Nonresident service providers (Group B/C): 6.5% WHT as final tax
- Draft new Personal Income Tax Code released for public consultation; anticipated in force 2027 (EY p.33)

## Social Security

> No ceiling on remuneration subject to social security. Employer rate 8%, employee rate 3%.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (Instituto Nacional de Segurança Social) | 3% | 8% | 8% (on notional salary) |
| **Total** | **3%** | **8%** | **8%** |

**Income ceiling:** None (EY p.35)
**Notes:**
- Employees working transitionally in Angola exempt if covered by social security in another country
- Self-employed pay 8% on a predefined monthly notional salary (may increase to 11% if additional benefits covered)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Group B (Self-Employed Professional Activities)
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals deriving income from self-employment activities listed in the Personal Income Tax Code annex
- **Tax base:** 100% of income (no organized accounting); income minus up to 30% of costs (with organized accounting)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates from Group A table applied to taxable base; WHT at 6.5% if payer is corporate/organized (creditable); 25% flat rate if income not subject to WHT
- **Social security:** 8% on predefined monthly notional salary
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT rules apply
- **Key limits:** 30% cost deduction only with organized accounting records

### Option B — Group C (Industrial and Commercial Activities)
- **Who qualifies:** Taxpayers with income from industrial and commercial activities
- **Tax base:** Corporate income tax rules (with accounting); no deductions without accounting
- **Rate / mechanism:** Corporate income tax base rules; 25% flat rate if no accounting records
- **Social security:** Standard employer/self-employed rates
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT rules apply
- **Key limits:** Organized accounting required for corporate-rule deductions

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

> No specific preferential tax regime for new tax residents or inbound workers identified in EY source. Angola applies pure source-based taxation.

### Source-Based Taxation
- **Eligibility:** All individuals; nonresidents taxed on Angola-source income only
- **Benefit:** Nonresidents not subject to tax on foreign-source income
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Nonresident service providers (Group B/C) subject to 6.5% final WHT

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 920 AOA (approximate mid-2025; AOA highly volatile — Medium confidence).
> Employment uses Group A progressive rates. B2B uses Group B (100% income taxable, progressive Group A rates) plus 8% social security.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (AOA) | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden (AOA) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈AOA 27,600,000) | Employment | 6,742,250 | 828,000 (3%) | 7,570,250 | ~27.4% |
| €30,000 (≈AOA 27,600,000) | B2B | 6,742,250 | 2,208,000 (8%) | 8,950,250 | ~32.4% |
| €60,000 (≈AOA 55,200,000) | Employment | 13,642,250 | 1,656,000 (3%) | 15,298,250 | ~27.7% |
| €60,000 (≈AOA 55,200,000) | B2B | 13,642,250 | 4,416,000 (8%) | 18,058,250 | ~32.7% |
| €100,000 (≈AOA 92,000,000) | Employment | 22,842,250 | 2,760,000 (3%) | 25,602,250 | ~27.8% |
| €100,000 (≈AOA 92,000,000) | B2B | 22,842,250 | 7,360,000 (8%) | 30,202,250 | ~32.8% |

**Working (income tax):**
- AOA 27,600,000: exceeds AOA 10,000,000 top bracket → tax = 2,342,250 + 25% × (27,600,000 − 10,000,000) = 2,342,250 + 4,400,000 = **6,742,250 AOA**
- AOA 55,200,000: tax = 2,342,250 + 25% × (55,200,000 − 10,000,000) = 2,342,250 + 11,300,000 = **13,642,250 AOA**
- AOA 92,000,000: tax = 2,342,250 + 25% × (92,000,000 − 10,000,000) = 2,342,250 + 20,500,000 = **22,842,250 AOA**

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 920 AOA (approximate, highly volatile)
- Single, no dependants, no meal/transport allowance exclusions (conservative)
- Brackets treated as annual figures
- Group B social security on full income (notional salary basis not quantified; 8% on full income used conservatively)
