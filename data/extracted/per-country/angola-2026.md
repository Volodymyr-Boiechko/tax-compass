## Personal Income Tax

Pure source-based system — Angola taxes only Angolan-source income for both residents and non-residents. Tax year = calendar year. Three groups of taxation: Group A (employment), Group B (self-employment), Group C (industrial/commercial). (EY p.33)

**Note:** A new Personal Income Tax Code was released for public consultation under Law No. 28/20 (22 July 2020); the government anticipates it will come into force in 2027. Current code: Law No. 18/14 of 22 October 2014. (EY p.33)

**Group A — Employment income tax brackets:**

| Taxable income (AOA) | Tax on lower amount (AOA) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 70,000 | — | 0% |
| 70,001 – 100,000 | 3,000 | 10% |
| 100,001 – 150,000 | 6,000 | 13% |
| 150,001 – 200,000 | 12,500 | 16% |
| 200,001 – 300,000 | 31,250 | 18% |
| 300,001 – 500,000 | 49,250 | 19% |
| 500,001 – 1,000,000 | 87,250 | 20% |
| 1,000,001 – 1,500,000 | 187,250 | 21% |
| 1,500,001 – 2,000,000 | 292,250 | 22% |
| 2,000,001 – 2,500,000 | 402,250 | 23% |
| 2,500,001 – 5,000,000 | 517,250 | 24% |
| 5,000,001 – 10,000,000 | 1,117,250 | 24.5% |
| 10,000,001+ | 2,342,250 | 25% |

(EY pp.34–35)

**Group B — Self-employment income:**
- If no organized accounting records: taxed on 100% of income
- If organized accounting records: deduction of up to 30% of costs incurred
- Income subject to withholding (paid by corporate entities with organized accounting): can be deducted from total taxable base
- Withholding tax rate: **6.5%** (for income subject to withholding assessed to Group B and C)
- Income not subject to withholding: taxed at **25%** (EY p.35)

**Group C — Industrial and commercial activities:**
- Taxpayers with organized accounting: taxable base follows corporate income tax rules
- Without organized accounting: no deductions permitted
- Withholding rate for nonresident service providers (Group B or C): **6.5%** (EY p.35)

**Taxable income — key exemptions (Group A):**
- Social payments from National Social Security Institute
- Shortfall allowances (per diem, representation, travel, displacement) within civil servant limits
- Family allowances
- Social security contributions
- Salaries up to AOA 100,000 for national seasonal agricultural employees and Angolan domestic employees registered with social security
- Subsidies for national citizens with disabilities
- Daily meal allowances up to AOA 30,000
- Daily transport allowances up to AOA 30,000
- Reimbursement of documented expenses per Angola General Tax Code rules

(EY p.34)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Group B: up to 30% of costs incurred (with organized accounting records)
- Group C: corporate income tax rules apply (with organized accounting)

## Social Security

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security | **3%** | **8%** | **8%** (or 11% with additional benefits) |

- No ceiling applies to the amount of remuneration subject to social security contributions (EY p.35)
- Self-employed: contributions based on a predefined monthly notional salary; rate is 8%, may increase to 11% if additional benefits are covered (EY p.35)
- Employees working transitionally in Angola are not required to contribute if covered by another country's social security system (EY p.35)

**Income ceiling:** None stated (EY p.35)

**Notes:**
- Social security contributions are listed among non-taxable items for Group A income (EY p.34)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Group B (Self-Employment with Organized Accounting)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals carrying out activities listed in the Personal Income Tax Code annex, with organized accounting records
- **Tax base:** 70% of gross income (30% cost deduction allowed)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive brackets as per Group A table (0%–25%); withholding at 6.5% by paying corporate entities; final tax at 25% if not subject to withholding
- **Social security:** 8% of notional monthly salary (self-defined base)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT may apply depending on activity and turnover
- **Key limits:** Must maintain organized accounting records to access the 30% deduction

(EY p.34–35)

### Option B — Group B (Self-Employment without Organized Accounting)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals without organized accounting records
- **Tax base:** 100% of gross income (no deductions)
- **Rate / mechanism:** 6.5% withholding if paid by corporate entity; 25% flat tax otherwise
- **Social security:** 8% of predefined notional monthly salary
- **VAT obligations:** VAT may apply
- **Key limits:** Less favorable — full income taxed without deductions

(EY p.34–35)

### Option C — Group C (Industrial/Commercial Activities with Organized Accounting)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals deriving industrial and commercial activity income, with organized accounting
- **Tax base:** Net profit per corporate income tax rules
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive brackets (Group A rates); withholding at 6.5%
- **Social security:** 8% of notional salary
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT obligations apply
- **Key limits:** Without organized accounting, no deductions permitted

(EY p.35)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No specific preferential tax regime for newcomers, expats, or digital nomads is described in EY. Angola applies a pure source-based system — only Angolan-source income is taxed. (EY pp.33–38)

## 2026 Changes

- No specific 2026 tax changes announced in the EY 2025-26 guide for Angola.
- **New Personal Income Tax Code anticipated for 2027:** A draft was released for public consultation under Law No. 28/20 (22 July 2020). The government anticipates the new code will come into force in 2027. (EY p.33)
- Current rates and brackets remain in force under Law No. 18/14 of 22 October 2014.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> **1 EUR ≈ AOA 920** (approximate mid-2025 rate; Angolan kwanza has depreciated significantly — verify at bna.ao).
> Employment scenario: Group A brackets applied to monthly income; PAYE withheld by employer.
> B2B scenario: Group B with organized accounting (30% cost deduction); 25% flat tax on non-withheld income; 8% social security on notional salary (assumed equal to gross).

### €30,000 Employment (≈ AOA 27,600,000/year = AOA 2,300,000/month)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross monthly income | AOA 2,300,000 | |
| Tax on first AOA 10,000,000 (annual) | per bracket table | AOA 2,342,250 |
| Remaining AOA 17,600,000 @ 25% | 25% × 17,600,000 | AOA 4,400,000 |
| Annual PIT | 2,342,250 + 4,400,000 | AOA 6,742,250 |
| Employee SS (3%) | 3% × 27,600,000 | AOA 828,000 |
| **Total annual burden** | 6,742,250 + 828,000 | **AOA 7,570,250** |
| **Effective rate** | 7,570,250 / 27,600,000 | **27.4%** |

### €30,000 B2B — Group B with organized accounting

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue | AOA 27,600,000 | |
| 30% cost deduction | 30% × 27,600,000 | AOA 8,280,000 |
| Taxable income | 27,600,000 − 8,280,000 | AOA 19,320,000 |
| PIT (25% flat, non-withheld) | 25% × 19,320,000 | AOA 4,830,000 |
| Self-employed SS (8% on notional) | 8% × 27,600,000 | AOA 2,208,000 |
| **Total burden** | 4,830,000 + 2,208,000 | **AOA 7,038,000** |
| **Effective rate** | 7,038,000 / 27,600,000 | **25.5%** |
