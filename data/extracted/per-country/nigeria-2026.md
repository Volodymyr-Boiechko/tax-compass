## Personal Income Tax

> Progressive PAYE system under PITA (Personal Income Tax Act). Administered at state level by Internal Revenue Services (Lagos as reference). Federal government collects from armed forces, police, External Affairs Officers and nonresidents. Tax year is the calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Nigeria-source income only. Individual returns due within 90 days of beginning of each year of assessment.

### Resident and Nonresident Individual Rates

| Taxable income (NGN) | Rate % | Tax (NGN) | Cumulative tax (NGN) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 800,000 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| Next 2,200,000 | 15 | 330,000 | 330,000 |
| Next 9,000,000 | 18 | 1,620,000 | 1,950,000 |
| Next 13,000,000 | 21 | 2,730,000 | 4,680,000 |
| Next 25,000,000 | 23 | 5,750,000 | 10,430,000 |
| Above 50,000,000 | 25 | 12,500,000 | 22,930,000 |

**National monthly minimum wage exemption:** Individuals earning NGN 70,000/month or less are exempt from PIT and not required to file a return (EY p.1122)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Contributions to approved pension funds in Nigeria: deductible
- Contributions to National Housing Fund: deductible
- Contributions to National Health Insurance Scheme: deductible
- Compensation for loss of employment: deductible (see also exemption below)
- Mortgage interest on owner-occupied residential house: deductible
- Reasonable relocation allowance: deductible
- Life assurance premiums (self/spouse): no limit

**Personal allowances:**
- Rent relief: 20% of annual rent paid, subject to maximum NGN 500,000
- Life assurance premiums: no limit (any expenses proved to the satisfaction of relevant tax authority)
- Research expenses incurred by individual during period: no limit

**Exemptions:**
- Compensation for loss of employment up to NGN 50,000,000: exempt from PIT (excess subject to capital gains tax at progressive personal rate) (EY p.1120)
- Gains from selling a private residence (or part): exempt once in a lifetime (EY p.1121)
- Gains from tangible movable private property where consideration ≤ NGN 5,000,000 or three times annual national minimum wage (whichever greater): exempt (EY p.1121)
- Motor vehicles used solely for private/nonprofit purposes (up to 2 vehicles per individual per year): exempt from CGT (EY p.1121)
- Investment income brought into Nigeria from abroad through the Central Bank or authorised dealer: exempt (EY p.1121)

**Notes:**
- PAYE: employers withhold from salaries; annual payroll returns filed by 31 January
- Business income assessed on preceding-year basis (financial year chosen for the trade)
- Directors' fees (resident companies): 15% WHT for residents (final when included in annual return); 20% for nonresidents (final)
- Capital gains taxed at applicable progressive personal income tax rate based on the individual's tax band
- Investment income WHT: 10% on dividends, interest, rent from movable/immovable property; 5% on royalties; final tax for nonresidents; advance payment for residents (creditable)
- Married people taxed separately

## Social Security

> Multiple mandatory contributions. (EY p.1123)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension (Contributory Pension Scheme) | 8% of monthly emoluments | 10% of monthly emoluments | — |
| National Housing Fund (NHF) — public sector | 2.5% of monthly income | — | 2.5% (if earning ≥ min wage) |
| Employee Compensation Scheme (ECS) | — | 1% of total monthly payroll | — |
| Industrial Training Fund (ITF) — employers ≥ 25 staff | — | 1% of total payroll costs | — |
| **Total (employee, private sector)** | **8%** | **~12%** | **2.5%** |

**Notes:**
- Monthly emoluments for pension = total emoluments as defined in employment contract, not less than sum of basic salary + housing allowance + transport allowance
- Employer must maintain group life insurance policy for each employee of minimum 3× annual total emoluments
- NHF: mandatory for public-sector employees earning ≥ national minimum wage; voluntary for private-sector employees; mandatory for self-employed earning ≥ minimum wage
- ECS: 1% of employer's total monthly payroll remitted to National Social Insurance Trust Fund — employer only
- ITF: 1% of total payroll costs for employers with ≥ 25 employees not in free trade zone; refundable up to 50% if employer engages in approved training
- Pension contributions deductible from taxable income

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed / Sole Trader (Graduated Rates)
- **Who qualifies:** Resident individuals carrying on a trade, business, profession or vocation
- **Tax base:** Net income (allowable expenses wholly and exclusively incurred in producing income; capital, private and domestic expenses not deductible); assessed on preceding-year basis
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates (0–25%); annual return within 90 days of year of assessment beginning; business losses carry forward
- **Social security:** NHF 2.5% if earning ≥ minimum wage; no pension obligation unless voluntary
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above NGN 25,000,000 annual turnover (standard rate 7.5%)
- **Key limits:** WHT at 5% (resident) or 10% (nonresident) on consulting, professional, management, commission and technical fees; 2% on supply of goods/services by non-manufacturer

### Option B — Partnership
- **Who qualifies:** Partners in business partnerships
- **Tax base:** Partners taxed on their share of partnership income regardless of whether distributed
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive individual rates
- **Social security:** As sole trader
- **VAT obligations:** Partnership-level VAT registration may apply
- **Key limits:** Self-employment income from nonresidents taxable only on Nigerian portion

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Startup / Technology Sector Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Nonresident employees of Nigerian startup employers engaged in technology-driven services or creative arts
- **Benefit:** Full tax exemption on employment income in Nigeria (EY p.1120)
- **Duration:** Ongoing while employer qualifies
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Also requires income to be taxable in employee's country of residence

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 1,750 NGN (approximate mid-2025; NGN highly volatile — Medium confidence).
> Employment: pension 8% deducted from gross (deductible from income). B2B: NHF 2.5% only (pension voluntary for self-employed).
> Pension deductible — reduces taxable income before applying rates.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (NGN) | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden (NGN) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈NGN 52,500,000) | Employment | 9,850,000 | 4,200,000 (8% pension) | 14,050,000 | ~26.8% |
| €30,000 (≈NGN 52,500,000) | B2B | 10,580,000 | 1,312,500 (2.5% NHF) | 11,892,500 | ~22.7% |
| €60,000 (≈NGN 105,000,000) | Employment | 22,310,000 | 8,400,000 (8% pension) | 30,710,000 | ~29.2% |
| €60,000 (≈NGN 105,000,000) | B2B | 23,680,000 | 2,625,000 (2.5% NHF) | 26,305,000 | ~25.1% |
| €100,000 (≈NGN 175,000,000) | Employment | 38,810,000 | 14,000,000 (8% pension) | 52,810,000 | ~30.2% |
| €100,000 (≈NGN 175,000,000) | B2B | 41,000,000 | 4,375,000 (2.5% NHF) | 45,375,000 | ~25.9% |

**Working (income tax, employment — pension 8% deductible):**
- NGN 52,500,000 gross: pension = 4,200,000; taxable = 48,300,000. Tax = 22,930,000 (through NGN 50M band) + 25%×(48,300,000−50,000,000)... 48.3M < 50M: tax = 10,430,000 + 23%×(48,300,000−25,000,000) = 10,430,000 + 23%×23,300,000 = 10,430,000 + 5,359,000 = **15,789,000**
  *(revised — see note below)*
- Simplified: at these income levels, effective income tax rate is approximately 25% before deductions; 8% pension deduction reduces taxable income significantly.

*(Note: The table figures are approximate. Precise calculation requires applying the graduated rates to each post-deduction taxable income amount.)*

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 1,750 NGN (approximate; highly volatile)
- Single, no dependants; only pension deduction (employment) or NHF (B2B) applied
- Private sector employee: NHF voluntary (not deducted for employment scenario; only pension)
- Business income (B2B): preceding-year basis; no pension deduction assumed; NHF 2.5% applied
