## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system under the Unified Tax Law. Employment income, commercial/industrial profits, non-commercial profits (self-employment), and income from immovable properties all subject to progressive rates. Married persons taxed separately. Tax year = calendar year. (EY p.377, p.381)

**Residency:** Residents are persons with permanent domicile in Egypt, or who reside in Egypt for a period exceeding 183 days in a calendar year. (EY p.377)

**Income subject to tax includes:** Employment income; business profits/non-commercial profits (self-employment); immovable property income (rental values of agricultural lands and buildings); dividends; capital gains on disposals of shares and securities. (EY p.377)

**Employment income:** Salaries, wages, compensation awards, overtime pay, all cash and in-kind fringe benefits. Certain collective in-kind allowances exempt (meals, transport, healthcare, tools, uniforms, housing provided by employer for work performance). (EY p.377–378)

**Progressive PIT Rates — 2025** (EY p.380–381)

The rate structure is complex: the applicable starting rate depends on total annual net income, creating a system where lower-income individuals begin at lower rates.

| Annual net income* | Tax brackets applied |
|---|---|
| Up to EGP 600,000 | 0% on EGP 1–40,000; 10% on EGP 40,001–55,000; 15% on EGP 55,001–70,000; 20% on EGP 70,001–200,000; 22.5% on EGP 200,001–400,000; 25% above EGP 400,000 |
| EGP 600,001–700,000 | 10% on EGP 1–55,000; 15% on EGP 55,001–70,000; 20% on EGP 70,001–200,000; 22.5% on EGP 200,001–400,000; 25% above EGP 400,000 |
| EGP 700,001–800,000 | 15% on EGP 1–70,000; 20% on EGP 70,001–200,000; 22.5% on EGP 200,001–400,000; 25% above EGP 400,000 |
| EGP 800,001–900,000 | 20% on EGP 1–200,000; 22.5% on EGP 200,001–400,000; 25% above EGP 400,000 |
| EGP 900,001–1,200,000 | 22.5% on EGP 1–400,000; 25% above EGP 400,000 |
| Above EGP 1,200,000 | 25% on EGP 1–1,200,000; 27.5% above EGP 1,200,000 |

*Annual net income rounded to nearest lower EGP 10.*

**Simplified bracket summary for incomes up to EGP 600,000 (most common):**

| Band (EGP) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 40,000 | 0% |
| 40,001 – 55,000 | 10% |
| 55,001 – 70,000 | 15% |
| 70,001 – 200,000 | 20% |
| 200,001 – 400,000 | 22.5% |
| Above 400,000 (up to 600,000) | 25% |
| Above 1,200,000 | 27.5% |

**Other income types:**
- Dividends (by domestic corporations to residents/nonresidents): 10% withholding (reduced to 5% if distributing company is listed on Egyptian Stock Exchange, effective September 2020). (EY p.379)
- Royalties (from Egypt sources): 20% on gross income for nonresidents; specific treaty rates may apply. Not applicable to Egyptian nationals. (EY p.379)
- Interest: 20% withholding with some exemptions. Not applicable to Egyptian nationals. (EY p.379)
- Capital gains from real estate: 2.5% tax on gross proceeds from total disposal value of built real estate and land. Not subject to income tax unless real estate is used in a trade or business. (EY p.379)
- Capital gains on personal property (automobiles, jewelry, shares) owned by individual: not taxed in Egypt unless used in trade/business. (EY p.379)
- Capital gains on securities listed on Egyptian Stock Exchange: 10% tax. (EY p.379)
- Capital gains on unlisted shares (resident/nonresident): progressive rates up to 27.5%. (EY p.379)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.379–380)
- Annual personal deduction: EGP 20,000 per individual (as of 1 March 2024). (EY p.379)
- Social insurance and other contributions deductible per social insurance law. (EY p.379)
- Employees' contributions to private insurance funds (per Law No. 54 for 1975): deductible. (EY p.379)
- Life and health insurance premiums for individual/spouse/minor children and pension insurance premiums: deductible up to 15% of net annual income or EGP 10,000 (whichever is lower). (EY p.379–380)
- Business deductions for commercial/industrial income: all costs generally deductible, including rental of premises, tax depreciation, taxes (except income tax), social insurance, pension/savings contributions, depreciation, amortization. (EY p.380)

**Notes:**
- No wealth tax, inheritance/estate tax, or gift tax in Egypt. (EY p.381)
- Losses: taxpayer may offset losses against business profits and carry forward up to 5 years; no carryback. Long-term project losses may be carried back within same project. Capital losses on securities in excess of capital gains: carryforward 3 years. (EY p.381)
- Employees not required to submit annual returns for employment income. (EY p.382)
- Business/self-employed: annual tax returns and payment due before 1 April. (EY p.381)

## Social Security

Social insurance contributions levied on Egyptian nationals and (from September 2021) on foreign nationals with a full-time employment contract in Egypt. (EY p.381)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social insurance | 18.75% | 11% | — |
| **Total** | **18.75%** | **11%** | — |

**Income ceiling:** Base salary up to EGP 14,500/month (2025). (EY p.381)

**Coverage:** Pensions, disability, death insurance, occupational accident insurance, sickness and injury insurance, unemployment insurance. (EY p.381)

**Notes:**
- Social insurance contributions ARE deductible from income tax base. (EY p.379)
- Totalization agreements with: Cyprus, Greece, Morocco, the Netherlands, Sudan, Tunisia. (EY p.381)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Non-Commercial Profits (Independent Professionals)
- **Who qualifies:** Professionals and independent persons practicing non-commercial activities in Egypt (lawyers, accountants, artists, writers, and similar professions where work is the primary element). (EY p.378)
- **Tax base:** Net non-commercial profits (gross revenue minus all related costs). If no proper books kept, gross revenue estimated using tax authority indicators and guidelines. (EY p.378)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual rates 0%–27.5% (same scale as employment).
- **Social security:** Self-employed social insurance rates not specified in EY source for this section.
- **VAT obligations:** Value-added tax may apply to professional services (rate not specified in personal income section).
- **Key limits:** Must notify tax authorities within 30 days of starting activities; must obtain tax identification card. (EY p.381)

### Option B — Commercial and Industrial Profits (Sole Traders)
- **Who qualifies:** Sole traders, partnerships, and limited partnerships operating in Egypt; commercial and industrial entities operating as sole traders.
- **Tax base:** Net profits from all business activities within a calendar year (after all costs, business charges, expenses, and personal allowances). (EY p.378–380)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual rates 0%–27.5%.
- **Social security:** Not specified for sole traders.
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT applies.
- **Key limits:** 5-year loss carryforward. Profits from outside Egypt not taxable if headquarters of commercial/industrial activities are outside Egypt. (EY p.378)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Nonresident Expatriate Experts
- **Eligibility:** Nonresident individuals and expatriate experts performing activities in Egypt (as defined).
- **Benefit:** Generally taxed on Egyptian-source income only at the same progressive rates as Egyptian residents. Amounts paid by domestic corporations to nonresidents are subject to withholding taxes. (EY p.381)
- **Duration:** Ongoing.
- **Caveats:** No special preferential rate — same rates as residents.

- **Social security ceiling (2025):** EGP 14,500/month; may be adjusted for 2026.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> EUR/EGP rate used: 1 EUR ≈ EGP 55 (approximate 2025 average; EGP has depreciated significantly; verify current rate).
> "Employment" = gross annual salary; employee SS 18.75% on base up to EGP 14,500/month (= EGP 174,000/year); EGP 20,000 personal deduction applied.
> "B2B" = non-commercial profits at same progressive rates; SS not specified for self-employed.
> Tax brackets for incomes up to EGP 600,000 (simplified scale) used throughout.

**EUR to EGP conversions:**
- €30,000 = EGP 1,650,000
- €60,000 = EGP 3,300,000
- €100,000 = EGP 5,500,000

Note: EGP 1,650,000 annual income exceeds EGP 1,200,000 threshold → 27.5% top rate applies.

### Working: EGP 1,650,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (18.75%, capped at EGP 14,500/month) | 18.75% × 174,000 (annual cap) | EGP 32,625 |
| Personal deduction | EGP 20,000 | EGP 20,000 |
| PIT base | 1,650,000 − 32,625 − 20,000 | EGP 1,597,375 |
| Annual income > EGP 1,200,000 → 27.5% on excess, 25% up to EGP 1,200,000 | | |
| Tax on EGP 1–1,200,000 at 25% | 25% × 1,200,000 | EGP 300,000 |
| Tax on EGP 1,200,001–1,597,375 at 27.5% | 27.5% × 397,375 | EGP 109,278 |
| Total PIT | 300,000 + 109,278 | EGP 409,278 |
| **Total burden** | **32,625 + 409,278** | **EGP 441,903** |
| **Effective rate** | **441,903 / 1,650,000** | **~26.8%** |

**Working — all income levels:**
- All three EUR income levels translate to >EGP 1,200,000 using 1 EUR = EGP 55.
- For incomes > EGP 1,200,000: tax = 25% × 1,200,000 + 27.5% × (income − 1,200,000) = 300,000 + 27.5% × excess.
- The SS cap (EGP 174,000/year) binds at all income levels → fixed SS of EGP 32,625.
- B2B has no SS (assumed 0; no personal deduction modeled for non-commercial profits).

**Note on exchange rate sensitivity:** 1 EUR = EGP 55 is approximate for 2025. The EGP has been volatile (EGP 31/USD in early 2024 → devalued to ~EGP 48/USD by March 2024). At EGP 30/EUR (pre-devaluation), €30,000 = EGP 900,000 → lower effective rates. Check current rate.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/EGP ≈ 55 (2025 approximate; highly uncertain due to EGP devaluation)
- Employee SS: 18.75% on base salary up to EGP 14,500/month (EGP 174,000/year cap)
- Personal deduction: EGP 20,000/year
- SS contributions deductible from PIT base for employment
- B2B: same progressive rates; SS not specified; assumed 0 for illustration
- All computed income levels exceed EGP 1,200,000, so 27.5% top rate structure applies
