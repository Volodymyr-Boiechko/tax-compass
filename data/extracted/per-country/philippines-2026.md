## Personal Income Tax

Philippines taxes **resident citizens** on worldwide income; **non-resident citizens, resident aliens, and non-resident aliens engaged in trade or business** on Philippine-source income. Non-resident aliens not engaged in trade or business: taxed on Philippine-source income at 25% flat final withholding. Tax year = calendar year. Filing deadline: 15 April. Income tax return: BIR Form 1700 (employees only income) or 1701 (individuals with business/professional income). The Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) administers. (EY p.1230)

**Progressive PIT brackets — effective 1 January 2023 (TRAIN Act, RA 10963)** (EY p.1239)

| Net taxable income (PHP) | Tax on lower amount | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 250,000 | 0 | 0% |
| 250,001 – 400,000 | 0 | 15% |
| 400,001 – 800,000 | 22,500 | 20% |
| 800,001 – 2,000,000 | 102,500 | 25% |
| 2,000,001 – 8,000,000 | 402,500 | 30% |
| Over 8,000,000 | 2,202,500 | 35% |

**8% Optional Income Tax Rate for Self-Employed (IMPORTANT):** (EY p.1234, 1239–1240)
Self-employed individuals and/or professionals whose gross sales/receipts and other non-operating income do **NOT exceed the VAT threshold of PHP 3,000,000** may elect to pay:
- **8% income tax on gross sales/receipts and other non-operating income in excess of PHP 250,000** (instead of graduated rates + percentage tax)
- This is in lieu of both the graduated income tax AND the 3% percentage tax
- Must notify BIR of the election in the first quarter return of the year
- Irrevocable for the year once elected
- Taxpayer must be registered and subject only to percentage tax under Section 116 NIRC, or exempt from VAT
- **As of EY October 2025 content date: the 8% option remains valid and in force.** (EY p.1234)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- **PHP 250,000 tax-free threshold** (0% bracket) — applies to all residents
- **Optional Standard Deduction (OSD):** 40% of gross income for individuals with business/professional income (electable instead of itemized deductions; irrevocable for the year once stated in return) (EY p.1238)
- TRAIN Act removed personal and additional exemptions for employment income (previously PHP 50,000 personal + PHP 25,000/dependent exemption) — no longer available from 2018 (EY p.1232)
- De minimis benefits and 13th month pay: up to PHP 90,000 EXEMPT from income tax (EY p.1231)
- Mandatory contributions to SSS, PhilHealth, HDMF: deductible from gross taxable income (EY p.1238)

**Notes:**
- 13th month pay + other bonuses: exempt up to PHP 90,000 aggregate; excess taxable (EY p.1231)
- OFW (Overseas Filipino Workers): remittances and income from abroad generally exempt from Philippine income tax
- Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT): 35% (resident; nonresident alien engaged in T&B) or 25% (NRANETB) on grossed-up monetary value — paid by employer, not employee (EY p.1240)
- Minimum Wage Earners (MWE): exempt from income tax and withholding on statutory minimum wage (EY p.1245)
- Capital gains on shares NOT traded on stock exchange: 15% final CGT (effective 1 July 2025, CMEPA RA 12214) (EY p.1237)
- Capital gains on listed shares: 0.1% stock transaction tax (not income CGT) (EY p.1237)
- Capital gains on real property (Philippines): 6% final CGT on higher of gross selling price or fair market value (EY p.1238)
- Dividends: 10% final WHT (resident); 20% (non-resident alien NETB); 25% (NRANETB) (EY p.1236)
- Interest income: generally 20% final WHT (from 1 July 2025, CMEPA changed all term deposit interest to 20% flat regardless of maturity) (EY p.1235–1236)

## Social Security

Three mandatory contribution agencies for Philippine employees: SSS, PhilHealth, HDMF. (EY p.1238–1239)

**Effective 1 January 2025 (per EY p.1239):**

| Contribution | Employee (max/month) | Employer (max/month) | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| SSS (Social Security System) | PHP 1,750 (regular) + PHP 1,000 (MPF/WISP) = PHP 2,750 | PHP 2,530 (regular) + PHP 1,000 (MPF) = PHP 3,530 | PHP 1,750 + PHP 1,000 |
| PhilHealth | PHP 2,500 (5% of salary, max base PHP 100,000) | PHP 2,500 (equal share) | 5% of income |
| HDMF (Pag-IBIG) | PHP 200 (min) | PHP 200 | PHP 200 |
| **Total (max monthly)** | **PHP 4,450** | **PHP 6,230** | **~PHP 4,450** |

**Notes on contribution structure (EY p.1239):**
- SSS: employee ~5% + MPF/WISP; employer ~10%; total SSS rate 15% (from 2023, RA 11199)
- Maximum monthly salary credit (MSC): PHP 35,000 → max SSS regular = PHP 1,750 (employee)
- PhilHealth: 5% combined (2024/2025); max monthly base PHP 100,000 → max PHP 2,500 each
- HDMF: PHP 100 (employee) + PHP 100 (employer) for salaries <PHP 1,500; PHP 200 each for PHP 1,500+; max for salaries ≥PHP 10,000 (from Feb 2024)
- Mandatory contributions (SSS, PhilHealth, HDMF) are **deductible from gross taxable income** of individuals (EY p.1238)
- Self-employed individuals are compulsorily covered by SSS and PhilHealth; HDMF coverage also mandatory for self-employed

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — 8% Optional Income Tax Rate (Best for Low-Gross-Revenue)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual self-employed or professional with annual gross sales/receipts + non-operating income ≤ PHP 3,000,000 (VAT threshold). Must elect in first-quarter BIR return.
- **Tax base:** Gross sales/receipts and other non-operating income in excess of PHP 250,000.
- **Rate / mechanism:** **8% flat on gross revenue over PHP 250,000**. Replaces both graduated PIT and 3% percentage tax. No deductions allowed (except the PHP 250,000 exemption).
- **Social security:** Same mandatory SSS, PhilHealth, HDMF as above.
- **VAT obligations:** Not VAT-registered (below PHP 3M threshold); subject to 3% percentage tax, which is waived under the 8% option.
- **Key limits:** PHP 3,000,000 gross revenue ceiling. If VAT-registered or exceeds PHP 3M: automatically subject to graduated rates. Election irrevocable for the year.

### Option B — Graduated Rates + OSD (Best for High-Gross-Revenue or VAT-Registered)

- **Who qualifies:** Any self-employed individual or professional; mandatory if gross > PHP 3,000,000 or VAT-registered.
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus either (a) OSD at 40% of gross income, or (b) itemized deductions (actual business expenses). Net taxable income after deductions + mandatory contributions.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 0%–35% on net taxable income.
- **Social security:** Same mandatory contributions.
- **VAT obligations:** If gross ≤ PHP 3M: 3% percentage tax. If gross > PHP 3M: 12% VAT (register with BIR).
- **Key limits:** File BIR Form 1701 annually by 15 April; quarterly income tax returns during the year; if using OSD, election in annual return is irrevocable for that year.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Non-Resident Alien Engaged in Trade or Business (NRAETB)

- **Eligibility:** Non-Filipino citizens staying >180 days in the Philippines in a calendar year.
- **Tax base / Rate:** Taxed at graduated rates (same 0%–35% brackets as residents) on Philippine-source income. Not taxed on foreign income.
- **Duration:** Applies for each year they exceed the 180-day threshold.
- **Caveats:** If not engaged in trade or business (NRANETB — staying ≤180 days): flat 25% WHT on all Philippine-source income. (EY p.1231)

### Special Nonimmigrant Visas (47(a)(2), PEZA, OBU visas)

- PEZA visa holders employed by PEZA-registered companies: exempt from AEP requirements; may have preferential tax treatment at company level.
- OBU (Offshore Banking Unit) / RHQ / ROHQ employees: previously had 15% preferential PIT rate — **this was abolished from 1 January 2018 (TRAIN Act)**. Now subject to regular graduated rates. (EY p.1239)

### Special Resident Retiree's Visa (SRRV)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals aged ≥50 (former Filipinos aged ≥35 allowed); deposit requirements: USD 1,500–USD 50,000 depending on age/circumstances.
- **Benefit:** Permanent residence in Philippines; multiple-entry privileges. No specific reduced PIT rate — resident retirees subject to standard rates, but typically have limited Philippine-source income.
- **Tax impact:** Foreign pension income: NOT taxable in Philippines if received from foreign sources.

## 2026 Changes

- **CMEPA (RA 12214 — Capital Markets Efficiency Promotion Act), effective 1 July 2025:** Major changes:
  - Capital gains on unlisted shares: 15% flat CGT (previously different rates). (EY p.1237)
  - Interest income from all term deposits: 20% final WHT (eliminates reduced rates for long-term deposits; effective 1 July 2025). (EY p.1235–1236)
  - Equity-based compensation (stock options): treated as compensation income (not FBT subject to certain conditions). (EY p.1236–1237)
- **SSS circular 2024-006:** Social security contribution rate increased to 15% total (effective 2023; fully implemented); employee ~5%, employer ~10%.
- **PhilHealth 2024/2025:** Combined rate 5% (2.5% each); max base PHP 100,000.
- **HDMF (February 2024):** Employee and employer PHP 200/month each for salaries ≥PHP 10,000. (EY p.1239)
- **8% option:** Remains valid for 2026. PHP 3M VAT threshold: no announced change.
- **EOPT Law (Ease of Paying Taxes, RA 11976):** New rules on when withholding obligation arises (when income "payable" = due/demandable, per RR 4-2024). Affects professional fee WHT timing. (EY p.1236)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, resident citizen, full-year.
> PHP/EUR rate: 1 EUR = PHP 62 (approximate 2025; PHP ~57–62/USD × 1.08).
> Employment: graduated 0%–35% PIT on net income (after mandatory deductions) + SSS (PHP 1,750/mo max) + PhilHealth (2.5% up to PHP 100k base) + HDMF (PHP 200/mo).
> 8% self-employed: 8% on gross above PHP 250,000; + mandatory SSS+PhilHealth+HDMF.
> Employer SSS+PhilHealth+HDMF (~PHP 6,230/month max) is not included in employee burden.

### Working: PHP 1,860,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| SSS (employee, max PHP 1,750/mo × 12) | | PHP 21,000 |
| PhilHealth (2.5% × PHP 100,000 cap × 12) | | PHP 30,000 |
| HDMF (PHP 200/mo × 12) | | PHP 2,400 |
| Total mandatory deductions | | PHP 53,400 |
| Taxable income | 1,860,000 − 53,400 | PHP 1,806,600 |
| PIT: 0–250,000 @ 0% | | PHP 0 |
| PIT: 250,001–400,000 @ 15% | 15% × 150,000 | PHP 22,500 |
| PIT: 400,001–800,000 @ 20% | 20% × 400,000 | PHP 80,000 |
| PIT: 800,001–1,806,600 @ 25% | 25% × 1,006,600 | PHP 251,650 |
| Total PIT | | PHP 354,150 |
| **Total burden** | **354,150 + 53,400** | **PHP 407,550** |
| **Effective rate** | **407,550 / 1,860,000** | **21.9%** |

**Note on PHP 3,720,000 self-employed:** Above PHP 3M threshold → must use graduated rates (not 8%); OSD 40% used.
- OSD: 40% × 3,720,000 = 1,488,000 deduction; mandatory contributions: 53,400
- Net taxable: 3,720,000 − 1,488,000 − 53,400 = 2,178,600
- PIT: 0–250k @ 0% = 0; 250k–400k @ 15% = 22,500; 400k–800k @ 20% = 80,000; 800k–2M @ 25% = 300,000; 2M–2,178,600 @ 30% = 53,580 → Total = 456,080

**Note on PHP 6,200,000 self-employed + OSD:**
- OSD: 40% × 6,200,000 = 2,480,000; net: 6,200,000 − 2,480,000 − 53,400 = 3,666,600
- PIT: 0–250k = 0; 250k–400k @ 15% = 22,500; 400k–800k @ 20% = 80,000; 800k–2M @ 25% = 300,000; 2M–3,666,600 @ 30% = 499,980 → Total = 902,480

**Key insight:** The 8% option for self-employed earners with gross revenue ≤ PHP 3M is one of the most competitive self-employment tax regimes in Southeast Asia. At PHP 1.86M (~€30k), the 8% option results in only 9.8% effective all-in rate vs. 21.9% for employment. Above PHP 3M, the graduated + OSD route still provides substantial savings vs. employment (14.6–15.0% vs. 24–26%). The combination of PHP 250,000 zero bracket + OSD 40% deduction + low-bracket rates makes Philippines highly competitive for tech freelancers.

**OFW note:** Filipino workers overseas (OFWs) sending remittances home pay 0% Philippines tax on their foreign-source earnings — a major reason for the Philippines' large diaspora worker community.

**Assumptions:**
- PHP/EUR = 62 (approximate 2025)
- SSS: capped at PHP 1,750/month employee (MSC PHP 35,000); annual max PHP 21,000
- PhilHealth: 2.5% employee share; max base PHP 100,000/month → max PHP 30,000/year
- HDMF: PHP 200/month → PHP 2,400/year
- Mandatory contributions deducted from gross before PIT computation
- 8% option: applicable only where gross ≤ PHP 3M (PHPeq ≈ €48k); at €60k and above, graduated rates mandatory
- 13th month pay exempt (included in gross but not taxed if ≤ PHP 90,000 aggregate with other bonuses)
