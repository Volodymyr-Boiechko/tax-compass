## Personal Income Tax

Jordan taxes both residents and nonresidents on income earned in, or derived from, Jordan, regardless of residency status. An individual is considered resident if present in Jordan for ≥183 consecutive or non-consecutive days per year. No net worth tax, no inheritance tax, no gift tax. Tax year = calendar year. Returns filed in Arabic within 4 months after year-end; total tax due at time of filing. (EY pp. 755–758)

### Income Tax Brackets (Ordinary Income)

| Annual taxable income (JOD) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 5,000 | **5%** |
| 5,001 – 10,000 | **10%** |
| 10,001 – 15,000 | **15%** |
| 15,001 – 20,000 | **20%** |
| 20,001 – 1,000,000 | **25%** |
| Above 1,000,000 | **30%** |

(EY p. 757)

### National Contribution Tax (Surcharge)

A **1% national contribution tax** is levied on the annual taxable income of natural persons exceeding **JOD200,000**. Effectively an additional 1% on income above JOD200,000, making the top marginal rate 31% on income above JOD1M. (EY p. 756)

### Personal and Family Allowances (from 2020 onwards)

| Allowance type | Annual amount (JOD) |
|---|---|
| Personal allowance | 9,000 |
| Dependants allowance | 9,000 |
| Additional allowances (medical expenses, education, rent, home loan interest, technical/professional services) | JOD1,000 per individual + JOD1,000 for spouse + JOD1,000 per child (up to JOD3,000) |

**Effective income tax threshold:** Personal allowance of JOD9,000 means income up to JOD9,000 is effectively tax-free.

### Investment Income

| Income type | Tax treatment |
|---|---|
| Interest income | 5% withholding tax (final) by banks |
| Foreign-source income (from Jordanian money or deposits) | 10% if generated from Jordanian monies/deposits |
| Capital gains from ASE shares (Amman Stock Exchange) | 0.08% of sales price per transaction (buyer and seller each pay) |
| Capital gains from direct share sales (natural persons) | Either: graduated 0.5%–5% on gross sale consideration, OR domestic PIT rates on capital gains — taxpayer chooses lower |
| Capital gains from shares outside Jordan (from Jordanian monies/deposits) | 10% |

### Withholding Taxes (Resident-to-Resident)

| Service type | WHT rate |
|---|---|
| Payments to resident individual/corporate service providers (engineers, auditors, lawyers) | 5% |
| Payments to nonresident service providers | 10% |
| Payments to corporate nonresidents for taxable services | National contribution tax 1%–7% (sector-dependent) |

## Social Security

Jordan's social security is administered by the Social Security Corporation (SSC). Contributions are levied on **basic salary** (not total compensation). (EY pp. 757–758)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (standard) | **7.5%** | **14.25%** | Total 21.75% |
| Social security (SME option — Regulation 45/2024) | **7.5%** + optional top-up | **9.85%** | SME employer rate reduced; employees may optionally top up |
| **Total (standard)** | **7.5%** | **14.25%** | **21.75% combined** |

**Income ceiling:** Maximum monthly salary base: **JOD3,668/month** (2025, adjusted annually for inflation; pre-May 2010 employees capped at JOD5,000/month if registered before then). (EY p. 758)

**Non-Jordanian employees:** SS contributions made by non-Jordanians may be partially refunded upon leaving Jordan. Refund process is case-by-case and may take time. (EY p. 758)

**Self-employed:** No specific self-employment SS framework described separately; self-employed individuals conducting business activities may have different registration requirements with SSC.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals, sole traders, and professionals (consultants, engineers, lawyers, doctors) deriving income from Jordan
- **Tax base:** Net income from business activities (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses); same brackets as employment income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates 5%/10%/15%/20%/25%/30% on annual taxable income above personal allowance; JOD9,000 personal allowance applies
- **Social security:** Self-employed social security registration obligations not explicitly detailed in EY; employers with employees pay SSC; sole self-employed persons should verify SSC requirements
- **VAT obligations:** General Sales Tax (GST) applies to goods and some services; standard rate 16%; registration required for businesses above threshold
- **Loss treatment:** Losses from trading may be carried forward up to 5 years (if supported by proper accounting records, acknowledged by tax assessor, and relating to taxable sources)

### Option B — No simplified regime

Jordan has no separate presumptive taxation or simplified regime for small self-employed individuals; all taxpayers use the standard progressive rate structure.

---

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Aqaba Special Economic Zone (ASEZ)

- **Benefit:** Companies operating in ASEZ are subject to a 5% income tax rate (vs standard corporate rates); personal income tax for employees working in ASEZ may also benefit from reduced rates or specific exemptions — verify current ASEZ rules
- **Location:** Aqaba city on the Red Sea; specific investment incentives available for qualifying businesses

### No Dedicated Personal Newcomer Tax Regime

Jordan does not have a remittance basis or dedicated new-resident income tax exemption for individuals. All residents and non-residents are subject to standard progressive rates on Jordan-source income.

## 2026 Changes

- **New SSC regulations effective August 2024:** Regulation No. 45/2024 (effective 1 August 2024) allows small and medium enterprises (SMEs) to reduce employer SS rate to 9.85% (from 14.25%); employees may optionally settle the difference (EY p. 757)
- **SSC base reporting updated (Regulation 56/2024, effective 1 September 2024):** SSC base must be modified whenever employee basic salary changes; employers with >20 employees must adjust reported wages whenever changes occur (EY p. 757–758)
- **Maximum SS salary cap adjusted:** JOD3,668/month for 2025 (inflation-adjusted annually)
- **No structural income tax changes announced** for 2026 as of EY data date (October 2025)
- **National contribution tax continues at 1%** on income exceeding JOD200,000

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants; personal allowance JOD9,000; dependants allowance not taken (single).
> **1 EUR ≈ JOD 0.80** (approximate; USD/JOD ≈ 0.708; EUR/USD ≈ 1.09; so EUR/JOD ≈ 0.77–0.80).
> Employment: progressive PIT + SS employee 7.5% (capped at JOD3,668/month = JOD44,016/year).
> B2B: same PIT rates; SS obligation for self-employed unclear — not included in B2B calculation.

### €30,000 Employment (≈ JOD 24,000)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | €30,000 × JOD 0.80 | JOD 24,000 |
| Personal allowance | | JOD 9,000 |
| Taxable income | 24,000 − 9,000 | JOD 15,000 |
| Tax on first JOD5,000 @ 5% | | JOD 250 |
| Tax on next JOD5,000 @ 10% | | JOD 500 |
| Tax on next JOD5,000 @ 15% | 15% × 5,000 | JOD 750 |
| **Total income tax** | | **JOD 1,500** |
| SS employee: 7.5% × 24,000 (< JOD44,016 ceiling) | | JOD 1,800 |
| **Total burden** | 1,500 + 1,800 | **JOD 3,300** |
| **Effective rate** | 3,300 / 24,000 | **13.8%** |

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **SS for self-employed:** EY does not explicitly describe the SSC obligations for self-employed individuals in Jordan. B2B figures above exclude SS contributions — verify with Jordan Social Security Corporation.
- [ ] **Interest WHT (5%) as final tax:** Banks withhold 5% on interest payments as a final tax. If an individual has significant bank interest income, this is not added to their annual taxable income for progressive rate purposes — it is final at 5%.
- [ ] **National contribution tax on income > JOD200,000:** The 1% national contribution tax on income > JOD200,000 is not reflected in the examples above (all income levels < JOD200,000). At higher income levels, add 1% to the effective rate.
- [ ] **Aqaba Special Economic Zone:** EY does not provide detailed personal income tax rates for ASEZ employees. The 5% corporate rate for ASEZ companies may translate to lower withholding on employee income — verify with local counsel.
- [ ] **JOD/EUR conversion:** The JOD is pegged to the USD at approximately JOD1 = USD1.41 (or USD1 = JOD0.708). The EUR/JOD rate fluctuates with EUR/USD; current EUR/JOD ≈ 0.77–0.80. Verify at cbj.gov.jo.
- [ ] **Additional allowances detail:** The JOD1,000/person additional allowances (medical, educational, rent, loan interest, technical services) can reduce taxable income by up to JOD3,000 for a single person with qualifying expenses. Not modeled above.
