## Personal Income Tax

Both resident and nonresident individuals are subject to tax on their **Honduras-source income only** (territorial system). Tax year = 1 January – 31 December. Returns filed by 30 April. (EY p.569, p.571)

**Residency:** Resident if living in Honduras for more than three consecutive months during a tax year. (EY p.569)

**Employment income:** Salary, pensions, bonuses, premiums, commissions, and allowances (including housing and educational allowances). Board members, executives and counselors not in the regular payroll: 12.5% withholding on professional fees, commissions and bonuses; 1% on payments for goods and services. (EY p.569)

**Progressive PIT brackets — Annual (employment and self-employment)** (EY p.570)

| Annual taxable income (HNL) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 217,493.16 | 0% |
| 217,493.16 – 331,638.50 | 15% |
| 331,638.50 – 771,252.38 | 20% |
| Above 771,252.38 | 25% |

*Note: The above tax rates are subject to change by the government.* (EY p.570)

**Age exemption:** Individuals older than 65 years with total annual income up to HNL 350,000 (approximately USD 14,000) are exempt from the above tax. (EY p.570)

**Other income types:**
- Dividends (local companies to resident/nonresident individuals): 10% withholding (final). (EY p.570)
- Royalties from franchises: 25% withholding. (EY p.570)
- Technical advice and similar payments: 25% withholding when paid abroad. (EY p.570)
- Directors' fees (nonresident): 25% withholding (final). (EY p.570)
- Directors' fees (resident): taxed at ordinary individual income tax rates. (EY p.570)
- Capital gains: flat 10% rate. Capital losses NOT deductible. Occasional (non-habitual) sales of non-depreciable assets: not subject to capital gains tax. Capital gains due within 10 business days of transaction; annual return also due by 30 April. Nonresident capital gains: 4% withholding on gross proceeds (remitted within 10 calendar days). (EY p.570)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.570)
- Annual deduction for medical and educational expenses: up to HNL 40,000 (approximately USD 1,605.01).
- For persons older than 65 years: medical and educational deduction up to HNL 80,000 (approximately USD 3,210.02).
- Business deductions: all costs and expenses necessary to generate taxable income and protect investments. (EY p.570)

**Notes:**
- No estate or gift taxes. Estates taxed as ordinary taxpayers if they derive income before asset distribution. (EY p.570)
- Self-employed individuals may not carry forward or carry back losses. (EY p.570)
- No double tax treaties with other countries. Tax information exchange agreements only (with Ecuador and United States). (EY p.571)
- HNL/USD: approximately USD 1 = HNL 24.85 (derived from HNL 11,109.30 ≈ USD 445). (EY p.571)

## Social Security (IHSS — Instituto Hondureño de Seguridad Social)

Honduras has multiple mandatory social security contributions for all companies. (EY p.570–571)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Government | Max monthly salary (HNL) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sickness & Maternity (Enfermedad y Maternidad) | 2.5% | 3.5% | 0.5% | 11,109.30 |
| Old Age, Invalidity & Death (Vejez, Invalidez y Muerte) | 2.5% | 3.5% | 0.5% | 11,109.30 |
| Professional Risk (Riesgo Profesional) | — | 0.2% | — | 11,109.30 |
| Social Housing Fund (Fondo Social para la Vivienda) | 1.5% | 1.5% | — | Monthly salary minus Old Age floor amount |
| **Total employee (approx.)** | **~6.5%** | **~8.7%** | | |

**Maximum monthly insurable salary for IHSS:** HNL 11,109.30 (approximately USD 445). (EY p.571)

**Annual maximum employee IHSS contributions (excl. Social Housing Fund):**
- 5% × HNL 11,109.30 × 12 = HNL 6,665.58/year (Sickness/Maternity + Old Age/Invalidity/Death combined = 2.5% + 2.5% = 5% employee)

**Social Housing Fund:** 1.5% employee + 1.5% employer on monthly salary minus the floor amount for Old Age/Invalidity/Death contribution. (EY p.571)

**Notes:**
- Social security contributions ARE deductible personal expenses for PIT purposes (not explicitly confirmed in EY p.570–571 but standard for Honduras).
- No totalization agreements specified in EY source.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment
- **Who qualifies:** All individuals deriving income from self-employment, trade or business in Honduras.
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross income minus all necessary business expenses). (EY p.570)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual rates 0%–25% (same scale as employment).
- **Social security:** IHSS employer + employee portions; self-employed regime not separately specified.
- **VAT obligations:** Sales tax (ISV — Impuesto Sobre Ventas) applies; standard rate 15% on most goods/services; 18% on alcoholic beverages and tobacco. (Not detailed in personal income section.)
- **Key limits:** No loss carryforward or carryback for self-employed. (EY p.570)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No specific preferential income tax regime for new residents or expats identified in EY source. Honduras taxes only source income for all individuals (territorial system).

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (HNL 40,000 medical/education deduction applied).
> EUR/HNL rate: 1 EUR ≈ HNL 26.8 (derived from HNL/USD 24.85 × EUR/USD 1.08; approximate).
> "Employment" = gross annual salary; employee IHSS ~6.5% (capped at max HNL 11,109.30/month = HNL 133,311.60/year for IHSS); zero-rate band HNL 217,493.16 applied.
> "B2B" = self-employment net profit; same progressive rates; IHSS not specified for self-employed.

**EUR to HNL conversions:**
- €30,000 = HNL 804,000
- €60,000 = HNL 1,608,000
- €100,000 = HNL 2,680,000

### Working: HNL 804,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee IHSS (5% of HNL, capped at HNL 133,312/yr) | 5% × 133,312 (cap binds: HNL 804k > HNL 133k/0.05=2.67M) | HNL 6,666 |
| Social Housing Fund (~1.5%) | 1.5% × 804,000 (approx.) | HNL 12,060 |
| Total employee SS | 6,666 + 12,060 | HNL 18,726 |
| PIT deduction (medical/education) | HNL 40,000 | HNL 40,000 |
| PIT base | 804,000 − 40,000 | HNL 764,000 |
| Band 1: 0–217,493 at 0% | 0% | HNL 0 |
| Band 2: 217,493–331,639 at 15% | 15% × 114,146 | HNL 17,122 |
| Band 3: 331,639–771,252 at 20% | 20% × 432,613 | HNL 86,523 |
| Band 4: above 771,252 at 25% | 25% × (764,000 − 771,252) | HNL 0 (764k < 771k) |
| Total PIT | 17,122 + 86,523 | HNL 103,645 |
| **Total burden** | **18,726 + 103,645** | **HNL 122,371** |

**Working — HNL 1,608,000 Employment:**
- SS: same capped amount ~HNL 18,726
- PIT base: 1,608,000 − 40,000 = 1,568,000
- Tax: 0 + 17,122 + 20% × (771,252 − 331,639) + 25% × (1,568,000 − 771,252)
  = 17,122 + 87,923 + 25% × 796,748 = 17,122 + 87,923 + 199,187 = HNL 304,232

**Working — HNL 2,680,000 Employment:**
- PIT base: 2,680,000 − 40,000 = 2,640,000
- Tax: 0 + 17,122 + 87,923 + 25% × (2,640,000 − 771,252) = 17,122 + 87,923 + 467,187 = HNL 572,232

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/HNL ≈ 26.8 (approximate; derived from HNL/USD 24.85 and EUR/USD 1.08)
- Employee IHSS: 5% (sickness 2.5% + old age 2.5%) capped at HNL 11,109.30/month = HNL 133,312/year; Social Housing Fund 1.5% on gross (approximate)
- HNL 40,000 medical/educational deduction applied for employment; no other personal deductions modeled
- Zero-rate band HNL 217,493.16 applied before first taxable bracket
- B2B: IHSS rates for self-employed not specified; assumed 0 for illustration
- No capital gains tax on employment/business income modeled
