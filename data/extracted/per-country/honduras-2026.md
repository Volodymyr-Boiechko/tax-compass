## Personal Income Tax

Territorial system — both resident and nonresident individuals are taxed on **Honduras-source income only**, regardless of nationality. Foreign-source income is not taxed. Residency: > 3 consecutive months (> 90 days) in a tax year. Tax year: 1 January to 31 December. Returns due 30 April of following year. (EY p.569)

**Annual PIT brackets (employment and self-employment):** (EY p.570)

| Annual taxable income — Exceeding (HNL) | Not exceeding (HNL) | Tax rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 217,493.16 | 0% |
| 217,493.16 | 331,638.50 | 15% |
| 331,638.50 | 771,252.38 | 20% |
| 771,252.38 | — | 25% |

**Note:** Above tax rates are subject to change by the government.

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.570)
- Medical and educational expenses: up to HNL 40,000/year (≈ USD 1,605) for individuals up to age 65
- Medical and educational expenses: up to HNL 80,000/year (≈ USD 3,210) for individuals over 65

**Exemption:** Individuals over 65 with total income up to HNL 350,000/year (≈ USD 14,000) are fully exempt from PIT. (EY p.570)

**Notes:**
- Territorial principle: only Honduras-source income is taxed (foreign-source income exempt)
- Dividends from local companies: 10% final withholding (residents and nonresidents) (EY p.569–570)
- Royalties from franchises: 25% withholding when paid abroad (EY p.570)
- Technical advice and similar payments: 25% withholding when paid abroad (EY p.570)
- Directors' fees (nonresidents): 25% withholding (EY p.570)
- Directors' fees (resident): taxed at ordinary individual income tax rates (EY p.570)
- Capital gains: flat 10% tax rate (EY p.570); capital losses are **not deductible**
- Capital gains (nonresidents): 4% withholding on gross proceeds; remitted within 10 calendar days of transaction
- Board members, executives, counselors not on payroll: 12.5% withholding; goods and services payments: 1% withholding (EY p.569)
- No estate or gift taxes (EY p.570)
- No loss carryforward or carryback for self-employed (EY p.570)
- No tax treaties with any country (Honduras has tax information exchange agreements with Ecuador and the United States only) (EY p.571)
- Tax authority: SAR (Servicio de Administración de Rentas)

## Social Security (IHSS — Instituto Hondureño de Seguridad Social)

Four separate contribution categories, each with the same HNL 11,109.30/month ceiling. (EY p.570–571)

| Contribution | Employee % | Employer % | Government % | Cap (monthly HNL) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sickness and Maternity (IHSS) | 2.5% | 3.5% | 0.5% | 11,109.30 |
| Old Age, Invalidity and Death (IVM) | 2.5% | 3.5% | 0.5% | 11,109.30 |
| Professional Risk (Riesgo Profesional) | — | 0.2% | — | 11,109.30 |
| Social Housing Fund (RAP — Régimen de Aportaciones Privadas) | 1.5% | 1.5% | — | Not capped same way (a) |
| **Total (employee)** | **~6.5%** | **~8.7%** | **~1.0%** | — |

(a) RAP contribution: calculated on monthly salary minus the floor amount of the Old Age, Invalidity and Death contribution × 1.5%.

**Income ceiling:** HNL 11,109.30/month ≈ USD 445/month for IHSS and IVM contributions.

**Notes:**
- The SS contribution caps are very low (≈ USD 445/month); high earners pay very little SS as a percentage of total income
- No totalization agreements mentioned in EY source; no tax treaties

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All individuals with self-employment or business income from activities in Honduras.
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses). All costs and expenses necessary to generate taxable income and protect investments are deductible.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT rates (0%–25%) on annual net taxable income. Advance income tax payments required for self-employed and business owners.
- **Social security:** IHSS and RAP contributions apply (employer and employee rates combined for self-employed — not specified; verify with IHSS).
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to ISV (Honduras VAT/Impuesto Sobre Ventas) at 15% standard rate; 18% on certain luxury goods, alcohol, tobacco.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; full expense deductibility; no loss carryforward.

### No Simplified / Alternative Regime
- Honduras does not have a simplified tax regime equivalent to Guatemala's optional simplified regime or Costa Rica's self-employment brackets as of EY data date. All self-employed use the single progressive rate structure.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Territorial Taxation (General)
- **Eligibility:** All individuals (resident and nonresident).
- **Benefit:** Only Honduras-source income is taxed. Foreign-source income is completely exempt — this is a permanent feature of the system.
- **Duration:** Permanent.
- **Caveats:** Residency threshold is only 3 consecutive months (90 days) — lower than many countries. Beyond 3 months, worldwide Honduran-source income is taxable but foreign income remains exempt. (EY p.569)

### No Specific Digital Nomad Visa
- Honduras does not have a formal digital nomad visa or special tax regime for remote workers as of EY data date. The territorial system means foreign-sourced remote work income is not taxable in Honduras.

## 2026 Changes

- **PIT brackets:** Subject to change by the government — verify updated HNL amounts with SAR.
- **SS caps:** HNL 11,109.30/month ceiling may be updated by decree — verify with IHSS.
- **No major structural PIT changes announced for 2026.**
- **No new tax treaties:** Honduras has no double tax treaties in effect.

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **SS deductibility from PIT:** EY source does not confirm whether employee SS contributions are deductible from PIT base. If deductible, effective rates would be modestly lower.
- [ ] **RAP ceiling:** The RAP (Social Housing Fund) calculation — "monthly salary minus the floor amount of IVM × 1.5%" — implies RAP applies to full salary with no separate cap; verify with IHSS.
- [ ] **Self-employed SS:** IHSS/RAP obligations for self-employed not specified in EY source. B2B calculations above exclude SS.
- [ ] **ISV (VAT) 15%:** Applies to self-employed services; not modeled in effective rate calculations.
- [ ] **PIT brackets stability:** "Subject to change by the government" per EY source — verify current brackets with SAR (sar.gob.hn).
- [ ] **No tax treaties:** Honduras has no double tax treaties in force; potential double taxation for residents with foreign income (mitigated by territorial system).
- [ ] **No digital nomad visa:** No formal digital nomad visa or regime confirmed.
- [ ] **HNL exchange rate:** Verify current rate at bchhonduras.hn.
