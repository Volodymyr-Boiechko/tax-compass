## Personal Income Tax

> Progressive income tax administered by BURS (Botswana Unified Revenue Service). Tax year runs 1 July to 30 June. Residents taxed on Botswana-source income (foreign-source income generally not taxable). Married persons taxed separately.

### Resident Individual Rates

| Taxable income (BWP) | Tax rate % | Tax due (BWP) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 to 48,000 | 0 | 0 |
| 48,001 to 84,000 | 5 | 0 + 5% of excess over 48,000 |
| 84,001 to 120,000 | 12.5 | 1,800 + 12.5% of excess over 84,000 |
| 120,001 to 156,000 | 18.75 | 6,300 + 18.75% of excess over 120,000 |
| Above 156,000 | 25 | 13,950 + 25% of excess over 156,000 |

### Non-Resident Individual Rates

| Taxable income (BWP) | Tax rate % | Tax due (BWP) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 to 84,000 | 5 | 5% of taxable income |
| 84,001 to 120,000 | 12.5 | 4,200 + 12.5% of excess over 84,000 |
| 120,001 to 156,000 | 18.75 | 8,700 + 18.75% of excess over 120,000 |
| Above 156,000 | 25 | 15,450 + 25% of excess over 156,000 |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Single tax-free allowance: BWP 48,000 (built into the rate table for residents)
- Approved pension fund contributions deductible, limited to 15% of income (excluding investment income)
- One-third of retrenchment packages exempt (or amount equal to tax threshold, whichever is higher)

**Notes:**
- PAYE scheme covers noncash benefits including employer-provided housing, utilities and cars (special tables used)
- Educational allowances provided to local and expatriate employees' children are taxable
- Interest paid to residents subject to 10% withholding tax (provisional; credited against assessment); interest income from banking institutions/building societies in excess of BWP 7,800 subject to 10% final WHT
- Dividends from resident companies: not included in taxable income; subject to 10% final WHT
- Director's fees (resident directors): subject to 10% WHT, added to taxable income; nonresident directors: 15% final WHT
- Returns due within 90 days after tax year end; payment within 30 days after assessment

## Social Security

> Social security taxes are not levied in Botswana (EY p.172).

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security | None | None | None |
| **Total** | **0%** | **0%** | **0%** |

**Income ceiling:** N/A — no social security system
**Notes:**
- Botswana has no statutory social security / national insurance contributions
- Some employers operate voluntary occupational pension schemes; contributions to approved pension funds are deductible up to 15% of income

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Self-Employed (Same Rate Scale)
- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals and sole traders
- **Tax base:** Net business profits (business expenses of sole proprietorships and partnerships deductible to the extent incurred in producing taxable income)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates as employment income (resident rate table above)
- **Social security:** None
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above BWP 1,000,000 annual turnover (standard VAT rate 14%)
- **Key limits:** Sole proprietors and partners may take the same salary deductions as employees; losses may be carried forward five years (FIFO)

### Option B — Partnership
- **Who qualifies:** Partners in business partnerships
- **Tax base:** Partners individually taxed on their share of business profits
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive individual income tax rates
- **Social security:** None
- **VAT obligations:** Partnership-level VAT registration may apply
- **Key limits:** Same loss carry-forward rules

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Tax-Free Gratuity for Non-Citizens
- **Eligibility:** Non-citizen employees who have completed a two-year employment contract
- **Benefit:** One-half of a gratuity payment is exempt from tax if the gratuity is provided for in the employment contract. For the first contract: one-half exemption up to 25% of cumulative salary; second contract: 27.5%; third and subsequent contracts: 30%
- **Duration:** Per contract period
- **Application deadline:** N/A (applies at time of payment)
- **Caveats:** Applies only to contractual gratuities, not general remuneration

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 11.8 BWP (approximate mid-2025 rate).
> No social security in Botswana — total burden equals income tax only.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (BWP) | Social security | Total burden (BWP) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈BWP 354,000) | Employment | 62,550 | 0 | 62,550 | ~17.7% |
| €30,000 (≈BWP 354,000) | B2B | 62,550 | 0 | 62,550 | ~17.7% |
| €60,000 (≈BWP 708,000) | Employment | 150,550 | 0 | 150,550 | ~21.3% |
| €60,000 (≈BWP 708,000) | B2B | 150,550 | 0 | 150,550 | ~21.3% |
| €100,000 (≈BWP 1,180,000) | Employment | 256,550 | 0 | 256,550 | ~21.7% |
| €100,000 (≈BWP 1,180,000) | B2B | 256,550 | 0 | 256,550 | ~21.7% |

**Working (income tax, resident rates):**
- BWP 354,000: tax = 13,950 + 25% × (354,000 − 156,000) = 13,950 + 49,500 = **BWP 63,450** (~17.9%)
  *(corrected: 13,950 + 25% × 198,000 = 13,950 + 49,500 = 63,450)*
- BWP 708,000: tax = 13,950 + 25% × (708,000 − 156,000) = 13,950 + 138,000 = **BWP 151,950** (~21.5%)
- BWP 1,180,000: tax = 13,950 + 25% × (1,180,000 − 156,000) = 13,950 + 256,000 = **BWP 269,950** (~22.9%)

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 11.8 BWP (approximate)
- Resident individual; tax year 1 July–30 June
- No approved pension contributions claimed
- Self-employment and employment rates identical (no social security either way)
- B2B = sole proprietor, full business income taxable at resident rates
