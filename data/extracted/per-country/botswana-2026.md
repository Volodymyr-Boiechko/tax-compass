## Personal Income Tax

Individuals earning income in Botswana are subject to income tax. Residents: taxed on Botswana-source income only (with a few exceptions — income from sources outside Botswana is generally **not subject to tax**). Non-residents: taxed on Botswana-source income. Tax year runs 1 July to 30 June. Returns due within 90 days after tax year end. (EY pp.169–171)

Resident = permanent place of abode in Botswana, OR physically present ≥ 183 days during tax year, OR having been present ≥ 183 days in previous year and physically remaining in the next year even without 183-day presence. (EY p.169)

**PIT rates — Residents:**

| Taxable income (BWP) | Tax rate | Tax due (BWP) | Cumulative tax due (BWP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 48,000 | **0%** | 0 | 0 |
| 48,001 – 84,000 | **5%** | 0 + 5% of excess over 48,000 | up to 1,800 |
| 84,001 – 120,000 | **12.5%** | 1,800 + 12.5% of excess over 84,000 | up to 6,300 |
| 120,001 – 156,000 | **18.75%** | 6,300 + 18.75% of excess over 120,000 | up to 13,050 |
| 156,001+ | **25%** | 13,950 + 25% of excess over 156,000 | — |

(EY p.171)

**PIT rates — Non-residents:**

| Taxable income (BWP) | Tax rate | Tax due (BWP) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 84,000 | **5%** | 5% of taxable income |
| 84,001 – 120,000 | **12.5%** | 4,200 + 12.5% of excess over 84,000 |
| 120,001 – 156,000 | **18.75%** | 8,700 + 18.75% of excess over 120,000 |
| 156,001+ | **25%** | 15,450 + 25% of excess over 156,000 |

(EY p.171)

**Investment income:**
- Dividends from resident companies: **not included in taxable income**; subject to **10% final withholding tax** (EY p.170)
- Interest paid to residents: **10% withholding** (provisional tax; credit granted against assessment); interest from banking institutions/building societies on amounts > BWP 7,800: 10% final withholding (EY p.170)
- Royalties, management/consulting fees, interest to non-residents: **15% final withholding** (EY p.170)
- Rental income: included with other taxable income at ordinary rates
- Royalties: included with other taxable income at ordinary rates

**Capital gains:**
Capital gains tax on gains from: (1) sale of immovable assets, and (2) sale of corporate shares and debentures. (EY p.170)

| Taxable gain (BWP) | Tax rate | Tax due (BWP) | Cumulative (BWP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 48,000 | 0% | 0 | 0 |
| 48,001 – 84,000 | 5% | 5% of excess over 48,000 | up to 1,800 |
| 84,001 – 120,000 | 12.5% | 1,800 + 12.5% of excess over 84,000 | up to 6,300 |
| 120,001 – 156,000 | 18.75% | 6,300 + 18.75% of excess over 120,000 | — |
| 156,001+ | 25% | 13,950 + 25% of excess over 156,000 | — |

- Only **75%** of gains from corporate share sales is subject to tax (EY p.170)
- Gains on BSE-listed shares held ≥ 1 year: **exempt** (EY p.170)
- Disposal of principal private residence (PPR) held ≥ 5 years: **exempt** (EY p.170)
- Loss carry-forward: 5 years on a first-in first-out basis (EY p.171)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Tax-free allowance (personal): **BWP 48,000/year** (incorporated into resident rate table as 0% band)
- Approved pension fund contributions: deductible, limited to **15% of income** (excluding investment income)
- Tax-free gratuities for non-citizens: one-half exempt if employee completed 2-year contract (max 25% of cumulative salary for 1st contract; 27.5% for 2nd; 30% for 3rd+)
- Business deductions (sole proprietors/partners): expenses incurred in producing taxable income, fully deductible; may take same salary deductions as employees (EY p.171)

## Social Security

**Social security taxes are NOT levied in Botswana.** (EY p.172)

There are no mandatory employee or employer social security contributions. There is no national health insurance, pension, or unemployment insurance scheme imposed by law on employers or employees.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security | **0%** | **0%** | **0%** |

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietorship / Partnership

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual or partners conducting business in Botswana
- **Tax base:** Business profits = gross revenue minus expenses incurred in producing taxable income; same salary deductions as employees available
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT rates (0%–25%) on taxable business profit; BWP 48,000 zero-rate band applies to residents
- **Social security:** None (not levied in Botswana)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover ≥ BWP 1,000,000; VAT rate 14%
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; losses carry-forward 5 years

(EY pp.169–171)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Territorial System (Key Feature)

- **Eligibility:** All residents
- **Benefit:** Income from sources **outside Botswana** is generally **not subject to Botswana income tax** — Botswana operates an effectively territorial system for residents. This is a major feature for remote workers and digital nomads with foreign-source income.
- **Caveats:** "With a few exceptions" — EY p.169 does not specify what those exceptions are; professional advice recommended for complex cross-border situations

### Tax-Free Gratuities for Non-Citizens

- **Eligibility:** Non-citizen employees completing 2-year employment contracts
- **Benefit:** One-half of gratuity is exempt from tax (exemption limited to 25% of cumulative salary for 1st contract; 27.5% for 2nd; 30% for 3rd and subsequent contracts)
- **Purpose:** Incentive for attracting foreign skilled workers

(EY p.171)

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (BWP 48,000 zero-rate band; no pension fund contributions).

> **No social security contributions in Botswana.**
> Employment scenario: progressive PIT 0%–25%; territory-based (only Botswana-source income taxed).

**Key observation — No social security:** Botswana's complete absence of social security taxes makes the total burden equal to income tax only. The effective rate caps at ~24% due to the 25% top marginal rate. This makes Botswana relatively attractive for high earners compared to many peer jurisdictions.

**Digital nomad opportunity:** Botswana's territorial system means residents with foreign-source income (e.g., remote workers invoicing foreign clients) may pay **0% Botswana income tax** on that foreign income. This needs professional verification given the "few exceptions" caveat in EY.
