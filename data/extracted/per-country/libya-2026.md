## Personal Income Tax

Libya imposes income tax on both Libyan nationals and foreigners on income arising in Libya. Residency for tax purposes: (a) Libyan national, (b) present in Libya with a work visa, or (c) undertakes employment in Libya. Residence results in liability for Libyan PIT for the year of residence. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Employees with only employment income are NOT required to file annual returns (PAYE withholding is final for them). Individuals with non-employment income must file within 90 days after year-end. Tax is payable in four quarterly installments from 10 June. (EY pp. 870–872)

**Important caveat:** EY notes: *"This chapter reflects the law in Libya at the time of writing. In view of the current transition in Libya, the legislative situation is difficult to assess and may be subject to change. Consequently, readers should obtain updated information before engaging in transactions."* (EY p. 870)

### Income Tax Rates

| Annual taxable income (LYD) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| First 12,000 | **5%** |
| Above 12,000 | **10%** |

(EY p. 871)

**Note:** The brackets in LYD are modest by international standards. At LYD1 ≈ USD0.20 (approximate), LYD12,000 ≈ USD2,400/year. In practice, almost all taxable income falls above the LYD12,000 threshold and is taxed at 10%.

**Effective top rate: 10%** — flat rate on almost all income.

### Personal Allowances

| Status | Annual allowance (LYD) |
|---|---|
| Single taxpayer | **LYD1,800** |
| Married taxpayer | **LYD2,400** |
| Each dependent child (up to age 18) | **LYD300** |

(EY p. 871)

**Single effective tax-free amount:** LYD1,800 deducted from taxable income before applying rates.

### Exempt Income

| Income type | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Income from savings account deposits | Exempt |
| Payments to life insurance beneficiaries | Exempt |
| Disability payments arising from employment | Exempt |
| Agricultural income | Exempt |
| Civil servants and state employee income | Exempt |
| Pension income | Exempt |
| Income from writing and research in science/culture | Exempt |
| Income of charitable organizations | Exempt |
| **Foreign income** | **Exempt** |
| **Export income** | **Exempt** |
| Development activities (government-determined) | Exempt |

(EY p. 871)

**Key note:** Foreign income is explicitly exempt for individuals in Libya. This is significant for expatriates with foreign-source income.

### Capital Gains

- The law does not make specific reference to tax on capital gains; company capital gains are treated as trading income
- Implication: individual capital gains are treated analogously — likely included in general taxable income if arising from business activity (EY p. 871)

### Social Unity Fund

- **1% of monthly gross salary** is withheld as a contribution to the Social Unity Fund — in addition to income tax
- This applies to all employees (EY p. 871)

## Social Security

Libya has a formal social security contribution system. (EY pp. 871–872)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security | **5.125%** | **15.375%** | Total 20.5% |
| State contribution (for Libyan companies) | — | **1.025%** | State pays part of employer share |
| **Total (employee)** | **5.125%** | | |
| **Total (employer, net of state contribution)** | | **14.35%** | Net for Libyan companies |

**No income ceiling stated** for social security contributions.

**Social Unity Fund:** Additional 1% of monthly gross salary withheld from employees (as noted above).

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals carrying out business activities independently, providing consulting services, or engaging in technical, artistic, or scientific projects
- **Tax base:** Net profit from business activities (gross income minus deductible expenses); losses may be carried forward up to 5 years
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same two-rate structure (5%/10%) as employment; personal allowances apply
- **Deductible expenses:** Life insurance premiums, general insurance premiums, social security contributions, medical insurance contributions
- **Social Unity Fund:** 1% of gross income
- **VAT obligations:** Libya does not have a comprehensive VAT system; specific consumption taxes may apply
- **Filing:** Annual return within 90 days after year-end (31 March); quarterly tax installments from 10 June

### Note on Foreign Self-Employment

- Foreign nationals generally cannot be self-employed in Libya; they must be employed by a Libyan entity or a licensed foreign company operating in Libya — **"foreign nationals may not be self-employed"** (EY p. 873)

---

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Foreign Income Exemption

- **Benefit:** All foreign income is explicitly exempt from Libyan income tax for individuals (EY p. 871)
- **Practical application:** Expatriates maintaining income sources in their home country (e.g., rental income, investment income, foreign employment income) pay no Libyan income tax on such income

### No Dedicated Newcomer Tax Incentive

Libya does not have a formal new-resident tax incentive beyond the general foreign income exemption.

## 2026 Changes

- **No structural PIT changes announced** as of EY data date (October 2025)
- **Legislative uncertainty:** The ongoing political transition in Libya means tax legislation may change at short notice; EY explicitly recommends obtaining updated information before transactions (EY p. 870)
- **No VAT system** currently in place
- **No inheritance or gift tax** in Libya (EY p. 872)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants; personal allowance LYD1,800.
> **1 EUR ≈ LYD 5.3** (approximate; USD/LYD ≈ 4.8 official rate; EUR/USD ≈ 1.09; verify at cbl.gov.ly).
> Employment: PIT (5%/10%) + SS employee 5.125% + Social Unity Fund 1%.
> B2B: same PIT rates; SS and Social Unity Fund apply; foreign nationals cannot be self-employed.

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | €30,000 × LYD 5.3 | LYD 159,000 |
| Personal allowance | | LYD 1,800 |
| Taxable income | 159,000 − 1,800 | LYD 157,200 |
| Tax on first LYD12,000 @ 5% | | LYD 600 |
| Tax on remaining LYD145,200 @ 10% | 10% × 145,200 | LYD 14,520 |
| **Total income tax** | | **LYD 15,120** |
| SS employee: 5.125% × 159,000 | | LYD 8,149 |
| Social Unity Fund: 1% × 159,000 | | LYD 1,590 |
| **Total burden** | 15,120 + 8,149 + 1,590 | **LYD 24,859** |
| **Effective rate** | 24,859 / 159,000 | **15.6%** |

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Legislative uncertainty:** EY explicitly warns that Libya's legislative situation is difficult to assess during the current political transition. All rates and rules should be verified with local Libyan counsel before making any decisions.
- [ ] **Multiple exchange rates:** Libya operates under both an official rate and parallel market rates. Tax calculations in LYD may not reflect economic reality in USD/EUR terms depending on which rate applies to salary payments.
- [ ] **Social security ceiling:** EY does not specify an income ceiling for Libyan social security contributions (5.125% employee / 15.375% employer). If there is no ceiling, SS becomes a significant percentage of total cost at high income levels. Verify with local counsel.
- [ ] **Foreign nationals and self-employment:** EY explicitly states foreign nationals may not be self-employed in Libya. All foreign workers must be employed by a licensed entity. This eliminates the B2B freelance option for expatriates.
- [ ] **LYD exchange rate:** Libya has maintained an official USD/LYD rate around 4.8 in recent years, but parallel rates exist. Capital controls and currency restrictions add complexity to practical planning.
- [ ] **No VAT:** Libya does not have a VAT or GST system as of October 2025. This simplifies self-employment compliance for Libyan nationals but may change as part of post-conflict economic reforms.
- [ ] **Civil servants exempt:** Civil servants and state employees are exempt from income tax — a significant carve-out given the large public sector in Libya.
