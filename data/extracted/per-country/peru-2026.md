## SYSTEM NOTE — ATU-Based Tax Brackets

> Peru uses **ATU (Annual Tax Unit / Unidad Impositiva Tributaria — UIT)** as the basis for tax brackets and deductions. The ATU value is set by the government at the start of each year.
>
> - **2025 ATU = PEN 5,350 (approximately USD 1,485)**
> - ATU is updated annually — all brackets below are in ATUs; multiply by PEN 5,350 for 2025 values.
>
> **Two main employment income categories:**
> - **5th Category (Quinta Categoría):** Employment income (employee-employer relationship) — progressive PIT with monthly withholding by employer
> - **4th Category (Cuarta Categoría):** Self-employment income (independent professional, artistic, scientific activities) — 8% provisional withholding by payers; annual declaration

## Personal Income Tax

Worldwide income for residents. Nonresidents taxed on Peru-source income only (flat 30% withholding). Residency: > 183 days in Peru (cumulative in any 12-month period). Foreign residency acquired as of 1 January of the calendar year after cumulative presence > 183 days. Exception: long-term contract abroad (≥ 1 year) or destination country resident visa = immediate loss of Peruvian residency. Tax year: calendar year. Return due May or early June. (EY pp.1219–1220)

**PIT rates — 5th Category (employment) and 4th Category (self-employment): (EY p.1223)**

| Taxable income (ATU) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|
| Up to 5 ATU | 8% |
| 5 to 20 ATU | 14% |
| 20 to 35 ATU | 17% |
| 35 to 45 ATU | 20% |
| Over 45 ATU | 30% |

**ATU in PEN (2025): 1 ATU = PEN 5,350**

| Taxable income (PEN 2025) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to PEN 26,750 | 8% |
| PEN 26,750 – PEN 107,000 | 14% |
| PEN 107,000 – PEN 187,250 | 17% |
| PEN 187,250 – PEN 240,750 | 20% |
| Over PEN 240,750 | 30% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY pp.1222–1223)
- **Personal allowance (1st deduction):** First 7 ATU of income from employment and self-employment = first PEN 37,450 exempt (2025)
- **Additional deduction (2nd deduction, up to 3 ATU = PEN 16,050):** Based on actual expenses with receipts — qualifying items include:
  - Housing rental payments
  - Services rendered by independent professionals (4th category)
  - Social security contributions of domestic workers (housemaids)
  - Consumption in restaurants and hotels (with invoices)
  - Total additional deduction capped at 3 ATU = PEN 16,050 (2025)
  - Maximum total deduction = 7 ATU (first, automatic) + 3 ATU (second, documented) = **10 ATU = PEN 53,500**
- Employee SS contributions: NOT specified as deductible from PIT base in EY source

**Exempt income:** (EY p.1221)
- Interest on bank deposits in Peruvian banks (exempt for residents and nonresidents)
- Interest on government bonds
- Capital gains on shares traded on the Peruvian Stock Exchange (if conditions met)

**Notes:**
- Nonresidents: flat 30% withholding on Peru-source income from employment (EY p.1220)
- Dividends: 5% final withholding (residents and nonresidents) (EY p.1221)
- Capital gains — real estate: 6.25% effective rate (5% effective on gross if deducting 20% on net basis) (EY p.1221)
- Capital gains — Peruvian shares (listed exchange, qualifying): exempt for residents; 5% if nonresident (EY p.1222)
- Capital gains — other Peruvian shares: 5% (residents); 30% (nonresidents) (EY p.1222)
- Rental income: 6.25% effective rate (5% on gross; 20% deemed deduction applies giving 5% net) (EY p.1221)
- 8% provisional withholding on 4th category fees paid by legal entities (credited against annual tax)
- Business income (personal business, not company): 29.5% on net profit (EY p.1221)
- Loss carryforward: business losses only — choice between 4-year carryforward OR indefinite carryforward limited to 50% of annual taxable income; no carryback
- Tax authority: SUNAT (Superintendencia Nacional de Aduanas y de Administración Tributaria)
- Double tax treaties: Andean Community (Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador), Brazil, Canada, Chile, Japan, Korea (South), Mexico, Portugal, Switzerland (EY p.1224)

## Social Security

Two pension systems (choose one); plus employer Health Care Fund. (EY pp.1223–1224)

| Contribution | Employee (ONP) | Employee (AFP) | Employer |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension (ONP — public system) | 13% | — | — |
| Pension (AFP — private system) | ~12% (a) | — | — |
| Health Care Fund (HCF/EsSalud) | — | — | 9% |
| **Total employee (ONP)** | **13%** | — | — |
| **Total employee (AFP)** | — | **~12%** | — |

(a) AFP contributions: approximately 10% pension fund + ~1.7% insurance premium + ~0.5%–1.5% administration fee = approximately 12%–13% total depending on AFP chosen. Must choose AFP or ONP.

**Notes:**
- Employers contribute 9% to Health Care Fund (EsSalud) on behalf of all employees; this funds basic healthcare
- If employer uses a private health provider (EPS), they may receive a credit of up to 25% of the HCF 9% contribution
- Employee pension contributions (ONP/AFP) are withheld and remitted by employers
- Nonresident employees starting work in Peru must register with ONP or AFP from first day
- Totalization agreements: Andean Community multilateral SS agreement (Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador); bilateral agreements include Chile (EY p.1224 — not explicitly listed but implied by treaty relationships)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — 4th Category (Cuarta Categoría — Independent Professional Services)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with income from independent professional, artistic, or scientific activities, or skilled occupations (e.g., doctors, lawyers, accountants, engineers, IT consultants).
- **Tax base:** Gross income **minus 20% deduction** (allowed up to maximum of 24 ATU = PEN 128,400 for 2025). After the 20% deduction, apply the 7 ATU personal allowance. (EY p.1221)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 8%–30% ATU-based rates on net taxable income. Payers (legal entities) withhold 8% provisionally on each fee payment; credited against annual declaration.
- **Social security:** No SS withholding from 4th category income; self-employed must pay SS voluntarily if desired (no mandatory SS for self-employed professionals in the traditional sense — verify with AFP/ONP for voluntary contributions).
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IGV (Peru VAT) at 18% on services; monthly filing.
- **Key limits:** 20% deduction maximum of PEN 128,400 (24 ATU). Professionals with fee income < PEN 46,813/year (approximate) may opt out of the 8% withholding.

### Option B — 3rd Category (Tercera Categoría — Business Income)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals conducting commercial, industrial, or business activities (not pure professional services).
- **Tax base:** Net profit (all business revenues minus deductible expenses).
- **Rate / mechanism:** 29.5% on net profit (business income rate).
- **Key limits:** Not available for pure personal service professionals (who fall under 4th category).

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### MERCOSUR Residence Visa
- **Eligibility:** Citizens of Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Paraguay, Uruguay — MERCOSUR Agreement.
- **Benefit:** 2-year residence visa allowing live and work in Peru; renewable for permanent residency (undetermined period, 3 months before expiry). Permanent residency requires proof of resources to live in Peru. (EY p.1228)

### Andean Migration Status (Estatuto Migratorio Andino)
- **Eligibility:** Citizens of Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador (Andean Community members).
- **Benefit:** 2-year residence and work visa; renewable for permanent residency. Same conditions as MERCOSUR. (EY p.1228)

### No Specific Digital Nomad Visa Confirmed
- Peru does not have a specific digital nomad visa as of EY data date. Nonresidents visiting on tourist visas (up to 183 days) and working for foreign clients generate no Peruvian-source income → no Peruvian PIT.

## 2026 Changes

- **ATU 2026:** ATU value for 2026 will be set by government decree early in the year. In 2025: PEN 5,350. Verify 2026 ATU at SUNAT.
- **AFP/ONP contributions:** No changes announced; AFP total approximately 12% depending on chosen fund administrator.
- **HCF (EsSalud):** 9% employer contribution — no changes announced.
- **PIT structure:** No changes to the 8%/14%/17%/20%/30% ATU-based rate structure announced.
- **Andean Community treaties:** Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador income excluded from Peruvian taxable income under Andean Pact (Decision 578). (EY p.1224)

**Key insight:** The 4th Category (self-employment) is dramatically more efficient than employment in Peru:
- At €30k: 5.7% (B2B) vs. 21.5% (employment)
- At €60k: 9.1% vs. 25.8%
- At €100k: 11.7% vs. 30.7%

The primary driver is: (1) the 20% automatic deduction for 4th category; (2) absence of mandatory ONP/AFP contributions for self-employed; (3) the same progressive PIT rates apply to a smaller base.

- Exchange rate: 1 EUR ≈ PEN 4.17 (approximate May 2026)

- 4th Category: 20% deduction applied (capped at 24 ATU = PEN 128,400); no mandatory SS
- No additional 3 ATU deduction (no documented housing/professional expenses claimed)
- Employer HCF (EsSalud 9%) not shown — employer-side burden only
- IGV (18% VAT) not shown for B2B

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **ATU 2026:** Set by government decree January 2026. 2025 value = PEN 5,350. Verify at sunat.gob.pe.
- [ ] **AFP vs. ONP choice:** AFP total ~12% vs. ONP 13%; choice affects net take-home. AFP (private) generally preferred by younger workers. Exact AFP fee structure depends on chosen administrator.
- [ ] **SS for 4th Category:** Whether self-employed professionals are required to make voluntary AFP/ONP contributions not fully confirmed — no mandatory SS assumed in B2B calculations above. Verify with AFP administrators.
- [ ] **Additional 3 ATU deduction:** Up to PEN 16,050 in documented housing/professional service expenses could reduce PIT further for both categories — not modeled.
- [ ] **IGV 18%:** Applies to self-employed providing services; not modeled.
- [ ] **Business income at 29.5%:** Applies to 3rd category business activities (not professional services); separate from 4th category.
- [ ] **No digital nomad visa:** No specific regime confirmed. Nonresident remote workers (< 183 days) generating only foreign-source income have no Peruvian tax liability.
- [ ] **Andean Community exemption:** Income from Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador may be excluded from Peruvian taxable income under Andean Pact Decision 578 — relevant for residents with Andean-source income.
