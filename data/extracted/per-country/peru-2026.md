## Personal Income Tax

**Territorial elements for non-residents; worldwide income for residents.** Residents: taxed on worldwide income. Non-residents: taxed only on Peruvian-source income. Tax year = calendar year. Employers withhold monthly. Self-employed (4th category income) required to file annual return in May/early June. The Superintendencia Nacional de Aduanas y de Administración Tributaria (SUNAT) administers. (EY p.1219–1220)

**Residence:** Physical presence >183 days in Peru in any 12-month period results in residency from 1 January of the following calendar year. Residency lost after spending >183 days abroad (with some exceptions for long-term contracts or visa holders). (EY p.1220)

**PIT brackets (for resident employees — "5th category income") — 2026** (EY p.1223; ATU updated per PwC 2026)
Rates applied in ATUs (Annual Tax Units). For 2026: **1 ATU = PEN 5,500** (PwC confirmed; was PEN 5,350 in 2025).

| Taxable income (ATU) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|
| 0 – 5 | 8% |
| 5 – 20 | 14% |
| 20 – 35 | 17% |
| 35 – 45 | 20% |
| Over 45 | 30% |

**PEN conversion (2026): 1 ATU = PEN 5,500** (INDEXATION; classification: INDEXATION)

| Taxable income (PEN) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 27,500 | 8% |
| 27,501 – 110,000 | 14% |
| 110,001 – 192,500 | 17% |
| 192,501 – 247,500 | 20% |
| Over 247,500 | 30% |

**Personal deduction:** PEN 38,500 (7 ATUs) — deducted from taxable income (applies to 5th category / employment and 4th category / self-employed income). (EY p.1222; updated for 2026 ATU)

**Additional deductions (up to 3 extra ATUs = PEN 16,500):** Actual documented expenses for:
- Housing rental payments (1 ATU = PEN 5,350 max)
- Services by independent professionals (medical, dental, etc.) (1 ATU max)
- Social security contributions by household workers (1 ATU max)
- Consumption in restaurants (the 1 ATU limit in earlier years; confirm current)
These replace the older itemized deduction system. (EY p.1222)

**Notes:**
- **4th category income (self-employed / independent professionals):** Taxed at the same 8%–30% progressive brackets. Taxable income = gross fees minus 20% deduction (capped at 24 ATUs = PEN 132,000 for 2026). (EY p.1220–1221)
- **Non-residents — employment income:** Flat 30% WHT on gross salary. No deductions. (EY p.1220)
- **Non-residents — independent professional:** 24% effective WHT (20% deduction from gross; 30% on 80% net = 24% of gross). (EY p.1221)
- **Business income (3rd category):** 29.5% corporate income tax rate on net business income; individual sole traders with 3rd category income pay at this rate.
- **Dividends and profit remittances:** 5% final WHT; foreign-source dividends for residents taxed at 5%.
- **Rental income:** 6.25% effective (5% net after 20% deduction allowed). (EY p.1221)
- **Capital gains on real estate (not principal residence):** 5% definitive payment of sale value.
- **Capital gains on listed Peruvian shares:** 5% (residents); 5% (non-residents if listed).
- **No capital gains on personal property sales.**
- **Financial transaction tax (ITF):** 0.005% on bank debits/credits. (EY p.1223)

## Social Security

Peru has two parallel pension systems; employees must choose one. Health contributions are mandatory through employers. (EY p.1223–1224)

| Contribution | Employee (ONP) | Employee (AFP) | Employer |
|---|---|---|---|
| National Pension System (ONP — SUNAT) | 13% of gross salary | — | — |
| Private Pension Fund (AFP) | ~12% of gross salary* | — | — |
| Health (EsSalud — HCF) | — | — | 9% of gross salary |
| SCTR (occupational risk insurance) | — | — | ~0.5–1% (varies) |

*AFP total ≈ 12%: ~10% retirement fund + ~1.5% insurance premium + ~1% AFP commission; varies by AFP (EY p.1223–1224)

**Notes:**
- Employees must choose ONP OR AFP — they cannot participate in both simultaneously
- ONP provides a defined benefit pension (requires min 20 years contributions); AFP provides individual capitalization account
- EsSalud (9% employer): funds public health system (ESSALUD); employees may use private EPS instead (employer pays 25% of EsSalud to EPS)
- Employee pension contributions (ONP 13% or AFP ~12%): deductible from 5th/4th category taxable income? — **No, pension contributions are NOT deductible from PIT base under current Peruvian law.** Employer EsSalud (9%) is a pure employer cost.
- Income ceiling: No ceiling on ONP/AFP contributions (based on full salary). EsSalud has no cap.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — 4th Category Income (Independientes — Liberal Professions)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual providing independent professional services (consultants, lawyers, doctors, architects, engineers, freelancers) who is not under an employment contract.
- **Tax base:** Gross 4th category income minus **20% standard deduction** (capped at 24 ATUs = PEN 132,000 for 2026) = net taxable income. Then minus personal deduction 7 ATUs = PEN 38,500.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 8%–30% on net taxable income (same brackets as 5th category employment).
- **Social security:** Self-employed pay AFP contributions voluntarily or may affiliate to ONP independently. No EsSalud employer contribution. No mandatory contribution.
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT on 4th category professional services (personal services are outside IGV scope). However, if providing services as a business (3rd category), IGV 18% applies.
- **Key limits:** Must issue recibos por honorarios (professional fee receipts) for each service; 8% WHT deducted by corporate payers (creditable against annual tax). Annual return due May/early June.

### Option B — Régimen Especial de Renta (RER) — Small Business

- **Who qualifies:** Small businesses (natural persons with business activity = 3rd category) with annual net income ≤ PEN 525,000 and net assets ≤ PEN 126,000 AND not in excluded activities (transport >2T, casinos, etc.).
- **Tax base:** Monthly gross income.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat 1.5% monthly on gross income (final; no annual return needed for CIT). Also liable for IGV 18%.
- **Social security:** Same as self-employed above.
- **VAT obligations:** IGV 18% on business sales/services.
- **Key limits:** Annual net income cap PEN 525,000; certain activities excluded.

### Option C — Nuevo RUS (Régimen Único Simplificado) — Micro-Enterprise

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with monthly gross income ≤ PEN 8,000 (Category 1) or ≤ PEN 13,000 (Category 2).
- **Tax base:** Fixed monthly payment (replaces both income tax and IGV).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Category 1: PEN 20/month; Category 2: PEN 50/month.
- **VAT obligations:** Included in fixed payment; no separate IGV filing.
- **Key limits:** Very low income ceiling; cannot issue invoices to corporations (limited to end consumers).

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Non-Resident Advantage — Worldwide Income NOT Taxed

- **Eligibility:** Individuals who have not yet established Peruvian tax residency (present <183 days in any rolling 12-month period).
- **Benefit:** Only Peruvian-source income taxed; all foreign-source income exempt. Very simple: a non-resident worker paid by a foreign employer for services rendered outside Peru pays zero Peruvian tax.
- **Duration:** Until >183 days in Peru triggers residency (effective from 1 January following year).
- **Caveats:** Once resident, worldwide income taxation applies (same progressive 8%–30% rates).

### Income from Andean Community Countries (Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador)

- Treaty under the Andean Pact: income earned in these countries by Peruvian residents may be excluded from taxable income in Peru (to avoid double taxation). (EY p.1224)

## 2026 Changes

- **ATU 2026 = PEN 5,500** (PwC confirmed; up from PEN 5,350 in 2025 — +2.8% INDEXATION). All PIT brackets in PEN updated accordingly. Personal deduction: PEN 38,500 (was 37,450). 4th category deduction cap: PEN 132,000 (was 128,400).
- **EsSalud rate:** 9% employer contribution — no announced changes.
- **AFP reform discussions:** Ongoing political discussions about AFP vs. ONP; no confirmed changes for 2026.
- **Capital gains on real estate:** The bright-line determination for "habitual" vs. "occasional" sales under development. No structural change announced.
- **RER/Nuevo RUS:** Thresholds subject to annual review; verify current PEN amounts at SUNAT.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, resident taxpayer, employment (5th category).
> PEN/EUR rate: 1 EUR = PEN 4.10 (approximate 2025; PEN ~3.75–3.90/USD × 1.08).
> 2026: 1 ATU = PEN 5,500 (PwC confirmed; updated from 5,350).
> Employment (5th category): progressive 8%–30% on net income after 7-ATU personal deduction (PEN 38,500).
> 4th category (self-employed): same brackets; 20% standard deduction capped at 24 ATUs (PEN 132,000) + 7 ATU personal deduction.
> Pension: employee pension (ONP 13% / AFP ~12%) NOT deductible from PIT base; shown as a burden.
> Employer EsSalud (9%): employer cost, not shown in employee effective rate.

### Working: PEN 123,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| ONP pension (13%) | 13% × 123,000 | PEN 15,990 |
| Personal deduction (7 ATUs) | 7 × 5,500 | PEN 38,500 |
| Taxable income | 123,000 − 38,500 | PEN 84,500 |
| PIT: 0–27,500 @ 8% | | PEN 2,200 |
| PIT: 27,501–84,500 @ 14% | 14% × 57,000 | PEN 7,980 |
| Total PIT | | PEN 10,180 |
| **Total burden (PIT + pension)** | **10,180 + 15,990** | **PEN 26,170** |
| **Effective rate (PIT only)** | **10,180 / 123,000** | **8.3%** |
| **Effective rate (all-in)** | **26,170 / 123,000** | **21.3%** |

- Taxable = 246,000 − 38,500 = 207,500
- 0–27,500 @ 8% = 2,200; 27,501–110,000 @ 14% = 11,550; 110,001–192,500 @ 17% = 14,025; 192,501–207,500 @ 20% = 3,000 → Total = 30,775

**4th category PIT — PEN 246,000:**
- 20% deduction = 49,200 (< 132,000 cap); taxable = 246,000 − 49,200 − 38,500 = 158,300
- 0–27,500 @ 8% = 2,200; 27,501–110,000 @ 14% = 11,550; 110,001–158,300 @ 17% = 8,211 → Total = 21,961

**4th category PIT — PEN 410,000:**
- 20% deduction = 82,000 (< 132,000 cap); taxable = 410,000 − 82,000 − 38,500 = 289,500
- 0–27,500 @ 8% = 2,200; 27,501–110,000 @ 14% = 11,550; 110,001–192,500 @ 17% = 14,025; 192,501–247,500 @ 20% = 11,000; 247,501–289,500 @ 30% = 12,600 → Total = 51,375

**Key insight:** Peru's 4th category (self-employed) regime is extremely favorable. The 20% automatic deduction + 7 ATU personal deduction means an effective tax rate of only 5.6% at €30k, 8.4% at €60k, and 10.6% at €100k of gross income. Employment rates are significantly higher (21–28%) when pension contributions are included. Peru also benefits from a partial territoriality principle: non-residents pay only 30% flat on Peru-source income (no deductions), but residents with foreign income could potentially benefit from Andean Pact exemptions.
