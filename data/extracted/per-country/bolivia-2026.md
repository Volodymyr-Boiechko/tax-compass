## Personal Income Tax

Bolivia uses an **unusual system**: the personal income tax is called **RC-IVA** (Régimen Complementario al Impuesto al Valor Agregado — Complementary Regime to Value-Added Tax). It is levied on all individuals domiciled or resident in Bolivia on their total income generated in Bolivia. The RC-IVA rate **equals the VAT rate: 13%**. (EY p.149, 151)

Non-residents are taxed on Bolivian-source income only. (EY p.149)

**Key feature of RC-IVA:** The tax base is structured to allow employees to offset the RC-IVA due by crediting:
- **13% of two minimum national salaries per month** (quarterly: 13% of six minimum national salaries per quarter) — the current minimum salary is BOB 2,750 (USD 395) (EY p.150)
- The VAT generated by personal purchases (invoices/receipts collected by the employee)

In practice, for most employees earning modest salaries, these credits entirely offset the RC-IVA liability, resulting in **zero effective income tax**.

**RC-IVA rate:**

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employment income (RC-IVA) | **13%** |
| Self-employment income (RC-IVA) | **13%** |
| Interest and royalties (residents) | **13%** (RC-IVA) |
| Dividends paid to Bolivian residents | **Not subject to RC-IVA** (EY p.150) |
| Dividends, interest, royalties to non-residents | **12.5% withholding** of corporate income tax |

(EY pp.149–151)

**From 1 January 2023 (Law 1448 and Supreme Decree 4850):**
- Income of professionals: now subject to RC-IVA at **13% quarterly** (previously subject to corporate income tax at 25% annually)
- RC-IVA triggered by fees/remuneration/income paid by foreign entities to non-domiciled individuals for work carried out in Bolivia: payable through Forms 613 and 614 within three days of following month or before the individual leaves Bolivia
- Income from accredited cultural delegation representations: exempt from RC-IVA (EY pp.149–150)

**Deductions and tax credits for employees (RC-IVA):**
- Employee social security contributions (12.71%) and national solidarity contribution (1.15%, 5.74%, or 11.48% depending on total salary): deductible from taxable income
- Non-taxable minimum: two minimum national salaries per month (2 × BOB 2,750 = BOB 5,500/month)
- Tax credits: 13% of one minimum national salary per month + VAT on personal purchases (invoices collected)

(EY p.151)

**Capital gains:** Capital gains derived by individuals are subject to RC-IVA. Capital losses can offset capital gains. (EY p.151)

**Non-taxable income (key items):**
- Christmas bonuses (first one mandatory; second only if annual GDP growth > 4.5%)
- Social benefits from indemnities and evictions
- Pre-family, wedding, birth, lactation, family and burial allowances per Social Security Code
- Retirement and pension income, sick allowances, professional risk allowances
- Life pensions for army members (wartime/peacetime injuries)
- Per diem and representation payments evidenced by local/foreign invoices (if company-related)
- Diplomatic/official personnel fees

(EY p.150)

## Social Security

Bolivia has a comprehensive social security system with both short-term (SSC) and long-term components. Based on total monthly remuneration. (EY pp.151–152)

**Short-Term SSC:**

| Institution | Employer | Employee |
|---|---|---|
| Healthcare entity (short-term health insurance) | **10%** | — |

**Long-Term SSC:**

| Institution | Employer | Employee |
|---|---|---|
| Housing Fund (Fondo de Vivienda / Provivienda, or FV) | **2%** | — |
| Professional risk (disability/death at work) | **1.71%** (2.3% for mining/metallurgy from Oct 2024) | — |
| Solidarity contribution | **3%** (increased to 3.5% from Oct 2024) | **0.5%** |
| Retirement fund (pension) | — | **10%** |
| Common risk (disability/death outside work) | — | **1.71%** |
| Administration commission | — | **0.5%** |
| National Solidarity Contribution (NSC) | — | **10%/5%/1.15%** (see below) |

**National Solidarity Contribution (employee, NSC) — graduated by salary level:**
- 11.48% of difference between total earned and BOB 35,000
- 5.74% of difference between total earned and BOB 25,000
- 1.15% of difference between total earned and BOB 13,000

(EY p.152)

**Long-term SSC ceiling:** Mandatory long-term SSCs paid on base of 60 national minimum wages = **BOB 165,000/year** (USD 23,707) (EY p.152)

**Total approximate social security (typical employee):**

| Contribution | Employee (approx.) | Employer (approx.) |
|---|---|---|
| All SS components combined | ~**12.71% + NSC** | ~**16.71%–17.21%** |

**Contributions of foreigners:** Under Law 65 (December 2010), expatriates must pay Bolivian social security. Contributions can potentially be transferred to home country if: (1) employment relationship ends definitively, (2) expatriate has not accessed Bolivian pension, and (3) bilateral/multilateral SS agreement is in effect. Transfer procedures not yet fully implemented. (EY p.152)

**Totalization agreements:** Ibero-American Multilateral Agreement on Social Security (with Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Dominican Republic, Ecuador, El Salvador, Spain, Paraguay, Peru, Portugal, Uruguay effectively implemented). (EY p.152)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed Professional (RC-IVA + VAT + Transaction Tax)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals providing professional services in Bolivia (from 1 January 2023)
- **Tax base:** Net income exclusive of VAT
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - **VAT: 13%** on monthly revenue (paid monthly)
  - **Transaction tax (IT): 3%** on each service rendered (paid monthly)
  - **RC-IVA: 13%** on net income exclusive of VAT (paid quarterly); credits: 13% of six minimum salaries per quarter + VAT on personal purchases
- **Social security:** Same contributions as employees (10% employer-equivalent + employee contributions)
- **VAT obligations:** 13% VAT on services; offset by input VAT credits
- **Key limits:** Revenue below BOB 8 million/year may qualify for simplified regime (STR)

(EY pp.149–151)

### Option B — Simplified Tax Regime (STR — Régimen Tributario Simplificado)

- **Who qualifies:** Small traders and artisans with annual revenue ≤ BOB 184,000 (~USD 26,500)
- **Tax base:** Fixed tax amount based on asset categories
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed quarterly payment (not revenue-based); very low amounts
- **Social security:** Voluntary
- **Key limits:** Strict revenue and asset limits; available to traders/artisans only (not professional services)

(General Bolivian tax law; not detailed in EY)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No specific preferential tax regime for newcomers or digital nomads described in EY. Bolivia taxes on a source/domicile basis; non-residents pay RC-IVA only on Bolivian-source income. (EY p.149)

## 2026 Changes

- **Professional income now under RC-IVA (from 1 January 2023):** Professionals previously taxed at 25% corporate income tax rate annually; now taxed at 13% RC-IVA quarterly — significant reduction for most professionals (EY p.149)
- **Non-domiciled individual withholding (from 1 January 2023):** RC-IVA applies to non-domiciled individuals working in Bolivia under contracts with foreign entities; must file Forms 613 and 614 (EY p.149–150)
- **Professional risk insurance increase (October 2024):** Employer contribution for professional risk increased from 1.71% to **2.3% for mining and metallurgical sector** (EY p.152)
- **Solidarity contribution increase (October 2024):** Employer solidarity contribution increased from 3% to **3.5%** (EY p.152)
- No changes to the fundamental 13% RC-IVA rate announced.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Bolivia's RC-IVA is unusual: the flat 13% rate is significantly offset by credits (minimum salary credits + personal VAT purchases). For most employees earning below ~BOB 30,000/month, the effective income tax after credits is near **0%**.
> **1 EUR ≈ BOB 7.5** (approximate; BOB/USD ≈ 6.96; EUR/USD ≈ 1.08; BOB 7.5/EUR approximate).
> Employment scenario: 13% RC-IVA minus credits; SS employee contributions ~12.71%.
> B2B scenario: 13% RC-IVA + 3% transaction tax + 13% VAT (VAT passed to clients); net income tax ~13%.

### Minimum salary credit calculation:
- Monthly credit: 13% × (2 × BOB 2,750) = 13% × 5,500 = BOB 715/month
- Plus VAT on personal purchases (variable; BOB 500–1,500/month typical)
- Effective RC-IVA credit: ~BOB 715–2,215/month depending on personal purchases

### €30,000 Employment (≈ BOB 225,000/year = BOB 18,750/month)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross monthly salary | BOB 18,750 | |
| SS deductions (12.71% + 0.5% solidarity + 10% pension + 1.71% common risk + 0.5% admin) | ~12.71% + ~12.72% employee-side | BOB 2,383 employee SS |
| SS-adjusted gross for RC-IVA | 18,750 − SS deductions | ~BOB 16,367 |
| RC-IVA (13%) | 13% × 16,367 | BOB 2,128 |
| Minimum salary credit (13% × 2 × BOB 2,750) | BOB 715/month | BOB 715 |
| VAT personal purchases credit (est.) | ~BOB 600/month | BOB 600 |
| Net RC-IVA after credits | 2,128 − 715 − 600 | BOB 813 |
| Monthly total burden (net RC-IVA + employee SS) | 813 + 2,383 | BOB 3,196 |
| Annual total burden | × 12 | BOB 38,352 |
| **Effective rate** | 38,352 / 225,000 | **17.0%** |

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/BOB = 7.5 (approximate; verify at bcb.gob.bo)
- Employment: employee SS modelled at ~12.71% (employee-side all contributions combined); RC-IVA credits modelled at BOB 715/month minimum salary credit + BOB 600/month personal purchase VAT credit
- B2B: RC-IVA 13% on net income + transaction tax (IT) 3% = 16% combined (VAT 13% excluded as passed to clients); no VAT credits on own purchases modelled
- National Solidarity Contribution (NSC) at higher income levels: 1.15% on salary above BOB 13,000 applied; 5.74% above BOB 25,000 — included in employee SS estimates
- SS ceiling BOB 165,000/year (long-term); above this only short-term health SS (10% employer) applies
