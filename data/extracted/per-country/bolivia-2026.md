## SYSTEM NOTE — Bolivia's Unusual RC-IVA Mechanism

> Bolivia does NOT have a conventional progressive income tax. Instead, personal income tax is the **RC-IVA** (Régimen Complementario al Impuesto al Valor Agregado), which works as follows:
>
> - **Rate:** Flat **13%** on all taxable income.
> - **Key offset mechanism:** Employees and self-employed persons can credit **13% of VAT invoices** (receipts for personal consumption) against their RC-IVA liability. In effect, if you collect enough fiscal invoices, you can reduce or eliminate your RC-IVA to zero.
> - **Automatic credit floor:** 13% of two minimum national salaries per month (2 × BOB 2,750 = BOB 715/month credit, automatic for employees).
> - **Practical result:** Many salaried employees pay little or no RC-IVA once invoices and the minimum credit are applied.
> - **Self-employed professionals (from 1 January 2023):** Previously taxed under corporate IUE at 25%. Now subject to RC-IVA at 13% quarterly (Law 1448, July 2022 reform).
> - **Businesses:** Subject to IUE (Impuesto sobre las Utilidades de las Empresas) at 25% on net profit — corporate income tax, not personal.

## Personal Income Tax (RC-IVA)

RC-IVA is a flat complementary tax on employment and personal income. Tax year runs quarterly for self-employed; employers withhold monthly for employees. Residents (domiciled in Bolivia) and nonresidents are both taxed on Bolivian-source income only — Bolivia is fully territorial. (EY pp.149–151)

**Rate:** Flat **13%** — no progressive brackets for individuals.

| Income type | RC-IVA treatment |
|---|---|
| Employment income (salaries, wages, bonuses) | 13% flat; employer withholds monthly |
| Self-employment / professional fees (from 2023) | 13% flat; quarterly self-assessment |
| Dividends paid to Bolivian residents | Not subject to RC-IVA |
| Interest and royalties (residents) | Subject to RC-IVA at 13% |
| Capital gains | Subject to RC-IVA; losses may offset gains |
| Nonresident dividends, interest, royalties | 12.5% corporate withholding |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Employee SS contributions (12.71% pension/solidarity) deducted from RC-IVA base before applying 13%
- National Solidarity Contribution (employee portion) deducted from base
- Minimum monthly credit: 13% × 1 minimum national salary = BOB 357.50/month (BOB 4,290/year)
- VAT invoice credit: 13% of value of personal purchase invoices submitted (no cap specified)

**Nontaxable items (excluded from RC-IVA base):** (EY p.150)
- Christmas bonuses (first mandatory; second only if annual GDP growth > 4.5%)
- Social benefits from indemnifications and evictions per labour law
- Pre-family, wedding, birth, lactation, family and burial allowances
- Retirement, pension, sick and professional risk allowances
- Life pensions (army)
- Per diem and representation payments evidenced by invoices as company-related
- Diplomatic personnel salaries

**Notes:**
- Bolivia is fully territorial: foreign-source income exempt for all individuals
- Self-employed professionals: switched from IUE 25% to RC-IVA 13% from 1 January 2023 (significant rate reduction)
- Non-domiciled individuals providing services under Bolivian contracts must pay RC-IVA via Forms 613 and 614 within 3 days of month-end (EY p.150)

## Business / Corporate Income Tax (IUE — for context)

Individuals conducting commercial/industrial activity (not professional services) remain subject to IUE:
- **Rate:** 25% on net profit
- **Nonresidents:** 12.5% withholding on dividends, interest, and royalties from Bolivian sources (EY p.150)

## Social Security

Mandatory contributions for all employees and employers based on total monthly remuneration. (EY pp.151–152)

| Contribution | Employer % | Employee % |
|---|---|---|
| Healthcare entity — Short Term SSC | 10 | — |
| Housing Fund (Provivienda/FV) | 2 | — |
| Professional risk insurance | 1.71 / 2.3 (a) | — |
| Solidarity Contribution | 3 / 3.5 (b) | 0.5 |
| Retirement fund (pension) | — | 10 |
| Common risk | — | 1.71 |
| Administration commission | — | 0.5 |
| National Solidarity Contribution (NSC) | — | 10 / 5 / 1.15 (c) |
| **Total (approx, excl. NSC)** | **~16.71%** | **~12.71%** |

(a) Professional Risk increased to 2.3% for mining/metallurgical sector (Law 1582, October 2024).  
(b) Solidarity Contribution increased to 3.5% (Law 1582, October 2024).  
(c) NSC tiers (employee only): 11.48% on difference above BOB 35,000/month; 5.74% above BOB 25,000/month; 1.15% above BOB 13,000/month.

**Income ceiling for long-term SSC:** BOB 165,000 (60 minimum national salaries × BOB 2,750). (EY p.152)

**Notes:**
- Foreigners must contribute to Bolivian SS under Law 65 (December 2010); contributions transferable to home country if expatriate definitively departs and home-country SS agreement exists
- Totalization agreement: Ibero-American Multilateral Agreement (active: Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Dominican Republic, Ecuador, El Salvador, Spain, Paraguay, Peru, Portugal, Uruguay)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — RC-IVA for Individual Professionals (post-2023)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual professionals providing personal services (freelancers, consultants, independent contractors). Effective 1 January 2023 (Law 1448 / Supreme Decree 4850).
- **Tax base:** Gross income from professional services, reduced by deductible SS contributions.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 13% RC-IVA flat; quarterly self-assessment. VAT invoice credits (13% of personal purchases) offset liability.
- **Social security:** Subject to Bolivian SS contributions under Law 65.
- **VAT obligations:** Also subject to IVA (VAT) at 13% on services (monthly), plus IT (Impuesto a las Transacciones — Transaction Tax) at 3% on each service rendered (monthly). (EY p.150)
- **Key limits:** The combination of IVA 13% + IT 3% + RC-IVA 13% means total statutory exposure can reach ~29% on gross revenue before invoice credits. IVA is charged to clients and remitted; IT and RC-IVA are the professional's own burden.

### Option B — IUE Regime for Businesses

- **Who qualifies:** Businesses, partnerships, individuals conducting commercial/industrial activity.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (revenues minus deductible business expenses).
- **Rate / mechanism:** 25% IUE on annual net profit.
- **VAT obligations:** IVA 13% applies.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; full expense deductibility.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Territorial Taxation Principle
- **Eligibility:** All individuals in Bolivia (resident or nonresident).
- **Benefit:** Only Bolivian-source income is taxed. Foreign-source income is completely exempt.
- **Duration:** Permanent feature of the system.
- **Caveats:** "Domicile" assessed on facts (employment, family location) — a work permit alone does not establish tax domicile. (EY p.149)

### No Specific Digital Nomad / NHR Regime
- Bolivia has no equivalent to Portugal's NHR or Costa Rica's Digital Nomads Law as of EY data date.
- Foreigners working remotely for foreign clients from Bolivia are subject to RC-IVA on Bolivian-source income only — if all clients/payers are abroad, Bolivian tax may not apply.

---

## 2026 Changes

- **RC-IVA for professionals (from 1 January 2023, fully operative in 2026):** Individual professionals pay RC-IVA at 13% quarterly rather than IUE at 25% annually — significant rate reduction.
- **Law 1582 (September 2024, operative October 2024):** Professional Risk employer contribution raised to 2.3% (mining/metallurgical); Solidarity Fund employer contribution raised to 3.5%.
- **Minimum national salary:** BOB 2,750/month (verify annually by government decree).
- No major structural changes to RC-IVA or IUE announced for 2026.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.  
> "Employment" = gross salary; RC-IVA withheld by employer; SS contributions deducted.  
> "B2B" = self-employed professional under RC-IVA regime; IVA 13% and IT 3% are excluded (separate obligations charged to clients / remitted separately).  
> 1 EUR ≈ BOB 7.56 (approximate May 2026; 1 USD ≈ BOB 6.86 official rate).

| Gross income | EUR equiv | Scenario | RC-IVA (net of credits) | SS employee | NSC | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| BOB 226,800 | ~€30,000 | Employment | ~15,444 | ~28,824 | ~814 | ~45,082 | **~19.9%** |
| BOB 226,800 | ~€30,000 | B2B (RC-IVA) | ~19,297 | ~28,824 | — | ~48,121 | **~21.2%** |
| BOB 453,600 | ~€60,000 | Employment | ~37,525 | ~28,824 | ~12,401 | ~78,750 | **~17.4%** |
| BOB 453,600 | ~€60,000 | B2B (RC-IVA) | ~42,884 | ~28,824 | — | ~71,708 | **~15.8%** |
| BOB 756,000 | ~€100,000 | Employment | ~65,482 | ~20,972 | ~47,117 | ~133,571 | **~17.7%** |
| BOB 756,000 | ~€100,000 | B2B (RC-IVA) | ~74,334 | ~20,972 | — | ~95,306 | **~12.6%** |

**Working — BOB 226,800/year (≈ €30,000) Employment:**
- Employee SS: 12.71% × 226,800 = BOB 28,824
- NSC: 1.15% × (226,800 − 156,000) = BOB 814 (1.15% tier: income > BOB 13k/month = > BOB 156k/year)
- RC-IVA base: 226,800 − 28,824 − 814 = BOB 197,162
- RC-IVA gross: 13% × 197,162 = BOB 25,631
- Credits: minimum BOB 4,290 + invoice credits (13% × 20% × 226,800) = BOB 4,290 + BOB 5,897 = BOB 10,187
- Net RC-IVA: 25,631 − 10,187 = BOB 15,444

**Working — BOB 756,000/year (≈ €100,000) Employment:**
- SS capped: 12.71% × 165,000 = BOB 20,972
- NSC (tiered): ≈ 11.48% × (756,000 − 420,000) + 5.74% × (420,000 − 300,000) + 1.15% × (300,000 − 156,000) = 38,573 + 6,888 + 1,656 = BOB 47,117
- RC-IVA base: 756,000 − 20,972 − 47,117 = 687,911
- RC-IVA gross: 13% × 687,911 = BOB 89,428
- Credits: 4,290 + 13% × 20% × 756,000 = 4,290 + 19,656 = BOB 23,946
- Net RC-IVA: 89,428 − 23,946 = BOB 65,482

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR ≈ BOB 7.56 (approximate May 2026)
- SS long-term cap: BOB 165,000/year (60 × BOB 2,750 minimum salary)
- Minimum monthly credit: BOB 357.50/month = BOB 4,290/year
- Invoice credits: 13% × 20% of gross income (moderate consumer spending; aggressive collectors could reduce RC-IVA further toward zero)
- NSC annual thresholds: BOB 156,000 (1.15% tier), BOB 300,000 (5.74%), BOB 420,000 (11.48%)
- B2B excludes IVA 13% (pass-through charged to clients) and IT 3% (transaction tax on gross revenue)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Invoice credit mechanism:** RC-IVA can be reduced to near zero with aggressive invoice collection. The 20% spending assumption is illustrative only — actual burden depends entirely on invoice habits.
- [ ] **Minimum national salary 2026:** BOB 2,750 is most recently confirmed; updated by decree — verify at min.gob.bo.
- [ ] **Exchange rate uncertainty:** Official BOB 6.86/USD maintained but Bolivia has experienced foreign currency shortages since 2023; parallel market rates may exist.
- [ ] **NSC for self-employed:** Applicability of National Solidarity Contribution tiers to self-employed not confirmed in EY source; excluded from B2B calculation above.
- [ ] **IT (transaction tax) not in effective rate table:** Self-employed owe 3% IT on each service transaction; this adds ~3 percentage points to the B2B effective rate if included.
- [ ] **IVA for B2B not quantified:** 13% IVA charged to clients and remitted to the state — not a net burden on the professional but affects cash flow and client pricing.
- [ ] **No digital nomad regime confirmed.**
- [ ] **SS for self-employed full structure:** Long-term SS rates for self-employed individuals not fully specified in EY source.
