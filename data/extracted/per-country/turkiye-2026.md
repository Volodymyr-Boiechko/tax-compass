## Personal Income Tax

Unitary progressive system — all income aggregated and taxed at progressive rates. Withholding taxes are advance payments credited against annual PIT. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return due 1–31 March of the following year. Employers withhold from salaries monthly and declare by the 26th of the following month. Administered by the Revenue Administration (Gelir İdaresi Başkanlığı — GİB).

**Residency:** Legal permanent residence in Türkiye; OR physical presence for more than 6 months in one calendar year (temporary absence does not interrupt continuity). "Intention to settle permanently" under civil law is key criterion.

**Non-residents (limited liability taxpayers):** Taxed only on Türkiye-sourced earnings and revenues.

**Exception:** Businesspersons, scientists, experts, government/international organization employees, those arriving for education/medical/treatment/rest/travel — treated as non-residents even if present >6 months.

**Note:** Brackets are adjusted annually for inflation. The 2025 brackets above (effective for 2025 income year) are as published in EY; 2026 brackets will be updated by the tax authority in late 2025 / early 2026.

**Stamp duty:** Remuneration paid by local employers is also subject to 0.759% stamp duty on gross salary (EY p. 1557).

**Minimum wage credit:** The minimum wage (TRY 26,005.50/month gross from 1 January 2025 to 31 December 2025) is exempt from income tax and stamp tax. Employees earning above minimum wage receive a credit equal to the tax that would be computed on minimum wage (EY p. 1557).

**Standard deductions / allowances (EY pp. 1556–1557):**
- Employee-side social security (SGK) and unemployment insurance premiums: deductible from gross employment income
- Life/health insurance premiums: deductible up to 15% of monthly salary, total annual premium ≤ annual legal minimum wage (TRY 26,005.50/month × 12 = TRY 312,066 for 2025); policy must be with a Türkiye-headquartered company
- Business expenses for self-employed / commercial activities: ordinary business-related expenses including salaries, rental payments, utility fees, cost of goods, depreciation (not penalties)
- Self-employed / commercial: business losses may be carried forward 5 years

**Notes:**
- Capital gains from disposal of listed shares (ISE): 0% withholding (final tax) if sold through a bank or intermediary institution (Council of Ministers' Decree)
- Capital gains from non-listed shares of Turkish resident companies held > 2 years: not subject to income tax
- Capital gains from non-listed shares held ≤ 2 years or non-resident-issued shares: subject to progressive rates
- Rental income and royalties (residents): subject to 20% withholding (advance); included in annual return
- Dividends: subject to withholding (final); progressive rates apply on top of corporate-level 15% withholding for large dividends (confirm current withholding rate)
- Self-employment: recipients withhold 20% on payments to independent service providers; service providers include in annual return

## Social Security

Turkish social security unified under SGK (Sosyal Güvenlik Kurumu) since 1 October 2008 (Law No. 5510 — EY p. 1558):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| SGK (health + pension + disability + death) | **14%** | **20.5%** | On gross monthly salary |
| Unemployment insurance | **1%** | **2%** | On gross monthly salary |
| **Total** | **15%** | **22.5%** | |

**SGK Salary Ceiling (1 January 2025 – 31 December 2025 — EY p. 1558–1559):**
- Upper limit: **TRY 195,041.40/month** (= ~7.5× minimum wage)
- Lower limit (minimum insurable salary): **TRY 26,005.50/month** (= minimum wage)

**Employer reimbursement:** 5% of employer SGK contribution (= 1% of total) may be reimbursed by the Republic of Türkiye Prime Ministry Undersecretary of Treasury if certain conditions are met (net employer cost = 19.5%).

**Notes:**
- SGK and unemployment insurance (employee portion) are deductible from taxable employment income
- Expatriates subject to social security in their home country may be exempt from Turkish SGK if they prove their status with documentation from the foreign social security institution (EY p. 1559)
- Türkiye has bilateral totalization agreements with many countries (Albania, Austria, Azerbaijan, Belgium, Bosnia, Canada, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, France, Georgia, Germany, Hungary, Iran, Italy, South Korea, Kyrgyzstan, Libya, Luxembourg, Moldova, Mongolia, Montenegro, Netherlands, North Macedonia, Norway, Poland, Quebec, Romania, Serbia, Slovak Republic, Sweden, Switzerland, Tunisia, UK, and others — EY p. 1559)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Independent Professional Service (Serbest Meslek Kazancı)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals providing independent professional services in their own name, using their own professional knowledge, without being dependent on an employer (EY p. 1554)
- **Tax base:** Gross fees received less deductible ordinary business expenses
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 15%–40% rates (same schedule as employment); withholding 20% on each payment made to independent service provider — credited against annual PIT
- **Social security:** SGK Bağ-Kur (self-employed): 20.5% employee + employer equivalent on declared income between lower and upper SGK limits (TRY 26,005.50–TRY 195,041.40/month)
- **VAT obligations:** Must register for VAT; standard rate 20% (reduced rates 1% and 10% for certain items); issue invoices; file monthly VAT returns
- **Key limits:** Quarterly advance PIT payments at 15% of net income due by 17th of second month after each quarter; annual return by 31 March

### Option B — Commercial Activity (Ticari Kazanç)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals operating commercial or industrial businesses in Türkiye
- **Tax base:** Net commercial profit = gross revenue less deductible business expenses (depreciation, wages, interest, rent, 75% of entertainment/meal costs)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 15%–40% rates; quarterly advance payments (15% of net income)
- **Social security:** SGK Bağ-Kur applies on same basis as Option A
- **VAT obligations:** Same as Option A (20% standard VAT)
- **Key limits:** Business losses carried forward up to 5 years; no loss carrybacks; annual PIT return by 31 March

### Option C — Digital Nomad Visa (Remote Workers)

- **Who qualifies:** Foreign citizens employed by a company outside Türkiye, working remotely in Türkiye, without needing a residence permit or work permit (EY p. 1567)
- **Tax treatment:** EY notes this visa option exists (Ministry of Culture and Tourism); tax implications not detailed — likely non-resident status if not present ≥ 6 months; otherwise normal resident PIT applies
- **Key limits:** Must maintain employment ties to foreign company; no local employer

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No General Newcomer Exemption

Türkiye does not have a general territorial or remittance-basis regime for new residents. All tax residents pay progressive PIT on worldwide income.

### Liaison Office Exemption (EY p. 1554)

- Individuals at liaison offices (irtibat bürosu) compensated in foreign currency qualify for full exemption if: (a) non-resident entity pays from earnings derived abroad; (b) salary not charged against Turkish profits; (c) compensation brought into Türkiye as foreign currency

### Turquoise Card (Turkuaz Kart) — EY pp. 1565–1566

- Highly qualified foreign professionals, investors, and scientists may obtain a Turquoise Card (indefinite work permit equivalent; 3-year transition period)
- Exempts holder from military duty obligations in Türkiye
- Social security rights preserved per applicable legislation
- Tax treatment: same progressive rates as residents once resident status established

## 2026 Changes

- **2025 brackets:** The brackets shown (TRY 158,000 first band at 15%) are 2025 figures; **2026 brackets will be significantly higher** due to inflation (annual bracket adjustments by GİB typically in December/January); as of EY data date, 2026 brackets not yet published
- **Minimum wage 2026:** Not yet announced at EY data date; will set new SGK lower limit and minimum wage credit amount for 2026
- **SGK ceiling 2026:** Will rise proportionally with minimum wage; upper limit = ~7.5× minimum wage
- **Stamp duty rate:** 0.759% on gross salary — unchanged
- **Digital Nomad Visa:** Introduced 2022; operational as of EY data date; tax framework not codified in EY guide
