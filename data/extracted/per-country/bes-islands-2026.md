## Personal Income Tax

Residents of the BES-Islands are taxable on worldwide income. Non-residents are taxable only on income derived from certain sources in the BES-Islands. The BES-Islands became extraordinary overseas municipalities of the Netherlands on 10 October 2010; the current tax regime has been in force since 1 January 2011. (EY p.156)

Currency: **US Dollar (USD)** — the official currency of the BES-Islands.

Residence is determined based on domicile (permanent home), physical presence, and location of vital personal and economic interests. (EY p.156)

**PIT rates (BES-Islands):**

| Taxable income (USD) | Tax on lower amount (USD) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 53,198 | 0 | **29.4%** |
| 53,198+ | 15,580 | **38.4%** |

(EY p.159)

**Special rates:**
- Income from a substantial share interest in a company: **5%** flat rate (EY p.159–160)
- Capital gains from disposal of business assets / independent activities: **29.4%–38.4%** (progressive)
- Capital gains from liquidation / share repurchase above paid-up capital (non-substantial): **29.4%–38.4%**
- Capital gains from substantial share interest (≥ 5% equity): **7.5%** (EY p.158)
- Capital gains (general): **exempt** from tax in most circumstances (EY p.158)

**Deemed salary (employees with >5% employer interest):**
- Minimum deemed salary: **USD 43,912** for the 2026 calendar year (EY p.157)

**Personal tax credits (residents only):**
- Fixed tax credit: **USD 21,956**
- Senior allowance: **USD 1,677**

(EY p.159)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**

| Deduction | Limit |
|---|---|
| Employment expense fixed deduction | USD 280 |
| Actual employment-related expenses (if > USD 560) | Fully deductible |
| Alimony to former spouse | Actual |
| Mortgage interest (taxpayer's dwelling) | Max USD 15,364/year |
| Dwelling maintenance expenses | 2% of dwelling value; max USD 1,676/year |
| Fire and natural disaster insurance premiums (dwelling) | Actual |
| Interest on consumer loans | Max USD 1,397/year (USD 2,794 if married) |
| Life insurance premiums entitling to annuity | Max 5% of income or USD 559/year |
| Pension premiums | Actual |
| Qualifying charitable donations | Above threshold |
| Extraordinary expenses (financial support to dependants 27+, medical, educational, support to second-degree relatives) | Above thresholds |

**Business deductions (self-employed):**
- Business expenses: fully deductible; limited deductions for certain items
- Accelerated depreciation: max 33⅓% per year
- Investment allowance: 8% for acquisitions of/improvements to fixed assets (12% for new buildings/improvements, if investment > USD 2,794/year)

(EY pp.158–159)

**Notes:**
- Capital gains are generally exempt from tax (EY p.158)
- No net worth tax or inheritance tax described in EY for BES-Islands
- Filing: annual personal income tax return within 2 months of issuance of tax return forms; wage tax (pre-levy) filed quarterly by employer by 15th of following month

## Social Security

All resident individuals must pay social security contributions. (EY p.160)

**Employee contributions:**

| Scheme | Employee rate |
|---|---|
| AOV (General Old Age Pension Act) | **25.0%** |
| AWW (General Widows, Orphans Act) | **1.3%** |
| Health Insurance (BZV) | **0.5%** |
| **Total employee** | **26.8%** |

**Employer contributions:**

| Scheme | Employer rate |
|---|---|
| OV (Accident Insurance Act) | **0.2%** |
| Cessantia (severance contribution) | **0.1%** |
| Health Insurance (BZV) | **9.3%** |
| ZV (Health Insurance Act) | **0.7%** |
| **Total employer** | **10.3%** |

**Income ceiling:** Maximum premium base for AOV, AWW, and Health Insurance (employee): **USD 38,390/year**. OV, Cessantia, ZV, and part of BZV are only due in case of employment. (EY p.160)

**Notes:**
- Social security contributions withheld by employer; declared with wage tax returns
- Collection interest rate: **6% as of 1 January 2026** (EY p.160)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed / Sole Trader

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual conducting business or professional activities in the BES-Islands
- **Tax base:** Annual profits = gross revenue minus fully deductible business expenses; personal deductions and allowances subtracted
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT rates: 29.4% on first USD 53,198; 38.4% above; fixed tax credit USD 21,956 applies
- **Social security:** Social security contributions apply (employee-equivalent rates; specific self-employed rates not separately stated)
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT in BES-Islands (the Netherlands' VAT system does not apply); instead a **general goods tax (ABB)** at 8% on goods and 6% on services applies in Bonaire
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling

(EY pp.156–159)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Facility

- **Eligibility:**
  - Must not have been a resident of the BES-Islands for the past 5 years
  - Must have special skills at college/university level + at least 5 years' relevant working experience
  - Must receive remuneration of at least **USD 83,500** from employer
  - Must possess skills scarcely available in the BES-Islands
- **Benefit:** Tax-free fringe benefits including wages in kind, travel expenses, hotel expenses, relocation expenses, and means of transportation
- **Application:** Employer must file application; applies retroactively to start of employment if filed within 3 months
- **Net employment contract:** Permitted; wage tax not grossed up as additional taxable benefit
- **Caveats:** Remainder of compensation taxed at progressive rates (29.4%–38.4%) (EY p.159)

## 2026 Changes

- **Deemed salary minimum increased to USD 43,912 for 2026 calendar year** (applies to employees with > 5% interest in their employer) (EY p.157)
- **Collection interest rate set at 6% from 1 January 2026** (EY p.160)
- No PIT rate changes announced; 29.4%/38.4% brackets remain in force.
- Fixed tax credit (USD 21,956) and senior allowance (USD 1,677) remain for 2026.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (fixed tax credit USD 21,956; employment deduction USD 280).
> **1 EUR ≈ USD 1.08** (BES-Islands use USD as official currency).
> Employment scenario: progressive PIT (29.4%/38.4%); employee SS 26.8% capped at USD 38,390 base.
> B2B scenario: self-employed; progressive PIT; SS at employee-equivalent rates.

### €30,000 Employment (≈ USD 32,400/year)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross income | USD 32,400 | |
| Employment deduction | USD 280 | |
| Fixed tax credit | USD 21,956 | |
| Taxable income | 32,400 − 280 − 21,956 | USD 10,164 |
| PIT (29.4% on USD 10,164) | 29.4% × 10,164 | USD 2,988 |
| Employee SS: AOV 25% + AWW 1.3% + Health 0.5% = 26.8% × min(32,400; 38,390) | 26.8% × 32,400 | USD 8,683 |
| **Total burden** | 2,988 + 8,683 | **USD 11,671** |
| **Effective rate** | 11,671 / 32,400 | **36.0%** |

### €30,000 B2B — self-employed (≈ USD 32,400/year)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue | USD 32,400 | |
| Fixed tax credit | USD 21,956 | |
| Taxable income | USD 10,444 | |
| PIT (29.4%) | 29.4% × 10,444 | USD 3,071 |
| SS (26.8%, capped) | 26.8% × 32,400 | USD 8,683 |
| **Total burden** | 3,071 + 8,683 | **USD 11,754** |
| **Effective rate** | 11,754 / 32,400 | **36.3%** |

### Summary table

| Gross income (USD) | EUR equiv | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| USD 32,400 | €30,000 | Employment | USD 2,988 | USD 8,683 | USD 11,671 | **36.0%** |
| USD 32,400 | €30,000 | B2B self-employed | USD 3,071 | USD 8,683 | USD 11,754 | **36.3%** |
| USD 64,800 | €60,000 | Employment | USD 11,786 | USD 10,289 (capped) | USD 22,075 | **34.1%** |
| USD 64,800 | €60,000 | B2B self-employed | USD 11,869 | USD 10,289 (capped) | USD 22,158 | **34.2%** |
| USD 108,000 | €100,000 | Employment | USD 28,462 | USD 10,289 (capped) | USD 38,751 | **35.9%** |
| USD 108,000 | €100,000 | B2B self-employed | USD 28,544 | USD 10,289 (capped) | USD 38,833 | **36.0%** |

**Key observation:** The very high employee AOV (old-age pension) contribution of 25% dominates the total burden and is capped at USD 38,390/year base (max USD 10,289/year). The fixed tax credit of USD 21,956 significantly reduces income tax at lower income levels.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/USD = 1.08 (BES-Islands use USD as official currency)
- Fixed tax credit USD 21,956 applied; no dependants; employment deduction USD 280
- Employee SS: 26.8% (AOV 25% + AWW 1.3% + Health 0.5%); capped at USD 38,390/year base
- Self-employed SS modelled at same employee rates (employer contributions OV/Cessantia/ZV not applicable)
- No extraordinary deductions applied
- Expatriate facility benefits (tax-free fringe benefits) not included
