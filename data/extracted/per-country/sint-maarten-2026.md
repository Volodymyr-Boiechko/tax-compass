## Personal Income Tax

Sint Maarten is an autonomous country within the Kingdom of the Netherlands. The former Netherlands Antilles tax laws remain applicable with the understanding that references to "the Netherlands Antilles" should be read as "Sint Maarten." (EY p.1381)

Residents are taxable on their worldwide income. Nonresidents are taxable only on income derived from Sint Maarten sources. A resident individual who receives income from former or current employment, wherever earned, is subject to income tax in Sint Maarten. Place of residence is determined based on facts and circumstances: domicile (permanent home), physical presence, and location of vital personal and economic interests. (EY p.1381)

Tax year = calendar year. Employers file wage withholding tax returns monthly (by 15th of following month). Wage withholding tax is the final tax for most employees; personal income tax returns must be filed within two months after issuance of tax return forms. (EY p.1388)

### PIT Brackets (2025 fiscal year)

Resident and nonresident individuals are subject to the same progressive rates. Minimum wage earners are not subject to income tax. Local interest income is taxed at 6.25% (including surcharges). (EY p.1386)

| Taxable income — Exceeding (XCG) | Taxable income — Not exceeding (XCG) | Tax on lower amount (XCG) | Tax rate on excess (%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 35,831 | 0 | 12.50 |
| 35,831 | 53,747 | 4,478.89 | 20.00 |
| 53,747 | 74,649 | 8,061.99 | 26.25 |
| 74,649 | 111,971 | 13,548.52 | 33.75 |
| 111,971 | 158,252 | 26,144.88 | 40.00 |
| 158,252 | — | 44,657.33 | 47.50 |

Note: Tax applies if taxable income equals or exceeds the minimum wage (not imposed on minimum wage earners). (EY p.1386)

**Capital gains:**
- Capital gains are generally exempt from tax
- Capital gains from disposal of business assets or other assets qualified as income from independently performed activities: up to 47.5% (including surcharges) (EY p.1384)
- Capital gains on liquidation of a company or repurchase of shares in excess of average paid-up capital (non-substantial share interest): up to 47.5% (including surcharges)
- Capital gains from sale of a substantial share interest (≥5% shareholding in a company): 18.75% (including surcharges) (EY p.1384)

**Substantial share interest:** ≥5% of issued share capital of a company. On request, resident individuals are taxed on dividend income and capital gains from substantial share interests at a fixed rate of 15% (not including 25% surcharges, or 18.75% including surcharges). (EY p.1383)

**Investment income — movable assets:**
- Dividends and interest from domestic and foreign sources: subject to income tax at PIT rates
- Fictitious yield of 4%/year on investments in foreign portfolio investment companies and investments in Sint Maarten-exempt companies (annual basis, fair market value)
- Local bank account interest: taxed at 6.25% (EY p.1383)

**65% rental income rule:** 65% of income from immovable property (rental income, grounds, mines, and waters) is taxed at PIT rates; principal residence is not taxed as rental income (EY p.1383)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**

*Fixed deduction from employment income:* XCG500; alternatively, actual employment-related expenses to the extent they exceed XCG1,000 annually. (EY p.1384)

*Personal deductions (residents):*
- Alimony payments to former spouse
- Mortgage interest on taxpayer's dwelling: up to XCG27,500 annually
- Maintenance expenses on dwelling: up to XCG3,000 annually
- Consumer loan interest: up to XCG2,500 (or XCG5,000 if married) annually
- Life insurance premiums entitling to annuity payments: up to 5% of income or XCG1,000 annually
- Pension premiums paid by employee
- AOV/AWW social security premiums paid by employee
- Qualifying charitable donations exceeding certain threshold

*Extraordinary expenses (residents):*
- Financial support for children over 27 or other dependants unable to support themselves
- Medical expenses, educational expenses, and support for up to second-degree relatives (threshold applies)

*Business deductions (self-employed):*
- Accelerated depreciation on fixed assets: up to 33⅓% (one-third of purchase value)
- Investment allowance of 8% for acquisitions of or improvements to fixed assets in year of investment and immediately following year; 12% for new buildings or improvements to existing buildings (when investment >XCG5,000)

*Personal tax credits (2025, residents):*
- Standard tax credit: XCG2,332
- Sole earner tax credit: XCG1,557
- Senior tax credit: XCG1,175
- Child tax credit per child: XCG85–XCG830 depending on children's age, residence, and education

Nonresidents may claim only the standard tax credit. (EY p.1385)

**Losses:** Individual taxpayers may carry losses forward for five years. (EY p.1386)

**Notes:**
- Married persons: employment income, self-employment and business income, and certain periodic allowances are taxed separately; investment income is included in the taxable income of the spouse with the higher individual income (EY p.1388)
- Nonresidents: not subject to income tax if temporarily performing employment activities for ≤183 days in any 12-month period AND remuneration is not borne by a Sint Maarten employer or permanent establishment (EY p.1382)

## Social Security

All resident individuals must pay social security contributions. Contributions provide benefits under AOV (General Old Age Pension), AWW (General Widows and Orphans), AVBZ (General Extraordinary Sickness), ZV (health insurance), and OV (disability insurance). Employers must also pay a Cessantia (severance) fee of XCG40/employee/year. (EY pp.1387–1388)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| AOV/AWW (Pension + Survivors) | 6.5% | 7.5% | 14% |
| AVBZ (Extraordinary Sickness) | 1.5% | 0.5% | 2% |
| ZV (Health Insurance) | 4.2% | 8.3% | 12.5% |
| OV (Disability Insurance) | 0.5–5% | 0.5–5% | 0.5–5% |
| **Total (excl. OV variable)** | **~12.2%** | **~16.3%** | **~28.5%** |

**Income ceilings:**
- AOV/AWW: maximum annual contributions due on earnings up to XCG136,966.57 (2025)
- AVBZ: maximum assessment base XCG428,626 (2025)
- ZV: maximum XCG10,000/month (= XCG120,000/year) for 2025
- OV: maximum XCG10,000/month

**Notes:**
- AOV/AWW: employer contributes 7.5%, employee 6.5%; self-employed pay total 14% (up to maximum; different percentages apply depending on income amount)
- ZV total rate: 12.5% (employer 8.3%, employee 4.2%)
- OV rate: varies 0.5%–5% depending on applicable risk category; fully payable by employer; self-employed also subject
- Cessantia fee: XCG40/employee/year (employer only)
- Nonresidents earning employment income in Sint Maarten are generally also subject to social insurance contributions (EY p.1388)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Sole Trader)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual operating a profession or business in Sint Maarten
- **Tax base:** Net annual profits; taxable income = annual profits minus allowances specified in Deductions
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 12.5%–47.5% applied to taxable income
- **Social security:** Self-employed pay combined employer+employee SS: AOV/AWW 14%, AVBZ 2%, ZV 12.5%, OV variable = total ~28.5% (plus variable OV)
- **VAT obligations:** Sint Maarten has no VAT; the Turnover Tax (BBO) applies at 5% on turnover
- **Key limits:** Losses carried forward 5 years; investment allowance available (8–12%); accelerated depreciation available (33⅓% p.a. on fixed assets)
- **Specific self-employed deductions:** Accelerated depreciation (33⅓%) and investment allowances (8–12%)

### Option B — Penshonado (Pensioner/Retiree) Regime

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals who have not been Sint Maarten residents for the past 5 years AND are at least 50 years old AND apply within 2 months of registration AND acquire a house (≥XCG450,000) within 18 months of registration
- **Tax base / benefit:** Option to pay income tax at 10% (including surcharges) on all foreign-source income; OR may declare a fixed income of XCG500,000/year as foreign income taxed at progressive PIT rates
- **Duration:** Available upon granting of penshonado status; ongoing as long as conditions are met
- **Caveats:** Must acquire qualifying house of ≥XCG450,000; does not apply to Sint Maarten-source income (taxed at normal rates) (EY p.1385)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Facility

- **Eligibility:**
  - Must NOT have been a Sint Maarten resident in the past 5 years
  - Must have special skills at college/university level with ≥3 years of relevant working experience, OR
  - Must have at least 5 years of relevant working experience at the required level AND receive remuneration of ≥XCG100,000
  - Must possess skills scarcely available in Sint Maarten or be employed by government with salary scale 11
- **Benefit:** May receive limited fringe benefits tax-free (wages in kind, travel, hotel expenses, transportation, relocation); remainder of compensation taxed at progressive PIT rates; net contract available (no grossing-up of wage tax)
- **Duration:** Applies retroactively to start of employment if filed within 3 months of beginning employment; employer files the application
- **Application deadline:** Within 3 months after beginning of employment (EY p.1386)

### Investors Permit

- **Eligibility:** Wealthy individuals meeting certain conditions
- **Benefit:** Residency permit allowing legal stay in Sint Maarten for up to 120 days/year; exceeding 120 days not prohibited but may result in being considered a resident taxpayer
- **Caveats:** Exceeding 120 days triggers potential resident tax status (worldwide income taxation) (EY p.1389)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard tax credit XCG2,332 applied.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side only; employer ~16.3% SS not shown).
> "B2B" = gross revenue, 30% assumed deductible business expenses.
> Currency: XCG (Netherlands Antillean Guilder / Antilliaanse Gulden). 1 EUR ≈ 1.97 XCG (1 USD = 1.79 XCG approximately).
> Employee SS: AOV/AWW 6.5% + AVBZ 1.5% + ZV 4.2% = ~12.2% (capped per scheme).
> Self-employed SS: ~28.5% (combined rate).

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **SS cap amounts for 2026:** AOV/AWW cap (XCG136,966.57), AVBZ cap (XCG428,626), and ZV cap (XCG10,000/month) are 2025 figures; verify 2026 amounts with Sint Maarten Social Insurance Bank (SZV)
- [ ] **Turnover Tax (BBO) rate and thresholds:** Sint Maarten has no VAT but applies BBO (Belasting op Bedrijfsomzetten) at 5%; registration threshold and applicability to professional services/IT freelancers should be verified
- [ ] **OV disability insurance rate:** Variable 0.5%–5% depending on risk category (employer-side); self-employed contribution rate not precisely stated by EY
- [ ] **Penshonado house requirement:** XCG450,000 acquisition requirement and 18-month deadline; verify current requirements with Sint Maarten Tax Receiver's Office
- [ ] **Expatriate facility in practice:** Employer must file application; scarcity of skill requirement may be difficult to demonstrate for IT freelancers; verify current administrative practice
- [ ] **Double tax treaty with Netherlands:** Sint Maarten–Netherlands treaty (1 March 2016) and Tax Regulation for the Kingdom cover income, capital, inheritance and gifts; important for Dutch nationals and companies operating across Kingdom borders
