## Personal Income Tax

> Ireland has a two-rate progressive income tax system plus the Universal Social Charge (USC) and Pay Related Social Insurance (PRSI). Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income; domicile rules apply for investment/foreign employment income. Married couples may elect joint or separate assessment.

**Income tax rates (2025 — single or widowed individual):**

| Taxable income (EUR) | Tax on lower amount (EUR) | Rate on excess % |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 44,000 | 0 | 20 |
| 44,000+ | 8,800 | 40 |

**Income tax rates (2025 — married couple, jointly assessed):**

| Taxable income (EUR) | Tax on lower amount (EUR) | Rate on excess % |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 53,000* | 0 | 20 |
| 53,000+ | 10,600 | 40 |

*\* The 20% band can be increased by up to EUR 35,000 if the second spouse has income (max 20% band EUR 88,000) (EY p. 664)*

**Universal Social Charge (USC — 2025):**

| Income (EUR) | Rate % |
|---|---|
| 0 – 12,012 | 0.5 |
| 12,012 – 27,382 | 2.0 |
| 27,382 – 70,044 | 3.0 |
| 70,044 – 100,000 | 8.0 |
| 100,000+ | 11.0 |

*Note: Income ≤ €13,000 is exempt from USC entirely. The 8% rate applies to employment income ≤ €100,000; 11% applies to "relevant income" (non-PAYE) above €100,000. (EY p. 664)*

**PRSI (Pay Related Social Insurance — from 1 October 2025):**
- Employee: 4.2% of gross income (no maximum); nil if weekly earnings ≤ EUR 352
- Self-employed: 4.2% of gross income; minimum annual contribution EUR 650

**Standard deductions / allowances (tax credits — 2025):**

| Credit | Amount (EUR) |
|---|---|
| Single person | 2,000 |
| Married persons/civil partners (jointly assessed) | 4,000 |
| PAYE allowance (salary subject to source deduction) | 2,000 |
| Earned Income Credit (self-employed) | 2,000 |
| Widowed person/surviving civil partner | 2,540 |
| Rent Credit | 1,000 |
| Medical and dental insurance | 20% of gross premium |

*Credits are deducted directly from the income tax liability (not from income).*

**Notes:**
- Tax credit system: credits reduce tax liability, not income
- Capital gains taxed at 33%; annual exemption EUR 1,270 (2025)
- Dividend withholding tax: 25% (creditable against income tax)
- Bank deposit interest: 33% DIRT (Deposit Interest Retention Tax)
- Local Property Tax (LPT): annual charge on residential properties based on valuation band

## Social Security

> Ireland's PRSI covers employees, employers, and self-employed. New rates effective from 1 October 2025.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| PRSI | 4.2% | 11.25% | 4.2% |
| USC | Employee only (see rates above) | — | Employee only |
| **Total** | **4.2% + USC** | **11.25%** | **4.2% + USC** |

**Income ceiling:** No maximum for PRSI or USC
**Notes:**
- Employee PRSI nil if weekly earnings ≤ EUR 352 (EY p. 666)
- Employer PRSI 8.9% if weekly earnings ≤ EUR 527 (EY p. 666)
- Self-employed minimum annual PRSI contribution: EUR 650 (EY p. 666)
- PRSI funds: contributory old-age pension, unemployment benefits (employees), sickness benefits (employees)
- Total employee-side burden = income tax + USC + 4.2% PRSI

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed persons are assessed under the self-assessment system. They pay income tax at 20/40% rates, USC, and PRSI (4.2%). The Earned Income Credit (EUR 2,000) provides partial parity with PAYE workers.

### Option A — Self-Employed Sole Trader
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a trade or profession in Ireland
- **Tax base:** Net income (revenue minus allowable expenses); same 20/40% bracket rates as employment
- **Rate / mechanism:** 20% on first EUR 44,000; 40% above; less Earned Income Credit (EUR 2,000); plus USC; plus 4.2% PRSI
- **Social security:** PRSI 4.2%; minimum annual EUR 650; no unemployment PRSI benefit
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above EUR 40,000 (services) or EUR 80,000 (goods); standard VAT 23%
- **Key limits:** Preliminary tax (90% of current year liability) due by 31 October; self-assessment return by 31 October (extended to mid-November via online ROS); no PAYE credit available (Earned Income Credit of EUR 2,000 used instead)

### Option B — Start-Up Relief for Entrepreneurs (SURE)
- **Who qualifies:** PAYE workers who become self-employed in a new company; must have been PAYE employee for at least 3 of 4 prior years
- **Tax base:** Investment in qualifying company shares
- **Rate / mechanism:** Income tax refund of up to EUR 100,000 (refund of PAYE taxes paid in previous 6 years) on investment in a new trading company
- **Social security:** Normal PRSI applies
- **VAT obligations:** Standard rules
- **Key limits:** Company must be a new qualifying company; investment must be minimum 30% of share capital; must work full-time in the business; 4-year qualifying period (EY p. 663)

### Option C — Incorporation (Limited Company)
- **Who qualifies:** Any entrepreneur
- **Tax base:** Company profits at 12.5% CIT (trading income); director salary deductible
- **Rate / mechanism:** Director draws salary (taxed at 20/40% + USC + PRSI) + dividends (taxed at marginal rate as income); effective overall rate often 40–52% on distributions
- **Social security:** Director (class S PRSI): 4.2% on total income including salary + dividends
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT rates (23% / 13.5% / 9%)
- **Key limits:** Must comply with Companies Act; close company surcharge (15% on undistributed passive income) may apply

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### SARP — Special Assignee Relief Programme
- **Eligibility:** Employees assigned to Ireland by a relevant employer (company incorporated and tax resident in a country with which Ireland has a DTT or tax information exchange agreement); must not have been Irish tax resident for 5 years prior to arrival; minimum base salary EUR 100,000 (from 2026: EUR 125,000); must perform duties in Ireland for minimum 12 consecutive months
- **Benefit:** 30% income tax relief on employment income exceeding EUR 100,000 (from 2026: exceeding EUR 125,000); relief capped at income of EUR 1 million; also tax-free: one return trip to home country per year, school fees up to EUR 5,000/child/year
- **Duration:** Up to 5 consecutive tax years (from 2026: applications commencing up to end of 2030)
- **Application deadline:** Employer must certify within 90 days of employee's first day of duties in Ireland (from 2026: 180 days for new arrivals, but relief only for max 4 years if certified between day 91–180)
- **Caveats:** SARP applies to income tax only — USC and PRSI still apply; individual must file self-assessment return while claiming SARP; assignment must commence up to end 2030 (EY p. 657–658)

### Foreign Earnings Deduction (FED)
- **Eligibility:** Irish-based employees of companies expanding into certain emerging markets; must spend ≥ 30 qualifying days/year in eligible countries
- **Benefit:** From 1 January 2026: income tax deduction up to EUR 50,000 against employment income (previously EUR 35,000)
- **Duration:** Extended to end of 2030
- **Application deadline:** Annual election on tax return
- **Caveats:** Applies to income tax only; USC and PRSI not reduced; qualifying countries listed by Irish Revenue (EY p. 658–659)

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 656–677)

- **SARP threshold increased to EUR 125,000** minimum salary (from EUR 100,000) effective 1 January 2026; relief on 30% of income above EUR 125,000
- **SARP certification period extended to 180 days** for assignments starting from 2026
- **FED deduction limit increased to EUR 50,000** (from EUR 35,000) from 1 January 2026; Türkiye added to eligible countries from 1 January 2026
- **Standard Fund Threshold (pension) increases to EUR 2.2 million** from 1 January 2026 (from EUR 2 million) (EY p. 663)
- PRSI rates increased from 1 October 2025 (employee 4.2% up from ~4%; employer 11.25% up from ~11%) — first full year effect in 2026
- Budget 2026 measures expected in October 2025 — details not yet available at time of EY review

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, Irish tax resident, standard credits only (single credit EUR 2,000 + PAYE credit EUR 2,000 for employment; Earned Income Credit EUR 2,000 for self-employed).

**Employment at €30,000:**
1. Income tax: €30,000 × 20% = €6,000; less credits (€2,000 single + €2,000 PAYE) = €2,000 tax
2. USC: €12,012 × 0.5% = €60; €15,370 × 2% = €307; €2,618 × 3% = €79 → USC = €446
3. PRSI: €30,000 × 4.2% = €1,260
4. Total = €2,000 + €446 + €1,260 = €3,706
5. Effective rate = €3,706 / €30,000 = **12.4%**

**Employment at €60,000:**
1. Income tax: €44,000 × 20% = €8,800; €16,000 × 40% = €6,400; gross IT = €15,200; less credits €4,000 → tax = €11,200
2. USC: €12,012 × 0.5% = €60; €15,370 × 2% = €307; €27,444 × 3% = €823; €5,174 × 8% = €414 → USC = €1,604
3. PRSI: €60,000 × 4.2% = €2,520
4. Total = €11,200 + €1,604 + €2,520 = €15,324
5. Effective rate = €15,324 / €60,000 = **25.5%**

**Employment at €100,000:**
1. Income tax: €44,000 × 20% = €8,800; €56,000 × 40% = €22,400; gross = €31,200; less €4,000 credits = €27,200
2. USC: €12,012 × 0.5% + €15,370 × 2% + €42,662 × 3% + €29,956 × 8% = €60 + €307 + €1,280 + €2,396 = €4,043
3. PRSI: €100,000 × 4.2% = €4,200
4. Total = €27,200 + €4,043 + €4,200 = €35,443
5. Effective rate = €35,443 / €100,000 = **35.4%**

**B2B (self-employed sole trader) at €60,000:**
1. Business costs ~10% = €6,000; net income = €54,000
2. Income tax: €44,000 × 20% = €8,800; €10,000 × 40% = €4,000; gross = €12,800; less Earned Income Credit €2,000 + single credit €2,000 = €8,800
3. USC on €54,000: €12,012 × 0.5% + €15,370 × 2% + €26,618 × 3% = €60 + €307 + €799 = €1,166 (below €70,044 threshold)
4. PRSI: €54,000 × 4.2% = €2,268
5. Total = €8,800 + €1,166 + €2,268 = €12,234
6. Effective rate on gross €60,000 = €12,234 / €60,000 = **20.4%**

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | USC + PRSI | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €2,000 | €1,706 | €3,706 | **12.4%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€1,800 | ~€1,500 | ~€3,300 | **~11.0%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €11,200 | €4,124 | €15,324 | **25.5%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | €8,800 | €3,434 | €12,234 | **20.4%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €27,200 | €8,243 | €35,443 | **35.4%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€26,000 | ~€7,500 | ~€33,500 | **~33.5%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, Irish resident, no dependants
- Employment: single credit €2,000 + PAYE credit €2,000; USC + PRSI as computed above
- B2B: 10% business costs; Earned Income Credit €2,000 + single credit €2,000; PRSI minimum €650 exceeded
- USC exemption threshold €13,000 not relevant here (all incomes exceed it)
- Local Property Tax not included
- Capital gains and investment income not modelled
