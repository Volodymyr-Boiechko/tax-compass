## Personal Income Tax

Ireland uses a two-rate income tax system combined with the Universal Social Charge (USC) and Pay Related Social Insurance (PRSI). Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Residents are taxed on worldwide income; non-domiciled residents are taxed on Irish-source income and foreign income remitted to Ireland. Ordinarily resident status continues for 3 years after departure. (EY pp. 656–664)

### Income Tax Rates (2025 — Single/Widowed)

| Taxable income (EUR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 44,000 | 20% |
| Above 44,000 | 40% |

### Income Tax Rates (2025 — Married/Civil Partners, jointly assessed)

| Taxable income (EUR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 53,000* | 20% |
| Above 53,000 | 40% |

*The 20% band for married couples increases by EUR1 for each EUR1 the lower-earning spouse earns, up to a maximum additional EUR35,000. Maximum 20% band for married couple: EUR88,000. (EY p. 664)

**Note:** Non-residents are taxed at the same rates as residents. (EY p. 664)

### Universal Social Charge (USC) — 2025

The USC applies to **gross income** before pension contributions; it is an additional charge on top of income tax. (EY p. 664)

| Income (EUR) | USC rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 12,012 | 0.5% |
| 12,013 – 27,382 | 2.0% |
| 27,383 – 70,044 | 3.0% |
| 70,045 – 100,000 | 8.0% |
| Above 100,000 | 11.0% (on "relevant income" excluding employment income; 8% applies to employment income above EUR70,044) |

**USC notes:**
- Income exempt if total income ≤ EUR13,000 (0% rate band)
- The 11% rate applies to "relevant income" excluding employment income that exceeds EUR100,000; for employment income above EUR100,000, the 8% rate applies
- USC applies to all income including noncash benefits-in-kind and equity compensation (except certain pension/capital allowance deductions)
- USC does not apply to social welfare payments

### Tax Credits (2025)

Credits are deducted from income **tax** (not USC or PRSI): (EY p. 663)

| Credit | Amount (EUR/year) |
|---|---|
| Single person | 2,000 |
| Married persons / civil partners (jointly assessed) | 4,000 |
| Widowed person / surviving civil partner | 2,540 |
| PAYE allowance (salary subject to PAYE) | 2,000 |
| Earned Income Credit (self-employed) | 2,000 |
| Rent Credit | 1,000 |
| Medical and dental insurance | 20% of gross premium (max EUR1,000/adult + EUR500/child policy) |

**Capital Gains Tax:** 33% on worldwide gains for Irish residents; annual exemption EUR1,270. (EY pp. 662–663)

**Capital Acquisitions Tax (CAT):** 33% on gifts/inheritances above thresholds: Group A (child) EUR400,000; Group B (sibling/nephew) EUR40,000; Group C (others) EUR20,000. (EY p. 665)

## Social Security (PRSI)

Pay Related Social Insurance (PRSI) contributions (new rates effective **1 October 2025**): (EY p. 666)

| Contribution | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee (PRSI) | **4.2%** on gross income | Nil if weekly earnings ≤ EUR352; no maximum income |
| Employer (PRSI) | **11.25%** on gross income | If weekly earnings ≤ EUR352, employer PRSI is 8.9% |
| Self-employed (PRSI) | **4.2%** on all income | Minimum annual contribution EUR650 |

**PRSI credit for employees:** Available for earnings between EUR352.01–EUR424/week; reduces employee PRSI (1/6 of employment income over EUR352.01, max credit EUR12). (EY p. 666)

**Notes:**
- No income ceiling on PRSI (unlike many EU countries)
- Self-employed pay PRSI on total income including unearned income
- Employed persons also pay PRSI on unearned income if net assessable non-employment income > EUR5,000 OR gross non-employment income ≥ EUR30,000
- PRSI funds contributory old-age pension, jobseeker's benefits (employees only), sickness benefits (employees only), limited dental/medical benefits
- Certain non-EEA nationals from countries with totalization agreements may be exempt for 52 weeks if on temporary assignment

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed Sole Trader (Standard)

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals, sole traders, professionals (freelancers, consultants, contractors)
- **Tax base:** Net profit from trade or profession (turnover minus allowable business expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Income tax at 20%/40% + USC + PRSI 4.2% (minimum EUR650). **Earned Income Credit EUR2,000** (same as PAYE credit). Preliminary tax (90% of current year liability) due 31 October
- **Allowances available:** All business expenses; pension contributions (15%–40% of earnings depending on age); Earned Income Credit (same as PAYE credit)
- **Social security:** PRSI 4.2% (self-employed class S); minimum annual contribution EUR650; qualifies for contributory pension and maternity benefit but not unemployment benefit
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if supplies of goods > EUR85,000/year or services > EUR42,500/year; standard VAT rate 23%
- **Key limits:** Must file self-assessment return by 31 October (or mid-November online via ROS); preliminary tax payment due same date

### Option B — Proprietary Director (Director/Shareholder of Irish Company)

- **Who qualifies:** Directors owning ≥ 15% of share capital of an Irish private company
- **Tax base:** Salary paid by company taxable as employment income; profits retained in company subject to corporate tax (12.5% trading income rate)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Director's salary taxed as employee via PAYE; must also file annual self-assessment return; USC at applicable rates; PRSI at employer (11.25%) + employee (4.2%) rates
- **Note:** Directors are regarded as self-employed for tax purposes but as employed for social security purposes — tax withheld via PAYE; must file personal tax return by 31 October

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Special Assignee Relief Programme (SARP)

- **Eligibility:** Employees assigned to Ireland by a "relevant employer" (company incorporated/tax-resident in a treaty country or an associated company). Must have salary ≥ EUR125,000/year (from 2026; was EUR100,000 until 2025). Must not have been Irish tax-resident for 5 years before assignment. Must have worked for the foreign employer for ≥ 6 months before arrival. Must perform employment duties in Ireland for ≥ 12 consecutive months. (EY pp. 657–658)
- **Benefit:** Income tax relief on **30% of employment income exceeding EUR125,000** (from 2026). For 2025 arrivals, relief on income exceeding EUR100,000. Ceiling: EUR1,000,000 of income eligible. Also: one tax-free return trip per year for employee and family; employer can reimburse tax-free education costs up to EUR5,000/child/year
- **Duration:** Up to **5 consecutive years**. For employees assigned from 1 January 2026, certification deadline extended to **180 days** (previously 90 days) from first duty in Ireland; if certified 91–180 days, maximum 4 years of relief
- **Caveats:** SARP relief applies to income tax only — USC and PRSI still apply in full. SARP claimant must use self-assessment system. (EY p. 658)

### Foreign Earnings Deduction (FED)

- **Eligibility:** Irish-based employees working for Irish companies that are expanding into specified emerging markets (extensive list including India, China, Brazil, Russia, UAE, Africa, SE Asia, etc.; Philippines and Türkiye added from 1 January 2026)
- **Benefit:** Deduction of up to **EUR50,000** from employment income (from 1 January 2026; was EUR35,000 until 31 December 2025). Must spend ≥ 30 qualifying days in relevant countries in tax year or 12-month period
- **Duration:** Extended to **2030** (EY pp. 658–659)

### Remittance Basis (Non-Domiciled Residents)

- **Eligibility:** Individuals resident but domiciled outside Ireland
- **Benefit:** Foreign investment income and income from employment duties performed outside Ireland is taxable only to the extent it is **remitted to Ireland**. Foreign employment income paid outside Ireland under a foreign employment contract is generally exempt. (EY p. 657)

## 2026 Changes

- **SARP threshold raised to EUR125,000** (from EUR100,000) for employees assigned to Ireland from 1 January 2026 (EY p. 658)
- **SARP certification deadline extended to 180 days** for 2026 assignments (employers must certify within 180 days; if 91–180 days, only 4 years of relief available) (EY p. 658)
- **FED deduction raised to EUR50,000** (from EUR35,000) effective 1 January 2026; Philippines and Türkiye added to qualifying countries; definition of qualifying day relaxed (no longer requires 3 consecutive days in the same country) (EY p. 659)
- **PRSI rate increases effective 1 October 2025:** Employee 4.2% (from 4.0%); employer 11.25% (from 11.05%); self-employed 4.2% (from 4.0%) (EY p. 666)
- **Standard Fund Threshold (pension) increased to EUR2.2 million** from 1 January 2026 (from EUR2.0 million) (EY p. 663)
- **Employment Permits Act 2024:** New online Employment Permits Online (EPO) system launched April 2025; CSEP minimum salary EUR64,000; GEP minimum salary EUR34,000; ICT minimum salary EUR46,000 (EY pp. 671–673)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, PAYE credit and Single credit for employment; Earned Income Credit and Single credit for B2B (self-employed).
> Currency: EUR (Ireland uses EUR).
> PRSI: employee 4.2% on all income (no cap). Self-employed: 4.2% on all income.
> USC: applied at banded rates on gross income.
> Employer PRSI (11.25%) not shown in employment scenario.

### €30,000 Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | | €30,000 |
| Income tax: 20% on first €44,000 | 20% × 30,000 | €6,000 |
| Income tax credits: Single (€2,000) + PAYE (€2,000) | | −€4,000 |
| **Net income tax** | 6,000 − 4,000 | **€2,000** |
| USC: 0.5% × 12,012 | | €60 |
| USC: 2.0% × (27,382 − 12,012) = 2% × 15,370 | | €307 |
| USC: 3.0% × (30,000 − 27,382) = 3% × 2,618 | | €79 |
| **Total USC** | | **€446** |
| PRSI: 4.2% × 30,000 | | **€1,260** |
| **Total burden** | 2,000 + 446 + 1,260 | **€3,706** |
| **Effective rate** | 3,706 / 30,000 | **12.4%** |

### €30,000 B2B (Self-employed)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue / profit | | €30,000 |
| Income tax: 20% × 30,000 | | €6,000 |
| Tax credits: Single (€2,000) + Earned Income Credit (€2,000) | | −€4,000 |
| **Net income tax** | 6,000 − 4,000 | **€2,000** |
| USC (same as employment) | | **€446** |
| PRSI: 4.2% × 30,000 (min €650) | | **€1,260** |
| **Total burden** | | **€3,706** |
| **Effective rate** | 3,706 / 30,000 | **12.4%** |

**Working for €60,000:**
- Income tax: 20% × 44,000 = 8,800 + 40% × 16,000 = 6,400 → 15,200; credits −4,000 → **11,200**
- USC: 60 + 307 + 3% × (60,000 − 27,382) = 366 + 978 = **1,344** → Corrected: 0.5% × 12,012 = 60; 2% × 15,370 = 307; 3% × (60,000 − 27,382) = 3% × 32,618 = 979; total USC = **1,346**
- PRSI: 4.2% × 60,000 = **2,520**; total = 11,200 + 1,346 + 2,520 = **15,066; effective 25.1%**

**Working for €100,000:**
- Income tax: 20% × 44,000 = 8,800 + 40% × 56,000 = 22,400 → 31,200; credits −4,000 → **27,200**
- USC: 60 + 307 + 3% × (70,044 − 27,382) = 3% × 42,662 = 1,280 + 8% × (100,000 − 70,044) = 8% × 29,956 = 2,396 → total USC = 60 + 307 + 1,280 + 2,396 = **4,043**
- PRSI: 4.2% × 100,000 = **4,200**; total = 27,200 + 4,043 + 4,200 = **35,443; effective 35.4%**

**SARP impact at €100,000 (for context):** Under SARP, only income above EUR125,000 gets 30% relief — so at €100k, SARP provides no relief (salary below threshold). SARP becomes valuable at e.g. €200k: relief on 30% × (200k − 125k) = 30% × 75k = €22,500 reduction in taxable income → income tax saving ~€9,000.

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants
- Employment: Single credit (€2,000) + PAYE credit (€2,000) = €4,000 total credits
- B2B: Single credit (€2,000) + Earned Income Credit (€2,000) = €4,000 total credits (same effective rate as employment)
- No pension contributions, no rent credit, no health insurance premiums
- PRSI 4.2% on all income (new rate effective 1 October 2025); no income ceiling
- USC: standard banded rates; no USC exemption (income > €13,000)
- Employer PRSI (11.25%) not included in employee burden
- 2025 income tax brackets used (2026 not yet revised per EY data)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **2026 income tax bands:** EY presents 2025 rates (€44,000 single band). Budget 2026 may adjust the standard rate band and/or tax credits — verify at revenue.ie after October 2025 Budget announcement.
- [ ] **USC 11% vs 8% threshold:** The 11% USC rate applies to "relevant income" (non-employment income) exceeding €100,000. Employment income above €100,000 is subject to 8%, not 11%. This distinction is relevant for self-employed at €100k+ where investment income and business income are mixed.
- [ ] **Proprietary director PRSI class:** Proprietary directors pay PRSI at Class S (self-employed) for most purposes, not Class A (employee). This means no access to jobseeker's or sickness benefits. Verify based on director's specific shareholding.
- [ ] **SARP at €30k/€60k:** SARP only benefits assignees earning above the €125k threshold (2026). Not relevant at €30k or €60k income levels.
- [ ] **Non-domiciled remittance basis:** The effective rates above assume the individual is domiciled in Ireland (or elects to be taxed on worldwide income). A non-domiciled resident with significant foreign income not remitted to Ireland would have materially lower effective rates.
- [ ] **Local Property Tax (LPT):** Annual charge on residential property; rates depend on valuation band (last valued November 2025). Not included in rate calculations above.
- [ ] **VAT threshold for services:** €42,500/year for service providers. IT freelancers above this threshold must register for 23% VAT; recoverable from B2B clients but compliance burden adds cost.
