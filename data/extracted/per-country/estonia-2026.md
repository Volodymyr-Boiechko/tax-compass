## Personal Income Tax

Flat rate system. Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Estonian-source income only.
Tax year = calendar year. Filing deadline: 30 April of the following year.
Tax due: 1 October of the following year. (EY p.402)

Residency: permanent place of residence in Estonia OR physically present ≥ 183 days in any 12 consecutive calendar months. (EY p.393)

**PIT rate** (EY p.399):

| Year | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 2025 | **22%** | Flat rate on all taxable income |
| 2026 | **24%** | Rate increase confirmed; applies from 1 January 2026 |

**Basic tax exemption (annual)** (EY p.399):

| Year | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Up to end 2025 | Up to EUR 7,848 | Regressive: full exemption at income ≤ EUR 14,400; zero exemption at income ≥ EUR 25,200. Formula: EUR 7,848 − (EUR 7,848 / EUR 10,800) × (income − EUR 14,400) |
| From 2026 | **EUR 8,400** | Universal flat exemption for ALL taxpayers regardless of income level — eliminates the regressive phase-out |

The 2026 change to a flat EUR 8,400 basic exemption is a significant reform: previously higher earners (income > EUR 25,200) received zero exemption; from 2026 all taxpayers receive EUR 8,400.

**Withholding tax rates** (EY p.400):

| Payment type | 2025 rate | 2026 rate |
|---|---|---|
| Wages and salaries | 22% | 24% |
| Interest | 22% | 24% |
| Royalties (to residents) | 22% | 24% |
| Rent | 22% | 24% |
| Dividends (resident companies) | 0%/7% (transitional) | 0%/7% (transitional) |
| Royalties (to nonresidents) | 10% | 10% |

**Dividend income** (EY p.395–396):
- Dividends from Estonian resident companies: generally **exempt** from personal income tax for individuals (corporate tax paid at company level at distribution)
- Transitional provision from 1 January 2025: if a dividend was previously subject to corporate tax at 14% (applicable until 31 December 2024), a 7% withholding tax applies when paid to individuals
- Dividends from foreign companies: taxable unless income tax was paid on the underlying profits abroad
- Lower corporate rate for regular distributions abolished from 2025

**Capital gains** (EY p.397–398):
- Sale of business property or securities: taxable at 22% (24% from 2026)
- Transfer of primary residence, summer cottage (< 0.25 ha), movable property (personal use): generally **exempt**
- Investment account regime: residents may defer taxation of financial income by registering bank accounts as investment accounts — tax deferred until withdrawal

**Deductible items for residents** (EY p.398–399):
- Mandatory social security contributions (paid in Estonia or abroad)
- Voluntary pension fund contributions: up to 15% of income or EUR 6,000/year
- Training and education expenses: up to EUR 1,200/year total (gifts/donations + training)
- Mortgage interest / housing loan interest: no longer mentioned as major deduction (replaced by corporate incentives)

**Notes:**
- No joint filing — spouses file separately; unused basic exemption may be transferred (EY p.402)
- Seamen special regime: 0% income tax on qualifying ship remuneration (EY p.395)

## Social Security

**Employment — Social Tax** (EY p.401):
- Social tax: **33%** paid entirely by the **employer** on gross salary. Employees do NOT pay social tax.
- No ceiling on the amount of salary subject to social tax — applies to all employment income.

**Employment — Other contributions** (EY p.401):
- Unemployment insurance: **0.8% employer + 1.6% employee** on gross salary
- Mandatory pension fund (II pillar): **2%, 4%, or 6% employee** (depending on individual's chosen rate); employer also contributes the social tax credit portion toward state pension

| Contribution | Employer % | Employee % |
|---|---|---|
| Social tax (pension + health) | 33.0% | 0.0% |
| Unemployment insurance | 0.8% | 1.6% |
| Mandatory pension fund (II pillar) | — | 2.0% / 4.0% / 6.0% |
| **Total (min pension rate)** | **33.8%** | **3.6%** |

**Social tax ceiling:** No ceiling — applies to full salary amount. (EY p.401)

**Self-employed (FIE) social tax 2026** (emta.ee; 1office.co):
- Rate: **33%** on net business income (self-reported)
- Minimum monthly base: **EUR 886** (2026) → minimum social tax EUR 292.38/month = **EUR 3,508.56/year**
- Maximum annual base: 10 × 12 × minimum wage = 10 × 12 × EUR 946 = **EUR 113,520/year**
- Maximum annual social tax: 33% × EUR 113,520 = **EUR 37,461.60/year**
- Annual cap (EY p.401): EUR 106,320 for 2025 (10 × minimum monthly wage × 12)
- Advance payments: quarterly, by 15th of last month of each quarter

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — FIE (Füüsilisest Isikust Ettevõtja — Registered Sole Proprietor)

- **Who qualifies:** Any natural person registered as a sole proprietor in the Estonian commercial
  register. Suitable for direct service provision or trade under own name.
- **Tax base:** Net business income (revenue minus documented business expenses). (EY p.395, 399)
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - Income tax: **22%** (2025) / **24%** (from 2026) on net profit after expenses and basic exemption
  - Basic exemption EUR 8,400 (2026) deducted from total taxable income
  - No separate corporate tax — all profit flows to personal return
- **Social security:**
  - Social tax: **33%** on net business income (self-assessed)
  - Minimum annual social tax: EUR 3,508.56 (2026; based on EUR 886/month minimum base)
  - Maximum annual social tax: EUR 37,462 (based on EUR 113,520 cap)
  - Social tax deductible from income tax base? Social tax is a business expense and may be deductible
- **Unemployment insurance:** FIEs are not automatically covered; optional participation
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory VAT registration at EUR 40,000 annual turnover; standard VAT rate 22%
  (rising to 24% from 1 July 2025; then 26% in 2026 per rate schedule — confirm at emta.ee)
- **Key limits:** All income and liabilities personal; full personal liability for business debts

### Option B — Business Income Account (Ettevõtluskonto)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals not registered as FIE who open a designated business bank account
  at a qualifying credit institution (EEA-based). (EY p.395)
- **Tax base:** All revenue received into the account (no expense deduction allowed)
- **Rate / mechanism:** **20% flat tax** on gross revenue. Tax automatically withheld and paid to
  tax authorities monthly by the credit institution. No separate income tax return needed for this income.
- **Social security:** No separate social security contributions — the 20% tax satisfies all payroll
  tax obligations including social tax. Individual is covered by social security.
  If participating in mandatory pension fund (II pillar), the 20% base tax increases by the chosen
  pension contribution rate (2%, 4%, or 6%). (EY p.395)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration threshold same as FIE (EUR 40,000). Revenue through the
  account counts toward the threshold.
- **Key limits:**
  - Only income from selling goods or services can be channeled through this account
  - No expense deductions — unfavorable if costs are significant
  - Simplest possible option — zero administration, tax handled automatically
  - Well-suited for very small freelancers with low costs

### Option C — OÜ (Osaühing — Private Limited Company) + e-Residency

- **Who qualifies:** Anyone (resident or non-resident/e-resident) who establishes an Estonian OÜ.
  No minimum share capital required since February 2023 (can start with EUR 0.01). Most popular
  structure for e-residents and digital nomads. (e-resident.gov.ee; EY p.395)
- **Corporate tax base:** Estonian companies are taxed on a **distribution basis only**:
  - **Retained earnings: 0%** — no corporate income tax on profits kept within the company
  - **Distributed profits (dividends, fringe benefits, gifts, non-business expenses): 22%** at
    company level (24% from 2026), calculated as 22/78 (or 24/76 from 2026) of the net distribution
    — i.e., to distribute EUR 78 net to shareholder, company pays EUR 22 tax = EUR 100 gross cost
- **2026 corporate tax rate increase:** From 1 January 2026, corporate income tax on distributions
  rises from **22% to 24%**. Gross-up: to distribute EUR 76 net, company pays EUR 24 tax. (EY p.396)
- **Dividend income at individual level:** Dividends from Estonian OÜ are **not subject to personal
  income tax** for individual shareholders — the corporate tax paid at distribution is the final tax.
  (EY p.395–396)
- **Social security for working shareholder:** If the OÜ director/shareholder receives a salary,
  the company must pay 33% social tax on that salary (employer side). To avoid social tax on salary,
  shareholders often take only dividends — but must ensure minimum social tax obligation is met
  (companies must pay social tax on at least minimum monthly wage basis for active directors).
- **e-Residency:** Estonia issues e-Residency digital ID cards enabling non-residents worldwide
  to establish and manage Estonian companies fully online. The e-Resident card enables digital
  signing and access to Estonian business services. Does NOT confer tax residency or right to reside. (EY p.409)
- **VAT:** OÜ mandatory VAT registration at EUR 40,000 turnover; all EU VAT rules apply.
- **Key limits:**
  - 2% defense/advance tax originally planned for 2026–2028 was **abolished** by Estonian Parliament
    in June 2025 — does NOT apply. (wisor.ee; ey.com/en_ee)
  - For e-residents NOT tax resident in Estonia: OÜ profits may be taxable in the country where
    the shareholder IS tax resident — Estonia's 0% on retained earnings does not eliminate home-country
    tax obligations
  - Genuine economic substance requirements for EU/OECD compliance apply

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Digital Nomad Visa (D-Visa for Teleworking)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign citizens working location-independently; must perform work duties for
  employers/clients registered outside Estonia, OR conduct business through own company registered
  abroad, OR freelance for clients mostly outside Estonia. Gross monthly income ≥ EUR 4,500.
  Effective from 27 April 2025: D-visa holders may also take up employment in Estonia (short-term
  employment registration required). (EY p.409)
- **Benefit:** Legal right to reside and work remotely from Estonia; access to Estonian services.
- **Duration:** Long-stay visa (Type D) valid for up to 12 months, with Schengen validity 90/180 days.
- **Caveats:** Does NOT automatically confer Estonian tax residency (stays < 183 days). If staying
  ≥ 183 days, Estonian tax residency triggered and worldwide income becomes taxable in Estonia.

### e-Residency (OÜ for Non-Residents)

- **Eligibility:** Any non-EU/EEA national worldwide (except Russian and Belarusian nationals for
  first-time applications as of March 2022 sanctions). (EY p.409)
- **Benefit:** Fully digital EU company management; 0% corporate tax on retained profits; EU payment
  processing; digital identity for Estonian business services.
- **Duration:** Ongoing; e-Residency card valid for 5 years, renewable.
- **Caveats:** Does NOT grant right to reside in Estonia; does NOT make holder an Estonian tax resident;
  home-country tax on corporate income / dividends still applies depending on residence.

## 2026 Changes

- **PIT rate increase:** From 22% to **24%** effective 1 January 2026. (EY p.399)
- **Corporate income tax rate increase:** Distribution tax rises from 22% to **24%** for dividends
  and profit distributions. (EY p.396)
- **Universal basic exemption:** Annual basic exemption changes from regressive sliding scale
  (EUR 0–7,848) to a flat **EUR 8,400 for all taxpayers** regardless of income. (EY p.399)
- **Rental withholding tax:** Rises from 22% to 24% from 2026. (EY p.396)
- **Capital gains tax rate:** Rises from 22% to 24% on business property and securities. (EY p.398)
- **VAT rate increases:** Standard VAT rate rising in steps; confirm current rate at emta.ee.
- **2% corporate advance tax ABOLISHED:** Originally planned for 2026–2028, the 2% tax on
  accounting profits was repealed by Estonian Parliament in June 2025. (wisor.ee; ey.com/en_ee)
- **Social tax minimum base 2026:** EUR 886/month (up from 2025 level), minimum social tax
  EUR 3,508.56/year. (emta.ee)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants. Estonia uses EUR.
> "Employment" = gross salary; employee-side only (employer 33% social tax not included in gross).
> "FIE" = sole proprietor with documented expenses deducted; social tax at actual rate.
> "OÜ" = single-owner OÜ taking all profit as dividends (no salary); 2026 rates used.
> 2026 rates: 24% PIT, 24% corporate distribution tax, basic exemption EUR 8,400.

### Working: €30,000 Employment (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Unemployment insurance (employee 1.6%) | 1.6% × 30,000 | EUR 480 |
| Mandatory pension II pillar (employee 2%) | 2% × 30,000 | EUR 600 |
| PIT base | 30,000 − 480 − 600 − 8,400 (basic exemption) | EUR 20,520 |
| PIT at 24% | 24% × 20,520 | EUR 4,924.80 |
| **Total employee burden** | 480 + 600 + 4,924.80 | **EUR 6,004.80** |
| **Effective rate** | 6,004.80 / 30,000 | **20.0%** |

*Employer additionally pays: 33% × 30,000 = EUR 9,900 social tax + 0.8% × 30,000 = EUR 240 unemployment = EUR 10,140 on top of gross salary (not shown above).*

### Working: €30,000 FIE (net business income = €30,000 after expenses, 2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Social tax (33% × 30,000) | 33% × 30,000 | EUR 9,900 |
| Social tax cap check | EUR 9,900 < EUR 37,462 max | Within cap |
| Social tax minimum check | EUR 9,900 > EUR 3,509 min | Above minimum |
| Income tax base | 30,000 − 9,900 − 8,400 (exemption) | EUR 11,700 |
| Income tax at 24% | 24% × 11,700 | EUR 2,808 |
| **Total burden** | 9,900 + 2,808 | **EUR 12,708** |
| **Effective rate** | 12,708 / 30,000 | **42.4%** |

*FIE pays both income tax AND social tax (33%), resulting in a higher effective rate than employment where employer bears the social tax. This makes the FIE structure unattractive for most unless substantial business expenses can be deducted.*

### Working: €30,000 OÜ (distributing all profit as dividends, 2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Retained profit (0% tax) | 30,000 in OÜ | EUR 30,000 |
| Corporate distribution tax at 24% | 24/76 × 30,000 = distribution tax on gross; to distribute EUR 30,000 net, gross cost = 30,000 / 0.76 = 39,473.68; tax = 39,473.68 × 24% | EUR 9,473.68 |
| Dividend received (net) | EUR 30,000 | EUR 30,000 |
| PIT on dividend | 0% (dividend from Estonian OÜ exempt at individual level) | EUR 0 |
| **Total tax burden** | EUR 9,473.68 on OÜ | **EUR 9,473.68** |
| **Effective rate on EUR 30,000 net distributed** | 9,473.68 / 39,473.68 (gross) | **24.0%** |
| **Effective rate on intended €30,000 net** | 9,473.68 / 30,000 | **31.6% of net** |

*Alternatively, stated on gross income basis (OÜ revenue = €30,000 before distribution tax): effective rate 9,473.68 / 30,000 = 31.6%. However, if profit is retained and NOT distributed, effective rate = 0%. This is the key advantage for reinvestors.*

### Working: €60,000 Employment (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Unemployment (1.6%) | 1.6% × 60,000 | EUR 960 |
| Pension II pillar (2%) | 2% × 60,000 | EUR 1,200 |
| PIT base | 60,000 − 960 − 1,200 − 8,400 | EUR 49,440 |
| PIT at 24% | 24% × 49,440 | EUR 11,865.60 |
| **Total burden** | 960 + 1,200 + 11,865.60 | **EUR 14,025.60** |
| **Effective rate** | 14,025.60 / 60,000 | **23.4%** |

### Working: €60,000 OÜ (distributing all profit as dividends, 2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate distribution tax | 60,000 / 0.76 = 78,947.37 gross; tax = 24% × 78,947.37 | EUR 18,947.37 |
| **Total burden** | | **EUR 18,947.37** |
| **Effective rate on net €60k** | 18,947.37 / 60,000 | **31.6% of net** |
| Retained (0% scenario) | | **0%** |

### Working: €100,000 Employment (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Unemployment (1.6%) | 1.6% × 100,000 | EUR 1,600 |
| Pension II pillar (2%) | 2% × 100,000 | EUR 2,000 |
| PIT base | 100,000 − 1,600 − 2,000 − 8,400 | EUR 88,000 |
| PIT at 24% | 24% × 88,000 | EUR 21,120 |
| **Total burden** | 1,600 + 2,000 + 21,120 | **EUR 24,720** |
| **Effective rate** | 24,720 / 100,000 | **24.7%** |

### Working: €100,000 OÜ (distributing all profit, 2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate distribution tax | 100,000 / 0.76 = 131,578.95 gross; tax = 24% × 131,578.95 | EUR 31,578.95 |
| **Total burden** | | **EUR 31,578.95** |
| **Effective rate on net €100k** | 31,578.95 / 100,000 | **31.6% of net** |
| Retained (0% scenario) | | **0%** |

### Summary table

| Gross/net income | Scenario | Income tax | SS / distribution tax | Total | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 gross | Employment (2026) | EUR 4,925 | EUR 1,080 | EUR 6,005 | **20.0%** |
| €30,000 net | OÜ full distribution (2026) | EUR 0 (individual) | EUR 9,474 (corporate) | EUR 9,474 | **31.6% of net** |
| €30,000 net | OÜ retained (0% scenario) | EUR 0 | EUR 0 | EUR 0 | **0%** |
| €60,000 gross | Employment (2026) | EUR 11,866 | EUR 2,160 | EUR 14,026 | **23.4%** |
| €60,000 net | OÜ full distribution (2026) | EUR 0 | EUR 18,947 (corporate) | EUR 18,947 | **31.6% of net** |
| €100,000 gross | Employment (2026) | EUR 21,120 | EUR 3,600 | EUR 24,720 | **24.7%** |
| €100,000 net | OÜ full distribution (2026) | EUR 0 | EUR 31,579 (corporate) | EUR 31,579 | **31.6% of net** |

**Key insight — OÜ vs Employment:** Employment is cheaper than OÜ full distribution at all income levels (24.7% vs 31.6% at €100k). However, if OÜ profits are **retained** (reinvested, kept in company), effective rate = 0%. The OÜ advantage is tax deferral and 0% on retained earnings, not lower tax on consumption/distribution. For digital nomads who can live on low personal drawings while growing their business, the OÜ is extremely efficient. Business income account (20% flat, no expenses) is competitive vs FIE for very low costs.

**OÜ effective rate note:** The 31.6% represents the corporate tax as % of the net amount distributed; on gross company revenue before the distribution tax, the rate is exactly 24% (the nominal rate). This is consistent regardless of income level — OÜ distribution tax is always 24% of gross (= 31.6% of net).

**Assumptions:**
- 2026 rates: 24% PIT, 24% corporate distribution tax
- Basic exemption EUR 8,400 (flat, all taxpayers, from 2026)
- Employee pension II pillar at 2% (minimum; employees may choose 4% or 6%)
- Unemployment insurance employee 1.6%
- FIE social tax: 33% on net profit; social tax treated as deductible for income tax purposes
- OÜ: single director/shareholder; no salary paid to shareholder (all profit distributed as dividends)
- OÜ retained earnings: 0% tax assumed; profit NOT distributed
- No VAT shown; standard VAT 22%+ applies to services
- 2% corporate advance tax abolished — NOT included

## Known Gaps

- [ ] FIE social tax deductibility from income tax: EY implies social tax is a business cost; exact
      treatment (deductible before or after PIT base) should be confirmed at emta.ee
- [ ] Business income account (ettevõtluskonto) exact VAT obligations: whether the 20% covers all
      obligations or VAT still applies separately above EUR 40,000 threshold needs verification
- [ ] OÜ salary vs dividend optimal split: the most tax-efficient OÜ structure often involves paying
      a salary equal to minimum social tax base (EUR 886/month) to meet social security obligations,
      then distributing remaining profit as dividends — this hybrid not modeled in examples
- [ ] VAT rate 2026: rate rising from 22% to 24% (standard); reduced rates — exact 2026 schedule
      from EMTA not detailed here; may affect service pricing for VAT-registered OÜs
- [ ] FIE vs Business Account at low income: at revenue < ~EUR 15,000/year with low expenses,
      business income account (20% gross) may be more cost-efficient than FIE — not modeled
- [ ] e-Residency home-country tax: non-Estonian-resident OÜ owners face tax in their country of
      residence on OÜ income — this is country-specific and NOT modeled (major practical caveat)
- [ ] Mandatory pension fund (II pillar) opt-out: residents can opt out (and re-join); pension
      contributions reduce take-home pay but increase future pension — not factored into effective
      rate comparisons
- [ ] Digital Nomad Visa: 183-day trigger for tax residency means long-stay visa holders may
      unexpectedly become Estonian tax residents — important warning not prominently noted above
