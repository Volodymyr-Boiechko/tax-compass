## Personal Income Tax

Progressive income tax system for residents on worldwide income; nonresidents on certain Curaçao-source income. Tax year = calendar year. Wage tax is a pre-levy to income tax; for most employees, wage tax is the final tax. Personal income tax return must be filed within two months after issuance of tax return forms. (EY p.311, p.318)

**Curaçao guilder (XCG):** Curaçao uses the Netherlands Antillean guilder (ANG/XCG); exchange rate is fixed: 1 USD = 1.79 XCG, so 1 XCG ≈ 0.559 USD ≈ ~0.52 EUR (approximate 2025 estimate using USD/EUR ≈ 0.93).

**Residency:** Determined by domicile (availability of permanent home), physical presence, and location of vital personal and economic interests. (EY p.311)

**Progressive PIT brackets — 2026 fiscal year** (EY p.316)

| Taxable income (XCG) | Tax on lower amount (XCG) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 39,094 | 0 | 9.75% |
| 39,094 – 52,127 | 3,812 | 15.00% |
| 52,127 – 78,190 | 5,767 | 23.00% |
| 78,190 – 110,769 | 11,761 | 30.00% |
| 110,769 – 162,894 | 21,535 | 37.50% |
| Above 162,894 | 41,082 | 46.50% |

**Personal tax credits — 2026 fiscal year (deducted from tax due):** (EY p.315)
- Standard tax credit: XCG 2,915
- Sole earner tax credit: XCG 1,779
- Senior tax credit: XCG 1,342
- Child tax credit: XCG 96 to XCG 948 per child (depending on age, residence, education)
- Nonresidents are NOT eligible for personal tax credits.

**Other income types:**
- Income from movable assets (dividends, interest from private foundations/trusts): flat 19.5% reduced rate. Interest from local financial institutions: 8.5% special flat rate (final withholding). (EY p.313)
- Substantial share interest (≥5% of issued share capital): dividends and capital gains taxed at 19.5% flat. (EY p.313)
- Foreign portfolio investments and Curaçao investment companies: fictitious yield of 4% per year of fair market value must be reported as income. (EY p.313)
- Capital gains: generally exempt, but capital gains on business assets and disposal of other assets (if qualifying as income from independently performed activities) taxed up to 46.5%. Capital gains on company liquidation: 17–34%. Share repurchase (non-substantial): 19.5%. Substantial share interest capital gains: 19.5%. (EY p.314)
- Rental income (immovable property): 65% of gross rental income included in taxable income at progressive rates. Personal residence rental income not taxed as immovable income. Mortgage interest deductible for acquisition/restoration. (EY p.313)
- Income from periodic allowances (pensions, alimony, disability): taxed at progressive rates on worldwide amounts. (EY p.313)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.314–315)
- Fixed employment expense deduction: XCG 500 from employment income (or actual employment expenses if exceeding XCG 1,000 annually).
- Alimony payments to former spouse.
- Mortgage interest related to taxpayer's dwelling (limited to XCG 27,500/year).
- Maintenance of protected monument dwelling.
- Consumer loan interest (limited to XCG 2,500, or XCG 5,000 if married, annually).
- Life insurance premiums (up to 10% of income or XCG 5,000/year).
- Pension premiums paid by employee.
- AOV/AWW premiums paid by employee.
- Qualifying donations (above certain threshold).
- Student loan repayments (principal, interest and costs) up to XCG 10,000/year for max 10 years.
- Investment allowance: 10% of acquisitions/improvements to fixed assets in year of investment (if investment >XCG 5,000). (EY p.315)

**Notes:**
- Individual taxpayers may carry losses forward for five years. (EY p.316)
- Deemed salary introduced from 1 January 2015 for employees with substantial share interest in employer; minimum salary treated as employment income. (EY p.312)
- Spouses taxed separately on employment, self-employment, and business income; investment income attributed to spouse with higher income. (EY p.318)
- No withholding taxes on dividends, interest and royalties earned by nonresidents. (EY p.313)

## Social Security

All resident individuals must pay social security. (EY p.317)

**BVZ (Basic Illness Insurance):** (EY p.317)
- Employer: 9.3% (income-based premium)
- Employee: 4.3%, up to income of XCG 150,000
- Pensioners: 6.5%

**AOV/AWW (General Old Age / Widows and Orphans Ordinance) — 2026:** (EY p.317)
- Employer: 9% of salary, up to max contribution XCG 9,000/year per employee
- Employee: 6% of salary, up to max contribution XCG 6,000/year per employee
- Maximum AOV premium income: XCG 100,000
- Additional 1% premium payable by individual if income exceeds XCG 100,000 (on excess)
- In principle, nonresidents are NOT subject to AOV/AWW.

**AVBZ (Extraordinary Sickness Insurance) — 2026:** (EY p.317–318)
- Employer: 0.5% of salary
- Employee: 1.5% of salary
- Self-employed: 2% of earnings
- Pensioners: 1.5% of pension income
- Income ceiling: XCG 606,247.08 (2026)

**OV/ZV (Disability Insurance):** (EY p.318)
- Employer: 0.5%–5% (depending on risk category; fully payable by employer)
- Income ceiling: XCG 7,146.10/month (2026)

**Cessantia (Severance contribution):** XCG 40/year per employee (2026 amount). (EY p.318)

- BVZ exemption may be requested for privately insured individuals with worldwide coverage or those needing temporary residency.
- Totalization agreements: double tax treaties with Aruba, Malta, the Netherlands (including BES-Islands), Sint Maarten. Treaty with Suriname concluded, expected to enter into force shortly. (EY p.319)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Organized Bookkeeping)
- **Who qualifies:** Resident individuals deriving income from business or profession.
- **Tax base:** Annual profits calculated per sound business practices; taxable income = annual profits minus deductions and personal allowances. Business expenses generally fully deductible; restrictions apply: donations/promotional gifts/courses/conferences/symposiums 100% deductible; related travel expenses 80% deductible; food and beverages 80% deductible. (EY p.315)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates 9.75%–46.5% on net profit (same scale as employment).
- **Social security:** AVBZ: 2% on earnings up to XCG 606,247. BVZ/AOV/AWW: self-employed rates not separately specified (likely different contribution basis).
- **VAT obligations:** Not specified in EY source.
- **Key limits:** 5-year loss carryforward. 10% investment allowance on qualifying fixed asset acquisitions. (EY p.315–316)

### Option B — Penshonado (Retiree) Regime
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals aged ≥50, not resident in Curaçao for past 5 years, who acquire/lease a house or Protected Monument of value ≥XCG 450,000 within 18 months of registration.
- **Tax base:** Either flat 10% on all foreign-source income, OR declare fixed income of XCG 500,000/year as foreign income (taxed at progressive rates).
- **Rate / mechanism:** 10% on net foreign-source income, OR progressive rates on deemed XCG 500,000.
- **Social security:** Standard rules apply.
- **VAT obligations:** Standard.
- **Key limits:** Must apply for penshonado status within two months of registration in Curaçao. (EY p.315–316)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Facility
- **Eligibility:** Individual who: has not been resident in Curaçao for past 5 years; completed college/university education with diploma; has ≥5 years relevant working experience; receives annual remuneration ≥XCG 150,000 from employer; possesses skills scarce in Curaçao. Application filed jointly by employer and employee within 3 months of employment start.
- **Benefit:** Limited amounts of fringe benefits received tax-free (wages in kind, travel expenses, hotel expenses, transportation and relocation). The remainder of compensation paid by employer taxed at progressive rates. A net employment contract may be entered into.
- **Duration:** Applies retroactively to start of employment if filed within 3 months.
- **Caveats:** Not all benefits exempt — only specific listed fringe benefits; remainder subject to full progressive tax. (EY p.316)

### "At Home in Curaçao" — Remote Work / Digital Nomad Program
- **Eligibility:** Remote workers and digital nomads who work location-independently and either: (a) work for a foreign employer, (b) conduct business as partner/shareholder in foreign company, or (c) offer freelance/consulting services to foreign clients. Also hibernators/snowbirds who own property in Curaçao.
- **Benefit:** Simplified and shortened visa procedure; 6-month stay permit (extendable by 6 more months).
- **Duration:** Up to 12 months total.
- **Caveats:** Cannot work for or be employed by a local Curaçao company/employer during stay. Return ticket required. Normal income tax applies to Curaçao-source income. (EY p.319–320)

### Penshonado (Pensioner) Regime
- See Self-Employment Regimes Option B above.

## 2026 Changes

- **AOV/AWW caps for 2026:** Employer max contribution XCG 9,000/year; employee max contribution XCG 6,000/year (confirming 2026 amounts). (EY p.317)
- **AVBZ income ceiling 2026:** XCG 606,247.08. (EY p.318)
- **OV maximum salary 2026:** XCG 7,146.10/month. (EY p.318)
- **Cessantia 2026:** XCG 40 per employee per year. (EY p.318)
- **Personal tax credits 2026:** Standard XCG 2,915; sole earner XCG 1,779; senior XCG 1,342. (EY p.315)
- **No announced PIT rate changes for 2026**; brackets confirmed for 2026 fiscal year. (EY p.316)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> XCG/EUR rate: 1 EUR ≈ XCG 1.93 (derived from 1 USD = 1.79 XCG; 1 EUR ≈ 1.08 USD → 1 EUR ≈ 1.79 × 1.08 ≈ 1.93 XCG; approximate 2025 estimate).
> "Employment" = gross salary; XCG 500 fixed employment deduction applied; standard tax credit XCG 2,915 applied.
> "B2B" = self-employment net profit at progressive rates; AVBZ 2% applied; standard credit applied.
> Employee SS: BVZ 4.3% (up to XCG 150k) + AOV/AWW 6% (up to max XCG 6,000/yr) + AVBZ 1.5%.

**EUR to XCG conversions:**
- €30,000 = XCG 57,900
- €60,000 = XCG 115,800
- €100,000 = XCG 193,000

- AOV (6%, max XCG 6,000): XCG 6,000; BVZ (4.3%): XCG 4,979; AVBZ (1.5%): XCG 1,737; SS total = XCG 12,716 → approx. XCG 12,065 (AOV capped at XCG 6,000 for employee)
- PIT base: 115,800 − 500 = 115,300

**Working — XCG 193,000 Employment:**
- AOV (6%, max XCG 6,000): capped XCG 6,000; BVZ (4.3%, up to XCG 150k): XCG 6,450; AVBZ (1.5%): XCG 2,895; total SS = XCG 15,345 → approx. XCG 14,797
- PIT base: 193,000 − 500 = 192,500
- Tax: 3,812 + 1,955 + 6,094 + 9,534 (30% × [110,769−78,190]) + 19,501 (37.5% × [162,894−110,769]) + 11,100 (46.5% × [192,500−162,894]) = XCG 51,996 − 2,915 = XCG 49,081 → approx. XCG 45,048

**Assumptions:**
- XCG/EUR ≈ 1.93 (derived from ANG/USD peg of 1.79; approximate)
- Standard XCG 500 employment expense deduction applied; standard tax credit XCG 2,915 applied
- Employee SS: AOV 6% (capped at XCG 6,000/year), BVZ 4.3% (up to XCG 150k), AVBZ 1.5%
- B2B SS: AVBZ 2% only (BVZ/AOV self-employed contributions not separately specified)
- B2B: no business expense deductions modeled (assumes net profit = gross revenue); actual expenses would reduce PIT base
- Top marginal rate of 46.5% applies above XCG 162,894 ≈ €84,400
