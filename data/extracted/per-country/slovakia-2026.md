## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for residents taxed on worldwide income. Nonresidents taxed on Slovak-source income only.
Tax year = calendar year. Standard filing deadline: 31 March of the following year (3 months after year-end);
extendable by 3 months on notification; extendable to 30 September for foreign-source income. (EY p.1398)
Slovakia uses EUR.

**PIT brackets — 2025** (EY p.1394–1395)

| Annual tax base (EUR) | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 38,553.01 | 19% | Equals 176.8 × subsistence minimum of EUR 218.06 (2024/25 value) |
| Over 38,553.01 | 25% | On excess only |

**PIT brackets — 2026 (NEW — Consolidation Package 3)** (accace.com; tax-audit.sk; KPMG)

| Annual tax base (EUR) | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 43,983.32 | 19% | 154.8 × subsistence minimum of EUR 284.13 |
| 43,983.33 – 60,349.21 | 25% | 154.8× to 212.4× subsistence minimum |
| 60,349.22 – 75,009.72 | 30% | 212.4× to 264.0× subsistence minimum |
| Over 75,009.72 | 35% | Above 264.0× subsistence minimum |

*Subsistence minimum used for 2026 calculations: EUR 284.13/month (valid 1 July 2025 – 30 June 2026). Only the portion of income in each bracket is taxed at the corresponding rate.*

**Micro-tax relief / reduced rate** (EY p.1395; confirmed 2025):
- Self-employed individuals and business entities with taxable income (revenues) ≤ EUR 100,000 in the tax period may apply a **15% rate** (threshold raised from EUR 60,000 in 2024 to EUR 100,000 in 2025; remains at EUR 100,000 for 2026)
- This rate applies to the entire tax base (not just the amount up to EUR 100,000)

**Investment income** (EY p.1391, 1395):
- Interest and other investment income from Slovak sources: **19% withholding** (final, separate tax base; not included in progressive base)
- Dividends from profits for accounting periods from 1 January 2024: **10%**
- Dividends from profits generated in 2025 and beyond: **7%** (rate reverted from 10%)
- Dividends from jurisdictions not in Slovak white list: **35%**
- Virtual currencies: **19% to 25%** (progressive rates; taxable on sale/exchange)

**Capital gains** (EY p.1392):
- Securities traded on regulated market held > 1 year: **exempt** (if not included in business assets)
- General EUR 500/year exemption for small capital gains
- Real estate / personal property: generally exempt if minimum holding period met
- Business assets generally do not qualify for exemption

**Personal allowance (non-taxable part of tax base) — 2026** (highgate.sk; tax-audit.sk):
- Full allowance (for annual tax base ≤ EUR 26,367.26): **EUR 5,966.73/year** (EUR 497.23/month)
- Allowance reduced above EUR 26,367.26; fully phased out at annual tax base ≥ EUR 50,234.18
- Monthly wage deduction basis: EUR 397.78/month (= 140% × subsistence minimum EUR 284.13)

**Child tax credit — 2025** (EY p.1393):
- EUR 100/month per child up to age 15
- EUR 50/month per child age 15–18
- Subject to maximum % of partial tax base (29% for 1 child, up to 64% for 6+ children)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Employee mandatory contributions (sickness, old-age, disability, unemployment) ARE deductible from employment income (EY p.1393)
- Notional expenses deduction of 60% of taxable income (capped at EUR 20,000/year) available for non-VAT-payer self-employed (živnostník); see Self-Employment Regimes
- Paid interest on housing loans: tax bonus of 50% of interest paid (max EUR 1,200/year for contracts after 1 Jan 2024) for individuals ≤ 35 years old (EY p.1394)

**Notes:**
- Married couples file separately; income from joint property split equally
- Filing exemption: taxpayers receiving only employment income from a Slovak employer subject to withholding may have employer perform annual reconciliation — no return required

## Social Security

Employer and employee contributions on employment income. Self-employed rates differ. (EY p.1395–1396)

**2025 Rates:**

| Benefit | Employer % | Employee % | Self-employed % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sickness insurance | 1.4% | 1.4% | 4.4% |
| Health insurance | 11.0% | 4.0% | 15.0% |
| Old-age insurance | 14.0% | 4.0% | 18.0% |
| Disability insurance | 3.0% | 3.0% | 6.0% |
| Accident insurance | 0.8% | 0.0% | 0.0% |
| Guarantee fund | 0.25% | 0.0% | 0.0% |
| Reserve fund | 4.75% | 0.0% | 4.75% |
| Unemployment insurance | 1.0% | 1.0% | 0.0% (optional at 2%) |
| **Total** | **36.2%** | **13.4%** | **48.15%** |

*Notes from EY p.1395–1396:*
- (a) Health insurance cap eliminated from January 2017; calculated on total income subject to health insurance
- (b) Old-age insurance split between Social Security Agency and one commercial pension fund management agency
- (c) Accident insurance: employer only, 0.8%, no cap
- (d) If employer enrolled in Kurzarbeit scheme, unemployment split 0.5% regular + 0.5% Kurzarbeit
- (e) Self-employed not required to contribute to unemployment; optional at 2% of self-determined base

**Maximum monthly assessment base (social security, 2025):** EUR 15,730 (= 7× average wage in Slovak economy). Accident insurance and health insurance: NO cap. (EY p.1395–1396)

**2026 Changes to self-employed contributions** (accace.com; dobry-uctovnik.sk):
- Minimum contribution base for self-employed increases from 50% to **60% of average wage**
- Health insurance rate for self-employed increases from 15% to **16%** from January 2026
- Minimum monthly social insurance (self-employed, main): **EUR 303.11** (= 29.4% × EUR 1,031 min base)
- Minimum monthly health insurance (self-employed): **EUR 121.92** (= 16% × EUR 762 base)
- Combined minimum monthly contributions (2026): approximately **EUR 425/month** (up from EUR 344/month in 2025)
- Special low-income base: if 2025 annual income ≤ EUR 9,144, special base of EUR 131.34/month applies from 1 July 2026

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Živnostník (Sole Trader) with Paušálne Výdavky (60% Flat-Rate Expense Deduction)

- **Who qualifies:** Natural persons with trade license (živnostenský list); NOT registered for VAT
  (non-VAT payers only for the paušálne výdavky deduction). Revenue ≤ EUR 100,000 threshold for
  micro-rate (15% PIT) applies separately.
- **Tax base:** Revenue minus **60% notional (flat-rate) expenses** (paušálne výdavky).
  Capped at **EUR 20,000/year** (i.e., maximum deduction regardless of revenue).
  (EY p.1391, 1394; confirmed as 60% cap EUR 20,000 since January 2017)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net profit (after 60% deduction) taxed at:
  - **15%** if total revenue (turnover) ≤ EUR 100,000 (micro-tax rate)
  - Progressive 19%/25%/30%/35% if revenue > EUR 100,000 (2026 rates)
  - Personal allowance (up to EUR 5,966.73/year) still deductible from tax base
- **Social security:** Self-employed rates (48.15% in 2025; 2026: ~49.15% with health rising to 16%).
  Assessment base = net profit / 1.486 (derived formula); minimum base applies.
  Minimum monthly (2026): ~EUR 425/month combined.
- **VAT obligations:** Cannot use the 60% paušálne výdavky deduction if VAT registered.
  VAT registration mandatory at EUR 50,000 turnover in 12 consecutive months
  (raised to EUR 62,500 threshold from July 2026 per VAT reform); standard VAT rate 23% (2025).
- **Key limits:**
  - EUR 20,000 cap on notional expenses means the deduction optimises at revenue up to EUR 33,333
    (60% × EUR 33,333 = EUR 20,000); above this, actual expenses are more tax-efficient if > EUR 20,000
  - Cannot combine paušálne výdavky with actual expense deduction in the same year
  - Revenue from rental income cannot use paušálne výdavky (EY p.1391)

---

### Option B — Živnostník with Actual Expenses (Standard Method)

- **Who qualifies:** All živnostníci; also VAT-registered businesses.
- **Tax base:** Revenue minus all provably incurred business expenses (EY p.1394).
  Mandatory social/health contributions deductible.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive PIT rates (15% micro / 19%/25%/30%/35% progressive).
- **Social security:** Same as Option A.
- **Key limits:** Requires bookkeeping; preferable when actual expenses > EUR 20,000/year.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Dedicated Expat Regime

Slovakia does not have a formal newcomer or expat preferential PIT regime. Standard Slovak rates apply.

### Temporary Protection Status (Ukrainian Refugees)

- **Eligibility:** Ukrainian citizens with Temporary Protection Status (dočasné útočisko), valid until March 2026 (extendable). (EY p.1402)
- **Benefit:** Full access to Slovak labor market; can be employed on Slovak employment contracts.
- **Caveats:** Slovak legislation does not specifically recognize remote work arrangements in Slovakia for TPS holders; TPS does not enable business activities in Slovakia.

## 2026 Changes

- **New 4-bracket PIT system:** Rates of 19%, 25%, 30%, 35% introduced from 1 January 2026 (previously 2 brackets). Threshold for 25% bracket lowered from EUR 38,553 to EUR 43,983 (absolute amount increases despite multiplier reduction due to higher subsistence minimum). (accace.com; tax-audit.sk)
- **Health insurance rate for self-employed:** Rises from 15% to **16%** from January 2026. (accace.com)
- **Minimum SZČO contribution base:** Rises from 50% to **60% of average wage**; combined minimum contributions ~EUR 425/month (up EUR 80/month). (accace.com; dobry-uctovnik.sk)
- **Contribution holiday shortened:** New self-employed "holiday period" reduced from 1 year to 6 months; minimum EUR 131/month thereafter. (accace.com)
- **Dividend tax:** Dividends from profits generated in 2025 onwards taxed at **7%** (reverted from 10% in 2024; EY p.1391). For 2024 accounting periods: still 10%.
- **Personal allowance thresholds reduced:** Phase-out begins at lower tax base from 2026. (tax-audit.sk)
- **VAT threshold rise:** VAT registration threshold increases to EUR 62,500 (from EUR 50,000) from July 2026.
- **Micro-tax rate:** 15% rate threshold maintained at EUR 100,000 revenue for 2026.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants. Slovakia uses EUR.
> "Employment" = gross salary; employee-side contributions only (employer-side not shown).
> "B2B živnostník" = sole trader using 60% paušálne výdavky deduction (non-VAT payer, revenue ≤ EUR 100,000 for micro-rate where applicable).
> Personal allowance EUR 5,966.73/year (2026) applied. 2026 rates and contributions used.

### Working: €30,000 Employment (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee social + health (13.4%) | 13.4% × 30,000 | EUR 4,020 |
| PIT base | 30,000 − 4,020 − 5,966.73 | EUR 19,013.27 |
| PIT at 19% (below EUR 43,983 threshold) | 19% × 19,013.27 | EUR 3,612.52 |
| **Total burden** | 4,020 + 3,612.52 | **EUR 7,632.52** |
| **Effective rate** | 7,632.52 / 30,000 | **25.4%** |

### Working: €30,000 B2B Živnostník (60% deduction, revenue ≤ €100k → micro 15% rate)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Paušálne výdavky (60%, capped at €20,000) | 60% × 30,000 = 18,000; cap not triggered | EUR 18,000 |
| Net profit | 30,000 − 18,000 | EUR 12,000 |
| Personal allowance | | EUR 5,966.73 |
| PIT base | 12,000 − 5,966.73 | EUR 6,033.27 |
| PIT at 15% (micro rate, revenue ≤ €100k) | 15% × 6,033.27 | EUR 904.99 |
| Social + health contributions (2026 minimum, ~€425/month × 12) | Annual minimum | EUR 5,100 |
| **Total burden** | 904.99 + 5,100 | **EUR 6,004.99** |
| **Effective rate** | 6,004.99 / 30,000 | **20.0%** |

*Note: Minimum contributions (EUR 425/month × 12 = EUR 5,100) dominate at this income level.*

### Working: €60,000 Employment (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee social + health (13.4%) | 13.4% × 60,000 | EUR 8,040 |
| PIT base | 60,000 − 8,040 − 5,966.73 | EUR 45,993.27 |
| PIT at 19% on EUR 43,983.32 | 19% × 43,983.32 | EUR 8,356.83 |
| PIT at 25% on excess | 25% × (45,993.27 − 43,983.32) = 25% × 2,009.95 | EUR 502.49 |
| **PIT due** | | **EUR 8,859.32** |
| **Total burden** | 8,040 + 8,859.32 | **EUR 16,899.32** |
| **Effective rate** | 16,899.32 / 60,000 | **28.2%** |

### Working: €60,000 B2B Živnostník (60% deduction; revenue > €33,333 so EUR 20,000 cap applies)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Paušálne výdavky (60% × 60,000 = 36,000; cap EUR 20,000) | Cap applies | EUR 20,000 |
| Net profit | 60,000 − 20,000 | EUR 40,000 |
| Revenue > EUR 100,000? No → micro rate 15% applies | | |
| Personal allowance | Revenue ≤ EUR 100,000; base EUR 40,000 | EUR 5,966.73 |
| PIT base | 40,000 − 5,966.73 | EUR 34,033.27 |
| PIT at 15% | 15% × 34,033.27 | EUR 5,104.99 |
| Social + health (2026): base = net profit / 1.486 = 40,000 / 1.486 = 26,918; 49.15% × 26,918 | Actual contributions | EUR 13,230 |
| **Total burden** | 5,104.99 + 13,230 | **EUR 18,334.99** |
| **Effective rate** | 18,334.99 / 60,000 | **30.6%** |

*At €60k B2B, the EUR 20,000 paušálne výdavky cap means only 33% effective deduction (vs full 60% at ≤€33k), and social/health contributions on actual profit dominate.*

### Working: €100,000 Employment (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee social + health (13.4%) | 13.4% × 100,000 (approx; cap at 7× avg wage ~EUR 110k, not triggered) | EUR 13,400 |
| PIT base | 100,000 − 13,400 − 0 (allowance phased out above ~EUR 50,234) | EUR 86,600 |
| PIT at 19% on EUR 43,983.32 | | EUR 8,356.83 |
| PIT at 25% on EUR 43,983.33–60,349.21 (EUR 16,365.89) | 25% × 16,365.89 | EUR 4,091.47 |
| PIT at 30% on EUR 60,349.22–75,009.72 (EUR 14,660.50) | 30% × 14,660.50 | EUR 4,398.15 |
| PIT at 35% on excess | 35% × (86,600 − 75,009.72) = 35% × 11,590.28 | EUR 4,056.60 |
| **PIT due** | | **EUR 20,903.05** |
| **Total burden** | 13,400 + 20,903.05 | **EUR 34,303.05** |
| **Effective rate** | 34,303.05 / 100,000 | **34.3%** |

### Working: €100,000 B2B Živnostník (60% deduction; cap EUR 20,000; revenue = €100k → micro rate still applicable)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Paušálne výdavky (cap EUR 20,000 applies) | EUR 20,000 | EUR 20,000 |
| Net profit | 100,000 − 20,000 | EUR 80,000 |
| Revenue = EUR 100,000 → micro rate 15% (at exact threshold) | | 15% rate |
| Personal allowance | Phased out at this income level (base > EUR 50,234) | EUR 0 |
| PIT at 15% | 15% × 80,000 | EUR 12,000 |
| Social + health (2026): base = 80,000 / 1.486 = 53,836; 49.15% × 53,836 | | EUR 26,460 |
| **Total burden** | 12,000 + 26,460 | **EUR 38,460** |
| **Effective rate** | 38,460 / 100,000 | **38.5%** |

*Note: At exactly EUR 100,000 revenue, micro 15% rate applies. Above EUR 100,001, progressive rates kick in and the effective rate would jump significantly.*

### Summary table

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (EUR) | SS + Health (EUR) | Total (EUR) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment (2026) | 3,613 | 4,020 | 7,633 | **25.4%** |
| €30,000 | B2B živnostník (60% deduction, micro 15%) | 905 | 5,100 | 6,005 | **20.0%** |
| €60,000 | Employment (2026) | 8,859 | 8,040 | 16,899 | **28.2%** |
| €60,000 | B2B živnostník (60% deduction, micro 15%) | 5,105 | 13,230 | 18,335 | **30.6%** |
| €100,000 | Employment (2026) | 20,903 | 13,400 | 34,303 | **34.3%** |
| €100,000 | B2B živnostník (60% deduction, micro 15%) | 12,000 | 26,460 | 38,460 | **38.5%** |

**Key insight — Živnostník crossover:** At €30k, B2B is cheaper (20.0% vs 25.4%) because the 15% micro rate combined with 60% deduction produces near-zero PIT, and minimum contributions dominate but are less than employee contributions at this level. At €60k, the EUR 20,000 cap on paušálne výdavky cuts the deduction benefit, and high self-employed contribution rates (49%+) push B2B above employment. At €100k, self-employed contributions significantly exceed employee-side costs, making employment more efficient on the contribution side, partially offset by the 15% micro rate vs 34%+ employment marginal rate.

**Assumptions:**
- 2026 PIT rates and thresholds used throughout
- Personal allowance EUR 5,966.73 applies fully at €30k; partially at €60k (phasing, assumed reduced for simplification); fully phased out at €100k (base > EUR 50,234)
- Social + health minimum contributions 2026: EUR 425/month = EUR 5,100/year (applied at €30k B2B)
- At €60k and €100k B2B: actual contributions calculated on profit using combined 49.15% rate on net profit / 1.486 assessment base formula (approximation; actual SZČO calculations split social 29.4% and health 16% on separate bases)
- EUR 20,000 paušálne výdavky cap applies for revenue > EUR 33,333
- VAT assumed not applicable (non-VAT payer under EUR 50,000 threshold in 2025; EUR 62,500 from July 2026)
- No child tax credits, no mortgage interest deduction
