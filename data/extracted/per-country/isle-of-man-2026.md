## Personal Income Tax

> The Isle of Man has a two-rate income tax system. Tax year runs 6 April – 5 April. Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Isle of Man-source income only. No capital gains tax, no inheritance/estate tax, no wealth tax, no stamp duty land tax.

**Income tax rates (tax year ending 5 April 2026):**

| Taxable income above personal allowance (GBP) | Rate % |
|---|---|
| First 6,500 | 10 (standard rate) |
| Above 6,500 | 21 (higher rate) |

*The 10% standard rate applies to the first GBP 6,500 above the personal allowance. The 21% higher rate applies to all income above that. (EY p. 681)*

**Personal allowances (tax year ending 5 April 2026):**

| Type of allowance | Amount (GBP) |
|---|---|
| Single person allowance | 14,750* |
| Married couples' / civil partners' allowance | 29,500* |
| Single parent additional allowance | 6,400 |
| Blind person (additional) | 2,900 |
| Disabled person (additional) | 2,900 |
| Nonresident | 0 |

*\* Phased out by GBP 1 for every GBP 2 of income above GBP 100,000 (single) / GBP 200,000 (jointly assessed). Single allowance fully withdrawn at income of GBP 129,500; married allowance at GBP 259,000.*

**Tax cap:** Maximum annual income tax for Isle of Man residents:
- Single individual: GBP 220,000 per year
- Married couple (jointly assessed): GBP 440,000 per year
- Applies to worldwide taxable income regardless of amount (EY p. 681)
- Election required; available for 5 or 10-year periods

**Deductions (10% tax reduction available on):**
- Mortgage/loan interest on Isle of Man lender: up to GBP 5,000 (GBP 10,000 jointly assessed) — 10% credit on amount
- Private medical insurance (age 60+): up to GBP 1,800 (GBP 3,600 jointly assessed) — 10% credit
- Charitable donations: up to GBP 7,000 — 10% credit
- Educational Deeds of Covenant: up to GBP 5,500 — 10% credit
- Nursing expenses for dependent relative: up to GBP 12,500 — 10% credit
- Pension contributions: deductible up to GBP 50,000 or 100% of relevant earnings

**Notes:**
- Benefits in kind: general exempt limit GBP 600 (excluding accommodation, company cars, fuel)
- Tax year: 6 April – 5 April (same as UK)
- Tax return due by 6 October following end of tax year
- Nonresidents taxed at 21% on all Isle of Man-source income; no personal allowance

## Social Security

> Isle of Man National Insurance (NI) contributions mirror UK NI but are paid to the Isle of Man Treasury. A reciprocal agreement with the UK allows contributions to count in either jurisdiction.

**Employee NI (tax year ending 5 April 2026):**
- 11% on weekly earnings between GBP 168 and GBP 1,032 (i.e., GBP 8,736 – GBP 53,664/year)
- 1% on earnings above GBP 53,664/year
- Annual ceiling on 11% band: GBP 53,664 (above this, only 1% applies)

**Employer NI (tax year ending 5 April 2026):**
- 12.8% on weekly earnings exceeding GBP 168 (GBP 8,736/year); no upper ceiling

**Self-employed NI (tax year ending 5 April 2026):**
- Class 2: flat rate GBP 6.45/week if annual profits expected to exceed GBP 8,736
- Class 4: 8% on profits between GBP 8,736 – GBP 53,664; 1% on profits above GBP 53,664

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| NI (main rate) | 11% (GBP 8,736–53,664) | 12.8% (above GBP 8,736) | Class 4: 8% (GBP 8,736–53,664) |
| NI (upper earnings) | 1% (above GBP 53,664) | No ceiling | 1% (above GBP 53,664) + Class 2 |
| **Employee effective max NI** | **~GBP 4,942 + 1% excess** | **No ceiling** | **Class 2 + Class 4** |

**Income ceiling:** 11%/8% applies only up to GBP 53,664; above that only 1% applies
**Notes:**
- National Insurance Holiday Scheme closed to new IoM residents from 6 April 2025 (still applies to IoM students returning to work and individuals who started before 5 April 2025)
- Students returning: may claim refund of Class 1 NI paid in first 12 months of employment, up to GBP 4,400

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals are assessed on business profits. Same income tax rates apply (10%/21%); self-employed NI (Class 2 + Class 4) applies instead of employee NI.

### Option A — Self-Employed Sole Trader
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on trade, profession, or vocation in the Isle of Man
- **Tax base:** Net business profits (revenue minus allowable expenses); capital allowances available (100% first-year on plant/machinery; 25% reducing balance for cars, capped at GBP 3,000/year)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same 10%/21% rates; personal allowance GBP 14,750 applies
- **Social security:** Class 2 NI (GBP 6.45/week if profits >GBP 8,736) + Class 4 NI (8% on GBP 8,736–53,664; 1% above)
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above GBP 90,000 annual turnover; standard VAT rate 20%
- **Key limits:** Business losses may be offset against other personal income or carried forward; payment on account due 6 January (105% of prior year liability if >GBP 250)

### Option B — Personal Service Company
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals providing services via a company to a client through a third-party arrangement
- **Tax base:** Deemed employment rules may apply — worker treated as employee of client company for NI and income tax purposes
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same as employment if deemed employed; otherwise company taxed at 0% CIT and director draws salary/dividend
- **Social security:** Normal employee NI if deemed employed
- **VAT obligations:** Standard rules
- **Key limits:** Deemed employment provisions apply if services not performed under direct worker-client contract (EY p. 679)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Tax Cap for New Residents
- **Eligibility:** Any Isle of Man tax resident; must make an election
- **Benefit:** Maximum annual income tax liability capped at GBP 220,000 (single) / GBP 440,000 (jointly assessed), regardless of worldwide income; effective top rate for income above ~GBP 1 million falls below 21%
- **Duration:** 5 or 10 years per election; renewable
- **Application deadline:** Election submitted to Assessor of Income Tax; available each year
- **Caveats:** Tax cap applies only to worldwide income; applies to Isle of Man-source income as well; nonresidents not eligible

### Relocation Key Employees Scheme
- **Eligibility:** Individuals contractually required to take residence in Isle of Man to start/diversify/expand a business; must obtain approval from appropriate body
- **Benefit:** Taxed on Manx-source income only for first 3 years of residence (worldwide income exemption during initial period)
- **Duration:** 3 years from date of residence
- **Application deadline:** Must apply before or upon taking up residence; business approval required
- **Caveats:** Individual and jointly assessed spouse/partner qualify together; financial assistance may be granted for reasonable relocation package (EY p. 679)

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 678–684; rates effective 6 April 2025 for tax year ending 5 April 2026)

- **Personal allowance: GBP 14,750** (single) for tax year 2025/26 — no change from prior year indicated
- **National Insurance Holiday Scheme closed to new residents from 6 April 2025** — only IoM students returning and those who started before 5 April 2025 remain eligible
- NI contribution rates unchanged: employee 11%/1%, employer 12.8%, self-employed Class 2 GBP 6.45/week + Class 4 8%/1%
- Tax cap amounts unchanged at GBP 220,000 / GBP 440,000

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, Isle of Man resident, standard allowances only.
> Currency: GBP. 1 GBP ≈ 1.17 EUR (approximate 2025 rate). EUR amounts are approximate conversions.

**Employment at €30,000 (≈ GBP 25,641):**
1. Less personal allowance GBP 14,750 → taxable = GBP 10,891
2. Tax: GBP 6,500 × 10% = GBP 650; GBP 4,391 × 21% = GBP 922 → total IT = GBP 1,572 ≈ €1,839
3. NI: GBP 25,641 − GBP 8,736 = GBP 16,905 within 11% band → NI = GBP 1,860 ≈ €2,176
4. Total = €1,839 + €2,176 = €4,015
5. Effective rate = €4,015 / €30,000 = **13.4%**

**Employment at €60,000 (≈ GBP 51,282):**
1. Less allowance GBP 14,750 (no phase-out below GBP 100,000) → taxable = GBP 36,532
2. Tax: GBP 6,500 × 10% = GBP 650; GBP 30,032 × 21% = GBP 6,307 → IT = GBP 6,957 ≈ €8,140
3. NI: (GBP 51,282 − GBP 8,736) × 11% = GBP 42,546 × 11% = GBP 4,680 ≈ €5,476
4. Total = €8,140 + €5,476 = €13,616
5. Effective rate = €13,616 / €60,000 = **22.7%**

**Employment at €100,000 (≈ GBP 85,470):**
1. Allowance phase-out: GBP 85,470 > GBP 100,000? No — below threshold; full allowance GBP 14,750
2. Taxable = GBP 85,470 − GBP 14,750 = GBP 70,720
3. Tax: GBP 6,500 × 10% = GBP 650; GBP 64,220 × 21% = GBP 13,486 → IT = GBP 14,136 ≈ €16,539
4. NI: GBP 8,736–53,664 = GBP 44,928 × 11% = GBP 4,942; above GBP 53,664: (GBP 85,470 − GBP 53,664) × 1% = GBP 318 → NI = GBP 5,260 ≈ €6,154
5. Total = €16,539 + €6,154 = €22,693
6. Effective rate = €22,693 / €100,000 = **22.7%**

**B2B (self-employed) at €60,000 (≈ GBP 51,282):**
1. Business costs ~10% = GBP 5,128; net profit = GBP 46,154
2. Less allowance GBP 14,750 → taxable = GBP 31,404
3. IT: GBP 6,500 × 10% + GBP 24,904 × 21% = GBP 650 + GBP 5,230 = GBP 5,880 ≈ €6,880
4. Class 2 NI: GBP 6.45 × 52 = GBP 335
5. Class 4 NI: GBP 46,154 − GBP 8,736 = GBP 37,418 × 8% = GBP 2,993 ≈ €3,502
6. Total NI = GBP 335 + GBP 2,993 = GBP 3,328 ≈ €3,894
7. Total = €6,880 + €3,894 = €10,774
8. Effective rate = €10,774 / €60,000 = **18.0%**

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | National Insurance | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €1,839 | €2,176 | €4,015 | **13.4%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€1,550 | ~€1,700 | ~€3,250 | **~10.8%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €8,140 | €5,476 | €13,616 | **22.7%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | €6,880 | €3,894 | €10,774 | **18.0%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €16,539 | €6,154 | €22,693 | **22.7%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€15,000 | ~€5,700 | ~€20,700 | **~20.7%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, IoM resident, no dependants; full personal allowance GBP 14,750 applies (income below GBP 100,000 phase-out threshold)
- Employment: employee NI 11% on GBP 8,736–53,664; 1% above
- B2B: 10% assumed business costs; Class 2 + Class 4 NI; same income tax rates
- No VAT in IoM below GBP 90,000 threshold
- No capital gains tax, no wealth tax, no inheritance tax
- Tax cap (GBP 220,000/year) not relevant at these income levels
- GBP/EUR ≈ 1.17 (approximate)
