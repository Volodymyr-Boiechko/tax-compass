## Personal Income Tax

The Isle of Man (IOM) is a Crown Dependency with its own tax authority (Assessor of Income Tax). Residents are taxed on worldwide income; non-residents on IOM-source income only. Tax year runs from 6 April to 5 April (same as UK). Rates effective from **6 April 2025** (tax year ending 5 April 2026). The IOM has **no capital gains tax, no inheritance or estate tax, no wealth tax, no stamp duty, and no stamp duty land tax**. (EY pp. 678–681)

### Income Tax Rates (Tax Year ending 5 April 2026)

| Taxable income (GBP, above personal allowance) | Rate |
|---|---|
| First GBP 6,500 | **10%** (standard rate) |
| Above GBP 6,500 | **21%** (higher rate) |

**Note:** The 10% rate applies to the first GBP6,500 of income **for each individual above their personal allowance**. For married couples/civil partners electing joint assessment, the 10% rate applies to the first GBP13,000 of joint income above the married couples' allowance of GBP29,500. (EY p. 681)

**Non-residents:** Flat 21% on all IOM-source income; not entitled to personal allowance. (EY p. 681)

### Annual Income Tax Cap

A resident individual's income tax liability is **capped at GBP220,000/year** (GBP440,000 for jointly assessed married couples/civil partners) for the tax year ending 5 April 2026, regardless of worldwide income. This cap applies on election and is made for a 5- or 10-year period. (EY p. 681)

### Personal Allowances (Tax Year ending 5 April 2026)

| Type of allowance | Amount (GBP) |
|---|---|
| Single allowance | **14,750** |
| Married couples' / civil partners' allowance | **29,500** |
| Single parent additional allowance | 6,400 |
| Blind person (additional) | 2,900 |
| Disabled person (additional) | 2,900 |
| Nonresident | **0** (no personal allowance) |

**Taper on personal allowance:** Reduced by GBP1 for every GBP2 of income exceeding GBP100,000 (GBP200,000 for jointly assessed couples). A person with total income ≥ GBP129,500 (or GBP259,000 jointly assessed) gets no personal allowance. (EY p. 680)

### 10% Tax Reduction Credits (on specific expenses)

A 10% reduction of the individual's tax liability (not income) is granted on the **lower of the amount paid or the maximum permitted** for: (EY pp. 679–680)

| Expense | Maximum amount (GBP) |
|---|---|
| Mortgage / loan interest (Isle of Man lender) | GBP5,000 (GBP10,000 jointly assessed) |
| Private medical insurance premiums (residents ≥ 60 years) | GBP1,800 (GBP3,600 jointly assessed, if at least one partner ≥ 60) |
| Charitable donations | GBP7,000 |
| Educational Deeds of Covenant (for higher education) | GBP5,500 |
| Nursing care expenses (for dependent relative) | GBP12,500 |

**Maximum pension deduction:** GBP50,000 or 100% of relevant earnings (whichever is less) for contributions to a personal pension scheme. (EY p. 679)

**Benefits in kind:** General BIKs excluding accommodation, bicycles, and cars/fuel are exempt up to GBP600/year; above GBP600 are taxable. (EY p. 680)

## Social Security (National Insurance Contributions)

The Isle of Man has a reciprocal NIC agreement with the UK — contributions paid in either jurisdiction count toward total entitlement. (EY p. 682)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Class 1 (employment) — main rate | **11%** of weekly earnings GBP168–GBP1,032 | **12.8%** on earnings above GBP168/week (no ceiling) | N/A |
| Class 1 (employment) — upper earnings | **1%** on earnings above GBP1,032/week (= above ~GBP53,664/year) | (no ceiling) | N/A |
| Class 4 (self-employed) | N/A | N/A | **8%** on profits GBP8,736–GBP53,664 |
| Class 4 upper profits | N/A | N/A | **1%** on profits above GBP53,664 |
| Class 2 (self-employed flat rate) | N/A | N/A | GBP6.45/week if profits ≥ GBP8,736 |

(EY p. 682 — tax year ending 5 April 2026 figures)

**Notes:**
- Employee NIC ceiling: 11% applies on weekly earnings GBP168–GBP1,032 (annual: ~GBP8,736–~GBP53,664); 1% above GBP53,664
- Employer NIC: 12.8% on earnings above GBP168/week; **no ceiling**
- Self-employed pay Class 2 (flat rate GBP6.45/week) + Class 4 (8% on profits GBP8,736–GBP53,664 + 1% above)
- Annual employee NIC cap: ~GBP5,703 (11% × (53,664 − 8,736)) + 1% on excess

### National Insurance Holiday Scheme (now closed to new Isle of Man residents)

- Effective 6 April 2025: the NI Holiday Scheme **closed to new Isle of Man residents** (previously available to those taking up IOM residence from 6 April 2019)
- Still applies to IOM students returning from studying abroad (for a full-time first degree or comparable course outside IOM) who return to live and work in IOM on or after 6 April 2019 — subject to specific qualifying conditions
- Benefit: refund of Class 1 NIC paid as an employee during the first 12 months of permanent employment, up to **GBP4,400** (EY p. 682)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader / Self-Employed (Standard)

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals operating a trade, profession, or vocation in the Isle of Man
- **Tax base:** Net business profit (taxable income = profits after business deductions); assessed on profits of accounting period ending in the tax year
- **Rate / mechanism:** 10% on first GBP6,500 above personal allowance; 21% on excess; subject to annual **cap of GBP220,000**
- **Deductions:** All wholly and exclusively business-related expenses; pension contributions up to GBP50,000/100% of earnings; capital allowances (100% first-year for plant and machinery; 25% reducing balance for cars, capped at GBP3,000/year)
- **Social security:** Class 2 NIC (GBP6.45/week if profits ≥ GBP8,736) + Class 4 NIC (8% on profits GBP8,736–GBP53,664 + 1% above)
- **VAT obligations:** IOM has its own VAT system (harmonized with UK); registration threshold GBP90,000/year; standard VAT rate 20%
- **Key limits:** Return due by 6 October following end of tax year; payment on account equal to 105% of prior year liability if > GBP250

### Option B — Personal Service Company (Deemed Employment)

- **Who qualifies / limitation:** If services are provided through a third-party company rather than directly, deemed employment provisions may apply — the worker is treated as an employee of the end client (not the intermediary company)
- **Effect:** Deemed employment means income taxed as employment income (PAYE + NIC); no opportunity to retain profits in company at lower corporate rate
- **Note:** IOM has its own corporate tax (standard rate 0% for most income; 10% for banking activities and retail businesses with taxable income > GBP500,000) — but personal service company structures may be challenged

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Relocation of Key Employees

- **Eligibility:** Individuals contractually obligated to take up residence in IOM to facilitate starting a new business or diversifying/expanding an existing one, subject to necessary approval
- **Benefit:** The individual and their jointly assessed spouse are taxed **only on Manx-source income** for the first **3 years** of residence; foreign income is exempt for this period. The company may deduct reasonable relocation package costs.
- **Caveats:** Requires specific approval; terms of approval govern the benefit (EY p. 679)

### Annual Income Tax Cap (for High Earners)

- **Eligibility:** Any resident individual or jointly assessed couple
- **Benefit:** Maximum annual income tax capped at **GBP220,000** (GBP440,000 jointly assessed), regardless of actual worldwide income
- **Election:** Must be made; applies for 5 or 10 consecutive years at the rate applicable in year 1 of the election
- **Key note:** At GBP220,000 cap, the effective rate on income above ~GBP1M becomes progressively lower, making IOM very attractive for very high earners (EY p. 681)

## 2026 Changes

- **Tax year ending 5 April 2026 rates confirmed:** Single allowance GBP14,750; married allowance GBP29,500; income tax cap GBP220,000 (GBP440,000 jointly assessed). (EY p. 680–681)
- **NI Holiday Scheme closed to new IOM residents** effective 6 April 2025 — only IOM students returning from studies abroad remain eligible (EY p. 682)
- **Car benefit-in-kind calculation updated:** From 6 April 2024, car BIK calculated based on CO2 emissions and list price (aligned with UK methodology) (EY p. 680)
- **No capital gains tax, no inheritance tax, no stamp duty** — confirmed to remain in force (EY p. 681)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **GBP/EUR conversion:** All EUR figures are approximate. IOM uses GBP; exchange rate affects effective rate comparisons with EUR-zone countries.
- [ ] **Personal allowance taper at €100k:** At GBP85,470 (€100k), the income is below the GBP100,000 taper threshold — full allowance applies. Above GBP100,000 (€117,000), the allowance begins to reduce, increasing the marginal rate. The 21% rate effectively becomes ~28% in the taper zone (GBP100,000–GBP129,500).
- [ ] **10% expense tax credits:** Not modeled above. Mortgage interest (max GBP5,000), medical insurance (for 60+ only), charitable donations provide a 10% reduction of tax liability on the lower of amount paid or cap — relevant for homeowners and charitable donors.
- [ ] **NI Holiday Scheme:** Now closed to new IOM residents from 6 April 2025. Only returning IOM students remain eligible. Not a planning tool for new arrivals as of 2026.
- [ ] **IOM vs UK comparison:** IOM effective rates are significantly lower than UK at the same income levels, primarily because: (1) flat 20%/21% rates (no 40% band as in UK until €117k equivalent), (2) no capital gains tax, (3) income tax cap for very high earners.
- [ ] **Deemed employment rules:** Personal service companies may trigger deemed employment provisions; contractors providing services through third parties should verify their status with IOM tax authorities.
- [ ] **VAT threshold:** IOM VAT is harmonized with UK; threshold GBP90,000 applies. Freelancers above this threshold must register and charge 20% VAT.
