## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system under the Income Tax Ordinance 2001 (ITO). Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents taxed on Pakistan-source income only. Tax year = 1 July to 30 June. Employers withhold tax at source (PAYE). Self-employed and business income assessed via annual return (typically by 30 September following year-end). The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) administers income tax. (EY p.1164)

**PIT brackets — Tax Year 2025 (1 July 2024 – 30 June 2025)** (EY p.1164–1165)

| Taxable salary income (PKR) | Tax on lower amount (PKR) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 600,000 | 0 | 0% |
| 600,001 – 1,200,000 | 0 | 5% |
| 1,200,001 – 2,200,000 | 30,000 | 15% |
| 2,200,001 – 3,200,000 | 180,000 | 25% |
| 3,200,001 – 4,100,000 | 430,000 | 30% |
| Over 4,100,000 | 700,000 | 35% |

**Note on super tax (surtax for high earners):** A super tax applies to individuals with income above PKR 150,000,000 (PKR 150 million) at 1%–4% additional rate (tiered). (EY p.1165)

**Self-employed / non-salary income brackets (business income — AOP/individual):**
The brackets above generally apply; however, for business income the FBR may apply slightly different thresholds or the same progressive schedule depending on the source/nature.

**Association of Persons (AOP):**
An AOP (partnership/joint business) is taxed as a single entity; partners are not separately taxed on their share of AOP income (already taxed at entity level at the same progressive individual rates). (EY p.1166)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Zakat contributions (mandatory for Muslims): deductible
- Charitable donations to government-approved institutions: deductible up to 30% of taxable income
- Education expenses: limited deduction
- Employer pension fund contributions: deductible
- No standard personal exemption per se; the 0% bracket effectively creates a PKR 600,000 tax-free threshold

**Notes:**
- The PKR 600,000 threshold is equivalent to approx. PKR 50,000/month (minimum wage is significantly lower)
- Investment income: dividends 15% withholding (final); interest on bank deposits 15% WHT (final for filers, 30% for non-filers)
- Capital gains on immovable property: tiered by holding period (0%–15%); on listed securities: 15% CGT
- Rental income: included in total income at progressive rates; 5–10% notional value based assessment also applicable
- Foreign-source income of residents: taxable; foreign tax credit available
- Non-filer surcharge: individuals not on the Active Taxpayers List (ATL) face doubled withholding tax rates on various transactions

## Social Security

Pakistan has a fragmented social security system with federal and provincial components. (EY p.1167–1168)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employees' Old Age Benefits Institution (EOBI) | PKR 250/month (fixed) | PKR 1,250/month per employee (fixed, or 5% of min wage) | Not applicable |
| Provincial Social Security (ESIC/PESSI/SESSI/KPESSI) | 1% of wages (approx.) | 5–6% of wages (approx.; varies by province) | Not applicable |
| Group Life Insurance (where applicable) | — | Varies | — |
| Workers' Welfare Fund (WWF) | — | 2% of total income (companies with income >PKR 500,000) | — |

**Income ceiling:** EOBI fixed amounts; provincial SS applies on wages up to PKR 30,000/month (this ceiling is frequently revised — verify current amount). (EY p.1167)

**Notes:**
- Social security in Pakistan is primarily for formal sector employees; self-employed have no mandatory coverage
- EOBI: provides old-age pension, survivor benefits; contributions are low
- Provincial ESSI/PESSI: health and injury coverage; employer-dominated system
- Self-employed in informal or professional sectors have no mandatory SS participation

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Business Income (Sole Proprietor)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual operating a business under their own name or a registered firm name; files under ITO as individual.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (revenue minus allowable business expenses). Expenses incurred wholly and exclusively for business deductible.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive PIT brackets 0%–35% on net profit.
- **Social security:** No mandatory SS for self-employed; voluntary pension funds available.
- **VAT obligations:** Register for Sales Tax (ST) at 18% standard rate with FBR if turnover exceeds PKR 10 million/year. Monthly ST returns.
- **Key limits:** Must maintain accounts if turnover exceeds PKR 10 million. Annual income tax return due 30 September.

### Option B — Freelancer / IT Services Export Income (Important Special Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals providing IT/IT-enabled services (software development, BPO, call centers, data processing, etc.) where the income is **received in foreign currency from outside Pakistan**.
- **Tax base / Rate:** Export income from IT/IT-enabled services is taxed at **1% of turnover** (final tax, no deductions) — or **0% if income repatriated through banking channels** (the zero-rate exemption). This was introduced and extended multiple times; the current status for Tax Year 2025/2026 should be verified. (EY p.1166; SRO 395 and subsequent notifications)
- **Current position:** As of EY content date (October 2025), freelancers repatriating foreign exchange earnings through designated bank accounts (Roshan Digital Accounts or similar) may qualify for the concessional rate. The Finance Act 2024 extended certain IT export incentives but the exact rate (0% or 1%) depends on repatriation method and registration with PSEB (Pakistan Software Export Board). **Verification recommended.**
- **Social security:** Not applicable.
- **VAT obligations:** IT exports are zero-rated for Sales Tax; no ST obligation on export services.
- **Key limits:** Must register with PSEB; must repatriate income through banking channels to qualify for reduced/zero rate; income from Pakistani clients taxed at normal progressive rates.

### Option C — Association of Persons (AOP)

- **Who qualifies:** Two or more individuals operating together as a partnership/firm (not incorporated as a company).
- **Tax base:** AOP-level net income; taxed at progressive rates at the AOP level.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 0%–35% at AOP level; partners not taxed separately on their share.
- **Social security:** Each partner self-responsible; no mandatory SS.
- **VAT obligations:** Same ST rules as sole proprietor.
- **Key limits:** AOP tax rates can be less favorable than individual rates at certain income levels; each partner must file a personal return showing AOP income (even though tax is at AOP level).

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Roshan Digital Account (RDA) — Foreign Nationals and Overseas Pakistanis

- **Eligibility:** Non-resident Pakistanis (overseas Pakistanis) and foreign nationals investing in Pakistan through designated Roshan Digital Accounts.
- **Benefit:** Income from Naya Pakistan Certificates (NPCs) held in RDA accounts: final withholding tax at reduced rates (10% for PKR NPCs; 5% for FCY NPCs — lower than standard rates). Capital gains on RDA investments: various exemptions. Repatriation of profits permitted.
- **Duration:** Ongoing; per investment in qualifying instruments.
- **Application deadline:** Open; account opening at designated banks.
- **Caveats:** Primarily for investment income, not employment income; exchange controls apply to repatriation of other income.

### Short-Term Visitors / Business Visitors

- Short-term business visitors (under 183 days) may not be considered resident and thus taxed only on Pakistan-source income.
- Treaty protection available for many nationalities (see treaty list: Austria, Belgium, China, France, Germany, Netherlands, UK, USA, etc.). (EY p.1180)

## 2026 Changes

- **Finance Act 2024 (effective July 2024):** Introduced increases to PIT rates for higher brackets; the 35% top rate confirmed. Super tax extended. (EY p.1165)
- **IT export incentives:** Extended but terms evolving; verify current PSEB registration requirements and 0%/1% rate applicability for Tax Year 2026 (July 2025 – June 2026) at fbr.gov.pk.
- **Non-filer regime:** Enhanced withholding on non-ATL individuals continues; double WHT rates for those not on Active Taxpayers List remain punitive.
- **AML/ATF compliance:** Pakistan's FATF grey-list exit (February 2023) brought stricter documentation requirements; banking verification for freelancer income more stringent.
- **Capital Value Tax (CVT):** On foreign assets of residents exceeding PKR 100 million: 1%. On motor vehicles >1,300cc: 1%. (EY p.1178)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants. Tax Year 2025 (July 2024 – June 2025).
> PKR/EUR rate: 1 EUR = PKR 310 (approximate mid-2025; PKR highly volatile, range PKR 280–320/USD × 1.08 USD/EUR).
> "Employment": progressive PIT + EOBI employee (minimal fixed amount PKR 250/month).
> "Freelancer IT export": 1% final tax on turnover (conservative; 0% if full repatriation via banking channels).
> SS for employees: EOBI PKR 3,000/year (fixed, minimal) + provincial SS ~1% — negligible in proportion.

**Key insight:** The IT/freelancer export income regime at 1% (or potentially 0%) is one of the most attractive in the world for technology professionals. A freelancer earning PKR 9.3 million (~€30,000) from foreign clients and repatriating through banking channels faces effectively a 1% tax burden vs. 27% for an equivalent employed person. This has driven significant growth in Pakistan's freelance IT sector. **However, the regime's continuity must be verified annually** as Pakistan frequently adjusts finance act provisions.

**Employment effective rates** of 27–33% are notable; Pakistan's rates are relatively high for a lower-middle-income country, driven by the steep bracket escalation (35% top rate kicks in above PKR 4.1M/year ≈ €13k/year at current exchange rates).

**Assumptions:**
- PKR/EUR = 310 (highly approximate; PKR floated since May 2023, significant volatility)
- EOBI: fixed PKR 250/month employee = PKR 3,000/year; negligible SS burden
- Provincial SS: too small/variable to include
- IT export: 1% final tax (conservative; verify 0% eligibility at PSEB/FBR)
- Super tax: not applicable at these income levels (applies above PKR 150 million/year ≈ €484k)
