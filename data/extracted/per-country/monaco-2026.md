## Personal Income Tax

**Monaco does not levy personal income tax** on Monegasque nationals and foreign nationals residing in Monaco, **with the exception of French nationals**, who are regulated by the 1963 bilateral tax treaty between France and Monaco and are **fully liable to French income tax** regardless of Monaco residence. (EY p. 1020)

**Key caveat:** The absence of income tax only relates to activities carried out and persons who are **genuinely established** in Monaco. Foreign-source income of non-Monegasque/non-French individuals is subject to domestic rules of the country where the income source is located — Monaco does not tax it, but the source country may. (EY p. 1020)

**PIT rate for residents (non-French nationals): 0%**
**PIT rate for French nationals residing in Monaco: French tax rates apply (see France page)**

## Other Taxes

**Wealth tax:** Does not apply in Monaco, except for French assets (French wealth tax rules may apply to French nationals). (EY p. 1020)

**Property tax:** Not levied in Monaco. (EY p. 1020)

**Inheritance and gift taxes:**
- Apply only to assets located in Monaco or with a situs in Monaco, regardless of nationality of deceased or donor
- Rates (EY p. 1021):

| Relationship | Rate |
|---|---|
| Spouses or children | **0%** (no tax) |
| Partners of civil union agreement | **4%** |
| Siblings | **8%** |
| Uncles/aunts, nephews/nieces | **10%** |
| Other relatives | **13%** |
| Unrelated persons (including charities and corporate entities) | **16%** |

**Transfer tax on real estate:**
- Standard rate: **6.5%** of sale proceeds (or fair market value if higher); reduced to **4.75%** for transactions meeting transparency criteria (beneficial ownership disclosure)
- Shares in Monegasque Société Civile Particulière (SCP — property holding company): 6.5% on portion relating to underlying real estate; 1% on other assets
- Shares in other companies: 1% transfer tax; sale of shares in foreign companies: **exempt** from Monaco transfer tax (EY p. 1022)

**Trust registration duty:** Proportional rate based on number of effective beneficiaries (1 beneficiary: 1.3%; 2: 1.5%; more than 2: 1.7%); may be converted to annual 0.2% fee. (EY p. 1022)

## Social Security

**Contributions (2024 rates; 2025 rates to be available in October 2025):** (EY p. 1023)

Total combined burden (employee + employer): approximately **10%–14%** for employees; **28%–40%** for employers depending on retirement fund contributions and remuneration level.

**Breakdown of key 2024 contributions (employee share):**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Monthly income ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sickness & family allocation | **0%** | **13.45%** | EUR 9,400 |
| Basic state pension | **6.85%** | — | EUR 6,028 |
| Unemployment | **2.4%** | **4%** | EUR 15,700 |
| **Employee total (approx.)** | **~9.25%–10%** | **~17.45%–28%+** | — |

Additional contributions for retirement funds and supplemental pension (dependant on fund tier) bring total employer burden to approximately 28%–40% of gross salary.

**Notes:**
- Social security taxes are withheld monthly by employers; contributions are due on compensation including bonuses and benefits in kind earned from performing an activity in Monaco and paid in Monaco
- The rule may be modified by a social security totalization agreement
- Totalization agreements: France and Italy only (EY p. 1023)
- **2025 rates and ceilings** will be available October 2025 (not yet confirmed at EY data date)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Genuine Establishment in Monaco (Self-employed activity)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals genuinely established in Monaco carrying on a business activity
- **Tax base / Rate:** **0% income tax** for non-French nationals on Monaco-source business income
- **Social security:** Standard social contribution rules apply; self-employed contribution rates broadly comparable to combined employer + employee rates (full details not specified in EY guide — contact Monaco social security office)
- **VAT obligations:** Monaco applies French VAT law; standard rate 20%; reduced rates 5.5%, 10%; Monaco forms a customs union with France
- **Key limits:** Must be genuinely established in Monaco; activity must be carried out in Monaco

### Option B — French National Established in Monaco

- **Who qualifies:** French nationals regardless of Monaco residence
- **Tax base / Rate:** **French income tax rates apply** (progressive to 45% + exceptional contribution 3%/4% on income >EUR 250,000/500,000)
- **Social security:** Monaco social security contributions apply to Monaco-sourced employment
- **Special rule:** French-Monaco 1963 bilateral treaty governs; French nationals pay French tax on worldwide income

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Residence Permit (for stays >3 months)

- **EU/EEA nationals:** Identity card or valid passport sufficient; no visa required for entry; must apply for residence permit if staying >3 months
- **Non-EU/EEA nationals:** Must apply for a long-stay visa for Monaco, issued by French authorities, before making a residence permit application (EY p. 1024)
- Residence permit applications handled by the Residents Section of the Police Department; process varies by applicant background
- **Under Sovereign Ordinance No. 8.372 (26 November 2020):** An individual can apply for a residency certificate with Monegasque authorities to complete a tax formality, particularly for automatic exchange of financial account information reporting obligations (EY p. 1025)

### Residency Without Tax Filing Obligation (Non-French nationals)

- Non-French nationals who establish genuine Monaco residence and carry out activities in Monaco pay **0% income tax**
- No filing requirement for personal income tax (no income tax system to file under)
- Must obtain Monaco residence permit; maintain genuine establishment

### Double Tax Treaties

Monaco has entered into DTTs with: France, Guernsey, Italy, Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, Mali, Malta, Mauritius, Montenegro, Qatar, St. Kitts and Nevis, Seychelles, United Arab Emirates (EY p. 1024)

## 2026 Changes

- **No specific 2026 changes** announced for Monaco's income tax (which remains 0% for non-French)
- **Social security 2025 rates and ceilings:** To be published in October 2025 (EY p. 1023); 2026 rates not yet available
- **Automatic exchange of information:** Monaco continues participating in OECD/G20 CRS automatic exchange of financial account information; CbCR exchange since 1 April 2017. Enhanced reporting obligations continue for Monaco entities.
- **Beneficial ownership register:** Monegasque Law No. 1.462 (28 June 2018) requires entities to communicate beneficial ownership information to the Minister of State; ongoing compliance obligation (EY p. 1023)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Social security 2025 and 2026 rates:** EY notes 2025 rates/ceilings available in October 2025 — not confirmed in the guide. All calculations use 2024 figures. Verify at Monaco social security (CCSS — Caisse de Compensation des Services Sociaux).
- [ ] **Self-employed social security exact rates:** EY describes combined employer+employee total of 10%–14% (employee) and 28%–40% (employer) for employed persons. Self-employed rates not separately stated — contact CCSS for exact rates.
- [ ] **Genuine establishment test:** EY notes the 0% tax only applies to persons genuinely established in Monaco. No specific definition provided. The Monaco tax administration may scrutinise arrangements where the individual does not genuinely carry out activities in Monaco.
- [ ] **French national exception nuances:** The 1963 France-Monaco treaty applies to French nationals; specific provisions relating to different income types (investment income, rental, capital gains) may have specific rules. Consult French tax administration or Monaco specialist.
- [ ] **Monaco residency minimum requirements:** No explicit financial minimum for Monaco residence permit stated in EY guide (unlike some other high-wealth jurisdictions). In practice, Monaco requires evidence of accommodation and financial self-sufficiency.
- [ ] **VAT on self-employed services:** Monaco applies French VAT law; self-employed providing services may be subject to French VAT rules, including the EUR 36,800 micro-entrepreneur threshold for VAT exemption (franchise en base).
