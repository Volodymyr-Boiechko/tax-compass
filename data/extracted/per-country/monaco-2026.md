## Personal Income Tax

> Monaco does not levy personal income tax on individuals, with one critical exception: French nationals residing in Monaco are subject to French income tax under the 1963 bilateral tax treaty between France and Monaco. All other nationals (Monegasque, other EU nationals, third-country nationals) who are genuinely established in Monaco pay no personal income tax. Foreign-source income of Monaco residents is subject to the domestic tax rules of the source country, not Monaco tax.

| Taxable income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All income (non-French residents) | 0% |
| French nationals (under 1963 Treaty) | French PIT rates apply (see France file) |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- None applicable for non-French nationals — no PIT law exists for them

**Notes:**
- The 0% rate applies only to persons "genuinely established" in Monaco — i.e., actual residents who are not French nationals
- French nationals who take up residence in Monaco remain subject to French PIT (progressive rates up to 45%) as if they still lived in France
- Monaco participates in OECD Common Reporting Standard (CRS) and FATCA for financial account information exchange (EY p. 1024)
- No capital gains tax for non-French residents
- No wealth tax for non-French residents (wealth tax does not apply in Monaco except for French assets under the France–Monaco treaty) (EY p. 1020)
- Property tax (taxe foncière / taxe d'habitation): not levied in Monaco (EY p. 1020)
- Inheritance/gift taxes: apply only to assets located in Monaco or with a situs in Monaco; rates 0% (spouses/children) to 16% (unrelated persons) (EY p. 1021)

## Social Security

> Monegasque social security contributions are withheld monthly by employers. The 2025 rates will be available in October 2025 (2024 rates shown below). Total employee contribution approximately 10–14% of gross salary depending on retirement fund and level of remuneration. Total employer contribution approximately 28–40%.

| Contribution | Employee % | Employer % | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sickness & family allocation | 0% | 13.45% | Monthly ceiling EUR 9,400 |
| Basic state pension (AV) | 6.85% | 8.31% | Monthly ceiling EUR 6,028 |
| Unemployment | 2.40% | 4.00% | Monthly ceiling EUR 15,700 |
| Additional retirement funds | varies | varies | Depends on convention chosen |
| **Total (approximate)** | **~10–14%** | **~28–40%** | |

*(EY p. 1023 — 2024 rates; 2025 rates available October 2025)*

**Income ceiling:** Varies by contribution type (EUR 9,400 / EUR 6,028 / EUR 15,700 monthly) (EY p. 1023)
**Notes:**
- Contributions apply to compensation earned from performing activity in Monaco and paid in Monaco
- Rule may be modified by a social security totalization agreement (Monaco has totalization agreements with France and Italy)
- Individual social security taxes withheld monthly by employers
- Self-employed persons are subject to different contribution schemes depending on their activity

## Self-Employment Regimes

> There is no personal income tax in Monaco for non-French nationals. Self-employed individuals still pay social security contributions. The main consideration is obtaining a business license (patente commerciale) and registering with the Trade and Industry Register (Répertoire du Commerce et de l'Industrie).

### Option A — Sole Trader / Individual Enterprise (Entreprise Individuelle)
- **Who qualifies:** Any non-French national genuinely established in Monaco carrying on a trade or profession
- **Tax base:** No personal income tax; business profits not subject to direct tax (unless the business has revenue exceeding EUR 25,000/year from activities outside Monaco — Monaco does not have a corporate tax on purely Monaco-based activities; foreign activities subject to separate rules)
- **Rate / mechanism:** 0% personal income tax for non-French nationals
- **Social security:** Employee-equivalent social security contributions apply; rates ~10–14% employee side; employer-equivalent contributions additional ~28–40%
- **VAT obligations:** Monaco applies French VAT rules (as part of the France-Monaco customs union); standard VAT rate 20%
- **Key limits:** Requires patente commerciale (business license); registration with IMSEE and trade register required; "genuinely established" means principal residence and principal business activity in Monaco

### Option B — Monaco SAM / SARL (Company)
- **Who qualifies:** Any entrepreneur registering a company in Monaco
- **Tax base:** Companies deriving more than 25% of revenue from outside Monaco are subject to Monaco business profits tax (impôt sur les bénéfices) at 26.5%; purely Monaco-based activity: no business profits tax
- **Rate / mechanism:** 26.5% CIT on foreign-sourced profits; 0% on Monaco-sourced profits
- **Social security:** Normal employee/employer contributions on salary paid
- **VAT obligations:** French VAT rules apply (20% standard)
- **Key limits:** Share capital requirements; auditing requirements for larger entities

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Monaco Residency — Zero PIT for Non-French Nationals
- **Eligibility:** Any non-French national (EU, EEA, non-EU) who takes up genuine residence in Monaco; must not be a French national
- **Benefit:** Complete exemption from personal income tax on all worldwide income (employment, self-employment, dividends, capital gains, rental income from outside Monaco)
- **Duration:** Indefinite while resident and non-French
- **Application deadline:** Residence permit required; apply to Monaco Police Department (Residents Section); must be present in Monaco >3 months/year and demonstrate genuine establishment (EY p. 1024–1025)
- **Caveats:** French nationals cannot benefit — subject to French PIT regardless; must obtain a Monaco residence permit (carte de résident); housing in Monaco is extremely expensive (among the most expensive real estate in the world); proof of accommodation and financial stability required

### Residence Permit Process
- **Eligibility:** Any non-French foreign national over 16 wishing to reside >3 months/year in Monaco
- **Benefit:** Residency entitles to zero PIT status
- **Duration:** 1–3 years initially; renewable
- **Application deadline:** Apply to Police Department; must provide proof of accommodation, stable income, and clean criminal record
- **Caveats:** EU/EEA nationals: identity card or valid passport sufficient (no visa required); non-EEA nationals must first obtain a long-term residence visa from French authorities (Monaco not an EU member); Monaco has very limited housing supply — obtaining qualifying accommodation is the primary practical constraint

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 1020–1025)

- Social security contribution rates for 2025 were to be published in October 2025 — 2026 rates will be published in October 2026 (EY p. 1023); 2024 rates shown above
- No changes to the 0% PIT status for non-French nationals
- French nationals remain subject to French PIT under the 1963 treaty
- Monaco has implemented Sovereign Ordinance No. 8.372 (26 November 2020) on residency certificates for tax transparency purposes — compliance obligations ongoing (EY p. 1025)
- Monaco is party to OECD/G20 Common Reporting Standard (CRS) for automatic exchange of financial account information

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, non-French national, genuinely established Monaco resident.
> Monaco uses EUR. No conversion needed.
> Because there is no PIT for non-French nationals, effective income tax rate is 0% at all income levels.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €0 | ~€3,000–€4,200 (10–14%) | ~€3,000–€4,200 | **~10–14%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | €0 | Varies by activity | ~€0–€3,000 | **~0–10%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €0 | ~€6,000–€8,400 (10–14%) | ~€6,000–€8,400 | **~10–14%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | €0 | Varies by activity | ~€0–€6,000 | **~0–10%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €0 | ~€10,000–€14,000 (10–14%) | ~€10,000–€14,000 | **~10–14%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | €0 | Varies by activity | ~€0–€10,000 | **~0–10%** |

**Step-by-step (€60,000 employment, non-French national):**
1. PIT = €0 (no PIT law for non-French nationals)
2. Employee SS: approximately 10–14% of gross salary depending on retirement fund elections; on salary within ceilings: ~10% effective
3. Sickness/family: 0% employee contribution; pension 6.85% on salary up to EUR 6,028/month = EUR 72,336/year; unemployment 2.4% on salary up to EUR 15,700/month
4. Total employee SS at €60,000: pension (on €60,000 within ceiling) + unemployment (on €60,000 within ceiling): ~(€60,000 × 6.85%) + (€60,000 × 2.4%) = €4,110 + €1,440 = €5,550; additional supplementary retirement contributions variable
5. Approximate total employee SS = €5,550–€8,400; effective rate = 9.3%–14%

**Assumptions for French nationals (reference only):**
- French nationals in Monaco pay French PIT on worldwide income
- See France file for French PIT rates — same progressive rates apply (20/30/41/45%)
- French nationals cannot benefit from Monaco's 0% PIT status
- Employer still pays Monaco social security (28–40% of gross salary)

**Assumptions:**
- Non-French national, genuine Monaco resident
- Social security contributions based on 2024 rates (2025 rates not yet published at EY review)
- Ceilings: sickness/family EUR 9,400/month; pension EUR 6,028/month; unemployment EUR 15,700/month
- B2B: self-employed; SS depends heavily on activity type and regime chosen
- No VAT impact on income calculations
- French VAT (20%) applies to business transactions
