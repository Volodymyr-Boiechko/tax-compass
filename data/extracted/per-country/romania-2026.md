## Personal Income Tax

Flat 10% PIT for residents and nonresidents on most types of income. Tax year = calendar year.
Exchange rate as of 30 September 2025: RON 5.0811 = EUR 1 (EY p.1321). Using **1 EUR = RON 5.0** for rounding.
Single tax return (Declarația Unică) filed annually by **25 May** of the following year.

**Standard PIT rate** (EY p.1321, 1326):

| Income type | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salary / employment income | 10% | Flat rate on gross after deductions |
| Freelance / independent activities (PFA) | 10% | On net income (gross minus expenses) |
| Rental income | 10% | 20% flat expense deduction from gross |
| Intellectual property rights | 10% | 40% flat expense deduction from gross (or actual) |
| Investment / interest income | 10% | Final withholding at source |
| Dividends (distributed on/after 1 Jan 2025) | 10% | Final withholding; **16% from 1 January 2026** |
| Capital gains — securities held < 365 days (via Romanian intermediary) | 3% | Reduced rate vs. standard |
| Capital gains — securities held ≥ 365 days (via Romanian intermediary) | 1% | Preferential long-term rate |
| Capital gains — other / foreign brokers | 10% | Annual regularization |
| Income from gambling | Special | Progressive or flat per separate rules |
| Unidentified income source | 70% | Applied to reconstructed tax base (EY p.1326) |

**Dividend tax — 2026 change confirmed** (EY p.1324; Law 141/2025):
- Dividends distributed from 1 January 2026: **16%** (up from 10% in 2025)
- Dividends based on interim financial statements prepared in 2025: **10%** transitional rate
- This is the most significant individual tax change for 2026

**Rental income deduction** (EY p.1323–1324):
- Flat 20% expense deduction from gross rental income; 80% is taxable at 10% PIT
- From 1 January 2024: entities with accounting obligations must withhold and pay the 10% tax on net
  rental income from individual landlords (except agricultural and personal property tourism rentals)

**Intellectual property deduction** (EY p.1323):
- 40% flat expense deduction from gross IP income; taxable at 10% on 60% of gross
- Or actual expenses under "real system" (requires minimum 2 consecutive years)

**Personal deductions** (EY p.1326):
- Basic personal deduction: available to employees with gross monthly salary ≤ RON 2,000 above the
  minimum gross basic salary (not available for gross monthly salary > that threshold)
- Additional personal deduction:
  - 15% of guaranteed minimum gross salary per month for individuals ≤ 26 years old with salary income
  - RON 100/month per child up to age 18 enrolled in education (for the parent with salary income)
- Voluntary pension contributions: deductible up to EUR 400/year equivalent
- Voluntary health insurance + medical subscriptions: deductible up to EUR 400/year equivalent (cumulative)
- Sport subscriptions: deductible up to EUR 100/year

**Non-taxable benefits limit** (EY p.1322):
- Monthly cumulative non-taxable limit of 33% of the employee's basic salary for qualifying benefits
  (food, accommodation, vacation, voluntary pension up to EUR 400, health insurance up to EUR 400, sport up to EUR 100)

## Social Security

**Employment income — standard rates** (EY p.1327):

| Contribution | Employee % | Employer % | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension (CAS) | 25% | — | Employee pays |
| Health fund (CASS) | 10% | — | Employee pays |
| Work insurance (employer) | — | 2.25% | Covers unemployment, medical leave, accidents, salary guarantee |
| **Total** | **35%** | **2.25%** | |

**Income ceiling (social security):** No annual cap on CAS or CASS for employment income — applies to all gross salary.

**Non-employment private income — CAS and CASS** (EY p.1327–1328):

For freelancers/PFA/independent activities and IP income, CAS (25%) and CASS (10%) apply:

**CAS (pension) for non-employment income:**
- Applies if cumulated net annual income ≥ 12 minimum national salaries per economy (~EUR 9,565 / RON 48,600 for 2025)
- Contribution base chosen by taxpayer; minimum:
  - 12 minimum salaries/year if income between 12× and 24× minimum salary
  - 24 minimum salaries/year if income ≥ 24× minimum salary
- Rate: **25%** on chosen base (minimum: 25% × RON 48,600 = RON 12,150/year for 12× band)

**CASS (health) for non-employment income:**
- Applies to broader range of private income (independent activities, IP rights, rental, agricultural, investment, other sources)
- Contribution calculation capped at:
  - 6× minimum salary base (minimum): if net annual income between 6× and 12× minimum salary
  - 12× minimum salary base: if income between 12× and 24× minimum salary
  - 24× minimum salary base: if income ≥ 24× minimum salary
- Rate: **10%** on applicable base
- For 2026: minimum base 6× = RON 24,300; maximum base 72× = RON 291,600 (CASS cap raised from 60× to 72× in 2026)
- CASS deductible from income tax base

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — PFA (Persoană Fizică Autorizată — Authorized Natural Person / Sole Trader)

- **Who qualifies:** Natural persons authorized to conduct independent economic activities. Common
  for freelancers, consultants, IT contractors, artists, liberal professions. Registered at the
  Trade Register Office. (EY p.1323)
- **Tax base:** Gross income from freelance activities minus deductible expenses.
  Option to use real-system (actual expenses, requires accounting records) or standard 40% deduction
  for IP-related activities. For general independent activities: actual documented expenses.
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - Income tax: **10%** flat on net income (gross − expenses)
  - Filed via Declarația Unică by 25 May; advance tax payments can be required
- **CAS (pension):** 25% if annual net income ≥ 12× minimum national salary (~RON 48,600 for 2026)
  - At income ≥ 12×: base = RON 48,600; CAS = 25% × 48,600 = **RON 12,150/year**
  - At income ≥ 24×: base = RON 97,200; CAS = 25% × 97,200 = **RON 24,300/year**
  - Below 12× minimum salary: CAS optional
- **CASS (health):** 10% on capped base
  - Minimum (income ≥ 6× minimum salary): 10% × RON 24,300 = **RON 2,430/year** (minimum CASS)
  - At 12× minimum salary: 10% × RON 48,600 = RON 4,860/year
  - At 24× minimum salary: 10% × RON 97,200 = RON 9,720/year
  - CASS is tax-deductible from PIT base
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory VAT registration at RON 300,000 (~EUR 59,000) turnover; standard
  VAT rate 19% (reduced 5%/9% for certain goods/services)
- **Key limits:**
  - PFA is personally liable; no liability protection
  - Most favorable for IT/creative freelancers using 40% IP expense deduction on IP income
  - If also employed: CASS already paid via employment → CASS from PFA income still applies separately
    (but there's an exemption mechanism — see Known Gaps)

### Option B — Micro-Company (SRL / LLC under Micro-Enterprise Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Romanian SRL (limited liability company) with revenue ≤ EUR 100,000 in the
  previous year (threshold dramatically reduced from EUR 250,000 in 2025 to EUR 100,000 for 2026).
  Must have at least one employee on a full-time labor contract. (OUG trenuleț; socotim.ro; termene.ro)
- **Tax base:** **Gross revenue** (turnover) — no expense deductions. All revenue taxed at flat rate.
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - **1% on revenue** (2026: unified rate; the 3% rate for companies with no employees has been
    eliminated from 2026 — all micro-enterprises pay 1%)
  - Previously (2025): 1% for companies with ≥ 1 employee; 3% for no-employee companies
  - Previously (pre-2025): threshold was EUR 500,000; reduced to EUR 250,000 in 2025; now EUR 100,000 in 2026
- **Dividend tax (when owner extracts profit):** **16%** from 1 January 2026 (up from 10% in 2025).
  Withheld at source. (EY p.1324; Law 141/2025)
- **Social security for owner-employee:** Owner must pay themselves a salary if also the sole employee
  to qualify for the 1% rate; salary subject to normal employment CAS 25% + CASS 10% + employer 2.25%.
- **Corporate income tax (CIT) alternative:** Companies above EUR 100,000 revenue pay standard CIT:
  - **16% on profit** (general CIT rate — not covered in EY individual pages)
  - Minimum tax: 1% of turnover if calculated CIT < 1% of turnover (introduced 2024)
- **VAT obligations:** Same as PFA — mandatory at RON 300,000 turnover.
- **Key limits:**
  - Must have at least 1 employee (full-time); owner-employees qualify
  - If revenue exceeds EUR 100,000 at any point in 2026, company exits micro-regime for that year
  - Revenue must come from allowed activities (certain financial services, insurance, gambling, etc. excluded)
  - The micro-regime is extremely favorable for service businesses: at EUR 100,000 revenue with one employee,
    total tax = 1% × 100,000 = EUR 1,000 (plus employment costs for the employee/owner)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No General Expatriate Tax Exemption

Romania does not have a general newcomer/expat preferential PIT rate. Standard 10% flat rate applies.

### R&D Tax Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Employees engaged in qualifying research and development activities (EY p.1322)
- **Benefit:** Tax exemption from salary income tax for qualifying R&D workers. (Remains in force
  despite withdrawal of software/construction/agriculture/food sector exemptions in January 2025)
- **Caveats:** Strict qualifying conditions; employer must certify the R&D activity

### Temporary Protection (Ukrainian Refugees)

- **Eligibility:** Ukrainian nationals, beneficiaries of international protection, family members,
  stateless persons with permanent stay in Ukraine before 24 February 2022. (EY p.1332)
- **Benefit:** Simplified administrative procedures, access to humanitarian assistance.
- **Caveats:** Labor and tax obligations apply if employed in Romania under normal terms.

## 2026 Changes

- **Dividend tax increase:** From **10% to 16%** effective 1 January 2026 (Law 141/2025). Major
  change for SRL owners and investors. Interim 2025 dividends remain at 10%. (EY p.1324)
- **Micro-enterprise revenue threshold reduced:** From EUR 250,000 to **EUR 100,000** for 2026.
  Companies above this threshold move to 16% CIT regime. (OUG trenuleț; termene.ro)
- **Micro-enterprise 3% rate eliminated:** Unified **1% rate** for all qualifying micro-enterprises
  from 2026 (previously 3% for those without employees). (socotim.ro; woborders.agency)
- **CASS maximum base raised:** From 60× to **72× minimum national salary** for 2026 (cap =
  RON 291,600 vs RON 243,000 in 2025) — affects high-income self-employed. (teaha.ro)
- **Software/construction sector PIT exemption withdrawn:** Applied until end of 2024; salary
  income exemptions for IT, construction, agriculture, food industries withdrawn from January 2025. (EY p.1322)
- **Bonds interest income:** From 1 August 2025, interest from Romanian legal entity bonds on
  foreign capital markets — withholding tax no longer applies; taxpayer declares via annual return. (EY p.1325)
- **CASS exemptions expanded:** From August 2025, new categories exempt from health contributions
  (spouses/parents without income, pensioners above RON 3,000 pension, unemployment benefit recipients,
  childcare leave recipients). (EY p.1328)
- **Income from scrapping metals:** From 1 August 2025, income from surrender of ferrous/non-ferrous
  metals classified as waste subject to 10% withholding at source. (EY p.1326)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants. 1 EUR = RON 5.0 (rounded from official 5.0811).
> "Employment" = gross salary; employee-side only (employer 2.25% work insurance not shown).
> "PFA" = authorized natural person; IP income with 40% expense deduction used (typical IT/creative PFA).
> "Micro SRL" = single-person SRL under micro-enterprise regime with 1% on revenue; owner extracts
>  profit as dividends (16% in 2026 on top of 1% company tax).
> 2026 rates used. CAS and CASS at thresholds per actual brackets.

### Working: RON 150,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee CAS (25%) | 25% × 150,000 | RON 37,500 |
| Employee CASS (10%) | 10% × 150,000 | RON 15,000 |
| PIT base | 150,000 − 37,500 − 15,000 − basic personal deduction (small; negligible above threshold) | RON 97,500 |
| PIT at 10% | 10% × 97,500 | RON 9,750 |
| **Total burden** | 37,500 + 15,000 + 9,750 | **RON 62,250** |
| **Effective rate** | 62,250 / 150,000 | **41.5%** |
| **EUR equiv** | 62,250 / 5.0 | **EUR 12,450** |

*Romania has one of the highest effective employment tax burdens in CEE due to the 35% employee-side contributions (CAS 25% + CASS 10%).*

### Working: RON 150,000 (≈ €30,000) PFA — IP income with 40% expense deduction (2026)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 40% expense deduction | 40% × 150,000 | RON 60,000 |
| Net income (taxable) | 150,000 − 60,000 | RON 90,000 |
| Income ≥ 12× min salary (RON 48,600)? Yes → CAS applies | CAS base = RON 48,600 | RON 48,600 |
| CAS (25%) | 25% × 48,600 | RON 12,150 |
| Income ≥ 6× min salary (RON 24,300)? Yes → CASS applies | CASS base = RON 48,600 (12× band; income between 12×−24×) | RON 48,600 |
| CASS (10%) | 10% × 48,600 | RON 4,860 |
| PIT base | 90,000 − 4,860 (CASS deductible) | RON 85,140 |
| PIT at 10% | 10% × 85,140 | RON 8,514 |
| **Total burden** | 12,150 + 4,860 + 8,514 | **RON 25,524** |
| **Effective rate** | 25,524 / 150,000 | **17.0%** |
| **EUR equiv** | 25,524 / 5.0 | **EUR 5,105** |

*PFA with IP income (40% deduction) is dramatically more efficient than employment: 17% vs 41.5% at €30k.*

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|

**Key insight — Employment vs PFA:** Romania has a fixed 41.5% effective rate on employment across all income levels (CAS 25% + CASS 10% on gross = 35% employee contributions, then 10% PIT on net ≈ 6.5%, total ≈ 41.5%). PFA with 40% IP deduction achieves ~17% effective rate because: (a) CAS/CASS contributions are capped (not proportional to gross revenue), and (b) the 40% expense deduction reduces the net taxable income significantly. This makes PFA (IP route) one of the most tax-efficient structures in Romania for IT/creative professionals.

**Warning — 2026 dividend tax impact on micro SRL:** The increase from 10% to 16% dividend tax significantly reduces the attractiveness of the micro-SRL structure for profit extraction. Previously: 1% company tax + 10% dividend = ~11% effective rate on revenue. Now: 1% + 16% dividend = ~17% effective rate on revenue (at 100% profit margin). Still competitive vs employment (41.5%) but gap has narrowed.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/RON = 5.0 (EY rate 5.0811; rounded for simplicity)
- Employment: no personal deduction modeled (phase-out at low income; negligible for these salary levels)
- PFA: 40% IP/copyright expense deduction used (typical for IT contractors, software developers, artists)
- CAS minimum national salary 2026: RON 4,050/month gross (minimum wage; RON 48,600/year for 12× base)
- CASS 2026: new maximum cap 72× minimum salary = RON 291,600 (increased from 60× in 2025)
- Micro SRL: 1% rate on revenue; 16% dividend tax on distributions in 2026; owner employment costs not modeled in summary table
- No child tax credits, no pension/health voluntary deductions modeled
