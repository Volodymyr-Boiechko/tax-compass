## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (Einkommensteuer) for residents taxed on worldwide income. Tax year = calendar year. Filing deadline: 31 July of the following year (self-filers); 28/29 February of the second following year (with tax advisor). Withholding at source (Lohnsteuer) for employment income via payroll. (EY p.481)

Germany distinguishes income categories: employment (Arbeitslohn), self-employment (selbständige Arbeit — Freiberufler), business (Gewerbebetrieb), investment, rental, and other. Self-employment income for liberal/academic professions is **not** subject to trade tax (Gewerbesteuer); business income is. (EY p.471–472, 478)

**Einkommensteuer — 2025 rates** (EY p.477–478)

The tax function is a continuously progressive formula (no simple bracket table). Key reference points from EY:

| Taxable income (EUR) — Single filer | Effective rate | Marginal rate | Tax due (EUR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 12,096 | 0% | 0% | 0 |
| 12,096 – 68,481 | Progressive | Rising to 42% | — |
| 50,000 | 21.38% | 35.47% | 10,691 |
| 68,481 – 277,826 | Progressive | 42% | — |
| 100,000 | 31.09% | 42.00% | 31,088 |
| 150,000 | 34.73% | 42.00% | 52,088 |
| 210,000 | 36.80% | 42.00% | 77,288 |
| Over 277,826 | 38.13%+ | 45% | 106,753+ |

**Basic tax-free allowance (Grundfreibetrag):** EUR 12,096 (2025, single). EUR 24,192 for married couples filing jointly. (EY p.476)

**Top rate thresholds (2025):**
- 42% rate applies from EUR 68,481 up to EUR 277,825 (single)
- 45% "rich tax" (Reichensteuer) applies above EUR 277,826 (single); EUR 555,652 (joint)

**Solidarity surcharge (Solidaritätszuschlag / Soli):** 5.5% surcharge on income tax liability; phased out from 2021 for most taxpayers. Applies only to those with high income tax liability (above EUR 18,130 for single filers in 2025 — approximately income above EUR 96,800). For most middle-income earners: Soli = 0. (EY p.478)

**Kirchensteuer (Church tax):** 8% or 9% of income tax liability (depending on state/Bundesland); only for registered members of a Catholic or Protestant church. Standard deductible as special expense. (EY p.472, 478)

**Investment income (Kapitalertragsteuer):** Flat 25% withholding tax (Abgeltungsteuer) + 5.5% Soli surcharge on the flat tax + church tax if applicable; final tax in most cases. Tax-free allowance EUR 1,000/year (single); EUR 2,000 (married). (EY p.472)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Grundfreibetrag: EUR 12,096 (effective zero-rate threshold) (EY p.476)
- Standard employment deduction (Arbeitnehmer-Pauschbetrag): EUR 1,230/year (EY p.475)
- Special expenses (Sonderausgaben): social security contributions (employee portion), church tax, EUR 36 flat charitable deduction; actual charitable donations deductible up to 20% of income
- Business deductions (Freiberufler): all actual professional expenses deductible from self-employment income (EY p.478)

**Capital gains:**
- Real estate: taxed at ordinary rates if held ≤10 years (Spekulationsfrist); exempt after 10 years or if primary residence in year of sale and 2 preceding years (EY p.474)
- Shares (post-2008): 25% flat withholding; gains on pre-2009 shares acquired before 1 January 2018 exempt; tax-free if total annual gains < EUR 600 (EY p.474)

**Notes:**
- Married couples may file jointly (Zusammenveranlagung); income splitting applies (each partner taxed on half combined income × 2) — significant benefit for single-earner couples
- Loss carryback: up to EUR 1 million (EUR 2M joint) to prior year; unlimited carryforward (with minimum taxation rule: only 60% of income above EUR 1M can be offset by losses in any year) (EY p.479)

## Social Security

Five pillars: old-age pension, unemployment, health, nursing care, accident insurance. Contributions shared equally between employer and employee (except accident insurance, employer-only). (EY p.480)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed (Freiberufler) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Old-age pension (RV) | 9.30% | 9.30% | 18.60% (optional/voluntary) |
| Unemployment (ALV) | 1.30% | 1.30% | Not applicable |
| Health insurance (GKV) | 7.30% + ~1.25% surcharge | 7.30% + ~1.25% surcharge | Full premium if voluntary GKV |
| Nursing care (PV) | 1.80% + 0.60% childless | 1.80% | — |
| Accident insurance (UV) | 0% | ~1–2% | — |
| **Total (approx., with health surcharge)** | **~21.0–21.5%** | **~21.0–21.5%** | **Varies** |

**Contribution ceilings 2025** (EY p.480):
- Old-age pension + unemployment: EUR 96,600/year (West Germany) = EUR 8,050/month. No contributions on income above this ceiling.
- Health + nursing care: EUR 66,150/year = EUR 5,512.50/month
- Health insurance compulsory if income ≤ EUR 73,800/year; above this, can opt for private insurance (PKV)

**Health insurance detail** (EY p.480):
- Statutory health insurance (GKV): 14.6% total (7.3% employee + 7.3% employer) + average individual surcharge ~2.5% split equally → ~16%–17% total; capped at EUR 66,150
- Private health insurance (PKV): available if income > EUR 73,800; employer reimburses up to EUR 471.32/month

**Nursing care** (EY p.480):
- 3.6% total: 1.8% employee + 1.8% employer
- Childless employees (age 23+): additional 0.6% employee surcharge → employee pays 2.4%
- Reduced rates for parents with multiple children (from 1 July 2023)

**Freiberufler (self-employed liberal professionals):**
- Not covered by statutory unemployment or accident insurance
- Pension: not automatically covered by German statutory pension (DRV); some Freiberufler professions (doctors, lawyers, architects, etc.) have mandatory professional pension chambers (Versorgungswerke); others may contribute voluntarily to DRV
- Health: must have health insurance — either statutory GKV (self-pay full 14.6% + surcharge, minimum ~EUR 200/month) or private PKV
- No employer matching — Freiberufler bears full health insurance cost

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Freiberufler (Liberal/Academic Profession)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals in qualifying "liberal professions" (freie Berufe) as defined by §18 EStG: doctors, dentists, lawyers, tax advisors, architects, engineers, journalists, artists, teachers, scientists, and **IT consultants/software developers** (if work is of a comparable scientific/creative nature). Key distinction from Gewerbebetrieb: no trade registration (Gewerbeanmeldung) required, activity is intellectual/creative by nature. (EY p.471–472)
- **Tax base:** Net profit = revenue minus all deductible professional expenses. Cash-basis accounting (Einnahmen-Überschuss-Rechnung, EÜR — simplified profit/loss statement, Form EÜR). All reasonable business expenses deductible: home office (EUR 1,260/year flat or actual), equipment, software, professional development, subscriptions, travel, professional insurance.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net profit taxed at progressive Einkommensteuer rates (0–45%). Grundfreibetrag EUR 12,096 applies. No Gewerbesteuer (trade tax). **This is the key advantage over Gewerbebetrieb.**
- **Social security:** No statutory employer-side; self-pays health insurance (GKV or PKV) and voluntary/mandatory pension (Versorgungswerk or DRV). Health costs approximately EUR 200–800/month depending on income and choice.
- **VAT (Umsatzsteuer):** Standard 19% on most professional services; 7% for certain cultural/educational services. Kleinunternehmer (small business) VAT exemption available if prior-year revenue ≤ EUR 22,000 (from 2025: threshold raised to EUR 25,000; EU-wide rule may raise further — check BMF). Must register for VAT and charge VAT once threshold exceeded (or from day 1 if B2B and opting in).
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling. Must ensure activity qualifies as Freiberuf (not trade/commercial). Gray area for IT: purely technical support/maintenance may be classified as Gewerbebetrieb; creative software development / intellectual design work qualifies as Freiberuf. Tax office (Finanzamt) classification can be challenged.

### Option B — Gewerbebetrieb (Trade/Commercial Business)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals whose activity does not qualify as Freiberuf (e.g., IT support/helpdesk, trading, retail, manufacturing).
- **Tax base:** Same net profit calculation; but subject to Gewerbesteuer (trade tax).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Income tax same as Freiberufler (0–45%). PLUS Gewerbesteuer:
  - Gewerbesteuer = net profit × 3.5% (federal Steuermesszahl) × municipal multiplier (Hebesatz)
  - Hebesatz varies by municipality: Frankfurt ~460%, Berlin ~410%, Munich ~490%, Hamburg ~470%
  - Effective Gewerbesteuer rate: ~14.4%–17.2% of profit (before Gewerbesteuer-free allowance of EUR 24,500 for individuals)
  - Partial credit against income tax (3.8× Steuermessbetrag deductible from income tax) — reduces but does not eliminate double burden
- **Social security:** Same as Freiberufler (no statutory employer SS; self-pays health + voluntary pension).
- **Key limits:** Significantly less favorable than Freiberufler due to Gewerbesteuer burden.

### Option C — GmbH (Limited Liability Company)

- **Who qualifies:** Any self-employed person wishing to incorporate; minimum capital EUR 25,000 (or EUR 12,500 paid-in at formation); alternative: UG (haftungsbeschränkt) with EUR 1 minimum capital.
- **Tax base / mechanism:** GmbH subject to corporate income tax (Körperschaftsteuer) 15% + solidarity surcharge + Gewerbesteuer (~14–17%) = total corporate tax ~29–32%. Managing director salary deductible from GmbH profit (subject to employment SS). Dividends distributed: 25% flat withholding + Soli + church tax.
- **Social security:** Managing director of own GmbH: if sole shareholder or majority shareholder (>50%), generally treated as self-employed — no mandatory statutory SS. If minority shareholder or employed by own GmbH: may be treated as employee → full SS applies. Complex classification; legal advice recommended.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Tax Regime (Impatriation / Article 155 B equivalent)

Germany does not have an NHR-style flat-rate regime. However: (EY p.431 [France comparison], German domestic rules)

- **30% standard deduction (Werbungskostenpauschale):** Standard EUR 1,230/year employment deduction; no special expat flat deduction exists in German law.
- **Tax equalization / reimbursement (Nettolohnvereinbarung):** Employers may agree to pay net salary; employer reimburses excess foreign tax. Standard practice for multinational assignees.
- **Fringe benefit exemptions:** Under certain conditions, employer-paid housing, cost-of-living allowances, home-leave travel, and school fees (30% of school fees up to EUR 5,000/child) are not taxable for expatriates. (EY p.471)
- **One-fifth rule (Fünftelregelung):** Severance payments and long-term bonus payments spread over 5+ years benefit from tax spreading: tax calculated on 1/5 of the amount × 5 — reduces effective marginal rate. Available in individual tax return (no longer via employer payroll from 2025). (EY p.476)

### EU Blue Card

- **Eligibility:** University degree + German employment contract ≥6 months + minimum salary EUR 48,300/year (2025) for standard; EUR 43,759.80 for shortage occupations and IT professionals. (EY p.487)
- **Benefit:** Accelerated path to permanent residence (21 months with B1 German; 33 months otherwise). Work permit tied to card, family members can accompany and work.

## 2026 Changes

- **Grundfreibetrag 2025:** EUR 12,096 (confirmed by EY p.476); annual adjustment for inflation expected for 2026 (typically EUR 100–300 increase; check BMF announcement).
- **Top rate threshold 2025:** EUR 277,826 (single); indexed annually.
- **Solidarity surcharge:** Fully phased out for most taxpayers since 2021; applies only to very high earners (~income tax liability > EUR 18,130 for single filers). No change announced.
- **One-fifth rule (Fünftelregelung):** From 1 January 2025, employers can no longer apply the one-fifth rule at payroll level (Lohnsteuer). Employees must apply for relief in their individual income tax return (Einkommensteuererklärung). (EY p.473)
- **Nursing care contributions:** Graduated rates based on number of children since July 2023 remain in force; childless employees bear higher rates.
- **Kleinunternehmer VAT threshold:** Raised to EUR 25,000 (from EUR 22,000) effective 2025; proposed EU harmonization may affect threshold going forward.
- **EU Blue Card salary thresholds:** Updated annually on 1 January; 2025 values EUR 48,300 (standard) / EUR 43,759.80 (shortage/IT). Expect similar adjustment for 2026. (EY p.487)

## Effective Rate Examples

> "Employment" = gross salary; employee SS ~21% included; employer SS not shown (~21% additional).

> Health insurance for Freiberufler: GKV self-pay ~EUR 350/month (EUR 4,200/year) assumed across all scenarios.
> No Gewerbesteuer for Freiberufler (key benefit vs. Gewerbebetrieb).
