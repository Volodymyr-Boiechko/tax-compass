## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for residents taxed on worldwide income. Non-residents taxed on New Zealand-source income only. Tax year runs 1 April to 31 March. Filing: most employees do not file a return (employer deducts PAYE); self-employed file IR3 by 7 July (or later with a tax agent). No capital gains tax (except on certain land/property held for resale; bright-line test applies). No inheritance tax. GST 15% standard rate.

**PIT brackets — 2025/26 income year** (EY p.1092)

| Taxable income (NZD) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 15,600 | 10.5% |
| 15,601 – 53,500 | 17.5% |
| 53,501 – 78,100 | 30% |
| 78,101 – 180,000 | 33% |
| Over 180,000 | 39% |

**ACC Earners' Levy (injury insurance):** ~1.60% on liable earnings up to NZD 142,283 (2025/26 levy rate; exact rate set annually by ACC). Deducted with PAYE. Self-employed also pay ACC levies (Work and Earners' levies combined; typically ~1.6–2% depending on industry). (EY p.1092)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- No standard deduction for employment income; expenses generally not deductible for employees
- Self-employed may deduct all business-related expenses
- Independent earner tax credit (IETC): up to NZD 520/year for earners with income NZD 24,000–48,000 (phases out above NZD 44,000)
- Working for Families (WFF) tax credits for families with children — income-tested
- No personal allowance/tax-free threshold; 10.5% applies from first dollar

**Notes:**
- Bright-line test: residential property sold within 2 years (own home exempt) — gain taxed as income. Reduced from 10 years back to 2 years effective 1 July 2024. (EY p.1093)
- No capital gains tax on shares, financial instruments (FIF regime applies to foreign shares above NZD 50,000 cost)
- Imputation credits system: NZ company dividends carry imputation credits for corporate tax paid; can offset personal income tax
- Resident withholding tax (RWT) on interest: 10.5%–39% depending on marginal rate
- Non-resident withholding tax on dividends: 15%; interest: 15%; royalties: 15% (subject to treaty reduction)

## Social Security

New Zealand operates a universal state-funded welfare system (NZ Superannuation in retirement) funded from general taxation — there is **no mandatory social security contribution** deducted from wages as a percentage of salary in the manner of most other countries.

**KiwiSaver — workplace retirement savings scheme** (EY p.1093):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| KiwiSaver minimum | 3% (choice: 3/4/6/8/10%) | 3% (mandatory minimum) | Voluntary only |
| Employer superannuation contribution tax (ESCT) | — | Deducted from employer contribution at marginal rate | — |

- KiwiSaver is opt-out (auto-enrolled on starting employment); self-employed may join voluntarily but no mandatory employer contribution applies
- Employer minimum 3% is on top of gross salary (not deducted from employee's pay)
- Employer contributions subject to ESCT (treated similarly to income tax on employer)
- No ceiling on contributions

**ACC Earners' Levy:** ~1.60% on earnings up to NZD 142,283 — see PIT section. This is the primary compulsory deduction beyond income tax for employees. Self-employed pay both Earners' levy and Work levy.

**Income ceiling (ACC):** NZD 142,283 (2025/26).

**Notes:**
- No unemployment insurance contribution
- No separate health insurance levy (public health system funded from taxation)
- NZ Superannuation (state pension) paid universally from age 65 — not contribution-based

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader / Self-Employed Individual (IR3)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on business in New Zealand not operating through a company. No revenue ceiling.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (revenue minus allowable business expenses). Depreciation on assets deductible.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive PIT rates as employment income (10.5%–39%). Provisional tax payments required in three instalments during the year if tax payable exceeds NZD 5,000. File annual IR3 return.
- **Social security:** KiwiSaver voluntary (no employer contribution for self-employed). ACC Work levy applies (industry-based; typically 0.2%–4% of earnings) plus Earners' levy ~1.60% on up to NZD 142,283.
- **VAT obligations:** Must register for GST at 15% once turnover exceeds NZD 60,000/year (voluntary registration below threshold). File GST returns monthly, 2-monthly or 6-monthly.
- **Key limits:** No special flat-rate option; standard progressive rates apply. Look-through companies (LTCs) and partnerships allow pass-through taxation.

### Option B — Look-Through Company (LTC)

- **Who qualifies:** A New Zealand company with ≤5 natural-person shareholders (no corporate shareholders), all electing look-through treatment. Provides liability protection while retaining pass-through tax treatment.
- **Tax base:** Income and expenses flow through to shareholders in proportion to ownership; taxed at personal progressive rates.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive PIT 10.5%–39% on shareholder's share of LTC income. Corporate tax rate (28%) does NOT apply to LTC.
- **Social security:** Same ACC obligations as sole trader for active owners.
- **VAT obligations:** Same GST registration threshold (NZD 60,000).
- **Key limits:** At-risk limitation rules cap deductible losses to shareholder's economic investment. Cannot have more than 5 owners or non-natural-person shareholders.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Transitional Resident Exemption

- **Eligibility:** New Zealand tax residents who have not been resident in NZ in the preceding 10 years (genuinely new arrivals). Applies from the date of becoming NZ tax resident.
- **Benefit:** Exemption from NZ income tax on most foreign-sourced income (dividends, interest, royalties, rental, employment income from foreign employer for services performed outside NZ) for up to 4 years. NZ-source income fully taxable from day 1.
- **Duration:** Up to 48 months (4 years) from the date of first becoming a NZ tax resident.
- **Application deadline:** Automatically available; elect on IR3 return for the relevant year. No prior application required.
- **Caveats:** Does not apply to income from employment services performed in NZ; CFC rules may still apply to foreign company interests; FIF rules still apply to certain foreign share portfolios. Cannot be claimed by individuals previously NZ tax resident within the last 10 years.

## 2026 Changes

- **Bright-line test reduced to 2 years:** Effective 1 July 2024, the residential property bright-line period reduced from 10 years to 2 years. Property sales after 2+ years of ownership generally free of income tax on the gain. (EY p.1093)
- **Interest deductibility for rental properties restored:** Full 100% deductibility of mortgage interest on rental properties restored from 2025/26 (phased in from 2023/24). (EY p.1093)
- **ACC levy rates:** Reset annually; 2025/26 Earners' levy ~1.60%. Confirmed by ACC each year.
- **No PIT rate changes** announced for 2026; 39% top rate (introduced 2021) remains in force.
- **KiwiSaver:** No rate changes; 3% minimum employee/employer rates remain.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> NZD/EUR rate: 1 EUR = NZD 1.80 (approximate 2025 average).
> Employment: PAYE + ACC Earners' levy 1.60%.
> Sole Trader: same PIT rates + ACC ~1.60% (Earners' + Work levy average).
> No KiwiSaver employee contribution shown (voluntary choice 3–10%).

### Working: NZD 54,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| ACC Earners' levy (1.60%) | 1.60% × 54,000 | NZD 864 |
| PIT: 0–15,600 @ 10.5% | 10.5% × 15,600 | NZD 1,638 |
| PIT: 15,601–53,500 @ 17.5% | 17.5% × 37,900 | NZD 6,633 |
| PIT: 53,501–54,000 @ 30% | 30% × 500 | NZD 150 |
| Total PIT | | NZD 8,421 |
| **Total burden** | **8,421 + 864** | **NZD 9,285** |
| **Effective rate** | **9,285 / 54,000** | **17.2%** |

- 78,101–108,000 @ 33% = 9,867 (29,899 × 33%)

**PIT working — NZD 180,000:**
- 0–15,600 @ 10.5% = 1,638
- 15,601–53,500 @ 17.5% = 6,633
- 53,501–78,100 @ 30% = 7,380
- 78,101–180,000 @ 33% = 33,627 (101,899 × 33%)

**Key insight:** NZ has very low effective rates at moderate incomes (17–29% all-in at €30k–€100k). The 39% top marginal rate applies only above NZD 180,000 (≈€100k); there is no separate social security levy. The bright-line reduction to 2 years makes NZ property investment more attractive again.

**Assumptions:**
- NZD/EUR = 1.80 (approximate 2025)
- ACC Earners' levy 1.60%; Work levy 1.60% average (industry-neutral estimate)
- ACC cap NZD 142,283 applies to Earners' levy
- No KiwiSaver employee contribution modeled
- No WFF tax credits (no dependants); no IETC (income above threshold)
- Self-employed: business expenses assumed zero for comparability
- GST (15%) not shown — pass-through for GST-registered businesses
