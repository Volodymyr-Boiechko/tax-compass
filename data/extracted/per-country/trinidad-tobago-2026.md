## Personal Income Tax

Two-rate system. Tax year = calendar year. Employment income is withheld under PAYE. Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-ordinarily-resident individuals taxed on T&T-source income and foreign income remitted to T&T. Administered by the Board of Inland Revenue (BIR).

**Residency:** Physically present in Trinidad and Tobago for more than six months in the income year. "Ordinary residence" expanded by common law factors (habitual place of abode).

### Tax Rates (2025 — EY p. 1537)

| Chargeable income (TTD) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 1,000,000 | 25% |
| Above 1,000,000 | 30% |

**Standard deductions / allowances (EY p. 1536):**
- Personal allowance: TTD 90,000
- Contribution to approved pension or retirement fund / deferred annuity: TTD 60,000 (combined with 70% of NIS contribution)
- Tertiary education expenses: TTD 72,000
- First-time homeowner allowance: TTD 30,000
- Maintenance or alimony (court order): unlimited
- Donations under deed of covenant: up to 15% of total income
- NIS contribution: 70% deductible (included in TTD 60,000 pension limit above)

**Tax credits:**
- Purchase/installation of CNG kit: 25% of cost, up to TTD 10,000
- Purchase of solar water heating equipment: 100% of cost, up to TTD 10,000
- Purchase of National Tax Free Savings Bonds ≤ TTD 5,000: 25% of face value

**Notes:**
- Married persons taxed separately, not jointly
- Long-term capital gains not subject to tax; short-term gains (asset held < 12 months) taxable as ordinary income
- Dividends from resident companies: exempt for resident individuals
- Interest from bank deposits and government bonds: exempt from income tax
- Rental income and royalties: taxed as ordinary income
- Non-residents: 15% withholding on interest, royalties, management fees; 8% on dividends (3% for dividends to parent companies)

## Social Security

Trinidad and Tobago has no social security program. A National Insurance Scheme (NIS) provides pension, sickness, and maternity benefits (EY p. 1538):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| National Insurance Scheme (NIS) | ~4% (TTD 11.90–138.10/week) | ~6% (TTD 23.80–276.20/week) | Weekly contributions; earnings-banded |
| Health Surcharge | TTD 4.80/week (income ≤ TTD 469.99/month) or TTD 8.25/week (income > TTD 469.99/month) | — | Levied on employed persons and individuals filing returns |
| **Total (approx. employee)** | **~4%** | **~6%** | |

**NIS Contribution bands (2025 — EY p. 1538):**
- Employee: TTD 11.90–TTD 138.10/week (earnings-based; varies by insurable earnings band)
- Employer: TTD 23.80–TTD 276.20/week

**Health Surcharge (EY p. 1538):**
- Weekly income ≤ TTD 109 (monthly ≤ TTD 469.99): TTD 4.80/week
- Weekly income > TTD 109 (monthly > TTD 469.99): TTD 8.25/week

**Notes:**
- NIS applies to employees; self-employed individuals are not compulsorily covered
- NIS 70% deductible from income tax (within combined TTD 60,000 pension limit)
- Exempt: employees under 16, unpaid apprentices, persons 60+ years old

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed / Sole Trader (General Assessment)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual engaged in trade, business, profession, or vocation
- **Tax base:** Net income from all sources (gross receipts less wholly and exclusively incurred business expenses); allowable deductions include wear-and-tear at 10%–40%, bad debts, interest on business loans, rental payments, 75% of business meals/entertainment
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard PIT rates — 25% on first TTD 1,000,000 of chargeable income; 30% above TTD 1,000,000; same brackets as employment after personal allowance (TTD 90,000)
- **Social security:** NIS: self-employed individuals not compulsorily enrolled
- **VAT obligations:** Must register for VAT if annual taxable supplies ≥ TTD 500,000; standard rate 12.5%
- **Key limits:** Quarterly installment payments on 31 March, 30 June, 30 September, 31 December; final balance by 30 April of following year

### Option B — Business Levy (Additional Obligation for Self-Employed)

- **Who qualifies:** Sole traders and self-employed persons engaged in trade or business with annual gross sales/receipts (excluding employment income) **exceeding TTD 360,000**
- **Tax base:** Gross sales or receipts (not net income)
- **Rate / mechanism:** **0.6%** of gross sales/receipts (EY p. 1537)
- **Credit:** Any income tax paid may be credited against the business levy liability if business levy is higher
- **Exemption:** Applies for first three years of a new business
- **Key limits:** Only applies above TTD 360,000 gross turnover; effectively a minimum tax on gross revenue

### Option C — Green Fund Levy

- **Who qualifies:** All companies and sole traders/self-employed engaged in business in T&T (may apply to self-employed with business income)
- **Rate:** 0.3% of gross revenue (levied on companies primarily; applicability to individuals varies)
- **Note:** Primarily a corporate obligation; confirm applicability with BIR for individual self-employed

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No General Newcomer / Expat Tax Regime

Trinidad and Tobago does not offer a general territorial or remittance-basis regime for new tax residents. All ordinarily resident individuals are taxed on worldwide income.

### Non-Resident Treatment

- Non-ordinarily resident individuals: taxed only on T&T-source income and on foreign income remitted to/received in T&T
- Non-resident employment income sourced in T&T: taxed at standard rates
- Short-term business visitors (<30 days): no work permit required; tax treatment depends on residency status and applicable treaty

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Personal allowance: TTD 90,000 deducted from gross income.
> Exchange rate: **1 EUR = TTD 7.40** (approximate 2025; USD/TTD ~6.75, EUR/USD ~1.09).
> "Employment" = gross salary. "B2B" = self-employed gross receipts; 20% deemed business expenses applied.
> NIS approximated at 4% of gross salary (employee side); Health Surcharge TTD 8.25/week = TTD 429/year (negligible).
> Business levy 0.6% applies above TTD 360,000 gross; no business levy for first 3 years (excluded from B2B scenarios).

### Working: TTD 222,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross income | | TTD 222,000 |
| Less: personal allowance | TTD 90,000 | |
| Chargeable income | 222,000 − 90,000 | TTD 132,000 |
| PIT @ 25% | 25% × 132,000 | **TTD 33,000** |
| NIS employee (~4%) | 4% × 222,000 | **TTD 8,880** |
| Health surcharge | TTD 8.25/week × 52 | TTD 429 |
| **Total burden** | 33,000 + 8,880 + 429 | **TTD 42,309** |
| **Effective rate** | 42,309 / 222,000 | **19.1%** |

**Working for €60,000 Employment (TTD 444,000):**
- Chargeable income: 444,000 − 90,000 = 354,000; PIT: 25% × 354,000 = TTD 88,500
- NIS: 4% × 444,000 = 17,760; health surcharge: 429; total social: TTD 18,189

**Working for €100,000 Employment (TTD 740,000):**
- Chargeable income: 740,000 − 90,000 = 650,000; PIT: 25% × 650,000 = TTD 162,500 (below TTD 1M threshold — 25% applies)
- NIS: 4% × 740,000 = 29,600; health surcharge: 429; total social: TTD 30,029

**Working for €60,000 B2B (TTD 444,000):**
- Net income: 444,000 × 80% = 355,200; chargeable: 355,200 − 90,000 = 265,200; PIT: 25% × 265,200 = TTD 66,300
- Business levy: 0.6% × 444,000 = TTD 2,664 (applicable above TTD 360,000 threshold; credit against PIT if levy > PIT — here levy < PIT, so both apply)

**Working for €100,000 B2B (TTD 740,000):**
- Net income: 740,000 × 80% = 592,000; chargeable: 592,000 − 90,000 = 502,000; PIT: 25% × 502,000 = TTD 125,500
- Business levy: 0.6% × 740,000 = TTD 4,440

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/TTD = 7.40 (approximate 2025)
- Personal allowance TTD 90,000 only (no pension, education, or other deductions)
- NIS employee contribution approximated at 4% (actual is a banded weekly amount; at higher incomes the maximum weekly contribution of TTD 138.10 applies = TTD 7,181/year)
- Health surcharge: TTD 8.25/week × 52 = TTD 429/year
- B2B: 20% deemed business expenses; no NIS for self-employed; business levy exemption for first 3 years not applied for established businesses
- No Green Fund Levy (primarily corporate)
- No benefit from pension/NIS deduction for simplicity
