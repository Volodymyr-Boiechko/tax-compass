## Personal Income Tax

Progressive two-bracket system. Tax year = calendar year. Each municipality/city sets its own rates within statutory ranges, so the effective rate depends on place of residence. Zagreb applies the highest permitted rates. (EY p. 306)

**Annual PIT rates — statutory ranges by municipality type:** (EY p. 306)

| Municipality type | Lower bracket (up to €60,000) | Higher bracket (above €60,000) |
|---|---|---|
| Municipalities | 15%–20% | 25%–30% |
| Towns (< 30,000 inhabitants) | 15%–21% | 25%–31% |
| Cities (≥ 30,000 inhabitants) | 15%–22% | 25%–32% |
| Zagreb | 15%–23% | 25%–33% |
| Default (if not set by 30 Nov) | 20% | 30% |

Zagreb 2025 rates (in effect): **23% / 33%** — used throughout this document.

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p. 305)
- Personal allowance: **EUR 600/month** (EUR 7,200/year) — non-taxable base for all resident taxpayers
- Dependent spouse or ascendants: EUR 300/month
- Dependent children: EUR 300/month (1st), EUR 420 (2nd), EUR 600 (3rd), EUR 840 (4th), EUR 1,140 (5th), EUR 1,500 (6th), EUR 1,920 (7th), EUR 2,400 (8th), EUR 2,940 (9th+)
- Disability: EUR 180/month (individual/dependent); EUR 600/month (severe disability)
- Donations in Croatia: up to 2% of prior-year income

**Age-based relief:**
- Under 25: 100% tax relief on lower-bracket employment income
- Ages 26–30: 50% tax relief on lower-bracket employment income

**Notes:**
- Employee social security contributions (20% of gross) are deductible from gross income before PIT is applied (EY p. 305)
- Business income can optionally be taxed under corporate tax rules (10% or 18%) instead of PIT (EY p. 304)
- Other income (directors' fees etc.) taxed at lower municipal rate prepayment (EY p. 305)

## Social Security

Employment income subject to: 16.5% employer (health insurance, uncapped) and 20% employee (pension, partially capped). (EY p. 307)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed (standard obrt) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension — Pillar I (MIO I) | 15% | — | 15% |
| Pension — Pillar II (MIO II) | 5% | — | 5% |
| Health insurance | — | 16.5% | 16.5% |
| **Total** | **20%** | **16.5%** | **36.5%** |

**Income ceiling (2025):**
- Monthly pension contribution cap: **EUR 11,958/month** (EUR 143,496/year)
- Health insurance: uncapped

**Paušalni obrt fixed contributions (2026):**
- Contribution base: **EUR 797.20/month**
- Pension (MIO I, 15%): €119.58/month
- Pension (MIO II, 5%): €39.86/month
- Health (16.5%): €131.54/month
- **Total: ≈ EUR 291/month (EUR 3,492/year)**
- Secondary-activity paušalac (alongside employment): MIO I 7.5%, MIO II 2.5%, health 7.5% — reduced contributions

**Standard self-employed minimum contribution base from 01/01/2026:** EUR 1,295.45/month (previously EUR 1,168.70 in 2025) → minimum monthly contributions ≈ EUR 474/month.

**Other income social contributions:** (EY p. 307)
- Self-employment as secondary activity: 10% (individual) + 7.5% healthcare (payer's expense)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Paušalni Obrt (Lump-Sum Sole Trader)

- **Who qualifies:** Sole traders (obrtnici) whose annual gross revenue did not exceed **EUR 40,000** in the previous calendar year. Not available to regulated professions, VAT-registered persons, or those with complex income structures.
- **Tax base:** Tax Administration issues a fixed (lump-sum) income tax decision. Standard computation: 85% notional expenses assumed → 15% of gross revenue is taxable income → 12% PIT rate → effective income tax ≈ **1.8% of gross revenue**. Actual amounts fixed per municipality and revenue bracket.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 12% PIT on lump-sum assessed income. Paid in **quarterly installments** (before 31 March, 30 June, 30 September, 31 December). No annual PIT return required for this income. (EY p. 304; solarstaff.com)
- **Social security:** Fixed monthly contributions at EUR 797.20 base (2026): **≈ EUR 291/month = EUR 3,492/year** (finacro.hr 2026)
- **VAT obligations:** Not VAT-registered (VAT registration ends paušal eligibility). Threshold coincides with paušal limit.
- **Key limits:**
  - Annual turnover ceiling: **EUR 40,000**
  - Opt-in: before 15 January of the tax year or at initial registration
  - Annual return PO-SD due **15 January** of following year
  - Maintain KPR (income/expense ledger)

### Option B — Standard Sole Trader (Obrt — Regular Tax)

- **Who qualifies:** All sole traders (obrts) above the EUR 40,000 paušal threshold, or those who prefer actual accounting.
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue minus documented business expenses. Personal allowance of EUR 7,200/year deductible.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT (Zagreb: 23%/33%) on net income after deductions.
- **Social security:** Full self-employed contributions on actual net income; minimum base EUR 1,295.45/month → minimum ≈ **EUR 474/month (EUR 5,688/year)** in 2026.
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory VAT registration above EUR 40,000 turnover.
- **Key limits:** Full bookkeeping; quarterly advance PIT prepayments.

### Option C — d.o.o. (Private Limited Company)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual; typically advantageous above EUR 60,000 income.
- **Tax base:** Corporate profit.
- **Rate / mechanism:** CIT 10% (revenue ≤ EUR 1M) or 18% (above EUR 1M) + 12% withholding on dividends.
- **Social security:** Owner takes minimum salary; minimum contributions apply.
- **Key limits:** Registered capital EUR 2,500; accountant required.

(EY p. 304)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Digital Nomad Temporary Stay

- **Eligibility:** Third-country nationals (non-EU/EEA) employed remotely by a foreign employer or their own foreign company. Company must not be registered in Croatia; no work for Croatian clients. (EY pp. 304, 309)
- **Benefit:** **Personal income tax is not payable** on salary of digital nomad status holders. (EY p. 304 — "Personal income tax is not payable on the salary generated by natural persons who have obtained the digital nomad status.")
- **Duration:** Temporary stay approval: **1 year**; can be renewed.
- **Application:** Croatian diplomatic mission/consular office abroad, or (if visa-free entry) local police department in Croatia.
- **Caveats:**
  - Applies to third-country nationals only; EU/EEA nationals use free movement rights (no special nomad status needed)
  - Must not work for Croatian employers or clients
  - Croatia is EU member — >183 days triggers tax residency; nomad PIT exemption continues under valid status
  - No social security exemption noted — contributions still apply if classified as employed

### Age-Based Tax Relief

- Under 25: 100% relief on lower-bracket PIT on employment income
- Ages 26–30: 50% relief on lower-bracket PIT on employment income
- Applied automatically at source; no application required

## 2026 Changes

- Croatia adopted the euro on **1 January 2023**; all taxes and contributions in EUR. (EY p. 303)
- **Paušalci contribution base from 01/01/2026:** EUR 797.20/month (up from EUR 624/month in 2024) → monthly contributions ≈ **EUR 291/month** (finacro.hr)
- **Minimum gross wage from 01/01/2026:** EUR 1,295.45 (standard employed/self-employed minimum base)
- No changes to PIT brackets or rates announced for 2026
- VAT threshold remains EUR 40,000

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, Zagreb rates (23% lower / 33% higher bracket).
> Employment: employee-side only; employer's 16.5% health not included.
> Paušalni obrt: income ≤ €40,000; income tax = 12% × 15% of gross; fixed social contributions EUR 3,492/year (2026).
> Standard obrt: minimum contribution base EUR 1,295.45/month = EUR 5,688/year; actual may be higher.

| Step | Calculation | Result |

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Paušalni obrt exact quarterly amounts by revenue bracket:** EY does not cover the paušalni regime; exact installment tables require porezna-uprava.gov.hr official decisions
- [ ] **Municipal coefficient for paušalni:** Some sources reference a coefficient applied to the lump-sum tax — may be the same municipal rate range; exact mechanism not fully confirmed
- [ ] **Standard obrt contribution basis above minimum:** At €60k and €100k, Tax Administration may set a higher monthly base — real social contributions could exceed the minimum shown
- [ ] **Digital nomad PIT exemption scope:** EY confirms exemption for salary income; whether it extends to self-employment invoicing is not confirmed
- [ ] **Age relief for self-employed:** Under-25 / 26-30 relief appears to apply to employment income only; applicability to obrt income not confirmed
- [ ] **Paušalni obrt income tax exact bracket amounts:** The 1.8% estimate is based on the standard 85% notional expense deduction and 12% rate; actual quarterly fixed amounts may differ slightly by municipality
