## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (IRPF — Imposto de Renda Pessoa Física) for residents taxed on worldwide income. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return (Declaração de Ajuste Anual) filed by last business day of May. Cash-basis system: income taxed when received. Married persons may file jointly if one spouse has no income (listed as dependant), otherwise separately. (EY p.176–184)

**Important 2026 update — effective exemption to BRL 5,000/month:**
From 1 January 2026, a monthly simplied discount (redutor mensal) of up to BRL 884.96 effectively zeroes IRPF for monthly taxable income up to BRL 5,000. For income between BRL 5,000.01 and BRL 7,350/month, partial and decreasing reduction applies. The underlying progressive table rates (7.5%–27.5%) remain unchanged; the exemption is achieved via the redutor mechanism. Annual equivalent: full exemption for annual income up to BRL 60,000; partial reduction for BRL 60,000.01–88,200. (Agência Brasil, gov.br; effective for 2026 tax year)

**IRPF computation method:**
Tax = (Taxable income × Rate) − Deductible amount
Example: BRL 4,000/month → (4,000 × 15%) − 394.16 = BRL 205.84/month

**Capital gains progressive rates** (EY p.180)

| Capital gain (BRL) | Rate (%) |
|---|---|
| 0 – 5,000,000 | 15.0 |
| 5,000,000 – 10,000,000 | 17.5 |
| 10,000,000 – 30,000,000 | 20.0 |
| Over 30,000,000 | 22.5 |

Special exemption: sale of only residence held ≥ 5 years if sale price ≤ BRL 440,000; or if proceeds reinvested in Brazilian residential property within 180 days. (EY p.180)

**Standard deductions / allowances (monthly IRPF):** (EY p.180)
- BRL 189.59/month per dependant (no limit on number of dependants)
- Social security (INSS) contributions: fully deductible from IRPF base
- Alimony/pensions per court decree: fully deductible
- Brazilian Private Pension Fund contributions (PGBL or Fapi): up to 12% of gross taxable income

**Annual return deductions (itemized — Declaração de Ajuste Anual):** (EY p.181)
- All the above monthly deductions apply annually
- Education expenses: up to BRL 3,561.50 per individual per year
- Medical/health expenses (doctors, dentists, psychologists, hospitals, lab tests): unlimited deduction (no cap)
- Medical treatment plans (health insurance premiums): unlimited deduction
- Alimony per court decree: fully deductible
- Dependant deduction: BRL 2,275.08/year per dependant (annual equivalent of monthly)
- Contributions to children's fund, elderly fund, culture, audiovisual, sports: up to 6%–7% of annual income tax due (not tax base)

OR: **Standard deduction:** 20% of taxable income, limited to BRL 16,754.34/year — elected at annual return in lieu of itemized deductions. (EY p.180)

**Notes:**
- FGTS (Severance Pay Indemnity Fund): employers deposit 8% of gross salary monthly; not taxed on employee at deposit; withdrawable tax-free on retirement/dismissal without just cause. (EY p.178)
- Investment income (domestic fixed income): withheld at source at rates varying 15%–22.5% based on investment term (EY p.178)
- Foreign investment income: flat 15% from 1 January 2026 (Provisional Measure 1,303/2025 pending conversion to law; previous rate 15% for most foreign investments) (EY p.178–179)
- Brazilian dividends paid to nonresidents: exempt from withholding tax (EY p.179)
- Rental income to nonresidents: 15% final withholding (EY p.179)
- Stock options: taxable as employment income (fringe benefit) at time of exercise at up to 27.5%; or capital gains if Supreme Court ruling criteria met (EY p.179)

## Social Security

**INSS — employee contributions (withheld at source):** Progressive scale; maximum monthly contribution BRL 951.62 for 2025. (EY p.182)

| Monthly income — Exceeding (BRL) | Not exceeding (BRL) | Rate (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 1,518.00 | 7.5 |
| 1,518.00 | 2,793.88 | 9.0 |
| 2,793.88 | 4,190.83 | 12.0 |
| 4,190.83 | 8,157.41 | 14.0 |

Note: INSS is progressive (applied to each tier, not the entire income at the top rate). Max employee INSS contribution: BRL 951.62/month (2025); updated annually. (EY p.182)

**Employer contributions:** (EY p.182–183)
- Standard rate: approximately 26.8%–28.8% of gross employee compensation (no ceiling)
- Ordinary social security: 20% of payroll
- "S" system contributions: ~5.8%
- Plus 1%–3% risk of activity additional
- FGTS: 8% of gross salary (paid to government-held account; not a tax but a mandatory deposit)
- Employer total (including FGTS): approximately 34.8%–36.8% of gross

**Self-employed INSS:** 20% of declared base salary (base salary set by government; max monthly contribution BRL 1,631.48 for 2025). Minimum base = national minimum wage. (EY p.183)

**Totalization agreements:** Brazil has social security agreements with Argentina, Belgium, Bolivia, Bulgaria, Canada, Cape Verde, Chile, Colombia, Czech Republic, Dominican Republic, Ecuador, El Salvador, France, Germany (informal), Greece, India, Italy, Japan, Korea (South), Luxembourg, Mozambique, Paraguay, Peru, Portugal, Quebec, Spain, Switzerland, United States, Uruguay, and others. (EY p.183)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — MEI (Microempreendedor Individual)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual entrepreneurs (sole proprietors) with annual gross revenue ≤ BRL 81,000/year (~€13,500 at 1 EUR ≈ BRL 6.0). Cannot have equity stake in other companies; limited to a defined list of permitted activities; maximum 1 employee. (Receita Federal; confirmed for 2026 — limit unchanged since 2018)
- **Tax base:** Fixed monthly DAS (Documento de Arrecadação do Simples) — not based on actual revenue.
- **Rate / mechanism — DAS monthly values 2026** (based on minimum wage BRL 1,621 for 2026):

  | Activity type | DAS components | Monthly DAS (BRL) |
  |---|---|---|
  | Commerce / Industry | INSS 5% (BRL 81.05) + ICMS | BRL 82.05 |
  | Services | INSS 5% (BRL 81.05) + ISS | BRL 86.05 |
  | Commerce + Services | INSS 5% (BRL 81.05) + ICMS + ISS | BRL 87.05 |

  INSS: 5% × BRL 1,621 (minimum wage) = BRL 81.05. ICMS: BRL 1.00. ISS: BRL 5.00.
  Annual DAS for services: ~BRL 1,032.60/year — extraordinarily low.

- **Social security:** INSS at 5% of minimum wage (included in DAS); this grants access to sick pay, maternity leave, retirement. Note: 5% rate provides proportional benefits (base pension = minimum wage only).
- **VAT/taxes:** MEI is exempt from federal taxes (IRPJ, PIS, Cofins, CSLL, IPI); pays only ICMS or ISS within DAS.
- **Key limits:** BRL 81,000/year (~€13,500); IT services may be permitted; MEI Caminhoneiro (truck driver) has higher limit BRL 251,600. Exceeding by up to 20% (≤ BRL 97,200): supplemental DAS + automatic downgrade to ME in January. Exceeding by > 20%: retroactive downgrade with penalties.

### Option B — Simples Nacional — Anexo III (Small Companies / IT/Software Services)

- **Who qualifies:** Microempresas (ME) and Empresas de Pequeno Porte (EPP) — typically via a single-person company (Empresa Individual de Responsabilidade Limitada, EIRELI, or SLU — Sociedade Limitada Unipessoal). Annual gross revenue ≤ BRL 4,800,000 (~€800k). IT software and technology services typically fall under Anexo III (if the company's payroll/pro-labore/INSS ≥ 28% of gross revenue — the "Fator R" test); otherwise Anexo V (higher rates). (LC 123/2006)
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue collected; Simples Nacional is a unified tax covering IRPJ + CSLL + PIS/Pasep + Cofins + IPI + CPP + ICMS + ISS in a single monthly DAS payment.
- **Rate / mechanism — Simples Nacional Anexo III table 2026** (structure unchanged from 2018 LC 155/2016; updated annually for inflation):

  | Gross revenue last 12 months (RBT12) — BRL | Nominal rate (%) | Deduction (BRL) | Effective rate at bracket floor (%) |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Up to 180,000 | 6.00 | 0 | 6.00 |
  | 180,000.01 – 360,000 | 11.20 | 9,360 | 8.00 (at 180k) |
  | 360,000.01 – 720,000 | 13.20 | 17,640 | 8.25 (at 360k) |
  | 720,000.01 – 1,800,000 | 16.00 | 35,640 | 14.03 (at 720k) |
  | 1,800,000.01 – 3,600,000 | 21.00 | 125,640 | 14.02 (at 1.8M) |
  | 3,600,000.01 – 4,800,000 | 33.00 | 648,000 | 16.50 (at 3.6M) |

  **Effective rate formula:** Alíquota Efetiva = [(RBT12 × Nominal Rate) − Deduction] / RBT12

  Example: RBT12 = BRL 360,000 → (360,000 × 11.2% − 9,360) / 360,000 = (40,320 − 9,360) / 360,000 = 8.6%

  **"Fator R" rule:** If payroll (pro-labore + INSS) over last 12 months ÷ gross revenue ≥ 28%, use Anexo III (starts at 6%). If < 28%, use Anexo V (starts at 15.5%). IT/software companies with reasonable pro-labore can usually qualify for Anexo III.

- **Social security for owner (pro-labore):** Company must pay owner a pro-labore (minimum = national minimum wage BRL 1,412 in 2025/BRL 1,621 in 2026); INSS on pro-labore at 11% (company contribution) + employee portion per INSS table. Simples Nacional companies pay a CPP (Contribuição Patronal Previdenciária) embedded in the Simples DAS rather than the standard 20% employer INSS.
- **VAT within Simples:** ISS (services tax) and PIS/Cofins embedded in single DAS; no separate invoicing of these taxes. ISS rate varies by municipality (2%–5%).
- **Key limits:** BRL 4.8M annual gross revenue; Fator R test determines Anexo III vs V.

### Option C — Lucro Presumido (Assumed Profit Regime — Standard Company)

- **Who qualifies:** Companies with annual gross revenue ≤ BRL 78M; optional (versus Lucro Real).
- **Tax base:** Assumed profit = 32% of gross revenue for services (presumed margin). IRPJ (15%) + CSLL (9%) on assumed profit = effective ≈ 13.33% of gross revenue for services + PIS/Cofins (3.65% cumulative on gross) + ISS (2%–5%) = total ~18–19% of gross. Better than Simples Nacional at higher revenues.
- **Note:** Discussed for completeness; Simples Nacional is typically more advantageous for IT professionals below BRL 4.8M.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Three-Year Foreign Income Tax Deferral (under review)

- Brazil previously had a regime where new tax residents from abroad were exempt from tax on foreign-sourced income for the first 5 years. This regime was eliminated by reform. Resident status attaches immediately upon arrival with a labor or investor visa; worldwide income taxed from day 1 of tax residency.

### 183-Day Nonresident Rule

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals holding temporary visas who do not work under local labor contract and stay fewer than 183 days (consecutive or non-consecutive) in any 12-month period.
- **Benefit:** Taxed as nonresident on Brazilian-source income only (not worldwide).
- **Caveats:** Foreign nationals with labor contracts, statutory representative status, or investor visas become residents immediately upon arrival regardless of days. (EY p.177)

### Digital Nomad Visa

- Published via Resolution 9 September 2021; allows foreign remote workers to reside in Brazil temporarily without a Brazilian employment relationship. Monthly income requirement ≥ USD 1,500 from foreign employer, or USD 18,000 in bank funds. Visa holder works for foreign employer; income may be foreign-source and therefore subject to nonresident treatment if < 183 days. (EY p.189)

## 2026 Changes

- **IRPF exemption expansion (effective 1 January 2026):** Monthly redutor zeroes IRPF for taxable income up to BRL 5,000/month; partial reduction for BRL 5,000.01–7,350/month. Annual exemption threshold: BRL 60,000/year; partial reduction to BRL 88,200. Mechanism: monthly simplified discount (redutor) up to BRL 884.96. Progressive table rates (7.5%–27.5%) and brackets unchanged. (gov.br, Agência Brasil, Law 15,191/2025 as extended)
- **IRPF table effective from May 2025 (continued in 2026):** Zero bracket raised to BRL 2,428.80/month from May 2025; this replaced the BRL 2,259.20 threshold from February 2024. (EY p.181)
- **Investment income foreign assets (Provisional Measure 1,303/2025):** If converted to law, flat 17.5% on foreign investment income from 1 January 2026 (up from 15%). Status: pending 120-day conversion window. If not converted, 15% rate reinstated. (EY p.179)
- **MEI 2026:** DAS values updated to reflect new minimum wage BRL 1,621 (2026): services DAS = BRL 86.05/month; commerce = BRL 82.05/month. Revenue ceiling BRL 81,000 unchanged.
- **Simples Nacional ceiling:** BRL 4,800,000 annual gross revenue limit unchanged.
- **Greater Brazil Plan (Plano Brasil Maior):** Extended to end of 2024 for 17 sectors by Law 14,973/2024; transition period regulated by Normative Instruction 2,242/2024. Certain companies can substitute the 20% employer INSS with 1%–2.5% on gross revenue. (EY p.183)

**Key insight — Simples Nacional vs. Employment:** The SLU/Simples Nacional III structure provides dramatically lower effective rates (7%–11%) vs. employment (26%–27%) across all income levels. The critical mechanism is: (1) Simples tax replaces IRPJ/CSLL/PIS/Cofins/ISS at low rates (6%–10% effective); (2) the owner takes minimum pro-labore (BRL 1,621/month), paying minimal INSS and zero IRPF on that portion; (3) the remainder is distributed as dividends which are currently **tax-free** under Brazilian law (dividends from companies taxed under Simples/Lucro Presumido are exempt from personal IRPF). This dividend exemption is a major feature of the Brazilian system and accounts for the large gap.

**Important caveat:** A dividend taxation reform has been under discussion in Brazil for years. If enacted, it could substantially increase the effective rate for SLU/company owners. As of May 2026, no dividend tax reform has been enacted.

**MEI context:** At the MEI ceiling (~€13,500), the DAS annual cost of ~BRL 1,033 represents roughly 1.3% of gross revenue — the lowest formal self-employment tax burden in this dataset by a very large margin. The limitation is the BRL 81,000 ceiling.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/BRL = 6.0 (approximate 2025 average; BRL has been volatile against EUR)
- INSS employee: progressive 7.5%–14% applied to each tier; capped at BRL 951.62/month for 2025; 2026 cap updated with salary adjustments (approximately BRL 986–1,000/month estimated)
- 2026 IRPF redutor (BRL 884.96/month): does not apply at income levels modeled (all above BRL 7,350/month threshold)
- Simples Nacional Anexo III: effective rate formula applied at bracket midpoints
- SLU pro-labore: minimum wage (BRL 1,621/month for 2026) — owner declares minimum to minimize SS burden; legal but standard practice
- Fator R assumed ≥ 28%: Simples Anexo III applies (not Anexo V); confirmed feasible at minimum pro-labore for lower revenue brackets
- Dividend income from SLU not taxed (current law); distributions above pro-labore assumed to be dividends
- No itemized deductions claimed (simplified baseline)
- FGTS (8% employer deposit) not shown — employer cost, not a direct employee tax burden

## Known Gaps

- [ ] 2026 INSS cap (maximum employee contribution) not confirmed precisely; EY cites BRL 951.62 for 2025; 2026 cap will be higher following salary adjustment — estimated ~BRL 986/month
- [ ] Simples Nacional Anexo III effective rates use mid-range RBT12 approximation; actual monthly DAS uses rolling 12-month revenue and applies effective rate formula month-by-month
- [ ] Fator R rule: minimum pro-labore strategy to qualify for Anexo III vs. Anexo V requires careful monthly monitoring; if payroll/revenue drops below 28%, Anexo V (starts at 15.5%) applies retroactively for the month
- [ ] Dividend tax reform: repeatedly discussed in Brazil; not yet enacted as of May 2026; if passed, SLU effective rates would increase significantly (dividends currently exempt)
- [ ] Provisional Measure 1,303/2025 (foreign investment income at 17.5%): pending conversion to law within 120-day window; if not converted, 15% reinstated
- [ ] Annual IRPF return deductions (medical expenses, education, dependants) not modeled; for high-income employment, itemized deductions can reduce IRPF significantly — effective rate could be 3–5pp lower for families with significant health/education expenditure
