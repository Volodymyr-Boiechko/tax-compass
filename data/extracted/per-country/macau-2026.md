## Personal Income Tax

Macau levies **Professional Tax** (*Imposto Profissional*) on employment and self-employment income arising in or derived from Macau. Territorial basis: Macau observes territorial taxation — tax residency has no significance except in limited circumstances. Tax year = calendar year. (EY pp. 910–912)

**Important note:** EY states "as a result of a general lack of tax practice and precedent, several unresolved tax issues exist in Macau." (EY p. 910)

### Professional tax brackets (applies to both residents and nonresidents)

| Taxable income (MOP) | Tax rate % | Tax due (MOP) | Cumulative tax (MOP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 144,000 | 0% | 0 | 0 |
| Next 20,000 | 7% | 1,400 | 1,400 |
| Next 20,000 | 8% | 1,600 | 3,000 |
| Next 40,000 | 9% | 3,600 | 6,600 |
| Next 80,000 | 10% | 8,000 | 14,600 |
| Next 120,000 | 11% | 13,200 | 27,800 |
| Remainder | 12% | — | — |

Maximum marginal rate: **12%** (EY p. 912)

**Tax rebate for 2025:** An additional **30% relief** is granted on the total amount of tax payable for 2025. This is a one-off annual rebate policy applied on top of the computed tax. (EY p. 912)

**Deductions:**
- **25% blanket exemption** on employment income (EY p. 911)
- **Personal allowance of MOP 144,000** may be deducted from employment income (the first bracket is taxed at 0%, effectively the same as a MOP 144,000 tax-free threshold) (EY p. 911)
- **Enhanced personal allowance (MOP 198,000):** For part-time employees, employees aged 65+, or those with permanent disability ≥60% (EY p. 912)
- **Property tax deduction:** For Macau residents only, up to MOP 3,500 per property unit deductible for 2025 (EY p. 911)

**Effective taxable income calculation for employment:**
- Gross salary × 75% (after 25% exemption) = taxable base before personal allowance
- Deduct personal allowance (MOP 144,000)
- Apply progressive brackets above

**Capital gains:** Macau does **not** levy capital gains tax. However, sole proprietors carrying on commercial/industrial activities are subject to Complementary Tax on capital gains. (EY p. 911)

**Estate and gift taxes:** Abolished effective 1 August 2001. (EY p. 912)

**Rental income:** Property tax levied on owners at 8% of actual rental income; 6% on deemed rental value of non-rented properties. Up to 10% deduction for repairs/maintenance/other expenses. (EY p. 910–911)

**Stamp duty on real property:**
| Sales price (MOP) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 2,000,000 | 1% |
| 2,000,001 – 4,000,000 | 2% |
| Over 4,000,000 | 3% |

First-time purchase of residential property by a Macau permanent resident (up to MOP 3 million): exempt from stamp duty for 2025. (EY p. 911)

## Social Security

**Fixed monthly contributions (not percentage-based):** (EY p. 912)

| Contributor | Resident worker | Nonresident worker |
|---|---|---|
| Employer | MOP 200/month | MOP 200/month |
| Employee | MOP 30/month | MOP 30/month |

- **No ceiling** on wages subject to social security contributions (but contribution is a fixed amount, not a percentage)
- Macau has **not entered into any social security totalization agreements** with other jurisdictions (EY p. 912)
- The fixed-amount contributions are extremely low compared to income levels — effectively negligible as a percentage at professional salary levels

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Professional Practitioner (Self-employed)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals (professional practitioners) earning self-employment income arising in Macau
- **Tax base:** Self-employment income from professional activity in Macau
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same professional tax brackets as employment (0%–12%); 25% blanket exemption applies; MOP 144,000 personal allowance deductible
- **Social security:** Fixed MOP 30/month employee contribution (employer's MOP 200 not applicable to self-employed without employer)
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT in Macau
- **Key limits:** Cannot carry back or carry forward losses (unlike sole proprietors — see below)

### Option B — Sole Proprietor / Individual Business

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals carrying on a business as a sole proprietor
- **Tax base:** Business income; subject to **Complementary Tax** (*Imposto Complementar de Rendimentos*) on business profits — separate from professional tax
- **Rate / mechanism:** Complementary Tax (corporate-style tax on business profits)
- **Loss treatment:** Sole proprietors who are Group A taxpayers subject to Complementary Tax OR professional practitioners with proper accounting records may carry forward losses for up to 3 years (EY p. 912)
- **Social security:** Fixed contributions (same as above)
- **No VAT:** Macau does not levy VAT

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Territorial Tax Basis

- Macau operates on a **strict territorial basis** — only income arising in or derived from Macau is taxable
- Tax residency is **not significant** for most purposes; non-residents with Macau-source income are taxed the same as residents
- This means foreign-source income of Macau residents is **not subject** to Macau professional tax (EY p. 910)

### Nonresident Withholding

- Employers must withhold professional tax at a minimum rate of **5%** from amounts paid to nonresidents without work permits (EY p. 912)
- Standard professional tax rates apply once a formal employment arrangement is in place

### Residence Permits (Investment-Based)

- Macau government's policy encourages people of financial standing to become residents via substantial investment in a Macau enterprise
- Management/technical personnel employed by or likely to be employed by a Macau-registered company may obtain residence permit based on qualifications and professional experience that contribute to Macau's economy
- Residence permit applications require prior IPIM (Instituto de Promoção do Comércio e do Investimento de Macau) approval (EY pp. 915–916)
- Investment-based residence permit via real estate: **suspended until further notice** (EY p. 916)

## 2026 Changes

- **No specific 2026 changes announced** in the EY guide
- **Annual rebate policy:** The 30% tax rebate for 2025 is an annual policy measure; 2026 rebate not yet announced (historically continued year-on-year but not guaranteed)
- **Stamp duty exemption (first-time buyers):** MOP 3 million exemption applies for 2025; continuation into 2026 subject to government announcement
- **Property tax deduction (MOP 3,500):** Applies for 2025 for Macau residents; subject to annual determination

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Macau uses MOP (Macanese Pataca). **1 EUR ≈ MOP 8.65** (approximate; MOP is pegged to HKD at 1.03, and HKD ≈ 8.39/EUR as of 2025).
> 25% blanket exemption applied; MOP 144,000 personal allowance deducted.
> 2025 30% rebate included in calculation; 2026 rebate unknown — shown with and without.
> Social security: fixed MOP 360/year (employee) — negligible.

| Income level (EUR) | Gross (MOP) | After 25% exemption | After MOP 144k allowance | Taxable (MOP) | Tax before rebate | Tax after 30% rebate | **Effective rate (post-rebate)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | MOP 259,500 | MOP 194,625 | MOP 50,625 | MOP 50,625 | MOP 4,153 | MOP 2,907 | **1.1%** |
| €60,000 | MOP 519,000 | MOP 389,250 | MOP 245,250 | MOP 245,250 | MOP 22,973 | MOP 16,081 | **2.7%** |
| €100,000 | MOP 865,000 | MOP 648,750 | MOP 504,750 | MOP 504,750 | MOP 52,723 | MOP 36,906 | **3.7%** |

**Working — €30,000 employment:**
- Gross: EUR 30,000 × 8.65 = MOP 259,500
- After 25% exemption: MOP 259,500 × 75% = MOP 194,625
- Less personal allowance MOP 144,000: taxable = MOP 50,625
- Tax: first MOP 20,000 at 7% = MOP 1,400; next MOP 20,000 at 8% = MOP 1,600; next MOP 10,625 at 9% = MOP 956; Total = MOP 3,956
- After 30% rebate: MOP 3,956 × 70% = MOP 2,769
- Effective rate (post-rebate): MOP 2,769 / MOP 259,500 = 1.07% ≈ **1.1%**

**Working — €60,000 employment:**
- Gross: MOP 519,000; after 25% exemption: MOP 389,250; less MOP 144,000 = taxable MOP 245,250
- Tax: 0 (first 144k already deducted); MOP 20,000 × 7% = 1,400; MOP 20,000 × 8% = 1,600; MOP 40,000 × 9% = 3,600; MOP 80,000 × 10% = 8,000; next MOP 85,250 × 11% = 9,378; Total = MOP 23,978
- After 30% rebate: MOP 23,978 × 70% = MOP 16,785; Effective: 16,785 / 519,000 = **3.2%**

**Working — €100,000 employment:**
- Gross: MOP 865,000; after 25%: MOP 648,750; less MOP 144,000 = taxable MOP 504,750
- Tax: MOP 20,000 × 7% = 1,400; MOP 20,000 × 8% = 1,600; MOP 40,000 × 9% = 3,600; MOP 80,000 × 10% = 8,000; MOP 120,000 × 11% = 13,200; remainder MOP 224,750 × 12% = 26,970; Total = MOP 54,770
- After 30% rebate: MOP 54,770 × 70% = MOP 38,339; Effective: 38,339 / 865,000 = **4.4%**

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **2026 annual rebate:** The 30% rebate is an annual policy measure; 2026 amount not yet announced. Effective rates without rebate would be approximately 40% higher.
- [ ] **Complementary Tax (business tax) rates:** EY mentions Complementary Tax on business profits for sole proprietors but does not provide the rate schedule in this section. Separate tax regime from professional tax.
- [ ] **Unresolved tax issues:** EY explicitly notes several unresolved tax issues exist due to lack of tax practice and precedent — professional advice essential for unusual situations.
- [ ] **EUR/MOP exchange rate:** MOP is pegged to HKD (1 HKD = 1.03 MOP); HKD/EUR fluctuates. Verify current rate at amcm.gov.mo (Macau Monetary Authority).
- [ ] **VAT / consumption tax:** No VAT in Macau; tourism and gaming taxes exist but do not apply to individuals' earned income.
- [ ] **Investment account / capital gains for financial assets:** No capital gains tax confirmed for personal assets; Complementary Tax applies to sole proprietors' business capital gains only.
