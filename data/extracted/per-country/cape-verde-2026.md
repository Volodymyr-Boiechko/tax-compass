## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (IRPS — Imposto sobre o Rendimento das Pessoas Singulares) for residents taxed on worldwide income. Nonresidents taxed on Cape Verde-source income only. Tax year = calendar year. Filing deadline: end of March (employment/pension income); end of May (other income). Form MOD 112 used; must be submitted electronically. (EY p.245, p.251)

**Residency criteria:** Stay >183 days in any 12-month period; maintain a dwelling on 31 December; exercise public functions abroad for Cape Verde; or crew member on ship/aircraft of a Cape Verde entity resident in past five years. (EY p.245)

**Progressive PIT brackets — 2025** (EY p.248)

| Taxable income (CVE) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 960,000 | 16.5% |
| 960,001 – 1,800,000 | 23.1% |
| Above 1,800,000 | 27.5% |

**Tax-free threshold:** CVE 220,000 is exempt from tax — subtracted from taxable amount before applying rates. Effective result: 16.5% applies to income between CVE 220,000 and CVE 960,000. (EY p.248)

**Other income types:**
- Rental income: 10% withholding at source (final, or creditable against annual tax at taxpayer's option). Maintenance/repair deductible up to 30% of gross rental. (EY p.246)
- Interest on public company bonds/state bonds, dividends: 10% withholding (final for nonresidents). (EY p.246)
- Other gross investment income: 20% final withholding. (EY p.246)
- Gambling, lotteries, prizes: flat 20%. (EY p.246)
- Capital gains on immovable property, IP, shareholdings: flat 1%. (EY p.246)
- Business/professional income — simplified regime: 4% on turnover. (EY p.246)
- Business/professional income — organized bookkeeping: net profit at standard IRPS rates. (EY p.246)

**Standard deductions / allowances (credits against tax liability, 2025):** (EY p.249)
- CVE 5,000 per dependent not subject to IRPS (cap CVE 25,000/year)
- 10% of documented medical expenses (cap CVE 25,000/year)
- 10% of alimony payments (cap CVE 25,000/year)
- 10% of rental expenses for taxpayer's residence (cap CVE 12,500/year)
- 10% of interest on certain housing loans (cap CVE 12,500/year)
- 10% of education expenses for household members up to 24 years old (cap CVE 12,500/year)
- Advance payments and taxes withheld at source

**Notes:**
- Directors' fees taxed same as employment income. (EY p.246)
- Inheritance and gift taxes eliminated effective April 1999. (EY p.249)
- Property tax (from 1 January 2026): 0.1% flat on taxable value of properties in Cape Verde. (EY p.249)
- Real estate transfer tax (from 1 January 2026): 1% (or 3% for preferential tax regime parties) on taxable value. (EY p.249)
- Touristic contribution: CVE 220/night per occupant, max 10 nights; children under 16 exempt. (EY p.250)

## Social Security

Contributions payable on salaries, wages, bonuses, subsidies and other regular income (excluding certain allowances and transport subsidies). Employer rate effective from 26 July 2017. (EY p.250)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security | 8.5% | 16% | 11% or 19.5% |
| **Total** | **8.5%** | **16%** | **11% or 19.5%** |

**Self-employed rates:** (EY p.250)
- 11% for the restricted social security regime
- 19.5% for the extended social security regime
- Contribution base is preselected by the individual.

**Income ceiling:** Not specified in EY source.

**Notes:**
- Employer must deduct employee's contribution and pay total by 15th of following month.
- Employment incentive: hiring workers up to 35 years old for minimum 1-year term exempts employer from mandatory social security contributions (conditions apply). (EY p.250)
- Totalization agreements with: Brazil, France, Italy, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Portugal, Spain, Sweden (new Brazil agreement under negotiation). (EY p.250)
- Foreigners working up to two years in Cape Verde who contribute to a compulsory home-country social security regime may not be subject to Cape Verdean social security. (EY p.250)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Simplified Regime (Micro/Small Companies)
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals meeting micro/small company criteria: up to 10 employees and/or gross turnover up to CVE 10 million. Taxpayer must choose this regime.
- **Tax base:** Gross turnover — no expense deductions.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Special flat rate of 4% on gross turnover.
- **Social security:** 11% (restricted) or 19.5% (extended) on self-selected base.
- **VAT obligations:** Standard rules apply (rates/threshold not specified in EY source).
- **Key limits:** Turnover ceiling CVE 10 million; must meet micro/small company legal requirements.

### Option B — Organized Bookkeeping Regime
- **Who qualifies:** Taxpayers who do not meet micro/small company requirements, or who voluntarily opt for this regime.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (revenue minus allowable activity-related expenses). Deductibility limitations: (EY p.248)
  - Travel and accommodation: max 10% of total revenue
  - Home office (if professional's house partly used): rent, electricity, water, telephone, depreciation deductible up to 50% of total expenses incurred
  - Per diem, kilometre allowances, and other remuneration allowances: NOT deductible
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive IRPS rates (16.5% / 23.1% / 27.5%).
- **Social security:** 11% (restricted) or 19.5% (extended) on self-selected base.
- **VAT obligations:** Standard rules apply.
- **Key limits:** Must maintain proper books. Tax credit available for internship costs. (EY p.246)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Non-Habitual Resident (NHR) Regime
- **Eligibility:** Individuals who become tax residents of Cape Verde and have not been taxed as such in any of the previous five years. Must register as NHR with tax authorities.
- **Benefit:**
  - Employment, pension, and business/professional income from high-value-added activities of a scientific, artistic or technical nature: flat 10% on net income.
  - Foreign-source income (employment, certain business/professional, IP rights, investment income, rental income, capital gains) is exempt in Cape Verde if taxed in: (a) a tax treaty country per treaty terms, or (b) a non-tax-treaty OECD-model country that is not a tax haven and the income cannot be deemed sourced in Cape Verde.
  - Foreign-source pension income exempt if taxed in a treaty country or not deemed sourced in Cape Verde.
  - Taxpayer may opt for the credit method instead of the exemption method.
- **Duration:** Up to 10 years.
- **Caveats:** Exemption method income still considered when determining rate on remaining taxable income; credit method available as alternative. (EY p.247)

### Remote Work Incentive
- **Eligibility:** Nonresident employees and self-employed professionals performing activities remotely for individuals/entities domiciled or headquartered outside Cape Verde.
- **Benefit:** Income tax exemption for one year; customs duties exemption for imported materials needed for the activity.
- **Duration:** One year. If the individual remains >1 year, they receive the NHR regime incentives.
- **Caveats:** Must demonstrate employment relationship or provision-of-services agreement. (EY p.247)

## 2026 Changes

- **Property tax introduced from 1 January 2026:** Annual levy at 0.1% flat rate on taxable value of rural, urban and mixed-use properties in Cape Verde; payable by property owners/users regardless of residence. (EY p.249)
- **Real estate transfer tax from 1 January 2026:** 1% (or 3% for those under a preferential tax regime) on taxable value of immovable properties transferred; applies also to surface rights and other acts with similar economic results. (EY p.249)
- **No announced PIT rate or bracket changes for 2026** as of EY content date (October 2025).

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (CVE 220,000 tax-free threshold applied; personal credits not modeled).
> EUR/CVE rate: 1 EUR = CVE 110.265 (official fixed peg since 1999).
> "Employment" = gross salary; employee SS 8.5% not deductible from PIT base (assumed; organized bookkeeping deductibility rules differ).
> "B2B Simplified" = gross revenue; 4% flat tax; SS at 11% restricted regime on CVE 1,000,000 estimated base.
> Note: EY source does not specify whether employee SS contributions are deductible from employment PIT base; conservative assumption (not deductible) used below.

**EUR to CVE conversions:**
- €30,000 = CVE 3,307,950
- €60,000 = CVE 6,615,900
- €100,000 = CVE 11,026,500

### Working: CVE 3,307,950 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (8.5%) | 8.5% × 3,307,950 | CVE 281,176 |
| PIT base (after CVE 220k exempt) | 3,307,950 − 220,000 | CVE 3,087,950 |
| PIT band 1: CVE 220k–960k at 16.5% | 16.5% × 740,000 | CVE 122,100 |
| PIT band 2: CVE 960k–1,800k at 23.1% | 23.1% × 840,000 | CVE 194,040 |
| PIT band 3: above CVE 1,800k at 27.5% | 27.5% × (3,087,950 − 1,800,000) | CVE 354,136 |
| Total PIT | 122,100 + 194,040 + 354,136 | CVE 670,276 |
| **Total burden** | **281,176 + 670,276** | **CVE 951,452** |
| **Effective rate** | **951,452 / 3,307,950** | **~28.8%** |

### Working: CVE 3,307,950 (≈ €30,000) B2B Simplified 4%

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Self-employed SS (11%, ~CVE 1M base est.) | 11% × 1,000,000 | CVE 110,000 |
| Simplified tax (4% on revenue) | 4% × 3,307,950 | CVE 132,318 |
| **Total burden** | **110,000 + 132,318** | **CVE 242,318** |
| **Effective rate** | **242,318 / 3,307,950** | **~7.3%** |

- B2B Simplified is dramatically advantageous due to 4% flat rate vs progressive employment rates
