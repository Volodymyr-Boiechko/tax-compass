## CURRENCY NOTE

> Cape Verde Escudo (CVE) is **pegged to the EUR** at a fixed rate: **1 EUR = CVE 110.265** (fixed since 1999, guaranteed by Portugal and the EU). This makes EUR conversions reliable and stable.
>
> - €30,000 = CVE 3,307,950
> - €60,000 = CVE 6,615,900
> - €100,000 = CVE 11,026,500

## Personal Income Tax (IRPS — Imposto sobre o Rendimento das Pessoas Singulares)

Progressive system. Residents taxed on worldwide income. Nonresidents taxed on Cape Verde-source income. Tax year = calendar year. Employment income IRPS filed by end of March; other income by end of May. (EY pp.245–248)

**2025 IRPS brackets (progressive rates):**

| Taxable income — Exceeding (CVE) | Not exceeding (CVE) | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 960,000 | 16.5% |
| 960,000 | 1,800,000 | 23.1% |
| 1,800,000 | — | 27.5% |

**Non-taxable minimum:** CVE 220,000 is exempt — effectively the 16.5% rate applies to income between CVE 220,000 and CVE 960,000.

**Key tax credits (claimable against IRPS liability):** (EY p.249)

| Credit | Amount |
|---|---|
| Each dependent not subject to IRPS (incl. disabled, ascendants with low income) | CVE 5,000 per person (annual cap CVE 25,000) |
| Documented medical expenses (taxpayer or household) | 10% (annual cap CVE 25,000) |
| Alimony payments | 10% (annual cap CVE 25,000) |
| Rental expenses (taxpayer's residence) | 10% (annual cap CVE 12,500) |
| Interest on housing loans | 10% (annual cap CVE 12,500) |
| Education expenses (household, to age 24) | 10% (annual cap CVE 12,500) |
| Advance IRPS payments / withheld tax | Credited in full |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Non-taxable minimum: CVE 220,000/year exempt from tax
- Organised bookkeeping regime: deduction of actual business/professional expenses
- Simplified regime: 4% flat tax on revenue (no expense deductions for individuals; see Self-Employment)

**Notes:**
- Employment and pension income: progressive IRPS rates apply; pensions up to CVE 960,000 exempt
- Termination payments: some exemptions available (EY p.245)
- Capital gains from disposal of immovable property, IP, or shareholdings: flat 1% rate (EY p.246)
- Gambling, lotteries, prizes: flat 20% rate (EY p.246)
- Rental income: 10% withholding (final or on-account); maintenance costs deductible up to 30% of gross rental income (EY p.246)
- Dividends from Cape Verde resident/nonresident companies (if not exempt from IRPC): 0% or 10% withholding depending on exempt status (EY p.249)
- General investment income: 20% final withholding (EY p.248)
- Business/professional income withholding (residents and nonresidents): 15% (EY p.248)

## Social Security

Payable on salaries, wages, bonuses, subsidies and other regular income (excluding some allowances and transport subsidies). (EY p.250)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (INPS) | 8.5% | 16% | 11% or 19.5% (a) |
| **Total** | **8.5%** | **16%** | **11% / 19.5%** |

(a) Self-employed choose their contribution base. Two regimes:
- Restricted social security regime: 11% of chosen base
- Extended social security regime: 19.5% of chosen base

**Income ceiling:** Not specified in EY source (contributions payable on full remuneration).

**Notes:**
- Employer must deduct employee contributions and pay total (employer + employee) by 15th of following month
- Employment incentive: companies hiring young employees up to age 35 for a first employment of at least 1 year are exempt from mandatory employer SS contributions (if certain conditions met)
- Totalization agreements: Brazil*, France, Italy, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Portugal, Spain, Sweden (*new agreement under negotiation). Cape Verde also signed a Portuguese-language countries multilateral agreement (Angola, Brazil, Equatorial Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Mozambique, Portugal, São Tomé and Príncipe, Timor-Leste — not yet in force). (EY p.250)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Simplified Regime for Micro and Small Companies

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with gross turnover up to CVE 10 million AND up to 10 employees, as defined by micro/small company legislation.
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue (no deductions).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat **4%** of turnover; paid as IRPS final tax.
- **Social security:** 11% or 19.5% on chosen contribution base (self-selected).
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IVA (Cape Verde VAT); check threshold.
- **Key limits:** Only available to qualifying micro/small entities; turnover cap CVE 10 million (≈ €90,700).

### Option B — Organised Bookkeeping Regime

- **Who qualifies:** Professionals and individuals with business income who do not qualify for (or do not elect) the simplified regime.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses), subject to IRPS rules.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard IRPS progressive rates (16.5% / 23.1% / 27.5%) on net profit.
- **Deductible expenses:** Travel and accommodation up to 10% of total revenue; home office: rent, electricity, water, telephone, depreciation up to 50% of expenses incurred; per diem, km allowances and remuneration allowances are not deductible. (EY p.248)
- **Internships:** Taxpayers under organised bookkeeping may benefit from a tax credit related to internships.
- **Social security:** 11% or 19.5% on chosen contribution base.
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IVA.
- **Key limits:** Full expense documentation required; no revenue ceiling.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Non-Habitual Residents (NHR) Regime
- **Eligibility:** Individuals who become tax residents of Cape Verde and have not been taxed as Cape Verde residents in any of the previous five years. Must register as NHR with tax authorities.
- **Benefit:**
  - Employment income, pensions, and business/professional income from **high value-added activities** (scientific, artistic, technical): taxed at flat **10%** on net income (instead of progressive rates up to 27.5%).
  - Foreign-source income: exempt if sourced from a tax treaty country (under treaty terms) or from a non-tax-haven country per OECD Model (for certain income types such as business income, IP rights, investment income, rental, and capital gains). Employment income: may be exempt or taxable at 10%. Foreign pensions: exempt if taxed in source country or not deemed sourced in Cape Verde.
- **Duration:** Up to **10 years**.
- **Application deadline:** Registration required with tax authorities.
- **Caveats:** The exemption method uses exempt income to determine rate on remaining income (except 10% flat rate categories). Credit method may be elected alternatively. (EY pp.247–248)

### Remote Worker Income Tax Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Nonresident employees and self-employed professionals who perform professional activities remotely for individuals/entities domiciled or headquartered outside Cape Verde.
- **Benefit:** Income tax exemption for **1 year**; also customs duty exemption on import of materials needed for the activity.
- **Escalation:** If individual remains more than 1 year, they receive the NHR regime incentives.
- **Caveats:** Must demonstrate employment relationship or provision of services for foreign entities/individuals. (EY p.247)

## 2026 Changes

- **Property tax (as of 1 January 2026):** Annual property tax levied on taxable value of rural, urban and mixed-use properties at flat **0.1%**; payable by property owners/users. Some exemptions. (EY p.249)
- **Real estate transfer tax (as of 1 January 2026):** Levied on onerous transfers of property rights over immovable properties at **1%** (or 3% if transferor/acquirer benefits from a preferential tax regime). Some exemptions. (EY p.249)
- **NHR regime:** Continues to attract high-value remote workers; no changes announced.
- **IRPS brackets for 2025:** Confirmed as shown above; 2026 brackets to be set by budget law (verify with Direcção Geral das Contribuições e Impostos — DGCI).
- **Social security:** Employer 16%, employee 8.5% — no changes announced.

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **IRPS 2026 brackets:** EY confirms 2025 rates; 2026 rates set by annual budget law — verify with DGCI (dgci.gov.cv).
- [ ] **SS income ceiling:** EY does not specify a ceiling for SS contributions — may apply on full remuneration; verify.
- [ ] **SS deductibility from IRPS:** Whether employee SS is deductible from taxable income not confirmed in EY source. If deductible, employment effective rates above would be lower.
- [ ] **Simplified regime (4%) interaction with SS:** The 4% flat tax applies to revenue for qualifying micro/small entities; no expense deduction available — this regime was not used in effective rate calculations as it applies to revenue, not income.
- [ ] **NHR high-value activity list:** EY does not enumerate which professions qualify as "high value-added scientific, artistic or technical activities." Verify with DGCI.
- [ ] **Business income withholding (15%):** Whether this is a final tax or advance payment for self-employed on the organised bookkeeping regime needs clarification.
- [ ] **Property tax 2026:** New 0.1% annual property tax applies from 1 January 2026; not modeled above.
- [ ] **No digital nomad visa confirmed:** The remote worker exemption (1-year income tax exemption) serves a similar purpose but is not a formal "digital nomad visa" in the legal sense.
