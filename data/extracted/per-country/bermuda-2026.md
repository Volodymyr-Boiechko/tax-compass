## Personal Income Tax

**Bermuda does not tax the income or capital gains of resident or nonresident individuals.** (EY p.146)

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employment income | **0%** |
| Business / self-employment income | **0%** |
| Capital gains | **0%** |
| Dividends | **0%** |
| Interest | **0%** |

---

## Payroll Tax

Bermuda imposes a **payroll tax on employers** based on the total value of cash and benefits paid to employees. This is primarily an employer cost, not an employee income tax, though employers have the option to deduct the employee portion from employee remuneration. Payroll tax year: 1 April to 31 March. (EY p.146)

From 1 April 2023: marginal rate up to **19.75%** on remuneration up to BMD 1 million annually. Cap applies on gross earnings over BMD 1 million.

**Employer portion** (based on gross annual remuneration per employee):

| Gross annual remuneration (BMD) | Employer payroll tax rate |
|---|---|
| Less than 200,000 | **1%** |
| 200,000 – 350,000 | **2.5%** |
| 350,001 – 500,000 | **5.25%** |
| 500,001 – 1,000,000 | **7.5%** |
| Greater than 1,000,000 | **10%** |

**Employee portion** (progressive, based on gross earnings):

| Gross annual earnings (BMD) | Employee payroll tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 48,000 | **0.5%** |
| 48,001 – 96,000 | **9.25%** |
| 96,001 – 200,000 | **10%** |
| 200,001 – 500,000 | **11.5%** |
| 500,001 – 1,000,000 | **12.5%** |
| Over 1,000,000 | Cap applies |

- The employer is responsible for payment of both employer and employee portions
- Employer may deduct employee portion from employee's remuneration (in full or in part)
- Both portions must be calculated separately and reported quarterly
- The combined marginal rate is up to 19.75% (employer + employee) on remuneration up to BMD 1 million

**Exempt from payroll tax base:** Employer contributions to social insurance, Hospital Insurance Plan, approved retirement plans, hospital and health schemes, life insurance schemes, and workers' compensation schemes. (EY p.147)

## Social Security / Social Insurance

Bermuda social insurance is a **flat-rate** weekly contribution per employee — not earnings-related. (EY p.147)

| Contribution | Amount |
|---|---|
| Total weekly contribution per employee | **BMD 75.30** |
| Employee share | **BMD 37.65/week** |
| Employer share | **BMD 37.65/week** |

- Employee deduction: approx. BMD 188.25/month (5-week month) or BMD 150.60/month (4-week month)
- Both portions remitted to the Bermuda Social Insurance Department
- **No income ceiling** — flat rate applies regardless of earnings level

**Exceptions (exempt from social insurance):**
- Full-time students under 26 working during vacations/weekends/public holidays
- Persons over pension age (65) — though employer must still contribute on their behalf
- Persons earning less than BMD 3,000/year
- Widows/widowers receiving widow's/widower's benefit
- Non-residents who have worked in Bermuda for less than 26 consecutive weeks

(EY p.148)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed / Sole Trader

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual conducting business in Bermuda
- **Tax base:** N/A — no income tax
- **Rate / mechanism:** **0%** income tax on all business income
- **Payroll tax:** Self-employed individuals are generally responsible for their own payroll tax (employer + employee portions if they have staff); sole traders with no employees have no payroll tax on their own drawings
- **Social insurance:** Social insurance contributions apply (flat BMD 75.30/week, shared with state for self-employed)
- **Key limits:** No income tax regardless of income level or structure

---

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No personal income tax means there is no preferential tax regime needed. Bermuda has no income tax for any individual, resident or non-resident.

**Note on Work Permits:** Bermuda requires work permits for foreign nationals to work in Bermuda. Bermuda does not offer a specific digital nomad visa but has a dedicated **Work From Bermuda Certificate** program for remote workers and students, allowing them to live and work remotely in Bermuda for up to 12 months (not covered in EY guide — verified from general knowledge).

## Effective Rate Examples

> Bermuda has NO income tax. The individual burden consists of: (1) employee payroll tax portion (if deducted by employer) and (2) flat social insurance.
> **1 EUR ≈ BMD 1.08** (BMD pegged 1:1 to USD; EUR/USD ~1.08).
> Employment scenario: employee payroll tax (progressive) + social insurance (flat BMD 37.65/week ≈ BMD 1,960/year).
> B2B scenario: no income tax; no payroll tax on own drawings if no employees; social insurance applies.

### Employee payroll tax (employee portion only, if deducted by employer):

| Gross income (BMD) | EUR equiv | Employee payroll tax | Social insurance (employee) | Total employee burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| BMD 32,400 | €30,000 | BMD 162 (0.5% × 32,400) | BMD 1,960 | BMD 2,122 | **6.5%** |
| BMD 64,800 | €60,000 | BMD 4,636 (calc. below) | BMD 1,960 | BMD 6,596 | **10.2%** |
| BMD 108,000 | €100,000 | BMD 9,836 (calc. below) | BMD 1,960 | BMD 11,796 | **10.9%** |

**Employee payroll tax step calculations:**

€60,000 (BMD 64,800):
- 0.5% × 48,000 = BMD 240
- 9.25% × (64,800 − 48,000) = 9.25% × 16,800 = BMD 1,554
- Total: BMD 1,794

*(Note: recalculated — 0.5% × 48,000 = 240; 9.25% × 16,800 = 1,554; total = BMD 1,794; above table revised)*

€100,000 (BMD 108,000):
- 0.5% × 48,000 = BMD 240
- 9.25% × (96,000 − 48,000) = 9.25% × 48,000 = BMD 4,440
- 10% × (108,000 − 96,000) = 10% × 12,000 = BMD 1,200
- Total: BMD 5,880

**Revised summary table:**

| Gross income (BMD) | EUR equiv | Scenario | Employee payroll tax | Social insurance | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| BMD 32,400 | €30,000 | Employment (employee portion) | BMD 162 | BMD 1,960 | BMD 2,122 | **6.5%** |
| BMD 32,400 | €30,000 | B2B (self-employed, no staff) | BMD 0 | BMD 1,960 | BMD 1,960 | **6.1%** |

| BMD 64,800 | €60,000 | B2B (self-employed, no staff) | BMD 0 | BMD 1,960 | BMD 1,960 | **3.0%** |
| BMD 108,000 | €100,000 | Employment (employee portion) | BMD 5,880 | BMD 1,960 | BMD 7,840 | **7.3%** |
| BMD 108,000 | €100,000 | B2B (self-employed, no staff) | BMD 0 | BMD 1,960 | BMD 1,960 | **1.8%** |

**Note on employer payroll tax:** The employer also pays a payroll tax (1%–10%) which is a cost of employment separate from employee burden. At BMD 64,800 salary, employer payroll tax = 1% × 64,800 = BMD 648 (below BMD 200,000 threshold). This makes Bermuda extremely attractive even accounting for total employment cost.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/BMD = 1.08 (BMD pegged 1:1 to USD; EUR/USD ~1.08)
- Social insurance: BMD 37.65/week × 52 weeks = BMD 1,958 ≈ BMD 1,960/year (employee portion)
- Employee payroll tax: progressive marginal rates applied to gross earnings
- B2B self-employed: no payroll tax on own drawings (no employees); social insurance applies
- Employer payroll tax NOT included in employee burden table (it is an employer cost)
