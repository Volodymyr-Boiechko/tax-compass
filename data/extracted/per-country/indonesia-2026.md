## Personal Income Tax

Indonesia uses a progressive income tax (PPh — Pajak Penghasilan) on worldwide income for residents. Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents taxed on Indonesia-source income at a flat 20%. Foreign expatriates with certain expertise are taxed only on Indonesia-source income for the first four years. The new Coretax system is being rolled out from the 2025 tax year; the 2025 annual return is due 31 March 2026. (EY pp. 636–644)

### Personal Income Tax Brackets (Resident Individuals)

|---|---|---|---|

| Next 190,000,000 (60M–250M) | 15% | 28,500,000 | 31,500,000 |
| Next 250,000,000 (250M–500M) | 25% | 62,500,000 | 94,000,000 |
| Next 4,500,000,000 (500M–5,000M) | 30% | 1,350,000,000 | 1,444,000,000 |
| Above 5,000,000,000 | 35% | — | — |

(EY p. 642)

**Without NPWP (Tax ID):** If a resident individual does not have a required Nomor Pokok Wajib Pajak (NPWP / Tax Identification Number), rates are increased by 20%, ranging from 6% to 42%. (EY p. 642)

**Non-residents:** Flat 20% on all Indonesia-source income. (EY p. 642)

**Standard deductions / allowances (Personal Allowances — PTKP):**

| Type of allowance | Annual amount (IDR) |
|---|---|
| Personal allowance (for taxpayer) | 54,000,000 |
| Married persons' additional allowance | 4,500,000 |
| Working wife's additional allowance | 54,000,000 |
| Each dependent family member (direct bloodline / adopted, max 3) | 4,500,000 |

**Employment deduction:** 5% of gross income, maximum IDR 6,000,000/year. (EY p. 640)

**Notes:**
- Pension deduction: 5% of gross pension, maximum IDR 2,400,000/year
- Zakat/religious contributions paid through employer are deductible
- BPJS contributions paid by employee are deductible (pension fund, TASPEN, old-age savings)
- Effective Tariff Rate (TER) system: employer applies monthly TER (0%–34%) for January–November; progressive rates used for December reconciliation

## Social Security

Indonesia's social security is administered by **BPJS Ketenagakerjaan** (Worker Social Security) and **BPJS Kesehatan** (Health Care). Both are mandatory. Expatriates working > 6 months must participate. (EY pp. 643–644)

### BPJS Ketenagakerjaan (Worker Social Security)

| Program | Employer | Employee | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Accident benefit (Jaminan Kecelakaan Kerja) | 0.24%–1.74% | 0% | Rate depends on industry |
| Life insurance (Jaminan Kematian) | 0.3% | 0% | No salary cap |
| Old-age benefit (Jaminan Hari Tua) | 3.7% | 2% | No salary cap |
| Pension benefit (Jaminan Pensiun) | 2% | 1% | Cap: IDR 10,547,400/month (from March 2025); employer max IDR 210,948/month; employee max IDR 105,474/month |

### BPJS Kesehatan (Health Care)

| Contribution | Employer | Employee | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Health care | 4% | 1% | Salary cap: IDR 12,000,000/month; max employer contribution IDR 480,000/month; max employee contribution IDR 120,000/month |

**Notes:**
- Pension benefit cap: IDR 10,547,400/month (effective March 2025); adjusted annually by GDP growth factor

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Final Income Tax Regime for Small Business (PP 23/2018)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual taxpayers (and small corporate taxpayers) with annual gross turnover < IDR 4,800,000,000 (~€280,000). Excludes income from independent personal services (lawyers, accountants, medical doctors, notaries, architects, consultants, insurance agents)
- **Tax base:** Monthly gross turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:** **0.5% of monthly gross turnover** — final tax, no deductions, no annual reconciliation
- **Duration:** Maximum 7 years for individual taxpayers (period begins from tax year of registration or year the PP regulation was issued, whichever applies)
- **Exception:** First IDR 500,000,000 of annual gross income is exempt from the 0.5% final tax for individual taxpayers
- **Social security:** Must register with BPJS independently
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if turnover > IDR 4,800,000,000 (PKP threshold); below threshold, may elect not to register
- **Key limits:** Cannot apply to: income from independent personal services; income earned/taxed abroad; income already subject to other final tax; gross turnover ≥ IDR 4,800,000,000

### Option B — Standard Progressive Tax (PPh 21/25/29 for Self-Employed Professionals)

- **Who qualifies:** Independent professional service providers (lawyers, accountants, doctors, architects, consultants, notaries, etc.); turnover ≥ IDR 4,800,000,000; or those who choose normal tax rates
- **Tax base:** For deemed profit basis (if income < IDR 4.8 billion): deemed profit percentage × gross income; for regular bookkeeping: actual net profit
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 5%/15%/25%/30%/35% rates applied to taxable income
- **PPh 21 withholding:** Professional services subject to Article 21 withholding tax at source; advance payment mechanism (PPh 25 = monthly instalments based on prior year liability); annual reconciliation via PPh 29 (SPT Tahunan)
- **Social security:** BPJS contributions payable; self-employed pay both employer and employee shares (total: ~8%–11% on income up to applicable caps)
- **VAT obligations:** PKP registration required if turnover > IDR 4,800,000,000; standard VAT 11%
- **Deemed profit rates (for independent professionals):** Typically 50% of gross revenue used as deemed profit base for those not keeping full books below IDR 4.8 billion; varies by profession as regulated by Directorate General of Taxation

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Qualifying Expatriate (4-Year Foreign Income Exemption)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign citizens with "certain expertise" (25 specified professions, primarily in science, engineering, mathematics — e.g., chemical, civil, environmental, geological engineers) who have been approved by the Director General of Taxation (DGT)
- **Benefit:** Taxed **only on Indonesian-source income** for the first **4 years** after becoming a tax resident — foreign-source income is generally exempt
- **Application:** Must request approval from DGT; verification within 10 days
- **Caveats:** Income from employment/services/activities in Indonesia paid outside Indonesia is still taxable; benefit does not apply to foreign citizens claiming treaty benefits; after 4 years, taxed on worldwide income (EY pp. 636–637)

## 2026 Changes

- **Coretax system:** New tax administration platform (Coretax) launched for 2025 tax year; 2025 annual return due 31 March 2026. Integrates TIN registration, payments, residency certificates, and return filing. (EY p. 644)
- **Pension benefit contribution cap updated:** IDR 10,547,400/month (effective March 2025); employer ceiling IDR 210,948/month; employee ceiling IDR 105,474/month. (EY p. 644)
- **New visa classification:** Indonesian government simplified visa classification codes in May 2025; Visit Visa and Limited Stay Visa classifications revised; effective 31 May 2025, only visa index B1 and B4 available (B2, B3 merged into B1). (EY pp. 649–650)
- **DKPTKA compensation fund:** USD 100/month/foreign employee continues to apply; employer must pay in advance to Ministry of Manpower. (EY p. 648)
- **Health Pass (Mpox):** Mandatory health declaration (SSHP QR Code) reactivated from 29 August 2024 for all travelers entering Indonesia. (EY p. 650)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, NPWP holder, standard personal allowance only (IDR 54,000,000).
> **Employment = PPh 21** (employment income with TER/progressive rates, personal allowance deducted).
> **B2B = Option B** (independent professional, standard progressive rates, 50% deemed profit ratio since professional services excluded from 0.5% regime).
> **1 EUR ≈ IDR 17,000** (approximate market rate, May 2026).
> BPJS contributions: Employee portion ~3% (old-age 2% + pension 1%) + health 1% = ~4% total, capped at IDR 12M/month for health and IDR 10.5M/month for pension.

### €30,000 Employment (IDR 510,000,000)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | €30,000 × IDR 17,000 | IDR 510,000,000 |
| Employment deduction (5%, max IDR 6M) | 5% × 510M = 25.5M, capped at 6M | IDR 6,000,000 |
| Personal allowance (PTKP) | Single, no dependants | IDR 54,000,000 |
| Total deductions | 6M + 54M | IDR 60,000,000 |
| Taxable income | 510M − 60M | IDR 450,000,000 |
| Tax on first IDR 60M @ 5% | | IDR 3,000,000 |
| Tax on next IDR 190M @ 15% | 15% × 190M | IDR 28,500,000 |
| Tax on next IDR 200M @ 25% | 25% × 200M | IDR 50,000,000 |
| Total income tax | | IDR 81,500,000 |
| BPJS employee (health 1% capped + pension 1% + old-age 2%) | ≈ 4% × 510M, but health capped at IDR 12M/month → IDR 120k/month × 12 = 1.44M; pension capped IDR 105,474/month × 12 = 1.27M; old-age 2% × 510M = 10.2M (no cap) | ≈ IDR 12,900,000 |
| **Total burden** | 81,500,000 + 12,900,000 | **IDR 94,400,000** |
| **Effective rate** | 94,400,000 / 510,000,000 | **18.5%** |

**Working for €60,000 employment (IDR 1,020,000,000):**
- Taxable = 1,020M − 6M (dedn) − 54M (PTKP) = 960,000,000
- Tax: 3M + 28.5M + 62.5M + 30% × (960M − 500M) = 94M + 30% × 460M = 94M + 138M = **232M**; effective on gross = 22.7%
- With BPJS: health IDR 1.44M/yr + pension IDR 1.27M/yr + old-age 2% × 1,020M = 20.4M → total BPJS ~23.1M
- Total burden ~255M; effective ~25.0%

**Working for €100,000 employment (IDR 1,700,000,000):**
- Taxable = 1,700M − 6M − 54M = 1,640,000,000
- Tax: 3M + 28.5M + 62.5M + 30% × (1,640M − 500M) = 94M + 30% × 1,140M = 94M + 342M = **436M**; effective on gross = 25.6%

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/IDR = 17,000 (approximate; verify at bi.go.id)
- Single person, no dependants, NPWP holder (no 20% uplift)
- Personal allowance (PTKP): IDR 54,000,000 (single, no dependants)
- Employment deduction capped at IDR 6,000,000
- B2B: professional services excluded from 0.5% final tax regime; 50% deemed profit assumed; personal allowance deducted from deemed profit
- BPJS capped contributions estimated; old-age savings (2%) has no cap so dominates at high incomes
- Employer-side BPJS (~7–8%) not shown in employment scenario

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Deemed profit percentage for professionals:** EY references deemed profit basis for self-employed professionals below IDR 4.8B but does not specify exact percentages by profession. DGT regulations prescribe varying rates (e.g., doctors, lawyers, etc.). The 50% assumption here is approximate — verify from DGT norm income tables.
- [ ] **PP 23/2018 exclusion of professional services:** The 0.5% final tax regime explicitly excludes "independent personal services" — EY confirms this. IT freelancers/software developers may or may not qualify as "professional services" under the exclusion; verify DGT guidance for specific activities.
- [ ] **4-year foreign income exemption — approved professions:** Only 25 specified ISCO-coded professions qualify (mostly engineers and scientists). IT professionals, accountants, and consultants typically do NOT qualify for this exemption. Verify full list from DGT.
- [ ] **Coretax transition issues:** The new Coretax system was launched in January 2025 and reported implementation difficulties. Compliance procedures may differ from prior years; monitor DGT updates.
- [ ] **BPJS for self-employed professionals:** Self-employed must register independently and pay both employer and employee portions of BPJS (effectively double the rate). Total health+worker security for self-employed could be 8–11% of income up to caps.
- [ ] **Self-employment legal restriction:** Only Indonesian citizens may conduct business as self-employed persons; foreigners must be employed by a sponsoring employer. This restricts true B2B freelancing options for expats in Indonesia.
