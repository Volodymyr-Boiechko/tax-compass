## Personal Income Tax

Flat 20% on all personal income for residents. No progressive brackets. Tax year = calendar year.
Filing deadline: 1 April of the following year. (EY p. 461)

**Flat PIT rate:**

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employment wages | **20%** |
| Self-employment / business income (standard) | **20%** |
| Small Business Status (SBS) — gross revenue ≤ GEL 500,000 | **1%** |
| Small Business Status — gross revenue > GEL 500,000 | **3%** (from month of breach) |
| Micro Business Status — gross revenue ≤ GEL 30,000 | **0%** (exempt) |
| Dividends from Georgian enterprises | **5%** (withholding; not included in resident's gross income if withheld) |
| Interest from Georgian banks/licensed financial institutions | **5%** (withheld; not further taxed) |
| Rental income (living space for living purposes) | **5%** |
| Capital gains (realised) — standard | **20%** |
| Non-resident Georgian-source income (not PE-related) | **10%** (withholding) |
| Income from oil/gas operations (non-resident subcontractors) | **4%** |
| Interest from low-tax jurisdiction payers | **15%** |

**Exempt income (key items for digital workers) (EY pp. 458–459):**
- **Income of resident individuals received from foreign sources** — fully exempt from Georgian PIT. This means a Georgian tax resident receiving salary/fees directly from a foreign employer (with no Georgian PE) pays 0% Georgian PIT on that income.
- Dividends withheld at 5% at source (not included in gross income; no further tax)
- Interest from Georgian banks (withheld at source)
- Capital gains from residential apartments held > 2 years
- Capital gains from vehicles held > 6 months after official registration
- Capital gains from other assets held > 2 years (if not used in economic activity)
- Property received by inheritance (I/II level legatees) up to GEL 150,000 (III/IV level)
- Lottery winnings up to GEL 1,000

(EY pp. 458–459, 461)

**Standard deductions:** None for employment income (no personal allowance under standard PIT). For individual entrepreneurs using standard 20% regime: all documented business expenses deductible.

**Non-deductible for standard PIT:** Entertainment expenses (unless in entertainment business), income tax itself, interest above 24%/year, representative expenses above 1% of prior-year gross income. (EY p. 460)

## Social Security

**Georgia does not impose social insurance / social security tax.** (EY p. 462)

**Mandatory funded pension scheme** (in force since 1 January 2019):

| Contributor | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee | **2%** of gross salary | Mandatory for all employees (except those 60+ / women 55+ before 2019) |
| Employer | **2%** of gross salary | Mandatory; paid on top of gross salary |
| State | **2%** of income | Only if annual salary < GEL 24,000 |
| State | **1%** of income | Only if annual salary GEL 24,000–60,000 |
| State | **0%** | No contribution if annual salary ≥ GEL 60,000 |

- Contributions go into the employee's individual pension savings account (private, not pooled)
- Pension savings are an asset of the individual, not a tax; however, they reduce net take-home
- **Self-employed individuals (including IE with Small Business Status):** Pension participation is **voluntary** at 4% of annual income (opt-in to private pension account)
- No state pension contribution for self-employed

(Sources: taxsummaries.pwc.com; justadvisors.ge; Law on Funded Pension — matsne.gov.ge)

**Note on state pension contribution:** At all income levels used in effective rate calculations below (GEL 88,500–295,000), annual salary exceeds GEL 60,000, so the state contributes 0%. The state contribution is only material for low-income workers.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Entrepreneur with Small Business Status (SBS)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered as an individual entrepreneur (IE) at the Revenue Service (rs.ge) who is granted Small Business Status. Available to both Georgian citizens and foreigners. IT/software services are explicitly permitted.
- **Tax base:** **Gross revenue** received from business activities (Georgian-source business income; excludes employment income). No expense deductions.
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - **1%** flat on gross revenue up to GEL 500,000/year
  - **3%** flat on gross revenue once the GEL 500,000 threshold is exceeded in a given month (3% applies from the beginning of that month until year-end)
  - Standard 20% PIT applies if status is revoked (see limits below)
  - No PIT at progressive rate; the 1%/3% IS the income tax
- **Social security:** No mandatory contributions. Voluntary pension participation at 4% of annual income.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds **GEL 100,000** in any consecutive 12-month period. VAT rate: 18%. Below GEL 100k: no VAT.
- **Key limits:**
  - Annual revenue ceiling: GEL 500,000 (~€170k at GEL 2.95/EUR) for 1% rate
  - Status revoked if revenue exceeds GEL 500,000 for **two consecutive calendar years**
  - Status revoked for repeated violations of cash-register rules (3+ fines in one year)
  - Prohibited activities: legal/notarial services, accounting/audit, medical services, veterinary services, gambling, certain import/export
  - IT services, software development, consulting: **permitted** ✓
- **Filing:** Monthly declarations due by the 15th of each month via rs.ge portal (not the standard April annual deadline)
- **Notable:** SBS does not require Georgian tax residency — it is based on business registration. A foreign national can register an IE and obtain SBS.

(EY pp. 463–464; gegidze.com 2026; ibccs.ge; expathub.ge)

### Option B — Micro Business Status

- **Who qualifies:** IE with annual gross income ≤ GEL 30,000, no employees, not VAT registered, not engaged in prohibited activities.
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue.
- **Rate:** **0%** — fully exempt from PIT on business income.
- **Social security:** Same as SBS — no mandatory contributions; voluntary pension at 4%.
- **VAT:** Not applicable (VAT registration would void micro status).
- **Key limits:** GEL 30,000/year (~€10,200) — too low for the income levels of interest.
- **When useful:** For very early-stage or part-time freelancers; transitions to SBS once income exceeds GEL 30,000.

(EY pp. 463–464)

---

### Option C — Foreign-Source Income Exemption (Informal Strategy)

- **Who qualifies:** Georgian tax residents who receive income **directly from a foreign employer** (no Georgian entity involved). The foreign employer must not have a PE in Georgia.
- **Tax base:** N/A — fully exempt from Georgian PIT.
- **Rate:** **0%** on the foreign-source income.
- **Social security:** No Georgian mandatory contributions on foreign-source income.
- **VAT:** No Georgian VAT obligation on wages from a foreign employer.
- **Key limits / risks:**
  - If the foreign employer creates a PE in Georgia through the employee's activity, the exemption is lost
  - No legal certainty / no formal status to obtain; relies on the PE-assessment of the GTA
  - No pension, social protection, or local record of income
  - Suitable for remote employees of foreign companies only; not for self-employed invoicing
- **Not recommended** as a standalone strategy due to legal uncertainty; SBS is the preferred transparent path for freelancers.

(EY p. 458 — exempt income list)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### High Net-Worth Individual (HNWI) Status

- **Eligibility:** Individuals holding property in Georgia valued at over USD 500,000, OR with Georgian-source annual income exceeding GEL 25,000, who hold either a Georgian passport, residence permit, or Georgian residence card. Annual income must exceed GEL 200,000 each of the preceding 3 years.
- **Benefit:** Granted Georgian tax residency on request even if not physically present 183 days. Allows use of Georgia as a tax residency base.
- **Caveats:** For qualifying individuals who structure their affairs appropriately, Georgian tax on worldwide income applies (20% PIT) — but since foreign-source income is exempt, the effective rate may be low.

(EY pp. 456–457)

### IT Residence Permit

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals employed in IT, OR registered as IE with Small Business Status in IT activities. Must have ≥ 2 years IT experience and annual income ≥ GEL equivalent of USD 25,000.
- **Benefit:** Path to work residence permit in Georgia for IT workers.
- **Note:** From **March 2026**, mandatory work permit required for foreign nationals working in Georgia (see 2026 Changes below).

(EY p. 467)

## 2026 Changes

- **Work permit requirement from March 2026 (EY p. 469):** Georgia introduces mandatory work permit and visa/residence permit requirements for foreign nationals immigrating to Georgia for employment (including **remote work**) or entrepreneurial activities. Starting 1 March 2026:
  - Foreign nationals must obtain a **work permit** AND either a **Georgian D1 Immigration Visa** or a **residence permit**
  - Standard processing time: 30 days
  - Exception: foreign nationals registered on the Labor Migrants Portal by 1 March 2026 have until **1 January 2027** to obtain the work permit
  - Non-compliance: fines applicable to employers, employees, and self-employed individuals
  - Under current legislation (before March 2026), visa-free nationals could work without permits for up to 1 year
- **No changes to PIT rate (flat 20%) for 2026** announced
- **No changes to SBS 1% regime** announced
- **Short-term residence permit property threshold:** Will increase to USD 150,000 (from USD 100,000) from March 2026 (EY p. 468)
- **Dividend exemption for agricultural cooperatives:** Extended until 1 January 2026 only — dividends from qualifying agricultural cooperatives re-enter the taxable regime from 2026 (EY p. 457)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> **1 EUR = GEL 2.95** (approximate; Georgian lari has been stable ~GEL 2.85–3.05/EUR in 2024–2025).
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side only; employer's 2% pension + other costs not shown).
> "SBS" = gross revenue; IT services; GEL 500k limit not exceeded; no voluntary pension contributions.
> Pension included as "employee burden" for employment scenario as it reduces take-home.
> State pension contribution = 0% at all income levels below (all exceed GEL 60,000 annual threshold).

### Working: GEL 88,500 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| PIT (20%) | 20% × 88,500 | GEL 17,700 |
| Mandatory pension (employee 2%) | 2% × 88,500 | GEL 1,770 |
| State pension (0%) | Income > GEL 60,000 → no state contribution | GEL 0 |
| **Total burden** | 17,700 + 1,770 | **GEL 19,470** |
| **Effective rate** | 19,470 / 88,500 | **22.0%** |

### Working: GEL 88,500 (≈ €30,000) SBS 1%

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| SBS tax (1%) | 1% × 88,500 | GEL 885 |
| Pension (voluntary — not included) | — | GEL 0 |
| **Total burden** | 885 | **GEL 885** |
| **Effective rate** | 885 / 88,500 | **1.0%** |

### Summary table

| Gross income (GEL) | EUR equiv | Scenario | PIT | Pension (employee) | Total | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| GEL 88,500 | €30,000 | Employment (20% PIT + 2% pension) | GEL 17,700 | GEL 1,770 | GEL 19,470 | **22.0%** |
| GEL 88,500 | €30,000 | SBS 1% (no deductions) | GEL 885 | GEL 0 | GEL 885 | **1.0%** |
| GEL 177,000 | €60,000 | Employment (20% PIT + 2% pension) | GEL 35,400 | GEL 3,540 | GEL 38,940 | **22.0%** |
| GEL 177,000 | €60,000 | SBS 1% | GEL 1,770 | GEL 0 | GEL 1,770 | **1.0%** |
| GEL 295,000 | €100,000 | Employment (20% PIT + 2% pension) | GEL 59,000 | GEL 5,900 | GEL 64,900 | **22.0%** |
| GEL 295,000 | €100,000 | SBS 1% | GEL 2,950 | GEL 0 | GEL 2,950 | **1.0%** |

All SBS income points are within the GEL 500,000/year limit (~€169,500 at GEL 2.95/EUR).

**Key insight — SBS is exceptional:** Georgia's 1% flat tax on revenue under SBS is one of the lowest self-employment tax rates in the world. At €30k the gap versus employment is 21 percentage points (1% vs. 22%). The employer also saves the 2% pension contribution (not shown above). The foreign-source income exemption (Option C) offers 0% but lacks legal certainty.

**Voluntary pension for SBS holders:** If an SBS entrepreneur opts into the pension at 4%, the effective rate rises to 5% — still far below the 22% employment burden.

**Employer-side costs for employment (not shown):**
- Employer pension: 2% × gross salary (additional cost on top of gross)
- No other social insurance obligations

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/GEL = 2.95 (approximate 2025 average; verify at nbg.gov.ge)
- Employment: standard flat 20% PIT; 2% mandatory employee pension; state pension = 0% (income > GEL 60k)
- SBS: IT/software services; no VAT (below GEL 100k threshold at €30k level; above at €60k and €100k — see note)
- **VAT note:** At €60k = GEL 177,000 and €100k = GEL 295,000, both exceed the GEL 100,000 VAT registration threshold. If clients are foreign B2B (export services), VAT may be zero-rated; if Georgian B2B, 18% VAT applies. This does not affect the income tax calculation but adds compliance obligation.
- No business expense deductions under SBS (gross revenue = taxable base)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Pension for SBS holders — mandatory vs voluntary:** PWC and secondary sources give contradictory signals; current understanding is that self-employed/IE pension participation is **voluntary** (after constitutional court ruling). Verify directly at rs.ge or the Pension Agency (pensia.ge).
- [ ] **VAT on export services:** Whether SBS holders exporting IT services to foreign clients must register for VAT above GEL 100k depends on place-of-supply rules. If services are zero-rated for export, the compliance burden exists but tax cost is zero — needs verification with a Georgian accountant.
- [ ] **March 2026 work permit implementation:** EY describes mandatory work permits from 1 March 2026 for foreign nationals. How this applies to remote workers (working in Georgia for foreign companies) and SBS entrepreneurs is not fully clear — the new Labor Migrants Portal registration may be required by 1 March 2026 to get the extended January 2027 deadline.
- [ ] **Foreign-source income exemption scope:** Confirmed in EY exempt income list; the boundary between "foreign-source income" (exempt) and "Georgian-source income via foreign client" (taxable under SBS) is a legal gray area — the SBS is the safe/compliant path.
- [ ] **No currency information:** GEL/EUR rate not stated in EY guide; 2.95 is an approximation from market rates.
- [ ] **Micro business status** threshold and prohibited activities not fully verified for 2026; GEL 30k limit assumed unchanged.
