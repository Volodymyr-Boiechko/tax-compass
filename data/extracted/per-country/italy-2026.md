## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (IRPEF — Imposta sul Reddito delle Persone Fisiche) for residents taxed on worldwide income. Tax year = calendar year. Filing deadline: 30 September (Modello 730 e-file) or 31 October (Modello Redditi). (EY p.715)

Self-employment income (reddito da lavoro autonomo) for professionals is taxed as ordinary income at IRPEF rates, OR under the flat-rate Regime Forfettario if conditions are met. IRAP (regional production tax, 3.9%) also applies to business income in certain cases. (EY p.708)

**IRPEF brackets** (EY p.712)

| Taxable income (EUR) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|
| 0 – 28,000 | 23% |
| 28,000 – 50,000 | 35% |
| Over 50,000 | 43% |

**Additional regional income tax:** 0.7%–3.63% on taxable income (depending on region). (EY p.712)

**Additional municipal income tax:** Up to 1.2% on taxable income (depending on municipality). (EY p.712)

**Combined top rate:** 43% IRPEF + up to 3.63% regional + up to 1.2% municipal = up to ~47.83% effective top rate.

**Additional 1% contribution:** On gross employment income exceeding EUR 55,448 (2025 threshold), an extra 1% employee SS contribution applies. (EY p.714)

**Investment income:**
- Dividends: 26% substitute withholding tax (final) (EY p.709)
- Interest (bank/bond): 26% final withholding (EY p.709)
- Capital gains from securities: 26% substitute tax (EY p.710)

**Standard deductions / allowances / credits:**
- Mandatory social security contributions (employee portion) deductible (EY p.711)
- Tax credit of 19% for: mortgage interest on primary home (up to EUR 4,000); medical expenses >EUR 129.11; life/health insurance premiums (up to EUR 530/750/1,291); school fees up to EUR 1,000 (EY p.711)
- From 2025: for taxable income >EUR 75,000, cap applies to deductible expenses depending on income level and number of dependent children (EY p.711)
- For taxable income >EUR 240,000: many deductions become non-deductible (EY p.711)

**Capital gains — real estate:**
- Taxable as ordinary income if sold within 5 years of purchase (unless used as primary residence for most of ownership period)
- Exempt if held >5 years (with exceptions for restructured properties) (EY p.711)
- Separate 26% final tax optionally applicable

**Notes:**
- Married persons file separately (no joint return option) (EY p.715)
- Advance tax payments: 100% of prior year liability; 40% by 30 June, 60% by 30 November (EY p.716)

## Social Security

Comprehensive system administered by INPS (National Institute for Social Security). (EY p.713–714)

**Employee contributions (general):**
- Employee share: approximately 9%–10% of gross remuneration (varies by sector and collective agreement) (EY p.714)
- Additional 1% employee contribution on gross income exceeding EUR 55,448 (2025) (EY p.714)
- Employer share: approximately 27%–32% of gross remuneration (varies by sector) (EY p.714)

**Maximum contribution base:** EUR 120,607/year (2025) — pension contributions capped at this level for those enrolled before 1 January 1996. (EY p.714)

**Self-employed — INPS Gestione Separata** (EY p.714):

| Contribution | Self-employed (consultants/freelancers) | Directors |
|---|---|---|
| INPS Gestione Separata | 26.07% | 35.03% |
| If already contributing to another mandatory SS scheme or retired | 24.00% | 24.00% |

- Contribution base: annual self-employment income up to EUR 120,607 (2025)
- Self-employed person pays full contribution (no employer share); client companies must withhold and pay the contribution
- Contribution split: 2/3 paid by client company, 1/3 withheld from professional's compensation (for co.co.co collaborators); for pure "lavoro autonomo" professionals registered for VAT, the self-employed person pays the full amount

**Note for Regime Forfettario:** Contributions to INPS Gestione Separata still apply in Regime Forfettario unless the professional is enrolled in a separate professional pension fund (Cassa di Previdenza — e.g., for lawyers, engineers, doctors).

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Regime Forfettario (Flat-Rate Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals (liberi professionisti, ditte individuali) with total gross revenue from all business/professional activities not exceeding **EUR 85,000** in the preceding year. Additional conditions: no employees/collaborators with cost > EUR 20,000; no income from employment or assimilated income > EUR 30,000 in the same year; no participation in transparent partnerships or in controlled companies in the same activity sector. (EY p.708; agenziaentrate.gov.it)
- **Tax base:** Taxable income = gross revenue × applicable "profitability coefficient" (coefficiente di redditività). Key coefficients:

  | Activity category | Coefficient | Applicable to |
  |---|---|---|
  | Professional services (general) | **78%** | IT consultants, consultants, most liberal professions without separate Cassa |
  | Professional services (with Cassa) | 78% | Engineers, architects, lawyers (with their professional pension fund) |
  | Artisans and craftsmen | 67% | Handicraft, repair services |
  | Manufacturing | 40% | Production activities |
  | Commerce (food/beverages, retail) | 40%–54% | Varies by sub-category |
  | Catering and accommodation | 40% | Hotels, B&Bs, agritourism |
  | Other services | 67% | Miscellaneous services |

  For most IT/tech/consulting professionals: taxable income = 78% of gross revenue.

- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - **Standard rate: 15%** substitute tax (imposta sostitutiva) on taxable income (= coefficient × revenue)
  - **5% rate for first 5 years:** Applies to newly started activities if: the individual has not exercised any business/professional activity (including as partner in a transparent entity) in the three calendar years preceding the activity start, AND the activity does not constitute a mere continuation of a prior activity (EY p.708–709)
  - The substitute tax replaces IRPEF, regional, and municipal income taxes; no IRAP
- **Social security:** INPS Gestione Separata 26.07% on actual net taxable income (coefficient × revenue). No deduction of SS from the tax base (unlike régime réel). Persons enrolled in professional pension funds (Casse) pay to their Cassa instead.
  - Additional flat contribution: self-employed artisans/traders enrolled in INPS Artigiani e Commercianti pay flat quarterly minimums (~EUR 4,000–4,200/year in 2025) plus 24% on income above minimum threshold. Professionals with Gestione Separata pay proportionally.
  - **Riduzione del 35% on INPS contributions:** Regime Forfettario taxpayers enrolled in INPS Gestione Commercianti/Artigiani may apply a 35% reduction to their contributions (available since 2016; optional election).
- **VAT obligations:** **Complete VAT exemption** (no VAT charged to clients, no VAT recovery on purchases). Must indicate "operazione esente IVA ai sensi dell'art. 1, commi 54-89, L. 190/2014" on invoices. Electronic invoicing (fatturazione elettronica) mandatory from 1 July 2022.
- **Key limits:** EUR 85,000 revenue ceiling (if exceeded during the year, standard IRPEF regime applies from the following year; if revenue exceeds EUR 100,000 in the year, exit is immediate). Cannot deduct actual business expenses from taxable income — coefficient is fixed. Advantageous when actual expenses < (1 − coefficient)% of revenue (i.e., for a 78% coefficient, breakeven at ~22% actual expenses).

### Option B — Regime Ordinario (Standard Self-Employment — Libero Professionista)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed professionals with revenue > EUR 85,000 or who opt out of Regime Forfettario.
- **Tax base:** Net income = gross receipts minus actual deductible professional expenses (cash-basis: "principio di cassa").
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net income taxed at progressive IRPEF rates (23%–43%) + regional (0.7–3.63%) + municipal (up to 1.2%).
- **Social security:** INPS Gestione Separata 26.07% of net income; or professional pension fund (Cassa) contributions.
- **VAT:** Must register for VAT, charge 22% (standard rate) or reduced rates; quarterly/annual VAT returns.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; requires proper bookkeeping (Form 2035 / professional register); access to all deductions (health insurance, pension supplements, home office, etc.).
- **IRAP:** Does not apply if the professional works without an autonomous "organization" (i.e., no employees and minimal capital investment in equipment). Case-by-case determination; frequently litigated. (EY p.708)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Regime degli Impatriati (Impatriate Tax Regime)

- **Eligibility (for transfers after 31 December 2023):** (EY p.706–707)
  - Must not have been tax resident in Italy for at least **3 years** (prior rule: 2 years) before transfer
  - Must commit to remaining tax resident in Italy for **4 years** (prior rule: 2 years)
  - Must hold a high-level qualification or specialization
  - For intra-group transfers: non-residency period extended to 6 years (if not previously employed in Italy by same group) or 7 years (if previously employed in Italy by same group)
- **Benefit:** 50% of Italian-source employment/self-employment income exempt from IRPEF, up to EUR 600,000/year. Exemption increased to 60% if employee transfers to Italy with a dependent child. (EY p.706)
- **Duration:** Tax year of transfer + 4 following years (5 years total). (EY p.707)
- **Earlier transfers (30 April 2019 – 31 December 2023):** 30% inclusion (70% exemption); or 90% exemption for certain southern Italian regions. (EY p.705–706)
- **Pre-2019 transfers:** 50% inclusion (50% exemption) for 5 years. (EY p.704–705)
- **Caveats:** Not cumulative with the flat tax for high net worth individuals. Applies to employment, self-employment (lavoro autonomo), and business income (reddito d'impresa). (EY p.707)

### Regime Flat Tax per Nuovi Residenti (Flat Tax for New High-Net-Worth Residents)

- **Eligibility:** Individuals who have not been Italian tax resident for at least **9 of the preceding 10 years**; transfer tax residency to Italy. (EY p.707)
- **Benefit:** Flat tax of **EUR 200,000/year** on all foreign-source income (regardless of actual amount). Family members may join: EUR 25,000/year each. For transfers after 10 August 2024, the flat tax is raised to **EUR 200,000** (from previous EUR 100,000). (EY p.707)
- **Duration:** Up to 15 years (regime lasts until revoked or conditions cease). (EY p.707)
- **Caveats:** Italian-source income taxed at ordinary IRPEF rates. Cannot combine with impatriate regime. Not available to those previously resident in Italy in the prior 9 years. (EY p.707)

## 2026 Changes

- **Regime Forfettario EUR 85,000 ceiling:** In force from 2023 (raised from EUR 65,000). No announced change for 2026; immediate exit if revenue exceeds EUR 100,000 in-year.
- **5% starter rate for Forfettario:** Continues for new activities in first 5 years.
- **INPS Gestione Separata rate 2025:** 26.07% (for self-employed without other SS schemes); rate confirmed for 2025 and expected to continue for 2026 (annual confirmation from INPS needed).
- **Maximum SS contribution base:** EUR 120,607 (2025); indexed annually by INPS.
- **Flat tax for new residents:** Raised to EUR 200,000/year for transfers after 10 August 2024 (from EUR 100,000). (EY p.707)
- **Expense deduction cap:** From 2025, cap on deductible expenses for taxable income >EUR 75,000 (dependent on family situation). (EY p.711)
- **Short-term rental cedolare secca:** Rate raised to 26% from 2024 (from 21%) for short-term rental income on first property; 21% option retained for one property at taxpayer's discretion. (EY p.708)
- **IRAP abolition for sole traders/professionals:** IRAP eliminated for natural persons (individuals) from 2022; continues to apply to corporations, partnerships, and some self-employed. (Not explicitly stated in EY p.700–720 but confirmed by Italian tax law 2022)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only. All figures in EUR (Eurozone).
> "Employment" = gross salary; employee SS ~9.49% (9% + 0.49% approx. composite) included; employer ~28% not shown.
> "Forfettario" = gross revenue; 78% coefficient (IT professional/consultant); INPS Gestione Separata 26.07%.
> Regional tax: 1.73% average (Milan/Lombardia rate); municipal: 0.8% (Milan).
> No IRAP for natural-person professionals.

### EUR 30,000 — Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (~9.49%) | 9.49% × 30,000 | EUR 2,847 |
| IRPEF base (SS deductible) | 30,000 − 2,847 | EUR 27,153 |
| IRPEF | 0–28,000 @ 23% = 6,440; 27,153: all in 23% bracket → 23% × 27,153 | EUR 6,245 |
| Regional tax (1.73%) | 1.73% × 27,153 | EUR 470 |
| Municipal tax (0.8%) | 0.8% × 27,153 | EUR 217 |
| **Total burden** | **2,847 + 6,245 + 470 + 217** | **EUR 9,779** |
| **Effective rate** | **9,779 / 30,000** | **32.6%** |

### EUR 30,000 — Regime Forfettario (78% coefficient, IT professional)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| INPS Gestione Separata (26.07%) | 26.07% × 30,000 | EUR 7,821 |
| Taxable income (78% × 30,000) | 78% × 30,000 | EUR 23,400 |
| Forfettario substitute tax (15%) | 15% × 23,400 | EUR 3,510 |
| **Total burden** | **7,821 + 3,510** | **EUR 11,331** |
| **Effective rate** | **11,331 / 30,000** | **37.8%** |

*With 5% starter rate (first 5 years): 5% × 23,400 = EUR 1,170 → total EUR 8,991 → **29.97%***

**Employer contribution (not included above):** ~27–32% of gross salary for employment scenarios.

**Key observations:**
- Forfettario 15% is most competitive at higher revenue levels (EUR 60–85k) where IRPEF marginal rate would otherwise be 35–43%; INPS Gestione Separata remains high at 26.07%
- At EUR 30,000 employment is more tax-efficient than standard Forfettario 15% due to lower IRPEF at that income level; but Forfettario 5% starter rate beats employment significantly
- Above EUR 85,000 revenue, Forfettario not available; Regime Ordinario with good expense deductions becomes preferable

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants; Lombardia regional rate 1.73%; Milan municipal rate 0.8%
- Employment SS: composite ~9.49% employee (10% approximate less small adjustments); employer ~28–30% not shown
- Forfettario: coefficient 78% for IT/consulting professionals (most common for tech freelancers)
- Regime Ordinario: 15% actual expenses assumed; INPS 26.07% deductible from IRPEF base
- INPS Gestione Separata cap EUR 120,607/year — not triggered at any modeled income level
- No professional Cassa contributions modeled (applies to lawyers, engineers, doctors, etc.)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] Employee SS composite rate used (~9.49%) is approximate; actual rate varies by sector and collective labor agreement (CCNL); verify exact rate for specific employment sector
- [ ] Employer SS rate range 27–32% is approximate; varies widely by industry and company size
- [ ] Regional income tax rate used (1.73% for Lombardia); rates vary 0.7%–3.63% by region — effective rate will differ for Rome (Lazio 1.73%), Naples (Campania 2.03%), etc.
- [ ] Professional Cassa di Previdenza (for lawyers, engineers, notaries, doctors, architects): contribution rates differ from INPS Gestione Separata (26.07%); separate modeling needed per profession
- [ ] Regime Forfettario INPS: the optional 35% contribution reduction for certain INPS categories not modeled
- [ ] IRAP: abolished for sole traders/natural persons from 2022; still applies for some activities through partnerships/entities — not modeled
- [ ] Impatriate regime (50% exemption for 5 years) effective rate not computed; would be highly attractive for high earners (effective IRPEF rate halved)
- [ ] Flat tax for new residents (EUR 200,000 fixed tax on foreign income) effective rate not computed; highly attractive for very high earners with foreign income
- [ ] 2026 INPS Gestione Separata rate not yet confirmed (2025 rate 26.07% used; check INPS circular for 2026 update)
- [ ] EUR 85,000 Forfettario ceiling — if exceeded mid-year, timing of exit from regime not fully modeled
