## CRITICAL EXCHANGE RATE AND INFLATION CAVEAT

> **Argentina has extreme inflation and a complex exchange rate history. All peso figures in this file depreciate rapidly and must be treated as approximate.**
>
> - **Inflation context:** Argentina's annual CPI was ~118% in 2024 and approximately 50–80% in 2025 (decelerating). All ARS-denominated thresholds are updated semiannually (January and July) by law, indexed to CPI.
> - **Exchange rate history:**
>   - Until April 2025: multiple exchange rates existed (official "Tipo de Cambio Oficial", MEP/bolsa rate, "dólar blue" informal, CCL rate). The spread between official and parallel rates reached 100%+ at various points.
>   - **April 2025:** Argentina lifted most capital controls backed by a $20 billion IMF agreement, effectively unifying exchange rates. The official and blue rates converged to approximately 1,450–1,500 ARS/USD.
>   - **For 2025 average (context):** ~1,100–1,244 ARS/USD (average incorporating the pre-unification period); the task reference rate of 1 EUR ≈ ARS 1,100 reflects the approximate pre-unification average.
>   - **As of May 2026:** Official rate ≈ 1,450–1,500 ARS/USD ≈ 1,580–1,650 ARS/EUR (approximate).
> - **Practical implication:** All EUR-equivalent figures in this file are approximations with ±30–50% uncertainty depending on which period and which rate is used. The Monotributo monthly payment in EUR terms in February 2026 is therefore substantially different from the 2025 average equivalent.
> - **Do NOT use ARS-denominated thresholds for multi-year planning** without re-indexing. Refer to ARCA/AFIP for current values.

## Personal Income Tax (Impuesto a las Ganancias)

Progressive system (Ganancias — 4th Category for employment and self-employment income) for residents taxed on worldwide income. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return filed in June of the following year. Self-employed must file annually in June declaring prior year earnings. Married couples taxed separately on income from personal activities. - Employer withholds Ganancias monthly from salary; annual return reconciles any difference.

**Nonresidents (< 6 months in Argentina):** Subject to 35% final withholding on Argentine-source income; standard 30% deemed-expense deduction allowed; effective withholding rate 24.5% on gross compensation. (EY p.43)

**Capital gains:** (EY p.41)
- Shares traded on Argentine stock exchange: exempt for individuals
- Shares of non-listed companies (foreign or domestic not on Argentine exchange): 15% flat rate on net gain
- Capital gains from real estate purchased on/after 1 January 2018: 15% flat rate (schedular); primary home sale: exempt
- Pre-2018 real estate: transfer tax abolished since 2018

**Dividends:** (EY p.40)
- From Argentine companies (profits previously taxed at corporate level): 7% final withholding on distribution (Argentine individuals and foreign shareholders); additional 35% equalization tax applies if company distributes profits in excess of accumulated taxable income
- Dividends from foreign companies to Argentine residents: taxed at regular progressive rates (5%–35%)

**SS contribution ceiling:** Monthly salary base capped at ARS 3,731,212.01 (December 2025). Cap updated monthly. A year for SS purposes = 13 months (used in cap calculations). (EY p.45)

**Employer contributions:** (EY p.45)
- Standard: 20.4% or 18% depending on company activity and turnover (amount of sales)
- Medical care: additional 6% (no ceiling for employer health contribution)
- Minimum non-taxable amount for employer SS: ARS 7,003.68 (applied to SS base)
- Employer total (SS + medical): approximately 26.4% or 24% (depending on activity) of gross salary, with 6% medical uncapped

**Self-employed contributions:** (EY p.45)
- 20% of declared base salary (base salary set by government)
- Maximum monthly contribution: BRL 1,631.48 — *Note: EY says ARS-denominated max; text references BRL in error — self-employed max monthly ARS contribution for 2025 = ARS 1,631.48 per the EY text (likely referring to the base multiplied by rate)*
- Correct: self-employed pay 20% × government-set base; maximum monthly contribution for 2025 ≈ ARS 1,631.48 (per EY p.45 text — this appears to be a low figure consistent with a minimum category)

**Important SS exceptions:** (EY p.45)
- Professionals, researchers, scientists, and technicians contracted from abroad for ≤ 2 years with temporary residence, covered by home-country SS system, providing proof of coverage for death/disability/retirement: exempt from Argentine SS
- Directors' fees: no employee or employer SS on directors' fees; directors pay fixed monthly amounts to the Self-Employed System
- Totalization agreements active with: Belgium, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, France, Greece, Israel, Italy, Luxembourg, Peru, Portugal, Slovenia, Spain, MERCOSUR countries (Brazil, Paraguay, Uruguay), Ibero-American Multilateral Agreement (Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Ecuador, El Salvador, Paraguay, Peru, Portugal, Spain, Uruguay)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Monotributo (Régimen Simplificado)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual taxpayers (personas humanas) with annual gross revenue below the ceiling of Category K. Revenue limit and monthly payment indexed semiannually to CPI. Includes: freelancers, independent professionals, sole traders, small service providers. NOT available to companies (only natural persons).
- **Tax base:** Fixed monthly contribution — does NOT depend on actual revenue or profit within the category. One payment covers income tax + social security (jubilación + obra social).
- **Rate / mechanism — Monotributo categories February–July 2026 (services — locaciones y servicios):**

  | Category | Annual gross income ceiling (ARS) | Monthly payment — Services (ARS) | Monthly equivalent (EUR approx.) |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | A | 10,277,988 | 42,387 | ~€26 |
  | B | 15,058,448 | 48,251 | ~€30 |
  | C | 21,113,697 | 56,502 | ~€34 |
  | D | 26,212,853 | 72,414 | ~€44 |
  | E | 30,833,964 | 102,538 | ~€62 |
  | F | 38,642,048 | 129,045 | ~€78 |
  | G | 46,211,109 | 197,108 | ~€120 |
  | H | 70,113,407 | 447,347 | ~€272 |
  | I | 78,479,212 | 824,802 | ~€501 |

  EUR conversions use 1 EUR ≈ ARS 1,645 (approximate May 2026 rate; highly volatile — see caveat above).

  Categories A–K apply to services (locaciones y servicios). For sale of goods, monthly payments are lower (same category income ceiling but reduced contribution). Categories H–K for services only (goods capped at Category H).

  (Source: ARCA/AFIP official table February–July 2026; eleconomista.com.ar, indicadores.ar)

- **What the monthly payment covers:**
  - **Componente impositivo (tax component):** Covers income tax (part of Ganancias replaced by fixed payment)
  - **Jubilación (pension component):** Covers pension/retirement contribution
  - **Obra social (health component):** Covers basic health coverage
  - Note: the "monthly payment" shown above is the TOTAL; for lower categories the health/pension components are fixed and the tax component is very small

- **Annual income limits in EUR terms (approximate, using 1 EUR ≈ ARS 1,645):**
  - Category A ceiling: ARS 10.3M ≈ €6,200/year
  - Category K ceiling: ARS 108.4M ≈ €65,900/year
  - Therefore, Monotributo covers income up to approximately €66k/year at current rates (extremely volatile)

- **VAT obligations:** Monotributo taxpayers are exempt from IVA (21% standard VAT). They do not charge, collect, or remit IVA. This is a significant simplification advantage.
- **Key limits:** Only for natural persons; 11 categories (A–K); semiannual recategorization in January and July; if income significantly exceeds Category K ceiling, must switch to Responsable Inscripto (standard regime). Does not allow expense deductions.

### Option B — Responsable Inscripto (Standard Self-Employment Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** All individuals with self-employment or business income exceeding Monotributo ceilings, or who opt out of Monotributo.
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross revenue minus deductible expenses). Self-employed may deduct actual business expenses. (EY p.42)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive Ganancias scale (5%–35%) on annual net taxable income (after all personal deductions). Bimonthly advance tax payments required (August–April, based on prior year's tax). (EY p.46)
- **Social security:** 20% of government-set base salary; maximum monthly contribution as set by ANSES.
- **VAT obligations:** Must register for IVA, charge 21% on services, file monthly IVA returns. Significant administrative burden.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; full expense deductibility.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Five-Year Assignment Exemption (Partial)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign individuals in Argentina solely because of their employment, remaining ≤ 5 years.
- **Benefit:** Not considered tax resident (and therefore taxed only on Argentine-source income, not worldwide income). Rule extends to accompanying family members.
- **Duration:** Up to 5 years from arrival.
- **Caveats:** Does not apply if the individual has other connections to Argentina beyond the employment assignment. (EY p.40)

### Professional/Researcher SS Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Professionals, scientists, and technicians contracted from abroad for ≤ 2 years, with temporary residence, covered by home-country SS.
- **Benefit:** Exempt from Argentine SS contributions (pension + healthcare).
- **Duration:** From date of application; valid as long as conditions met; usable only once.
- **Caveats:** Must prove SS coverage in home country for death/disability/retirement. Does not exempt from Argentine income tax. (EY p.45)

### Simplified Income Tax Return (Optional)

- **Eligibility:** Individuals whose total annual income does not exceed ARS 1 billion AND total wealth ≤ ARS 10 billion during the last 3 fiscal years.
- **Benefit:** Reduced reporting obligations; prefilled return via ARCA (formerly AFIP); presumption of accuracy for prior periods.
- **Note:** This is an administrative simplification, not a tax reduction regime. (EY p.46)

Argentina levies a wealth tax on personal assets. (EY p.43–44)

## 2026 Changes

> **Note: All ARS figures below are provisional and may have changed. Verify at ARCA (arca.gob.ar) — formerly AFIP.**

- **Semiannual Ganancias scale update (January 2026):** Brackets increased by ~14.29% (H2 2025 CPI). Next update: July 2026 (based on H1 2026 CPI). The 5%–35% rate structure remains unchanged.
- **Monotributo update (February 2026):** Category ceilings and monthly payments adjusted for CPI. Next adjustment: August 2026.
- **Exchange rate unification (April 2025, operative through 2026):** Argentina unified its exchange rate system after lifting capital controls backed by IMF agreement. The official, MEP, and blue dollar rates have largely converged at ARS ~1,450–1,500/USD as of early 2026. This fundamentally changes the calculation of tax burdens in EUR/USD terms.
- **Personal assets tax simplification (2026 fiscal year):** Reduced to two-tier structure with max 1.5%; 2027 further reduction to 0.25% announced. (EY p.44)
- **ARCA replaces AFIP:** The Agencia de Recaudación y Control Aduanero (ARCA) replaced AFIP as the tax authority from late 2024. All filings now via arca.gob.ar.
- **Simplified tax return (Law 27,799, new):** Optional simplified filing for individuals with income < ARS 1 billion and wealth < ARS 10 billion. (EY p.46)
- **Ganancias restitution (Law 27,725, 2024):** The December 2023 law that had exempted most employees from Ganancias was partially reversed; 4th Category Ganancias was reinstated for employees by mid-2024. The current progressive scale again applies to employees above the MNI threshold.

- [ ] Self-employed SS contribution exact base salary not confirmed: EY p.45 states "20% of declared base salary (fixed by government)"; the monthly amounts change frequently; ARS 5M/year estimate used is rough — verify at ANSES/ARCA
- [ ] Ganancias H1 2026 bracket amounts: derived by applying 14.29% CPI adjustment to EY H2 2025 table; not confirmed directly from official ARCA document — verify at arca.gob.ar/gananciasYBienes
- [ ] Employee SS cap for 2026: using December 2025 cap of ARS 3,731,212/month; 2026 monthly caps will be higher (updated monthly); this affects effective SS rate calculation
- [ ] Monotributo: August–January 2026 table amounts differ from February–July 2026 shown; next update August 2026 — all amounts will change
- [ ] IVA implications not quantified: Monotributo = IVA exempt (cannot recover IVA on purchases, cannot invoice IVA to clients); Responsable Inscripto = 21% IVA charged to clients + recoverable input credits. The IVA regime choice has significant cash flow and client-relationship implications
- [ ] Ingresos Brutos (IIBB — provincial gross receipts tax): levied by provinces at 1%–5% of gross revenue; Monotributo contributors may still be subject to IIBB in some provinces. Not modeled.
- [ ] Ganancias 4th Category reinstatement (post-December 2023 law): The history of the exemption/reinstatement creates potential prior-year liability issues for employees who did not pay Ganancias in 2024. Not modeled.
- [ ] Personal assets tax (Bienes Personales) threshold in EUR: ARS 384.7M ÷ 1,645 ARS/EUR ≈ €234k threshold — relevant for any Argentine resident or expat with significant assets
- [ ] Simplified Income Tax Return (Law 27,799): eligibility thresholds (ARS 1 billion income, ARS 10 billion wealth) in EUR terms are ARS 1 billion ÷ 1,645 ≈ €608k and ARS 10 billion ≈ €6.08M respectively — primarily relevant to very high earners
- [ ] The April 2025 exchange rate unification: the IMF agreement and lifting of currency controls may not be permanent; any reversal would require re-evaluation of all EUR-equivalent figures
