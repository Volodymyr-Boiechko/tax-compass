## Personal Income Tax

Cambodia uses a **Tax on Salary (ToS)** system rather than a traditional personal income tax. Cambodian residents are subject to ToS on worldwide employment income; non-residents on Cambodian-source employment income only. Resident = principal place of abode in Cambodia, OR present > 182 days in any 12-month period ending in the current tax year. (EY p.211)

**Important procedural note:** Currently no procedure exists for individuals to file income tax returns on self-employment income. Tax on individuals' income is collected **only through withholding tax**. Employers withhold and remit ToS monthly by the 25th of the following month (e-filing) or 20th (manual filing). No annual ToS/ToFB personal income tax return is required. (EY pp.212–214)

**ToS rates — Residents (monthly taxable salary, from 1 January 2023):**

| Monthly taxable salary (KHR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 1,500,000 | **0%** |
| 1,500,001 – 2,000,000 | **5%** |
| 2,000,001 – 8,500,000 | **10%** |
| 8,500,001 – 12,500,000 | **15%** |
| 12,500,001+ | **20%** |

(EY p.213)

**ToS rate — Non-residents:** Flat **20%** on Cambodian-source employment income. (EY p.212–213)

**Self-employment and business income — annual brackets:**

| Annual taxable profit (KHR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 18,000,000 | **0%** |
| 18,000,001 – 24,000,000 | **5%** |
| 24,000,001 – 102,000,000 | **10%** |
| 102,000,001 – 150,000,000 | **15%** |
| 150,000,001+ | **20%** |

(EY p.212)

**Withholding tax on other income (residents):**

| Income type | Withholding rate |
|---|---|
| Services (to non-self-assessment taxpayers / payments < KHR 50,000) | **15%** |
| Royalties (software licenses, shrink-wrap, digital bundled software — to real regime VAT taxpayers) | **15%** |
| Interest (non-fixed term deposits at domestic banks) | **4%** |
| Interest (fixed term deposits at domestic banks) | **6%** |
| Interest (paid by resident taxpayers except domestic banks) | **15%** |
| Rental income (except payments to real regime VAT taxpayer with VAT invoice) | **10%** |
| Payments from resident/PE to non-resident (WHT on Cambodian-source income) | **14%** |

(EY p.212)

**Capital gains tax (CGT):**
- Flat rate: **20%** on capital gains
- CGT on immovable property: effective **1 January 2026**
- CGT on other types of assets: effective **1 September 2025**
- Capital gains = taxable income from revenue from sale/transfer of capital asset less allowable expenses
- Applicable to resident physical persons and non-resident taxpayers (physical persons or legal entities)

(EY p.212)

**Tax on Fringe Benefits (ToFB):**
- Rate: **20%** of fair market value of fringe benefit
- Covers: private use of vehicles, accommodation, food, utilities, household personnel, low-interest loans, discounted sales, educational assistance, insurance premiums (social security/pension in excess of Labor Law limits), entertainment, shares
- Borne by the beneficiary (employer calculates and withholds)
- Exempt from ToFB: travel allowances home-to-work, food allowances (all employees), Social Security Fund contributions (within legal limits), life and health insurance (all employees), severance pay within Labor Law

(EY p.213)

**Deductions:**
- Allowance for spouse and minor dependent children: **KHR 150,000 per person per month** deductible from taxable salary

**Other taxes:**
- Net worth, gift, inheritance, and estate taxes: **not imposed in Cambodia** (EY p.213)

## Social Security

Two types of social security schemes in Cambodia: (1) pension and (2) occupational risks (ORC + HCC). (EY p.213–214)

**Occupational Risks (ORC) and Healthcare Contribution (HCC):**

| Contribution | Rate | Ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| ORC (occupational risk) | **0.8%** of monthly average wages (employer only) | Max monthly average wage: KHR 1,200,000; min: KHR 400,000 |
| HCC (health contribution) | **2.6%** of monthly average wages (employer only) | Same ceiling KHR 1,200,000 |

- Maximum ORC per employee: ~KHR 9,600/month (0.8% × KHR 1,200,000)
- Maximum HCC per employee: ~KHR 31,200/month (2.6% × KHR 1,200,000)
- Both are employer-only contributions; **not deducted from employee salary**
- ORC and HCC are not refundable

(EY p.214)

**Pension scheme (implemented 1 October 2022 — jointly paid by employer and employee):**

| Period | Employee rate | Employer rate | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| October 2022 – September 2027 | **2%** | **2%** | **4%** |
| October 2027 – September 2032 | **4%** | **4%** | **8%** |
| October 2032 – September 2042 | **5.375%** | **5.375%** | **10.75%** |
| Every additional 10 years thereafter | +1.375% each | +1.375% each | +2.75% |

- Pension is compulsory; voluntary additional contributions allowed
- Pension refundable at age 60

(EY p.214)

**Summary — current employee SS burden (2025–2026):**

| Scheme | Employee rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pension | **2%** (through Sep 2027) | On all wages |
| ORC | 0% employee | Employer-only |
| HCC | 0% employee | Employer-only |
| **Total employee SS** | **2%** | |

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Partnership (Real Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with annual revenue above the simplified or estimated regime thresholds; generally businesses with revenue > KHR 250 million/year
- **Tax base:** Net profit = gross revenue minus deductible business expenses
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual brackets (0%–20%) on taxable profit; withholding at 15% by paying entities applies as advance
- **Social security:** Employer SS (ORC 0.8%, HCC 2.6%); pension contributions apply
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required for real regime taxpayers; VAT rate 10%
- **Key limits:** No ceiling; must register with GDT; monthly filing required

(EY pp.211–212)

### Option B — Small Taxpayer Simplified Regime

- **Who qualifies:** Businesses with annual revenue KHR 60 million – KHR 250 million (~USD 15,000–USD 62,500)
- **Tax base:** Revenue (gross)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Lower flat taxes; quarterly filing
- **Social security:** Applies if employing workers
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT registration requirement at simplified level
- **Key limits:** KHR 250 million annual revenue ceiling

(General Cambodian tax law; EY focuses on real regime)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No specific preferential income tax regime for newcomers or digital nomads described in EY. The 20% flat non-resident rate applies to non-residents' Cambodian-source employment income. (EY pp.211–213)

**Foreign tax credit:** Resident persons who receive foreign-source salary and pay taxes on it under foreign tax law receive a tax credit against their ToS/ToFB if supporting documents are available. (EY p.215)

---

## 2026 Changes

- **Capital gains tax on immovable property effective 1 January 2026:** 20% flat rate on gains from sale/transfer of immovable assets. CGT on other asset types already effective 1 September 2025. (EY p.212)
- **Pension contribution phase-up:** Rate remains at 4% total (2% each employer/employee) through September 2027; next increase to 8% total from October 2027. (EY p.214)
- No changes to ToS brackets (in force since 1 January 2023).
- No changes to non-resident 20% flat rate.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants (no spouse/child deduction).
> **1 EUR ≈ KHR 4,400** (approximate; KHR/USD ~4,050; EUR/USD ~1.08; KHR ~4,374/EUR; using KHR 4,400/EUR for round estimates — verify at nbc.gov.kh).
> Employment scenario: monthly ToS progressive (0%–20%); employee pension 2%.
> B2B scenario: annual self-employment profit brackets (0%–20%); 15% WHT on service payments as advance; pension 2%.

- Monthly ToS: 0%(1.5M) + 5%(0.5M=25k) + 10%(6.5M=650k) + 15%(4M=600k) + 20%(9.5M=1,900k) = KHR 3,175,000/month

- 0% × 1,500,000 = 0

## Known Gaps

- [ ] EUR/KHR exchange rate not stated in EY; KHR 4,400/EUR is an approximation — verify at nbc.gov.kh
- [ ] **Capital gains tax on immovable property (1 January 2026):** CGT at 20% on property gains now in force; specific rules on cost base, allowable deductions, and filing procedure to be confirmed with GDT (gdt.gov.kh)
- [ ] **Self-employment income collection:** EY states "currently no procedure exists for individuals to report income tax on their income" — only withholding operates in practice; sole proprietors registering as real regime taxpayers have different obligations — verify
- [ ] **Pension contribution ceiling:** EY does not state a ceiling for pension contributions; currently 2% employee/2% employer on all wages — verify cap at NSSF (nssf.gov.kh)
- [ ] **15% WHT as advance vs. final tax:** For payments to non-self-assessment taxpayers, 15% WHT may be final — distinction between advance (creditable) and final (not creditable) WHT needs clarification
- [ ] No specific digital nomad visa or remote worker regime documented in EY; Cambodia e-Visa is available but standard 30-day business visa for short-term remote workers
- [ ] **Non-resident ToS at flat 20%:** Applies regardless of income level — significantly higher burden than progressive resident rates at lower income levels
