## Personal Income Tax

Gibraltar operates two alternative income tax systems for residents; the tax authority applies whichever is most beneficial to the taxpayer on final assessment. Tax year runs from **1 July to 30 June**. GIP (Gibraltar Pound) is at parity with GBP. (EY p.502, p.512)

**Residency:** "Ordinarily resident" if present in Gibraltar for 183+ days in a tax year, or more than 300 days total during three consecutive tax years. "Present" means being in Gibraltar at any time during a 24-hour period commencing at midnight. (EY p.502)

**Two alternative tax systems:**
1. **GIB (Gross Income Based) system:** Flat-ish rates applied to gross assessable income; fewer deductions/reliefs; generally lower effective rates for higher earners.
2. **AB (Allowances Based) system:** Progressive rates applied after generous personal allowances; generally more beneficial for lower earners and those with many dependants.
3. **Tax Office applies whichever system produces the lower liability** on final assessment.

**GIB system rates — Income up to GIP 25,000 (resident)** (EY p.508)

| Taxable income (GIP) | Tax rate | Tax due (GIP) | Cumulative (GIP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 10,000 | 6% | 600 | 600 |
| Next 7,000 | 20% | 1,400 | 2,000 |
| Above 17,000 | 28% | — | — |

**GIB system rates — Income above GIP 25,000 (resident)** (EY p.508)

| Taxable income (GIP) | Tax rate | Tax due (GIP) | Cumulative (GIP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 17,000 | 16% | 2,720 | 2,720 |
| Next 8,000 | 19% | 1,520 | 4,240 |
| Next 15,000 | 25% | 3,750 | 7,990 |
| Next 65,000 | 28% | 18,200 | 26,190 |
| Above 105,000 | 25% | — | — |

**Key GIB feature:** Effective tax rate under the GIB system never exceeds 25%. (EY p.508)

**AB system rates** (EY p.508)

| Taxable income (GIP, after allowances) | Tax rate | Tax due (GIP) | Cumulative (GIP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 4,000 | 14% | 560 | 560 |
| Next 12,000 | 17% | 2,040 | 2,600 |
| Above 16,000 | 39% | — | — |

AB system: taxpayers receive a minimum tax credit of GIP 300 or 2% of AB tax payable (whichever is greater). (EY p.508)

**Nonresident rates (AB system):** (EY p.508)

| Taxable income (GIP) | Tax rate | Tax due (GIP) | Cumulative (GIP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 16,000 | 17% | 2,720 | 2,720 |
| Above 16,000 | 39% | — | — |

*Note: Nonresidents present for <30 days in a tax year: no tax on directors' fees, employment income, or self-employment income ancillary to work carried out outside Gibraltar.* (EY p.508–509)

**Other income types:**
- Savings income (dividends from listed stocks, interest income): NOT subject to Gibraltar income tax. (EY p.503)
- Dividends from Gibraltarian companies: taxable only if underlying profits were subject to Gibraltar tax (with tax credit mechanism). (EY p.503)
- No capital gains tax; gains on disposal of real estate taxable in some circumstances (see below). (EY p.504)
- Rental income from Gibraltar property: taxable for both residents and nonresidents. (EY p.503)
- Real estate gains: taxable if person owns ≥5 "taxable properties" (excludes individual's private residence and certain older properties). (EY p.503)
- State pensions: not taxable. Approved pension schemes: tax-free on retirement at 60+ (compulsory at 55 for certain professions). (EY p.504)
- Pension receipts (from approved scheme): taxed at 2.5%. (EY p.504)
- Student income from full-time employment: government has announced restriction to GIP 11,450/year earned outside term times. (EY p.504)

**GIB system deductions:** (EY p.505)
- Approved expenditure on premises (painting, decorating, repairing, enhancing frontage): up to GIP 5,000 (GIB system).
- Mortgage interest on main residential property: up to GIP 1,500.
- First-time buyer deduction: up to GIP 7,500.
- Approved pension contributions: up to GIP 1,500/year.
- Private medical insurance premiums: up to GIP 3,000/year.
- Solar/wind energy installations: up to GIP 6,000 over two years.
- Tax credits: up to GIP 2,000 (electric vehicle charging); 10% of gym membership or personal trainer fees; 10% of private school fees.

**Notes:**
- No wealth tax, inheritance tax, gift tax, or estate duty in Gibraltar. (EY p.511)
- Proprietary trading in securities not taxable. (EY p.503)
- Losses: may be offset against any assessable profits or gains in subsequent years; no carryback. (EY p.509)
- Tax year: 1 July – 30 June; PAYE for employees; self-employed pay 50% installments on 31 January and 30 June. (EY p.512)
- No double tax treaties with most countries; only UK (in force March 2020) and Spain (international agreement ratified 2021). Unilateral relief available for foreign taxes similarly chargeable to Gibraltar tax. (EY p.513)

## Social Security

Social insurance contributions payable on earnings of individuals working in Gibraltar. (EY p.512)

| Contributor | Rate (% of gross earnings) | Minimum (GIP/week) | Maximum (GIP/week) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employer | 18% | 31.98 | 56.22 |
| Employee (under 60) | 10% | 14.34 | 40.79 |
| Employee (60 and over) | — | — | — |
| Self-employed | 20% | 30.45 | 53.55 |

**Notes:**
- No contributions if individual does not receive earnings. Full-time students earning on holiday: exempt. (EY p.512)
- Workers over 60 are exempt from employee social insurance contributions. (EY p.512)
- Self-employed: 20% rate; minimum GIP 30.45/week, maximum GIP 53.55/week (annual max ≈ GIP 2,785).
- Under AB system, employee social insurance contributions (GIP 335) and self-employed SI (GIP 432) are deductible allowances. (EY p.506)
- UK workers seconded to Gibraltar: normally remain subject to UK NI; need valid A1 certificate. (EY p.512)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — GIB System (Self-Employment)
- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals; GIB system generally available.
- **Tax base:** Gross assessable income from self-employment activities.
- **Rate / mechanism:** GIB system rates; effective rate never exceeds 25%. Tax Office applies most beneficial system.
- **Social security:** 20% of gross earnings; minimum GIP 30.45/week, maximum GIP 53.55/week.
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT in Gibraltar.
- **Key limits:** Losses may be offset against future assessable profits; no carryback.

### Option B — AB System (Self-Employment)
- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals; AB system with generous personal allowances.
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus AB system allowances (including GIP 432 self-employed SI allowance + personal allowance GIP 3,455 + other allowances).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates 14%/17%/39% on net income after allowances.
- **Social security:** Same as GIB system.
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT.
- **Key limits:** 39% marginal rate applies above GIP 16,000 of taxable income (after allowances); most taxpayers will not reach this given the generous allowances.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Category 2 (High Net Worth Individuals)
- **Eligibility:** (EY p.509)
  - Available approved residential accommodation exclusively for self/family in Gibraltar.
  - Not resident in Gibraltar for preceding 5 years.
  - Not engaged (and will not be engaged) in trade, business or employment in Gibraltar (except ancillary services based outside Gibraltar or consultancy).
  - Two character references from recognized professionals.
  - Adequate financial standing (in practice >GIP 2 million net worth).
  - Application fee GIP 1,233.
- **Benefit:** Tax assessed on first GIP 118,000 of income only (AB system applied); maximum tax in a full year = GIP 42,380. Income from Gibraltar activities above the cap taxed at 39%.
- **Duration:** Ongoing while conditions are met.
- **Caveats:** Minimum tax GIP 37,000/year (prorated if obtained/expires mid-year). Spouse/children income may be deemed as part of certificate holder's income. Tax benefits for trusts available. (EY p.509)

### HEPSS (High Executives Possessing Specialist Skills)
- **Eligibility:** (EY p.509–510)
  - Skills/experience not available in Gibraltar.
  - Necessary to promote and sustain economic activity of particular economic value to Gibraltar.
  - Must occupy high executive or senior management position.
  - Basic salary >GIP 160,000/year.
  - Approved residential accommodation exclusively for self/family.
  - Not resident or employed in Gibraltar for 3 preceding years (Finance Centre Director may waive).
  - Application fee GIP 1,233.
- **Benefit:** Tax payable limited to first GIP 160,000 of earned income (GIB system applied); maximum tax payable = GIP 39,940.
- **Duration:** Ongoing while conditions are met.

## 2026 Changes

- **EEA/UK right to reside suspension (6 October 2025):** The right of EEA/UK nationals who exercised EEA/UK rights to reside in Gibraltar beyond three months was suspended from 6 October 2025, except for those already holding documentation or with applications submitted before that date. New residency criteria were to be announced from 15 October 2025. (EY p.515)
- **No announced PIT rate changes for the 2025–26 tax year** as of EY content date.
- **Student income restriction:** Government announced restriction of full-time student employment income exemption to GIP 11,450/year earned outside term times (timing not confirmed at EY content date). (EY p.504)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants (personal allowance GIP 3,455 under AB system), no church membership.
> GIP = GBP (parity); EUR/GIP ≈ EUR/GBP ≈ 1 EUR = 0.86 GBP → 1 EUR ≈ GIP 0.86 (approximate 2025); 1 GIP ≈ EUR 1.163.
> Tax year 1 July–30 June; using annual figures.
> "Employment" = gross salary; Tax Office applies whichever of GIB or AB gives lower tax.
> "B2B" = self-employment gross income; self-employed SI 20% (capped at ~GIP 2,785/year); GIB or AB whichever is lower.

**EUR to GIP conversions:**
- €30,000 ≈ GIP 25,800
- €60,000 ≈ GIP 51,600
- €100,000 ≈ GIP 86,000

### Working: GIP 25,800 (≈ €30,000) Employment

**GIB system (income above GIP 25,000 table, since 25,800 > 25,000):**
- First GIP 17,000 at 16% = GIP 2,720
- Next GIP 8,000 at 19% = GIP 1,520
- Next GIP 800 at 25% = GIP 200
- GIB tax = GIP 4,440

**AB system:**
- Allowances: personal GIP 3,455 + employee SI GIP 335 = GIP 3,790
- Net taxable: 25,800 − 3,790 = GIP 22,010
- First GIP 4,000 at 14% = 560; Next GIP 12,000 at 17% = 2,040; Balance GIP 6,010 at 39% = 2,344
- AB tax before credit = GIP 4,944; less credit GIP 300 → GIP 4,644
- GIB is lower (GIP 4,440 vs GIP 4,644) → GIB applies

**Employee SI: 10% of 25,800 = GIP 2,580 (but max GIP 40.79/week × 52 = GIP 2,121)**

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SI (10%, capped at GIP 40.79/week) | 40.79 × 52 | GIP 2,121 |
| Income tax (GIB) | | GIP 4,440 |
| **Total burden** | **2,121 + 4,440** | **GIP 6,561** |
| **Effective rate** | **6,561 / 25,800** | **~25.4%** |

**Working — GIP 51,600 GIB tax:**
- First 17,000 at 16% = 2,720; Next 8,000 at 19% = 1,520; Next 15,000 at 25% = 3,750; Next 11,600 at 28% = 3,248 → GIB tax = GIP 11,238 (corrected)

**Working — GIP 86,000 GIB tax:**
- First 17,000 at 16% = 2,720; Next 8,000 at 19% = 1,520; Next 15,000 at 25% = 3,750; Next 65,000 capped at GIP 105,000 ceiling: 28% × (86,000 − 40,000) = hmm, bracket is "Next 65,000 at 28%": 28% × 46,000 = 12,880 → GIB tax = 2,720 + 1,520 + 3,750 + 12,880 = GIP 20,870 (corrected)

**Key insight:** GIB system caps effective rate at 25% of gross income. For most income levels €30k–€100k, total burden (tax + SI) is approximately 25–30%. This makes Gibraltar one of the most attractive low-tax jurisdictions in Europe.

**Comparison with HEPSS:** A HEPSS executive earning GIP 86,000 would pay max GIP 21,560 (25% effective under HEPSS cap), similar to GIB system result — HEPSS is mainly beneficial for very high earners above GIP 160,000.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/GIP ≈ 0.86 (approximate; GIP = GBP; floating EUR/GBP rate)
- Tax year 1 July–30 June; using annual income figures
- GIB system applied throughout (produces lower tax in most cases for these income levels)
- Employee SI: 10%, capped at GIP 40.79/week (annual max GIP 2,121)
- Self-employed SI: 20%, capped at GIP 53.55/week (annual max GIP 2,785)
- No personal deductions/allowances modeled under GIB system (few deductions available)
- Savings income (dividends, interest) not included — exempt from Gibraltar tax
