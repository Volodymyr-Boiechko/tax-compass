## Personal Income Tax

> Gibraltar offers two alternative tax systems: the Gross Income Based (GIB) system and the Allowances Based (AB) system. The Tax Office applies whichever is most beneficial to the taxpayer. Tax year runs 1 July – 30 June. Residents are taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Gibraltar-source income. No capital gains tax. No inheritance tax. No wealth tax. No estate duty.

**GIB System — for assessable gross income up to GIP 25,000:**

| Taxable income (GIP) | Tax rate % | Tax due (GIP) | Cumulative (GIP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 10,000 | 6 | 600 | 600 |
| Next 7,000 | 20 | 1,400 | 2,000 |
| Above 17,000 | 28 | — | — |

**GIB System — for assessable gross income above GIP 25,000:**

| Taxable income (GIP) | Tax rate % | Tax due (GIP) | Cumulative (GIP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 17,000 | 16 | 2,720 | 2,720 |
| Next 8,000 | 19 | 1,520 | 4,240 |
| Next 15,000 | 25 | 3,750 | 7,990 |
| Next 65,000 | 28 | 18,200 | 26,190 |
| Above 105,000 | 25 | — | — |

*Notable: effective tax rate under GIB system never exceeds 25%. (EY p. 508)*

**AB System (Allowances-Based):**

| Taxable income after allowances (GIP) | Tax rate % | Tax due (GIP) | Cumulative (GIP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 4,000 | 14 | 560 | 560 |
| Next 12,000 | 17 | 2,040 | 2,600 |
| Above 16,000 | 39 | — | — |

*Taxpayers receive a tax credit of GIP 300 or 2% of tax payable (whichever is greater) under the AB system.*

**GIB System deductions:**
- Approved expenditure on premises: max GIP 5,000
- Mortgage interest on main residence: max GIP 1,500
- First-time buyer allowance: up to GIP 7,500
- Pension contributions to approved schemes: up to GIP 1,500/year
- Private medical insurance: up to GIP 3,000/year
- Solar/wind energy installations: up to GIP 6,000 over two years

**Notes:**
- Tax Office automatically applies the most beneficial system
- Low-income earner credit: individuals with income <GIP 11,450 pay no tax; phased out between GIP 11,451–GIP 19,950
- Savings income (dividends from recognized stock exchanges, bank interest) not subject to tax (EY p. 503)
- No capital gains tax; gains from real estate disposal taxable only in specific circumstances (5+ "taxable properties")
- Tax year: 1 July – 30 June

## Social Security

> Gibraltar social insurance applies to employed and self-employed persons working in Gibraltar.

| Contributor | Rate on gross earnings | Minimum/week (GIP) | Maximum/week (GIP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employer | 18% | 31.98 | 56.22 |
| Employee (under 60) | 10% | 14.34 | 40.79 |
| Employee (60 and over) | 0% | — | — |
| Self-employed | 20% | 30.45 | 53.55 |

*(EY p. 512)*

**Income ceiling:** Weekly contributions capped at maximum amounts above
**Notes:**
- No contributions payable if individual does not receive earnings
- Full-time students exempt from social insurance on holiday earnings
- Persons over pension age (60 for women, 65 for men): no employee contributions
- Worker seconded from UK: normally subject to UK social security; A1 certificates recognised (with caveats re EU post-Brexit)
- Self-employed rate 20% applies to net self-employment income

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals in Gibraltar use the GIB or AB system (Tax Office applies most beneficial). Self-assessment applies to self-employed persons.

### Option A — Self-Employed under GIB System
- **Who qualifies:** Any self-employed person with Gibraltar-source business income
- **Tax base:** Gross assessable income (net profits); same GIB brackets as employment
- **Rate / mechanism:** GIB rates (max effective rate never exceeds 25%); Tax Office applies best system
- **Social security:** 20% of self-employment income; weekly maximum GIP 53.55 (annual approx. GIP 2,785)
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT in Gibraltar (Gibraltar does not have a VAT system)
- **Key limits:** Accounts must be prepared for tax year ending 30 June; two advance payments by 31 January and 30 June; business expenses wholly and exclusively incurred are deductible

### Option B — Self-Employed under AB System
- **Who qualifies:** Same as above; Tax Office chooses best system
- **Tax base:** Net income minus AB allowances (personal GIP 3,455 + social insurance GIP 432 + other applicable allowances)
- **Rate / mechanism:** AB rates 14/17/39% on income after allowances; plus GIP 300 or 2% tax credit
- **Social security:** Same 20% self-employed rate
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT in Gibraltar
- **Key limits:** AB system often less favourable at higher incomes due to 39% top rate; GIB generally preferred at higher income levels

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Category 2 — High Net Worth Individuals
- **Eligibility:** Must not have been resident in Gibraltar for 5 preceding years; must have available approved residential accommodation exclusively for own/family use; must not engage in trade/business/employment in Gibraltar (certain incidental activities permitted); adequate financial standing (in practice >GIP 2 million); must submit two character references; pay application fee GIP 1,233
- **Benefit:** Tax payable only on first GIP 118,000 of income; maximum tax payable GIP 42,380/year under AB system; minimum tax payable GIP 37,000/year (prorated if certificate obtained mid-year); tax cap means worldwide income above GIP 118,000 is effectively tax-free in Gibraltar
- **Duration:** Ongoing while conditions met
- **Application deadline:** Apply via Finance Centre Director
- **Caveats:** Any income derived from Gibraltar activities above the cap taxable at 39% under AB system; spouse/children's income may be included if elected; applicable only to non-Gibraltarian income activities (EY p. 509)

### HEPSS — High Executives Possessing Specialist Skills
- **Eligibility:** Must possess skills not available in Gibraltar and necessary to promote economic activity; must occupy senior management positions; minimum salary GIP 160,000/year; must have approved residential accommodation; must not have been resident or employed in Gibraltar in preceding 3 years
- **Benefit:** Tax payable limited to first GIP 160,000 of earned income; maximum annual tax GIP 39,940 under GIB system
- **Duration:** Ongoing while conditions met; application fee GIP 1,233
- **Application deadline:** Apply to Finance Centre Director
- **Caveats:** Fee of GIP 1,233 payable for issuance or renewal (EY p. 509–510)

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 502–516)

- **Standard charge increased to GIP 50,000 for 2026** (from GIP 40,000 in 2025) — applies to individuals who are resident but not solely/principally resident
- **Personal allowance 2026: GIP 15,200** (up from GIP 14,600 in 2025)
- **Tax cap (open market new residents): GIP 60,000** for new residents purchasing open-market property (minimum document duty GIP 50,000); applies for up to 4 years
- **Full worldwide tax cap: GIP 320,000** for 2026 (GIP 320,000 also for 2025)
- **Non-Guernsey source tax cap: GIP 160,000** for 2026 (same as 2025)
- EEA/UK right-to-reside rules changed effective 6 October 2025 — new residency criteria announced by Gibraltar Chief Minister on 15 October 2025
- Employer-provided stock options: from 1 January 2026, tax point is date of vesting, exercise, or 7 years from grant (whichever earlier) — previously at grant date (EY p. 553 cross-reference applies to Guernsey; Gibraltar's own rules as stated in EY p. 502–516)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single person, no dependants, ordinarily resident in Gibraltar, GIB system applied (Tax Office uses most beneficial).
> Gibraltar uses GIP (Gibraltar Pound) = GBP at par; 1 GBP ≈ 1.17 EUR (approximate 2025 rate).
> EUR amounts shown for comparability; GIP amounts are primary.

**Employment at €30,000 (≈ GIP 25,640):**
- GIB system (income above GIP 25,000 table applies):
  1. GIP 17,000 × 16% = GIP 2,720
  2. GIP 8,000 × 19% = GIP 1,520
  3. Remaining GIP 640 × 25% = GIP 160
  4. Total tax = GIP 4,400 ≈ €5,148
  5. Social insurance: GIP 25,640 × 10% = GIP 2,564 ≈ €2,999 (but subject to weekly max ~GIP 40.79 × 52 = GIP 2,121)
  6. Total burden ≈ €5,148 + €2,482 = €7,630
  7. Effective rate = €7,630 / €30,000 = **~25.4%**

**Employment at €60,000 (≈ GIP 51,280):**
- GIB system (above GIP 25,000 table):
  1. GIP 17,000 × 16% = GIP 2,720
  2. GIP 8,000 × 19% = GIP 1,520
  3. GIP 15,000 × 25% = GIP 3,750
  4. GIP 11,280 × 28% = GIP 3,158
  5. Total tax = GIP 11,148 ≈ €13,043
  6. Social insurance: capped at ~GIP 2,121/year ≈ €2,481
  7. Total burden ≈ €15,524; effective rate = **~25.9%**

**Employment at €100,000 (≈ GIP 85,470):**
- GIB system (above GIP 25,000):
  1. GIP 17,000 × 16% = GIP 2,720
  2. GIP 8,000 × 19% = GIP 1,520
  3. GIP 15,000 × 25% = GIP 3,750
  4. GIP 45,470 × 28% = GIP 12,732 (within Next 65,000 band)
  5. Total tax = GIP 20,722 ≈ €24,245
  6. Effective tax rate already = GIP 20,722 / GIP 85,470 = 24.2% (below 25% cap)
  7. SS capped ~GIP 2,121 ≈ €2,481
  8. Total burden ≈ €26,726; effective rate = **~26.7%**

**B2B (self-employed) at €60,000 (≈ GIP 51,280):**
- Same GIB rates; social insurance 20% on self-employment income (capped at GIP 53.55/week = GIP 2,785/year ≈ €3,259)
- Tax same as employment ≈ GIP 11,148 ≈ €13,043
- SS ≈ €3,259 (slightly higher cap for self-employed)
- Total ≈ €16,302; effective rate = **~27.2%**

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | ~€5,148 | ~€2,482 | ~€7,630 | **~25.4%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€5,148 | ~€2,700 | ~€7,848 | **~26.2%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | ~€13,043 | ~€2,482 | ~€15,525 | **~25.9%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | ~€13,043 | ~€3,259 | ~€16,302 | **~27.2%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | ~€24,245 | ~€2,482 | ~€26,727 | **~26.7%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€24,245 | ~€3,259 | ~€27,504 | **~27.5%** |

**Assumptions:**
- GIB system applied (Tax Office selects most beneficial)
- Single person, no dependants, no special allowances
- Employee SS capped at GIP 40.79/week × 52 = GIP 2,121/year; self-employed capped at GIP 53.55/week × 52 = GIP 2,785/year
- GIP/EUR ≈ 1.17 (GIP pegged at par to GBP)
- No VAT in Gibraltar — no VAT cost for self-employed
- Effective GIB tax rate never exceeds 25% of gross assessable income
