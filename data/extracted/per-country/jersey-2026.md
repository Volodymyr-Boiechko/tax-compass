## Personal Income Tax

Jersey (Channel Islands) imposes a flat **20% income tax** on resident and ordinarily resident individuals on worldwide income. There is **no capital gains tax, no inheritance tax, no wealth tax** in Jersey. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). ITIS (Income Tax Instalment System) deductions at source for employment income; returns filed by 31 May (paper) or 31 July (online). (EY pp. 745–751)

### Income Tax Rate

| Taxable income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All taxable income above exemption threshold | **20%** |

**No capital gains tax:** Jersey does not impose tax on capital gains. (EY p. 747)

### Exemption Thresholds (2025) and Marginal Relief

| Category | Exemption threshold (GBP) |
|---|---|
| Single person | **20,700** |
| Married or civil partnership | **33,200** |

**Mechanism:** No income tax arises for taxpayers earning below their applicable exemption threshold. For taxpayers earning above the threshold, two calculations are made:
1. Standard calculation: 20% on all taxable income
2. Alternative: 26% on income that exceeds the threshold only

The lower of the two produces "marginal relief" — a benefit for low-to-middle-income earners where income slightly exceeds the threshold. (EY p. 748)

**Additional allowances** that can be added to the exemption threshold for marginal-rate taxpayers (2025):
- Childcare tax allowance: up to GBP7,850 (can be increased to GBP20,400 for children below school age)
- Second earner's allowance: 100% of lower-earning spouse/partner's earnings, up to GBP8,200 (jointly assessed couples)
- Children's allowance: GBP5,750 per child (single parents or where one parent is wholly incapacitated)
- Qualifying interest payments: interest on loan to purchase main residence, max capital GBP300,000, max relief GBP1,500
- Child deduction for marginal-rate taxpayers: GBP3,850 per child under 16 or over 16 in full-time education

**Pension deductions:** Contributions to approved superannuation fund or retirement annuity contract are deductible, up to GBP50,000 or 100% of relevant earnings (lower of the two). Phase-out begins at income > GBP150,000 (GBP1 relief withdrawn per GBP1 income over GBP150,000). (EY p. 747)

### High-Value Resident Regime

- Tax charged at 20% on first GBP1,250,000 of worldwide income (generating GBP250,000 of tax)
- Additional 1% tax on income exceeding GBP1,250,000
- **Minimum annual tax payment: GBP250,000** (even if 20%/1% calculation produces less)
- Threshold of GBP1,250,000 may increase every five years from 2024 in line with inflation
- High-value residents must purchase freehold property valued at GBP3,500,000+ (or GBP1,750,000 for an apartment) (EY pp. 752–753)

### Investment Income

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (Jersey resident company, taxable at 0%) | 0% (cash/stock dividends from 0%-taxed Jersey companies) |
| Dividends (other) | 20% |
| Interest, royalties, property rental | 20% |
| Property rental expenses | Fully deductible |

**Short-term business visitors (from 2024):** Income tax exemption for visitors who are non-resident for Jersey tax purposes and spend ≤60 days in Jersey per year. (EY p. 746)

## Social Security

Jersey has a compulsory social security scheme. Everyone between school-leaving age and pension age is covered under Class 1 (employed) or Class 2 (self-employed/unemployed). (EY pp. 749–750)

### Class 1 — Employment

| Contribution | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee (primary) | **6%** | On earnings GBP0 – SEL (GBP5,800/month) |
| Employer (secondary) | **6.5%** | On earnings GBP0 – SEL (GBP5,800/month) |
| Employer (secondary, SEL to UEL) | **2.5%** | On earnings GBP5,800 – GBP26,442/month |
| **Total (up to SEL)** | **12.5%** | Employee 6% + Employer 6.5% |

**Standard earnings limit (SEL):** GBP5,800/month (updated 1 January each year)
**Upper earnings limit (UEL):** GBP26,442/month

### Class 2 — Self-Employed

| Contribution | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Self-employed (combined) | **12.5%** | On earnings up to SEL (GBP5,800/month) = combined employer+employee equivalent |
| Above SEL to UEL | **2.5%** | On earnings GBP5,800–GBP26,442/month |

**Maximum monthly Class 2 contribution:** 12.5% × 5,800 + 2.5% × (26,442 − 5,800) = 725 + 516 = GBP1,241/month → annual max = GBP14,892/year

**Reduced rate scheme:** For new/small businesses or low-income self-employed (2023–2024 income below SEL), a fixed rate of GBP241.67/month is available in 2025.

### Long-Term Care Charge

- **Rate:** 1.5% on taxable income (payable in addition to social security)
- **Cap:** Based on social security UEL — cap for 2025: GBP4,759.56/year (= 1.5% × 26,442 × 12 = ~GBP4,760)
- Collected by Taxes Office for the Social Security Department (EY p. 750)

**Summary:**

| Contribution | Employee total | Employer total | Self-employed total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (up to SEL) | 6% | 6.5% | 12.5% |
| Above SEL to UEL | 0% | 2.5% | 2.5% |
| Long-term care | 1.5% (capped at GBP4,760/yr) | — | 1.5% (capped) |

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Sole Trader)

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals carrying on a trade, business, or profession in Jersey
- **Tax base:** Net accounting profits adjusted for tax purposes; current-year basis; capital allowances at 25% reducing balance for machinery/equipment; 10% for greenhouses
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat **20% income tax** on net profit above the GBP20,700 single exemption threshold; marginal relief available if income is slightly above threshold
- **Social security:** Class 2 contributions — 12.5% up to SEL (GBP5,800/month) + 2.5% above SEL to UEL
- **Long-term care:** 1.5% of taxable income, capped at ~GBP4,760/year
- **VAT obligations:** Jersey has its own Goods and Services Tax (GST) at **5%**; registration threshold GBP300,000/year of international or Jersey-taxed supplies; very low rate
- **Loss treatment:** Noncorporate business losses may be carried forward indefinitely if business continues; current-year losses may be offset against other income or carried back against prior-year profits from same trade

### Option B — No simplified regime

Jersey has no separate presumptive or simplified taxation regime for self-employed individuals; all self-employed file under the standard system with actual profit calculations.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### High-Value Resident Regime

- **Eligibility:** Individuals admitted to Jersey on **economic grounds** by the Housing Minister; must demonstrate they will make a major contribution to Jersey tax revenues
- **Benefit:** Flat tax: 20% on first GBP1,250,000 + 1% on excess, with minimum annual payment of GBP250,000
- **Property requirement:** Freehold property purchase valued at GBP3,500,000+ (GBP1,750,000 for apartment)
- **Note:** This is effectively a territorial/capped tax for ultra-high-net-worth individuals; on income of GBP5M+, effective rate is well below 20% (EY pp. 752–753)

### New Unilateral Double Tax Relief (from 2024)

- **Benefit:** Jersey residents taxed on employment income from working in another jurisdiction (without a DTT) can receive relief against their Jersey income tax
- **Maximum relief:** Lower of foreign tax paid or the standard Jersey rate (20%)
- Expands effective relief to non-treaty countries (EY p. 752)

### Non-Ordinarily Resident Status

- **Eligibility:** Resident but not ordinarily resident in Jersey (present > 6 months but not meeting "ordinarily resident" criteria)
- **Benefit:** Taxed on Jersey-source income and non-Jersey-source income **remitted to Jersey**; non-remitted foreign income exempt
- **Note:** This can be valuable for individuals with significant foreign investment income who do not remit it to Jersey (EY p. 745)

## 2026 Changes

- **From 2026: Individual tax assessments** — all individuals will be independently assessed (joint filing option retained); shift from prior system where married couples were jointly assessed by default (EY p. 750)
- **Frozen 2019 tax liability resolution by September 2026:** Prior-year basis taxpayers' frozen 2019 tax liability must be registered for payment plan or lump-sum by 30 September 2026 (EY p. 751)
- **Short-term business visitor exemption (from 2024):** Income tax exemption confirmed for non-residents spending ≤60 cumulative days in Jersey per year (EY p. 746)
- **New unilateral double tax relief (from 2024):** Employment income from non-treaty jurisdictions now eligible for relief against Jersey income tax (EY p. 752)
- **No rate changes announced** — flat 20% rate and GBP20,700/GBP33,200 exemption thresholds confirmed for 2025 (verify 2026 adjustments at gov.je)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **2026 exemption threshold:** The GBP20,700 single threshold is confirmed for 2025. Jersey adjusts thresholds periodically; verify 2026 amount at gov.je.
- [ ] **Marginal relief complexity:** At income just above the exemption threshold, the marginal relief calculation (26% on excess vs 20% on all) benefits low earners. At GBP25,640 (€30k), the standard 20% calculation is lower than the 26% marginal calculation, so standard applies — there is no marginal relief at this level. Marginal relief is only beneficial where 26% × (income − threshold) < 20% × income, i.e., income < threshold × 26/6 ≈ GBP89,700; verify crossover point.
- [ ] **Self-employed GST (5%):** Jersey GST at 5% with a GBP300,000 registration threshold is very low and unlikely to affect most freelancers. At €60k and €100k gross, registration is not required.
- [ ] **Individual assessment from 2026:** From 2026, all individuals assessed separately (joint option remains). This may affect couples who previously benefited from joint marginal relief calculation using the GBP33,200 married threshold.
- [ ] **Long-term care charge ceiling:** Cap for 2025 is GBP4,759.56/year. For incomes above ~GBP317,304/year (where 1.5% × income = GBP4,760), the long-term care charge is effectively capped and becomes a decreasing percentage of income.
- [ ] **0% dividend rate:** Dividends from Jersey-resident companies taxed at 0% corporate rate are received tax-free by shareholders. This creates potential planning opportunities for owner-managed businesses (retain at 0% corporate, extract as dividends at 0%). Verify current rules with local counsel as anti-avoidance rules may apply.
