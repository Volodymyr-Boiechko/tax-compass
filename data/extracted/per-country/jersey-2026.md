## Personal Income Tax

> Jersey has a flat 20% personal income tax rate. Alternatively, marginal relief applies at the same effective rate via an exemption threshold system for lower and middle incomes. Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income. No capital gains tax. No wealth tax. No estate tax.

| Taxable income (GBP) | Rate |
|---|---|
| All taxable income | 20% |

**Alternative: Marginal relief (exemption threshold method)**
- Income tax exemption thresholds (2025):
  - Single person: GBP 20,700
  - Married / civil partnership: GBP 33,200
- For income exceeding the exemption threshold: two calculations performed — (a) 26% on income above threshold, OR (b) 20% on total income — the lower result applies
- "Marginal relief" = difference between the two calculations; ensures low/middle earners benefit

**High-value resident regime:**
- Tax at 20% on first GBP 1,250,000 of worldwide income → GBP 250,000 of tax
- 1% on income above GBP 1,250,000
- Minimum tax payment: GBP 250,000/year
- Expected to purchase freehold property with value exceeding GBP 3,500,000 (or GBP 1,750,000 for an apartment) (EY p. 753)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Pension contributions to approved superannuation/retirement annuity contracts: deductible up to lower of GBP 50,000 or 100% of relevant earnings; phased-out above GBP 150,000 (GBP 1 withdrawn per GBP 1 of income above GBP 150,000)
- Child deduction (marginal-rate taxpayers): GBP 3,850 per child under 16 (or 16+ in full-time education)
- Childcare tax allowance: up to GBP 7,850 (extendable to GBP 20,400 for pre-school children)
- Second earner's allowance (jointly assessed): 100% of spouse/civil partner earnings, capped at GBP 8,200
- Qualifying interest payments on main residence loan (capital up to GBP 300,000): max relief GBP 1,500
- Benefits in kind: first GBP 250 exempt; health insurance scheme benefits exempt

**Notes:**
- No capital gains tax
- No wealth or estate tax (stamp duty payable on probate)
- Dividends from Jersey resident companies at 0% (standard CIT rate is 0%)
- Property rental income taxed at up to 20% (same schedule)
- Land transaction tax: 0–11% on sales of shares in land-holding companies
- Long-term care charge: 1.5% on taxable income, capped at social security UEL; 2025 cap GBP 4,759.56/year
- From 2026: all individuals assessed independently (joint assessment removed — joint filing option available for those who previously filed jointly) (EY p. 750)

## Social Security

> Jersey has a compulsory social security scheme with Class 1 (employed) and Class 2 (self-employed) contributions.

**Class 1 (Employed):**
- Employee: 6% primary contributions + some secondary contribution arrangements
- Employer: 6.5% secondary contributions
- Standard Earnings Limit (SEL): GBP 5,800/month
- Upper Earnings Limit (UEL): GBP 26,442/month
- Above SEL and up to UEL: employer pays 2.5% (secondary only)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed (Class 2) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Up to SEL (GBP 5,800/month) | 6% | 6.5% | 12.5% |
| SEL to UEL (GBP 26,442/month) | — | 2.5% | 2.5% |
| Above UEL | — | — | — |

**Income ceiling (annual):** SEL GBP 69,600/year; UEL GBP 317,304/year
**Notes:**
- Class 2 self-employed rate of 12.5% combines employee + employer equivalent up to SEL
- Long-term care charge: 1.5% on taxable income up to UEL (cap approx. GBP 4,760/year)
- New business fixed rate: GBP 241.67/month (2025) for recently established businesses applying for reduced contributions
- Totalization agreements in force with many jurisdictions including UK, Guernsey, Ireland, France, USA (EY p. 750)

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals in Jersey are assessed on business profits at the flat 20% rate. Class 2 social security contributions apply.

### Option A — Self-Employed Sole Trader
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on trade, business, or profession in Jersey
- **Tax base:** Net accounting profits adjusted for tax purposes; capital allowances at 25% reducing balance for machinery/equipment; 10% for greenhouses; depreciation not deductible
- **Rate / mechanism:** 20% flat rate on net taxable income (or marginal rate if income below exemption threshold GBP 20,700)
- **Social security:** Class 2: 12.5% up to SEL (GBP 5,800/month); 2.5% above SEL up to UEL; plus long-term care 1.5% up to UEL
- **VAT obligations:** Jersey does not have VAT (Goods and Services Tax / GST at 5% applies above GBP 300,000 annual turnover for most businesses)
- **Key limits:** Business losses: carried forward indefinitely (continuing business); current year losses can offset other income; prior year losses: carry back one year; advance payments based on prior year liability

### Option B — Incorporation (Jersey Company)
- **Who qualifies:** Any entrepreneur
- **Tax base:** Company profits at 0% CIT (standard rate); financial services companies 10%; utility companies 20%
- **Rate / mechanism:** Director salary at 20% PIT; retained profits at 0% CIT; dividends to Jersey-resident shareholders taxed as personal income at 20%
- **Social security:** Normal Class 1 applies to director salary
- **VAT/GST obligations:** GST 5% if turnover above GBP 300,000
- **Key limits:** Complex shareholder taxation rules — value extraction from company (including loan repayments, share redemptions) may be taxed; professional advice recommended (EY p. 747)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### High-Value Resident Regime
- **Eligibility:** Individuals granted permission to reside in Jersey on economic grounds by the Housing Minister; expected to purchase freehold property >GBP 3,500,000 (or flat >GBP 1,750,000); Housing Minister must be satisfied that residency will make major contribution to island's tax revenues
- **Benefit:** Tax at 20% on first GBP 1,250,000 of worldwide income; only 1% tax on income above GBP 1,250,000; minimum annual tax GBP 250,000 (effective rate falls well below 20% for very high earners)
- **Duration:** Ongoing while resident status maintained; status lost upon emigration
- **Application deadline:** Application to Housing Minister; individual consideration required
- **Caveats:** GBP 1,250,000 threshold may increase every 5 years from 2024 in line with inflation (EY p. 753); standard Jersey social security contributions still apply

### New Unilateral Relief (from 2024)
- **Eligibility:** Jersey residents taxed on employment income from working in another jurisdiction
- **Benefit:** Foreign income tax credit available against Jersey income tax liability even where no double tax treaty exists; relief limited to lower of: foreign tax paid, or Jersey standard rate (20%) on that income
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Application deadline:** Claimed annually on tax return
- **Caveats:** Only available for jurisdictions with which Jersey does not have a full DTT; maximum credit = 20% (EY p. 752)

### Short-Term Business Visitor Exemption (from 2024)
- **Eligibility:** Individuals not resident in Jersey for income tax purposes; present in Jersey for 60 days or less in the calendar year (cumulative)
- **Benefit:** Exemption from Jersey income tax on salary and associated remuneration while working in Jersey
- **Duration:** Up to 60 cumulative days per year
- **Application deadline:** Employer must operate simplified payroll rules; employee exemption applies automatically if conditions met
- **Caveats:** Social security contributions may still apply; professional advice recommended for threshold (EY p. 746)

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 745–754)

- **Independent assessment from 2026:** All individuals assessed independently from 2026; married persons no longer automatically jointly assessed (joint filing option still available for those previously jointly assessed) (EY p. 750)
- Frozen 2019 tax liability (prior-year basis transition): Jersey residents who had frozen 2019 tax liability must register for payment by 30 September 2026 (lump sum by September 2026 or payment plan commencing 2027) (EY p. 751)
- Exemption thresholds expected to be updated (2025 thresholds: single GBP 20,700; married GBP 33,200)
- GST rate: 5% standard; no changes announced for 2026

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, ordinarily resident in Jersey, standard deductions only.
> Currency: GBP. 1 GBP ≈ 1.17 EUR (approximate 2025 rate). EUR amounts are approximate conversions.
> Marginal rate method applied where beneficial; otherwise flat 20%.

**Employment at €30,000 (≈ GBP 25,641):**
- Income GBP 25,641 vs exemption threshold GBP 20,700
- Option (a): (GBP 25,641 − GBP 20,700) × 26% = GBP 4,941 × 26% = GBP 1,285
- Option (b): GBP 25,641 × 20% = GBP 5,128
- Marginal relief applies: use lower = GBP 1,285 ≈ €1,503
- Social security: GBP 25,641 × 6% = GBP 1,538 (within SEL GBP 69,600) ≈ €1,800
- Long-term care: GBP 25,641 × 1.5% = GBP 385 ≈ €450
- Total = €1,503 + €1,800 + €450 = €3,753
- Effective rate = €3,753 / €30,000 = **12.5%**

**Employment at €60,000 (≈ GBP 51,282):**
- GBP 51,282 > GBP 20,700: flat 20% applies (20% always ≤ 26% marginal on excess above threshold at this income level: check: GBP 51,282 × 20% = GBP 10,256; (GBP 51,282 − GBP 20,700) × 26% = GBP 30,582 × 26% = GBP 7,951 → marginal calculation lower)
- Tax = GBP 7,951 ≈ €9,302 (marginal method better)
- SS: GBP 51,282 × 6% = GBP 3,077 ≈ €3,600
- Long-term care: GBP 51,282 × 1.5% = GBP 769 ≈ €900
- Total = €9,302 + €3,600 + €900 = €13,802
- Effective rate = €13,802 / €60,000 = **23.0%**

**Employment at €100,000 (≈ GBP 85,470):**
- Marginal: (GBP 85,470 − GBP 20,700) × 26% = GBP 64,770 × 26% = GBP 16,840; flat: GBP 85,470 × 20% = GBP 17,094 → use lower = GBP 16,840 ≈ €19,703
- SS: GBP 69,600 (SEL) × 6% = GBP 4,176; above SEL: no employee contribution → SS = GBP 4,176 ≈ €4,886
- Long-term care: GBP 85,470 × 1.5% = GBP 1,282 (below UEL cap GBP 4,760) ≈ €1,500
- Total = €19,703 + €4,886 + €1,500 = €26,089
- Effective rate = €26,089 / €100,000 = **26.1%**

**B2B (self-employed) at €60,000 (≈ GBP 51,282 revenue):**
1. Business costs ~10% = GBP 5,128; net profit = GBP 46,154
2. Tax (marginal method): (GBP 46,154 − GBP 20,700) × 26% = GBP 25,454 × 26% = GBP 6,618 ≈ €7,743 (check flat: GBP 46,154 × 20% = GBP 9,231 → marginal lower)
3. Class 2 SS: GBP 46,154 × 12.5% = GBP 5,769 (within SEL GBP 69,600) ≈ €6,750
4. Long-term care: GBP 46,154 × 1.5% = GBP 692 ≈ €810
5. Total = €7,743 + €6,750 + €810 = €15,303
6. Effective rate = €15,303 / €60,000 = **25.5%**

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, ordinarily resident, no dependants
- Marginal rate method applied where it produces a lower result than flat 20%
- Employee SS: 6% up to SEL (GBP 69,600/year); no employee contribution above SEL
