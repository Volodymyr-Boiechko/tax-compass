## Personal Income Tax

> Progressive income tax administered by LRA (Lesotho Revenue Authority). Tax year runs 1 April to 31 March. Citizen residents and permanent residents taxed on worldwide income (except employment income sourced and taxed abroad). Expatriate tax residents taxed on worldwide income, but not on property income from foreign sources or income from disposals of foreign investment assets. Nonresidents taxed on Lesotho-source remuneration only. Married persons taxed separately.

### Resident Individual Rates

| Taxable income — Exceeding (LSL) | Taxable income — Not exceeding (LSL) | Rate % |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 74,040 | 20 |
| 74,040 | — | 30 |

**Non-refundable tax credit:** LSL 970 per month (LSL 11,640 per year) — directly subtracted from tax after applying marginal rates (EY p.868)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Non-refundable tax credit of LSL 11,640 per year (LSL 970/month) deducted from tax liability
- Expenses directly related to employment are deductible; no deductions for personal expenses (clothing, commuting, income tax, capital expenses, gifts, fines, insurance premiums to nonresident insurers)
- Business deductions: expenses incurred in earning taxable income deductible, except capital in nature
- Mortgage interest deductible in general (taxed with other income at Rates)
- First LSL 500 of interest from a single savings account at resident financial institutions: exempt

**Notes:**
- War and disability pension benefits: tax-exempt
- Agricultural income: tax-exempt
- Gains from sale of personal assets: not taxable provided not used in production of income
- Gains from business and investment assets: included in normal taxable income
- Resident individuals: not subject to tax on dividends
- Foreign-source employment income exempt if subject to tax in the foreign country
- Fringe benefit tax imposed on employer (not employee) on non-monetary benefits
- Tax returns must be filed within three months after end of tax year (i.e., within three months of 31 March)
- Losses may be carried forward indefinitely and offset against income of similar nature

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employment / Business Income (Same Rate Scale)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual earning self-employment or business income from a source within Lesotho or a source deemed to be within Lesotho
- **Tax base:** All profits or gains from business, including capital gains; less exempt income and allowable deductions
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates as employment income (20% / 30%); non-refundable tax credit of LSL 11,640/year applies
- **Social security:** None
- **VAT obligations:** VAT applies; registration threshold not specified in EY individual tax section
- **Key limits:** Business must arise from a Lesotho source; losses carry forward indefinitely (same source)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Tax Treatment
- **Eligibility:** Tax-resident expatriates
- **Benefit:** Not taxed on property income derived from foreign sources or income from disposals of foreign investment assets generating foreign-source income
- **Duration:** Ongoing while expatriate status applies
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Citizen residents and permanent residents do not benefit from this exclusion — taxed on worldwide income
