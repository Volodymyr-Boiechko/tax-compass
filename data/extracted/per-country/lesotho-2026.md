## Personal Income Tax

Lesotho taxes citizen residents and permanent residents on worldwide income (with exception: employment income sourced and taxed abroad is exempt). Tax-resident expatriates are taxed on worldwide income, except property income derived from foreign sources and income from disposal of investment assets generating foreign-source income. Nonresidents are taxable on Lesotho-source remuneration income only. Tax year runs 1 April to 31 March. Individual tax returns due within 3 months of the tax year-end (or 3 months of financial year-end if different). Employers withhold tax from employees monthly. Married persons taxed separately. (EY pp. 867–869)

### Income Tax Brackets (Residents)

| Annual taxable income (LSL) | Rate | Tax due (LSL) | Cumulative tax (LSL) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 74,040 | **20%** | 14,808 | 14,808 |
| Above 74,040 | **30%** | — | — |

(EY p. 868)

**Tax credit:** A non-refundable tax credit of **LSL970/month** (LSL11,640/year) is available to all individuals with taxable income. The credit is subtracted directly from the computed tax. This effectively creates a zero-tax band for income up to approximately LSL58,200/year (= 11,640 / 0.20).

**Effective zero-rate threshold:** LSL58,200/year (~USD3,230/year) — income below this level has zero net tax after the credit.

**Capital gains:** Gains or losses from sale of business and investment assets are included in normal taxable income; gains from sale of personal assets (not used to produce income) are NOT taxable. (EY p. 868)

**Dividends:** Resident individuals are NOT subject to tax on dividends received. (EY p. 868)

**Interest income:** Taxable; however the first LSL500 of interest from a single savings account is exempt. (EY p. 868)

**Foreign employment income:** Exempt if the income is chargeable to tax in the foreign country. (EY p. 868)

### Deductions

**Employment deductions:** Only expenses directly related to employment are deductible. No deductions for personal expenses (clothing, commuting), income tax, gifts, fines, or insurance premiums to nonresident insurers.

**Business deductions:** Generally, expenses incurred in earning taxable income are deductible, except capital expenditures.

### Withholding Taxes on Nonresidents

| Income type | WHT rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends, interest, royalties, natural resource payments, management fees | **25%** |
| Payments for services | **10%** |
| Payments to resident contractors | **5%** |
| Interest paid to residents | **10%** |

## Social Security

**Lesotho does not impose any social security taxes.** There are no mandatory employee or employer social security contributions. (EY p. 869)

**Fringe benefit tax:** Imposed on the employer (not the employee) based on the taxable value of non-monetary benefits provided to employees. This is an employer-side cost, not a deduction from employee wages.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals and sole traders deriving income from Lesotho sources
- **Tax base:** All profits and gains from business, including capital gains on business and investment assets; business income = total profits and gains minus exempt income and allowable deductions
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same two-rate structure (20% on first LSL74,040; 30% above) + tax credit of LSL11,640/year
- **Social security:** None mandatory
- **VAT obligations:** VAT in Lesotho; registration required if taxable supplies exceed threshold
- **Loss treatment:** Losses may be carried forward indefinitely; offset against income of similar nature in subsequent years
- **Key limits:** Business income must arise from a Lesotho source or source deemed to be within Lesotho

### Option B — No separate simplified regime

Lesotho has no separate presumptive or flat-rate regime for small self-employed individuals; all use the standard two-rate structure.

---

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Foreign Tax Credit

- Tax-resident expatriates taxed on worldwide income but receive a **foreign tax credit** against their Lesotho income tax for income tax borne directly or indirectly with respect to any foreign income subject to Lesotho tax
- Maximum credit = Lesotho income tax on the foreign income (credit cannot exceed domestic tax on that income)

### No Dedicated Newcomer Tax Regime

Lesotho does not have a remittance basis or new-resident exemption regime.

### Foreign Employment Income Exemption

- Employment income sourced in and taxed in a foreign country is exempt from Lesotho tax — relevant for employees who work abroad temporarily while remaining Lesotho residents

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants.
> **Tax credit: LSL11,640/year** deducted from computed income tax.
> **1 EUR ≈ LSL 20.7** (approximate; pegged to South African Rand at 1:1; ZAR/EUR ≈ 0.048 → LSL/EUR ≈ 20.7).
> No social security contributions (none in Lesotho).
> Employment and B2B use the same rate structure.

**Working for €60,000 (LSL1,242,000):**
- Tax: 14,808 + 30% × (1,242,000 − 74,040) = 14,808 + 30% × 1,167,960 = 14,808 + 350,388 = 365,196; credit −11,640 = **353,556; effective 28.5%**

**Working for €100,000 (LSL2,070,000):**
- Tax: 14,808 + 30% × (2,070,000 − 74,040) = 14,808 + 30% × 1,995,960 = 14,808 + 598,788 = 613,596; credit −11,640 = **601,956; effective 29.1%**

**Effective rate asymptote:** As income rises, the effective rate approaches but never exceeds the 30% marginal rate. The 20% band and the tax credit both produce rates below 30% at all income levels. At very high incomes, the effective rate converges to ~29.9%.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/LSL = 20.7 (LSL pegged to ZAR at 1:1; verify ZAR rate)
- Single person, no dependants; tax credit LSL11,640/year
- No deductions assumed (no business expenses for employment; no allowances)
- No social security contributions (none exist)
- Employment and B2B produce identical effective rates (same brackets, no SS differentiation)
- Employer fringe benefit tax on non-cash benefits not shown (employer-side cost)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **LSL/EUR conversion:** The Lesotho Loti (LSL) is pegged to the South African Rand (ZAR) at 1:1. EUR/LSL depends on EUR/ZAR rate, which fluctuates. Verify current rate at centralbank.org.ls.
- [ ] **Tax credit phase-out:** EY does not indicate a taper on the LSL11,640 tax credit. If it is a flat non-refundable credit with no income phase-out, it applies equally at all income levels — this is what the calculation above assumes.
- [ ] **VAT threshold:** EY does not specify the VAT registration threshold for Lesotho. Self-employed individuals above the threshold must register and charge VAT. Verify with Lesotho Revenue Authority (lra.org.ls).
- [ ] **Business income capital gains inclusion:** Capital gains on business and investment assets are included in taxable income and taxed at the same 20%/30% rates. For self-employed individuals disposing of business assets, the gain is added to regular income and taxed progressively.
- [ ] **Dividends exempt:** Resident individuals receiving dividends from Lesotho companies are not subject to income tax on those dividends. This makes owner-managed businesses potentially tax-efficient — retain and distribute profits without individual dividend tax.
- [ ] **Tax year:** Lesotho's tax year is 1 April to 31 March — not calendar year. This affects timing of income and deductions. The brackets above are annual amounts based on the tax year, not calendar year.
- [ ] **No social security:** Lesotho has no mandatory employee or employer social security contribution system. This significantly reduces the total employment cost compared to other African countries (e.g., Kenya, South Africa). There is no public pension or health insurance levy.
