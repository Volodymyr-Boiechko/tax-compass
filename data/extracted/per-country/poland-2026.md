## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (skala podatkowa) for residents taxed on worldwide income. Tax year = calendar year.
Filing deadline: 30 April of the following year (pre-filled PIT-37/PIT-36 available via e-Tax portal).
Three main tax regimes for self-employed; see Self-Employment Regimes.

**Progressive PIT brackets — from 1 January 2025** (EY p.1261)

| Taxable income (PLN) | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| PLN 1 – 120,000 | 12% | Tax-free threshold PLN 30,000 applies (see below) |
| Over PLN 120,000 | 32% | On the excess only |

**Tax-free threshold:** PLN 30,000 — effective as a deduction from the 12% bracket (i.e., tax on
the first PLN 30,000 = PLN 0; tax = 12% × income − PLN 3,600 for income ≤ PLN 120,000).

**32% bracket formula:** PLN 10,800 + 32% × (income − PLN 120,000)

**Investment income** (dividends, interest, capital gains from shares): flat 19%; withheld at source.
(EY p.1260–1261)

**Rental income** (private, since 2023): lump-sum only:
- 8.5% up to PLN 100,000/year
- 12.5% on excess over PLN 100,000/year
(EY p.1260)

**Capital gains from real estate sale:** 19%, exempt if held >5 years from end of acquisition year
(6 months for other property). (EY p.1261)

**Solidarity tax:** 4% on total income exceeding PLN 1,000,000 in a year (additional levy on top of
32% bracket; applies to employment + business income). (EY p.1262)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Basic employee deductible cost (koszty uzyskania przychodu): PLN 250/month × 12 = PLN 3,000/year
  (PLN 300/month if commuting from another location = PLN 3,600/year) (EY p.1259)
- Extended 50% deductible costs for creative/IP work (software, journalism, R&D, etc.): up to
  PLN 120,000/year; requires copyright transfer and specific fields of activity (EY p.1259)
- Charitable donations: up to 6% of taxable income (EY p.1261)
- Internet access: up to PLN 760/year for two consecutive years (EY p.1261)
- **Youth exemption (zero PIT for under-26):** Employment income up to PLN 85,528/year exempt for
  taxpayers aged ≤26 (EY p.1259–1260)

**Notes:**
- Healthcare contributions (9% or 4.9%) are NOT deductible from PIT base since 2022 (EY p.1263)
- Social insurance contributions ARE deductible from PIT base for employed workers
- Married couples may file jointly; same progressive scale applied to half the combined income × 2

## Social Security

> Employer and employee contributions calculated on gross salary. Annual cap applies to retirement
> and disability. Healthcare contribution is levied separately and is not deductible from PIT.
> All rates apply to gross remuneration. (EY p.1262–1263)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Retirement (emerytalne) | 9.76% | 9.76% |
| Disability (rentowe) | 1.50% | 6.50% |
| Sickness (chorobowe) | 2.45% | — |
| Industrial injuries (wypadkowe) | — | 0.67–3.33%* |
| Labor Fund (FP) | — | 2.45% |
| Employees Guaranteed Fund (FGSP) | — | 0.10% |
| **Total social insurance** | **~13.71%** | **~20.48–23.14%** |
| Healthcare (NFZ) | 9% of base** | — |

*Sector-dependent; 1.67% approximate average. (EY p.1262)
**Healthcare base = gross − employee social insurance contributions. NOT deductible from PIT since 2022. (EY p.1263)

**Annual contribution cap (retirement + disability):** PLN 260,190 (2025) = 30 × projected average monthly salary (EY p.1263). Above the cap: retirement and disability contributions cease; sickness continues.

**Self-employed healthcare note:** Rates differ by tax regime — see Self-Employment Regimes below.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Ryczałt od Przychodów Ewidencjonowanych (Registered Revenue Lump Sum)

- **Who qualifies:** JDG (jednoosobowa działalność gospodarcza — sole proprietorship) with prior-year
  revenue ≤ EUR 2,000,000. Cannot be used if switching from the same client that was an employer in
  the same year (anti-avoidance rule).
- **Tax base:** **Gross revenue** — no expense deductions allowed. Social ZUS contributions are
  deductible from the revenue before applying the rate. (podatki.gov.pl)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Rate depends on PKWiU activity classification. **Key rates:**

  | Rate | Activity |
  |---|---|
  | 2% | Trade in food/agricultural produce |
  | 3% | Trade, catering (food only) |
  | 5.5% | Manufacturing, construction |
  | 8.5% | Most service activities; IT support/helpdesk (PKWiU 62.02 if advisory/mgmt only) |
  | **12%** | **Software development, programming, IT services (PKWiU 62.01.x, 62.02, 62.09)** |
  | 14% | Medical services, architectural services |
  | 15% | Financial advisory, certain consultancy |
  | 17% | Certain personal services, representation |

  Source: podatki.gov.pl; the 12% rate for IT confirmed since 2022 reform.

- **Social security (ZUS) for JDG — 2025:**

  | Contribution | Rate | Min base/month | Monthly min (PLN) | Annual min (PLN) |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | Retirement | 19.52% | PLN 5,203.80 | 1,016 | 12,192 |
  | Disability | 8.00% | PLN 5,203.80 | 416 | 4,992 |
  | Sickness (optional) | 2.45% | PLN 5,203.80 | 127 | 1,524 |
  | Accident | ~1.67% | PLN 5,203.80 | 87 | 1,044 |
  | Labor Fund | 2.45% | PLN 5,203.80 | 127 | 1,524 |
  | **Total social (with sickness)** | **~34.09%** | | **~1,773** | **~21,276** |

  Minimum base = 60% × projected average monthly salary = 60% × PLN 8,673 = PLN 5,203.80
  (derived from EY cap: PLN 260,190 / 30 = PLN 8,673/month)

- **Healthcare contribution (Ryczałt) — 2025:** Fixed based on annual revenue bracket:

  | Annual revenue | Monthly healthcare base | Monthly contribution | Annual total |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | ≤ PLN 60,000 | 60% × avg salary = PLN 5,204 | 9% × 5,204 = PLN 468 | PLN 5,620 |
  | PLN 60,000 – 300,000 | 100% × avg salary = PLN 8,673 | 9% × 8,673 = PLN 781 | PLN 9,367 |
  | > PLN 300,000 | 180% × avg salary = PLN 15,611 | 9% × 15,611 = PLN 1,405 | PLN 16,861 |

  Source: woborders.agency (2025 data, confirmed by zus.pl reform notice)

- **VAT obligations:** Must register for VAT once revenue exceeds PLN 200,000/year; standard rate
  23% on services. Optional registration from day 1 if invoicing B2B (recommended).
- **Key limits:** Revenue ceiling EUR 2,000,000; cannot combine Ryczałt with other tax forms in same year.
  New JDG may use "ulga na start" (6-month social ZUS waiver) + "Mały ZUS Plus" (2 years reduced ZUS).

### Option B — Podatek Liniowy (Flat 19% Tax)

- **Who qualifies:** JDG; no revenue ceiling. Cannot receive income from former employer in same tax year
  under the same contract category.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (revenue minus allowable business expenses). All reasonable business costs
  deductible. Social ZUS also deductible.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat 19% on net profit. No tax-free threshold (PLN 30,000 exemption does NOT
  apply). Solidarity tax (4%) still applies above PLN 1M.
- **Social security:** Same ZUS social contributions as Ryczałt (PLN ~21,276/year at minimum base).
- **Healthcare (Liniowy):** 4.9% of net profit (revenue − expenses − social ZUS).
  Minimum healthcare: 9% × 75% of minimum wage = 9% × PLN 3,499.50 = PLN 315/month = PLN 3,780/year.
- **VAT:** Same as Ryczałt.
- **Key limits:** Optimal when expenses > ~35-40% of revenue (where Ryczałt 12% base reduction advantage
  is lost) or for high earners who need expense deductibility.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### IP Box (Ulga IP Box — Innovation Box)

- **Eligibility:** JDG taxpayers using Liniowy (19%) who generate income from qualifying intellectual
  property: software copyrights, patents, utility models, integrated circuit topographies. Requires
  keeping a detailed IP income/cost register.
- **Benefit:** 5% preferential tax rate on IP-sourced income (instead of 19% Liniowy). Particularly
  powerful for software developers who own copyright to their work.
- **Duration:** Ongoing, annual election.
- **Application deadline:** Must elect IP Box in the annual tax return; keep qualifying records throughout
  the year. Cannot combine with Ryczałt.
- **Caveats:** Strict documentation requirements; IP must be created/developed/improved by the
  taxpayer's own activity; income tracking must be maintained per-project; risk of disqualification
  by tax authority audit if records incomplete.

### Return to Poland Relief (Ulga na Powrót)

- **Eligibility:** Individual who was a tax non-resident for at least 3 consecutive calendar years
  immediately before the year of return to Poland.
- **Benefit:** Exemption from PIT on income (employment + B2B) up to PLN 85,528/year, for 4 consecutive
  years starting from the year of return.
- **Duration:** 4 tax years.
- **Caveats:** Only one-time use; does not exempt from social security/healthcare contributions.

## 2026 Changes

- **ZUS healthcare reform effective January 2025 continues:** The revised Ryczałt healthcare contribution
  (fixed tiers based on revenue brackets rather than % of revenue) introduced in 2025 remains in force.
  No announced reversal. (zus.pl)
- **OECD EES system:** Since 12 October 2025, the Entry/Exit System (EES) records border crossings in
  Schengen; full border rollout by 10 April 2026. Affects 90/180-day tracking for Ukrainian freelancers
  working from Poland. (EY p.1264–1265)
- **Mały ZUS Plus:** Reform of reduced ZUS for small entrepreneurs was discussed in 2024-2025; no
  confirmed changes for 2026 as of EY content date.
- **No PIT rate changes for 2026** announced as of October 2025.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants. EUR/PLN rate used: 1 EUR = PLN 4.25 (approximate 2025 average).
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side costs only; employer contributions not shown).
> "B2B Ryczałt 12%" = gross JDG revenue, full ZUS at minimum base, Ryczałt IT rate.
> PIT base for employment = gross − social SS − PLN 3,000 basic deductible cost.
> PIT base for Ryczałt = revenue − social ZUS (healthcare NOT deductible from 2022).
> No solidarity tax (applies only above PLN 1M / ~€235k).

### Working: PLN 127,500 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (13.71%) | 13.71% × 127,500 | PLN 17,480 |
| Healthcare base | 127,500 − 17,480 | PLN 110,020 |
| Healthcare (9%) | 9% × 110,020 | PLN 9,902 |
| PIT base | 110,020 − 3,000 (deductible) | PLN 107,020 |
| PIT (12% bracket) | 12% × (107,020 − 30,000) | PLN 9,242 |
| **Total burden** | **17,480 + 9,902 + 9,242** | **PLN 36,624** |
| **Effective rate** | **36,624 / 127,500** | **28.7%** |

### Working: PLN 127,500 (≈ €30,000) Ryczałt 12% B2B

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Social ZUS (annual min) | Fixed minimum | PLN 21,276 |
| Healthcare (60k–300k bracket) | Fixed annual | PLN 9,367 |
| Ryczałt tax base | 127,500 − 21,276 | PLN 106,224 |
| Ryczałt 12% | 12% × 106,224 | PLN 12,747 |
| **Total burden** | **21,276 + 9,367 + 12,747** | **PLN 43,390** |
| **Effective rate** | **43,390 / 127,500** | **34.0%** |

### Summary table

| Gross income | EUR equiv | Scenario | Income tax | SS + Health | Total | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PLN 127,500 | €30,000 | Employment (skala 12%/32%) | PLN 9,242 | PLN 27,382 | PLN 36,624 | **28.7%** |
| PLN 127,500 | €30,000 | Ryczałt 12% JDG B2B | PLN 12,747 | PLN 30,643 | PLN 43,390 | **34.0%** |
| PLN 255,000 | €60,000 | Employment (skala 12%/32%) | PLN 41,852 | PLN 54,765 | PLN 96,617 | **37.9%** |
| PLN 255,000 | €60,000 | Ryczałt 12% JDG B2B | PLN 28,047 | PLN 30,643 | PLN 58,690 | **23.0%** |
| PLN 425,000 | €100,000 | Employment (skala 12%/32%) | PLN 96,025 | PLN 70,712 | PLN 166,737 | **39.2%** |
| PLN 425,000 | €100,000 | Ryczałt 12% JDG B2B | PLN 48,447 | PLN 38,137 | PLN 86,584 | **20.4%** |

**SS + Health working (Employment):**
- PLN 127,500: employee SS 17,480 + healthcare 9,902 = 27,382
- PLN 255,000: employee SS 34,961 + healthcare 19,804 = 54,765
- PLN 425,000: capped SS 35,673 + healthcare 35,039 = 70,712 (retirement/disability capped at PLN 260,190)

**SS + Health working (Ryczałt B2B):**
- All income levels: social ZUS PLN 21,276 (fixed minimum base)
- PLN 127,500 + PLN 255,000: healthcare bracket 60k–300k = PLN 9,367 → total PLN 30,643
- PLN 425,000: healthcare bracket >300k = PLN 16,861 → total PLN 38,137

**Key insight — Ryczałt 12% crossover:** At ≈PLN 150–170k annual revenue (≈€35–40k), Ryczałt 12%
starts to beat standard employment. Below this, the PLN 30k tax-free threshold + lower employee ZUS
make employment more efficient. Above PLN 170k, Ryczałt 12% advantage grows rapidly (23% vs 38% at
€60k; 20% vs 39% at €100k). This is one of the most favorable tech-worker regimes in the EU.

**Liniowy 19% B2B (for reference, not shown above):**
- At PLN 255,000: tax 19% × 233,724 = 44,408 + SS 21,276 + health 11,452 = 77,136 → 30.2%
- Liniowy beats employment but loses to Ryczałt 12% for IT services (assumes near-zero expenses)
- Liniowy is superior to Ryczałt when business expenses > ~30-40% of revenue

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/PLN = 4.25 (approximate 2025 average; fluctuations will shift the exact crossover point)
- Ryczałt 12% at IT services rate; full ZUS social at minimum base; all figures are after full ZUS period
  (not Ulga na Start / Mały ZUS Plus reduced contribution periods)
- Healthcare contribution for Ryczałt: fixed-tier system per 2025 ZUS reform
- Employment: PLN 3,000/year basic deductible cost included; no 50% creative-work deduction
- No solidarity tax scenarios shown (applies only above PLN 1,000,000 ≈ €235k)
- IP Box (5% on software IP income) not modeled — could reduce Liniowy effective rate dramatically
  for qualifying developers
