## Personal Income Tax

Namibia levies income tax on a **source basis** — income tax is assessed only on income from sources within or deemed to be within Namibia, and is generally **not affected by the residence of the taxpayer**. Namibia thus extends to territorial shelf and exclusive economic zone for tax purposes. Income for services related to employment or profession in Namibia is deemed to be Namibian-source regardless of where performed or payment made. Tax year: 1 March to end of February. Annual return due by 30 June (extension possible). PAYE system for employment income. NamRA administers. Husbands and wives taxed separately. (EY pp. 1055, 1059–1060)

### Income tax rates (applies to all individuals — same progressive rates)

| Taxable income (NAD) | Tax rate | Tax due (NAD) | Cumulative tax due (NAD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 100,000 | **0%** | 0 | 0 |
| Next 50,000 (100,001–150,000) | **18%** | 9,000 | 9,000 |
| Next 200,000 (150,001–350,000) | **25%** | 50,000 | 59,000 |
| Next 200,000 (350,001–550,000) | **28%** | 56,000 | 115,000 |
| Next 300,000 (550,001–850,000) | **30%** | 90,000 | 205,000 |
| Next 700,000 (850,001–1,550,000) | **32%** | 224,000 | 429,000 |
| Above 1,550,000 | **37%** | — | — |

Maximum marginal rate: **37%** (EY p. 1057)

**Note:** The same progressive rates apply to all individuals — employment, self-employment, directors' fees, and other income all taxed at these rates. (EY p. 1057)

**Capital gains:**
- **Generally exempt** from tax in Namibia (EY p. 1057)
- **Exceptions:** Gains on sale of shares in a company holding a mineral mining or exploration license, or petroleum mining/exploration license, are taxable at the **marginal rate** applicable to the taxpayer
- Gain on sale of a mineral mining/exploration license or petroleum mining/exploration license: taxable at marginal rate

**Investment income:**
- Interest from local commercial banks: **10% withholding** (final; exempt from normal income tax) (EY p. 1056)
- Interest on government securities/local authority securities/Namibia Post Office Savings Bank: exempt (EY p. 1056)
- Worldwide dividends on ordinary and preference shares: **exempt** (EY p. 1056)
- Building society interest/dividends from any source worldwide for Namibian residents: taxable with other income at progressive rates (EY p. 1056)
- Rental income: taxable at progressive rates (EY p. 1056)

**Nonresident withholding taxes:**
- Services fees (consulting, management, admin, technical): **10%** (EY p. 1055)
- Entertainment, artists, directors' fees, sportspersons: **25%** (EY p. 1056)
- Royalties (commercial, industrial, scientific equipment use): **10%** (EY p. 1056)
- Dividends declared: **20%** nonresident shareholders' tax (EY p. 1056)
- Interest from Namibian residents to nonresidents: **10%** (EY p. 1056)

**Deductions:**
- All non-capital expenses incurred in production of income are deductible
- Annual deduction of **NAD 150,000** per person allowed for contributions to: approved retirement annuity funds, pension funds, provident funds; premiums for study insurance policies for children or stepchildren (EY p. 1057)
- Donations to registered welfare organisations and approved educational institutions: deductible (certificate required)
- **Business deductions:** Non-capital expenses incurred in producing taxable income are deductible (EY p. 1057)

**Relief for losses:**
- Loss carryforward to offset income of same type in next year (EY p. 1057)
- From 1 March 2024:
  - Balance of assessed loss from previous years limited to offset of **NAD 1,000,000** or **80%** of current-year taxable income (whichever is greater)
  - Losses generally limited to 5-year carryforward; 10 years for mining/petroleum/green hydrogen entities
  - Losses from certain trades are **ring-fenced** (part-time farming, animal showing, racing/sport activities, collectibles dealing, betting) when taxable income ≥ NAD 200,000 and losses incurred in at least 3 of the most recent 5 years (EY pp. 1057–1058)

## Social Security

**Social security contributions (Namibia Social Security Commission):** (EY p. 1059)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (pension/disability/maternity/health) | **0.9%** of monthly compensation | **0.9%** (equal match) | **1.8%** of monthly compensation |
| **Minimum** | NAD 2.70/month | NAD 2.70/month | N/A |
| **Maximum** | NAD 81/month | NAD 81/month | NAD 162/month |

- Employee: 0.9% of monthly compensation; minimum NAD 2.70; maximum **NAD 81/month** (= NAD 972/year)
- Employer: matches employee contribution (equal 0.9%)
- Self-employed: 1.8% of monthly compensation; maximum **NAD 162/month** (= NAD 1,944/year)

**Workers' Compensation Fund:** Additional contribution for each employee earning < NAD 81,300/year; amount not specified as a percentage in EY guide (EY p. 1059)

**Namibia is not a party to any totalization agreement.** (EY p. 1059)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader / Individual Business

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual conducting business as a sole trader in Namibia
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus allowable business deductions (all non-capital expenses incurred in production of income); self-employment income taxed with other income at progressive rates
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates 0%–37%; tax year 1 March–28/29 February
- **Social security:** 1.8% of monthly compensation; maximum NAD 162/month (NAD 1,944/year)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds NAD 500,000; standard rate 15%
- **Loss treatment:** Carryforward up to 5 years (from 1 March 2024); offset limited to NAD 1,000,000 or 80% of taxable income per year; ring-fencing rules apply to certain suspect trades
- **Provisional tax:** Two provisional payments per year (last weekday of August and last weekday of February); balance of assessment due by 30 June each year; sole traders' balance due by 30 September

### Option B — Partnership

- Partnerships not treated as separate taxable entities; partners taxed on their share of net partnership income at progressive individual rates (EY p. 1057)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Source-Based Tax System

- Namibia's **source-based** system means foreign-source income of Namibian residents is generally **not taxable** in Namibia
- A non-resident working in Namibia for a Namibian employer pays Namibian income tax on Namibia-source income only
- Income from services rendered on temporary absence from Namibia (for Namibian employer) remains Namibian-source

### Work Visas and Permits

- **Work visa:** Up to 6 months; 3-month period + 3-month renewal; fees increased from NAD 1,200 to NAD 1,600 per person (non-AU nationals) as of 1 April 2024 (EY p. 1062)
- **Work permit (12 months):** NAD 2,600 fee; **24-month permit:** NAD 5,200; renewable 3 months before expiry
- Foreign nationals may accept employment in Namibia only with valid work permits AND temporary/permanent residence permits (EY p. 1062)
- Business establishment: foreign nationals may establish a business but must hold work permit or permanent residence permit; applications can be submitted via Investment Centre if employment will be created (EY p. 1062–1063)

### MICE Visa (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, Events)

- Launched 3 March 2025; full enforcement from 1 April 2025
- Designed for professionals attending business-related events; replaces need for short-term work permits in many cases
- Application via MHAISS E-Services Portal (EY pp. 1063)

### Permanent Residence

- Issued after holding work permit or temporary residence permit for 10 years; cost NAD 20,000; renewable
- Comprehensive documentation required (EY pp. 1063–1064)

### Double Tax Treaties

Botswana, France, Germany, India, Malaysia, Mauritius, Romania, Russian Federation, South Africa, Sweden, United Kingdom (EY p. 1060). OECD model treaties. Namibia is negotiating with: Canada, Liberia, Seychelles, Singapore, Spain, Tunisia, Zambia, Zimbabwe.

## 2026 Changes

- **Loss carryforward limits effective 1 March 2024 (already in force — EY pp. 1057–1058):** From years of assessment commencing on/after 1 March 2024: assessed loss offset limited to NAD 1,000,000 or 80% of current taxable income; carryforward generally limited to 5 years (10 years for mining/petroleum/green hydrogen). This was a significant 2024 change now fully applicable.
- **No specific additional 2026 changes** announced in EY guide
- **MICE Visa implementation (EY p. 1063):** Launched 3 March 2025; full enforcement from 1 April 2025 — already in force

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Namibia uses NAD (Namibian Dollar), pegged to ZAR at 1:1. **1 EUR ≈ NAD 20.4** (approximate; verify at namibiafinance.com.na).
> Tax year 1 March–28/29 February; progressive rates 0%–37%.
> Employee social security: capped at NAD 81/month = NAD 972/year — extremely low.
> Self-employed social security: capped at NAD 162/month = NAD 1,944/year.
> No capital gains tax (general). Foreign-source income: not taxable in Namibia.

### €30,000 Employment
- NAD equivalent: EUR 30,000 × 20.4 = NAD 612,000/year
- PIT:
  - 0% on NAD 100,000 = 0
  - 18% on NAD 50,000 = NAD 9,000
  - 25% on NAD 200,000 = NAD 50,000
  - 28% on NAD 200,000 = NAD 56,000
  - 30% on NAD 62,000 (550,001–612,000) = NAD 18,600
  - Total PIT: NAD 133,600
- Social security: capped at NAD 972/year
- Total: NAD 134,572; Effective rate: 134,572 / 612,000 = **22.0%**

### €60,000 Employment
- NAD: EUR 60,000 × 20.4 = NAD 1,224,000
- PIT:
  - 0% on NAD 100,000 = 0
  - 18% on NAD 50,000 = NAD 9,000
  - 25% on NAD 200,000 = NAD 50,000
  - 28% on NAD 200,000 = NAD 56,000
  - 30% on NAD 300,000 = NAD 90,000
  - 32% on NAD 374,000 (850,001–1,224,000) = NAD 119,680
  - Total PIT: NAD 324,680
- Social security: capped at NAD 972
- Total: NAD 325,652; Effective rate: 325,652 / 1,224,000 = **26.6%**

### €100,000 Employment
- NAD: EUR 100,000 × 20.4 = NAD 2,040,000
- PIT:
  - Bands 1–5: cumulative NAD 205,000
  - 32% on NAD 700,000 = NAD 224,000
  - 37% on NAD 490,000 (1,550,001–2,040,000) = NAD 181,300
  - Total PIT: NAD 610,300
- Social security: NAD 972
- Total: NAD 611,272; Effective rate: 611,272 / 2,040,000 = **30.0%**

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **EUR/NAD exchange rate:** NAD is pegged to ZAR at 1:1; EUR/ZAR fluctuates. Approximate rate 20.4 used; verify at Bank of Namibia (bon.com.na).
- [ ] **Workers' Compensation Fund contribution:** EY mentions this for employees earning < NAD 81,300/year but does not specify the rate. Verify with Social Security Commission of Namibia.
- [ ] **VAT threshold NAD 500,000:** Assumed based on EY guide context; verify current threshold with NamRA.
- [ ] **Self-employment social security calculation:** Self-employed rate is 1.8% of monthly compensation capped at NAD 162/month. For low-income self-employed, actual 1.8% may be below the NAD 162 maximum — both minimum (NAD 2.70) and maximum (NAD 162) apply.
- [ ] **Loss carryforward from 1 March 2024:** EY notes carryforward is "generally limited to five years" but this seems to represent a change from the prior indefinite carryforward. Confirm the transition rules for losses incurred before 1 March 2024.
- [ ] **Rental income deductions:** Rental income is taxable at progressive rates; specific deductible expenses for rental properties (maintenance, interest, rates) not detailed in EY guide.
