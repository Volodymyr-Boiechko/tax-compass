## Personal Income Tax

> Source-based taxation: income tax assessed only on income from sources within or deemed to be within Namibia; generally not affected by residence of taxpayer. Administered by NamRA (Namibia Revenue Agency). Tax year runs 1 March to end of February. Annual returns due by 30 June; husbands and wives taxed separately.

### Individual Rates (same scale for all individuals)

| Taxable income (NAD) | Tax rate % | Tax due (NAD) | Cumulative tax due (NAD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 100,000 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| Next 50,000 | 18 | 9,000 | 9,000 |
| Next 200,000 | 25 | 50,000 | 59,000 |
| Next 200,000 | 28 | 56,000 | 115,000 |
| Next 300,000 | 30 | 90,000 | 205,000 |
| Next 700,000 | 32 | 224,000 | 429,000 |
| Above 1,550,000 | 37 | — | — |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Annual deduction of NAD 150,000 for total contributions to approved retirement annuity funds, pension funds, provident funds, and premiums for study insurance policies for children/stepchildren
- Donations to registered welfare organisations and approved educational institutions: deductible (recipient must issue certificate)
- No capital gains tax in Namibia (capital gains generally exempt); exception: gains on shares in companies holding mineral mining/exploration or petroleum mining/exploration licences taxed at individual's marginal rate

**Notes:**
- PAYE system: individuals earning only employment income remunerated by the same employer throughout the year need not file returns unless requested
- Provisional tax: individuals with annual Namibia-source income ≥ NAD 5,000 not subject to PAYE must make two provisional payments (last weekday of August and last weekday of February)
- Balance of tax due payable by 30 June (employment and most income); sole traders: by 30 September
- Interest from local commercial banks: 10% WHT (final; exempt from normal income tax)
- Exempt interest: government stocks/securities, Namibia Post Office Savings Bank deposits, worldwide dividends on ordinary and preference shares
- Interest income from non-Namibian unit trusts: taxable at marginal rate
- Rental income: aggregated with other income and taxed at Rates
- Restraint-of-trade payments: included in gross income even if capital in nature
- Directors' fees (Namibian-source, resident): taxed with other income at Rates; nonresident: 25% WHT (final)
- Loss carryforward: generally 5 years (from 1 March 2024); mining/petroleum/green hydrogen: 10 years; prior to 1 March 2024: indefinite; limited to NAD 1,000,000 or 80% of current-year taxable income per year
- Ring-fencing of losses from suspect trades applies for assessments from 1 March 2012 where taxable income ≥ NAD 200,000

## Social Security

> Employees under 65 and all employers subject to social security contributions. (EY p.1059)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security Commission (SSC) | 0.9% of monthly compensation | 0.9% of monthly compensation (matching) | 1.8% of monthly compensation |
| Workers' Compensation Fund | — | Contribution required for employees earning < NAD 81,300/year | — |
| **Total** | **0.9%** | **0.9%+** | **1.8%** |

**Income ceiling:** Employee: minimum NAD 2.70/month; maximum NAD 81/month (i.e., effective ceiling on pensionable earnings of ~NAD 9,000/month)
**Self-employed ceiling:** NAD 162/month maximum
**Notes:**
- Namibia is not party to any totalization agreement
- Workers' Compensation Fund: employer contributes for each employee earning less than NAD 81,300/year
- SSC contributions are very low in absolute terms due to the monthly caps

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader / Self-Employed (Same Rate Scale)
- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals and sole traders with Namibia-source income
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross income less capital and exempt receipts, less allowable deductions for non-capital expenses incurred in production of income)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates as employment income (0–37%); provisional tax (two payments per year); balance due by 30 September
- **Social security:** SSC 1.8% of monthly compensation, capped at NAD 162/month
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above NAD 500,000 annual turnover (standard rate 15%)
- **Key limits:** NAD 150,000 annual deduction for retirement/pension fund contributions; loss carry-forward limited to 5 years (from 1 March 2024) with annual set-off capped at NAD 1 million or 80% of taxable income

### Option B — Partnership
- **Who qualifies:** Partners in business partnerships
- **Tax base:** Partners taxed on their share of net partnership income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive individual rates
- **Social security:** As sole trader
- **VAT obligations:** Partnership-level VAT registration may apply
- **Key limits:** Same as sole trader

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

> No specific preferential tax regime for new tax residents identified in EY source. Namibia's source-based system means nonresidents are generally not taxed on non-Namibia-source income regardless.

### Source-Based Taxation
- **Eligibility:** All individuals; income only taxable if from Namibia source
- **Benefit:** Non-Namibia-source income not subject to Namibia income tax regardless of residence
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Income from services rendered for Namibian employer during temporary absence still taxable in Namibia

## 2026 Changes

- Loss carryforward rules tightened effective 1 March 2024: losses limited to NAD 1,000,000 or 80% of current-year taxable income per year; carryforward period generally capped at 5 years (previously indefinite) (EY p.1057-1058)
- No other specific 2026 rate changes identified
