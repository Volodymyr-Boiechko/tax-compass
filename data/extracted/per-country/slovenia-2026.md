## Personal Income Tax

> Slovenia has a progressive personal income tax. Individuals aggregate their active income (employment, business, agricultural, rental, property rights) and apply the progressive scale below. Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Slovenian-source income. Married persons assessed separately.

**PIT rates (2025 income):**

| Taxable income (EUR) | Rate % |
|---|---|
| 0 – 9,210.26 | 16 |
| 9,210.26 – 27,089.00 | 26 |
| 27,089.00 – 54,178.00 | 33 |
| 54,178.00 – 78,016.32 | 39 |
| Above 78,016.32 | 50 |

*(EY p. 1406)*

**Tax reliefs (deductible from taxable income, 2025):**
- General relief: EUR 5,260 if no other resident claims dependent family member relief with respect to the taxpayer; if annual tax base <EUR 16,832, general relief increased: EUR 5,260 + (EUR 19,736.99 − 1.17259 × total income)
- Dependent family member relief: EUR 2,838.30 per dependent; EUR 3,085.52 for second dependent; EUR 5,146.39 for third; EUR 7,207.26 for fourth; EUR 9,268.12 for fifth
- Special needs child relief: EUR 10,285.40
- Disability relief (severe physical disability): EUR 19,134.42
- Student relief (if no dependent family member claim): EUR 3,682.00
- Special assignment relief: up to EUR 12,000/year (assignment >30 days, prior location >200km away, salary ≥1.5× average, not resident in host country for 5 years prior) (EY p. 1406)
- Self-employed cultural workers / journalists (income ≤EUR 25,000): 15% deduction of annual income

**Special rates for specific income types:**
- Rental income: 25% on 90% of gross rental income (10% standardized costs); optional actual costs or progressive tax
- Property rights transfers: 25% on 90% of gross income (10% standardized costs)
- Capital gains: 25%; reducing 5% per 5 years of ownership: 20% (>5 years), 15% (>10 years), exempt (>15 years)
- Dividends: 25% flat final tax
- Interest income: 25% flat (first EUR 1,000 exempt for EU bank deposits)

**Notes:**
- No joint filing — married persons taxed separately
- Temporary workers: 25% withholding on income from services, after deducting 10% standardized costs
- Work contracts: additional 25% employer tax
- Losses from business: carried forward indefinitely (EY p. 1406)

## Social Security

> Slovenia has comprehensive social security covering pension/disability, health insurance, unemployment, and maternity. No ceiling on contributions. Self-employed pay all contributions; tax base = 60% of profit.

| Type of contribution | Employer % | Employee % | Total % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension and disability insurance | 8.85 | 15.50 | 24.35 |
| Health insurance | 6.56 | 6.36 | 12.92 |
| Unemployment insurance | 0.06 | 0.14 | 0.20 |
| Maternity allowance | 0.10 | 0.10 | 0.20 |
| Worker's compensation insurance | 0.53 | 0.00 | 0.53 |
| **Total** | **16.10** | **22.10** | **38.20** |

*(EY p. 1408)*

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling — contributions apply to full salary (EY p. 1408)
**Notes:**
- Self-employed persons must pay all contributions (employer + employee = 38.2% total); tax base = 60% of net business profit
- Contract workers: health insurance 6.36% employee + 0.53% employer; pension/disability 15.5% employee + 8.85% employer (if not already insured)
- Social security contributions are paid monthly; contributions considered final (no annual adjustment at year-end)
- Self-employed SS base: 60% of profit generated by the business; minimum and maximum bases apply

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals in Slovenia can use the standard progressive PIT system or elect the "normiranec" flat-rate regime. The normiranec scheme is the key benefit for freelancers.

### Option A — Normiranec (Flat-Rate Expense Deduction Scheme)
- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals (s.p. — samostojni podjetnik) whose annual income does not exceed EUR 100,000 (up to 2025 threshold; may be adjusted annually); election valid for 2 calendar years after which taxpayer returns to standard taxation unless conditions still met
- **Tax base:** 80% automatic expense deduction from gross revenue; maximum deductible expenses EUR 40,000 (for revenue up to EUR 50,000) or EUR 80,000 (for revenue up to EUR 100,000); net taxable income = 20% of gross revenue (within limits)
- **Rate / mechanism:** 20% of gross revenue treated as taxable income; PIT at progressive rates applied to that reduced base; effective total PIT burden typically 3.2–10% of gross revenue at lower income levels
- **Social security:** Normiranec pays fixed monthly SS contributions based on a fixed base (not actual income); in 2025 approximately EUR 400–800/month in total SS (employee + employer equivalent); these are deducted from the normiranec's PIT base
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above EUR 50,000 annual turnover; standard VAT 22%; reduced 9.5%
- **Key limits:** Cannot exceed EUR 100,000 annual income (or EUR 50,000 to benefit from full EUR 40,000 deduction); income above limits must use standard taxation; 2-year lock-in; no actual expense deduction allowed once normiranec elected; must have valid s.p. registration

### Option B — Standard Self-Employment (Standardna dejavnost)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered as sole proprietor (s.p.)
- **Tax base:** Revenue minus all actual documented business expenses; progressive PIT rates apply (16/26/33/39/50%)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net profit taxed at progressive rates; standard deductions (general relief EUR 5,260) available
- **Social security:** 38.2% on 60% of net profit (employer + employee combined)
- **VAT obligations:** Registration above EUR 50,000 turnover; 22% standard rate
- **Key limits:** No income cap; actual bookkeeping required; losses carried forward indefinitely

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Special Assignment Tax Relief
- **Eligibility:** Individuals assigned to work in Slovenia from another location: (1) assignment longer than 30 days, (2) prior working location was >200km from assignment location, (3) monthly salary received during assignment ≥1.5× average annual salary in prior year ÷ 12, (4) individual was not a tax resident of the host country for 5 years prior
- **Benefit:** Tax relief up to EUR 12,000/year against employment income
- **Duration:** Duration of qualifying assignment
- **Application deadline:** Claim via employer or annual tax return
- **Caveats:** All four conditions must be met simultaneously; strict distance and salary conditions (EY p. 1406)

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 1403–1414)

- PIT brackets and tax relief amounts are indexed annually — 2026 thresholds not yet published at EY review (2025 values shown above)
- Normiranec thresholds (EUR 50,000 / EUR 100,000) expected to remain unchanged for 2026
- Social security rates unchanged (38.20% total, no ceiling)
- No major tax reform announced for 2026 in EY source

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, Slovenian tax resident.
> Slovenia uses EUR. General relief EUR 5,260 applied (no dependants).

**Employment at €30,000:**
1. Employee SS: €30,000 × 22.10% = €6,630
2. Taxable income = €30,000 − €6,630 − €5,260 (general relief) = €18,110
3. PIT: €9,210.26 × 16% = €1,474; (€18,110 − €9,210.26) × 26% = €8,900 × 26% = €2,314 → PIT = €3,788
4. Total burden = €3,788 + €6,630 = €10,418
5. Effective rate = €10,418 / €30,000 = **34.7%**

**Employment at €60,000:**
1. Employee SS: €60,000 × 22.10% = €13,260
2. Taxable = €60,000 − €13,260 − €5,260 = €41,480
3. PIT: €9,210 × 16% = €1,474; (€27,089 − €9,210) × 26% = €17,879 × 26% = €4,649; (€41,480 − €27,089) × 33% = €14,391 × 33% = €4,749 → PIT = €10,872
4. Total burden = €10,872 + €13,260 = €24,132
5. Effective rate = €24,132 / €60,000 = **40.2%**

**Employment at €100,000:**
1. Employee SS: €100,000 × 22.10% = €22,100
2. Taxable = €100,000 − €22,100 − €5,260 = €72,640
3. PIT: €9,210 × 16% + €17,879 × 26% + €27,089 × 33% + (€72,640 − €54,178) × 39%
   = €1,474 + €4,649 + €8,939 + €18,462 × 39%
   = €1,474 + €4,649 + €8,939 + €7,200 = €22,262
4. Total burden = €22,262 + €22,100 = €44,362
5. Effective rate = €44,362 / €100,000 = **44.4%**

**B2B (normiranec) at €60,000 revenue:**
1. Normiranec: taxable income = 20% × €60,000 = €12,000 (80% expense deduction, within €50,000 cap)
2. Fixed SS: approximately €500/month × 12 = €6,000 (deducted from normiranec's base — may reduce PIT; assume SS already accounted for separately)
3. PIT on €12,000 (less €5,260 general relief = €6,740): €6,740 × 16% = €1,078
4. SS (fixed): approximately €6,000/year total
5. Total = €1,078 + €6,000 = €7,078
6. Effective rate = €7,078 / €60,000 = **11.8%**

*The normiranec is highly beneficial compared to standard employment. At €60,000 revenue, the effective total burden is ~11.8% vs 40.2% for employment. This is the key feature of the Slovenian tax system for freelancers.*

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €3,788 | €6,630 | €10,418 | **34.7%** |
| €30,000 | B2B (normiranec) | ~€540 | ~€6,000 | ~€6,540 | **~21.8%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €10,872 | €13,260 | €24,132 | **40.2%** |
| €60,000 | B2B (normiranec) | ~€1,078 | ~€6,000 | ~€7,078 | **~11.8%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €22,262 | €22,100 | €44,362 | **44.4%** |
| €100,000 | B2B (normiranec)* | ~€4,200 | ~€7,200 | ~€11,400 | **~11.4%** |

*\*At €100,000 revenue under normiranec: only first €50,000 gets 80% deduction (max EUR 40,000 expenses); above €50,000 up to €100,000 gets proportionally higher deduction (max EUR 80,000 total). Effective taxable income ~€20,000 at this revenue level.*

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants, Slovenian resident
- General relief EUR 5,260 applied
- Employee SS: 22.10% (no ceiling)
- Normiranec: 80% expense deduction; fixed SS ~EUR 500/month (actual varies)
- Standard (non-normiranec) B2B not shown — rates between employment and normiranec
- EUR currency — no conversion needed
