## Personal Income Tax

Residents are subject to income tax on their worldwide income. Nonresidents are subject to income tax on income from sources in Slovenia. Employment income and income from performance of services and business income are considered to be derived from sources in Slovenia if the employment, services, and business are carried out in Slovenia. Income is also deemed to be from a Slovenian source if it is paid or borne by a Slovenian tax resident. (EY p.1403)

**Residency:** An individual is considered a Slovenian tax resident if during the fiscal year they fulfill any of the following:
- Have an officially registered permanent residence in Slovenia
- Have habitual abode or center of personal and economic interests in Slovenia
- Are present in Slovenia for a total of more than 183 days

Tax year = calendar year. Married persons taxed separately on all types of income. The tax authorities prepare an informative calculation based on payers' information; the individual has 30 days to object. Final assessment issued by 31 October of the following year. (EY pp.1403, 1409)

### PIT Brackets (2025 rates — five-bracket progressive scale)

Individuals aggregate their active income (employment, business, agricultural/forestry, rental, property rights transfer) and apply the progressive rates below. (EY p.1406)

| Taxable income — Exceeding (EUR) | Taxable income — Not exceeding (EUR) | Rate (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 9,210.26 | 16 |
| 9,210.26 | 27,089.00 | 26 |
| 27,089.00 | 54,178.00 | 33 |
| 54,178.00 | 78,016.32 | 39 |
| 78,016.32 | — | 50 |

(EY p.1406)

### Investment Income (Passive — Separate Flat Rate)

Not included in the aggregated progressive tax base. (EY p.1404–1405)

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends | 25% (flat, final) |
| Interest (deposits, bonds, financial instruments, life insurance) | 25% (flat, final) |
| Capital gains (within 5 years) | 25% (flat) |
| Capital gains (after 5 years) | 20% (flat) |
| Capital gains (after 10 years) | 15% (flat) |
| Capital gains (after 15 years) | 0% (exempt) |
| Interest on deposits ≤ EUR1,000 at Slovenian/EU banks | 0% (exempt) |

Taxpayer may opt to include capital income in the active income progressive tax base; in that case the flat rate already paid counts as advance payment. (EY p.1405)

### Property Leases and IP Rights

- Property (rental income): 25% flat rate on 65% of gross income (standardized 35% deduction), or actual costs may be claimed; may opt for progressive taxation (EY p.1404)
- Transfer of property rights (IP, materials, copyrights, methods): 25% on 90% of gross income (10% standardized deduction) (EY p.1404)

**Notes:**
- Temporary workers: 25% withholding on income from work and services, reduced by 10% standardized costs; payments on work contracts subject to additional 25% employer tax
- Nonresidents from other EU/EEA Member States with ≥90% of worldwide income sourced in Slovenia may apply the general relief and dependent family member relief
- Capital gains from private immovable property sale are generally exempt after 15 years; from securities if held >1 year on regulated market and not held in business assets
- EUR500/year general capital gains exemption from securities sale
- Losses from business can be carried forward indefinitely; rental losses cannot be offset against other income (EY p.1406)

## Social Security

Slovenia imposes social security taxes to cover health insurance, pension/disability insurance, and unemployment insurance. No ceiling applies to the amount of salary subject to contributions. (EY p.1408)

| Type of contribution | Employer (%) | Employee (%) | Total (%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension and disability insurance | 8.85 | 15.50 | 24.35 |
| Health insurance | 6.56 | 6.36 | 12.92 |
| Unemployment insurance | 0.06 | 0.14 | 0.20 |
| Maternity allowance | 0.10 | 0.10 | 0.20 |
| Worker's compensation insurance | 0.53 | 0.00 | 0.53 |
| **Total** | **16.10** | **22.10** | **38.20** |

(EY p.1408)

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling applies to the salary amount subject to contributions.

**Self-employed persons:** Must pay all of the above contributions, unless they are also employees. The tax base for the self-employed contributions is **60% of the profit** generated by the business. (EY p.1408)

**Contract workers:** Health insurance at 6.36% of earned income (employee); employer 0.53%. Pension/disability at 15.5% for employees not otherwise covered (8.85% employer). (EY p.1408)

**Notes:**
- Contributions paid during the year are final — no adjustment at year-end
- Contributions paid by employees are deductible from taxable employment income (EY p.1393 — referring to Slovak data; for Slovenia: SS contributions are deductible from employment income as mandatory contributions)
- Self-employed SS base: 60% of net business profit; this is a key planning consideration for the normiranec scheme

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Normiranec (Normalized Expenses / 80% Automatic Deduction)

This is the KEY regime for IT freelancers and self-employed professionals in Slovenia. (EY pp.1403–1404)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals with annual gross turnover ≤ EUR50,000 who are not VAT payers (non-VAT payers threshold applies). Available for sole traders and individuals performing activities independently. Turnover limit increased from EUR50,000 (prior EUR12,500 limit was raised significantly).
- **Tax base:** 20% of gross turnover (i.e., 80% of gross revenue is treated as automatically deductible expenses — "normirana" expenses — regardless of actual costs)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 16/26/33/39/50% applied to 20% of gross revenue (net taxable income = 20% × gross revenue); general personal relief (EUR5,260) deductible from normiranec income
- **Social security:** Full SS applies at 38.2% total; self-employed SS base = 60% of net profit = 60% × (20% × gross) = 12% of gross revenue
- **VAT obligations:** Normiranec must be a non-VAT payer to qualify for the 80% expense deduction; if VAT-registered, the normiranec regime applies differently (60% expense deduction instead of 80%); VAT registration threshold in Slovenia is EUR50,000/year
- **Key limits:**
  - Annual gross turnover ≤ EUR50,000 (mandatory threshold)
  - Cannot be simultaneously employed and claim normiranec on same activity with the same employer
  - No actual expense deductions allowed in normiranec — only the 80% flat deduction
  - Very favorable for high-margin services (IT, consulting, software)

### Option B — Standard Business Income Accounting

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals; mandatory if turnover > EUR50,000 or if VAT-registered and prefer actual costs
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus actual documented business expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 16/26/33/39/50%
- **Social security:** SS at 38.2% total on base = 60% of net profit
- **VAT obligations:** If turnover exceeds EUR50,000: mandatory VAT registration (standard rate 22%; reduced 9.5%)
- **Key limits:** Full accounting and bookkeeping required; actual costs must be documented

### Option C — Notional 60% Expense Deduction (VAT Payers)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed VAT payers with business income
- **Tax base:** 40% of gross business income (60% notional deduction, increased from prior 30% — EY p.1394 for Slovak; EY p.1391 for Slovak details; for Slovenia this is the VAT-payer normiranec form at 60%)
- Note: EY notes a 60% notional deduction for non-VAT payers was formerly available; from 2017 onwards non-VAT payers up to EUR50k have the 80% deduction; VAT payers use 60% deduction up to EUR100,000 turnover

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Special Tax Relief for Assignees (Short-Term Assignment Allowance)

- **Eligibility:** Individual was NOT a Slovenian tax resident for 5 years before assignment; assignment to Slovenia from outside; assignment >30 days; prior work location ≥200km from Slovenia; monthly salary ≥1.5× average annual Slovenian salary divided by 12; assignment through employer
- **Benefit:** Tax relief up to EUR12,000/year on assignment-related income above certain threshold
- **Duration:** Duration of the qualifying assignment
- **Application:** Claimed on informative tax calculation/return

### EU Blue Card / Single Permit

- **Eligibility:** Non-EU highly qualified professionals; employer must register job vacancy; EU Blue Card issued for highly qualified employees
- **Benefit:** Right to reside and work in Slovenia; family unification rights
- **Note:** EU citizens do not need single permits to work in Slovenia but must register at National Employment Office; non-EU nationals need single residence and work permit (EY pp.1411–1412)

## 2026 Changes

- PIT brackets for 2025 (five-bracket scale 16/26/33/39/50%) apply; no announced changes for 2026 as of October 2025
- Normiranec EUR50,000 turnover threshold remains in force for 2026; no announced changes
- SS rates (16.10% employer / 22.10% employee / 38.20% total) unchanged for 2026
- No new special tax regimes announced as of October 2025
- Informative calculation system continues; advance payments due upon receipt of income

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, general relief EUR5,260 applied.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side only; employer 16.1% SS not shown).
> "Normiranec B2B" = best-case self-employment regime; 80% automatic deduction; non-VAT payer.
> Currency: EUR.
> Employee SS: 22.10% on gross (no ceiling).
> Self-employed SS: 38.20% on 60% of net profit = 38.20% × (60% × 20% of gross) = 4.58% of gross.

### Working calculations:

**€30,000 Employment:**
- SS (employee 22.1%): 22.1% × 30,000 = €6,630
- Gross income for PIT: €30,000; SS is deductible → taxable base = 30,000 − 5,260 (general relief) = €24,740
  - Note: SS contributions are deductible from employment income before applying PIT brackets
  - Taxable base after SS deduction: 30,000 − 6,630 − 5,260 = €18,110
- PIT: 16% × 9,210.26 + 26% × (18,110 − 9,210.26) = 1,473.64 + 26% × 8,899.74 = 1,473.64 + 2,313.93 = €3,787.57
- Total: 3,788 + 6,630 = €10,418; effective = 34.7%

**€30,000 Normiranec B2B:**
- Net taxable income = 20% × 30,000 = €6,000
- Deduct general relief EUR5,260 → taxable = €740
- PIT: 16% × 740 = €118
- SS base: 60% × 6,000 = €3,600; SS = 38.2% × 3,600 = €1,375
- Total: 118 + 1,375 = €1,493; effective = 5.0%

**€60,000 Employment:**
- SS: 22.1% × 60,000 = €13,260
- Taxable after SS + relief: 60,000 − 13,260 − 5,260 = €41,480
- PIT: 16%×9,210 + 26%×(27,089−9,210) + 33%×(41,480−27,089) = 1,474 + 4,649 + 33%×14,391 = 1,474 + 4,649 + 4,749 = €10,872
- Total: 10,872 + 13,260 = €24,132; effective = 40.2%

**€60,000 Normiranec B2B:**
- Note: €60,000 exceeds EUR50,000 normiranec threshold → cannot use 80% deduction
- Must use standard accounting; assume 30% actual expenses → net profit = €42,000
- Taxable: 42,000 − 5,260 = €36,740; but SS contributions also deductible
- SS: 38.2% × (60% × 42,000) = 38.2% × 25,200 = €9,626; deduct SS: taxable = 42,000 − 9,626 − 5,260 = €27,114
- PIT: 16%×9,210 + 26%×(27,089−9,210) + 33%×(27,114−27,089) = 1,474 + 4,649 + 8 = €6,131
- Total: 6,131 + 9,626 = €15,757; effective = 26.3%
- **Note:** At €60k, normiranec is NOT available (exceeds EUR50k cap). Standard regime shown.

**€100,000 Employment:**
- SS: 22.1% × 100,000 = €22,100
- Taxable: 100,000 − 22,100 − 5,260 = €72,640
- PIT: 1,474 + 4,649 + (33%×27,089) + (39%×(72,640−54,178)) = 1,474 + 4,649 + 8,939 + 39%×18,462 = 1,474 + 4,649 + 8,939 + 7,200 = €22,262
- Total: 22,262 + 22,100 = €44,362; effective = 44.4%

**€100,000 B2B (standard, 30% expenses):**
- Net profit: €70,000
- SS: 38.2% × (60% × 70,000) = 38.2% × 42,000 = €16,044
- Taxable: 70,000 − 16,044 − 5,260 = €48,696
- PIT: 1,474 + 4,649 + 8,939 + 33%×(48,696−54,178) → 48,696 < 54,178 so: 1,474 + 4,649 + 33%×(48,696−27,089) = 1,474 + 4,649 + 33%×21,607 = 1,474 + 4,649 + 7,130 = €13,253
- Total: 13,253 + 16,044 = €29,297; effective = 29.3%

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €3,788 | €6,630 (22.1%) | €10,418 | **34.7%** |
| €30,000 | Normiranec B2B (80% deduction, ≤€50k) | €118 | €1,375 (SS on 60%×net) | €1,493 | **5.0%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €10,872 | €13,260 (22.1%) | €24,132 | **40.2%** |
| €60,000 | B2B standard (30% exp.) — normiranec N/A at €60k | €6,131 | €9,626 (38.2% on 60% net) | €15,757 | **26.3%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €22,262 | €22,100 (22.1%) | €44,362 | **44.4%** |
| €100,000 | B2B standard (30% exp.) | €13,253 | €16,044 (38.2% on 60% net) | €29,297 | **29.3%** |

**Key insight — Normiranec at €30k:** With 80% automatic expense deduction, only 20% of gross (€6,000) is taxable before applying the general relief (€5,260), leaving just €740 taxable. PIT = €118. Social security base = 60% of net profit = €3,600; SS = €1,375. Total burden = €1,493 on €30,000 gross → effective rate of just 5.0%. This is one of the most favorable IT freelancer regimes in the EU, capped at €50,000/year turnover.

**Assumptions:**
- General relief EUR5,260 applied (single, no dependants)
- SS contributions are deductible from employment income before PIT calculation
- Self-employed SS base: 60% of net profit
- Normiranec: 80% expense deduction; non-VAT payer; gross ≤ EUR50,000 (only €30k scenario qualifies)
- Standard B2B: 30% assumed actual expenses (for €60k and €100k)
- No ceiling on SS contributions
- Employer SS (16.1%) not shown in employment effective rate

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Normiranec exact EUR50,000 threshold:** EY references the normiranec 80% deduction for non-VAT payers; the EUR50,000 threshold should be confirmed at e-uprava.gov.si or with FURS (Financial Administration of the Republic of Slovenia)
- [ ] **VAT-payer normiranec (60% deduction):** The 60% expense deduction for VAT payers (turnover up to EUR100,000) needs confirmation; EY p.1404 references a 60% notional deduction for property transfers and similar; the business income normiranec for VAT payers should be separately verified
- [ ] **SS deductibility from employment income:** EY Slovenia states SS contributions by employees are deductible from employment income for PIT; the exact mechanism (deducted from gross before PIT brackets or as a credit) should be confirmed with FURS
- [ ] **Self-employed SS minimum and maximum contribution base:** No ceiling on SS is noted; however, the self-employed SS assessment base (60% of net profit) likely has a minimum monthly base — verify the minimum contribution for new registrations
- [ ] **Normiranec and employment combination:** Whether a person can be both employed and a normiranec for different activities (not the same employer/activity) should be verified — this "hybridization" is common in practice
- [ ] **2026 bracket adjustments:** PIT brackets are indexed annually in Slovenia; verify 2026 brackets once published (likely minor adjustments from 2025)
- [ ] **Property income deductibility:** The option to elect progressive taxation for rental and IP income (instead of flat 25%) may be beneficial for lower-income earners; the interaction with SS deductibility needs clarification
