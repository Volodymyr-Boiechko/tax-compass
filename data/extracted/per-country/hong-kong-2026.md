## Personal Income Tax

Hong Kong levies **three separate taxes** rather than a unified income tax:
1. **Salaries Tax** — on employment income (progressive 2%–17% OR standard rate 15%, whichever is lower)
2. **Profits Tax** — on self-employment/business income (15% for unincorporated businesses; 7.5% on first HKD 2 million under two-tiered regime)
3. **Property Tax** — on rental income (15% on net assessable value after 20% deduction)

**Territorial basis:** Hong Kong observes a territorial basis of taxation. Tax residency has no significance in determining tax liability (except in limited circumstances for treaty purposes). Income from services rendered outside Hong Kong is generally not taxable. (EY p.574)

**Employment income taxed if:** income arises in or is derived from a Hong Kong office or Hong Kong employment, or from services rendered in Hong Kong during visits of more than 60 days in any tax year. Employees whose duties are all performed outside Hong Kong (or visits ≤60 days/year): statutory exemption applies. (EY p.574–575)

**Tax year:** 1 April to 31 March. (EY p.579)

**Self-employed/business income:** Anyone carrying on a profession, trade or business in Hong Kong subject to profits tax. (EY p.575)

**Investment income:** Interest income not derived from investing funds of a business, and all dividend income: EXEMPT from taxation. No withholding taxes on dividends or interest paid to nonresidents. (EY p.575)

**Capital gains:** NOT taxed in Hong Kong. (EY p.576)

**Royalties paid to nonresidents:** Withholding at 2.25% (first HKD 2 million) and 4.5% (remainder) effective rates if subject to two-tiered profits tax regime; 7.5% (first HKD 2 million) and 15% (remainder) if recipient is related to payer or IP was previously used in Hong Kong business. (EY p.575)

**Salaries Tax progressive rates — 2025-26 tax year (1 April 2025 – 31 March 2026)** (EY p.578)

| Net chargeable income (HKD) | Tax rate | Tax due (HKD) | Cumulative (HKD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 50,000 | 2% | 1,000 | 1,000 |
| Next 50,000 | 6% | 3,000 | 4,000 |
| Next 50,000 | 10% | 5,000 | 9,000 |
| Next 50,000 | 14% | 7,000 | 16,000 |
| Remainder | 17% | — | — |

**Standard rate method (2025-26):**
- 15% on assessable income less personal deductions (but NOT less personal allowances).
- Two-tiered standard rate: 15% on first HKD 5 million + 16% on the portion exceeding HKD 5 million.
- **Tax Office applies whichever of progressive or standard rate gives a lower tax liability.** (EY p.578)

**Tax rebate — 2024-25:** 100% one-off tax rebate on final tax payable under salaries tax and personal assessment, subject to a ceiling of HKD 1,500 in each case. (EY p.578)

**Profits Tax rates (unincorporated business — self-employed):**
- Two-tiered regime: 7.5% on first HKD 2 million of assessable profits; 15% on the remainder.
- Only one entity per connected group may benefit from two-tiered regime. (EY p.578)

**Standard deductions (for salaries tax):** (EY p.576)
- Education expenses to specified institutions: up to HKD 100,000/year.
- Charitable donations: up to 35% of assessable income.
- Mandatory retirement scheme contributions ("recognized retirement schemes"): up to HKD 18,000/year.
- Qualifying Deferred Annuity Policy (QDAP) and/or tax-deductible MPF voluntary contributions: in aggregate up to HKD 60,000/taxpayer/year.
- Voluntary Health Insurance Scheme (VHIS) premiums: up to HKD 8,000/qualified insured person/year.
- Home mortgage interest: up to HKD 100,000/year for maximum 20 years.
- Rent (qualifying domestic tenancy): up to HKD 100,000/year.
- Additional HKD 20,000 deduction for mortgage/rent for qualifying individuals who reside with qualifying child (from 2024-25).
- Assisted reproductive services: up to HKD 100,000/year (from 2024-25). (EY p.576)

**Business deductions (profits tax):** Expenses incurred wholly, exclusively and necessarily in production of taxable profits. Capital expenditure deducted via capital allowances (100% for qualifying IT/green tech; 60% initial + reduced balance for other plant and machinery; 4% initial + annual for industrial/commercial buildings). (EY p.577)

**Notes:**
- Estate duty abolished effective 11 February 2006. (EY p.579)
- No inheritance tax, gift tax, or capital gains tax. (EY p.576, p.579)
- Personal assessment: resident taxpayers may elect to be assessed under personal assessment on the aggregate of all income from all sources; beneficial when total deductions exceed total income from a single source, or to offset business losses against employment income. (EY p.575)
- Business losses: carried forward indefinitely against future income from same business OR offset under personal assessment. Cannot be carried back. (EY p.578)
- **"Break-even point"** for progressive vs standard rate: progressive rates apply to income below a threshold (point where standard rate of 15% first applies = approximately HKD 5 million assessable income for two-tiered standard rate). (EY p.578)

## Social Security (MPF — Mandatory Provident Fund)

Hong Kong does not impose any social security taxes. Employers and employees are each required to contribute the **lower of 5% of employee's salary or HKD 1,500/month** to mandatory provident fund (MPF) schemes, unless covered by other recognized occupational retirement schemes. (EY p.579)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| MPF (mandatory) | lower of 5% or HKD 1,500/month | lower of 5% or HKD 1,500/month | lower of 5% or HKD 1,500/month |
| Social security tax | 0% | 0% | 0% |

**MPF caps:**
- Employee: max HKD 1,500/month = HKD 18,000/year.
- Self-employed: max HKD 1,500/month = HKD 18,000/year.
- Employee contributions up to HKD 18,000/year ARE deductible from salaries tax. (EY p.576)

**Notes:**
- No unemployment insurance, health insurance tax, disability tax or other social security levies.
- Mandatory retirement scheme contributions deductible up to HKD 18,000/year from salaries tax assessable income. (EY p.576)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Profits Tax (Sole Trader / Partnership)
- **Who qualifies:** Anyone carrying on a profession, trade or business in Hong Kong (resident or nonresident).
- **Tax base:** Assessable profits = accounting profits adjusted per tax rules. Business expenses wholly, exclusively and necessarily incurred for producing taxable profits deductible. Capital allowances available. (EY p.575, p.577)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Two-tiered profits tax: 7.5% on first HKD 2 million; 15% on remainder.
- **Social security:** MPF: lower of 5% or HKD 1,500/month (deductible from profits tax base up to HKD 18,000/year).
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT or GST in Hong Kong.
- **Key limits:** Only one entity per connected group may use two-tiered regime. Loss carryforward indefinitely. (EY p.578)

### Option B — Personal Assessment (Combined Tax Calculation)
- **Who qualifies:** Resident taxpayers with income from multiple sources (employment + business + rental).
- **Tax base:** Aggregate of all income from all sources; all deductions and allowances applied collectively.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive salaries tax rates 2%–17% (or standard rate 15%) applied to combined net chargeable income.
- **Social security:** Standard MPF rules apply.
- **Key limits:** Beneficial when business losses or deductions exceed income from one source; allows cross-source offsetting. (EY p.575)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Top Talent Pass Scheme (TTPS)
- **Eligibility:** (EY p.583)
  - Category A: Individuals who earned income of HKD 2.5 million (approximately) or more in the 12 months preceding application; OR
  - Category B/C: Graduates from world's top 100 universities (by recognised international rankings) with at least 3 years' work experience (Category B) or recent graduates without work experience (Category C).
- **Benefit:** Initial stay of 36 months (Category A) or 24 months (Category B/C); extendable in 3-year increments. Not required to have a job offer on initial application.
- **Duration:** Initial 24–36 months; renewable.
- **Caveats:** Extension requires secured employment at market-level remuneration.

### Quality Migrant Admission Scheme (QMAS)
- **Eligibility:** Highly skilled or talented persons seeking to settle in Hong Kong; assessed via General Points Test or Achievement-based Points Test.
- **Benefit:** Initial 36 months; extendable. No job offer required on initial application.
- **Duration:** 36 months initial; extensions in 3-year/8-year patterns.

### New Capital Investment Entrant Scheme (New CIES — from 1 March 2024)
- **Eligibility:** Investors who invest qualifying assets in Hong Kong.
- **Benefit:** Initial 24-month stay; extendable up to 3 years; permanent residence after 7 years.
- **Duration:** Ongoing while investment conditions are met.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants; using 2025-26 Salaries Tax year (1 April 2025 – 31 March 2026).
> EUR/HKD rate: 1 EUR ≈ HKD 8.44 (approximate 2025; HKD/USD fixed at ~7.80; EUR/USD ~1.08 → 1 EUR ≈ 8.44 HKD).
> "Employment" = gross salary; MPF employee contribution lower of 5% or HKD 1,500/month (max HKD 18,000/year); single personal allowance HKD 132,000; MPF deductible from salaries tax base.
> "B2B" = sole trader profits tax; two-tiered 7.5%/15%; MPF up to HKD 18,000/year; no personal allowances (profits tax only; personal assessment available but not modeled separately).
> Tax Office applies whichever of progressive rate or standard rate (15%) gives lower tax.

**Key insight:** Hong Kong is one of the most attractive jurisdictions globally for both employees and self-employed. Employment effective rates of 7–14% at €30k–€100k (including MPF) reflect the combination of the very low salaries tax rates and the large personal allowance (HKD 132,000 ≈ €15,600). The profits tax two-tiered rate (7.5%/15%) for self-employed is also very competitive. No capital gains tax, no VAT, no inheritance tax, and no social security taxes (beyond minimal MPF).

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/HKD ≈ 8.44 (HKD/USD 7.80 fixed peg; EUR/USD 1.08 approximate)
- Tax year 1 April 2025 – 31 March 2026; using annualised income
- Single personal allowance HKD 132,000 for employment
- MPF: 5% of monthly salary capped at HKD 1,500/month (HKD 18,000/year max)
- MPF deductible from salaries tax base (up to HKD 18,000)
- B2B profits tax: two-tiered (7.5% for first HKD 2 million); no personal allowances deducted (profits tax has no personal allowance — personal assessment would combine with employment if applicable)
- No additional deductions (education expenses, donations, VHIS, etc.) modeled
