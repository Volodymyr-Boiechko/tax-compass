## Personal Income Tax

Box system (Wet inkomstenbelasting 2001): income taxed in three separate boxes, each with its own rate. Negative income in one box cannot offset positive income in another. Tax year = calendar year. Filing deadline: 1 May of the following year; PAYE (Pay-As-You-Earn / loonbelasting) applies to employment income. (EY p.1080)

**Box 1 — Work and primary residence income (loon, winst, primaire woning)** (EY p.1066, 1075)

Includes employment income, business profits, income from independently performed activities (including ZZP freelancers), and deemed rental value of primary residence.

| Taxable income (EUR) — 2025 | Rate of tax | National Insurance (NI) — type A* | Total rate — type A | Total rate — type B** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 38,441 | 8.17% | 27.65% | 35.82% (~35.82%) | 37.48% |
| 38,441 – 76,817 | 37.48% | 0.00% | 37.48% | 37.48% |
| Over 76,817 | 49.50% | 0.00% | 49.50% | 49.50% |

*Type A: persons entitled to AOW state pension (age ≥ 67 in 2025). National Insurance included up to EUR 38,441.
**Type B: persons not yet entitled to AOW (age < 67). National Insurance (27.65%) included in combined rate.

**Maximum Box 1 combined rate: 49.5%** on income over EUR 76,817. (EY p.1075)

**Box 2 — Substantial shareholding (aanmerkelijk belang ≥ 5%)** (EY p.1069):
- 24.5% on first EUR 67,804 of income
- 31% on the excess above EUR 67,804
(From 1 January 2025, upper tier reduced from 33% to 31%)

**Box 3 — Savings and investments (sparen en beleggen)** (EY p.1069–1070):
- Tax-free exemption: EUR 57,684 per taxpayer (EUR 115,368 for fiscal partners) in 2025
- Deemed income percentages on net assets above exemption (provisional 2025 rates, confirmed after year-end):
  - Savings: 1.44%
  - Debts: 2.62% (reduces taxable base)
  - Other investments: 5.88%
- Flat tax rate on deemed income: **36%** in 2025
- Effective rate on investments: 5.88% × 36% = ~2.12% of asset value per year

**Personal tax credits:** Fixed amounts directly reducing tax payable (not deductions). Key credits (EY p.1075–1076):
- General credit (algemene heffingskorting): maximum EUR 3,068; phased out from EUR 28,406; EUR 0 above EUR 76,817
- Employment credit (arbeidskorting): maximum EUR 5,599; phased out from EUR 43,071; EUR 0 above EUR 129,078

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Mortgage interest deduction on primary residence: deductible against Box 1 at basic rate 37.48% (2025) (EY p.1069, 1072)
- Pension premium deduction: limited based on pension accrual space (jaarruimte/reserveringsruimte)
- Alimony payments to ex-partner (EY p.1072)
- Extraordinary expenses exceeding threshold (EY p.1072)
- Business costs (for ZZP/Freiberufler): all actual expenses deductible from Box 1 profit

**Capital gains:**
- Generally exempt from Dutch income tax (EY p.1071)
- Exceptions: capital gains on business assets (Box 1 — normal rates); capital gains on liquidation of a company (Box 1); capital gains from substantial interest ≥5% in company (Box 2 at 24.5%/31%) (EY p.1071)

**Notes:**
- Married/partners file separately on employment and business income; Box 3 assets may be freely allocated between partners (EY p.1080)
- Loss carryback: 3 years; loss carryforward: 9 years (Box 1) (EY p.1076)

## Social Security

Three categories: National Insurance Acts (Volksverzekeringen), Employee Insurance Acts (Werknemersverzekeringen), and Health Insurance Act (Zorgverzekeringswet). (EY p.1077)

**National Insurance contributions — employees 2025** (EY p.1078):

| Contribution | Rate | Max income (EUR) | Max contribution (EUR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| General Old Age Pension (AOW) | 17.90% | 38,441 | 6,881 |
| Survivor Benefits (ANW) | 0.10% | 38,441 | 38 |
| Special Healthcare (WLZ) | 9.65% | 38,441 | 3,709 |
| **Total NI (employee)** | **27.65%** | **38,441** | **10,628** |

National Insurance contributions are included in the Box 1 combined rate (see Rates above). Employer pays no National Insurance on behalf of employee — it is embedded in the employee-side rate.

**Employee Insurance contributions — employer only 2025** (EY p.1078):

| Contribution | Rate | Max income (EUR) |
|---|---|---|
| Childcare (employer) | 0.50% | 75,864 |
| Return to Work Fund (WW/RvW, average) | 1.33% | 75,864 |
| Disability Fund (Aof) — small employer | 6.28% | 75,864 |
| Disability Fund (Aof) — medium employer | 7.64% | 75,864 |
| Disability Fund (Aof) — large employer | Individual | 75,864 |

Note: Employer contributions under Employee Insurance Acts are zero for the employee (EY p.1077).

**Health Insurance (Zorgverzekering)** (EY p.1079):
- Employee standard contribution (premium to insurer): ~EUR 1,880/year (average 2025)
- Employer income-related contribution: 6.51% of salary, capped at EUR 75,864 → max EUR 4,939; paid by employer to tax authorities
- Self-employed income-related contribution: 5.26% of profit, capped at EUR 75,864 → max EUR 3,990; paid by self-employed person
- Income-related contribution is NOT deductible for income tax

**Self-employed (ZZP / eenmanszaak):**
- No unemployment insurance (WW) — one of the key disadvantages of self-employment in the Netherlands
- Health insurance: pay income-related contribution 5.26% (capped) + standard premium EUR 1,880/year
- AOW pension: ZZP'ers do accumulate AOW (state pension) rights automatically as Dutch residents, but no supplementary pension unless self-insured (third-pillar arrangement)
- No disability insurance (AOV) mandatory, but strong recommendation; market rates EUR 1,500–4,000+/year depending on coverage level and health

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — ZZP / Eenmanszaak (Sole Proprietorship — IB onderneming)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual operating a business or performing independent professional services (onafhankelijke diensten) with sufficient independence, regularity, and profit motive. Must meet Belastingdienst "ondernemerscriteria": works for multiple clients, bears financial risk, has independence over work. (belastingdienst.nl)
- **Tax base:** Box 1 profit = revenue minus all deductible business expenses, plus applicable deductions:
  1. **Zelfstandigenaftrek (self-employed deduction):** EUR 2,470 (2025) — phasing down annually toward EUR 900 by 2027. Requires ≥1,225 hours worked in own business. (belastingdienst.nl)
  2. **MKB-winstvrijstelling (SME profit exemption):** 12.7% of profit after zelfstandigenaftrek and other allowances; exempt from Box 1 tax. Available without hour requirement. (belastingdienst.nl)
  3. **Startersaftrek:** Additional EUR 2,123 for first 3 years of business (on top of zelfstandigenaftrek); max 3 times in first 5 years.
  4. **FOR (Fiscale Oudedagsreserve):** Abolished from 1 January 2023; existing FOR can be maintained or unwound.
  5. **Kleinschaligheidsinvesteringsaftrek (KIA):** Investment deduction for equipment purchases, sliding scale.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net profit after above deductions taxed at Box 1 progressive rates (combined rate 37.48% up to EUR 76,817; 49.50% above).
- **Social security:** Health insurance income-related contribution 5.26% (max EUR 3,990); standard premium ~EUR 1,880/year. No WW unemployment. AOV disability insurance: private (not mandatory).
- **VAT:** Btw (VAT) registration mandatory; standard rate 21%; reduced rate 9% for food/books/medicines; small businesses may use KOR (kleineondernemersregeling — VAT exemption) if revenue ≤ EUR 20,000/year (forfeit to input tax recovery).
- **Key limits:** Revenue cap: none. Must meet 1,225-hour criterion for zelfstandigenaftrek. "Bogus self-employment" enforcement tightened from 1 January 2025 (EY p.1067); risk of reclassification as employment if insufficient independence.

### Option B — BV (Besloten Vennootschap — Private Limited Company)

- **Who qualifies:** Any ZZP'er or professional who incorporates; minimum capital EUR 1 (formality only); recommended when profit exceeds ~EUR 100,000–150,000.
- **Tax base:** Corporate tax (vennootschapsbelasting — VPB): 19% on profit up to EUR 200,000; 25.8% above EUR 200,000.
- **DGA (Director-Major Shareholder) salary:** Deemed salary minimum EUR 56,000/year (2025) or arm's-length salary if higher; subject to full employment SS (employer + employee side).
- **Dividends:** Box 2 taxation at 24.5% (first EUR 67,804) / 31% (excess) when distributed to director-shareholder.
- **Key benefit:** VPB rate 19–25.8% vs top Box 1 rate 49.5%; retained profits taxed at lower rate; useful for high earners deferring income.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### 30% Facility (30%-regeling)

- **Eligibility:** (EY p.1072–1074)
  - Employee recruited or assigned from abroad to work in the Netherlands
  - Dutch wage tax must be withheld
  - Employee must have specific expertise that is "scarce or not available" in the Dutch labor market
  - Must have resided >16 of the 24 months prior to Dutch employment in an area >150 km from Dutch border (measured as the crow flies)
  - Minimum taxable salary threshold: EUR 46,660/year (2025; excluding 30% allowance); EUR 35,468 for holders of Dutch master's degree graduated within 3 years ("young master")
- **Benefit:** Employer may pay a tax-free allowance of up to **30% of the taxable employment income** (no upper limit); plus tax-free reimbursement of school fees for children attending international schools.
- **Duration:** (EY p.1073–1074)
  - **2025 and 2026:** 30% flat reimbursement for full duration of 60 months
  - **From 1 January 2027:** Flat 27% (reduced from 30%); salary threshold increased to EUR 50,436 (EUR 38,338 for young master)
  - Maximum total duration: 60 months (5 years); reduced by any prior Dutch residency/work periods
  - Budget Day 2025: confirmed no further cuts beyond the announced 30%→27% reduction from 2027 (EY p.1074–1075)
- **Salary cap:** From 1 January 2026 (grandfathering rules apply for those on 30% ruling before): public sector cap EUR 246,000 (2025) / EUR 262,000 (2026). Any income above this cap does not qualify for the 30% allowance. Maximum tax-free allowance = 30% × EUR 246,000 = EUR 73,800 (2025). (EY p.1073)
- **Partial non-resident status:** From 1 January 2025, the option to be treated as partial non-resident taxpayer (exempt from Box 3 on foreign assets) has been abolished. Grandfathering applies until 1 January 2027 for those who applied the 30% facility by 2023. (EY p.1066, 1072)
- **Application:** Joint request by employer and employee within 4 months of start of Dutch employment; "30% statement" issued by Belastingdienst. (EY p.1072)
- **Caveats:** From 1 January 2026, certain actual extraterritorial costs (utility bills, private telephone calls) may no longer be reimbursed tax-free when opting for the 30% facility. Choice between actual costs and 30% facility must be made at first wage period of each year. (EY p.1075)

## 2026 Changes

- **30% ruling — 2026:** Flat 30% reimbursement rate confirmed for full year 2026 (the previously announced phase-down to 30-20-10% schedule was reversed on Budget Day 2025). Salary cap EUR 262,000 (indexing from EUR 246,000 in 2025). (EY p.1073–1075)
- **30% ruling — from 2027:** Rate reduces to 27%; salary threshold increases to EUR 50,436 (EUR 38,338 young master). (EY p.1074)
- **Box 1 rates 2025:** Combined top rate 49.5% above EUR 76,817; lower bracket threshold EUR 38,441. Subject to annual indexation.
- **Box 2 rates 2025:** 24.5% / 31% (upper rate reduced from 33% as of 1 January 2025). (EY p.1069)
- **Box 3 reform:** Box 3 under ongoing litigation (2021 Supreme Court ruling). Deemed income approach (provisional percentages) used for 2025 pending structural reform. New "actual return" Box 3 system proposed from 2027; details still being finalized.
- **Zelfstandigenaftrek 2025:** EUR 2,470 (phasing down; EUR 1,200 in 2026; target EUR 900 in 2027). belastingdienst.nl
- **Bogus self-employment enforcement:** Full enforcement from 1 January 2025 (Belastingdienst no longer only enforcing in "evident" cases). Risk of reclassification of ZZP contracts to employment increased significantly. (EY p.1067)
- **Partial non-resident status abolished:** From 1 January 2025 (transitional rule to 2027 for pre-2023 30% ruling holders). (EY p.1072)
- **Health insurance income-related contribution:** Employer 6.51%; self-employed 5.26% (2025; subject to annual update by Ministry of Health).
- **AOW entitlement age:** 67 in 2025; may increase in future based on life expectancy adjustments.

> Health insurance for ZZP: 5.26% income-related (capped) + EUR 1,880 standard premium.

**Key observations:**
- ZZP becomes more tax-efficient than employment above ~EUR 45–50k thanks to zelfstandigenaftrek + MKB-winstvrijstelling
- ZZP at EUR 30k is less efficient due to health insurance cost and reduced credits
- At EUR 100k ZZP saves ~9 percentage points vs employment (32.9% vs 42.0%)
- 30% ruling dramatically reduces effective rate for qualifying expat employees: at EUR 60k gross, 30% tax-free → effective taxable only EUR 42k → substantial reduction

**Assumptions:**
- Single, type B (age < 67 in 2025); National Insurance fully embedded in 37.48% bracket rate
- Tax credits approximate (general + employment); exact values depend on precise income and age
- ZZP: 15% business expenses; zelfstandigenaftrek EUR 2,470 (2025); MKB 12.7%; no startersaftrek; no AOV disability insurance premium modeled
- Health insurance: standard premium EUR 1,880; income-related 5.26% for ZZP (capped EUR 75,864); employer pays 6.51% for employment (not shown)
- Box 3 wealth tax not modeled (assumed < EUR 57,684 exemption threshold)
