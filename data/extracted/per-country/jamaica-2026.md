## Personal Income Tax

Jamaica taxes residents on worldwide income; non-domiciled residents are taxed on Jamaican-source income plus foreign income remitted to Jamaica. Individuals resident for 3+ months in a tax year who perform work in Jamaica are taxed in Jamaica regardless of where payment is received. Nonresidents taxed only on Jamaican-source income. Tax year = calendar year. PAYE withholding by employers; annual return by 15 March. No capital gains tax in Jamaica. (EY pp. 721–727)

### Income Tax Rates (2025)

| Annual statutory income (JMD) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 1,774,554 | **0%** (income tax threshold — exempt) |
| 1,774,555 – 6,000,000 | **25%** |
| Above 6,000,000 | **30%** (i.e., 25% on first JMD6M + additional 5% surtax on excess over JMD6M) |

**Key notes:**
- The JMD1,774,554 annual income tax threshold applies only to **Jamaican residents** (including resident self-employed) — nonresidents are not eligible for this exemption
- Resident self-employed individuals are also entitled to the JMD1,774,554 effective income tax exemption
- The additional 5% tax on statutory income above JMD6,000,000 makes the effective rate on excess 30% (25% base + 5% additional)
- **No capital gains tax** in Jamaica (EY p. 723)

### Withholding Taxes

| Income type | WHT rate |
|---|---|
| Interest (paid by prescribed persons to residents) | 25% |
| Dividends (Jamaican resident companies to residents) | 15% (final) |
| Dividends to nonresidents | 15% (from 1 April 2025) |
| Directors' fees | 25% |
| Management fees, technical service fees, royalties to nonresidents | 33⅓% |

### Key Exempt Income

- Pension income from approved superannuation/pension scheme: up to JMD250,040 exempt (for pensioners under 55); JMD250,040 + additional age exemption for those 65+ (effective 1 April 2024)
- Lump sums from government's Consolidated Fund: exempt
- Handicapped individuals: exempt on all salary and pension income

## Social Security

Jamaica has three main payroll-related contributions: NIS, NHT, and Education Tax. (EY pp. 726–727)

### National Insurance Scheme (NIS)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| NIS | **3%** | **3%** | **6%** |

**Income ceiling:** JMD5,000,000/year (annual insurable wage ceiling). Maximum annual employee contribution: JMD150,000; maximum employer contribution: JMD150,000. Above ceiling: no contributions. (EY p. 726)

**Domestic workers:** Employer and employee each pay JMD250/week (flat rate, not income-based).

### National Housing Trust (NHT)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| NHT | **2%** | **3%** | **3%** of gross taxable income |

**Notes:**
- NHT contributions are **refundable** after 7 years of contribution, or in full on retirement at 65, or if leaving Jamaica permanently
- No stated income ceiling on NHT

### Education Tax

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Education Tax | **2.25%** | **3.5%** | **2.25%** of net taxable income (after NIS/pension deductions) |

**Summary table:**

| Contribution | Employee total | Employer total | Self-employed total |
|---|---|---|---|
| NIS (up to JMD5M ceiling) | 3% | 3% | 6% |
| NHT | 2% | 3% | 3% |
| Education Tax | 2.25% | 3.5% | 2.25% |
| **Total** | **7.25%** | **9.5%** | **11.25%** |

**Pension deductions:** Employee contributions of up to 10% of annual remuneration to approved pension schemes are deductible for income tax. (EY p. 723)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals and sole traders carrying on business or professional activities in Jamaica
- **Tax base:** Net profit (gross receipts minus wholly and exclusively incurred business expenses); self-employed may also deduct charitable donations to approved charities up to 5% of taxable income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same rates as employment income (0% threshold JMD1,774,554; 25%/30%); self-employed entitled to income tax threshold
- **Social security:** NIS 6% (up to JMD5M ceiling) + NHT 3% + Education Tax 2.25% = **11.25% total** on income
- **GCT (VAT) obligations:** General Consumption Tax (GCT) at 15%; registration mandatory if annual turnover > JMD15,000,000 (effective 1 April 2025; raised from prior threshold)
- **Loss carryforward:** Tax losses from trading carried forward; annual deduction capped at 50% of aggregate income from all sources (exception: first 5 years of trade, or if gross revenue < JMD15M)
- **Filing:** Annual return due 15 March; PAYE estimated payments not applicable for self-employed; quarterly estimates typically required

### Option B — No separate simplified regime

Jamaica has no separate flat-rate or presumptive taxation regime for self-employed individuals; all self-employed are subject to the standard progressive rates.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Dedicated Newcomer Tax Regime

Jamaica does not have a preferential income tax regime for new tax residents, inbound assignees, or returning Jamaicans. Standard rates apply from day one of tax residency.

### Income Tax Threshold for Residents

- The JMD1,774,554 income tax threshold (2025) is available only to persons present in Jamaica for 183 days or more in the tax year
- Non-resident employees and self-employed individuals cannot benefit from this threshold

## 2026 Changes

- **NIS ceiling maintained at JMD5,000,000/year** for 2025; verify for 2026 adjustment (EY p. 726)
- **GCT registration threshold raised to JMD15,000,000** (effective 1 April 2025, from prior lower threshold) (EY p. 724)
- **Dividends WHT to nonresidents:** Rate confirmed at 15% effective 1 April 2025 (EY p. 722)
- **Loss carryforward threshold:** JMD15 million gross revenue threshold for unlimited loss carryforward confirmed effective 1 April 2025 (EY p. 724)
- **No structural income tax changes announced** for 2026 as of EY data date

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, resident (183+ days), no dependants.
> Income tax threshold JMD1,774,554 applied.
> **1 EUR ≈ JMD 172** (approximate; USD/JMD ≈ 158; EUR/USD ≈ 1.09).
> Employment: income tax + NIS (employee 3%, capped at JMD5M) + NHT (2%) + Education Tax (2.25%).
> B2B: same income tax rates + NIS (self-employed 6%) + NHT (3%) + Education Tax (2.25%).
> Employer contributions (NIS 3%, NHT 3%, Education Tax 3.5%) not shown.

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Income tax threshold for 2026:** The JMD1,774,554 threshold is confirmed for 2025. Jamaica periodically adjusts the threshold; verify the 2026 amount at taj.gov.jm.
- [ ] **Education Tax base (employment vs self-employed):** EY notes Education Tax for self-employed is 2.25% of "net taxable income (after deduction of pension and NIS contributions)." The exact base may differ slightly from gross — simplified above.
- [ ] **NIS ceiling for 2026:** JMD5,000,000 ceiling stated for 2025 (EY p. 726); this is adjusted periodically. Verify for 2026.
- [ ] **Pension deductibility:** Employee contributions up to 10% of annual remuneration to approved pension schemes are deductible. This would reduce income tax at €30k–€100k if pension contributions are made, improving the effective rate.
- [ ] **No capital gains tax:** Jamaica explicitly has no capital gains tax. Investment income (interest, dividends) subject to WHT at 25%/15% — potentially final. Not modeled above.
- [ ] **GCT compliance:** Registration threshold JMD15,000,000 (≈€87k). At €60k and €100k B2B, freelancers may need to register and charge 15% GCT; cost depends on client's ability to claim input credits.
