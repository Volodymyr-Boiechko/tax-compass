## Personal Income Tax

The US Virgin Islands (USVI) income tax system **mirrors the US IRC** — the applicable law is the US Internal Revenue Code with "US Virgin Islands" substituted for "United States" throughout. Tax rates, bracket thresholds, deductions, allowances, capital gains rules, and employer-provided stock option rules are identical to the United States. (EY p. 1683)

**Residency (Bona Fide Resident):** An individual is a bona fide resident of the USVI if they satisfy ALL of:
- Present for at least 183 days in the USVI during the tax year (substantial presence test under applicable rules)
- Do not have a tax home outside the USVI during the tax year
- Do not have a closer connection to the United States or a foreign country than to the USVI

**Liability:**
- Bona fide USVI residents: taxed on worldwide income; file with the Virgin Islands Bureau of Internal Revenue (VIBIR)
- US citizens/resident aliens who are NOT bona fide USVI residents but have USVI-source income or income effectively connected with a USVI trade or business: file identical returns with both the US and USVI; USVI tax = total US tax × (USVI AGI / worldwide AGI), computed via Form 8689
- Non-US-citizens who are not USVI residents or US residents: file Form 1040 NR with the USVI and pay tax on USVI-source income
- Worldwide gross income > USD 75,000 (per spouse separately): must file Form 8898 in the year of becoming or ceasing to be a bona fide USVI resident (EY p. 1684)

**Notes:**
- All US federal deductions and allowances apply equally in the USVI (mortgage interest, charitable contributions, state/local taxes up to USD 10,000, retirement contributions, etc.)
- Capital gains rates: same as US federal (0%, 15%, or 20% based on income; 0% rate up to ~USD 48,350 for single filers in 2025)
- Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT): 3.8% surtax on investment income for high earners (same US threshold: AGI > USD 200,000 single / USD 250,000 married)
- Additional Medicare Tax: 0.9% on wages/SE income above USD 200,000/USD 250,000 — same as US federal
- Non-resident withholding: 11% (= 10% base + 10% surcharge) instead of the US 30% federal withholding rate (EY p. 1684)
- **Money-transfer charge:** 3% charge on money transfers out of the USVI, enacted late 2024, effective mid-December 2024 (EY p. 1685)

## Social Security

US Social Security (FICA) and self-employment taxes are imposed in the USVI. **Payments are remitted to the US mainland** (not to the USVI Bureau of Internal Revenue). Same rates as US federal (EY p. 1685):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security (OASDI) | 6.2% | 6.2% | 12.4% |
| Medicare (HI) | 1.45% | 1.45% | 2.9% |
| Additional Medicare Tax | 0.9% above USD 200k | — | 0.9% above USD 200k |
| **Total (standard)** | **7.65%** | **7.65%** | **15.3%** |

**Income ceiling:**
- Social Security wage base 2025: **USD 176,100**
- Medicare: no ceiling (1.45% on all wages; +0.9% above USD 200k)

**Notes:**
- Self-employed individuals pay both employee and employer portions (15.3%) but deduct 50% of SE tax from AGI
- Same FICA rules and caps as continental US

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Schedule C (US Federal Rules)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual conducting business or professional services in the USVI; same rules as US federal Schedule C filer
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue less allowable business deductions (ordinary and necessary business expenses); same rules as US
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive federal rates 10%–37% on net income (after standard deduction or itemized deductions)
- **Social security:** Self-employment tax 15.3% on net SE income up to Social Security wage base; 2.9% Medicare above; deduct 50% of SE tax from AGI
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT in the USVI; Gross Receipts Tax (GRT) applies to business revenue at approximately 5% (USVI-specific; not federal)
- **Key limits:** Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction of up to 20% of qualified business income may apply for pass-through entities (same as US federal Section 199A); SE tax deduction (50%) reduces AGI

### Option B — S Corporation or LLC (Pass-Through)

- **Who qualifies:** USVI-resident business owners operating through S corporations or LLCs
- **Tax base:** Pass-through income subject to owner's individual progressive rates; same as US federal rules
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates; QBI deduction may apply
- **USVI Economic Development Program (EDP):** Significant tax incentives available for qualifying USVI businesses — see Special Regimes below

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### USVI Economic Development Program (EDP) — EY p. 1684 (referenced via US chapter)

- **Eligibility:** Businesses that establish genuine operations in the USVI, create local jobs, and meet minimum investment/employment requirements
- **Benefit:** Qualifying businesses and their bona fide USVI resident owners may receive:
  - Up to 90% reduction in USVI income tax (effective rate = 10% of normal)
  - Up to 100% exemption from USVI gross receipts tax, excise tax, and property tax
- **Duration:** Up to 30 years
- **Key conditions:** Bona fide residency in USVI required; genuine business operations (not a paper office); minimum employment and investment thresholds
- **Caveats:** IRS scrutiny; must file Form 8898; worldwide income must be properly allocated; US citizens receive only a credit for USVI tax, not full elimination of US federal liability

### Bona Fide Residency — Path for US Citizens

- US citizens who become bona fide USVI residents and meet the 183-day presence test can redirect their income tax payments entirely to the USVI (at normal federal rates) rather than to the IRS, while potentially qualifying for EDP reductions
- Combined with EDP, effective income tax rate may be reduced to ~3.7% (10% of normal rate = 37% → 3.7%) for high earners qualifying under EDP

## 2026 Changes

- **PIT brackets:** Standard annual inflation adjustment; 2026 brackets will be published by IRS in late 2025 (same mechanism as US federal)
- **Standard deduction 2026:** Will increase with inflation; 2025 single = USD 15,000
- **Social Security wage base 2026:** Will be adjusted; 2025 = USD 176,100
- **Money-transfer charge 3%:** Enacted late 2024, effective mid-December 2024; no regulations or administrative guidance issued as of EY data date; person facilitating transfer is responsible for collecting and remitting (EY p. 1685)
- **EDP rules:** No announced changes at EY data date
