## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PIT on worldwide income for residents; Aruban-source income for non-residents. Tax year = calendar year. Rates range from 0% to 52%. Currency: Aruban florin (AWG); AWG 1.79 ≈ USD 1 (fixed peg). (EY p.66)

Residents and non-residents are subject to the same progressive rates. Certain income types (dividends, severance payments, pension commutations) are taxed at a flat 25%. (EY p.68)

**Progressive PIT rates (employment and business income):**

EY states rates range from 0% to 52% on a progressive scale. The exact bracket thresholds are not fully listed in EY — only that the scale runs 0%–52% with certain income taxed at up to 52%. (EY p.68)

| Taxable income (AWG) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Lower brackets | 0% – progressive |
| Upper bracket | Up to **52%** |

**Flat rates for specific income types:**

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends from Aruban resident companies (withholding) | **10%** (may be reduced to 0% if conditions met) |
| Capital gains — liquidation of company / share repurchase above paid-up capital | **25%** |
| Capital gains — substantial interest (≥ 25% equity) in domestic corporation | **25%** |
| Capital gains — disposal of business assets / qualifying independent activities | **Up to 52%** |
| Deemed dividend (excessive loan > AWG 500,000 from own company) | **25%** |
| Certain pension commutations / surrender of pension rights | **25%** |
| Interest from savings accounts at local/foreign banks | **0%** (exempt) |

(EY pp.67–68)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**

| Deduction | Amount (AWG) |
|---|---|
| Fixed tax credit | 30,000 |
| Employment expenses (3% of employment income, max) | 1,500/year |
| Entrepreneur's deduction (self-employed/sole proprietors) | 2,400/year |
| Employee savings funds / pension funds (annual tax-free allowance) | 3,360/year |
| Each child under 18 | 750 |
| Each child 16–27 | 1,200 |
| Additional per child 16–27 studying abroad | 3,800 |
| Disabled child 16–27 (earning < 50% of healthy children's salary) | 1,200 |
| Mortgage interest + life insurance premium for home ownership (max) | 40,000/year |
| Maintenance costs for protected monument principal residence (max) | 5,000/year |
| Life insurance premiums entitling to annuity (max) | 10,000/year |
| Charitable donations (charities, churches, sports institutions, max) | 50,000/year |
| Study loan interest/costs (max, 10-year limit) | 10,000/year |
| Extraordinary expenses (medical, relatives support, education — above threshold) | Actual |

(EY pp.67–68)

**Notes:**
- Losses may be carried forward for five years (EY p.68)
- Married couples are taxed separately on employment income and AOV pension income unless they request combined taxation (EY p.71)
- Capital gains are generally tax-free unless they qualify as income from independently performed activities or fall into special categories (EY p.67)

## Social Security

All resident individuals registered at the Aruba registry office are subject to social security contributions. Non-residents not registered at the registry office are not subject to AZV or AOV/AWW. (EY p.70)

**AOV/AWW — General Old Age Pension Act / General Widows and Orphans Act:**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| AOV/AWW | **5%** | **10.5%** |

- Applied to premium income up to **AWG 85,000/year** (USD 47,486)
- Contributions cease at age 65

**AZV — General Medical Insurance:**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| AZV (medical) | **1.6%** | **8.9%** |

- Applied to salary up to **AWG 85,000/year** (USD 47,486)
- Resident must have main residency in Aruba for AZV coverage

**OV / ZV — Disability and Sickness Insurance (employer-paid only):**

| Contribution | Employer |
|---|---|
| OV (General Disablement Insurance Act) | **0.25%–2.5%** of salary |
| ZV (General Sickness Insurance Act) | **2.65%** up to AWG 70,200/year (USD 39,218) |

(EY p.70)

**Summary — employee-side social security:**

| Scheme | Employee rate | Salary ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| AOV/AWW (pension/survivors) | 5.0% | AWG 85,000/year |
| AZV (medical) | 1.6% | AWG 85,000/year |
| **Total employee** | **6.6%** | AWG 85,000/year |

**Summary — employer-side social security:**

| Scheme | Employer rate | Salary ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| AOV/AWW | 10.5% | AWG 85,000/year |
| AZV | 8.9% | AWG 85,000/year |
| OV (disability) | 0.25%–2.5% | no cap stated |
| ZV (sickness) | 2.65% | AWG 70,200/year |
| **Total employer (approx.)** | **~22%–24%** | varies |

**Self-employed:** Must pay social security contributions within two months after receipt of assessment. Rates not separately stated for self-employed in EY — general coverage applies. (EY p.71)

**Totalization agreements:** Individuals temporarily employed in Aruba registered at the registry are subject to Aruban social security; may be subject to contributions in both home country and Aruba. Double-contribution relief available through social security agreements. (EY p.70)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietorship / Independent Professional

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying out an independent business or professional activity in Aruba
- **Tax base:** Annual profit (gross revenue minus fully deductible business expenses); fair market value of in-kind receipts included
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT rates (0%–52%) on annual profit; entrepreneur's deduction of AWG 2,400/year
- **Social security:** Social security contributions apply (same as employees, registered at registry office); exact self-employed rates not separately stated
- **VAT obligations:** Turnover tax (BBO) applies; standard rate 1.5%; special rates for certain sectors
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; losses carried forward 5 years

(EY pp.66–68)

### Option B — Company (NV/VBA)

- **Who qualifies:** Entrepreneurs who incorporate; corporate tax applies at entity level; salary/dividends extracted by individual owner
- **Tax base:** Salary: progressive PIT 0%–52%; Dividends: 10% withholding (may reduce to 0%)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Corporate income tax (separate regime) + personal PIT on distributions
- **Social security:** Standard employee SS applies to salary drawn
- **VAT obligations:** Turnover tax applies at entity level
- **Key limits:** Excessive loans > AWG 500,000 from own company deemed as dividend (25% tax)

(EY pp.67–68)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Facility

- **Eligibility:** Must not have been an Aruba resident for at least 5 consecutive years prior to employment; must receive annual remuneration of at least AWG 150,000; must possess scarce skills in Aruba
- **Benefit:** The following fringe benefits are received tax-free:
  - Wages in kind: up to AWG 15,000/year tax-free
  - Education allowances for children: up to AWG 25,000/child/year tax-free
  - Housing rental allowance: up to AWG 2,500/month tax-free
- **Additional benefits for employees recruited abroad:**
  - Travel expense reimbursements on arrival and repatriation (including household shipping and one personal car)
  - Hotel expenses for first two months after arrival
  - Relocation allowance on arrival: up to AWG 15,000 (max 2 months' salary)
  - Annual travel reimbursement for one return ticket to country of origin for first 5 years
- **Application deadline:** Must be filed within 4 months of start of employment; applies retroactively to start of employment if filed in time
- **Caveats:** If net employment contract is concluded, the taxable wage will not be grossed up (EY pp.68–69)

## 2026 Changes

- **Work permit rule change (25 April 2025):** Until further notice, work permit applications for all jobs in all sectors do not require the Departamento di Progreso Laboral job market test (arbeidsmarkttoets). This simplifies hiring of foreign workers. (EY p.72)
- As of 1 January 2023, transfer tax is no longer due on the inheritance of immovable property located in Aruba. (EY p.69)
- No specific PIT rate changes for 2026 announced in EY guide.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (fixed tax credit AWG 30,000; employment deduction AWG 1,500).
> **1 EUR ≈ AWG 1.95** (AWG is pegged at AWG 1.79/USD; using EUR/USD ≈ 1.09 → AWG 1.95/EUR approximately).
> Employment scenario: progressive PIT 0%–52%; employee SS 6.6% capped at AWG 85,000/year.
> B2B scenario: sole proprietorship; entrepreneur deduction AWG 2,400; progressive PIT 0%–52%; self-employed SS (modelled as 6.6%, capped AWG 85,000).
> Note: EY does not publish full bracket table — effective rates are estimated using the 0%–52% progressive scale. Exact bracket thresholds not available from EY; estimates below are indicative.
