## Personal Income Tax

There is NO personal income tax in Saudi Arabia for individuals. Individuals conducting business activities in Saudi Arabia may be subject to Saudi Arabia corporate tax. (EY p.1353)

**Tax residency:** Under Article 3(a) of the Income Tax Law, a natural person is considered a Saudi tax resident if:
- Has a permanent place of residence in Saudi Arabia AND resides in Saudi Arabia for a total period of not less than 30 days in the tax year, OR
- Resides in Saudi Arabia for a period of not less than 183 days in the tax year

A tax residency certificate can be obtained for both domestic legislation purposes and for double tax treaty application. (EY p.1353)

### PIT Rate Table

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| All employment income (all individuals) | 0% |
| Investment income (individuals) | 0% |
| Business / professional income (sole traders) | Subject to corporate income tax at 20% (or 2.5% zakat for Saudis/GCC nationals) |

**Standard deductions / allowances:** Not applicable — no individual PIT.

**Capital gains:** Taxed as part of regular business profits (corporate tax); not separately taxed for individuals on personal investments. (EY p.1353)

**Zakat:** A religious levy payable by Saudi or GCC nationals resident in Saudi Arabia engaged in business activities. Applied on net worth or zakat base as adjusted for zakat purposes. (EY p.1353)

**Notes:**
- No personal income tax for any individual (Saudi, GCC, or non-GCC)
- No wealth tax, no individual capital gains tax (outside of business context)
- No inheritance or gift tax for individuals
- VAT: Saudi Arabia has 15% VAT (introduced 2018, increased from 5% in July 2020) — applies to B2B/B2C transactions but is not an income tax

## Social Security

### Saudi Nationals

Saudi nationals working in public and private sectors are subject to the Saudi social security scheme (GOSI — General Organization for Social Insurance). Monthly contributions calculated on salary specified in employment contract, minimum SAR1,500 and maximum SAR45,000. (EY p.1354)

**New hires (joining on/after 3 July 2024 — New Social Insurance Law):**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Unemployment (SANED) | 0.75% | 0.75% |
| Retirement | 9.5% | 9.5% |
| Compensation and disability | — | 2% |
| **Total** | **10.25%** | **12.25%** |

**Existing contributors under 50 (as of July 2024) or with <20 years of service:**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Unemployment (SANED) | 0.75% | 0.75% |
| Retirement | 9% | 9% |
| Compensation and disability | — | 2% |
| **Total** | **9.75%** | **11.75%** |

**Existing contributors aged 50+ (as of July 2024) or with ≥20 years of service:**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Unemployment (SANED) | 0.75% | 0.75% |
| Retirement | 9% | 9% |
| Compensation and disability | — | 2% |
| **Total** | **9.75%** | **11.75%** |

Note: Retirement contribution will increase by 0.5% annually for both employers and employees under the new system (new hires only), up to 2% over the multi-year phase-in. (EY p.1354)

**Income ceiling:** SAR1,500/month minimum; SAR45,000/month maximum contribution base.

### GCC (Non-Saudi) Nationals

GCC non-Saudi nationals continue to be subject to their home country social security scheme while working in Saudi Arabia (GCC Unified Law of Insurance Protection Extension). Home country rates and caps apply. (EY p.1355)

### Non-GCC (Expat) Nationals

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Occupational hazards insurance | 0% | 2% |
| **Total** | **0%** | **2%** |

- Non-GCC national employees are NOT subject to GOSI pension or unemployment contributions
- Employer pays 2% occupational hazards insurance on salary between SAR400 minimum and SAR45,000 maximum monthly
- Non-GCC national employees are entitled to end-of-service benefit upon termination after 2+ years of continuous employment: half monthly wage per year for first 5 years, full monthly wage per year from year 6 onwards (EY p.1355)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Operating as Professional / Sole Trader

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals carrying on professional services or trade in Saudi Arabia
- **Tax base:** Net business income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Subject to Saudi Arabia corporate income tax at 20% on net profits (individuals conducting business); Saudi/GCC nationals subject to zakat instead (2.5% on zakat base)
- **Social security:** GOSI applies for Saudi nationals; 2% occupational hazard (employer-side) for non-GCC nationals; 0% employee SS for non-GCC nationals
- **VAT obligations:** 15% VAT applies to taxable supplies; VAT registration threshold SAR375,000 mandatory / SAR187,500 voluntary annual turnover
- **Key limits:** Foreign professionals cannot carry out trading activities without local sponsorship; a foreign professional (accountant, engineer, lawyer, consultant) may conduct business by setting up a professional partnership with a Saudi national under professional partnership regulations (EY p.1358)

### Option B — Premium Residency / Entrepreneur Category

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals establishing a startup company to develop specific industries in Saudi Arabia; minimum investment SAR400,000 for 5-year residency, SAR15,000,000 for permanent residency
- **Tax base / rate:** Corporate tax applies to business profits; no individual PIT
- **Social security:** Depends on nationality
- **Key benefits:**
  - Startup companies held by PRP holders exempt from Saudization requirements for up to 3 years
  - Self-sponsored long-term residence permit (no employer sponsorship needed)
  - 5-year permit (renewable once) for SAR400,000 investment; permanent residency for SAR15,000,000

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Premium Residency Permit (PRP) — Introduced 2019, Expanded 2023

- **Eligibility (five categories):**
  1. **Special Talent:** Executives (min. SAR80,000/month), researchers in specified fields (SAR14,000/month), healthcare/scientific professionals (SAR35,000/month); valid 5 years, renewable once
  2. **Gifted Individuals:** Exceptional record in culture, arts, or sports; requires approved award and official recommendation; 5 years renewable
  3. **Investors:** Minimum investment SAR7,000,000 into Saudi economy + create ≥10 jobs; permanent residency granted immediately (conditional first 2 years)
  4. **Entrepreneurs:** Startup company in specific industries; SAR400,000 → 5-year residency; SAR15,000,000 → permanent residency
  5. **Real Estate Owners:** Own or have right to benefit from residential real estate in Saudi Arabia valued ≥SAR4,000,000; residency maintained while ownership continues
- **Benefit:** Self-sponsored long-term residency; not tied to employer; PRP holders not counted in Saudization (Nitaqat) ratios for certain categories
- **Duration:** 5 years renewable (Special Talent, Gifted) or permanent (Investors, Entrepreneurs ≥SAR15M, Real Estate Owners)
- **Tax benefit:** 0% personal income tax applies equally to all — no additional tax benefit specifically for PRP holders vs. regular expats (EY pp.1358–1360)

### Standard Employment Visa / Iqama

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals with employment contracts with Saudi-registered companies
- **Benefit:** Residence permit (Iqama), renewable annually; 0% personal income tax
- **Key requirement:** Must be sponsored by employer; Saudization (Nitaqat) ratios apply to employers with 5+ employees

## 2026 Changes

- **New Social Insurance Law (effective 3 July 2024):** New hire Saudi nationals subject to 10.25% employee / 12.25% employer contributions; retirement rate increasing 0.5% annually. Existing contributors continue under prior rates (9.75%/11.75%). This change was effective in 2024/2025. (EY p.1354)
- No personal income tax changes — Saudi Arabia has no PIT and none is planned
- **15% VAT** remains in force (increased from 5% in July 2020)
- PRP scheme (five categories) in force as expanded in 2023
- No additional announced changes for 2026 as of October 2025

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants.
> Saudi Arabia has 0% personal income tax for all individuals.
> Only applicable cost for non-GCC expats: no employee SS; end-of-service gratuity is employer cost.
> Currency: SAR (Saudi Arabian Riyal; 1 EUR ≈ 4.05 SAR approximate; USD/SAR ≈ 3.75 fixed peg).

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment (non-GCC expat) | 0 | 0 | 0 | **0%** |
| €30,000 | B2B sole trader (non-GCC expat) | ~20% on net profit | 0 | ~14–20% of gross | **~14–20%** |
| €60,000 | Employment (non-GCC expat) | 0 | 0 | 0 | **0%** |
| €60,000 | B2B sole trader (non-GCC expat) | ~20% on net profit | 0 | ~14–20% of gross | **~14–20%** |
| €100,000 | Employment (non-GCC expat) | 0 | 0 | 0 | **0%** |
| €100,000 | B2B sole trader (non-GCC expat) | ~20% on net profit | 0 | ~14–20% of gross | **~14–20%** |

**Employment effective rate: 0% at all income levels for non-GCC expats.**
(Saudi nationals: 0% PIT + 9.75%–10.25% employee GOSI → effective ~10% SS only)

**Sole trader working (illustrative at €60,000 gross):**
- Gross revenue: ~SAR243,000 (at 4.05 SAR/EUR)
- Assume 30% deductible expenses → net profit = SAR170,100
- Corporate tax (non-Saudi individual operating as business): 20% × 170,100 = SAR34,020 (~€8,400)
- Occupational hazard: employer-side cost only (0% employee SS)
- Effective rate on gross: ~14%

**Key insight for expats:** Saudi Arabia is one of the most favorable jurisdictions globally for employed professionals. Combined with 0% PIT, non-GCC expats also pay 0% social security contributions as employees. The only cost is the standard of living; there is no income tax burden whatsoever. This makes Saudi Arabia extremely attractive for high-earning IT professionals, consultants, and executives willing to relocate.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/SAR = 4.05 (approximate; USD/SAR = 3.75 fixed peg; EUR/USD ≈ 1.08)
- Non-GCC national expat
- Sole trader: 30% assumed deductible expenses; corporate tax rate 20% applies
- Saudi national SS (GOSI): new hire rate 10.25% employee on salary up to SAR45,000/month
- 15% VAT not modeled (pass-through cost, not income tax)
