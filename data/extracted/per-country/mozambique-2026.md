## Personal Income Tax

> Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents taxed on Mozambique-source income. Tax year is the calendar year. Employment income taxed at source as a final tax (PAYE monthly). Self-employed / other income: annual return by 30 April; balance due by 30 May. Administered by AT (Autoridade Tributária de Moçambique).

### Resident Individual Rates

| Annual taxable income — Exceeding (MZN) | Not exceeding (MZN) | Rate % | Rebate (MZN) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 42,000 | 10 | — |
| 42,000 | 168,000 | 15 | 2,100 |
| 168,000 | 504,000 | 20 | 10,500 |
| 504,000 | 1,512,000 | 25 | 35,700 |
| 1,512,000 | — | 32 | 141,540 |

**Annual income of MZN 225,000 or less is not taxable** (EY p.1050)

**Tax credits (personal deductions):**
- MZN 1,800 per taxpayer
- MZN 600 for one dependent child
- MZN 900 for two dependent children
- MZN 1,200 for three dependent children
- MZN 1,800 for four or more dependent children

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Meal subsidies not exceeding the minimum salary in force: exempt
- Other allowances and similar payments within legally established limits: exempt
- Pensions: exempt
- Compensation within the scope of a dismissal process: exempt
- Compensation paid by employee to employer: deductible expense

**Notes:**
- Employment income: withheld monthly as a final tax; employees not required to file annual returns unless they have other income
- Residents with other income: file annual return by 30 April
- Capital gains: taxable at rates varying from 10% to 32%; tax base from share transfers decreases with holding period; capital losses may offset capital gains only
- Investment income (shares listed on Mozambique Stock Exchange, except debt titles): 10% final withholding tax
- Investment income (other): 20% WHT; resident taxpayers include in annual return with credit for WHT
- Gambling: 10% final WHT
- Nonresidents: general WHT rate 20%; artistic/entertainment income: 10%
- Nonresident capital gains: taxed at 32%
- Losses in business/professional activities: carry forward 5 years; no carryback
- INSS (social security) contributions: deductible in computing taxable income (implied by exclusion of social security from taxable income)

## Social Security

> Social security contributions payable monthly on salaries, wages, bonuses and regular compensation income (including productivity premiums and housing allowances). Employer withholds employee contributions monthly. (EY p.1051)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| INSS (Instituto Nacional de Segurança Social) | 3% | 4% | — |
| **Total** | **3%** | **4%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** None specified in EY source
**Notes:**
- Resident foreign employees exempt if they prove they contribute to a similar social security regime in another country
- Foreign employees may not be subject to social security under totalization agreements and specific employment conditions
- Self-employed social security treatment not specified in EY individual tax section

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Business / Self-Employed Professional
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals carrying out business activities independently, providing consulting services, or engaging in technical, artistic or scientific projects
- **Tax base:** Income derived from such activities; business deductions allowed for non-capital expenses wholly and exclusively incurred to produce income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates (10%–32%); annual return by 30 April; quarterly installments
- **Social security:** INSS 3% (employee portion) unless exempted
- **VAT obligations:** VAT applies; standard rate 17%
- **Key limits:** Annual income ≤ MZN 225,000 not taxable; losses carry forward 5 years

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

> No specific preferential tax regime for new tax residents identified in EY source. Residents taxed on worldwide income.

### Source-Based Exemption for Nonresidents
- **Eligibility:** Nonresidents
- **Benefit:** Taxed only on Mozambique-source income (WHT at 20% general rate as final tax)
- **Duration:** Ongoing while nonresident
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Petroleum sector workers may have separate treatment (common in Mozambique) — not detailed in EY individual tax section

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 70 MZN (approximate mid-2025; MZN subject to volatility — Medium confidence).
> Employment uses progressive rates (employment income is final at source). B2B uses same progressive rates plus 3% INSS.
> Tax credit of MZN 1,800 (taxpayer credit) applied.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (MZN) | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden (MZN) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈MZN 2,100,000) | Employment | 527,460 | 63,000 (3%) | 590,460 | ~28.1% |
| €30,000 (≈MZN 2,100,000) | B2B | 527,460 | 63,000 (3%) | 590,460 | ~28.1% |
| €60,000 (≈MZN 4,200,000) | Employment | 1,091,460 | 126,000 (3%) | 1,217,460 | ~29.0% |
| €60,000 (≈MZN 4,200,000) | B2B | 1,091,460 | 126,000 (3%) | 1,217,460 | ~29.0% |
| €100,000 (≈MZN 7,000,000) | Employment | 1,931,460 | 210,000 (3%) | 2,141,460 | ~30.6% |
| €100,000 (≈MZN 7,000,000) | B2B | 1,931,460 | 210,000 (3%) | 2,141,460 | ~30.6% |

**Working (income tax using rebate method):**
- MZN 2,100,000: in 32% band (above 1,512,000). Tax = 32% × 2,100,000 − 141,540 = 672,000 − 141,540 = 530,460; less taxpayer credit 1,800 = **528,660** (~25.2%)
- MZN 4,200,000: tax = 32% × 4,200,000 − 141,540 = 1,344,000 − 141,540 = 1,202,460; less 1,800 = **1,200,660** (~28.6%)
- MZN 7,000,000: tax = 32% × 7,000,000 − 141,540 = 2,240,000 − 141,540 = 2,098,460; less 1,800 = **2,096,660** (~30.0%)

*(Table figures use approximate calculations; working above is more precise)*

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 70 MZN (approximate; volatile)
- Single, no dependants; only MZN 1,800 taxpayer credit applied
- INSS 3% applied to both employment and B2B (conservative for B2B — self-employed treatment not confirmed)
