## Personal Income Tax

Mozambique levies *Imposto Sobre o Rendimento das Pessoas Singulares* (IRPS) on residents' worldwide income. Nonresidents are subject to income tax on income arising in Mozambique. Residents: satisfy any of the following — physically present >180 days in a calendar year; present <180 days but maintain Mozambican residence; perform public functions abroad for Mozambique; crew of vessel/aircraft with Mozambican effective management. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return due by 30 April (other income); employment income is final tax (no return required). (EY pp. 1049–1051)

### Progressive income tax rates (annual taxable income in MZN)

| Annual taxable income | Rate | Rebate (MZN) |
|---|---|---|
| MZN 0 – MZN 42,000 | **10%** | — |
| MZN 42,001 – MZN 168,000 | **15%** | MZN 2,100 |
| MZN 168,001 – MZN 504,000 | **20%** | MZN 10,500 |
| MZN 504,001 – MZN 1,512,000 | **25%** | MZN 35,700 |
| Above MZN 1,512,000 | **32%** | MZN 141,540 |

Maximum marginal rate: **32%** (EY p. 1050)

**Non-taxable threshold:** Annual income ≤ MZN 225,000 is not taxable (via rebate mechanism — low-income employees benefit from the rebate reducing tax to zero). (EY p. 1050)

**Tax credits (personal deductions):**
- MZN 1,800 per taxpayer
- MZN 600 per 1 dependent child
- MZN 900 per 2 dependent children
- MZN 1,200 per 3 dependent children
- MZN 1,800 per 4 or more dependent children

**Other income rates:**
- Investment income (withholding): **20%** general rate; 10% for income from shares listed on Mozambique Stock Exchange (final tax for listed shares)
- Gambling: **10%** withholding (final)
- Nonresident withholding: **20%** general; 10% for artistic/entertainment/sports; nonresident capital gains: **32%**

**Capital gains:**
- Subject to IRPS at rates varying from **10% to 32%** depending on holding period
- Tax base on share transfers decreases according to holding period length
- Capital losses may only offset capital gains (EY p. 1050)

**Exempt income:** (EY p. 1050)
- Meal subsidies not exceeding minimum salary
- Allowances within legally established limits
- Pensions
- Compensation received within scope of dismissal process

**Employment income:** Income tax withheld monthly by employers as a **final tax**; employees with only employment income are not required to file annual returns. (EY p. 1049)

**Relief for losses:** Business/professional losses may be carried forward for 5 years; no carryback. (EY p. 1051)

## Social Security

**INSS (Instituto Nacional de Segurança Social) contributions:** (EY p. 1051)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security | **3%** | **4%** | varies |
| **Total combined** | **3%** | **4%** | — |

**Notes:**
- Contributions payable monthly on salaries, wages, bonuses, and regular compensation (including productivity premiums and housing allowances)
- Employer withholds employee contributions monthly
- Resident foreign employees: **exempt** from social security if they prove they are contributing to a similar regime in another country
- Foreign employees: may not be subject to Mozambican social security under totalization agreements and specific employment conditions (EY p. 1051)

**Totalization agreements:** None mentioned specifically; foreign employees may claim exemption if contributing to equivalent scheme in home country (EY p. 1051)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Business/Professional Activities)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals carrying out business activities independently, providing consulting services, or engaging in technical/artistic/scientific projects
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus allowable expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive IRPS rates (10%–32%)
- **Social security:** Standard INSS contributions apply
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds MZN 2,500,000; standard rate 17%
- **Loss treatment:** Carryforward 5 years; no carryback
- **Filing:** Annual return by 30 April

### Option B — Simplified Regime (Presumptive)

- **Who qualifies:** Small businesses with turnover below specified thresholds (details not specified in EY guide for Mozambique)
- **Mechanism:** Not detailed in this EY chapter

### Self-employed foreign nationals

- Must obtain work authorization from Ministry of Labour before starting work
- Self-employed foreign nationals, shareholders, and representatives of shareholders must also obtain work authorizations (EY p. 1053)
- Work permit valid up to 2 years; renewable; short-term work permit: 120 days/calendar year

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Work Permits

- Foreign nationals must obtain work permit from Ministry of Labour; valid up to 2 years; renewable
- Companies may employ foreign nationals without work permits (via communication to Ministry of Labour) for same period up to a certain percentage of total workforce
- Short-term work permit: up to 120 days per calendar year (EY p. 1053)
- Oil and gas and mining sectors: pre-approval by Minister of Foreign Affairs required (EY p. 1054)

### Residence Permits

- Precarious residence permit: foreigners staying >90 days (not tourists/visitors/businesspersons); renewable annually
- Temporary residence permit: valid up to 1 year; for those with 5+ years of precarious residence or entering for residence purposes; renewable annually
- Permanent residence: 10+ years in Mozambique; renewable every 5 years (EY p. 1053)

### Double Tax Treaties

Botswana, India, Italy, Macau SAR, Mauritius, Portugal, South Africa, United Arab Emirates, Vietnam (EY p. 1052)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Mozambique uses MZN (Mozambican Metical). **1 EUR ≈ MZN 69** (approximate; MZN has depreciated substantially; verify at bm.gov.mz).
> Rebates applied to reduce tax burden (tax = rate × income − rebate).
> Employee social security: 3% of gross salary.
> MZN 225,000/year ≈ EUR 3,260 — employees below this threshold pay no income tax.

### €30,000 Employment
- MZN equivalent: EUR 30,000 × 69 = MZN 2,070,000/year
- Tax (using bracket and rebate):
  - Rate: 32% (income > MZN 1,512,000); Rebate: MZN 141,540
  - Tax = 32% × MZN 2,070,000 − MZN 141,540 = MZN 662,400 − MZN 141,540 = MZN 520,860
- Tax credit (personal, single): MZN 1,800
- Net PIT: MZN 520,860 − MZN 1,800 = MZN 519,060
- Social security (3%): MZN 2,070,000 × 3% = MZN 62,100
- Total burden: MZN 581,160
- Effective rate: 581,160 / 2,070,000 = **28.1%**

### €60,000 Employment
- MZN: EUR 60,000 × 69 = MZN 4,140,000
- Tax = 32% × MZN 4,140,000 − MZN 141,540 = MZN 1,324,800 − MZN 141,540 = MZN 1,183,260
- Net PIT (less credit MZN 1,800): MZN 1,181,460
- Social security: 3% × MZN 4,140,000 = MZN 124,200
- Total: MZN 1,305,660; Effective rate: 1,305,660 / 4,140,000 = **31.5%**

### €100,000 Employment
- MZN: EUR 100,000 × 69 = MZN 6,900,000
- Tax = 32% × MZN 6,900,000 − MZN 141,540 = MZN 2,208,000 − MZN 141,540 = MZN 2,066,460
- Social security: 3% × MZN 6,900,000 = MZN 207,000
- Total: MZN 2,273,460; Effective rate: 2,273,460 / 6,900,000 = **32.9%**
