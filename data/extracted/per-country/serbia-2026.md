## Personal Income Tax

Serbia uses a **flat 10% PIT** on employment income (salary tax) and self-employment income. An **annual surtax** applies to high earners (those exceeding approximately 3× and 9× the average annual salary). Tax year = calendar year; annual return due **15 May** of the following year. (EY pp. 1361–1362)

**Salary / employment income:**

| Taxable income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All net salary income (above non-taxable threshold) | **10%** |

Non-taxable monthly threshold (2025): **RSD 28,423/month** (approx. EUR 243/month at EUR 1 = RSD 117).

**Self-employment income:** 10% flat on net accounting profit (bookkeeping entrepreneurs). (EY p. 1361)

**Investment income:**

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Interest, dividends, investment units | **15%** |
| Property leasing (net of 25% standard deduction) | **20%** |
| Royalties | **20%** (deductions of 34%–50% allowed) |
| Directors' fees (net of 20% deduction) | **20%** |
| Capital gains (securities, real estate, IP) | **15%** |
| Freelance income — Option 1 | **20%** (with fixed quarterly deduction) |
| Freelance income — Option 2 | **10%** (with fixed + percentage deduction) |

(EY pp. 1361–1362)

**Annual surtax (godišnji porez na dohodak):** Not a separate "bracket" but an additional reconciliation tax for high earners. Applies when total personal net income exceeds **3× average annual salary** (~RSD 5,439,096 in 2025 threshold for filing; surtax at 10% on income up to ~RSD 10,878,192, then 15% above). (EY p. 1362; KPMG 2026)

| Annual net personal income threshold (2025) | Surtax rate |
|---|---|
| Up to RSD 5,439,096 (~EUR 46,488) | 0% — no annual return required |
| RSD 5,439,096 – RSD 10,878,192 | **10%** on the excess |
| Above RSD 10,878,192 (~EUR 92,976) | **15%** on the excess above this point |

Taxpayers under 40: additional deduction equal to RSD 5,439,096 before applying annual surtax. (KPMG 2026)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal deduction: RSD 725,213/year (~EUR 6,198)
- Each dependent family member: RSD 271,955/year (~EUR 2,324)
- Maximum total deductions: 50% of taxable income (EY p. 1362)

**Notes:**
- Annual personal deduction = 40% of average annual salary per employee in Serbia
- Dependent deduction = 15% per dependent
- Interest from government bonds and local-currency deposits: **exempt** (EY p. 1361)
- For taxpayers < 40 years of age at year-end 2025: bonus deduction equal to 3× average salary (EY p. 1363; KPMG)

## Social Security

Employment income subject to social security at rates below. (EY p. 1363)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed / contractor |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension and disability (PIO) | 14% | 10% | 24% |
| Health insurance | 5.15% | 5.15% | 10.3% |
| Unemployment | 0.75% | — | — |
| **Total** | **19.9%** | **15.15%** | **34.3%** |

**Contribution bases (2025):**
- Minimum monthly base: **RSD 45,950** (~EUR 393) = 35% of average monthly salary
- Maximum monthly base: **RSD 656,425** (~EUR 5,611) = 5× average monthly salary
- Average monthly gross salary (Serbia, 2025): approx. RSD 131,300 (~EUR 1,122)

**For contractors/freelancers (royalties, services) without other insurance:**
- Pension (24%) and healthcare (10.3%) at the contractual amount (no employer split)
- Health contribution for those without other insurance: 10.3% at payer's expense (EY p. 1363)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Preduzetnik Paušalac (Lump-Sum Entrepreneur)

The most popular option for IT freelancers and sole traders with stable income. Tax authority issues a fixed monthly decision; no bookkeeping needed beyond KPO invoice log.

- **Who qualifies:** Registered sole traders (preduzetnici) with annual revenue below **RSD 6,000,000/year** (~EUR 51,282 at 117 RSD/EUR). Not available for certain regulated activities or those in the VAT system.
- **Tax base:** Not income-dependent. Tax authority calculates a fixed monthly amount based on: business activity (NACE code), municipality of registration, average salary in that municipality vs. national average, entrepreneur's age, and employment status. The Tax Administration sends the decision via E-Porezi portal within 48 hours of registration.
- **Rate / mechanism:** The fixed decision includes income tax (10%) and social contributions (PIO 24–26%, health 10.3%, unemployment 1.5%) applied to an administratively determined base. Must pay by **15th of each month**.
- **Typical total monthly amounts (IT activity, 2025):**
  - Belgrade/Novi Sad (large cities): **approximately EUR 250–350/month** (RSD ~29,000–41,000)
  - Smaller municipalities: EUR 100–200/month
  - (Example from secondary source: car mechanic Novi Beograd ~EUR 350; IT activity slightly higher)
- **Social security:** Included in the fixed monthly amount. Entrepreneur accrues pension rights.
- **VAT obligations:** Below RSD 8,000,000 → VAT registration not required. Must register if annual turnover exceeds RSD 8,000,000.
- **Key limits:**
  - Annual income ceiling: **RSD 6,000,000** (~EUR 51,282); must transition to bookkeeping entrepreneur above this
  - Annual opt-in/opt-out: must notify Tax Administration by 30 November for following year
  - Not permitted for trade/commerce activities; IT services and consulting: permitted

(Sources: EY p. 1361; purs.gov.rs; frilenseri.purs.gov.rs; pmadvokati.com)

### Option B — Freelancer Self-Taxation (Fizičko Lice — Natural Person)

For natural persons (not registered entrepreneurs) who earn income from copyright/IP and service contracts. Introduced 2023.

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with freelance income from copyright, service contracts; includes foreign entrepreneurs registered abroad (e.g., programmer or translator based abroad). Not required to register a business in Serbia.
- **Tax base & rate: Two options — chosen quarterly:**

**Option 1 (Quarterly):**

| Component | Rate / Amount |
|---|---|
| Standardized costs deduction | RSD 110,647/quarter (fixed) |
| Income tax rate | **20%** on income above deduction |
| PIO (pension) | **24%** of taxable income |
| Health insurance | **10.3%** of taxable income (min. RSD 4,789/quarter) |

**Option 2 (Quarterly):**

| Component | Rate / Amount |
|---|---|
| Standardized costs deduction | RSD 66,733 + **34%** of gross quarterly income |
| Income tax rate | **10%** on remaining taxable income |
| PIO (pension) | **24%** with mandatory minimum **RSD 25,218/quarter** |
| Health insurance | **10.3%** (min. RSD 4,789/quarter) |

- Freelancer may switch between Option 1 and Option 2 each quarter.
- Quarterly returns due **within 30 days after end of each quarter**.
- At quarterly income of RSD 300,000: Option 1 total ≈ RSD 89,000–111,000; Option 2 ≈ RSD 47,000–62,000.

(Source: frilenseri.purs.gov.rs)

### Option C — Standard Bookkeeping Entrepreneur (Preduzetnik — Knjigovo|dstveni)

- **Who qualifies:** Registered sole trader maintaining full books; no income ceiling.
- **Tax base:** Net accounting profit (revenue minus documented expenses).
- **Rate:** 10% PIT on taxable profit.
- **Social security:** 24% PIO + 10.3% health on self-employed contribution base (minimum RSD 45,950/month in 2025).
- **VAT:** Mandatory if annual turnover > RSD 8,000,000.
- **Key limits:** Full bookkeeping; quarterly advance PIT payments.

---

### Option D — d.o.o. (Private Limited Company)

- **Rate:** Corporate income tax 15% on profit; dividends: 15% withholding.
- **Owner salary:** Standard employment PIT (10%) + social contributions.
- **Typical use:** For revenues above EUR 50k; provides liability shield.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Newly Settled Taxpayers Relief

- **Eligibility:** Natural persons who settle tax residency in Serbia for the first time, who have not been Serbian tax residents for the previous 5 years, are under 40 years of age, AND earn income from employment or self-employment in Serbia. (EY p. 1361 implied; welcometoserbia.gov.rs)
- **Benefit:** Reduction of **70%** on the tax base and social contributions for **5 years**.
- **Caveats:** Conditions and eligibility criteria require confirmation with local tax advisor; EY mentions this benefit exists but is brief.

### EU/Schengen Visa Holders — Visa-Free Entry

- Holders of Schengen, EU, US, or UK visas/residence permits may enter Serbia visa-free for up to 90 days within a 6-month period. (EY p. 1365)
- For stays > 90 days: must apply for Single/Unified permit (residence + work).

## 2026 Changes

- **New PIT Law announced:** Serbia is implementing amendments to the Personal Income Tax Law effective 2026 (Zunic Law, 2026); full details pending publication in Službeni glasnik.
- **Annual surtax thresholds** adjusted annually based on average salary statistics; 2025 non-taxable base = RSD 5,439,096 (up from RSD 4,717,440 in 2024). (KPMG 2026)
- **Non-taxable monthly amount:** RSD 28,423/month from January 2025 (up from RSD 25,000).
- **Minimum contribution base:** RSD 45,950/month from January 2025.
- No changes to flat employment PIT rate (10%) announced.
- Paušalac income ceiling (RSD 6M) and VAT threshold (RSD 8M) unchanged.
