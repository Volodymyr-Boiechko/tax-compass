## Personal Income Tax

**There is no personal income tax in the Cayman Islands.** The Cayman Islands government does not tax the income or capital gains of resident or nonresident individuals. (EY p.252)

No PIT brackets exist.

**Standard deductions / allowances:** Not applicable — no income tax.

**Notes:**
- No capital gains tax.
- No inheritance or gift tax (not mentioned as existing).
- The Cayman Islands is a pure zero-income-tax jurisdiction.

---

## Social Security

**No welfare or social security taxes are imposed.** No deductions or contributions are required for any other government-sponsored programs. (EY p.252)

**Medical insurance:** Mandatory for all private employees. (EY p.252)

**Pension:** Employees must contribute a minimum of 5% of their earnings to a mandatory pension plan, up to KYD 87,000. Exemption applies for foreign workers who have completed less than nine months of their employment contract. (EY p.252)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |

**Income ceiling:** KYD 87,000 for pension contribution.

**Notes:**
- No government social security taxes of any kind.
- Medical insurance is mandatory but handled privately, not through government scheme.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — No Income Tax Regime
- **Who qualifies:** All individuals and businesses.
- **Tax base:** No income tax base — income is not taxed.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 0% income tax on all income types.
- **Social security:** No mandatory government social security for self-employed.
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT or goods and services tax in the Cayman Islands.
- **Key limits:** None from a tax perspective.

## Business Licenses

Every person or company carrying on a trade or business in the Cayman Islands must hold a **business license** for each location where trade/business is conducted (unless exempt). (EY p.252)

- License cost depends on type and location of business, number and type of employees.
- Payable annually; renewal application must be made at least 28 days before anniversary of grant.
- Companies not 60% owned and controlled by Caymanians also need a license under the Local Companies (Control) Law — this license normally has a 12-year term. (EY p.252)
