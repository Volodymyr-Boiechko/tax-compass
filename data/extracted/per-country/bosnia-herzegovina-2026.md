## Personal Income Tax

Bosnia and Herzegovina has a **complex three-entity tax structure**: the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina (FBH), the Republic of Srpska (RS), and the Brčko District (BD). Each entity has its own separate PIT and social security systems. **This file covers primarily the FBH framework (the largest jurisdiction), with RS noted.** (EY p.162)

**FBH — Personal Income Tax:**
- Residents: worldwide income
- Non-residents: FBH-source income only
- Resident = domicile, residence or centre of business/vital interests in FBH, OR present > 183 days in calendar year
- Tax year = calendar year; annual returns due by 31 March of following year
- **Flat rate: 10%** applies to each type of income below (EY p.162)

**RS — Personal Income Tax:**
- Also a **flat rate: 10%** on income (consistent with FBH structure; separate RS legislation applies)

**Income types subject to FBH PIT at 10%:**

| Income type | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employment income (salary tax) | **10%** | On income exceeding non-taxable threshold |
| Income from independent activities (self-employment, freelance, agriculture/forestry) | **10%** | Expenses deductible |
| Property income (lease/alienation of immovable property, equipment, vehicles) | **10%** | |
| Property rights income (copyrights, patents, licenses, franchises) | **10%** | 30% expense deduction for royalties |
| Capital investment income (interest from loans, life/pension insurance payouts) | **10%** | |
| Dividends | **Exempt** | (EY p.163) |
| Pensions | **Exempt** | (EY p.163) |

(EY pp.162–163)

**Expense deductions for non-employment income:**
- Occasional independent activities: 20% of realized income (expense deduction)
- Royalties: 30% of realized income (expense deduction)

**Annual personal deductions:**
- Monthly personal deduction: **BAM 300/month (BAM 3,600/year)** on total realized income
- Additional deductions possible for: dependent children, support of spouse/close family members, health service costs/orthopaedic aids (for self or dependent family members), interest paid on housing loan (EY p.163)

**Notes:**
- No withholding tax on dividends stated (exempt from PIT)
- Pensions are exempt from FBH PIT
- Capital gains: included in capital investment income category at 10%

## Social Security

**FBH — Social Security Contributions (employment):**

| Fund | Employer rate | Employee rate |
|---|---|---|
| Pension and Disability Fund | **2.5%** (since July 2025) | **17%** |
| Health Care Fund | **2%** | **12.5%** |
| Unemployment Fund | **0.5%** | **1.5%** |
| **Total** | **5%** | **31%** |

- Applied on gross salary
- Note (a): Slightly different contribution rates may apply when employer hires employees who are residents of another tax jurisdiction within BiH (BD or RS) (EY p.164)
- Note (b): Employer pension rate of 2.5% has been applicable since July 2025 (EY p.164)

**Additional employer contributions:**
- Fund for Promotion of Rehabilitation and Employment of Persons with Disabilities: contribution on every 16 employees (25% of average FBH salary per month per required but unfulfilled position) — for employers not meeting disability employment quota
- Natural disasters contribution: **0.5%** (employer)
- Water fee: **0.5%** (employer)

(EY p.164–165)

**Self-employed (individual entrepreneurs) — FBH:**

| Fund | Rate |
|---|---|
| Pension and Disability Fund | **19.5%** |
| Health Care Fund | **14.5%** |
| Unemployment Fund | **2%** |
| **Total** | **36%** |

- Base: published by competent Ministry of Finance (EY p.165)

**Income under contracts (royalties, services, additional work):**

| Fund | Rate |
|---|---|
| Pension and Disability Fund | **6%** |
| Health Care Fund | **4%** |
| **Total** | **10%** |

- Applies to income received under contracts relating to royalties, services and additional work, and other independent activities (EY p.165)

**Totalization agreements:** FBH applies agreements with: Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Denmark, France, Germany, Hungary, Italy, Luxembourg, Montenegro, Netherlands, North Macedonia, Norway, Poland, Serbia, Slovak Republic, Slovenia, Sweden, Switzerland, Türkiye, United Kingdom (some are former Yugoslavia agreements still applicable). Agreements provide 12-month or 24-month exemption, extendable. (EY p.165)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Entrepreneur (FBH)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered as an independent entrepreneur (obrtnik) in the FBH
- **Tax base:** Net profit = gross revenue minus business expenses; income from independent activities
- **Rate / mechanism:** **10%** flat PIT on net income; BAM 3,600/year personal deduction
- **Social security:** Self-employed SS contributions: 19.5% (pension) + 14.5% (health) + 2% (unemployment) = **36%** on base published by Ministry of Finance
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover ≥ BAM 50,000; VAT rate 17%
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling for standard regime; advance monthly PIT payments required

(EY pp.162–163, 165)

### Option B — Freelancer / Contract Worker (Occasional Independent Activities)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals providing occasional services under contracts (not full-time employment)
- **Tax base:** 80% of gross income (20% expense deduction automatically applied)
- **Rate / mechanism:** **10%** flat PIT on 80% of gross income = effective 8% on gross
- **Social security:** 6% pension + 4% health = **10%** withheld from payment (EY p.165)
- **VAT obligations:** May apply if turnover exceeds BAM 50,000
- **Key limits:** "Occasional" nature; if ongoing, should register as entrepreneur

(EY pp.162–163, 165)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No specific preferential tax regime for newcomers, expats, or digital nomads described in EY for BiH. Standard 10% flat PIT applies to all residents on all taxable income. (EY pp.162–168)

For expatriate employees: social security contributions may also be payable on salaries received outside FBH in some cases; under bilateral conventions, expatriates may pay SS contributions in their jurisdiction of residence only. (EY p.165)

## 2026 Changes

- **Employer pension contribution reduced to 2.5% (from July 2025):** Previously higher; reduction takes effect from July 2025. (EY p.164)
- **Electronic submission of tax returns:** The process of establishing mandatory electronic submission using qualified electronic signatures is under progress for legal entities and should be applicable in 2026. (EY p.165)
- No changes to the fundamental 10% PIT flat rate announced.
- No changes to employee social security rates (17% pension, 12.5% health, 1.5% unemployment) announced.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (BAM 3,600/year personal deduction).
> **1 EUR ≈ BAM 1.956** (BAM is pegged to EUR via currency board: BAM 1.95583 = EUR 1, fixed).
> Employment scenario: flat 10% PIT on net income after personal deduction; employee SS 31% on gross.
> B2B scenario: individual entrepreneur, 10% PIT on net profit; self-employed SS 36% on Ministry base.

### €30,000 Employment (≈ BAM 58,675/year)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross annual salary | BAM 58,675 | |
| Personal deduction | BAM 3,600 | |
| Taxable income | 58,675 − 3,600 | BAM 55,075 |
| PIT (10%) | 10% × 55,075 | BAM 5,508 |
| Employee SS (31%) | 31% × 58,675 | BAM 18,189 |
| **Total burden** | 5,508 + 18,189 | **BAM 23,697** |
| **Effective rate** | 23,697 / 58,675 | **40.4%** |

### €30,000 B2B — Individual entrepreneur

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue | BAM 58,675 | |
| Business expenses (assumed 20%) | BAM 11,735 | |
| Net income before personal deduction | BAM 46,940 | |
| Personal deduction | BAM 3,600 | |
| Taxable income | BAM 43,340 | |
| PIT (10%) | 10% × 43,340 | BAM 4,334 |
| Self-employed SS (36% on Ministry base; ~BAM 40,000 est.) | 36% × 40,000 | BAM 14,400 |
| **Total burden** | 4,334 + 14,400 | **BAM 18,734** |
| **Effective rate** | 18,734 / 58,675 | **31.9%** |

**Key observations:**
- **Employment is heavily SS-loaded:** Employee contributions of 31% on gross significantly raise the effective rate. Total employment burden (employee PIT + employee SS) ~40% at all income levels.
- **Self-employed SS base is fixed** by Ministry of Finance (not actual income) — at higher incomes this creates a "cap" effect, making self-employment progressively more favorable vs employment.
- **BAM is fixed to EUR at 1.95583:1** — no exchange rate risk.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/BAM = 1.95583 (fixed currency board peg — exact rate)
- Employment: employee SS 31% on gross; PIT 10% on gross minus BAM 3,600 personal deduction
- B2B entrepreneur: 20% business expenses assumed; SS 36% on Ministry-published base (estimated at BAM 40,000/year — verify at fmf.gov.ba); PIT 10% on net after personal deduction
- RS entity (Republic of Srpska): also flat 10% PIT with different SS structure — rates similar but verify separately at mf.vladars.net
- No disability/natural disaster/water fee employer contributions included in employee burden

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **RS (Republic of Srpska) PIT and SS rates:** EY covers primarily FBH framework; RS has separate legislation with flat 10% PIT and different SS rates — verify at mf.vladars.net
- [ ] **Brčko District (BD):** Separate tax system; EY does not cover BD details — verify at bdcentral.net
- [ ] **Self-employed SS base:** Ministry of Finance publishes the base on which self-employed pay 36% SS; estimated at BAM 40,000/year but actual base must be verified at fmf.gov.ba

- [ ] **BAM/EUR peg:** Fixed at 1.95583 per currency board; no conversion risk but confirm peg remains in force
- [ ] **VAT threshold:** BAM 50,000/year for mandatory VAT registration (~€25,500) — relevant for self-employed service providers
