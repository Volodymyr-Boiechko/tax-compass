## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for residents taxed on worldwide income. Nonresidents taxed on Czech-source income only.
Tax year = calendar year. Filing deadline: 1 April (paper) or 1 May (electronic) of the following year;
extension to 31 October available for taxpayers with foreign-source income. (EY p.347)

**Progressive PIT brackets — 2025 / 2026** (EY p.344)

| Taxable income (CZK) | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| CZK 0 – 1,676,052 | 15% | 2025 threshold; CZK 1,762,812 applies for 2026 |
| Over CZK 1,676,052 | 23% | On excess only (2025); over CZK 1,762,812 in 2026 |

The 36× average wage threshold (CZK 1,676,052 in 2025, CZK 1,762,812 in 2026) represents the boundary between the 15% and 23% bands. (EY p.344)

**Monthly payroll withholding thresholds** (EY p.344):
- Monthly income ≤ CZK 139,671 (2025) / CZK 146,901 (2026): subject to 15%
- Monthly income above these thresholds: subject to 23%

**Investment income** (EY p.342):
- Dividends, limited partners' shares: 15% final withholding tax
- Interest income (personal investments): 15% final withholding tax
- Non-Czech-source dividends/interest: taxed in annual return at 15% (optional separate tax base)

**Capital gains** (EY p.342–343):
- Immovable property: exempt if held ≥ 10 years (primary residence: 2 years)
- Securities: exempt if held ≥ 3 years AND total gross income ≤ CZK 100,000 in calendar year; OR total proceeds ≤ CZK 100,000
- From 1 January 2025: cap of CZK 40 million on tax-exempt gains from securities/shares held ≥ 3 years (cryptocurrencies still subject to this cap from 1 January 2026)
- From 1 January 2026: the CZK 40 million cap no longer applies to securities and company shares (only cryptocurrencies remain capped)

**Lump-sum expense deductions** (instead of actual expenses) — OSVČ option (EY p.344):

| Activity | Deductible rate | Maximum deduction (CZK) |
|---|---|---|
| Agriculture, forestry, fishing, farming and craft | 80% | 1,600,000 |
| Trade license income | 60% | 1,200,000 |
| Licensing IP rights (inventions, copyrights), sole proprietorships and other business income | 40% | 800,000 |
| Rental | 30% | 600,000 |

**Tax reliefs — 2025** (EY p.345):

| Relief | Amount (CZK/year) |
|---|---|
| Basic personal tax relief | 30,840 |
| Spouse relief (income ≤ CZK 68,000/year, lives in household) | 24,840 |
| Dependent child — 1st child | 15,204 |
| Dependent child — 2nd child | 22,320 |
| Dependent child — 3rd+ child | 27,840 |
| Partially disabled person | 2,520 |
| Totally disabled person | 5,040 |

**Notes:**
- The basic personal tax relief (CZK 30,840) operates as a credit subtracted from tax liability, not from taxable income
- The personal tax relief effectively creates a tax-free allowance of approximately CZK 205,600/year at the 15% rate
- Social security contributions (employee share) and health insurance premiums ARE deductible from the PIT tax base for employees
- Paušální daň (lump-sum) taxpayers do not file an annual PIT return; their monthly payment finalises tax liability (see Self-Employment Regimes)
- Czech-source income subject to withholding tax is generally excluded from the annual return

## Social Security

Employee and employer contributions on gross salary. (EY p.346)

| Contribution | Employer % | Employee % | Total % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Old-age pension | 21.5% | 6.5% | 28.0% |
| Sickness | 2.1% | 0.6% | 2.7% |
| Unemployment | 1.2% | — | 1.2% |
| **Total social security** | **24.8%** | **7.1%** | **31.9%** |
| Health insurance | 9.0% | 4.5% | 13.5% |
| **Grand total** | **33.8%** | **11.6%** | **45.4%** |

**Annual social security cap (2025):** CZK 2,234,736 (= 48 × monthly average salary). For 2026: CZK 2,350,416. (EY p.346)
Income above the cap is not subject to social security contributions (but health insurance has NO cap).

**Notes:**
- Health insurance: no maximum assessment base — applies to all income (EY p.346)
- Employee social security contributions are deductible from the PIT base
- Health insurance premiums are NOT deductible from the PIT base
- Maximum assessment base applies per employer; employees with multiple employers have separate caps per employer but remain subject to one overall annual cap

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Paušální Daň (Lump-Sum / Flat Tax Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** OSVČ (self-employed, živnostník) with annual turnover ≤ CZK 2,000,000 whose
  self-employment income is their only taxable income (minor exceptions apply: rental up to CZK 50,000,
  capital income up to CZK 50,000 from Czech sources). Cannot be a VAT payer.
- **Tax base:** No return required; monthly flat payment covers PIT + social insurance + health insurance.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Three bands based on income level and income type. Monthly amounts for 2026:

| Band | Monthly payment (CZK) | Of which: PIT | Of which: Social | Of which: Health | Annual income limit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Band 1 | 9,984 | 100 | 6,578 | 3,306 | CZK 1,000,000 (any income type) |
| Band 2 | 16,745 | 4,963 | 8,191 | 3,591 | CZK 1,500,000 (≥75% income from activities with 80% or 60% expense deduction) |
| Band 3 | 27,139 | 9,320 | 12,527 | 5,292 | CZK 2,000,000 (≥75% income from activities with 80% expense deduction) |

  Source: expattaxes.cz (2026 data); expat-tax.cz (2026 changes); dostupnyadvokat.cz

  Band 1 was increased for 2026 (from CZK 7,498 in prior discussions); Bands 2 and 3 unchanged from 2025.

- **Annual totals (2026):**
  - Band 1: CZK 9,984 × 12 = **CZK 119,808/year** covering all PIT + SS + health
  - Band 2: CZK 16,745 × 12 = **CZK 200,940/year**
  - Band 3: CZK 27,139 × 12 = **CZK 325,668/year**

- **Social security:** Included in monthly payment above. No separate declaration needed.
- **VAT obligations:** Cannot register for VAT while in Paušální daň; exceeding CZK 2,000,000 turnover
  triggers mandatory VAT registration and exit from the regime.
- **Key limits:**
  - Registration/change deadline: 10 January of the tax year
  - If conditions are breached during year, taxpayer must notify the tax authority and file a standard PIT return
  - Does NOT allow deduction of actual expenses or personal reliefs beyond what is built into the payment
  - Particularly efficient at Band 1 for low-to-medium trade-license income with minimal expenses

---

### Option B — OSVČ Standard Regime (Živnostník with actual or lump-sum expenses)

- **Who qualifies:** All OSVČ; no turnover limit for standard regime. Mandatory if turnover > CZK 2,000,000
  or if ineligible for Paušální daň.
- **Tax base:** Revenue minus actual deductible expenses (or lump-sum % deductions per table above).
  Social and health insurance contributions are deductible from PIT base.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT at 15% / 23% on net profit after deductions; personal tax relief
  (CZK 30,840) applies.
- **Social security (OSVČ — main activity) 2026:**
  - Social insurance rate: **29.2%** on assessment base
  - Assessment base = **50% of net profit** (revenue − expenses)
  - Minimum monthly social insurance advance: **CZK 5,720** (2026) based on minimum assessment base
    of CZK 19,587/month (= 40% of CZK 48,967 average monthly wage)
  - Maximum annual assessment base: CZK 2,350,416 (same as employee cap, 2026)
  - Source: ČSSZ (cssz.cz); BDO Czech Republic (bdo.cz, 2026)

- **Health insurance (OSVČ) 2026:**
  - Health insurance rate: **13.5%** on assessment base
  - Assessment base = **50% of net profit**
  - Minimum monthly health insurance advance: **CZK 3,306** (2026)
  - No maximum assessment base for health insurance
  - Source: expat-tax.cz (2026); accace.com

- **Combined minimum monthly contributions (2026): CZK 9,026** (social CZK 5,720 + health CZK 3,306)
- **Annual minimum contributions:** CZK 108,312 (social CZK 68,640 + health CZK 39,672)

- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory VAT registration at CZK 2,000,000 annual turnover; optional voluntary
  registration below the threshold (recommended for B2B services).
- **Key limits:** Annual PIT return due 1 April/1 May; social and health annual reconciliation forms
  also required. Loss from self-employment may be carried forward 5 years.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Dedicated Newcomer PIT Regime

The Czech Republic does not have a dedicated expat/newcomer preferential PIT regime (unlike Portugal's NHR or Poland's return relief). Standard rates apply from day one of tax residency.

### Startup Company Special Regime (From January 2026)

- **Eligibility:** Qualifying startup companies; subject to strict rules. Mainly intended for startup
  companies, not individual freelancers. (EY p.344)
- **Benefit:** Favorable tax and social security/health insurance arrangement for startup participants.
- **Duration:** Details to be confirmed; announced in EY October 2025 data.
- **Caveats:** Strict qualification criteria; full scope not published at EY content date.

## 2026 Changes

- **PIT threshold increase:** The 23% bracket threshold rises from CZK 1,676,052 (2025) to CZK 1,762,812
  (2026), reflecting the 36× average wage formula. (EY p.344)
- **Monthly withholding threshold:** Monthly income threshold rises from CZK 139,671 (2025) to
  CZK 146,901 (2026). (EY p.344)
- **Paušální daň Band 1 increase:** Monthly flat payment for Band 1 rises to CZK 9,984;
  Bands 2 and 3 unchanged. (expattaxes.cz; expat-tax.cz)
- **Social security cap increase:** Annual cap rises to CZK 2,350,416 for 2026. (bdo.cz)
- **Minimum OSVČ contributions:** Social minimum rises to CZK 5,720/month; health minimum to
  CZK 3,306/month (combined CZK 9,026/month). (bdo.cz; expat-tax.cz)
- **Securities/shares capital gains:** CZK 40 million limit no longer applies to securities and
  company shares from 1 January 2026 (only cryptocurrencies remain capped). (EY p.343)
- **Stock options taxation:** Cessation of Czech tax residency will no longer be a taxing point from
  January 2026; 10-year deferral limit extended to 15 years. (EY p.343–344)
- **Startup regime:** New special favorable tax/social arrangement for startup companies introduced
  from January 2026. (EY p.344)
- **Health insurance structural changes:** New calculation methodology for advance payments effective
  2026. (accace.com)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants. EUR/CZK rate: 1 EUR = CZK 25 (approximate 2025 average).
> "Employment" = gross salary paid by employer; employee-side costs only (employer contributions not shown).
> "B2B OSVČ" = standard OSVČ regime with 60% lump-sum expense deduction (trade license / živnost).
> Personal tax relief CZK 30,840 applied. Social security cap not reached at these income levels (except €100k).

### Working: CZK 750,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee social security (7.1%) | 7.1% × 750,000 | CZK 53,250 |
| Employee health insurance (4.5%) | 4.5% × 750,000 | CZK 33,750 |
| PIT base | 750,000 − 53,250 − 33,750 | CZK 663,000 |
| PIT at 15% | 15% × 663,000 | CZK 99,450 |
| Less: personal tax relief | −30,840 | −CZK 30,840 |
| **PIT due** | | **CZK 68,610** |
| **Total burden** | 53,250 + 33,750 + 68,610 | **CZK 155,610** |
| **Effective rate** | 155,610 / 750,000 | **20.7%** |

### Working: CZK 750,000 (≈ €30,000) OSVČ Standard (60% expense deduction)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Lump-sum expenses (60%) | 60% × 750,000 | CZK 450,000 |
| Net profit | 750,000 − 450,000 | CZK 300,000 |

### Paušální daň comparison (Band 1, income ≤ CZK 1,000,000)

| Annual revenue | Annual flat payment | Effective rate |
|---|---|---|
| CZK 750,000 | CZK 119,808 | 16.0% |
| CZK 1,000,000 | CZK 119,808 | 12.0% |

*Band 1 Paušální daň (CZK 119,808/year) is slightly costlier than standard OSVČ at CZK 750,000 (16.0% vs 13.4%) due to higher built-in minimum contributions, but offers complete administrative simplicity. At CZK 1,000,000 (≈€40k), Paušální daň matches standard OSVČ cost efficiency.*

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/CZK = 25 (approximate 2025 average)
- OSVČ uses 60% lump-sum expense deduction (trade license / živnostenské podnikání); cap CZK 1,200,000 not reached
- Social insurance minimum annual base CZK 235,044 (= 12 × CZK 19,587); actual base = 50% of net profit when higher
- Health insurance minimum base: same minimum CZK 235,044 applies
- Personal tax relief CZK 30,840 applied for both employment and OSVČ standard regime
- Employment: employee SS 7.1% + health 4.5% = 11.6% total
- Social security cap (CZK 2,234,736 / 2025) applied to €100k employment scenario
- 2025 PIT threshold (CZK 1,676,052) used; no solidarity tax scenarios (applies far above these incomes)
