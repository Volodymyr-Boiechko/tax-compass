## Personal Income Tax

Progressive Individual Income Tax (IIT — 个人所得税) system. Chinese tax residents subject to IIT on worldwide income; nonresidents subject to IIT on China-source income only. Tax year = calendar year. Spouses taxed separately. (EY p.266, p.274)

**Tax residency (from 1 January 2019):** Individuals with domicile in China, OR individuals without domicile who reside in China for 183 days or more in a tax year, are Chinese tax residents. (EY p.266)

**Consolidated income:** For Chinese tax residents, employment income, labor services income, author's remuneration, and royalties are aggregated as consolidated income subject to the annual progressive rates. (EY p.266)

**Progressive Annual IIT Brackets — Consolidated Income (residents)** (EY p.271)

| Taxable income (CNY/year) | Tax rate | Tax due (CNY) | Cumulative tax due (CNY) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 36,000 | 3% | 1,080 | 1,080 |
| Next 108,000 (36,001–144,000) | 10% | 10,800 | 11,880 |
| Next 156,000 (144,001–300,000) | 20% | 31,200 | 43,080 |
| Next 120,000 (300,001–420,000) | 25% | 30,000 | 73,080 |
| Next 240,000 (420,001–660,000) | 30% | 72,000 | 145,080 |
| Next 300,000 (660,001–960,000) | 35% | 105,000 | 250,080 |
| Above 960,000 | 45% | — | — |

**Monthly progressive rates for nonresidents** (EY p.272)

| Taxable income (CNY/month) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| First 3,000 | 3% |
| Next 9,000 (3,001–12,000) | 10% |
| Next 13,000 (12,001–25,000) | 20% |
| Next 10,000 (25,001–35,000) | 25% |
| Next 20,000 (35,001–55,000) | 30% |
| Next 25,000 (55,001–80,000) | 35% |
| Above 80,000 | 45% |

**Flat rate income types:**
- Investment income (interest, dividends, other income from China sources): flat 20%, no deductions allowed. (EY p.267)
- Capital gains from sale or transfer of movable/immovable property: flat 20%. (EY p.269)
- Rental/leasing income: flat 20%. (EY p.272)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Basic standard deduction: CNY 60,000/year for Chinese tax residents; CNY 5,000/month for nonresident foreign employees on employment income. (EY p.270)
- Business deduction for independent personal services income and royalties: 20% of income. (EY p.271)
- Author's remuneration: additional 30% deduction on top of 20% deduction (i.e., effectively 44% deduction). (EY p.271)
- Rental/leasing income: deduction of CNY 800 or 20% of income, whichever is higher. (EY p.271)
- Repair fees from rental income: up to CNY 800/month on presentation of official invoices. (EY p.271)
- Private pension contributions: deductible up to CNY 12,000/year (nationwide from 1 January 2024). (EY p.270)
- Commercial health insurance: deductible up to CNY 2,400/year (CNY 200/month). (EY p.270)

**Specific additional deductions (from 1 January 2023):** (EY p.270)

| Deduction | Amount |
|---|---|
| Children's education | CNY 2,000/month per qualified child |
| Academic continued education | CNY 400/month |
| Skilled professional/technical continued education | CNY 3,600/year |
| Medical expenses for serious illness | Expense above CNY 15,000, capped at CNY 80,000/year |
| Housing loan interest | CNY 1,000/month |
| Housing rent | CNY 800/1,100/1,500/month (by city category) |
| Caring for elderly (single child) | CNY 3,000/month |
| Caring for elderly (non-single child) | Up to CNY 1,500/month |
| Caring for baby under 3 years old | CNY 2,000/month per qualified child |

**Notes:**
- No distinction between married and single taxpayers. (EY p.271)
- Annual bonus method: For Chinese tax residents, annual bonus can be computed as 1/12 of annual bonus amount using monthly rates (one-time per year). This favorable treatment may be unavailable from January 2028. (EY p.267)
- Non-China-domiciled individuals in China ≤6 consecutive tax years: subject to IIT on China-source income and on income paid/borne by China employer for overseas services. (EY p.266)
- Non-China-domiciled individuals in China >6 consecutive years without 30+ consecutive day absences: subject to IIT on worldwide income. (EY p.266)
- Income may be apportioned between China and non-China portions based on days present. (EY p.271)

## Social Security

Chinese nationals employed by China entities, and (since 1 July 2011) foreign nationals working in China, must participate in the China social security system. (EY p.273)

**Average rates (vary by city):** (EY p.274)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (all schemes avg.) | ~11% | ~25% | varies |
| **Total (approx.)** | **~11%** | **~25%** | — |

**Income ceiling:** Capped at three times the average salary in the city in the preceding year as published by the local government. (EY p.274)

**Schemes included:** Basic pension, basic medical insurance, work-related injury insurance, maternity insurance, unemployment insurance; housing provident fund (for HK/Macau/Taiwan residents from 28 November 2017). (EY p.273)

**Notes:**
- Social security tax rates vary significantly among cities. EY states averages of ~25% (employer) and ~11% (employee) of gross income. (EY p.274)
- Foreign nationals from countries with totalization agreements may be exempt from China social security. (EY p.273–274)
- Overseas social security contributions by foreign employees are NOT deductible in China IIT. (EY p.270)
- China has totalization agreements with: Canada, Denmark, Finland, France (not yet in force), Germany, Japan, Korea (South), Luxembourg, Netherlands, Serbia, Spain, Switzerland. (EY p.274)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Proprietorship / Independent Personal Services
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals performing independent personal services in China (自雇 / 个体工商户).
- **Tax base:** Income minus 20% business deduction; consolidated with other income at annual progressive rates.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual rates 3%–45% on net income (after 20% deduction).
- **Social security:** Must participate in China social security (employer + employee portions if applicable); rates vary by city.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT applies to services rendered in China.
- **Key limits:** No special simplified regime like in some other jurisdictions. Individual proprietorship enterprises cannot carry forward losses. (EY p.272)

### Option B — Simplified Tax for Nonresidents (Monthly)
- **Who qualifies:** Non-China-domiciled individuals (nonresidents) earning labor services income, author's remuneration, royalties from China sources.
- **Tax base:** Income minus 20% deduction; taxed monthly using nonresident monthly rates.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Monthly progressive rates 3%–45%.
- **Social security:** Depends on totalization agreement status and duration of China stay.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT may apply.
- **Key limits:** Each payment/installment taxed separately at monthly rates.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Non-Taxable Benefits (Temporary Exemption — until January 2028)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign expatriate employees who are Chinese tax residents.
- **Benefit:** The following employer-provided benefits are non-taxable (until January 2028):
  - Housing rental paid directly or reimbursed by employer
  - Home leave (limited to twice per year for employee only)
  - Relocation or moving costs
  - Meals and laundry
  - Language training
  - Children's education in China
  (Provided items are paid directly or reimbursed on presentation of official tax invoices.)
- **Duration:** Currently valid; will be unavailable from **January 2028** when the non-taxable treatment of housing rental, language training and children's education will cease for foreign employees. (EY p.267, p.271)
- **Caveats:** From January 2028, only children's education, housing rent/loan interest, and continued education deductions available (but foreign employees cannot claim both the non-taxable housing benefit and the specific additional deduction simultaneously — must choose one). (EY p.271)

### Non-Domiciled Resident: ≤6 Consecutive Years Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Non-China-domiciled individuals who have been Chinese tax residents for no more than six consecutive years, and who had absences from China for more than 30 consecutive days in any of those six years.
- **Benefit:** Taxed on China-source income only (not worldwide income); specifically, income earned from services rendered outside China but paid/borne by China employer is subject to IIT, but income paid/borne by overseas employer for overseas services is not.
- **Duration:** Resets each time there is a 30+ consecutive day absence from China. (EY p.266)

### Private Pension Tax Deferral
- **Eligibility:** All individuals (piloted from 1 May 2018, expanded nationwide from 1 January 2024).
- **Benefit:** Contributions up to CNY 12,000/year to qualified private pension fund accounts are deductible from taxable consolidated income; investment income in the account is temporarily exempt; withdrawals taxed as wages and salaries at a flat 3%. (EY p.268)

### Annual Bonus Favorable Tax Treatment
- **Eligibility:** Chinese tax residents; must register the stock option plan with in-charge tax bureau (for stock options).
- **Benefit:** Annual bonus or several-month bonus may be divided evenly into up to 12 installments, each taxed at monthly tax rates (rather than being aggregated into the annual consolidated income). (EY p.267)
- **Duration:** Can be used only once per calendar year; may be unavailable from **January 2028**. (EY p.267)

## 2026 Changes

- **Expatriate non-taxable benefits sunset approaching:** Housing rental, language training, and children's education non-taxable treatment for foreign employees will cease from January 2028; no change for 2026 itself. (EY p.267)
- **Annual bonus favorable treatment:** The method of computing annual bonus as 1/12 of annual amount using monthly rates will be unavailable from January 2028 for Chinese tax residents. (EY p.267)
- **Internet platform withholding (from 1 October 2025):** Internet platform enterprises are required to fulfill IIT withholding obligations for individual platform users who receive labor services income rendered through the internet platform. (EY p.275)
- **K visa category (from 1 October 2025):** New visa category for young science and technology talent. (EY p.277)
- **Visa-free transit expansion:** 240-hour (10-day) visa-free transit policy effective from 19 December 2024 for nationals of 55 jurisdictions. Extended to 60 open ports in 24 provinces. (EY p.280)
- **Private pension:** Nationwide expansion of private pension scheme was from 1 January 2024; continues into 2026 with no announced changes.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> EUR/CNY rate used: 1 EUR ≈ CNY 7.8 (approximate 2025 average).
> "Employment" = gross salary for a Chinese tax resident; CNY 60,000/year standard deduction applied; monthly withholding reconciled annually.
> "B2B" = independent personal services income; 20% business deduction applied before progressive rates.
> Social security: ~11% employee contribution (city average); no ceiling binding at these income levels (ceiling at 3× local average salary).

**EUR to CNY conversions:**
- €30,000 = CNY 234,000
- €60,000 = CNY 468,000
- €100,000 = CNY 780,000

### Working: CNY 234,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (~11%) | 11% × 234,000 | CNY 25,740 |
| Standard deduction | CNY 60,000 | CNY 60,000 |
| PIT base | 234,000 − 60,000 | CNY 174,000 |
| Band 1: First CNY 36k at 3% | 3% × 36,000 | CNY 1,080 |
| Band 2: CNY 36k–144k at 10% | 10% × 108,000 | CNY 10,800 |
| Band 3: CNY 144k–174k at 20% | 20% × 30,000 | CNY 6,000 |
| Total IIT | 1,080 + 10,800 + 6,000 | CNY 17,880 |
| **Total burden** | **25,740 + 17,880** | **CNY 43,620** |
| **Effective rate** | **43,620 / 234,000** | **~18.6%** |

### Working: CNY 234,000 (≈ €30,000) B2B Independent Services

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| SS (~11%, similar to employment est.) | 11% × 234,000 | CNY 25,740 |
| 20% business deduction | 20% × 234,000 | CNY 46,800 |
| PIT base | 234,000 − 46,800 − 60,000 | CNY 127,200 |
| Band 1: First CNY 36k at 3% | 3% × 36,000 | CNY 1,080 |
| Band 2: CNY 36k–127.2k at 10% | 10% × 91,200 | CNY 9,120 |
| Total IIT | 1,080 + 9,120 | CNY 10,200 |
| **Total burden** | **25,740 + 10,200** | **CNY 35,940** |
| **Effective rate** | **35,940 / 234,000** | **~15.4%** |

- SS: 11% × 468,000 = CNY 51,480
- PIT base: 468,000 − 60,000 = CNY 408,000
- Tax: 1,080 + 10,800 + 31,200 + 23,400 (25% × [408k−300k]) = CNY 66,480

**Working — CNY 780,000 Employment:**
- SS: 11% × 780,000 = CNY 85,800
- PIT base: 780,000 − 60,000 = CNY 720,000
- Tax: 1,080 + 10,800 + 31,200 + 30,000 + 72,000 + 9,600 (35% × [720k−660k]) = CNY 154,680

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/CNY ≈ 7.8 (2025 approximate average)
- Social security: ~11% employee share (city average; actual rate varies by city and scheme)
- No social security ceiling binding at these income levels (ceiling ≈ 3× average local salary, typically CNY 200k–400k depending on city)
- Standard CNY 60,000/year deduction applied for employment; no additional specific deductions modeled
- B2B: 20% business deduction applied; CNY 60,000 standard deduction also applied (as consolidated income)
- No annual bonus or stock option favorable treatment modeled
- Expatriate non-taxable benefits not modeled (available for foreign employees until January 2028)
