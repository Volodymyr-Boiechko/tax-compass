## Personal Income Tax

**Individuals are exempt from income tax in Brunei Darussalam.** Individuals who are partners are also not subject to tax on their apportioned shares of partnership income. (EY p.195)

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employment income | **0%** |
| Business / self-employment income | **0%** |
| Partnership income | **0%** |
| Capital gains | **0%** (no capital gains tax) |
| Dividends | **0%** |
| Interest | **0%** |
| Estate / inheritance | **0%** (no estate tax) |

---

## Other Taxes

**Capital gains tax:** No capital gains tax is levied in Brunei Darussalam. (EY p.195)

**Estate tax:** No estate tax is levied in Brunei Darussalam. (EY p.195)

**Note on corporate tax:** While individuals pay no income tax, companies operating in Brunei are subject to corporate income tax (standard rate 18.5%). Individual sole proprietors are effectively exempt as individuals.

## Social Security / SPK (Skim Persaraan Kebangsaan)

Brunei Darussalam does not impose social security taxes in the traditional sense. However, **Bruneian citizens and permanent residents** and their employers must contribute to the government-run **Skim Persaraan Kebangsaan (SPK)** (National Pension Scheme). This scheme does **not apply to foreign nationals who are not permanent residents**. (EY p.195)

**Employee contribution:** Minimum **8.5%** of basic salary

**Employer contributions** (based on employee's salary group):

| Employee salary group (BND/month) | Employer contribution rate |
|---|---|
| BND 500.00 and below | Fixed BND 57.50/month |
| BND 500.01 – BND 1,500.00 | **10.5%** (minimum BND 57.50/month) |
| BND 1,500.01 – BND 2,800.00 | **9.5%** |
| BND 2,800.01 and above | **8.5%** |

- Employers withhold employee contributions and pay monthly to SPK fund
- SPK is a benefit scheme for Bruneian citizens and permanent residents only
- **Foreign nationals (non-permanent residents): NOT subject to SPK contributions**

(EY p.195)

**Summary for foreign nationals (typical expat/digital nomad):**

| Contribution | Foreign national employee | Foreign national self-employed |
|---|---|---|
| Income tax | 0% | 0% |
| SPK (pension) | **0%** (exempt as non-permanent resident) | **0%** (exempt) |
| Total mandatory burden | **0%** | **0%** |

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed Professional / Sole Trader

- **Who qualifies:** Foreign national professionals (doctors, dentists, lawyers, accountants, architects, consulting engineers) and others
- **Tax base:** N/A — no income tax
- **Rate / mechanism:** **0%** income tax on all self-employment income
- **SPK:** Not applicable to non-permanent residents
- **Employment visa requirement:** Must apply for employment visa before taking up work; professional must obtain practicing license from relevant government ministry (case-by-case basis) (EY p.197)
- **Key limits:** No income tax; employment visa and practice license required

---

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No income tax means no preferential tax regime is needed. Brunei levies no income tax on any individual, resident or non-resident.

**Key practical note for expats:**
- Foreign nationals working in Brunei must obtain **employment visas** (not work permits per se) — issued initially for up to 2 years, renewable
- Employer must apply to Department of Labour through National Labour Management System (NLMS); takes ≥ 14 working days
- Medical screening required for all approved foreign workers
- **Permanent residence:** Restrictive policy; generally requires ≥ 20 years of residence/business in Brunei

(EY pp.196–198)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Brunei has NO personal income tax and NO capital gains tax. For **foreign nationals** (non-permanent residents), there are also **no SPK contributions**.

> The effective rate for a foreign national in Brunei is **0%** at all income levels.

| Gross income (BND) | EUR equiv | Scenario | SPK (employee 8.5%) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| BND 43,500 | €30,000 | Employment (Bruneian citizen) | BND 3,698 | BND 3,698 | **8.5%** |

- EUR/BND = 1.45 (approximate; BND is pegged 1:1 to SGD; SGD/USD ~0.74; EUR/USD ~1.08 → BND ~1.46/EUR; using 1.45)

- Bruneian citizen/permanent resident: 8.5% employee SPK contribution (no ceiling stated — modelled as flat 8.5% on all salary)
- No VAT in Brunei (no goods and services tax)
- Employer SPK contribution is an employer cost, not deducted from employee salary — not included in employee burden
