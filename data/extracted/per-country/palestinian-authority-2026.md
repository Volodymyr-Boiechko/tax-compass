## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system under the Income Tax Law (Law No. 17 of 2004, as amended). Income tax is imposed on all income realized by any individual in Palestine (West Bank and Gaza, subject to administrative jurisdiction). Residents: individuals residing in Palestine for at least 120 days/year (Palestinian nationals) or 183 days/year (non-Palestinian nationals). Tax year = calendar year. Returns filed within 4 months of year-end. Dual currency: Israeli New Shekel (ILS) and Jordanian Dinar (JD) are both used. (EY p.1186)

Exchange rate note from EY: USD 0.29 = ILS 1 and USD 1 = ILS 3.38 (as of 14 August 2025). Thus 1 EUR ≈ ILS 3.65 (approximate 2025 rate).

**PIT brackets** (EY p.1188)

| Taxable income (ILS) | Tax rate | Tax due | Cumulative tax due |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 75,000 | 5% | ILS 3,750 | ILS 3,750 |
| Next 75,000 (75,001–150,000) | 10% | ILS 7,500 | ILS 11,250 |
| Over 150,000 | 15% | — | — |

**Standard deductions / allowances (EY p.1187–1188):**
- Residency allowance: ILS 36,000/year
- One-time housing allowance: ILS 30,000 (for building/purchase of house) or mortgage interest deduction up to ILS 4,000/year (for housing loans, up to 10 years)
- Transportation: lesser of actual expenses or 10% of gross salary
- University student exemption: ILS 6,000/year (individual or spouse/children; max 2 students)
- Employee pension/provident fund contributions: deductible (approved by Minister of Finance)
- Business expenses: all reasonable business expenses deductible for self-employed

**Notes:**
- Dividends: currently treated as exempt (Ministry of Finance placed application of 10% WHT rate on hold; no taxes currently on dividends paid to individuals) (EY p.1186–1187)
- Interest income: taxable as ordinary income
- Rental income: taxed at ordinary progressive rates
- Capital gains: generally taxable; financial securities from investment portfolio may qualify for exemption (EY p.1187)
- Property tax: 17% of assessed rental value; 20% deduction from tax base allowed
- Individuals with income below ILS 50,000/year from business, or solely salary income, or agricultural income <ILS 30,000, or rental income <ILS 30,000: exempt from filing (EY p.1189)
- Withholding tax: 10% on payments to non-residents; resident WHT rates in DSC (Deduction at Source Certificate) system

## Social Security

The Palestinian social security system is nascent and partially implemented. There is no unified mandatory national social insurance scheme covering all private sector employees with full pensions in the same manner as neighboring countries. (EY p.1188)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security Law (Law No. 19/2016): pension | ~7% (when fully implemented) | ~8.5% (when fully implemented) | Separate provisions |
| Work injury insurance | — | ~1% (estimated) | — |
| Existing employer end-of-service gratuity | — | By Labour Law (not fixed %) | — |

**Note:** The Palestinian Social Security Law (Law No. 19 of 2016) was under phased implementation as of EY content date; exact contribution rates in force for 2025 should be verified with the Palestinian Social Security Corporation (SSC). The law has faced implementation delays and amendments. (EY p.1188)

**Income ceiling:** To be confirmed per SSC implementing regulations.

**Notes:**
- Many Palestinian employees' social protection comes through employer-provided end-of-service gratuity (severance) payments under the Labour Law
- UNRWA employees, UN and diplomatic staff: separate arrangements
- No established unemployment insurance

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Business / Sole Proprietor

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered with the Palestinian Income Tax Authority conducting business in Palestine.
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross revenue minus allowable business expenses). Losses may be carried forward for up to 5 years (EY p.1188).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT 5%–15% on net income after deductions and residency allowance.
- **Social security:** Subject to SSC once fully implemented; currently uncertain.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT (17% standard; 0% for some services) applies; register if annual taxable turnover exceeds threshold. Monthly filing with Palestinian Tax Authority.
- **Key limits:** Full accounting records required; annual return within 4 months of year-end. Advance tax payment incentives available (4% credit for first-month payment, 2% for second/third month).

### Option B — Partnership / Shirkah

- **Who qualifies:** Two or more individuals forming a partnership.
- **Tax base:** Partnership taxed at entity level; same progressive rates.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 5%–15% on net partnership income.
- **Social security:** Same as individual.
- **VAT obligations:** Same 17% VAT rules.
- **Key limits:** Partners must file individual returns showing partnership income allocation.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Employees / Non-Palestinian Nationals

- Non-Palestinian nationals are considered resident if present 183+ days/year. Below this threshold: taxed only on Palestine-source income.
- Treaty protection available with limited treaty network (Ethiopia, Jordan, Serbia, Sri Lanka, Sudan, Türkiye, UAE, Venezuela, Vietnam; negotiating with Oman and Turkmenistan). (EY p.1189)
- UN and diplomatic staff: exempt per bilateral agreements.

### Investment Incentives (Company Level)

- Palestinian Investment Promotion Agency (PIPA) offers various incentives for qualifying investments; primarily at company CIT level.

## 2026 Changes

- **Social Security Law implementation:** The SSC implementation timeline remains the most significant pending change; phased enrollment of private sector employees has been ongoing. Final rates and universal coverage timeline uncertain due to ongoing political situation.
- **Income Tax Law amendments:** The Palestinian Ministry of Finance periodically amends tax regulations; no specific 2026 PIT rate changes announced as of EY content date.
- **Gaza / West Bank administrative split:** Palestinian Authority administers West Bank tax; Gaza Strip tax administration has been separately managed since 2007. EY data primarily reflects West Bank/PA administration.
- **VAT:** 17% standard rate; no announced changes for 2026.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, West Bank jurisdiction.
> ILS/EUR rate: 1 EUR = ILS 3.65 (approximate 2025).
> "Employment": PIT at progressive rates + residency allowance (ILS 36,000) applied.
> "B2B Sole trader": same progressive rates; residency allowance applied; no other deductions.
> SS contributions omitted (SSC rates uncertain/not yet fully implemented for private sector).

### Working: ILS 109,500 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross income | | ILS 109,500 |
| Residency allowance | | ILS 36,000 |
| Taxable income | 109,500 − 36,000 | ILS 73,500 |
| PIT: 0–75,000 @ 5% | 5% × 73,500 | ILS 3,675 |
| Total PIT | | ILS 3,675 |
| **Effective rate** | **3,675 / 109,500** | **3.4%** |

### Working: ILS 219,000 (≈ €60,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross income | | ILS 219,000 |
| Residency allowance | | ILS 36,000 |
| Taxable income | 219,000 − 36,000 | ILS 183,000 |
| PIT: 0–75,000 @ 5% | | ILS 3,750 |
| PIT: 75,001–150,000 @ 10% | | ILS 7,500 |
| PIT: 150,001–183,000 @ 15% | 15% × 33,000 | ILS 4,950 |
| Total PIT | | ILS 16,200 |
| **Effective rate** | **16,200 / 219,000** | **7.4%** |

**ILS 365,000 working:** Taxable = 365,000 − 36,000 = 329,000
- 0–75,000 @ 5% = 3,750; 75,001–150,000 @ 10% = 7,500; 150,001–329,000 @ 15% = 26,850
- Total PIT = 38,100; **Effective rate = 38,100/365,000 = 10.4%** (table above uses this corrected figure)

**Key insight:** Palestinian Authority PIT has very low effective rates (3–10%) — among the lowest in the Middle East — due to the low brackets (max 15%) and generous residency allowance (ILS 36,000 = approx. €9,800/year). The system closely resembles the Jordanian income tax framework (Jordan administered the West Bank historically). The practical limitation is the political/economic environment rather than the tax burden.

**Assumptions:**
- ILS/EUR = 3.65 (ILS is pegged to the USD; USD/ILS ~3.38 per EY source)
- Residency allowance ILS 36,000 applied as standard deduction
- SSC contributions not included (implementation incomplete; rates unconfirmed)
- No transportation or other deductions beyond residency allowance
