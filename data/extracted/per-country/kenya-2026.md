## Personal Income Tax

Kenya taxes resident individuals on worldwide income; nonresidents on Kenya-source income only. Residency: no permanent home in Kenya and present ≥183 days in a fiscal year, or present on average >122 days/year in the current plus two preceding years; or has a permanent home in Kenya and spends any time in Kenya. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). PAYE withholding by employers; annual self-assessment return by 30 June of the following year. (EY pp. 783–792)

**Personal relief:** KES28,800/year (deducted from computed income tax). (EY p. 788)

**Insurance relief:** 15% of premiums paid on life, education, or health insurance, up to max relief of KES60,000/year. (EY p. 788)

**Affordable Housing Relief:** 15% of employee's AHL contribution, up to max relief of KES108,000/year (ceased 27 December 2024). (EY p. 788)

**Postretirement Medical Fund Relief:** 15% of employee's contribution, max relief KES60,000/year (effective 1 January 2024; ceased 27 December 2024). (EY p. 788)

### Key Deductions from Taxable Income

- Contributions to a registered pension or provident fund: up to KES360,000/year (effective 27 December 2024; previously KES240,000)
- Interest on borrowings to finance purchase, construction or improvement of owner-occupied residential property: up to KES360,000/year (effective 27 December 2024; previously KES300,000)
- Employee's contribution to Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF)
- Employee's contribution to Affordable Housing Levy (AHL)
- Noncash benefits: up to KES60,000/year exempt (effective 27 December 2024)
- Daily allowance while on official duty outside workplace: first KES10,000/day deemed reimbursement, not taxable (effective 1 July 2025)

## Social Security

Kenya has three mandatory payroll levies: NSSF, SHIF, and AHL. (EY pp. 791–792)

### National Social Security Fund (NSSF Act 2013)

The new NSSF Act (confirmed valid by Court of Appeal, February 2023): employer and employee each contribute **6% of pensionable earnings** subject to Tier I and Tier II categories:

| Tier | Contribution | Rate | Cap (per employer + employee) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier I | Lower limit (pensionable earnings ≤ KES8,000/month) | 6% each | Max Tier I: KES480/month (employee) from Feb 2025 |
| Tier II | Pensionable earnings KES8,001 – KES72,000/month | 6% each | Max Tier II: KES3,840/month (employee) from Feb 2025 |
| **Total max** | Employee + Employer | 6% each | **Employee max: KES4,320/month** (= KES51,840/year) |

**From 2026:** Lower limit → KES9,000/month; upper limit → KES108,000/month; Tier I max KES540/month; Tier II max KES5,940/month; total employee max KES6,480/month = KES77,760/year. (EY p. 791)

### Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF) — replaces NHIF

- **Effective:** 1 October 2024 (formal transition from NHIF to SHIF)
- **Rate:** **2.75%** of gross monthly income
- **Minimum:** KES300/month
- **No upper limit** on contributions
- Contributions deductible from taxable income (EY p. 792)

### Affordable Housing Levy (AHL)

- **Rate:** **1.5%** of gross monthly salary (employer matches at 1.5%)
- **Base:** Gross monthly salary = basic salary + regular cash allowances (travel, housing, commuter, car allowances, regular cash payments) — excludes leave allowances, bonuses, gratuity, pension, severance pay, terminal dues
- **Remittance:** By the 9th day of the following month (EY p. 792)
- Contributions deductible from taxable income

**Summary table:**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| NSSF (new Act) | **6%** (capped ~KES4,320–6,480/month) | **6%** (same) | Tiers I+II |
| SHIF (health) | **2.75%** | — | No ceiling |
| AHL (housing) | **1.5%** | **1.5%** | On gross salary |
| **Approximate employee total** | **~4.25% + NSSF cap** | | |

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals deriving income from trade, profession, or vocation in Kenya
- **Tax base:** Net income from business (gross receipts minus wholly and exclusively incurred expenses; accrual method; 31 December year-end mandatory)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same PAYE progressive rates (10%/25%/30%/32.5%/35%); personal relief KES28,800/year; quarterly estimated tax payments on the 20th of months 4, 6, 9, and 12
- **Social obligations:** NSSF, SHIF (2.75% of income, no cap), AHL (1.5%)
- **VAT obligations:** Value Added Tax at 16%; registration mandatory if annual taxable turnover > KES5,000,000; monthly filing
- **Key limits:** Annual return by 30 June; married women file separately from 1 July 2023

### Option B — Turnover Tax (for small businesses)

- **Who qualifies:** Businesses with annual turnover KES1,000,000–KES25,000,000
- **Rate:** **1.5% of gross turnover** (reduced from 3% per 2023 Finance Act, further from 1% per Affordable Housing Act March 2024 → now 1.5% per current rules; verify effective current rate)
- **Notes:** Replaces standard income tax for qualifying small businesses; no expense deductions; monthly filing

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Digital Nomad Visa (Class N)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals who work remotely within Kenya for a company registered outside the country
- **Tax implication:** If resident in Kenya (≥183 days), worldwide income taxable; if non-resident (<183 days), only Kenya-source income taxable — remote work for foreign employer may not generate Kenya-source income if services deemed performed for non-Kenya entity
- **Work permit:** Class N permit issued for this category (EY p. 795)

### Foreign Technician 50% Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Certain foreign technicians rendering services in Korea [Note: Kenya does not have this; this is a Korea provision] — ignore; Kenya does not have an equivalent
- **Kenya expat deduction:** Expatriate employees of accredited regional offices of foreign corporations who spend ≥120 days outside Kenya during the fiscal year may deduct **one-third** of their total income (EY p. 787)

## 2026 Changes

- **2025 Finance Act (effective 1 July 2025):** Digital Service Tax replaced by Significant Economic Presence Tax (SEPT) for nonresidents with Kenya-source digital service income; daily allowance KES10,000/day outside workplace now not taxable (EY pp. 785, 792)
- **Tax Laws Amendment Act 2024 (effective 27 December 2024):** Pension deduction raised to KES360,000/year; mortgage interest deduction raised to KES360,000/year; noncash benefits exempt up to KES60,000/year; affordable housing relief and postretirement medical fund relief both ceased 27 December 2024 (EY pp. 785, 788)
- **NSSF new limits from 2026:** Lower limit KES9,000/month; upper limit KES108,000/month; employee max Tier I KES540/month + Tier II KES5,940/month = KES6,480/month total (EY p. 791)
- **SHIF effective 1 October 2024:** 2.75% of gross income replaces NHIF; no cap; minimum KES300/month (EY p. 792)
- **AHL (1.5% employee + 1.5% employer):** On gross monthly salary; AHL contributions are deductible (EY pp. 788, 792)
- **Gratuity exemption (from 27 June 2025):** Gratuity paid by either public or private schemes is exempt from tax (previously only public scheme exempt) (EY p. 785)
- **Virtual assets:** Digital Asset Tax repealed; excise duty at 10% on fees charged by virtual asset providers effective 1 July 2025 (EY p. 787)
- **Tax loss carryforward:** 2025 Finance Act introduced 5-year time limit on deductibility of tax losses (previously no time limit); extension possible via KRA application (EY p. 791)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **NSSF 2026 transition:** From 2026, NSSF caps increase significantly (employee max KES6,480/month = KES77,760/year). This will increase the total burden by ~KES26,000/year for higher-income employees. Verify exact effective date.
- [ ] **SHIF no cap:** SHIF at 2.75% with no upper limit means high earners pay significant health insurance. At €100k gross (KES14.5M), SHIF = KES398,750/year. This is a notable cost compared to capped systems in other countries.
- [ ] **Turnover Tax for B2B:** Self-employed individuals with annual turnover KES1M–KES25M may elect the 1.5% Turnover Tax on gross revenue instead of the standard progressive regime. At €30k gross (KES4.35M), Turnover Tax = 1.5% × 4,350,000 = KES65,250 vs standard income tax of KES1,158,138. Turnover Tax is dramatically lower — but verify eligibility and exclusions (e.g., professional services may be excluded).
- [ ] **NSSF self-employed obligations:** It is unclear from EY whether self-employed individuals must pay both employee and employer NSSF contributions (6% + 6% = 12% of pensionable earnings). If so, add KES103,680/year (2 × KES51,840) instead of KES51,840 for the B2B scenario.
- [ ] **Pension deduction at €100k:** The KES360,000/year pension deduction limit is relatively small as a % of gross at €100k level (~2.5%) but could reduce taxable income meaningfully for lower earners. Not modeled above.
- [ ] **Digital Nomad (Class N) visa:** Kenya now issues Class N work permits for remote workers. If a digital nomad is non-resident (< 183 days), their foreign-employer income may not be Kenya-source income. Tax implications require careful case-by-case analysis.
- [ ] **Capital Gains Tax:** CGT at 15% applies to transfers of property located in Kenya (effective 1 January 2023 rate). Sale of shares on Nairobi Securities Exchange: exempt. Sale of unlisted shares and real property: 15% CGT.
