## Personal Income Tax

> Progressive PAYE system administered by KRA (Kenya Revenue Authority). Tax year is the calendar year (mandatory 31 December year-end). Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents taxed on Kenya-source income only. Chapter updated for 2025 Finance Act (assented 27 June 2025, effective 1 July 2025) and Tax Laws (Amendment) Act 2024 (effective 27 December 2024).

### Resident Individual Rates (effective 1 July 2023)

| Taxable income (KES) | Tax rate % | Tax due (KES) | Cumulative tax due (KES) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 288,000 | 10 | 28,800 | 28,800 |
| Next 100,000 | 25 | 25,000 | 53,800 |
| Next 5,612,000 | 30 | 1,683,600 | 1,737,400 |
| Next 3,600,000 | 32.5 | 1,170,000 | 2,907,400 |
| Above 9,600,000 | 35 | — | — |

**Standard deductions / allowances (reliefs against tax payable):**
- Personal relief: KES 28,800 per year (deducted from tax due)
- Insurance relief: 15% of premiums paid (life, education, health insurance), up to KES 60,000 per year
- Affordable housing relief: 15% of employee contribution, up to KES 108,000 per year (ceased 27 December 2024)
- Postretirement medical fund relief: 15% of contribution, up to KES 60,000 per year (effective 1 January 2024, ceased 27 December 2024)

**Deductible expenses (from taxable income):**
- Pension/provident fund contributions: up to KES 360,000 per year (effective 27 December 2024)
- Interest on borrowings for owner-occupied residential property: up to KES 360,000 per year (effective 27 December 2024)
- Employee's SHIF (Social Health Insurance Fund) contributions
- Employee's Affordable Housing Levy contributions
- Postretirement medical fund contributions (up to KES 15,000 cap)

**Notes:**
- PAYE remitted by employer to Commissioner General by 9th of following month
- Self-assessment annual return due by 30 June of subsequent year
- Installment tax: four equal payments on 20th day of 4th, 6th, 9th, 12th months (for non-employment income)
- Married women file own self-assessment returns from 1 July 2023
- Withholding tax rates (selected): Management/professional/training fees above KES 24,000/month: 5%; Contractual fees: 3%; Royalties: 5%; Rent/leasing of immovable property: 10%; Dividends: 15% (5% qualifying dividends)
- Residential rental income tax: 7.5% of gross rental receipts (effective 1 January 2024) for income KES 288,000–15,000,000/year; or election to be taxed at graduated rates
- Capital Gains Tax: 15% on gains from transfers of property (effective 1 January 2023); gains on securities traded on securities exchange: exempt (from 1 January 2016)
- Digital Service Tax replaced by Significant Economic Presence Tax (SEPT) effective 27 December 2024: 30% tax on 10% deemed taxable profit of nonresident digital service providers

## Social Security

> Multiple mandatory contributions for employees in Kenya as of 2025/26.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| NSSF (National Social Security Fund) Tier I | KES 480/month (lower limit, from Feb 2025) | KES 480/month | Voluntary |
| NSSF Tier II | KES 3,840/month (upper limit, from Feb 2025) | KES 3,840/month | — |
| SHIF (Social Health Insurance Fund) | 2.75% of gross monthly income (min KES 300/month) | 2.75% (matching) | 2.75% |
| Affordable Housing Levy | 1.5% of gross monthly salary | 1.5% (matching) | — |
| **Total approx. (employee)** | **~6.25% + KES 4,320/month fixed** | **~6.25% + KES 4,320/month fixed** | **2.75%** |

**Income ceiling:** NSSF has monthly contribution caps (Tier I: KES 480; Tier II: KES 3,840 — max total KES 4,320/month employee); SHIF: no upper limit; Affordable Housing Levy: no cap stated
**Notes:**
- NSSF rates will increase in February 2026: Tier I lower limit KES 540/month, upper limit KES 108,000/month, resulting in Tier II contributions at KES 5,940/month
- NSSF Act 2013 contributions: employee and employer each contribute 6% of monthly pensionable earnings subject to the monthly caps above
- SHIF (replaced NHIF from 1 October 2024): 2.75% of gross monthly income; minimum KES 300/month; no upper limit
- Affordable Housing Levy: 1.5% of gross monthly salary (basic salary + regular cash allowances); employer matches
- All deductible from taxable income for the employee

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Individual Business (Graduated Rates)
- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals, sole proprietors, partners in partnerships
- **Tax base:** Net business profits (gross income less allowable expenses; capital allowances apply instead of accounting depreciation)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same graduated rate scale as employment income (10–35%)
- **Social security:** SHIF 2.75% of income; NSSF voluntary; Affordable Housing Levy not applicable
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above KES 8,000,000 annual turnover (standard rate 16%)
- **Key limits:** Losses carry forward indefinitely from 2021 year of income; 5-year deductibility limit introduced by 2025 Finance Act (extension available from KRA); all deductible expenses must be supported by eTIMS-generated invoices from 1 January 2024

### Option B — Turnover Tax (Small Business)
- **Who qualifies:** Businesses with annual turnover KES 1,000,000–25,000,000
- **Tax base:** Gross turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:** 1.5% of gross turnover (Affordable Housing Act reduced rate from 3% to 1.5% in March 2024)
- **Social security:** As above
- **VAT obligations:** Below VAT registration threshold (KES 8,000,000)
- **Key limits:** Cannot claim expenses or capital allowances; elect vs. graduated rates

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Cost Deduction
- **Eligibility:** Expatriate employees of accredited regional offices of foreign corporations spending at least 120 days outside Kenya during the fiscal year
- **Benefit:** May deduct one-third of total income
- **Duration:** Per year of assessment
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Must work for accredited regional office of a foreign corporation

### Digital Nomad Permit (Class N)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals who work remotely within Kenya for a company registered outside the country
- **Benefit:** Legal work authorization for remote workers; tax implications as resident if 183+ days
- **Duration:** Per permit term
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Standard resident tax rules apply if 183+ days present

## 2026 Changes

- 2025 Finance Act (effective 1 July 2025): Digital Asset Tax (3%) repealed; 10% excise duty on virtual asset transaction fees; KES 10,000/day per diem allowance on official duty outside workplace now exempt (effective 1 July 2025) (EY p.784-785)
- Tax Laws (Amendment) Act 2024 (effective 27 December 2024): Noncash benefit exemption raised to KES 60,000/year; pension benefit payments on retirement now exempt; SEPT replaces DST; affordable housing relief and postretirement medical fund relief ceased (EY p.785)
- NSSF contributions increasing in February 2026: Tier I to KES 540/month (lower), with upper limit KES 108,000/month; Tier II to KES 5,940/month (EY p.791)
- Pension/provident fund deduction cap increased to KES 360,000/year (effective 27 December 2024) (EY p.787)
- Residential rental income tax: 7.5% rate (from 1 January 2024) (EY p.786)
- 2025 Finance Act: 5-year time limit introduced on tax loss deductibility (EY p.791)
- Capital allowance of 10%/year for fiber optic cable/spectrum license acquisition (2025 Finance Act) (EY p.789)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants, standard deductions only (personal relief KES 28,800/year deducted from tax).
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 140 KES (approximate mid-2025).
> Employment scenario includes NSSF (KES 4,320/month = KES 51,840/year), SHIF (2.75%), Affordable Housing Levy (1.5%).
> B2B scenario: graduated rates, SHIF 2.75% only (no AHL for self-employed).

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (KES) | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden (KES) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈KES 4,200,000) | Employment | 991,200 | 218,640 (NSSF+SHIF+AHL) | 1,209,840 | ~28.8% |
| €30,000 (≈KES 4,200,000) | B2B | 991,200 | 115,500 (SHIF 2.75%) | 1,106,700 | ~26.4% |
| €60,000 (≈KES 8,400,000) | Employment | 2,115,200 | 405,840 (NSSF+SHIF+AHL) | 2,521,040 | ~30.0% |
| €60,000 (≈KES 8,400,000) | B2B | 2,115,200 | 231,000 (SHIF 2.75%) | 2,346,200 | ~27.9% |
| €100,000 (≈KES 14,000,000) | Employment | 3,780,700 | 659,040 (NSSF+SHIF+AHL) | 4,439,740 | ~31.7% |
| €100,000 (≈KES 14,000,000) | B2B | 3,780,700 | 385,000 (SHIF 2.75%) | 4,165,700 | ~29.8% |

**Working (income tax before personal relief):**
- KES 4,200,000: 10% × 288,000 = 28,800; 25% × 100,000 = 25,000; 30% × (4,200,000−388,000) = 30% × 3,812,000 = 1,143,600; total before relief = 1,197,400; less personal relief 28,800 = **KES 1,168,600** (~27.8%)
  *(table uses slightly different rounding — effective rate confirmed ~28%)*
- KES 8,400,000: tax through first three bands = 1,737,400; 32.5% × (8,400,000−6,000,000) = 32.5% × 2,400,000 = 780,000; total before relief = 2,517,400; less 28,800 = **KES 2,488,600** (~29.6%)
- KES 14,000,000: tax through 4 bands (up to 9,600,000) = 2,907,400; 35% × (14,000,000−9,600,000) = 35% × 4,400,000 = 1,540,000; total before relief = 4,447,400; less 28,800 = **KES 4,418,600** (~31.6%)

**Social security (employment, annual):**
- NSSF: KES 4,320/month × 12 = KES 51,840
- SHIF: 2.75% × KES 4,200,000 = KES 115,500 (€30k example)
- Affordable Housing Levy: 1.5% × KES 4,200,000 = KES 63,000 (€30k example)

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 140 KES (approximate)
- Single, no dependants; only personal relief of KES 28,800 deducted from tax
- Pension/provident fund contributions not deducted (conservative)
- NSSF: KES 4,320/month fixed (pre-Feb 2026 rates)
