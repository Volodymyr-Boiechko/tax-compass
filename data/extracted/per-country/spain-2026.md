## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (IRPF). Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income. Total
liability = state (estatal) component + autonomous community (autonómica) component. The combined
marginal rate varies by region; Madrid and Cataluña are shown as primary references.

**Investment income** (dividends, interest, capital gains) is taxed on a separate progressive scale:
19% (0–6,000), 21% (6,000–50,000), 23% (50,000–200,000), 27% (200,000–300,000), 30% (300,000+).
(EY p.1439)

### Combined top marginal rates (state + autonomous)

| Region | Top combined marginal rate | Above (EUR) |
|---|---|---|
| Madrid | 45.00% | 300,000 |
| Cataluña | 50.00% | 300,000 |

(EY p.1439: "the maximum marginal tax rate is 45% for an individual resident in Madrid and 50% for a
resident in Cataluña (for income above EUR300,000)")

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Employee SS contributions: fully deductible from employment income (EY p.1437)
- Employment income reduction (reducción por rendimientos del trabajo):
  - Net employment income ≤ €14,852: deduction €7,302
  - Net employment income €14,852–€17,673.52: €7,302 − 1.75 × (income − 14,852)
  - Net employment income €17,673.52–€19,747.50: €2,364.34 − 1.14 × (income − 17,673.52)
  - Net employment income > €19,747.50: €0
  (EY p.1437; applies only if other income ≤ €6,500)
- Pension contributions: deductible up to lesser of 30% of net employment/business income or €1,500/year
  (extendable to €8,500 if employer also contributes) (EY p.1438)
- Personal allowance (mínimo personal): €5,550 — functions as a tax credit (tax on €5,550 deducted from
  liability); not a reduction of the taxable base (EY p.1438)

**Notes:**
- Tax is computed separately on two bases: (1) general income (employment, business, rental) and
  (2) savings/investment income; they cannot be offset against each other
- Foreign employment income earned abroad: exemption up to €60,100 if conditions met (EY p.1435)
- Irregular employment income (>2 years or by nature irregular): 30% reduction, once per 5 years (EY p.1435)

- Employee SS is capped at €58,914 × 6.48% = €3,817/year maximum (2025)
- Solidarity contribution applies on the uncapped excess but generates no additional pension rights

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Autónomo (RETA — Régimen Especial de Trabajadores Autónomos)

- **Who qualifies:** Any self-employed individual, freelancer, or sole trader
- **Tax base:** Net business income (revenue minus allowable expenses); subject to regular IRPF
  progressive scale (same brackets as employment income above). Quarterly advance payments (pago
  fraccionado) of 20% of net profit.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive IRPF on net profit. No special flat rate — this is the
  primary distinction from, e.g., Poland's Ryczałt or Ukraine's ФОП. SL (limited company) is the
  main alternative for high earners (see Known Gaps).
- **Social security:** Income-based RETA system; 15 brackets from monthly net ≤€670 to >€6,000.
  Contribution rate ≈ 31.3% applied to chosen base within bracket limits. Minimum base contributes
  first to pension, so choosing minimum reduces pension rights proportionally.

  effective rate calculations below.
- **Key limits:** No turnover ceiling; autónomo applies to any income level. New autónomos: reduced
  "tarifa plana" of €80/month for first 12 months (2025).

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Beckham Law (Régimen Especial de Impatriados — Art. 93 LIRPF)

- **Eligibility:** Individual who transfers tax residence to Spain and:
  (1) was not tax resident in Spain in any of the 5 preceding years, AND
  (2) arrival is connected to: labor contract with Spanish entity, director appointment, entrepreneurial
  activity (Law 14/2013), or highly qualified professional activity for a startup.
  As of 2023, can be extended to accompanying spouse and children under 25.
  (EY p.1434)
- **Benefit:** Taxed as a non-resident on employment and self-employment income:
  - Up to €600,000: flat **24%**
  - Above €600,000: flat **47%**
  Regular personal allowances and the employment income reduction do NOT apply (non-resident rules).
  Investment income retains the savings income scale (19–30%).
  (EY p.1434)
- **Duration:** Year of arrival + 5 subsequent tax years = up to 6 years total
- **Application deadline:** Must elect within 6 months of registering with Spanish Social Security.
  No extensions allowed. (EY p.1434)
- **Caveats:**
  - Cannot receive income through a permanent establishment in Spain (except from the enumerated
    qualifying activities)
  - Social security contributions remain at normal employee/employer rates (6.48% / 30.57%)
  - Beckham Law is tax-advantageous vs standard rates only above approximately €45–55k annual
    income (below this, progressive rates + personal allowances beat the flat 24%)
  - Golden visa (residence by investment) was abolished as of 4 April 2025 (EY p.1444); Digital
    Nomad Visa (International Teleworker) remains available

## 2026 Changes

- **RETA table unchanged for 2026:** No revision to the 15-tranche autónomo contribution table;
  same minimum/maximum bases and quotas as 2025. (infoautonomos.com)
- **Solidarity contribution increases continue:** Annual step-up per the 2022 law schedule; 2026
  values to be confirmed by Seguridad Social
- **Beckham Law unchanged:** No announced modifications for 2026
- **Golden Visa abolished:** New applications closed since 4 April 2025; existing holders may
  renew while maintaining investment (EY p.1444)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only. Region = Madrid.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee pays 6.48% SS up to €58,914 cap).
> "Autónomo B2B" = net freelance revenue invoiced; RETA at minimum contribution base for bracket.
> "Beckham Law" = new resident employment, flat 24% (no personal allowances applied).
> IRPF computed: tax(taxable base) − tax(personal minimum €5,550). Autónomo: SS deducted from base before IRPF.

### Working: €30,000 Employment (Madrid)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS | 6.48% × 30,000 | €1,944 |
| Taxable base | 30,000 − 1,944 (employment reduction = 0 as base > €19,747.50) | €28,056 |
| State IRPF | 12,450×9.5% + 7,750×12% + 7,856×15% − 5,550×9.5% | €2,764 |
| Madrid IRPF | 13,362×8.5% + 5,642×10.7% + 9,052×12.8% − 5,550×8.5% | €2,426 |
| Total IRPF | | €5,190 |
| **Total burden** | **5,190 + 1,944** | **€7,134** |
| **Effective rate** | **7,134 / 30,000** | **23.8%** |

### Working: €60,000 Employment (Madrid)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS | 6.48% × 58,914 (capped) | €3,818 |
| Taxable base | 60,000 − 3,818 | €56,182 |
| State IRPF | …+24,800×18.5% − 5,550×9.5% | €7,717 |
| Madrid IRPF | …+20,757×17.4% − 5,550×8.5% | €6,981 |
| Total IRPF | | €14,698 |
| **Total burden** | **14,698 + 3,818** | **€18,516** |
| **Effective rate** | **18,516 / 60,000** | **30.9%** |

### Working: €100,000 Employment (Madrid)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS | 6.48% × 58,914 (capped) | €3,818 |
| Taxable base | 100,000 − 3,818 | €96,182 |
| State IRPF | …+36,182×22.5% − 5,550×9.5% | €16,565 |
| Madrid IRPF | …+38,862×20.5% − 5,550×8.5% | €15,146 |

**Autónomo RETA SS working:**
- €30k (€2,500/month → Tranche 10, min base €1,600): 1,600 × 31.3% × 12 = **€6,010/year**
- €60k (€5,000/month → Tranche 14, min base €2,000): 2,000 × 31.3% × 12 = **€7,512/year**
- €100k (€8,333/month → Tranche 15, min base €2,000): 2,000 × 31.3% × 12 = **€7,512/year**

**Key insight — Beckham Law breakeven:** Beckham Law (24% flat) beats standard employment in Madrid
above approx. €55k gross. At €30k, standard rates with personal allowances are cheaper (23.8% vs
30.5%). At €100k, Beckham Law saves ~7.7 pp (27.8% vs 35.5%).

**Cataluña surcharge note:** At €100k employment, Cataluña autonomous rates add ~1.7 pp vs Madrid
(approx. 37.2% total effective rate vs 35.5%). At income >€300k, Cataluña peaks at 50% combined
marginal rate vs 45% in Madrid. (EY p.1439)

**Assumptions:**
- Single, no dependants, no disability, no other income sources
- Madrid autonomous community throughout
- No pension contributions, no mortgage relief
- Autónomo: minimum RETA contribution base for income bracket; B2B revenue = net profit (no expenses)
- Beckham Law: no personal allowances; SS applied normally
- All figures 2025; 2026 rates assumed unchanged pending announcement

- [ ] SL (Sociedad Limitada) path not modeled: high earners (>€60k) often use SL + salary +

      19–30% investment scale + autónomo societario SS)
- [ ] Cataluña effective rate examples not computed in full (only narrative note)
- [ ] Módulos simplified regime not covered (narrow applicability; excludes most tech/professional workers)
- [ ] Wealth tax (Impuesto sobre el Patrimonio) and Solidarity Tax (>€3M) not modeled
- [ ] Autonomous communities other than Madrid and Cataluña not covered
- [ ] Beckham Law for self-employment (as opposed to employment) not separately computed

      confirm no further changes for 2026 fiscal year
