## Personal Income Tax

The Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands (CNMI) operates a "mirror" tax system based on the US federal Internal Revenue Code (IRC), modified by local CNMI provisions. Residents file CNMI income tax returns (not US federal returns, except for CNMI residents who are US citizens and have non-CNMI income — they may also owe US federal tax). The CNMI has its own tax authority (Division of Revenue and Taxation — DRT). Tax year = calendar year. (EY p.1138)

**How the mirror system works:**
- CNMI adopts the US federal IRC as modified by CNMI law
- The federal income tax brackets, standard deductions, and credits apply with "CNMI" substituted for "IRS" and "CNMI" substituted for "United States" in most instances
- A CNMI local surcharge (formerly called the "surtax") may apply on top of the mirrored federal tax
- CNMI residents pay CNMI income tax rather than US federal income tax (on their CNMI-source income); US citizens who are CNMI residents pay CNMI tax as their satisfaction of the federal tax obligation under the CNMI Covenant

**US Federal (mirrored) PIT brackets — 2025, Single filer (USD)** (EY p.1138; federal brackets)

| Taxable income (USD) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 11,925 | 10% |
| 11,926 – 48,475 | 12% |
| 48,476 – 103,350 | 22% |
| 103,351 – 197,300 | 24% |
| 197,301 – 250,525 | 32% |
| 250,526 – 626,350 | 35% |
| Over 626,350 | 37% |

**CNMI local surcharge:** The CNMI imposes an additional local income tax (surcharge) on CNMI taxable income. Historically this has been set as a percentage of federal tax liability computed under the mirrored system. The current CNMI surcharge rate is approximately 5% of the computed federal tax liability (this supplements, not replaces, the mirrored federal tax). (EY p.1139)

**Standard deductions / allowances (2025, federal):**
- Standard deduction (single): USD 15,000
- Standard deduction (married filing jointly): USD 30,000
- Personal exemptions: phased out for high earners; the TCJA eliminated personal exemptions at federal level (currently USD 0 federal exemption); CNMI mirrors this
- Child Tax Credit: USD 2,000 per qualifying child (subject to phase-out above USD 200,000)

**Notes:**
- US citizens residing in CNMI: satisfy US federal tax obligation by paying CNMI tax on CNMI-source income; any non-CNMI-source income may trigger additional US federal filing
- Non-US-citizen residents: pay CNMI income tax only; no US federal obligation
- CNMI has a territorial element: income from sources outside CNMI that is not US-source may have specific treatment under the Covenant
- Self-employment tax: same as US federal (15.3% on first USD 176,100 of self-employment net income; 2.9% above for Medicare — federal rates mirrored)

## Social Security

CNMI participates in the US federal Social Security and Medicare programs. (EY p.1140)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security (OASDI) | 6.2% (on wages up to USD 176,100) | 6.2% (up to USD 176,100) | 12.4% (up to USD 176,100) |
| Medicare (HI) | 1.45% (no ceiling) | 1.45% (no ceiling) | 2.9% (no ceiling) |
| Additional Medicare | 0.9% (on wages above USD 200,000 single) | — | 0.9% (above USD 200,000) |
| **Total (standard)** | **7.65%** | **7.65%** | **15.3%** |

**Income ceiling (OASDI):** USD 176,100 (2025 federal wage base).

**Notes:**
- Self-employed individuals pay both employee and employer shares (15.3% combined); 50% of self-employment tax is deductible from federal/CNMI income tax
- Medicare Additional Tax (0.9%) applies on wages/SE income above USD 200,000 (single)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Schedule C (mirrored US system)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a trade or business in CNMI; reports on CNMI Schedule C equivalent.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (revenue minus all ordinary and necessary business expenses).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive federal rates (mirrored) 10%–37% + CNMI local surcharge (~5% of computed tax). Self-employment tax (SE tax) 15.3%/2.9% also applies on net earnings.
- **Social security:** SE tax = 15.3% on first USD 176,100 net SE income + 2.9% Medicare above; 50% deductible from income tax.
- **VAT obligations:** No VAT/GST; CNMI has a Business Gross Revenue Tax (BGRT) at varying rates (0.5%–5% depending on activity) — separate from income tax.
- **Key limits:** Estimated tax payments required quarterly if SE tax + income tax expected to exceed USD 1,000. File CNMI Form 1040-CM.

### Option B — S-Corporation / Pass-Through Entity

- **Who qualifies:** Small business owners seeking liability protection; S-Corp election available under CNMI mirror rules.
- **Tax base:** Income flows through to individual shareholders; taxed at personal rates.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates as individual.
- **Social security:** Active owners/shareholders subject to FICA/SE on reasonable compensation.
- **VAT obligations:** BGRT applies at entity level.
- **Key limits:** Corporate formalities required; more complex than sole proprietor.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### CNMI Covenant Status

- **Eligibility:** Non-US citizens who become CNMI permanent residents.
- **Benefit:** Pay CNMI income tax only; no US federal income tax obligation on CNMI-source income. This can be advantageous vs. US mainland residency where worldwide income is taxed.
- **Duration:** Ongoing for CNMI residents.
- **Caveats:** Non-immigrant workers in CNMI (CW-1 visa holders) are subject to CNMI tax on CNMI income; complex US citizenship/residency interactions.

### Foreign Investor Certificate

- Business investment incentives exist for qualifying foreign investors in CNMI; primarily company-level tax benefits, not individual PIT reduction.

## 2026 Changes

- **TCJA sunset risk:** Key provisions of the US Tax Cuts and Jobs Act 2017 (which CNMI mirrors) were set to expire 31 December 2025. The Tax Relief and Jobs Act / One Big Beautiful Bill Act (2025) is expected to extend or make permanent many TCJA provisions (including the 37% top rate, doubled standard deduction, SALT cap). CNMI mirrors whatever federal law is in effect; monitor US federal legislative developments. (EY p.1138)
- **Social Security wage base 2026:** Expected to increase above USD 176,100 (adjusted annually by SSA).
- **CNMI local surcharge:** May be adjusted annually by CNMI Legislature; verify current rate at cnmidrt.com.

**Key insight:** CNMI offers among the lowest income tax burdens of any US territory for moderate incomes (13–23% for employment). The mirror system with TCJA provisions (low 10%/12% brackets up to ~USD 48k) is very favorable. The main disadvantage vs. mainland US is the BGRT for businesses. Self-employed pay ~7% more than employed due to double SE tax burden.
