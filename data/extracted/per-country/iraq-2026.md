## Personal Income Tax

Iraq imposes income tax under Income Tax Law No. 113 of 1982 (as amended). Iraqi nationals residing in Iraq are taxed on worldwide income; non-Iraqi nationals and Iraqi nationals working in Iraq but residing elsewhere are taxed on Iraq-source income only. An Iraqi national is considered resident if residing in Iraq for even one day; non-Iraqis are resident if they meet certain residency criteria. The Kurdistan Region of Iraq (KRI) applies different rules (flat 5% rate and different allowances). Tax is withheld monthly by employers (PAYE) and remitted to the General Commission for Taxes (GCT). Annual filing required by 31 March. (EY pp. 651–654)

### Income Tax Brackets (Iraq — excluding Kurdistan Region)

Progressive rates applied to **annual taxable income** (after personal allowances and deductions): (EY p. 653)

| Annual taxable income (IQD) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 250,000 | 3% |
| 250,001 – 500,000 | 5% |
| 500,001 – 1,000,000 | 10% |
| Above 1,000,000 | 15% |

**Note on top rate:** The maximum statutory rate is **15%** — one of the lowest flat-cap rates in the region. The brackets in IQD are very low relative to typical salaries, meaning most workers quickly reach the 15% band.

### Kurdistan Region of Iraq (KRI)

| Taxable income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All taxable income | **Flat 5%** |

KRI applies a flat 5% personal income tax rate on all taxable income. (EY p. 653)

### Standard Deductions / Allowances (Iraq — excluding KRI)

Available to **residents of Iraq** only: (EY p. 652)

| Allowance type | Annual amount (IQD) |
|---|---|
| Single deduction | 2,500,000 |
| Wife deduction | 4,500,000 |
| Each child (under 18 or student under 25) | 200,000 |
| Widow or divorcee | 3,200,000 |
| Over retirement age (63) | 300,000 |
| Employee contributions to Iraqi social security | Deductible |
| Delegation / overseas allowances (foreign employees) | Up to 25% of basic salary |
| General allowances (cash or non-cash, non-Iraqis) | Up to 25% of basic salary (non-taxable) |
| General allowances (Iraqi employees) | Up to 30% of basic salary (non-taxable) |

**Kurdistan Region allowances:**
- First IQD 1,000,000 of an employee's monthly income is deductible
- Employee contributions to social security are deductible
- General allowances up to 30% of basic salary (EY p. 652–653)

**Notes:**
- Dividends paid from previously taxed income: exempt from tax
- Interest income: subject to income tax at normal rates
- Capital gains from sale of shares/bonds NOT in the course of a trading activity: **exempt**
- Capital gains from shares/bonds IN the course of a trading activity: taxable at normal rates
- Capital gains on fixed assets: taxable at normal rates

## Social Security

Iraq mandates registration with the Iraqi social security system for all companies operating in Iraq, regardless of employee nationality. (EY p. 653)

### Iraq (excluding KRI) — General sector (non-oil and gas)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (Iraqis) | **5%** | **12%** | |
| Social security (non-Iraqis) | **5%** | **20%** | Higher employer rate for foreigners |
| **Total (Iraqis)** | **5%** | **12%** | **17% total** |
| **Total (non-Iraqis)** | **5%** | **20%** | **25% total** |

### Iraq (excluding KRI) — Oil and gas sector

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Social security (Iraqis) | **5%** | **25%** |
| Social security (non-Iraqis) | **5%** | **33%** |

**Income ceiling:** Maximum monthly salary subject to social security: **IQD 1,750,000/month** (~USD 1,326) based on the minimum wage for 2024. (EY p. 653)

### Kurdistan Region of Iraq (KRI)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (Iraqis and non-Iraqis) | **5%** | **12%** | No salary cap |

**Notes:**
- KRI: No salary cap — contributions apply to total compensation
- Iraq (ex-KRI): Cap of IQD 1,750,000/month; above this, no additional contributions
- Self-employed: No specific self-employment social security regime described

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals and business owners conducting taxable business activities in Iraq
- **Tax base:** Net profit after business expenses (ordinary deductible expenses connected with earning income); entertainment expenses subject to limitations
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates as employment income (3%/5%/10%/15% in Iraq; flat 5% in KRI); personal allowances available to Iraqi residents
- **Social security:** Self-employed individuals are not explicitly covered by the mandatory employer/employee social security framework described in EY; no specific self-employment SS regime disclosed
- **VAT obligations:** Iraq does not currently have a VAT system (no VAT/GST)
- **Key limits:** No separate simplified or presumptive regime described; full bookkeeping with deductible business expenses

**Note:** Iraq's income tax system is relatively simple — no VAT, no wealth tax, no gift/inheritance tax (EY p. 653). The practical burden for self-employed is primarily the 15% top income tax rate plus social security.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Foreign Employee Allowance Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Non-Iraqi foreign employees working in Iraq
- **Benefit:** Delegation or overseas allowances up to 25% of basic salary are **non-taxable** for foreign employees (up to 30% for Iraqi employees)
- **General allowances:** Non-cash and cash allowances up to 25% of basic salary are non-taxable for non-Iraqis (vs. 30% for Iraqis)
- **Caveats:** Applies only to residents of Iraq for the personal deductions; the allowance exemptions apply regardless of residency status (EY p. 652)

### Kurdistan Region — Monthly Exemption

- **Benefit:** First IQD 1,000,000 of monthly income (~USD 760/month / ~USD 9,120/year) is exempt from tax in the KRI; combined with flat 5% rate, effective tax burden is very low
- **Applies to:** All employees (Iraqi and non-Iraqi) working in the KRI

## 2026 Changes

- No specific 2026 legislative changes announced in the EY data (as of October 2025)
- The Iraqi tax system (Law No. 113 of 1982) has not been comprehensively reformed; rates and brackets remain as described
- **eVisa Portal:** Iraq launched the eVisa Portal (evisa.iq) for online visa applications; some countries still require Embassy activation (EY p. 655)
- **Work permit fees:** IQD 1,000,000 application fee + IQD 250,000 issuance fee per work permit (EY p. 655)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, Iraqi resident.
> Single personal allowance IQD 2,500,000 applied.
> **1 EUR ≈ IQD 1,740** (approximate; USD/IQD ≈ 1,310; EUR/USD ≈ 1.08).
> Employment and B2B use the same progressive rate structure (no separate regime).
> Social security: employee 5% on IQD 1,750,000/month cap = IQD 87,500/month = IQD 1,050,000/year.

### €30,000 Employment (IQD 52,200,000)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | €30,000 × IQD 1,740 | IQD 52,200,000 |
| Single allowance | | IQD 2,500,000 |
| SS deduction (employee 5% on cap) | 5% × 1,750,000 × 12 | IQD 1,050,000 |
| Total deductions | 2,500,000 + 1,050,000 | IQD 3,550,000 |
| Taxable income | 52,200,000 − 3,550,000 | IQD 48,650,000 |
| Tax on first IQD 250,000 @ 3% | | IQD 7,500 |
| Tax on next IQD 250,000 @ 5% | | IQD 12,500 |
| Tax on next IQD 500,000 @ 10% | | IQD 50,000 |
| Tax on remaining IQD 47,650,000 @ 15% | 15% × 47,650,000 | IQD 7,147,500 |
| **Total income tax** | | **IQD 7,217,500** |
| SS employee (5%, capped) | | IQD 1,050,000 |
| **Total burden** | 7,217,500 + 1,050,000 | **IQD 8,267,500** |
| **Effective rate** | 8,267,500 / 52,200,000 | **15.8%** |

**Working for €60,000 (IQD 104,400,000):**
- Taxable = 104,400,000 − 2,500,000 − 1,050,000 = 100,850,000
- Tax = 7,500 + 12,500 + 50,000 + 15% × 100,100,000 = 70,000 + 15,015,000 = **15,085,000**; with SS: **16,135,000**; effective ~15.4%

**Working for €100,000 (IQD 174,000,000):**
- Taxable = 174,000,000 − 2,500,000 − 1,050,000 = 170,450,000
- Tax = 70,000 + 15% × 169,700,000 = 70,000 + 25,455,000 = **25,525,000**; with SS: **26,575,000**; effective ~15.3%

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/IQD = 1,740 (approximate; verify at cbi.iq)
- Single person, no dependants, Iraqi resident; single deduction IQD 2,500,000
- SS cap: IQD 1,750,000/month; employee 5% × 1,750,000 × 12 = IQD 1,050,000/year
- B2B: no separate regime; same rates; no SS shown (no self-employment SS framework described)
- The IQD brackets (IQD 0–1M) are tiny relative to income — essentially all income is taxed at 15%
- No VAT in Iraq

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **IQD brackets extremely low:** The tax brackets (IQD 0–1,000,000) were set in 1982 and have not been inflation-adjusted. At current exchange rates (~IQD 1,310/USD), IQD 1,000,000 = ~USD 760. Essentially all income is taxed at 15%; the lower rates (3%, 5%, 10%) apply only to the first ~USD 760 of taxable income. Confirm with local counsel whether updated brackets exist.
- [ ] **Self-employment SS:** EY does not describe a specific social security framework for self-employed individuals; only employer/employee rates are given. Self-employed persons' SS obligations are unclear — verify with local counsel.
- [ ] **KRI vs. Iraq rates:** Companies operating in KRI use KRI rules (5% flat, no cap on SS, monthly IQD 1M exemption). Verify whether a single employer operating in both regions applies different rates per location.
- [ ] **Oil and gas sector rates:** Significantly higher employer SS rates (25%–33%) for oil/gas sector. Not modeled above.
- [ ] **Exchange rate volatility:** IQD/USD has been held at approximately 1,310 by the Central Bank of Iraq; verify current official rate at cbi.iq for accurate EUR conversions.
