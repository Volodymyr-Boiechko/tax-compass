## Personal Income Tax

Japan's individual income tax consists of **national income tax** (progressive) plus **local inhabitant tax** (prefectural + municipal, effectively a flat 10%). A **2.1% reconstruction surtax** on national income tax applies 2013–2037. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Residents file between 16 February and 15 March. Three categories of taxpayer: permanent resident (worldwide income), non-permanent resident (Japan-source + income remitted to Japan + capital gains on securities acquired post-1 April 2017), and nonresident (Japan-source only). (EY pp. 730–736)

**Reconstruction Surtax:** 2.1% applied to national income tax amount (2013–2037). Effective top national rate: 45% × 1.021 = **45.945%**. (EY p. 734)

### Local Inhabitant Tax (Flat)

- **Prefectural:** 4% flat rate + JPY1,000/year per capita levy
- **Municipal:** 6% flat rate + JPY3,000/year per capita levy
- **Forest environmental tax:** JPY1,000/year per capita levy
- **Effective local rate:** approximately **10%** on taxable income (plus ~JPY5,000 per capita levies)
- Local inhabitant tax is assessed on the prior year's income and imposed on individuals registered at a municipality as of 1 January (EY p. 734)

## Social Security

Japan's social security consists of health insurance, nursing care insurance (40–64 year olds), welfare pension insurance, unemployment insurance, and workers' accident compensation. Costs are shared equally between employer and employee (except workers' accident compensation — employer only). Rates as of **1 May 2025**: (EY p. 736)

| Program | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Health insurance | **9.91%** | **9.91%** | Bonus ceiling: JPY567,843/payment; monthly ceiling JPY137,749 (employee portion) |
| Nursing care insurance (age 40–64) | **1.59%** | **1.59%** | Bonus ceiling: JPY91,107/payment (employee portion) |
| Welfare pension insurance | **18.3%** | **18.3%** | Bonus ceiling: JPY274,500/month (employee JPY118,950); monthly max JPY118,950 (employee) |
| Unemployment insurance | **0.6%** | **0.9%** | Employee 0.6%; employer 0.9% (general industry) |
| Workers' accident compensation | **0%** | **0.3%** | General office workers; employer only |
| **Total (approx, age 40–64)** | **~15.2%** | **~15.5%** | |
| **Total (under 40 or 65+)** | **~13.6%** | **~14.0%** | No nursing care |

**Notes:**
- All rates split equally (50/50) between employer and employee for health, nursing care, and pension
- Pension bonus ceiling: JPY274,500/month per bonus payment; max employee pension premium JPY118,950/month
- Annual income ceiling for welfare pension (employee): ~JPY1,427,400/year (JPY118,950 × 12 months)
- Health insurance ceiling: JPY137,749/month (employee) → annual ~JPY1,652,988
- Self-employed are covered by National Health Insurance and National Pension (separate programs); rates differ significantly

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Blue Form Return / Regular)

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals, freelancers, sole proprietors, professionals
- **Tax base:** Gross receipts minus allowable business expenses (ordinary and necessary expenses); self-employed may also use "blue form" tax return for additional benefits
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same national progressive rates (5%–45%) + 2.1% reconstruction surtax + 10% local inhabitant tax; total effective top rate ~55.945%
- **Blue form benefits:** Individuals filing a "blue form" tax return can deduct business expenses more liberally; business losses can be carried forward 3 years; additional JPY650,000 special deduction for blue form filers with e-filing (JPY550,000 for paper filing)
- **Social security:** National Health Insurance (NHI) and National Pension (mandatory for 20–60 year olds not covered by employer scheme):
  - National Pension: flat JPY16,980/month (2025) = JPY203,760/year
  - National Health Insurance: income-based; varies by municipality; approximately 8–12% of taxable income + per capita levy; max around JPY870,000/year
- **VAT (Consumption Tax) obligations:** Registration required if taxable sales > JPY10,000,000 in the base period (typically 2 years prior); standard rate 10% (8% for food)
- **Key limits:** Blue form filing requires advance application to tax office; annual return by 15 March

### Option B — No separate simplified/presumptive regime

Japan has no simplified presumptive taxation regime for self-employed professionals comparable to India's Section 44ADA. All self-employed compute actual net profits with deductions.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Non-Permanent Resident Status

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals who have not had a domicile or residence in Japan for five or more years within the past 10 years (EY p. 730)
- **Benefit:** Taxed on Japan-source income **plus** non-Japan-source income paid in Japan or remitted to Japan. Foreign-source income **not** remitted to Japan is **exempt** from Japanese tax. (EY p. 731)
- **Duration:** Until the individual qualifies as a "permanent resident" (5+ years in Japan within past 10 years)
- **Key limitation:** Capital gains from securities acquired on/after 1 April 2017 during the period of non-permanent residency are fully taxable even if proceeds are not remitted to Japan (EY p. 731)
- **Planning note:** Non-permanent residents can legally receive foreign income in overseas accounts without Japan tax, making Japan relatively attractive for the first 5 years for high-earning expatriates with significant foreign assets

### Highly Skilled Professional (HSP) / J-SKIP

- **Eligibility:** Points-based system (academic background, work experience, annual income); 70+ points → preferential treatment; 80+ points → accelerated permanent residency
- **J-SKIP:** Available for researchers/ICT transferees/engineers with annual income ≥ JPY20M and doctorate/master's/MBA or 10 years experience; Business Managers: JPY40M + 5 years experience
- **Benefits:** Preferential immigration treatment; accelerated path to permanent residency (can apply for PR after 1 year as J-SKIP holder); two domestic workers permitted (EY pp. 739–740)
- **J-FIND:** For job-seekers from top-100 universities; within 5 years of graduation; JPY200,000+ bank balance (EY p. 740)

## 2026 Changes

- **Social security rates updated as of 1 May 2025:** Health insurance 9.91%/9.91%; nursing care 1.59%/1.59%; welfare pension 18.3%/18.3%; unemployment 0.6%/0.9%. (EY p. 736)
- **Business Manager visa — additional requirements from October 2025:** JPY30M total capital/investment; at least one full-time employee; Japanese language proficiency; education and professional experience criteria. (EY p. 739)
- **JPETS (Japan Pre-Entry Tuberculosis Screening):** Mandatory TB screening for nationals of China, Indonesia, Myanmar, Nepal, Philippines, Vietnam applying for mid- to long-term residence visas. Nepal/Philippines: mandatory from 23 June 2025; Vietnam: from 1 September 2025. (EY pp. 743–744)
- **No structural income tax rate changes for 2026** announced as of EY data date; reconstruction surtax (2.1%) continues through 2037

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Non-permanent resident planning:** Non-permanent residents (first 5 years in Japan) can earn foreign-source income in overseas accounts free of Japanese tax. This materially changes the effective rate for high-earning expatriates with significant foreign investments. Should be modeled separately.
- [ ] **NHI rate variation by municipality:** National Health Insurance rates vary significantly by municipality. The 10% approximation above is a rough average; actual rates in Tokyo, Osaka, etc. may differ. Verify with local accountant.
- [ ] **Blue form vs white form:** Blue form filers can deduct business expenses more liberally and carry forward losses 3 years. The effective rate above assumes standard deductions; with aggressive business expense claims, taxable income could be substantially lower.
- [ ] **Local inhabitant tax timing:** Local inhabitant tax is assessed on prior-year income and collected in the following year. New arrivals in Year 1 owe no local inhabitant tax; Year 2 onwards it applies. This creates a temporary cash-flow advantage in Year 1.
- [ ] **Pension deductibility:** National Pension contributions (JPY203,760/year) and the employee's share of welfare pension contributions are fully deductible from taxable income for national income tax purposes. Not modeled in the effective rate table above; would reduce the income tax slightly.
- [ ] **30% cost assumption for B2B:** The effective rate for self-employed is highly sensitive to the net profit margin. At 30% costs, net profit is 70% of gross. At 50% costs, net profit is 50%. IT freelancers may have very low actual costs (high net profit margin), making the effective rate approach the employment scenario.
- [ ] **Consumption tax (VAT):** Japanese consumption tax is 10% (8% for food). Registration mandatory for annual taxable sales > JPY10M. B2B clients can recover consumption tax; it is compliance-neutral for B2B but adds administrative burden.
