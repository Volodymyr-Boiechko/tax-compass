## Personal Income Tax

Mongolia levies personal income tax (PITL) on residents' worldwide income. Nonresidents are subject to 20% tax on income earned in or sourced from Mongolia on a gross basis. Tax year = calendar year. Employers withhold income tax quarterly (provisional) and employers file annual returns by 10 February of the following year. Individual annual returns due by 15 February. (EY pp. 1026–1030)

**Residency:** 183+ days in any 12-month period; OR Mongolian-source income > 50% of worldwide income (first criterion checked first). (EY p. 1026)

### Employment income — progressive tax rates

| Annual income (MNT) | Tax on lower amount (MNT) | Marginal rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 120,000,000 | 0 | **10%** |
| 120,000,001 – 180,000,000 | 12,000,000 | **15%** |
| Above 180,000,000 | 21,000,000 | **20%** |

Maximum marginal rate: **20%** (EY p. 1027)

**Employment income annual tax credits (offset against income tax):** (EY pp. 1028–1029)

| Annual employment income (MNT) | Annual tax credit (MNT) |
|---|---|
| 0 – 6,000,000 | 240,000 |
| 6,000,001 – 12,000,000 | 216,000 |
| 12,000,001 – 18,000,000 | 192,000 |
| 18,000,001 – 24,000,000 | 168,000 |
| 24,000,001 – 30,000,000 | 144,000 |
| 30,000,001 – 36,000,000 | 120,000 |
| Above 36,000,000 | 0 |

**Other income tax rates:** (EY p. 1028)
- Dividends, interest, royalties: **10%** withholding (final); no further tax for residents
- Investment income, capital gains: **10%** standard rate
- Self-employment/business income: **10%** on net profit (after allowable deductions)
- Self-employed alternative: elect **1%** gross tax instead of 10% on taxable net income from business operations
- Gambling/lottery: **40%** of gross receipts
- Scientific/literary/artistic/creative income: **5%** flat on gross
- Land transfers: **10%** on gross transfer value (certain family transfers exempt)
- Capital gains on shares/securities/movable property: **10%** on net basis
- Sale of immovable property by resident: **2%** on gross basis
- Nonresidents: **20%** flat on Mongolian-source income (gross basis) (EY p. 1028)

**Deductions:**
- Statutory social and health insurance contributions paid by employees are the **only** tax deductions permitted for employment income in Mongolia
- No deductions allowed for personal expenses
- Self-employment income: all documented business expenses deductible (cost of goods, salaries, depreciation, interest, rent, advertising); personal/undocumented expenses are non-deductible (EY pp. 1027–1028)

**Tax-exempt income (key items):** (EY pp. 1027–1028)
- Pensions, allowances, payments under Mongolian legislation
- Income of individuals with disability
- International organization/foreign government assistance to Mongolia
- Payments/interest/discounts on government of Mongolia/Ulaanbaatar/Development Bank of Mongolia bonds
- Transfer of land certificates between certain family members
- Income from VAT-related incentives
- Salary of foreign diplomat/consulate/UN staff permanently residing in Mongolia assigned to a foreign mission

**Relief for losses:** No measures exist for carryover of tax losses in Mongolia. (EY p. 1029)

**No wealth or net worth tax.** No inheritance or gift taxes. (EY p. 1029)

## Social Security

**Compulsory social and health insurance contributions:** (EY p. 1029)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social & health insurance (combined) | **11.5%** | **12.5%–14.5%** (sector dependent) | Voluntary |
| **Employee contribution cap** | MNT 910,800/month | No cap | N/A |

- Rate for employees: **11.5%** flat; capped at **MNT 910,800/month**
- Rate for employers: **12.5%–14.5%** depending on economic sector; **no cap** for employers
- Coverage: pension, unemployment, health insurance, benefits insurance (short-term disability, maternity), industrial accident/occupational disease insurance
- **Self-employed individuals:** may register as **voluntary** social and health insurance payers (not mandatory) (EY p. 1029)

**Totalization agreements:** Korea (South) and the Russian Federation (EY p. 1030)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Business Income (10% on net profit)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual undertaking business activities independently (manufacturing, trade, rental, services including professional services) without establishing a legal entity
- **Tax base:** Net income after all allowable business expenses (cost of goods sold, salaries, depreciation, interest, rent, advertising, etc.)
- **Rate / mechanism:** **10%** on net taxable profit
- **Social security:** Voluntary; may register as voluntary payer
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds MNT 50 million; standard rate 10%
- **Key limits:** No loss carryforward; expenses must be documented and business-related

### Option B — 1% Gross Tax Alternative

- **Who qualifies:** Individual entrepreneurs earning business income who elect this alternative; persons paying 1% gross tax are NOT eligible for the general income tax exemptions listed above
- **Tax base:** Total operational (gross) income from business activities
- **Rate / mechanism:** **1%** flat on gross revenue; replaces the standard 10% net profit tax
- **Social security:** Voluntary
- **Key limits:** Cannot use the 1% rate if: activities do not qualify; individuals paying 1% are excluded from the general tax-exempt income provisions
- **Best for:** Businesses with low margins or difficulties documenting expenses

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Work Visas (C Visa)

- **Work visa (C visa):** Required for all foreign nationals wishing to work in Mongolia; 11 general types + 20 detailed subtypes; applicant may only work in the field specified on the visa type
- **Process:** Obtain C visa from Mongolian embassy/consul before travel; then obtain work permit from General Department of Labor and Welfare Services; obtain long-term residency permit from Immigration Agency of Mongolia
- **Single-entry C visa + temporary work permit** required before entering Mongolia; processing begins inside Mongolia
- Temporary visitors may stay up to 90 days without a work visa (EY pp. 1031–1032)

### Investor Visa (B Visa)

- **B visa:** For foreign investors; three subtypes (B1, B1-1, B2); B1 for foreign investors, B1-1 for dependants of foreign investors, B2 for executive management of foreign-invested entities (EY p. 1031)

### Double Tax Treaties

Mongolia has DTTs with: Austria, Belarus, Belgium, Bulgaria, Canada, China Mainland, Czech Republic, France, Germany, Hungary, India, Indonesia, Italy, Kazakhstan, Korea (North), Korea (South), Kyrgyzstan, Malaysia, Poland, Russian Federation, Singapore, Switzerland, Türkiye, Ukraine, United Kingdom, Vietnam (EY p. 1030)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Mongolia uses MNT (Mongolian Tögrög/Tugrik). **1 EUR ≈ MNT 3,920** (approximate; MNT/USD ≈ 3,593 per EY; EUR/USD ≈ 1.09; verify at mongolbank.mn).
> Employment: progressive 10%/15%/20%; tax credits applied; social insurance 11.5% capped at MNT 910,800/month.
> Self-employed 1% option shown as best-case scenario.

### Employment social security annual cap
- MNT 910,800/month × 12 = MNT 10,929,600/year (approx. MNT 10.93M maximum)

### €30,000 Employment
- MNT equivalent: EUR 30,000 × 3,920 = MNT 117,600,000/year
- PIT: MNT 117,600,000 × 10% = MNT 11,760,000 (entire income within 10% band)
- Tax credit: income = MNT 117.6M > MNT 36M → credit = **MNT 0**
- Net PIT: MNT 11,760,000
- Social insurance (11.5%, capped): MNT 910,800 × 12 = MNT 10,929,600 (capped; actual 11.5% × 117.6M = MNT 13,524,000 > cap)
- Total burden: MNT 11,760,000 + MNT 10,929,600 = MNT 22,689,600
- Effective rate: 22,689,600 / 117,600,000 = **19.3%**

### €60,000 Employment
- MNT: EUR 60,000 × 3,920 = MNT 235,200,000
- PIT:
  - 10% on MNT 120,000,000 = MNT 12,000,000
  - 15% on MNT 60,000,000 = MNT 9,000,000
  - 20% on MNT 55,200,000 = MNT 11,040,000
  - Total PIT: MNT 32,040,000
- Tax credit: 0 (income > MNT 36M)
- Social insurance: capped at MNT 10,929,600
- Total: MNT 42,969,600; Effective rate: 42,969,600 / 235,200,000 = **18.3%**

### €100,000 Employment
- MNT: EUR 100,000 × 3,920 = MNT 392,000,000
- PIT:
  - 10% on MNT 120,000,000 = MNT 12,000,000
  - 15% on MNT 60,000,000 = MNT 9,000,000
  - 20% on MNT 212,000,000 = MNT 42,400,000
  - Total PIT: MNT 63,400,000
- Social insurance: capped at MNT 10,929,600
- Total: MNT 74,329,600; Effective rate: 74,329,600 / 392,000,000 = **19.0%**
