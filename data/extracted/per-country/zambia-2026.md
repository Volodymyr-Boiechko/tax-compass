## Personal Income Tax

> Residents taxed on income derived, or deemed derived, from a source in Zambia. An individual who lives and works in Zambia for more than 183 days in a tax year is considered resident. Tax year is the calendar year (31 December). Annual returns due by 21 June following the year-end. Administered by ZRA (Zambia Revenue Authority). New rates effective from 1 January 2026.

### Individual Rates (effective 1 January 2026)

| Annual taxable income — Exceeding (ZMW) | Not exceeding (ZMW) | Tax rate % |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 61,200 | 0 |
| 61,200 | 85,200 | 20 |
| 85,200 | 110,400 | 30 |
| 110,400 | — | 37 |

**Personal exemption:** ZMW 61,200 (built into zero-rate band)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal exemption: first ZMW 61,200 of annual income exempt (zero-rate band)
- Business deductions: all expenses (except capital in nature) incurred in earning taxable income are deductible
- Capital expenditure: not deductible (capital allowances apply)
- Losses: carry forward up to 5 years from a similar source (mining and electricity: 10 years; rail: 12 years from 2026)

**Property Transfer Tax (PTT):**
- PTT is levied at 8% on the realized value of property before transfer (not income tax — replaces CGT)
- PTT Act of 2025 applies
- Mineral processing licence transfer: 10%
- Mining right for exploration licence: 8%
- Mining right for mining licence: 8%
- Transfer of shares in Zambia-incorporated companies: 8%
- Transfer of intellectual property (patents, trademarks, copyrights, industrial designs): 8%

**Notes:**
- Employment income: all salaries and benefits for work performed in Zambia, regardless of where paid, subject to PAYE; employer withholds and remits to ZRA by 10th of each month
- Self-employment / business income: estimated quarterly installments (10 April, 10 July, 10 October, 10 January); individuals only required to file returns if they have income sources other than employment
- Partners taxed individually on their respective shares of partnership income
- Directors' fees from resident companies: included in taxable income
- Dividends (resident individuals): 15% final WHT
- Dividends to nonresidents: 20% final WHT
- Interest (resident individuals and nonresidents on deposit accounts): 0% WHT
- Royalties to nonresidents: 20% WHT
- Management and consultancy fees (resident): 15% WHT; nonresidents: 20% WHT
- No estate or gift tax in Zambia (EY p.1724)

## Social Security

> All employees must contribute 5% of gross salary to NAPSA (National Pensions Scheme Authority). Employers match at 5%. (EY p.1724)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| NAPSA (National Pensions Scheme Authority) | 5% of gross salary | 5% of gross salary | — |
| **Total** | **5%** | **5%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** None specified in EY source
**Notes:**
- Employer and employee each contribute 5% (total 10% of gross salary)
- Self-employed social security treatment not specified in EY source

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader / Self-Employed (Graduated Rates)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual earning income from a Zambian source or deemed Zambian source through self-employment or business activities
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross income less deductible non-capital expenses incurred in earning taxable income)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates (0–37%) as employment income; quarterly installment payments; annual return by 21 June
- **Social security:** NAPSA — not specified for self-employed; assumed voluntary
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above ZMW 800,000 annual turnover (standard rate 16%)
- **Key limits:** Losses carry forward 5 years; capital gains exempt (PTT applies on property transfers instead)

### Option B — Partnership
- **Who qualifies:** Partners in business partnerships
- **Tax base:** Partners taxed individually on their share of partnership income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive individual rates
- **Social security:** As above
- **VAT obligations:** Partnership-level VAT registration may apply
- **Key limits:** Same as sole trader

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

> No specific preferential tax regime for new tax residents identified in EY source. Zambia uses source-based taxation — residents taxed only on Zambia-source income.

### Source-Based Taxation
- **Eligibility:** All residents
- **Benefit:** Only income from Zambian sources taxable; foreign-source income not taxable in Zambia
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Residency threshold: > 183 days in tax year

## 2026 Changes

- New individual income tax rates effective 1 January 2026: 0% on first ZMW 61,200; 20% on ZMW 61,200–85,200; 30% on ZMW 85,200–110,400; 37% above ZMW 110,400 (EY p.1724)
- Property Transfer Tax Act 2025 (PTT Act): replaced CGT for property transfers at 8% of realized value effective from 2025 (EY p.1724)
- Loss carry-forward for rail extended to 12 years effective 2026; mining/electricity remains 10 years (EY p.1724)

**Working (income tax, 2026 rates):**
- Bands: 0% on 61,200; 20% on 24,000 (61,200–85,200) = 4,800; 30% on 25,200 (85,200–110,400) = 7,560; 37% above 110,400
- Cumulative through 110,400: 0 + 4,800 + 7,560 = **12,360**
- ZMW 840,000: tax = 12,360 + 37% × (840,000 − 110,400) = 12,360 + 37% × 729,600 = 12,360 + 269,952 = **282,312** (~33.6%)
- ZMW 1,680,000: tax = 12,360 + 37% × (1,680,000 − 110,400) = 12,360 + 37% × 1,569,600 = 12,360 + 580,752 = **593,112** (~35.3%)
- ZMW 2,800,000: tax = 12,360 + 37% × (2,800,000 − 110,400) = 12,360 + 37% × 2,689,600 = 12,360 + 995,152 = **1,007,512** (~36.0%)

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 28 ZMW (approximate; ZMW subject to managed float)
- Single, no dependants; only zero-rate band applied
- NAPSA 5% on full gross income (employment); nil for B2B (self-employed treatment not confirmed)
