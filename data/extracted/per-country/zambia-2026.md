## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PIT system. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Employment income withheld at source by employers. Individual returns filed by 21 June of the following year. Residents taxed on Zambia-source income; non-residents taxed only on Zambia-source income. Administered by the Zambia Revenue Authority (ZRA).

**Residency:** Lives and works in Zambia for more than 183 days in a tax year (EY p. 1723).

### Tax Rates — effective from 1 January 2026 (EY p. 1724)

| Annual taxable income (ZMW) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 61,200 | 0% |
| 61,201 – 85,200 | 20% |
| 85,201 – 110,400 | 30% |
| Above 110,400 | 37% |

**Monthly equivalents (÷12):**

| Monthly taxable income (ZMW) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 5,100 | 0% |
| 5,101 – 7,100 | 20% |
| 7,101 – 9,200 | 30% |
| Above 9,200 | 37% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal exemption: ZMW 61,200/year (= first bracket at 0% — effectively a personal allowance)
- Business deductions for self-employed: all expenses (except capital expenses) incurred in earning taxable income are deductible (EY p. 1724)

**Notes:**
- Non-residents: subject to 20% withholding tax on management and consulting fees; 20% withholding on payments to non-resident contractors (EY p. 1723)
- Resident individuals: 15% withholding on management and consultancy fees (EY p. 1723)
- Dividends: 15% withholding (final tax for resident individual shareholders); 20% for non-residents (EY p. 1723)
- Interest on deposit accounts: 0% for both residents and non-resident individuals (EY p. 1724)
- Royalties to non-residents: 20% withholding
- Capital gains: no capital gains tax in Zambia; however, Property Transfer Tax (PTT) at 8% on realized value applies to real property, certain mining rights, and intellectual property transfers; 10% for mineral processing license transfers (EY p. 1724)
- No estate or gift tax in Zambia (EY p. 1724)
- Loss carry-forward: up to 5 years (10 years for mining and electricity; 12 years for rail from 2026) (EY p. 1724)

## Social Security

National Pension Scheme Authority (NAPSA) contributions (EY p. 1724):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| NAPSA (pension) | **5%** | **5%** | On gross salary |
| **Total** | **5%** | **5%** | |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling stated in EY for NAPSA; 5% on gross salary

**Notes:**
- NAPSA contributions are employee + employer 5% each; employer also contributes 5% matching
- No separate health insurance or unemployment insurance contributions for employees beyond NAPSA
- Workers' Compensation Fund (WCF): employer-only contribution; rate not specified in EY personal tax chapter

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — General Self-Assessment (Business Income)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual earning self-employment or business income from a Zambia source
- **Tax base:** Gross business income less all allowable expenses incurred in earning that income (except capital expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates 0%–37% on net income (same as employment schedule); annual return filed by 21 June
- **Social security:** NAPSA: if the self-employed individual employs staff, NAPSA mandatory; sole traders without employees may not be compulsorily enrolled
- **VAT obligations:** Register for VAT if annual turnover exceeds ZMW 800,000; standard rate 16%
- **Key limits:** Loss carry-forward up to 5 years; no loss carrybacks

### Option B — Withholding on Services (Contractors)

- **Who qualifies:** Resident individuals providing management or consultancy services
- **Rate / mechanism:** 15% withholding on management and consultancy fees (final or advance depending on context); included in annual return and credited against PIT liability
- **Key limits:** Non-resident contractors: 20% final withholding; no annual return required for non-residents

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No General Newcomer Exemption

Zambia does not offer a territorial or newcomer exemption regime. All residents pay progressive PIT on Zambia-source income. There is no general worldwide income taxation — Zambia taxes only Zambia-source income for all individuals (residents and non-residents) (EY p. 1723).

### Non-Resident Employment

Non-residents working in Zambia: taxed on Zambia-source employment income at the same progressive rates as residents. Management and consulting fees paid to non-residents: 20% final withholding.

## 2026 Changes

- **Updated tax brackets effective 1 January 2026** (EY p. 1724): New bracket structure with 0% up to ZMW 61,200; 20% on next ZMW 24,000 (61,200–85,200); 30% on next ZMW 25,200 (85,200–110,400); 37% above ZMW 110,400
- **Rail sector loss carry-forward extended to 12 years** (effective 2026 — EY p. 1724)
- **Property Transfer Tax Act 2025:** Enacted; PTT at 8% on realized value of real property transfers; replacing prior property transfer arrangements
- No announced changes to NAPSA rates or VAT rate
