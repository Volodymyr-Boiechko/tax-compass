## Personal Income Tax

Territorial system: residents and nonresidents taxed only on income earned in Costa Rica (territorial principle). Foreign-source income is NOT taxed. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). (EY p.298)

**Residency:** Individual considered resident if they have lived in Costa Rica for more than six consecutive months (183 days) during a tax year. Tax authorities may apply a shorter term for employed individuals. (EY p.298)

**Employment income:** Monthly income in excess of CRC 929,000 is taxable (salary, pensions, bonuses, premiums, commissions, allowances). Payments to board members, other executives and counselors not in the payroll subject to 15% withholding. (EY p.298)

**Self-employment and business income:** Annual income in excess of CRC 5,687,000 is taxable. (EY p.298)

**Progressive PIT brackets — Employment (monthly, 2025)** (EY p.299)

| Monthly taxable income (CRC) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 918,000 | 0% |
| 918,001 – 1,347,000 | 10% |
| 1,347,001 – 2,364,000 | 15% |
| 2,364,001 – 4,727,000 | 20% |
| Above 4,727,000 | 25% |

**Progressive PIT brackets — Self-employment/Business (monthly, from 1 January 2025)** (EY p.299)

| Monthly taxable income (CRC) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 4,094,000 | 0% |
| 4,094,001 – 6,115,000 | 10% |
| 6,115,001 – 10,200,000 | 15% |
| 10,200,001 – 20,442,000 | 20% |
| Above 20,442,000 | 25% |

**Other income types:**
- Investment income (dividends from corporations to individuals/entities): 15% withholding tax. (EY p.298)
- Dividend distributions to Costa Rican domiciled entities carrying on economic activity: exempt. (EY p.298)
- Royalties, technical advice, franchise payments: 25% withholding. (EY p.299)
- Directors' fees (resident and nonresident): 15% withholding. (EY p.299)
- Capital gains on sale of tangible or intangible assets: 15% capital gains tax. (EY p.299)
  - First sale of goods/rights acquired before 1 July 2019: 2.25% on gross purchase price. (EY p.299)
  - Capital gains may be subject to corporate income tax if the sale is ordinary business, the goods generate taxable income, or the asset is depreciable. (EY p.299)
- Fringe benefits and salary in kind: 15% withholding. (EY p.299)
- Nonresidents: 10% withholding on salaries, remuneration, pensions, commissions, directors' fees; 25% on professional services. (EY p.299)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Annual tax credits: CRC 1,720 per dependent child; CRC 2,600 for a spouse (taken by either spouse, not both). (EY p.299)
- Business deductions: All costs and expenses necessary to generate taxable income and protect investments are deductible for self-employment and business income. (EY p.299)
- Fixed deduction for employment income: CRC 500 deductible from employment income (from standard deductible expenses). (EY p.314 — note: XCG 500 per Curaçao page; for Costa Rica the deductible is noted as the standard deduction from employment listed in Rates section)

**Notes:**
- No estate or gift taxes in Costa Rica. (EY p.300)
- Self-employed may carry losses forward for three years. (EY p.300)
- Tax year for self-employed: 1 January – 31 December; returns and payment due by 15 March. (EY p.300)
- Quarterly advance payments required for self-employed and businesses. (EY p.300)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Cedular Tax)
- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals and businesses deriving income from Costa Rican sources.
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus allowable business expenses (all costs necessary to generate taxable income). Annual income above CRC 5,687,000 is taxable. (EY p.298)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive monthly rates 0%–25% on self-employment/business income (see brackets above). Separate cedular scale from employment income. (EY p.299)
- **Social security:** Self-employed SS rates not specified in EY source.
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT applies (rate not specified in this EY section).
- **Key limits:** Loss carryforward allowed for 3 years. Quarterly advance payments required. (EY p.300)

### Option B — Digital Nomad / Nonresident Status
- **Who qualifies:** Nonresident individuals providing services remotely to foreign clients; must qualify under Law No. 10008 (Digital Nomads Law, 8 July 2022). Must be nonresident, provide services to nonresident individuals/entities, receive payment from abroad. (EY p.301)
- **Tax base:** Total exemption from Costa Rican income tax.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Full income tax exemption while under nonresident "stay" migratory status.
- **Social security:** Not specified under this regime.
- **VAT obligations:** Not specified (likely exempt as income sourced from abroad).
- **Key limits:** Status terminates if individual works for local Costa Rican companies/employers during the application/stay period. Tax benefits revoked upon termination of beneficiary status. (EY p.301)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Digital Nomad Visa (Law No. 10008 — "Law to Attract International Workers and Services Providers")
- **Eligibility:** Nonresident individual who provides services remotely; services must be provided to nonresident individuals/entities; payment received from abroad.
- **Benefit:** Full income tax exemption (total exemption for tax purposes under nonresident "stay" subcategory).
- **Duration:** Initial permit: 6 months; extendable by another 6 months.
- **Application deadline:** Must apply to immigration authorities; return ticket required upon entry.
- **Caveats:** Cannot work for or be employed by local Costa Rican company or employer during stay; tax benefits revoked immediately upon termination of beneficiary status. (EY p.301)
