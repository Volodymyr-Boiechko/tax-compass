## Personal Income Tax

Territorial system — only income derived within Costa Rica and from Costa Rican sources is subject to income tax. Foreign-source income is NOT taxed. Residency: individuals living in Costa Rica for more than six consecutive months (183 days) during a tax year are considered resident. Tax authority may apply a shorter term for employed individuals. (EY pp.298–299)

**Tax year:** 1 October to 30 September (for businesses); 1 January to 31 December for self-employed with a trade/business.

**Employment income — monthly progressive rates (employer withholds):** (EY p.299)

| Monthly taxable income — Exceeding (CRC) | Not exceeding (CRC) | Tax rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 918,000 | 0% |
| 918,000 | 1,347,000 | 10% |
| 1,347,000 | 2,364,000 | 15% |
| 2,364,000 | 4,727,000 | 20% |
| 4,727,000 | — | 25% |

**Self-employment and business income — monthly progressive rates (effective 1 January 2025):** (EY p.299)

| Monthly taxable income — Exceeding (CRC) | Not exceeding (CRC) | Tax rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 4,094,000 | 0% |
| 4,094,000 | 6,115,000 | 10% |
| 6,115,000 | 10,200,000 | 15% |
| 10,200,000 | 20,442,000 | 20% |
| 20,442,000 | — | 25% |

**Taxable employment income threshold:** Monthly income in excess of CRC 929,000 is taxable (amounts below this are exempt). Annual income for self-employment: in excess of CRC 5,687,000 is taxable. (EY p.298)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Annual tax credits: CRC 1,720 per dependent child; CRC 2,600 for a spouse (may be taken by either spouse but not both)
- Business deductions: all costs and expenses necessary to generate taxable income are deductible for self-employment
- Fringe benefits and salary in kind: subject to 15% withholding tax

**Notes:**
- Territorial principle: foreign-source income completely exempt from Costa Rican income tax
- Capital gains: 15% flat tax on sale of tangible or intangible assets (15% CGT); for assets acquired before 1 July 2019, taxpayers may apply 2.25% on gross purchase price
- If the asset sale is ordinary business activity, regular income tax rates apply
- Dividends from Costa Rican corporations: 15% withholding tax (final) for residents and nonresidents
- Technical advice, franchises, royalties to nonresidents: 25% withholding
- Directors' fees: 15% withholding (residents and nonresidents)
- Nonresidents: 10% flat withholding on salaries, commissions; 25% on professional services
- Relief for losses: self-employed may carry forward losses for 3 years

## Social Security (CCSS — Caja Costarricense de Seguro Social)

Levied on salaries; computed on gross compensation with no deductions allowed. (EY p.300)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security (CCSS total) | 10.67% (from Jan 2026) | 26.83% (from Jan 2026) | N/A |
| **Total** | **10.67%** | **26.83%** | — |

**Transition rates (until December 2025):** Employee 10.67%, Employer 26.67%.  
**From 1 January 2026:** Employers contribute 26.83%; employees contribute 10.83%. (EY p.300)

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling specified — contributions computed on full gross compensation.

**Notes:**
- No deductions allowed from gross compensation for SS calculation
- The total employer + employee SS burden is approximately 37.5% of gross salary — one of the highest in Central America
- Self-employed and business owners must register with CCSS separately; rates vary

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Impuesto sobre la Renta Cedular)

- **Who qualifies:** All individuals deriving income from self-employment, trade, or business activity in Costa Rica.
- **Tax base:** Monthly net income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses). Annual income above CRC 5,687,000 is taxable.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Monthly progressive rates (effective 1 January 2025) — 0% to 25% on monthly net income (as shown in rate table above). Returns filed annually (1 January to 31 December fiscal year); advance quarterly payments required.
- **Social security:** Self-employed register with CCSS; contribution rates differ from employment regime (EY does not specify exact rate for self-employed in this section — verify with CCSS).
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IVA (Costa Rica VAT) at 13% on most services and goods.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; all necessary expenses deductible; losses carry forward 3 years.

### Option B — Digital Nomads Law (Law 9738 / Law 10008)

- **Who qualifies:** Nonresident individuals who provide services remotely from Costa Rica for foreign individuals or entities and receive payment from abroad.
- **Tax base:** Foreign-source income (exempt under territorial principle).
- **Rate / mechanism:** **Full income tax exemption** — income earned abroad by digital nomads is not subject to Costa Rican income tax (applies under the territorial principle, reinforced by Law 10008 of 8 July 2022).
- **Social security:** Not subject to mandatory CCSS while under digital nomad status (nonresident subcategory of "stay").
- **VAT obligations:** Not applicable for income from foreign sources.
- **Duration:** Visa valid for up to 1 year; renewable.
- **Conditions:**
  - Must be a nonresident providing services remotely for foreign nonresident individuals/entities
  - Services rendered for a non-Costa Rican individual or entity
  - Payment/remuneration received from abroad
- **Tax exemption revocation:** When the Immigration and Foreign Affairs Department terminates the digital nomad status, all tax benefits are immediately revoked. (EY pp.301–302)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Digital Nomads Law (Law 10008 — "Law to Attract International Workers and Services Providers")

- **Eligibility:** Nonresident individuals providing services remotely from Costa Rica for foreign entities/individuals. Published 8 July 2022.
- **Benefit:** Complete income tax exemption (migratory "stay" subcategory of nonresident). No requirement to pay CCSS contributions.
- **Duration:** Visa granted for up to 1 year; renewable for equivalent period.
- **Conditions:** Must prove nonresident status; services provided for foreign (non-Costa Rican) entity/person; payment received from abroad.
- **Application deadline:** Apply at DGME (Dirección General de Migración y Extranjería).
- **Caveats:** If digital nomad status is terminated by authorities, all tax benefits end immediately. Income from Costa Rican sources would be taxable. (EY pp.301–302)

### Territorial Taxation (General)
- **Eligibility:** All residents and nonresidents.
- **Benefit:** Foreign-source income permanently exempt from Costa Rican income tax.
- **Duration:** Permanent feature of the tax system.

## 2026 Changes

- **Social security rate increase (1 January 2026):** Employer contribution increases from 26.67% to 26.83%; employee contribution increases from 10.67% to 10.83%. (EY p.300)
- **Self-employment tax brackets (effective 1 January 2025):** New monthly progressive brackets for self-employment/business income introduced, with a much higher zero-rate threshold (CRC 4,094,000/month vs. employment CRC 918,000/month). This is already in effect for 2025 and continues in 2026.
- **Digital Nomads Law (Law 10008):** Fully operative since July 2022; continues to provide full income tax exemption for qualifying nonresident remote workers.
- No major structural income tax changes announced for 2026 beyond the SS rate adjustment.
