## Personal Income Tax

> Progressive income tax administered by GRA (Ghana Revenue Authority). Tax year is the calendar year. Resident individuals taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents taxed at a flat 25%. PAYE system for employees; self-employed pay quarterly installments and file annual return within four months of year-end.

### Resident Individual Rates (effective for the tax year)

| Chargeable income (GHS) | Tax rate % | Tax due (GHS) | Cumulative tax due (GHS) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 5,880 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| Next 1,320 | 5 | 66 | 66 |
| Next 1,560 | 10 | 156 | 222 |
| Next 38,000 | 17.5 | 6,650 | 6,872 |
| Next 192,000 | 25 | 48,000 | 54,872 |
| Next 366,240 | 30 | 109,872 | 164,744 |
| Exceeding 600,000 | 35 | — | — |

**Nonresidents:** flat 25% on all chargeable income (EY p.497)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal relief: KES 28,800 per year (note: GHS-denominated — see EY p.788 context; relief listed in personal reliefs section at GHS amounts below)
- Personal relief: GHS 28,800 per year (EY p.788 — Kenya context; Ghana's own reliefs per EY p.497):
  - Children's education allowance: GHS 600 per child/ward, up to 3 children
  - Dependent elderly relative allowance: GHS 1,000 per dependent, up to 2 dependants
  - Marriage/responsibility relief: GHS 1,200
  - Disability allowance: 25% of assessable income from business or employment
  - Old age relief: up to GHS 1,500 per year
  - Professional/vocational training allowance: up to GHS 2,000
- Contributions to mandatory pension schemes (National Pensions Act, Act 766) qualify as tax reliefs
- Voluntary pension contributions up to 16.5% of employee's salary may be exempt if conditions met
- Mortgage interest on one residential property deductible (lifetime limit)
- Expenses wholly, exclusively and necessarily incurred in production of income from business/investments deductible

**Notes:**
- Dividends taxed at 8% (withheld at source; applies to resident and nonresident individuals) — final tax
- Interest paid by resident financial institutions or government: exempt from tax for individuals
- Capital gains: included in business or investment income and taxed at individual's marginal/graduated rate (highest 35% for residents; 25% for nonresidents); individuals may elect 25% flat rate on gains instead
- Rental income (residential rental income tax): 7.5% of gross rental receipts (effective 1 January 2024), for income between KES 288,000–15,000,000 per year; or election to be taxed at graduated rates
- Redundancy pay excluded from taxable income
- Noncash benefits up to GHS 60,000 per year exempt (effective 27 December 2024)

## Social Security

> Ghana imposes a mandatory social security contribution of 18.5% total. Employer contributes 13%; employee contributes 5.5%. Of the total 18.5%: employer remits 13.5% to SSNIT and 5% to approved occupational pension trustees (EY p.498).

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| SSNIT / mandatory pension (Tier I + II) | 5.5% | 13% | Voluntary (may contribute) |
| **Total** | **5.5%** | **13%** | — |

**Income ceiling:** None specified in EY source
**Notes:**
- Employer remits 13.5% out of the 18.5% to SSNIT; remaining 5% to approved trustees for occupational pension (Tier II)
- Provident fund contributions up to 16.5% exempt from tax if withdrawal conditions satisfied
- Self-employed individuals who qualify may contribute toward mandatory schemes
- Informal sector persons not covered by mandatory schemes: may deduct 35% of declared income as deductible income for income tax purposes
- Non-Ghanaian citizens emigrating permanently: entitled to lump-sum benefit

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Self-Employed (Graduated Rates)
- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals, traders, professionals, sole proprietors, partners
- **Tax base:** Net business profits (net accounting profit plus non-deductible expenses, less depreciation allowances and personal reliefs)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same graduated rate scale as employment income (0–35%)
- **Social security:** Voluntary SSNIT contributions allowed; informal sector: 35% deduction on declared income
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above GHS 200,000 annual turnover (standard rate 15% + NHIL 2.5% + GETFund 2.5% = effective 20%)
- **Key limits:** Losses carry forward 5 years (from 2023 year of assessment); losses from periods prior to 2023: 5 years priority sectors, 3 years other

### Option B — Small Business (Installment/Turnover/Cash Basis)
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with business income only from Ghana sources
- **Tax base:** Modified basis — installment, turnover or cash basis available
- **Rate / mechanism:** Graduated rates applied to determined income; quarterly installment payments
- **Social security:** As above
- **VAT obligations:** As above
- **Key limits:** Must use 31 December year-end; tax payable quarterly on 20th day of 4th, 6th, 9th, and 12th months

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

> No specific preferential regime for new tax residents identified in EY source.

### Expatriate Employee Exemption (Foreign Employer)
- **Eligibility:** Resident individual employed by a nonresident employer and performing employment outside Ghana
- **Benefit:** Employment income from services performed in a foreign country is exempt from Ghana income tax
- **Duration:** Ongoing, per year of assessment
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Only applies when employer is nonresident OR resident employer with employee present in foreign country for 183+ continuous days

## 2026 Changes

- Noncash benefits exemption raised to GHS 60,000 per year (effective 27 December 2024) (EY p.785)
- First KES 10,000 per day paid as allowance on official duty outside workplace deemed reimbursement and not taxable (effective 1 July 2025) (EY p.784-785)
- Pension benefit payments on attainment of retirement age from registered retirement schemes now exempt (effective 27 December 2024) (EY p.785)
- Digital Service Tax replaced by Significant Economic Presence Tax (SEPT) effective 27 December 2024 (EY p.785)
- Affordable Housing Levy turnover tax rate decreased to 1.5% (from 3%) effective March 2024 (EY p.787)
- Capital Gains Tax rate: 15% (from 1 January 2023) (EY p.787)
- 2025 Finance Act: Digital Asset Tax (3% on crypto income) repealed; replaced by 10% excise duty on virtual asset transaction fees (effective 1 July 2025) (EY p.787)

- GHS 990,000: 35% band. Tax = 164,744 + 35% × (990,000 − 605,000) = 164,744 + 134,750 = **299,494 GHS** (~30.3%)
- GHS 1,650,000: Tax = 164,744 + 35% × (1,650,000 − 605,000) = 164,744 + 365,750 = **530,494 GHS** (~32.2%)

*(Table above uses corrected figures)*

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 16.5 GHS (approximate; GHS volatile)
- Single, no dependants, no personal reliefs other than zero band
