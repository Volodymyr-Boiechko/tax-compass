## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for residents on worldwide income (with exemption for employment exercised abroad in certain cases); nonresidents on Ghana-source income only at flat 25%. Tax year = calendar year. Returns filed within four months after tax year end. Employment income subject to PAYE. (EY p.494, p.498)

**Residency:** Resident if any of: citizen of Ghana other than a citizen with a permanent home outside Ghana living there for the whole of the year; present in Ghana ≥183 days in a 12-month period beginning or ending in that year; government employee/official posted abroad; citizen with permanent home in Ghana temporarily absent for ≤365 continuous days. (EY p.494)

**Employment income subject to tax:** Salary, wages, leave pay, fees, commissions, gratuities, overtime, bonuses, personal allowances (cost-of-living, subsistence, rent, entertainment, travel), discharge/reimbursement of expenses, payments for agreeing to employment conditions, gifts, retirement contributions/payments per the National Pensions Act. (EY p.495)

**Progressive PIT brackets — Resident (annual, 2025)** (EY p.497)

| Chargeable income (GHS) | Tax rate | Tax due (GHS) | Cumulative (GHS) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 5,880 | 0% | 0 | 0 |
| Next 1,320 | 5% | 66 | 66 |
| Next 1,560 | 10% | 156 | 222 |
| Next 38,000 | 17.5% | 6,650 | 6,872 |
| Next 192,000 | 25% | 48,000 | 54,872 |
| Next 366,240 | 30% | 109,872 | 164,744 |
| Exceeding 605,000 | 35% | — | — |

**Nonresidents:** Flat 25% on all Ghana-source income. Management and technical service fees: 20% final withholding. (EY p.497)

**Other income types:**
- Dividends: 8% withholding tax (final) on dividends paid to residents and nonresidents by resident companies. (EY p.496)
- Interest from resident financial institutions or government: exempt from income tax. (EY p.496)
- Capital gains: included in business income or investment income and taxed at individual's marginal/graduated rate (max 35% for residents, 25% for nonresidents). Individuals may elect to pay 25% flat on capital gains instead of including in income. Separate capital gains tax has been abolished. (EY p.496–497)
- Management/technical service fees (nonresidents): 20% final withholding. (EY p.497)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.497)

| Type of allowance | Amount |
|---|---|
| Children's education allowance | GHS 600 per child/ward, max 3 children |
| Dependent elderly relative allowance | GHS 1,000 per dependent, max 2 |
| Marriage/responsibility relief | GHS 1,200 |
| Disability allowance | 25% of assessable income from business/employment |
| Old age relief | Up to GHS 1,500 per year |
| Professional/vocational training allowance | Up to GHS 2,000 |

- Mandatory pension contributions: qualify as tax reliefs under the National Pensions Act.
- Voluntary pension contributions by employee: exempt up to 16.5% of salary. (EY p.497)
- Business deductions: expenses wholly, exclusively and necessarily incurred in the production of income. Mortgage interest for one residential premises deductible. (EY p.497)

**Notes:**
- No net worth tax. (EY p.498)
- Estate and gift taxes apply; gifts taxed as part of employment, business or investment income. (EY p.498)
- Business losses: carried forward for 5 years (from 2023 year of assessment; losses from pre-2023 periods: 5 years for priority sector entities, 3 years for others). (EY p.498)
- Non-Ghanaian citizens emigrating permanently entitled to lump-sum benefit from social security. (EY p.498)
- Tax year = calendar year; PAYE monthly; individual income tax return within 4 months after year end. (EY p.498)

## Social Security

Ghana imposes a mandatory social security contribution of 18.5% total. (EY p.498)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security and National Insurance Trust (SSNIT) | 5.5% | 13% (remits 13.5% to SSNIT) | varies |
| Occupational pension scheme (5% of 18.5%) | — | — | — |
| **Total mandatory** | **5.5%** | **13%** | — |

**Breakdown:**
- Employer: 13% of employee's salary; remits 13.5% out of 18.5% to SSNIT (Social Security and National Insurance Trust).
- Employee: 5.5% withheld and remitted.
- Remaining 5% goes to approved occupational pension scheme trustees.
- Either employer or employee or both may contribute to a provident fund up to 16.5% combined (exempt from tax if withdrawal conditions are met).
- Self-employed who qualify may contribute to mandatory schemes.
- Informal sector: 35% of declared income treated as deductible for calculating income. (EY p.498)

**Income ceiling:** Not specified in EY source.

**Notes:**
- Double tax treaties in force: Belgium, Czech Republic, Denmark, France, Germany, Italy, Mauritius, Morocco, Netherlands, Qatar, Singapore, South Africa, Switzerland, United Kingdom. (EY p.499)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Business Income)
- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals including traders, professionals, partners in partnerships, sole proprietors.
- **Tax base:** Net accounting profit plus non-deductible expenses, less capital allowances and personal reliefs. Income from business = gains and profits from the business for the year. Includes service fees, trading stock consideration, amounts connected with business activities, gains from capital assets, gifts received with respect to business, amounts for accepting restrictions on business capacity. (EY p.496)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual rates 0%–35% (same scale as employment).
- **Social security:** Self-employed who qualify may contribute to mandatory SSNIT scheme; rates not separately specified.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT applies; standard rate 15%.
- **Key limits:** Modified basis assessment available for individuals with Ghana-only business income (installment, turnover or cash basis). Loss carryforward 5 years (from 2023). (EY p.496, p.498)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No specific preferential income tax regime for new residents or expats identified in EY source. Foreign employment exercised abroad is exempt from tax if employer is nonresident OR is a resident employer and individual is in foreign country ≥183 continuous days. (EY p.494)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (no personal reliefs beyond zero-rate band modeled).
> EUR/GHS rate used: 1 EUR ≈ GHS 16 (approximate 2025 average; GHS has depreciated; check current rate).
> "Employment" = gross annual salary; employee SS 5.5% on gross; GHS 5,880 zero-rate band applied.
> "B2B" = self-employment net profit at same progressive rates; SS not specified for self-employed.

**EUR to GHS conversions:**
- €30,000 = GHS 480,000
- €60,000 = GHS 960,000
- €100,000 = GHS 1,600,000

### Working: GHS 480,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (5.5%) | 5.5% × 480,000 | GHS 26,400 |
| Band 1: GHS 0–5,880 at 0% | 0% | GHS 0 |
| Band 2: GHS 5,880–7,200 at 5% | 5% × 1,320 | GHS 66 |
| Band 3: GHS 7,200–8,760 at 10% | 10% × 1,560 | GHS 156 |
| Band 4: GHS 8,760–46,760 at 17.5% | 17.5% × 38,000 | GHS 6,650 |
| Band 5: GHS 46,760–238,760 at 25% | 25% × 192,000 | GHS 48,000 |
| Band 6: GHS 238,760–480,000 at 30% | 30% × 241,240 | GHS 72,372 |
| Total PIT | 0 + 66 + 156 + 6,650 + 48,000 + 72,372 | GHS 127,244 |
| **Total burden** | **26,400 + 127,244** | **GHS 153,644** |
| **Effective rate** | **153,644 / 480,000** | **~32.0%** |

**Working — GHS 960,000 Employment:**
- SS: 5.5% × 960,000 = GHS 52,800
- Tax: 0 + 66 + 156 + 6,650 + 48,000 + 30% × (605,000 − 238,760) + 35% × (960,000 − 605,000)
  = 66 + 156 + 6,650 + 48,000 + 30% × 366,240 + 35% × 355,000
  = 66 + 156 + 6,650 + 48,000 + 109,872 + 124,250 = GHS 288,994 (revised)

**Working — GHS 1,600,000 Employment:**
- SS: 5.5% × 1,600,000 = GHS 88,000
- Tax: 0 + 66 + 156 + 6,650 + 48,000 + 109,872 + 35% × (1,600,000 − 605,000)
  = 164,744 + 35% × 995,000 = 164,744 + 348,250 = GHS 513,000 (revised)

- EUR/GHS ≈ 16 (approximate; GHS has been volatile — check Banco de Ghana for current rate)
- Employee SS: 5.5% on gross; no ceiling specified
- GHS 5,880 zero-rate band applied; no personal allowances/reliefs modeled
- B2B: self-employed SS not specified; assumed 0 for illustration
- 35% top rate applicable above GHS 605,000 (cumulative)
