## TERRITORIAL SYSTEM NOTE

> Panama uses a **strictly territorial** income tax system. **Only Panamanian-source income is taxed.** Foreign-source income — including salaries paid by foreign companies for work done outside Panama, investment income from foreign sources, and remote work income for foreign clients — is completely and permanently exempt from Panamanian income tax.
>
> Panama uses the **Balboa (PAB)** which is pegged **1:1 to the USD**. All amounts in PAB = USD.

## Personal Income Tax

Resident and nonresident individuals taxed on Panamanian-source income only, regardless of nationality or where payment occurs. Residency: > 183 continuous or noncontinuous days in the calendar year OR if the individual has established permanent residence in Panama. Tax year: calendar year. Returns due 15 March. (EY pp.1191, 1195–1196)

**Annual PIT brackets (employment and self-employment income):** (EY p.1193)

| Taxable income — Exceeding (PAB) | Not exceeding (PAB) | Tax on lower amount (PAB) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 11,000 | 0 | 0% |
| 11,000 | 50,000 | 0 | 15% |
| 50,000 | — | 5,850 | 25% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY pp.1193–1194)
- Mortgage interest on principal residence: up to PAB 15,000/year
- Educational loan interest: deductible
- Charitable donations: up to USD 50,000 if made to recognized charitable organizations
- Medical expenses not reimbursed by insurance: deductible
- Medical and hospitalization insurance premiums (excluding SS payments): deductible
- School expenses: up to USD 3,600 per dependent
- Pension plan contributions: up to 10% of gross salary or PAB 15,000
- Spouse filing jointly: additional PAB 800 deduction
- Severance/seniority payments upon termination: 1% deduction per complete year of service with same employer; additional PAB 5,000 deductible
- Business deductions: all costs and expenses necessary to generate taxable income

**Notes:**
- Foreign-source income: completely exempt (EY p.1192)
- Interest from Panamanian savings/time deposits and government securities: exempt (EY p.1192)
- Dividends from Panamanian companies: 10% final withholding (bearer shares: 20%); 5% for free-trade zone companies; foreign-source dividends from Panamanian companies operating abroad: exempt (EY p.1192)
- Capital gains on bonds/shares/quotas from legal entities: 10% (EY p.1192)
- Capital gains on real estate: 10% (seller must pay 3% of gross price as advance; buyer withholds and deposits with authorities within 10 days) (EY p.1193)
- Additional 2% transfer tax on real estate
- Stock Exchange listed shares: exempt from capital gains tax (EY p.1192)
- Farming income: exempt if gross sales < PAB 300,000 (EY p.1191)
- Representation expenses: 10% withholding on amounts up to PAB 25,000; 10% on first PAB 25,000 + 15% on excess for amounts > PAB 25,000 (EY p.1191)
- Nonresidents: 15% + 2.75% educational tax withholding on Panamanian-source income (EY p.1194)
- Loss carryforward for self-employed: 20% of loss deductible in each of 5 subsequent years (limited to 50% of taxable income in each subsequent year); nondeductible amounts cannot be carried forward (EY p.1194)
- Married persons: may file jointly or separately (taxpayer's election)
- Tax authority: DGI (Dirección General de Ingresos) / MEF (Ministerio de Economía y Finanzas)
- Double tax treaties: Barbados, Czech Republic, France, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Korea (South), Luxembourg, Mexico, Netherlands, Portugal, Qatar, Singapore, Spain, UAE, UK, Vietnam (EY p.1196)

## Social Security (CSS — Caja de Seguro Social)

Starting April 2025, staggered increase in employer contributions. (EY p.1195)

| Contribution | Employee % | Employer % | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| CSS (social security) | 9.75% | 13.25% (until Feb 2027) | From Mar 2027: 14.25%; from Mar 2029: 15.25% |
| Education tax | 1.25% | 1.50% | Fixed |
| Workers' compensation | — | 0.056%–5.67% | Varies by business/risk type |
| **Total (employee)** | **11.00%** | **~14.75%–15.8%** | — |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling applies to the amount of remuneration subject to SS contributions. Contributions computed on employee's gross compensation. (EY p.1195)

**Notes:**
- Employer SS: 13.25% until 28 February 2027; 14.25% from 1 March 2027 to 28 February 2029; 15.25% from 1 March 2029 onwards
- Employee SS: 9.75% (fixed, no change announced)
- Education tax: employee 1.25%; employer 1.50%
- No SS totalization agreements with any country (EY p.1195)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All individuals with self-employment, business, commercial or agricultural income from Panamanian sources.
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses). Farming exempt if gross < PAB 300,000.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT rates (0%–25%) on annual net Panamanian-source income. Annual return by 15 March; estimated tax payments in installments on 30 June, 30 September, 31 December (based on prior year's estimated tax).
- **Social security:** CSS 9.75% employee + 1.25% education tax applies; must register with CSS.
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to ITBMS (Panama VAT) at 7% standard rate (10% on alcoholic beverages, 15% on tobacco).
- **Key limits:** Only Panamanian-source income taxable; loss carryforward 20%/year × 5 years.

### Option B — Remote Worker Visa (Visa para Trabajo Remoto)

- **Who qualifies:** Foreign nationals who wish to reside in Panama for no longer than 18 months working remotely for a foreign employer or independently via telecommuter mode.
- **Tax treatment:** Under the territorial principle, income from a foreign source for work having effects abroad is **NOT Panamanian-source income → completely exempt from PIT, CSS, and education tax**.
- **Income requirement:** Annual income from foreign source of at least PAB 36,000 (≈ USD 36,000/year = USD 3,000/month) or equivalent in foreign currency.
- **Conditions:** Employment contract with foreign transnational company or independent telecommuter; duties have effects abroad; income from foreign source.
- **Duration:** 9 months, renewable for equivalent period (total up to 18 months).
- **Visa cancellation:** If the holder represents the foreign company for local activities, the visa is cancelled. (EY p.1198)

### Option C — MHQ (Multinational Company Headquarters) Permanent Visa

- **Who qualifies:** Foreign personnel and dependants of companies holding MHQ Permanent Personnel Visa (Law No. 41, 27 August 2007).
- **Tax exemption:** Salary and all labor remuneration (including in-kind) received by MHQ Permanent Personnel Visa holders are **completely exempt from income tax, CSS, and educational insurance tax**, provided salary is recognized as a payroll expense in the MHQ company's accounting. (EY p.1198)
- **Key benefit:** Total PIT + SS exemption on all compensation — one of the most comprehensive expat tax exemption regimes in Latin America.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Territorial System (Permanent)
- **Benefit:** All foreign-source income permanently exempt. No time limit.

### Remote Worker Visa (Executive Decree No. 198, 7 May 2021)
- **Benefit:** Full PIT + CSS exemption on foreign-source remote work income.
- **Duration:** 9 months + renewable (up to 18 months total).
- **Income floor:** PAB 36,000/year from foreign source.
- **Caveats:** Must not perform local Panamanian activities. (EY p.1198)

### International Executives — Special Temporary Visa
- **Eligibility:** International executives with a special temporary visa who receive monthly salary ≥ USD 1,000 from a foreign source for transactions/services having effects abroad.
- **Benefit:** Income not subject to income tax, educational tax or SS in Panama.
- **Caveats:** Must receive income from foreign source; transactions must have effects abroad. (EY p.1198)

### Friendly Countries Visa (Executive Decree No. 343, 16 May 2012, modified by Decree No. 197, 7 May 2021)
- **Eligibility:** Citizens of 50 designated "friendly" jurisdictions (includes Argentina, Australia, Austria, Belgium, Brazil, Canada, Chile, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Japan, Korea (South), Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Mexico, Monaco, Montenegro, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Paraguay, Peru, Poland, Portugal, San Marino, Serbia, Singapore, Slovak Republic, South Africa, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, UK, USA, Uruguay) plus others. (EY pp.1200–1201)
- **Benefit:** Permanent residence permit obtainable within 2 years (initial 2-year temp permit, then 3-year permanent renewable permit).
- **Investment requirement:** Real estate investment ≥ USD 200,000 OR fixed-term deposit ≥ USD 200,000.
- **Duration:** Indefinite.

### Qualified Investor Permanent Resident (Executive Decree No. 722, 15 October 2020, modified Decree 193, 15 October 2024)
- **Benefit:** Expedited permanent legal residence for international investors.
- **Investment options:** Real estate ≥ USD 300,000; Panama Stock Exchange securities ≥ USD 500,000; fixed-term deposits ≥ USD 750,000.
- **Duration:** Permanent (5 years minimum investment hold).
- **Path to citizenship:** Eligible to naturalize after 5 years of permanent residence. (EY p.1202)

### MHQ Permanent Personnel Visa
- **Benefit:** Total exemption from income tax + CSS + education tax on all compensation.
- **Eligibility:** Employees of companies holding MHQ status (Law 41/2007).

## 2026 Changes

- **CSS employer rate increase (April 2025):** From 13.25% until 28 February 2027. Next increase to 14.25% from 1 March 2027.
- **Remote Worker Visa:** Fully operative since May 2021; 9-month + renewable; income floor PAB 36,000/year.
- **Friendly Countries Visa:** Modified by Decree 197 (May 2021); 50 qualifying jurisdictions; permanent residence path.
- **Qualified Investor Visa:** Real estate threshold confirmed at USD 300,000 (Decree 193, October 2024).
- **No structural PIT changes announced for 2026.**

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **CSS deductibility from PIT base:** EY source does not explicitly confirm whether CSS employee contributions (9.75%) are deductible from PIT taxable income. If deductible, effective rates would be modestly lower.
- [ ] **ITBMS (VAT) 7%:** Applies to self-employed services; not modeled in effective rate calculations.
- [ ] **Remote Worker Visa income floor:** PAB 36,000/year minimum income requirement — whether this is gross or net not specified.
- [ ] **Education tax deductibility:** The 1.25% employee education tax — deductibility not confirmed.
- [ ] **Employer CSS rate 2027/2029:** Rate increases to 14.25% (March 2027) and 15.25% (March 2029) will increase total employer burden.
- [ ] **Friendly Countries Visa investment requirement:** USD 200,000 minimum — whether this can be financed (mortgaged) or must be equity is important for practical planning.
- [ ] **MHQ eligibility:** Only companies holding formal MHQ status under Law 41/2007 can sponsor this visa — not available to all employers.
