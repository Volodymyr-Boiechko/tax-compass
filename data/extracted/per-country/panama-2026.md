## Personal Income Tax

**Territorial system:** Both residents and non-residents are taxed **only on Panama-source income**. Foreign-source income is completely exempt from Panamanian income tax, regardless of residence status. This is one of the most important features for international tax planning. (EY p.1191)

Tax year = calendar year. Employers withhold PAYE on employment income. Self-employed and individuals with additional income file annual returns by 15 March. Residents: 183+ consecutive or non-consecutive days in Panama in a calendar year, or the preceding year if permanent residence established.

**PIT brackets** (EY p.1193)

| Taxable income (PAB / USD) | Tax on lower amount | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 11,000 | 0 | 0% |
| 11,001 – 50,000 | 0 | 15% |
| Over 50,000 | 5,850 | 25% |

Note: PAB (Panamanian Balboa) is pegged 1:1 to the US Dollar.

**Education tax:** Employers and employees pay an education tax in addition to income tax:
- Employee: **1.25%** of gross salary
- Employer: **1.5%** of gross payroll
(EY p.1195)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Mortgage interest on principal residence: up to PAB 15,000/year
- Interest on educational loans: deductible
- Charitable donations: up to USD 50,000/year (to government-approved charities)
- Medical expenses not reimbursed by insurance: deductible
- Medical/hospitalization insurance premiums: deductible (excluding social security amounts)
- School expenses: up to USD 3,600/child/year
- Pension plan contributions: up to 10% of gross salary or PAB 15,000/year
- Personal deduction for joint filers: PAB 800 (EY p.1193)

**Notes:**
- Representation expenses: flat 10% WHT on amounts up to PAB 25,000; excess subject to 10%/15% WHT (EY p.1191)
- Capital gains on bond/share sales (legal entities): 10% capital gains tax; 5% final WHT advance (EY p.1192)
- Capital gains on real estate: 10% rate; 3% advance WHT on sale price (EY p.1193)
- Dividends from Panamanian companies: 10% WHT (final); bearer shares: 20% WHT; free-trade zone/foreign-source dividends: 5% WHT (EY p.1192)
- Foreign-source income: 100% EXEMPT — key territorial feature
- Non-residents: 15% flat WHT + 1.25% education tax = 16.25% effective WHT rate (EY p.1194)
- Self-employed loss relief: may deduct 20% of loss in each of the next 5 years; maximum 50% offset per year (EY p.1194)

## Social Security

Panama's social security system (Caja del Seguro Social — CSS) was reformed with staggered contribution increases starting April 2025. (EY p.1195)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer (current April 2025 – Feb 2027) | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security (CSS) | 9.75% | 13.25% | Varies |
| Education tax | 1.25% | 1.50% | — |
| Workers' compensation | — | 0.056%–5.67% (varies by business) | — |
| **Total (approx.)** | **11.0%** | **~14.75%–20.42%** | — |

**Staggered employer CSS increases (EY p.1195):**
- April 2025 – February 2027: 13.25%
- March 2027 – February 2029: 14.25%
- From March 2029: 15.25%

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling on CSS contributions (applied to full gross salary). (EY p.1195)

**Notes:**
- CSS provides health care, disability, maternity, retirement, and death benefits
- Panama has not entered into any social security totalization agreements with other countries (EY p.1195)
- Self-employed: can voluntarily affiliate with CSS; mandatory for some professions

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual / Sole Trader (Persona Natural)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual operating a business or profession in Panama.
- **Tax base:** Net profit from business/commercial/agricultural activities in Panama (territorial — foreign-source income excluded).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates 0%/15%/25% on net Panama-source income. Farming income exempt if gross sales < PAB 300,000. (EY p.1191)
- **Social security:** Voluntary CSS affiliation; education tax (1.25% employee portion) may apply on self-employment income.
- **VAT obligations:** Register for ITBMS (equivalent to VAT; 7% standard rate; 10% on alcohol/hotels; 15% on tobacco) if annual taxable income/sales exceed threshold. Monthly filing.
- **Key limits:** Annual return by 15 March; estimated tax payments due 30 June and 30 September. Must maintain adequate accounting records.

### Option B — Multinational Company Headquarters (SEM) — Employee Benefits

- **Who qualifies:** Employees of companies holding an MHQ (Multinational Company Headquarters) license under Law No. 41/2007.
- **Tax base:** Salary and labor remuneration paid by MHQ companies.
- **Rate / mechanism:** MHQ employees holding a Permanent Personnel Visa are **EXEMPT** from income tax, social security, and education tax on their MHQ salary. (EY p.1198)
- **Social security:** Exempt from CSS contributions for qualifying MHQ staff.
- **Key limits:** Requires specific MHQ visa; employer must hold MHQ license from Panama's MICI (Ministry of Commerce).

### Option C — Remote Worker / Short-Stay Visa (Visa de Corta Estancia como Trabajador Remoto)

- **Who qualifies:** Foreign nationals working for a foreign company while residing in Panama; Executive Decree No. 198 of 7 May 2021. Minimum income: PAB 36,000/year from foreign source.
- **Tax base:** Foreign-source income is EXEMPT from Panamanian income tax (territorial system). No Panamanian tax on remote work income.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 0% Panama income tax on foreign-source employment/self-employment income.
- **Social security:** Exempt if activities have effects abroad only.
- **Duration:** 9-month visa, renewable for additional 9 months. (EY p.1198)
- **Key limits:** Must have contract with foreign company; must not perform local-effect activities; income must be from foreign source.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Pensionado Program (Retired Persons)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals who receive a lifetime pension/annuity of at least USD 1,000/month (or USD 750/month + USD 100,000 real estate investment in Panama).
- **Benefit:** Permanent residence in Panama; extensive discounts on consumer goods (20–50% on utility bills, healthcare, entertainment, transport, restaurants, hotels, etc.); Panamanian territorial tax system means pension income from foreign sources is NOT taxed by Panama.
- **Duration:** Permanent; renewable annually.
- **Application:** Via Servicio Nacional de Migración.
- **Caveats:** The pension must be lifetime (e.g., government pension, annuity); employment pension from company may qualify. The tax benefit is implicit — no Panama income tax on foreign pension. (EY p.1199–1200)

### Friendly Countries Visa (Visa de Países Amigos)

- **Eligibility:** Citizens of 50 designated "friendly" countries (including USA, EU member states, Australia, Canada, UK, Japan, Korea, Israel, etc.). Requires purpose statement and either real estate investment ≥ USD 200,000 or fixed-term deposit. (EY p.1200)
- **Benefit:** Permanent residence permit + work permit for own business (profession must not be legally reserved for Panamanians).
- **Duration:** 2-year temporary → 3-year renewable → permanent.
- **Tax impact:** Full Panamanian territorial tax applies; foreign income exempt.

## 2026 Changes

- **CSS employer contribution increase:** From April 2025: 13.25% (currently); from March 2027: 14.25%; from March 2029: 15.25%. The staggered increase represents a significant rise in employer labor costs. (EY p.1195)
- **No PIT rate changes** announced for 2026; 25% top rate remains. (EY p.1193)
- **Remote worker visa:** Decree No. 198/2021 continues in force; no announced modifications for 2026.
- **ITBMS (VAT):** 7% standard rate; no announced changes.
- **Education tax:** 1.25%/1.50% (employee/employer) — no changes announced.

**Key insight for international workers and digital nomads:** Panama's territorial system is its defining advantage — foreign-source income faces 0% Panamanian income tax. A digital nomad earning from foreign clients who moves to Panama under the remote worker visa pays no Panamanian tax at all. Even for Panama-source income, the 25% top rate and generous 0% bracket (first PAB 11,000) create competitive rates. The Pensionado program for retirees is world-famous for combining easy residence with zero tax on foreign pension income.

- Self-employed: no CSS, no education tax on self-employment income (verify current rules)
- MHQ employees: fully exempt; not shown in table
- Remote worker (foreign income): 0% across all income levels — not shown (trivially 0%)
