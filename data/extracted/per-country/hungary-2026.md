## Personal Income Tax

**Flat 15% personal income tax** applies to all consolidated income (employment + self-employment + most other income) and investment income. Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Hungarian-source income only. Tax year = calendar year. Pre-filled returns; if not modified, enter into force on 20 May (filing deadline). Married couples taxed separately. (EY p.586, p.592)

**Residency:** Hungarian citizens considered tax residents. EEA nationals spending ≥183 days/year in Hungary are residents. Third-country nationals with permanent residence status or a permanent residence permit are residents. Foreign individuals with permanent home in Hungary are residents. Dual citizens without permanent home or habitual abode in Hungary are NOT considered Hungarian tax residents. (EY p.586)

**Flat PIT rate: 15%** on consolidated tax base and investment income. (EY p.590)

**Employment income:** All compensation items relating to the employment relationship. Benefits in kind (from 1 January 2011) replaced by two categories — "certain specific benefits" and "fringe benefits" — with different effective rates. (EY p.586–587)

**Certain specific benefits (e.g., phone for private use, business travel, meals, gifts):**
- Tax base = 1.18× arm's length value not reimbursed.
- PIT = 15% on tax base.
- 13% social tax also applies.
- **Effective tax rate: 33.04%.** Payable by employer. (EY p.587)

**Fringe benefits (e.g., Széchenyi Recreation Card):**
- Tax base = arm's length value (no 1.18× multiplication).
- PIT = 15% + 13% social tax.
- **Effective tax rate: 28%.** Payable by employer. (EY p.587)
- Széchenyi Recreation Card max annual: HUF 450,000 (accommodation sub-account). Active Hungarians sub-account: additional HUF 120,000/year (max HUF 60,000/6 months). (EY p.587)

**Investment income:**
- Dividends: 15% withholding (final). From 1 July 2023: 13% social tax also applies to dividend income and capital gains until total of employment income + these investment incomes reaches 24× annual minimum wage (HUF 290,800 in 2025). (EY p.588)
- Capital gains: flat 15% rate. 15% of substantiated transaction costs may be deducted. Capital losses from controlled capital market transactions: 15% offsettable against gains from such transactions (previous or next 2 years). (EY p.589)
- Interest income: 15% final withholding; 13% social tax on interest from securities.
- Royalties: 15% on taxable income (revenue minus deductible expenses).
- Rental income: part of consolidated tax base at 15%; 10% standard deduction or actual documented expenses.
- Income from independent activities (self-employment): 15% on taxable income; 10% standard deduction or actual documented expenses. (EY p.590)

**Personal allowances and tax credits:** (EY p.589–590)
- **Family allowance (from 1 July 2025):** Reduces monthly tax base:
  - 1 dependent: HUF 15,000/month per dependent (increased from HUF 10,000)
  - 2 dependents: HUF 30,000/month per dependent (increased from HUF 20,000)
  - 3+ dependents: HUF 49,500/month per dependent (increased from HUF 33,000)
  - From 1 January 2026: further increase scheduled (specific amounts not yet published at EY content date).
  - Fetus from 91st day of pregnancy eligible. Tax base reduction may be shared between spouses/cohabitants. Total credit may not exceed total tax payable (non-refundable). (EY p.589)
  - Applicable only if ≥75% of annual income taxable in Hungary; cannot combine with same/similar benefits from another country. (EY p.590)
- **Youth allowance (under 25):** Specified; young persons under 25 exempt from PIT on income up to HUF 576,601 (2025 average wage level). (Not explicitly in EY source extract, but confirmed by Hungary tax law — known gap.)
- No general personal deduction or standard personal allowance (unlike many EU countries).
- **Business deductions:** 10% standard deduction from self-employment income, OR actual documented deductible expenses. (EY p.590)

**Other notes on income:**
- Income from non-treaty "low-tax" countries (interest, dividends, capital gains relating to share of entity in low-tax country without valid DTA with Hungary): part of consolidated tax base at 15% + social tax. OECD countries exempted from this rule. (EY p.588)

**Inheritance and gift duty:** (EY p.590)
- Rates 0%–18% on assets located in Hungary (on market value).
- Assets transferred between lineal descendants and spouses: NOT subject to inheritance and gift duty.
- Resident foreigners and nonresidents also subject to these rates.

**Notes:**
- US-Hungary DTA terminated effective 1 January 2024 — US citizens/residents in Hungary no longer have treaty relief from Hungarian tax. (EY p.593)
- Russia: suspended Articles 5–22 and 24 from 8 August 2023; Belarus suspended Articles 10–11 and 13 from 1 January 2024. Hungary still applies treaty rules unilaterally. (EY p.593)
- No wealth tax.

## Social Security

**From 1 July 2020:** Integrated 18.5% social security contribution for employees (replaces previous three components: 10% pension, 8.5% healthcare, 0% labor). No employee pension contribution cap. (EY p.591)

**Social tax (employer contribution from 1 January 2023):** 13% of gross salary. No ceiling applies. (EY p.591)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (integrated) | 18.5% | — | 18.5% |
| Social tax | — | 13% | 13% |
| **Total** | **18.5%** | **13%** | **31.5%** |

**Investment income social tax (from 1 July 2023):** 13% social tax applies to dividends, capital gains from securities, and interest from securities, until total of employment income + these investment incomes reaches 24× minimum wage (HUF 290,800 in 2025 → annual cap ≈ HUF 6,979,200). (EY p.588, p.591)

**Income ceiling for SS:** None (no employee pension contribution cap as of 1 July 2020). (EY p.591)

**Notes:**
- Non-Hungarian, non-EEA, non-totalization-agreement national seconded by a foreign employer not registered in Hungary: exempt from SS for up to 2 years (extension possible; if no notification filed, SS applies retroactively from day one after 2 years). (EY p.590)
- A1 certificate holders exempt from Hungarian SS. (EY p.591)
- Totalization agreements (bilateral): Albania, Australia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Canada, CIS, India, Japan, Korea (South), Kosovo, Moldova, Mongolia, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Quebec, Russian Federation, Serbia, Türkiye, United States. (EY p.591)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Independent Activity Income (Standard Self-Employment)
- **Who qualifies:** All individuals performing activities not classified as employment; includes private entrepreneurs and agricultural producers. (EY p.588)
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue minus 10% standard deduction OR actual documented deductible expenses; then 15% PIT on net.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 15% flat on taxable income (after 10% standard deduction).
- **Social security:** 18.5% employee SS + 13% social tax = 31.5% total on self-employment income.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required; standard rate 27% (highest in EU); reduced 18% (selected food/accommodation) and 5% (books, drugs, certain housing, professional services).
- **Key limits:** Self-employed may elect 10% standard deduction without documentation; loss carryforward rules apply. Business losses carried forward; no carryback. (EY p.590)

### Option B — EKHO (Simplified Contribution for Entertainment and IP)
- **Note:** EY source does not mention EKHO in the provided pages. EKHO (Egyszerűsített Közteher-viselési Hozzájárulás) is a special simplified contribution regime applicable to qualifying activities (writers, performing artists, athletes, IT developers under specific conditions). Provides flat contribution in lieu of full SS + social tax.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Under EKHO, total contribution approximately 13% for qualifying individuals on qualified income (employer/payer side 13%; employee side small additional levy). Significantly reduces total burden compared to 31.5% standard SS.
- **Key limits:** Only specific activities qualify; income ceiling applies annually. Verification recommended — not confirmed in EY extract.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Tax Relief
- **Eligibility:** Non-Hungarian nationals not having been tax residents in Hungary for at least 3 of the preceding 5 years; working in Hungary under employment contract or as self-employed.
- **Benefit:** Certain in-kind benefits (housing allowances, home leave, etc.) may be exempt under applicable rules. (EY p.587 — expat benefits partly exempt.)

### Youth (Under 25) Tax Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Individuals under 25 years of age.
- **Benefit:** Exempt from PIT on employment income up to the average wage (approximately HUF 576,601/month in 2025); 15% applies on excess.
- **Duration:** Until age 25.
- **Caveats:** Must be tax resident in Hungary. (Not confirmed in EY extract — based on known Hungarian law; noted as gap.)

## 2026 Changes

- **Family allowance further increase from 1 January 2026:** Additional scheduled increase in family allowance tax base reduction announced; specific 2026 amounts not yet published at EY content date. (EY p.590)
- **Family allowance from 1 July 2025 (already in force):** Increased to HUF 15,000/HUF 30,000/HUF 49,500 per dependent per month for 1/2/3+ dependents. (EY p.589)
- **No announced PIT rate changes for 2026:** Flat 15% rate unchanged. (EY p.590)
- **No announced SS rate changes for 2026:** 18.5% employee SS + 13% employer social tax unchanged. (EY p.591)
- **US-Hungary DTA terminated (from 1 January 2024):** US citizens working in Hungary no longer have treaty protection; higher exposure to double taxation. (EY p.593)
- **New immigration categories from 1 March 2024:** Hungarian Card, EU Blue Card, National Card, guest worker permits introduced. (EY p.597)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants (no family allowance applicable).
> EUR/HUF rate: 1 EUR ≈ HUF 395 (approximate 2025 average).
> "Employment" = gross salary; employee SS 18.5% applied; 15% PIT on gross (flat rate — no deduction from employment income beyond family allowance, which is 0 for single no dependants).
> "B2B" = independent activity income; 10% standard deduction applied; 15% PIT on 90% of revenue; self-employed SS 18.5% + social tax 13% = 31.5%.
> Note: Hungary PIT = 15% of gross employment income (no general personal deduction reduces the employment tax base).

**EUR to HUF conversions:**
- €30,000 = HUF 11,850,000
- €60,000 = HUF 23,700,000
- €100,000 = HUF 39,500,000

### Working: HUF 11,850,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (18.5%) | 18.5% × 11,850,000 | HUF 2,192,250 |
| PIT (15% of gross) | 15% × 11,850,000 | HUF 1,777,500 |
| **Total burden** | **2,192,250 + 1,777,500** | **HUF 3,969,750** |
| **Effective rate** | **3,969,750 / 11,850,000** | **~33.5%** |

### Working: HUF 11,850,000 (≈ €30,000) B2B Independent Activity

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| SS (18.5% + 13% = 31.5%) | 31.5% × 11,850,000 | HUF 3,732,750 |
| 10% standard deduction | 10% × 11,850,000 | HUF 1,185,000 |
| PIT base | 11,850,000 − 1,185,000 | HUF 10,665,000 |
| PIT (15%) | 15% × 10,665,000 | HUF 1,599,750 |
| **Total burden** | **3,732,750 + 1,599,750** | **HUF 5,332,500** |
| **Effective rate** | **5,332,500 / 11,850,000** | **~45.0%** |

**Key insight — Employment vs B2B:** Hungary's flat 15% PIT is the lowest in the EU, but the 18.5% employee SS makes the total employment burden ~33.5%. For self-employed, the combination of 15% PIT + 31.5% SS (18.5% + 13% social tax) creates a very high total B2B burden of ~45%. Self-employed bear both the employee and employer-equivalent contributions.

**Key insight — EKHO regime (if applicable):** For qualifying activities (writers, artists, IT under some interpretations), EKHO can dramatically reduce the total contribution burden. At a combined ~13% EKHO rate (vs 31.5% standard SS), the effective total B2B rate would drop to approximately 15% + 13% = ~28% — competitive with employment.

**Key insight — Family allowance impact:** For families with 3+ dependents, the HUF 49,500/month/dependent tax base reduction significantly lowers PIT (and also SS up to the base). A family with 3 children would receive HUF 148,500/month (≈ HUF 1,782,000/year) in tax base reduction, saving 15% × 1,782,000 = HUF 267,300 in annual PIT (~€676/year). With the 2026 increase, these savings will be higher.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/HUF ≈ 395 (approximate 2025 average; floating rate)
- Flat 15% PIT on gross employment income (no general personal deduction for employment)
- B2B: 10% standard deduction reduces PIT base; SS = 18.5% + 13% social tax = 31.5%
- No family allowance (single, no dependants)
- No fringe benefits modeled (certain specific benefits / Széchenyi Card have special rates)
- Investment income social tax (13%) not modeled (applies until 24× minimum wage threshold)
