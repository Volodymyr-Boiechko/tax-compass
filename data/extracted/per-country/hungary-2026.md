## Personal Income Tax

> Hungary has a flat personal income tax rate of 15% on the consolidated tax base (employment, business, and most other income). The tax year is the calendar year. Residents are taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Hungarian-source income. Capital gains are also taxed at 15%. No net wealth tax.

| Taxable income (HUF) | Rate |
|---|---|
| All consolidated income | 15% |
| Capital gains | 15% |
| Dividend income | 15% (+ 13% social tax where applicable) |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Family tax allowance (from 1 July 2025): reduces monthly tax base
  - 1 dependent: HUF 15,000/month tax reduction per dependent
  - 2 dependents: HUF 30,000/month per dependent
  - 3 or more dependents: HUF 49,500/month per dependent
  - Further increase scheduled for 1 January 2026 (EY p. 590)
- Business deductions: standard 10% deduction OR actual documented expenses for income from independent activities
- Personal tax credits are non-refundable and cannot exceed total tax payable

**Notes:**
- Consolidated tax base includes employment and income from independent activities; taxed at flat 15%
- Family allowance: can be shared between spouses/cohabitants; may not exceed total tax liability
- No personal allowance (tax-free minimum) for adults without dependants
- "Certain specific benefits" (e.g., phone, travel, business gifts): effective rate 33.04% (tax base = 1.18 × benefit value; 15% PIT + 13% social tax)
- "Fringe benefits" (e.g., Széchenyi Recreation Card): effective rate 28% (base = face value, no 1.18× multiplier; 15% PIT + 13% social tax); annual SZÉP Card limit HUF 450,000 + HUF 120,000 "Active Hungarians" (EY p. 587)

## Social Security

> Hungary has a single integrated employee social security contribution of 18.5% since 1 July 2020. Employers pay a 13% social tax. No contribution ceiling applies.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (integrated) | 18.5% | — | 18.5% |
| Social tax (szocho) | — | 13% | 13% (on certain income) |
| **Total** | **18.5%** | **13%** | **~31.5%** |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling — contributions apply to full gross salary (EY p. 591)
**Notes:**
- Employee 18.5% social security: consists of 10% pension + 7% healthcare + 1.5% labour market (integrated since 2020)
- Employer 13% social tax (szociális hozzájárulási adó): applies to gross salary; no ceiling
- 13% social tax also payable on capital gains, dividends, and income from securities until the total of these income types plus employment income reaches 24× the minimum wage (24 × HUF 290,800 in 2025 = HUF 6,979,200) for the current year
- EKHO (simplified contribution) available for certain eligible activities (artists, athletes, etc.): flat-rate regime replacing normal contributions (EY p. 588 — reference to independent activities)

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals (private entrepreneurs) can choose between the standard income tax rules or simplified regimes (KATA abolished for most, KIVA available for micro-businesses).

### Option A — Standard Sole Trader (Egyéni vállalkozó — standard)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered as a private entrepreneur
- **Tax base:** Revenue minus deductible expenses; 10% standard deduction OR actual documented costs
- **Rate / mechanism:** 15% PIT on net income (after 10% deduction); minimum tax base rules may apply if income is below the minimum wage level
- **Social security:** 18.5% employee contribution + 13% social tax on drawn income (salary); total ~31.5% on professional income
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory VAT registration above HUF 12 million annual turnover; standard rate 27%
- **Key limits:** Must draw at least minimum wage as salary (HUF 290,800/month in 2025); minimum social security applies even if no income

### Option B — KIVA (Small Business Tax — Kisvállalati adó)
- **Who qualifies:** Micro- and small businesses with revenue below HUF 3 billion; available for entities with 1–50 employees
- **Tax base:** Cash-flow basis; payments to shareholders + wages + social security contributions
- **Rate / mechanism:** 10% KIVA replaces 13% social tax and 9% CIT; total employer cost reduced
- **Social security:** Employee SS still 18.5%; KIVA replaces employer social tax
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT rules apply
- **Key limits:** Must elect KIVA at incorporation or by November 1 for next year; annual revenue/balance sheet limit HUF 3 billion

### Option C — EKHO (Simplified Contribution — Egyszerűsített Közteherviselési Hozzájárulás)
- **Who qualifies:** Specific eligible professions (artists, writers, athletes, certain IT professionals, etc.)
- **Tax base:** Gross income from eligible activities
- **Rate / mechanism:** 15% PIT (final) + 13% EKHO (replaces social contributions); total ~28% effective burden
- **Social security:** Covered by EKHO contribution; no separate SS required
- **VAT obligations:** Standard rules
- **Key limits:** Only available for listed eligible activities; EKHO income does not accumulate towards pension entitlements beyond minimum

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Under 25 Personal Income Tax Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Hungarian tax residents under 25 years of age
- **Benefit:** Full exemption from 15% PIT on employment and self-employment income up to the average gross salary (approximately HUF 590,000/month in 2025); amounts above are taxable
- **Duration:** Until the person reaches 25 years of age
- **Application deadline:** Automatic; no application required
- **Caveats:** Applies to employment and self-employment income only; social security contributions (18.5%) still apply

### Under 30 Family Tax Allowance for Mothers
- **Eligibility:** Hungarian mothers under 30 with children
- **Benefit:** Full PIT exemption on employment income (no HUF ceiling) — introduced from 1 January 2023
- **Duration:** Until the mother reaches 30 years of age
- **Application deadline:** Automatic
- **Caveats:** Applies only to employment income; social security contributions still due

### First Marriage Allowance
- **Eligibility:** Individuals getting married for the first time where either spouse is under 25 years of age at time of marriage
- **Benefit:** Monthly tax base reduction of HUF 33,335 (joint) for 24 months
- **Duration:** 24 months from the month following the marriage
- **Application deadline:** Must be claimed via the employer or self-assessment
- **Caveats:** Cannot be combined simultaneously with family allowance for the same income (though both can apply simultaneously if different thresholds are met)

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 586–600)

- **Family allowance increase from 1 January 2026:** Further increase to family tax allowance per dependent — specific new amounts not yet published at time of EY review (EY p. 590)
- Minimum wage indexation: HUF 290,800/month in 2025; expected increase for 2026 — affects minimum social security base for entrepreneurs
- US–Hungary tax treaty terminated effective 1 January 2024 — US citizens working in Hungary are no longer protected by a DTT (EY p. 592)
- 13% social tax applies to dividends/capital gains until 24× minimum wage threshold reached for the year

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, Hungarian tax resident.
> Currency: HUF. 1 EUR ≈ 400 HUF (approximate 2025 rate). EUR amounts are approximate conversions.
> Employment: includes employee 18.5% SS. B2B: sole trader with 10% standard deduction + 18.5% SS + 13% social tax.

**Employment at €30,000 (≈ HUF 12,000,000):**
1. PIT: HUF 12,000,000 × 15% = HUF 1,800,000 ≈ €4,500
2. Employee SS: HUF 12,000,000 × 18.5% = HUF 2,220,000 ≈ €5,550
3. Total burden = €4,500 + €5,550 = €10,050
4. Effective rate = €10,050 / €30,000 = **33.5%**

**Employment at €60,000 (≈ HUF 24,000,000):**
1. PIT: HUF 24,000,000 × 15% = HUF 3,600,000 ≈ €9,000
2. Employee SS: HUF 24,000,000 × 18.5% = HUF 4,440,000 ≈ €11,100
3. Total burden = €9,000 + €11,100 = €20,100
4. Effective rate = €20,100 / €60,000 = **33.5%**

**Employment at €100,000 (≈ HUF 40,000,000):**
1. PIT: HUF 40,000,000 × 15% = HUF 6,000,000 ≈ €15,000
2. Employee SS: HUF 40,000,000 × 18.5% = HUF 7,400,000 ≈ €18,500
3. Total burden = €15,000 + €18,500 = €33,500
4. Effective rate = €33,500 / €100,000 = **33.5%**

*Note: Flat rate means effective rate is constant at 33.5% (15% PIT + 18.5% SS) regardless of income level — no brackets.*

**B2B (sole trader standard) at €60,000 (≈ HUF 24,000,000 revenue):**
1. Standard 10% deduction: HUF 24,000,000 × 10% = HUF 2,400,000; net = HUF 21,600,000
2. Must draw minimum wage as salary: assume full profit drawn as professional income
3. Social security (18.5% + 13% social tax) on drawn salary: HUF 24,000,000 × 31.5% = HUF 7,560,000 ≈ €18,900
4. PIT on net income after 10% deduction: HUF 21,600,000 × 15% = HUF 3,240,000 ≈ €8,100
5. Total burden = €8,100 + €18,900 = €27,000
6. Effective rate = €27,000 / €60,000 = **45.0%**

*Note: B2B effective rate is significantly higher because self-employed must pay both employee (18.5%) and employer (13%) social contributions on drawn income.*

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €4,500 | €5,550 | €10,050 | **33.5%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€4,050 | ~€9,450 | ~€13,500 | **~45.0%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €9,000 | €11,100 | €20,100 | **33.5%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | ~€8,100 | ~€18,900 | ~€27,000 | **~45.0%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €15,000 | €18,500 | €33,500 | **33.5%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€13,500 | ~€31,500 | ~€45,000 | **~45.0%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants, no family tax allowance
- Employment: 15% flat PIT + 18.5% employee SS; employer pays 13% social tax separately (not shown)
- B2B: 10% standard expense deduction; 18.5% employee SS + 13% employer social tax both borne by entrepreneur
- EUR/HUF ≈ 400 (approximate; HUF not pegged to EUR)
- KIVA or EKHO could reduce the B2B burden for eligible entrepreneurs
- Social tax on dividends (13%) not modelled — applies to dividend income up to 24× minimum wage
