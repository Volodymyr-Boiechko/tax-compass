## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PIT system. Tax year = calendar year. Employment income withheld at source by employers monthly. Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents subject to final withholding tax on Tunisia-source income. Administered by the Direction Générale des Impôts.

**Residency:** Principal home in Tunisia; OR present for at least 183 days (continuously or intermittently) in the calendar year; OR civil servants or state officials performing duties in a foreign country who are not subject to personal tax on global income there.

**Non-residents:** Final withholding tax at 10%–20% (varying by income type); may be increased to 25% for residents of low-tax jurisdictions. Employees staying less than six continuous or non-continuous months: flat 20% on gross Tunisian-source salary. (EY pp. 1543, 1547)

### Resident Tax Rates — effective from 1 January 2025 (EY p. 1546)

| Taxable income (TND) | Tax rate | Effective rate on higher amount |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 5,000 | 0% | 0% |
| 5,001 – 10,000 | 15% | 7.50% |
| 10,001 – 20,000 | 25% | 16.25% |
| 20,001 – 30,000 | 30% | 20.83% |
| 30,001 – 40,000 | 33% | 23.88% |
| 40,001 – 50,000 | 36% | 26.30% |
| 50,001 – 70,000 | 38% | 29.64% |
| Above 70,000 | 40% | — |

**Social Contribution of Solidarity (EY p. 1547):** An additional tax equal to the difference between (a) PIT calculated at the progressive scale plus 0.5 percentage points per bracket, and (b) standard PIT. In practice this adds approximately 0.5%–1% to the effective rate on each bracket. Exemption: net annual income ≤ TND 5,000 after allowances (from 2020 onward).

**Standard deductions / allowances (EY pp. 1545–1546):**
- Social security contributions (CNSS): mandatory contributions withheld by employer are fully deductible
- Professional expenses: 10% of balance of income after deducting social contributions, with a threshold of TND 2,000 (for income realized from 1 January 2017); also: after deducting annuities and mandatory social contributions
- Life insurance and capitalization policy premiums: up to TND 100,000/year (minimum tax applicable)
- Savings accounts in shares (comptes épargne en actions): up to TND 100,000/year (minimum tax applicable)
- Interest on special savings accounts / Caisse d'Epargne / bond loans: up to TND 10,000/year (max TND 6,000 for regular savings)
- Family allowances: TND 300 for head of household + TND 100 per dependent child under 20 (up to 4 children) + TND 2,000 for disabled children + TND 1,000 per child at university without scholarship (max 5% of net income per dependent parent, up to TND 450 combined)
- Mortgage interest / construction loan interest: deductible if property acquisition/construction cost ≤ TND 200,000 and taxpayer had no home at acquisition date
- Principal and interest on university loans: deductible
- Mandatory annuities / alimony paid free of charge: deductible
- Foreign-source pensions not repatriated: 25% excluded from taxable income (80% if repatriated)

**Notes:**
- Capital gains on listed securities acquired before 1 January 2011 (or acquired after and sold after end of fiscal year of acquisition/subscription): 0%
- Capital gains on listed securities sold before end of fiscal year of acquisition: 10%
- Capital gains on unlisted securities: 10% (after year-end) or 20% (before year-end)
- Real property capital gains: 15% if held ≤ 5 years; 10% if held > 5 years; inherited property: 10%; withholding 2.5% at transfer
- Dividends: 10% final withholding (25% for low-tax jurisdiction residents)
- Interest income: 20% final withholding (25% for low-tax jurisdiction residents)

## Social Security

Employees pay CNSS (Caisse Nationale de Sécurité Sociale) contributions on their salaries (EY p. 1548):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| CNSS (all social security) | **9.68%** | **17.07%** | On gross salary; no ceiling stated |
| Work accident contribution | — | 0.4%–4% | Employer only; varies by activity sector |
| **Total** | **9.68%** | **17.07%–21.07%** | |

**Additional (2025 Finance Act):** 0.5% increase in employer contributions for establishment of Insurance Fund Against Job Loss for Economic Reasons (already included in the 17.07% employer rate above — EY p. 1549)

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling on wages subject to social security contributions (EY p. 1548)

**Notes:**
- CNSS contributions fully deductible from employee's taxable income
- Foreign assignees may be exempt if their home country has a totalization agreement with Tunisia and a certificate of coverage is obtained
- Foreign nationals (non-residents prior to assignment) may opt for a different social security regime if assigned to petroleum companies, offshore business parks, international trading companies, or financial/banking organizations (EY p. 1549)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Liberal Profession / Independent Activity (General Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Independent professionals (lawyers, doctors, accountants, consultants, etc.) and individuals engaged in commercial or industrial activities
- **Tax base:** Net income = gross revenue less deductible professional expenses; professional expense deduction is 10% of net income after social contributions (threshold TND 2,000 minimum)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT rates 0%–40%; advance tax payments quarterly; final return by relevant deadline
- **Social security:** Contributions to social security funds by independent self-employed are deductible (EY p. 1546); CNSS for independent workers applies at different rates (confirm current self-employed CNSS rate)
- **VAT obligations:** Professional/commercial turnover subject to VAT; standard rate 19%; reduced rates 7% and 13% for certain goods/services
- **Key limits:** Annual accounts required; records must comply with accounting legislation

### Option B — Régime Forfaitaire (Small Traders / Smallholders)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals meeting all of: unstable income; engaged in small businesses, craftsmen, or itinerant traders; no permanent premises; activity started 1 January 2019 (or spontaneous declaration filed on/after 1 January 2019); within competent tax authority territory (EY pp. 1543–1544)
- **Tax base:** Fixed amount (forfait) regardless of actual income
- **Rate / mechanism:** TND 200/year in communal areas; TND 100/year in other areas — paid over a three-year period
- **Social security:** Reduced / simplified
- **VAT obligations:** Exempt
- **Key limits:** Three-year fixed assessment period; not available in financial sector, energy (except renewables), mining, real estate development, on-site consumption, trade, or telecommunications

### Option C — New Enterprise Tax Holiday (Self-Employed)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals who create new enterprises and obtain investment declaration certificate during 2024 or 2025; activity must commence within 2 years; records must comply with accounting law
- **Tax benefit (degressive):**
  - Year 1: 100% deduction from tax base
  - Year 2: 75% deduction
  - Year 3: 50% deduction
  - Year 4: 25% deduction
- **Duration:** Four years from effective start of activity (2024 Finance Act renewal, EY p. 1547–1548)
- **Excluded sectors:** Financial sector, energy (except renewables), mining, real estate development, on-site consumption, trade, telecommunications
- **Key limits:** Must keep regular accounting books; must start activity within 2 years of certificate date

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Non-Resident / Short-Term Employee

- **Eligibility:** Non-resident employees working in Tunisia for less than 6 continuous or non-continuous months in a calendar year
- **Benefit:** Flat 20% tax on gross Tunisian-source salary (instead of progressive rates)
- **Duration:** Per tax year; year-by-year assessment
- **Caveats:** Oil/gas, mining, offshore banking, export-sector employees: also eligible for 20% flat rate even if >6 months (with prior approval from competent authorities)

### French Citizens — Special Treaty Provision

- French citizens residing in Tunisia for ≥ 3 years under the Tunisia/France Residence and Work Treaty (17 March 1988) may automatically obtain residence and work permits valid for 10 years. (EY p. 1552)

## 2026 Changes

- Tax brackets updated effective 1 January 2025 (EY p. 1546); applicable for 2025 income year filed in 2026
- **2025 Finance Act:** Additional 0.5% employer social contribution for Insurance Fund Against Job Loss for Economic Reasons (EY p. 1549)
- **2024 Finance Act (Article 27):** Renewed four-year PIT holiday for new self-employed enterprises obtaining investment certificates in 2024 or 2025 (EY p. 1547–1548)
- **2023 Finance Act:** Eliminated TND 10,000/year capital gains deduction on securities disposals (EY p. 1545)
- No further announced 2026 rate changes at EY data date

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **CNSS self-employed rate:** EY states employee 9.68% and employer 17.07% but does not specify the self-employed (independent) CNSS contribution rate; estimated at 7%–10%; verify with CNSS Tunisia
- [ ] **Social Contribution of Solidarity exact computation:** Complex formula (PIT at scale + 0.5pp vs standard PIT); not separately itemized in effective rate table
- [ ] **Professional expenses for employed individuals:** 10% is the standard deduction; higher actual expenses may be deductible if documented
- [ ] **VAT threshold for self-employed:** Current annual turnover threshold for VAT registration not specified in EY; verify with tax authority
- [ ] **2026 bracket update:** Brackets shown are effective from 1 January 2025; any 2026 Finance Act changes not captured at EY data date
- [ ] **Regime forfaitaire thresholds:** TND 200/year and TND 100/year are fixed amounts — essentially symbolic; full details of qualifying conditions need verification with tax authority
