## Personal Income Tax

Territorial system: both resident and nonresident individuals taxed on Guatemalan-source income only. Tax year = 1 January – 31 December. Filing within 3 months after tax year end (31 March). (EY p.545, p.548)

**Residency:** Resident if present >183 days in a calendar year; center of economic interests in Guatemala; Guatemalan diplomat with residence abroad; Guatemalan employed abroad by a private entity for <183 days/year; foreign diplomat on assignment (unless reciprocity). (EY p.545)

**Employment income:** Wages and all types of remuneration or payments received in cash by employed resident individuals. Annual employment income taxes at progressive rates below. (EY p.545)

**Progressive PIT brackets — Employment (annual, 2025)** (EY p.547)

| Annual taxable income (GTQ) | Tax on lower amount | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 300,000 | GTQ 0 | 5% |
| Above 300,000 | GTQ 15,000 | 7% |

**GTQ/USD exchange rate as of 24 September 2025:** GTQ 7.53683 = USD 1. (EY p.547)

**Personal deduction:** GTQ 48,000 (approximately USD 6,167.42) deducted from taxable income before applying rates — no documentation required. (EY p.546)

**Additional deductible personal expenses:** (EY p.546–547)
- Social security contributions
- Life insurance premiums
- Exempt income (special bonus and Christmas bonus)
- Charitable contributions (max annual deduction 5% of net income)
- Maximum GTQ 12,000 (approximately USD 1,541.86) of VAT paid during the tax year

**Self-employment income — two regime options:** (EY p.545–546)
1. **Regime on Profits from Business Activities:** Net income basis (authorized expenses deductible). Net taxable income taxed at 25%. PLUS 1% Solidarity Tax on gross income.
2. **Optional Simplified Regime on Revenue from Business Activities:** Gross income basis (no deductions allowed). Final withholding rates: 5% on first GTQ 30,000 of monthly taxable income (approximately USD 3,882); 7% on amount exceeding GTQ 30,000/month.

**Other income types:**
- Dividends: 5% withholding tax regardless of beneficiary's country of residence. (EY p.546)
- Interest paid to residents: 10% final withholding. Exempt: interest paid by local taxpayers to local banks; interest paid to non-domiciled first-order banks (criteria set by tax authorities; private letter ruling advisable). (EY p.546)
- Royalties paid to nonresidents: 15% withholding. (EY p.546)
- Directors' fees (nonresident): 15% final withholding. Resident directors' fees: considered self-employment income, taxed at ordinary rates. (EY p.546)
- Capital gains: flat 10% on gains from movable assets, immovable assets, lottery winnings; also on royalties, leasing/subleasing income, gains from shares, and income from lotteries/raffles. (EY p.546)
- Nonresidents: 15% final withholding on salaries, fees, commissions and allowances. Foreign individuals rendering local services >183 days: treated as self-employed residents. (EY p.547)

**Notes:**
- Inheritance tax: 0%–7% for bequests/gifts from death to spouse, ascendants, descendants; 3%–12% for relatives within second, third, fourth degree of consanguinity; 9%–25% for relatives and unrelated parties. VAT 12% on inter vivos gifts. (EY p.547)
- Self-employed may not carry forward losses. (EY p.547)
- No treaty with any country currently in effect (Mexico treaty signed but not yet approved by Congress). (EY p.548)
- VAT: 12% on services rendered in Guatemala applies for foreign individuals rendering local services >183 days. (EY p.547)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Regime on Profits from Business Activities
- **Who qualifies:** Resident and nonresident individuals deriving self-employment or business income from Guatemalan sources.
- **Tax base:** Net taxable income = gross income minus authorized deductible business expenses. Solidarity Tax: 1% on gross income (separate levy). (EY p.546)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat 25% on net taxable income. Plus 1% Solidarity Tax on gross income.
- **Social security:** Self-employed SS rates not specified.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT 12% on services; must register and file monthly VAT returns. (EY p.548)
- **Key limits:** May not combine with Simplified Regime for same activity.

### Option B — Optional Simplified Regime on Revenue from Business Activities
- **Who qualifies:** Resident and nonresident individuals deriving self-employment or business income from Guatemalan sources; also foreign individuals rendering local services >183 days.
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus exempt income (no expense deductions allowed).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Final withholding rates: 5% on first GTQ 30,000/month of taxable income; 7% on excess over GTQ 30,000/month.
- **Social security:** Self-employed SS rates not specified.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT 12% on services; must register and file monthly VAT returns.
- **Key limits:** No deductions allowed; simpler compliance. (EY p.546)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Temporary Residence for Workers with Foreign Employers (Digital Nomad)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals employed by a company located outside Guatemala; must demonstrate employment with foreign employer; monthly income ≥USD 2,000 (or USD 3,000 if applying with dependants); valid passport; no criminal record; health insurance.
- **Benefit:** Legal authorization to reside and work remotely in Guatemala for up to 5 years; can renew.
- **Duration:** Up to 5 years (temporary residence permit).
- **Caveats:** Cannot work for Guatemalan-based companies/clients; must maintain foreign employment relationship. (EY p.550)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants.
> EUR/GTQ rate: 1 EUR ≈ GTQ 8.14 (derived from GTQ/USD 7.537 and EUR/USD ≈ 1.08).
> "Employment" = gross annual salary; employee SS 4.83% deducted; GTQ 48,000 personal deduction applied; then progressive employment rates.
> "B2B Simplified" = gross monthly revenue × 12; Optional Simplified Regime rates 5%/7%.
> "B2B Profits Regime" = net profit (assuming 80% net margin); 25% flat + 1% Solidarity Tax on gross.

**EUR to GTQ conversions:**
- €30,000 = GTQ 244,200
- €60,000 = GTQ 488,400
- €100,000 = GTQ 814,000

| Total PIT | | GTQ 9,810 |

| **Effective rate** | **21,605 / 244,200** | **~8.8%** |

**Working — GTQ 488,400 Employment:**
- SS: 4.83% × 488,400 = GTQ 23,590
- PIT base: 488,400 − 48,000 = GTQ 440,400
- Tax: 5% × 300,000 + 7% × (440,400 − 300,000) = 15,000 + 7% × 140,400 = 15,000 + 9,828 = GTQ 24,828

**Working — GTQ 488,400 B2B Simplified:**
- Monthly: 488,400 / 12 = GTQ 40,700/month
- Tax: 5% × 30,000 + 7% × (40,700 − 30,000) = 1,500 + 749 = GTQ 2,249/month × 12 = GTQ 26,988

**Working — GTQ 814,000 B2B Simplified:**
- Monthly: 814,000 / 12 = GTQ 67,833/month
- Tax: 5% × 30,000 + 7% × 37,833 = 1,500 + 2,648 = GTQ 4,148/month × 12 = GTQ 49,776

**Key insight:** Guatemala has extremely low effective tax rates compared to European countries, particularly for self-employed under the Simplified Regime (5%–7% gross receipts tax). Even employment PIT is low due to the large zero-rate band (GTQ 48,000 personal deduction) and modest top rate (7%). The Profits Regime (25% flat on net) is competitive with the Simplified Regime for high-expense businesses.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/GTQ ≈ 8.14 (derived from GTQ/USD 7.537 × EUR/USD 1.08; floating rate)
- Employment: employee SS 4.83%; GTQ 48,000 personal deduction; no other deductions modeled
- B2B Simplified: no expense deductions; 5% on first GTQ 30,000/month, 7% on excess; no SS modeled
- B2B Profits Regime not shown in table (25% on net profit + 1% Solidarity Tax; varies by profit margin)
- VAT (12%) obligations for self-employed not included in effective rate
