## Personal Income Tax

Territorial system — both resident and nonresident individuals are taxed on Guatemalan-source income only. Foreign-source income is not taxed. Residency: > 183 days in a calendar year (not necessarily continuous). Tax year: 1 January to 31 December. (EY pp.545–548)

**Employment income — annual progressive rates:** (EY p.547)

| Annual taxable income — Exceeding (GTQ) | Not exceeding (GTQ) | Tax on lower amount (GTQ) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 300,000 | 0 | 5% |
| 300,000 | — | 15,000 | 7% |

**Exchange rate:** GTQ 7.53683 = USD 1 (as of 24 September 2025). (EY p.547)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY pp.546–547)
- Personal deduction: GTQ 48,000/year (≈ USD 6,167.42) — no documentation required
- Social security contributions: deductible
- Life insurance premiums: deductible
- Exempt income: special bonuses and Christmas bonus
- Charitable contributions: up to 5% of net income
- VAT paid during the year: up to GTQ 12,000 (≈ USD 1,541.86)

**Notes:**
- Dividends: 5% withholding tax regardless of beneficiary's country of residence (EY p.546)
- Interest to residents and nonresidents: 10% final withholding (with exemptions for bank-to-bank interest) (EY p.546)
- Royalties to nonresidents: 15% withholding (EY p.546)
- Capital gains: flat 10% tax rate; applies to movable assets, immovable assets, lottery winnings, royalties to residents, leasing income (when not ordinary business), share transfers (EY p.546)
- Nonresident individuals: flat 15% withholding on salaries, fees, commissions and allowances (final) (EY p.547)
- Foreign individuals providing local services > 183 days must register as self-employed and pay taxes under one of the self-employment regimes, plus 12% VAT on services (EY p.547)
- No loss carryforward for self-employed (EY p.547)
- No tax treaties in effect (Guatemala signed a treaty with Mexico but Congress has not yet approved it) (EY p.548)

## Social Security (IGSS — Instituto Guatemalteco de Seguridad Social)

Levied on salaries, wages and all types of remuneration. No ceiling on earnings subject to SS contributions. (EY p.547)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| IGSS (all contributions) | 4.83% | 12.67% | N/A specified |
| **Total** | **4.83%** | **12.67%** | — |

**Income ceiling:** No limits imposed on the amount of earnings subject to SS contributions.

**Notes:**
- Employee SS (4.83%) is deductible from PIT base
- Self-employed SS treatment not specified in EY source for individual professionals

## Self-Employment Regimes

Guatemala offers **two optional regimes** for self-employed and business income: (EY pp.545–546)

### Option A — Regime on Profits from Business Activities (Régimen sobre las Utilidades de Actividades Lucrativas)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual (resident or nonresident) with self-employment or business income from trade or occasional activity.
- **Tax base:** **Net income** (gross revenue minus authorized deductible expenses).
- **Rate / mechanism:** **25%** on net taxable income. Plus **1% Solidarity Tax** (Impuesto de Solidaridad) on net income.
- **Social security:** SS applies.
- **VAT obligations:** 12% IVA on services; monthly filing required. Monthly or quarterly income tax reporting depending on regime elected. Must issue invoices.
- **Key limits:** Full expense deductibility (with limits on certain items); no revenue ceiling; capital gains taxed separately at 10% flat.

### Option B — Optional Simplified Regime on Revenue from Business Activities (Régimen Opcional Simplificado sobre Ingresos de Actividades Lucrativas)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual with self-employment or business income who elects this regime (available to all qualifying self-employed).
- **Tax base:** **Gross income** (no deductions allowed) minus only the exempt income amount.
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - **5%** on first GTQ 30,000/month of taxable income (≈ USD 3,882/month)
  - **7%** on amount exceeding GTQ 30,000/month
- **Social security:** SS applies.
- **VAT obligations:** 12% IVA on services; monthly VAT returns required; monthly or quarterly income tax reporting.
- **Key limits:** No expense deductions; no revenue ceiling; simple calculation but potentially higher effective rate at high gross revenues.

**Comparison of the two self-employment regimes:**

| Feature | Option A (Net profit, 25%) | Option B (Gross revenue, 5%/7%) |
|---|---|---|
| Tax base | Net income | Gross income |
| Expense deduction | Yes (full) | No |
| Top marginal rate | 25% + 1% solidarity | 7% on excess over GTQ 30k/month |
| Better for | High-margin professionals (low expenses) | Lower-margin operators; simplicity |
| Documentation | Full accounting records required | Minimal records required |

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Digital Nomad / Remote Worker Temporary Residence
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals employed by a company outside Guatemala.
- **Benefit:** "Temporary residence for workers with foreign employers" permit — allows eligible foreigners to reside and work remotely in Guatemala for up to **5 years**.
- **Income requirement:** Monthly income of at least USD 2,000 (or USD 3,000 if applying with dependants).
- **Conditions:** Employed by a company located outside Guatemala; proof of employment; valid passport; criminal record certificates; certification of last entry to Guatemala; valid health insurance.
- **Tax treatment:** Under the territorial principle, income from foreign employer for remote work is NOT Guatemalan-source income → **not subject to Guatemala income tax**.
- **Caveats:** If the individual provides local services to Guatemalan clients, that income becomes Guatemalan-source and taxable. The permit category was introduced relatively recently. (EY p.550)

### Territorial Taxation (General)
- **Eligibility:** All individuals.
- **Benefit:** Only Guatemalan-source income taxed. All foreign-source income completely exempt.
- **Duration:** Permanent feature of the tax system.
- **Caveats:** Foreign individuals > 183 days providing local services must register and pay Guatemalan taxes. (EY pp.545, 547)

**Key insight:** Guatemala has very low effective rates — among the lowest in Central America. The 5%/7% simplified regime for self-employed is particularly attractive. Employment effective rates of ~9–10% are also very low due to the 5%/7% progressive structure with the GTQ 48,000 personal deduction. The territorial system means all foreign-source income is completely tax-free.

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR ≈ GTQ 8.29 (approximate May 2026)
- IGSS employee: 4.83% (no ceiling)
- Personal deduction: GTQ 48,000/year
- SS deductible from PIT base for employment
- B2B Option A: 30% expenses; 25% + 1% solidarity = 26% effective on net
- B2B Option B: 5% on first GTQ 30,000/month, 7% on excess; no SS modeled (not specified for self-employed)
- No VAT (12% IVA) shown — pass-through for self-employed

- [ ] **1% Solidarity Tax:** Applies to Option A (net profit regime); verify current applicability — has been suspended/modified in some years.
