## Personal Income Tax

> Belgium has a progressive personal income tax (PIT) system. The tax year follows the calendar year. Residents are taxed on worldwide income; non-residents on Belgian-source income. Filing is annual (tax return due ~June/July of the following year). Withholding (précompte professionnel / bedrijfsvoorheffing) is deducted monthly by employers.

| Taxable income (EUR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 15,820 | 25% |
| 15,820 – 27,920 | 40% |
| 27,920 – 48,320 | 45% |
| 48,320+ | 50% |

*Brackets are for 2025 income (indexed annually). (EY p. 130)*

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal allowance (tax-free minimum): approximately €10,160 for a single person (2025, indexed)
- Professional costs: flat-rate deduction of 30% of gross professional income, capped at approximately €5,520 (2025); OR actual costs if higher
- Employee social security contributions (13.07%) are fully deductible from gross salary before applying PIT brackets
- Additional municipal surcharges (communale opcentiemen / centimes additionnels): typically 6–9% of federal tax due, varying by municipality (average ~7%)

**Notes:**
- Tax-free minimum is increased for dependants (children, disabled spouse, etc.)
- Municipal tax is levied on top of federal tax and varies by municipality
- Non-residents pay the same rate scale but do not benefit from the personal allowance unless ≥75% of income is Belgian-sourced
- Crisis surcharge (bijzondere bijdrage sociale zekerheid): additional social security contribution on higher salaries

## Social Security

> Employees pay 13.07% of gross salary (no ceiling for most contributions). Employer contribution is approximately 25% (including various levies). Self-employed pay contributions based on net professional income.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Health & invalidity (sickness/disability) | 3.55% | ~8.86% | included in flat rate |
| Pension | 7.50% | ~8.86% | included in flat rate |
| Unemployment | 0.87% | ~included | not applicable |
| Other (accidents, family, etc.) | 1.15% | ~7.28% | — |
| **Total** | **13.07%** | **~25%** | **~20.5%** |

**Income ceiling:** No general ceiling for employee social security (13.07% applies to full gross salary)
**Notes:**
- Self-employed: quarterly provisional contributions based on net professional income from 3 years prior; effective rate approximately 20.5% on net income (lower rate on income tranches above ~€69,000)
- Special social security contribution (bijzondere bijdrage): additional levy on household income €18,592–€81,000, capped at €731.28/year (employee share) (EY p. 132)
- Crisis levy (cotisation spéciale de sécurité sociale): 0% to max ~€731 depending on salary band

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals in Belgium can operate as sole traders or through a company. As a sole trader, income is taxed as personal income at progressive PIT rates. Operating through a company (SRL/BV) allows salary/dividend splitting.

### Option A — Sole Trader (Zelfstandige / Indépendant)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying out professional activity in own name
- **Tax base:** Net professional income (revenue minus business expenses) taxed at progressive PIT rates (25–50%) plus municipal tax
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same bracket table as employment income (25/40/45/50%); professional expenses fully deductible (actual costs)
- **Social security:** ~20.5% on net professional income (quarterly contributions to social insurance fund); minimum annual contribution ~€916 (2025)
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory registration above €25,000 annual turnover; standard VAT rate 21%
- **Key limits:** No flat-rate expense deduction available for self-employed (actual costs only, unlike the 30% flat rate for employees); professional costs must be documented

### Option B — Small Company (SRL/BV — Société à Responsabilité Limitée)
- **Who qualifies:** Any entrepreneur; minimum share capital not required for SRL
- **Tax base:** Company profits taxed at 20% CIT (on first €100,000) or 25% standard CIT; director salary deductible from company
- **Rate / mechanism:** Director takes salary (taxed at progressive PIT) + dividends (30% withholding tax); overall effective tax can be optimised
- **Social security:** Director must pay self-employed social security (~20.5%) on professional income drawn
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT rules apply; 21% standard rate
- **Key limits:** Must draw minimum salary of €45,000 (approx.) from the company to qualify for 20% reduced CIT rate (small company status)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Special Tax Regime for Foreign Executives (Expat Tax Regime — New Rules from 2022)
- **Eligibility:** Employees recruited abroad OR seconded to Belgium by a foreign group company; must not have been Belgian tax resident for 60 months prior; minimum gross annual salary €75,000; must be recruited/seconded to Belgium as of 1 January 2022 onwards
- **Benefit:** 30% of gross salary treated as non-taxable cost reimbursement (tax-free allowance, capped at €90,000/year); certain additional allowances for school fees and housing can be paid tax-free
- **Duration:** 5 years, extendable by 3 years (total maximum 8 years)
- **Application deadline:** Application must be filed within 3 months of first working day in Belgium
- **Caveats:** Must remain non-resident for Belgian tax purposes (or resident but special status maintained); minimum salary threshold strictly enforced; replaces the former "special status" regime (abolished for new cases from 2022)

## 2026 Changes

> Belgium underwent a significant tax reform in 2024 ("Arizona" coalition agreement); various measures are being phased in for 2025–2026. (EY pp. 128–145)

- The tax reform bill ("Wet Diverse Bepalingen") includes lowering the 40% bracket threshold and widening lower brackets — details still being finalised for 2026 implementation
- Work bonus (werkbonus / bonus à l'emploi) for low-income earners has been enhanced
- Increased personal allowance for single-income families under consideration
- Flat-rate professional cost deduction ceiling indexed annually

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (flat-rate professional costs at 30% capped). Municipal tax assumed at 7% of federal tax.
> "Employment" = gross salary. "B2B" = sole trader net invoiced revenue (after 20.5% social security, professional expenses minimal — using 10% actual costs assumption).

**Employment at €30,000:**
1. Employee social security: €30,000 × 13.07% = €3,921 → taxable income = €26,079
2. Professional costs (30% flat rate, capped): €26,079 × 30% = €7,824 → net taxable = €18,255
3. Less personal allowance ~€10,160 → taxable after allowance = €8,095
4. PIT on €8,095: €8,095 × 25% = €2,024
5. Municipal tax: €2,024 × 7% = €142
6. Total tax = €2,166; total burden = €2,166 + €3,921 SS = €6,087
7. Effective rate = €6,087 / €30,000 = **20.3%**

**Employment at €60,000:**
1. SS: €60,000 × 13.07% = €7,842 → taxable = €52,158
2. Prof. costs flat rate: capped at ~€5,520 → net taxable = €46,638
3. Less personal allowance ~€10,160 → €36,478
4. PIT: €15,820 × 25% = €3,955; next €12,100 × 40% = €4,840; next €8,558 × 45% = €3,851 → total PIT = €12,646
5. Municipal tax: €12,646 × 7% = €885
6. Total tax = €13,531; total burden = €13,531 + €7,842 = €21,373
7. Effective rate = €21,373 / €60,000 = **35.6%**

**Employment at €100,000:**
1. SS: €100,000 × 13.07% = €13,070 → taxable = €86,930
2. Prof. costs capped at ~€5,520 → net taxable = €81,410
3. Less personal allowance ~€10,160 → €71,250
4. PIT: €15,820 × 25% = €3,955; €12,100 × 40% = €4,840; €20,400 × 45% = €9,180; €22,930 × 50% = €11,465 → PIT = €29,440
5. Municipal: €29,440 × 7% = €2,061
6. Total tax = €31,501; total burden = €31,501 + €13,070 = €44,571
7. Effective rate = €44,571 / €100,000 = **44.6%**

**B2B (sole trader) at €30,000:**
1. Social security: ~€30,000 × 20.5% = €6,150 → net = €23,850
2. Assume 10% business costs: taxable = €21,465
3. Less personal allowance ~€10,160 → taxable = €11,305
4. PIT: €11,305 × 25% = €2,826; municipal €2,826 × 7% = €198 → total tax = €3,024
5. Total burden = €3,024 + €6,150 = €9,174
6. Effective rate = €9,174 / €30,000 = **30.6%**

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €2,166 | €3,921 | €6,087 | **20.3%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | €3,024 | €6,150 | €9,174 | **30.6%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €13,531 | €7,842 | €21,373 | **35.6%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | ~€11,500 | €12,300 | ~€23,800 | **~39.7%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €31,501 | €13,070 | €44,571 | **44.6%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€22,000 | €20,500 | ~€42,500 | **~42.5%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants, Belgian resident
- Municipal tax at average 7% of federal tax
- Employment: 30% flat-rate professional cost deduction (capped at ~€5,520); personal allowance ~€10,160
- B2B: 20.5% self-employed social security; 10% actual business costs assumed; actual professional expense deduction
- Brackets indexed to 2025 values; 2026 indexation expected to shift thresholds slightly upward
- "Special social security contribution" (bijzondere bijdrage) not included above for simplicity
