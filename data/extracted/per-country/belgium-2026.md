## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PIT on worldwide income for residents; Belgian-source income for non-residents. Tax year = calendar year (income year). Tax return filed by 15 July (residents) or before November (non-residents). (EY p.140)

The overall tax due is split into a **federal base** and a **regional surcharge** (Brussels, Flemish, Walloon regions). Municipal (commune) taxes are levied at up to 9% of income tax due (2025 income year). (EY pp.128–129)

**Federal PIT rates (2025 income year / 2026 tax year):**

| Taxable income (EUR) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|
| 0 – 16,310* | **25%** |
| 16,310 – 28,790 | **40%** |
| 28,790 – 49,820 | **45%** |
| 49,820+ | **50%** |

*This bracket applies to amounts exceeding the taxpayer's tax-free amount. The standard personal allowance is EUR 10,910 per person (2025 income year). (EY p.134–135)

**Municipal (commune) surcharge:** Up to **9%** of federal income tax (2025 income year; varies by municipality). (EY p.135)

**Non-residents:** Same progressive rates as residents; personal allowances available only if at least 75% of professional income is earned in Belgium. Additional federal tax of 7% of income tax applies (instead of municipal tax). (EY p.135)

**Special flat rates (selected income types):**

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (withholding tax) | **30%** |
| Interest (general; withholding) | **30%** |
| Interest on regulated savings accounts (above exempt amount) | **15%** |
| Dividends exempt annual amount | EUR 859 (2025 income year) |
| Capital gains on financial assets (shares, crypto, foreign currencies, insurance contracts) — new from 1 Jan 2026 | **10%** (draft law; annual exemption EUR 10,000) |
| Capital gains from speculation / outside normal management | **33%** |
| Capital gains — significant shareholding (≥ 20%) to non-EEA company | **16.5%** |
| Royalties / copyright income (up to certain amount) | **15%** |
| Severance payments | Average rate applicable in last normal activity year |
| Holiday pay | Average rate applicable in payment year |
| Life insurance / group insurance lump sums | **10% or 16.5%** |
| Miscellaneous occasional benefits (certain capital gains, prizes) | **16.5% or 33%** |

(EY pp.133–136)

**Personal allowances (2025 income year / 2026 tax year):**

| Allowance | Amount (EUR) |
|---|---|
| Standard personal allowance | **10,910** |
| Basic allowance for pensioners (over 60, extended — EY region-specific) | varies |
| Dependent spouse income attribution (max) | 13,460 |
| Dependent children (1 child) | 1,980 |
| Dependent children (2 children) | 5,110 |
| Dependent children (3 children) | 11,440 |
| Dependent children (4 children) | 18,510 |
| Each additional child | 7,070 |

(EY p.134)

**Professional expense deductions (employees):**
- Standard deduction: maximum **EUR 5,930** (2025 income year) — most employees use this
- Or actual expenses:
  - 75% of gasoline costs for professional car use
  - 75% of other automobile expenses (cars acquired before 1 January 2018)
  - Commuting by car: EUR 0.4449/km (EUR 0.36/km by bicycle for 2025)
  - 69% of restaurant expenses; 50% of other entertainment expenses in Belgium

(EY p.129)

**Directors' fees:** Included in total earned income; standard deduction of EUR 3,120 or actual expenses (uncapped) available. (EY p.129–130)

**Self-employment / business income:** Included in total earned income; actual professional expenses deductible; 2.25% tax surcharge applies if advance payments are insufficient. (EY p.130)

**Notes:**
- Losses from earned income may be offset against other earned income; carried forward indefinitely; no carryback; professional losses may not be deducted from other income types (EY p.137)
- No net worth tax in Belgium (EY p.137)
- Spouse/registered cohabitant income: if only one spouse earns income, 30% (max EUR 13,460) is attributed to non-earning spouse and taxed at lower brackets (EY p.135)

## Social Security

**Employees (white-collar, private sector, 2025):**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Basic social security | **13.07%** | **19.88%** (basic rate) |
| Total employer (average) | — | **~25%** (including specific contributions) |

- No ceiling on employee contributions (13.07% on all gross monthly compensation) (EY p.138)
- Employer 19.88% basic rate; actual average ~25% including sector-specific contributions
- Some employer contributions vary by parameters and sector; reductions available based on eligibility criteria
- Benefits in kind: company car — no employee SS; employer pays a CO2-based contribution

**Self-employed (2025 income year):**

| Quarterly net income (EUR) | SS rate |
|---|---|
| Up to EUR 73,447.52/year | **20.5%** |
| EUR 73,447.52 – EUR 108,238.40/year | **14.16%** |
| Above EUR 108,238.40/year | Fixed maximum annual contribution |

- Maximum annual contribution for self-employed: **EUR 20,443.85** (+ 3%–5% administration fees for the social insurance fund)
- Minimum annual contribution: **EUR 871.71**
- Self-employed SS contributions are **deductible** for income tax purposes (EY pp.138–139)

**Special social security contribution (2025):**
- Additional mandatory contribution (not deductible): maximum EUR 731.28/year; exact amount depends on salary and partner's situation (EY p.139)

**Coverage:** Healthcare, disability, unemployment allowances, family allowances, retirement and survivor's benefits. (EY p.139)

**Totalization agreements:** EU Regulation 883/2004 applies (plus EFTA countries). Bilateral agreements with: Algeria, Argentina, Australia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Brazil, Canada, Chile, Congo, India, Israel, Japan, Korea (South), Kosovo, Moldova, Montenegro, Morocco, North Macedonia, Philippines, San Marino, Serbia, Switzerland, Tunisia, Türkiye, US, Uruguay (EY pp.139–140)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed Natural Person (Sole Trader / Freelancer)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual conducting professional activities independently (not under employment contract); includes directors, freelancers, independent consultants
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue minus actual professional expenses (all expenses wholly and exclusively incurred for business); depreciation allowed
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive federal PIT (25%–50%) on net income, plus municipal surcharge up to 9%; standard personal allowance EUR 10,910 deducted first; 2.25% surcharge if advance payments insufficient
- **Social security:** 20.5% on net income up to EUR 73,447/year; 14.16% on EUR 73,447–108,238; max EUR 20,444/year; min EUR 872/year; SS deductible for income tax
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover ≥ EUR 25,000 (small business exemption below); standard VAT 21%
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; losses carried forward indefinitely

(EY pp.129–130, 138–139)

### Option B — Management Company / Company Structure

- **Who qualifies:** Professionals who incorporate (BVBA/SRL, NV/SA); draw salary and/or dividends
- **Tax base:** Salary: progressive PIT + SS; Dividends: 30% WHT (after corporate tax at 25% / 20% for small companies)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Corporate income tax at entity level; personal PIT on salary extracted; dividends 30% WHT
- **Social security:** Director SS at self-employed rates on salary drawn; management company director includes in professional income
- **Key limits:** VAT applies; significant compliance cost

(EY pp.129–130, 138)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### New Expatriate Tax Regime (from 1 January 2022)

- **Eligibility:** Inbound taxpayers and researchers who have not had a connection with Belgium during the 60-month period prior to the start of Belgian employment; not taxed as Belgian resident or non-resident on professional income; not living within 150 km of Belgian border in that 60-month period; minimum gross remuneration **EUR 75,000/year** (salary threshold reduced to **EUR 70,000** retroactively from 1 July 2025 per draft law — not yet voted at time of EY writing); researchers: master's degree in specified sciences + 10 years' experience (no salary threshold)
- **Benefit:**
  - **30%** of gross Belgian remuneration paid tax-free and SS-free as "costs proper to the employer" (up to a ceiling — currently EUR 90,000/year; ceiling to be abolished under draft law, with cap rising to 35%)
  - Tax-free reimbursement of: moving/relocation costs, school fees, education expenses for international schooling, accommodation (reasonable amounts)
  - New regime under draft law (3 July 2025): recurring expenses cap to rise to 35% (from 30%) with EUR 90,000 annual cap abolished
- **Duration:** 5 years from start of Belgian employment; 3-year extension possible (total up to 8 years)
- **Application deadline:** Joint application by employee and employer within 3 months of start of employment
- **Caveats:** Employee/director is a Belgian tax resident (no longer non-resident fiction); must provide tax residency certificate from another jurisdiction annually; salary threshold EUR 75,000 (being reduced to EUR 70,000); applicable to employment income only (not directors without employment relationship to the extent their director's fees don't include employment income) (EY pp.130–132)

## 2026 Changes

- **Capital gains tax on financial assets (1 January 2026 — draft law):** New 10% tax on capital gains realized by individuals on shares, crypto assets, foreign currencies, and insurance contracts. Annual exemption of EUR 10,000; only net capital gains exceeding this taxed. Significant shareholdings (≥ 20%) subject to progressive rates 1.25%–10% (first EUR 1 million exempt; EUR 1 million cap per 5-year reference period). Transfers to non-EEA entities taxed at 16.5%. **Note: This was a draft law at time of EY writing, not yet voted on.** (EY pp.133–134)
- **New expat regime salary threshold reduction (retroactive from 1 July 2025 — draft law):** Threshold reduced from EUR 75,000 to EUR 70,000; recurring cost cap rising from 30% to 35% with EUR 90,000 annual ceiling abolished. **Note: draft law, not yet voted on.** (EY p.132)
- **Stock exchange transaction tax:** 0.35% remains in force for purchases/sales by Belgian residents; max EUR 1,600 per transaction (EY p.138)
- **Tax on securities accounts:** 0.15% annual tax on qualifying financial instruments in accounts with average value > EUR 1 million remains in force (EY p.138)
- **NIS insurable earnings / SS rates for 2026:** No specific 2026 changes announced; 2025 self-employed rates apply

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (standard professional expense deduction EUR 5,930; personal allowance EUR 10,910).
> Belgium uses EUR; no currency conversion needed.
> Employment scenario: federal PIT (25%–50%) + municipal surcharge (assumed 7% average); employee SS 13.07% (no ceiling).
> B2B scenario: self-employed sole trader; SS deducted before income tax; 20.5%/14.16% SS; actual expenses assumed at standard deduction equivalent.
> Expatriate scenario: 30% cost reimbursement tax-free + SS-free (current regime).

### €30,000 Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross income | EUR 30,000 | |
| Employee SS (13.07%) | 13.07% × 30,000 | EUR 3,921 |
| After SS | 30,000 − 3,921 | EUR 26,079 |
| Professional expense deduction (max EUR 5,930) | EUR 5,930 | |
| Personal allowance | EUR 10,910 | |
| Taxable income | 26,079 − 5,930 − 10,910 | EUR 9,239 |
| Federal PIT (25% on EUR 9,239) | 25% × 9,239 | EUR 2,310 |
| Municipal surcharge (7% × 2,310) | 7% × 2,310 | EUR 162 |
| Total PIT + municipal | 2,310 + 162 | EUR 2,472 |
| **Total burden** | 3,921 + 2,472 | **EUR 6,393** |
| **Effective rate** | 6,393 / 30,000 | **21.3%** |

### €30,000 B2B — Self-employed

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue | EUR 30,000 | |
| Self-employed SS (20.5% on net; iterative — approx.) | ~20.5% × 24,070 (net est.) | EUR 4,934 |
| SS deductible from income | EUR 4,934 | |
| Professional expenses (EUR 5,930 equivalent) | EUR 5,930 | |
| Personal allowance | EUR 10,910 | |
| Taxable income | 30,000 − 4,934 − 5,930 − 10,910 | EUR 8,226 |

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Capital gains tax (1 January 2026):** 10% CGT on financial assets was a draft law at time of EY writing (not yet voted on); confirm final passage and exact scope at minfin.fgov.be
- [ ] **New expat regime salary threshold reduction to EUR 70,000:** Draft law (retroactive from 1 July 2025) not yet voted on at time of EY writing; confirm at minfin.fgov.be
- [ ] **Municipal surcharge:** Exact rate varies by municipality (Brussels ~9%; others lower); 7% used as approximate average — verify for specific location
- [ ] **Regional differences:** Belgian regions (Brussels, Flanders, Wallonia) have separate regional tax deductions, surcharges, and conditions; these significantly affect total tax burden — professional advice required for accurate regional calculation
- [ ] **Special social security contribution:** EUR 731.28/year maximum; exact amount depends on salary and partner situation; not modelled in standard scenarios
- [ ] **Self-employed SS iterative calculation:** SS base is net income (after SS), creating a circular reference; approximate rate used; exact calculation requires iteration
- [ ] **VAT small business threshold:** EUR 25,000/year for exemption; most B2B service providers above this level must register for 21% VAT
- [ ] **Copyright / royalty regime:** New scope restrictions from 1 January 2023 significantly narrowed eligible income; not modelled
