## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for tax residents. Tax year = calendar year; Singapore operates on a **preceding-year basis** — tax for the 2026 year of assessment (YA 2026) is levied on income earned in the 2025 calendar year. Returns due by 18 April of the year of assessment. The Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS) provides a No-Filing Service for many salaried employees.

**Territorial system:** Foreign-source income received in Singapore by **resident individuals** is generally exempt from tax (subject to conditions). Foreign-source dividends, foreign branch profits and foreign-source service income received through partnerships may qualify for exemption if prescribed conditions are met. This makes Singapore attractive for global professionals — only Singapore-sourced income is typically taxable. (EY p.1367)

**Non-resident individuals** employed for not more than 60 days in a calendar year are exempt from tax on employment income derived from Singapore. Non-residents are generally taxed at 24% on trade/business/professional income, or the greater of 15% and the resident rate on employment income. (EY p.1372)

**Notes:**
- Capital gains are not taxed in Singapore. However, gains from trading in real estate, stocks or shares may be treated as trading income and taxed if the tax authorities determine it constitutes a trade. (EY p.1369)
- Dividends from Singapore-resident companies under the one-tier system are exempt from income tax in shareholders' hands. (EY p.1368)
- Interest from standard savings/current/fixed deposits with approved banks and interest from debt securities are exempt from income tax. (EY p.1368)
- No inheritance tax or estate duty for deaths on or after 15 February 2008. No gift tax. (EY p.1373)
- Net rental income is aggregated with other income and taxed at progressive rates.
- Employer CPF contributions do **not** constitute taxable income for the employee. Employer contributions to provident/pension funds **outside** Singapore are taxable as income when paid. (EY p.1368)

## Social Security

### CPF (Central Provident Fund)

The CPF is a statutory savings scheme for old-age retirement, healthcare and housing. **Only Singapore citizens and permanent residents (PRs) are required to contribute.** Foreigners (including Malaysians) are **fully exempt** from CPF contributions and may not make voluntary contributions. (EY p.1373)

**CPF rates from 1 January 2026 (Singapore citizens and 3rd-year-onward PRs):**

| Age | Employer (%) | Employee (%) | Total (%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 55 and below | 17 | 20 | 37 |
| Above 55 to 60 | 16 | 18 | 34 |
| Above 60 to 65 | 12.5 | 12.5 | 25 |
| Above 65 to 70 | 9 | 7.5 | 16.5 |
| Above 70 | 7.5 | 5 | 12.5 |

(EY p.1373 — rates effective 1 January 2026)

**CPF rates from 1 January 2025 (for reference — previous year):**

| Age | Employer (%) | Employee (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 55 and below | 17 | 20 |
| Above 55 to 60 | 15.5 | 17 |
| Above 60 to 65 | 12 | 11.5 |
| Above 65 to 70 | 9 | 7.5 |
| Above 70 | 7.5 | 5 |

(EY p.1373)

**Ordinary Wage (OW) ceiling:** SGD 8,000/month effective 1 January 2026 (raised from SGD 7,400 in 2025). CPF is payable only on the monthly ordinary wage up to this ceiling.

**Annual wage cap:** SGD 102,000/year combined (ordinary + additional wages subject to CPF).

**Notes:**
- First-year and second-year PRs have transitional lower contribution rates.
- Self-employed individuals may participate in the CPF scheme voluntarily for retirement and housing; only **MediSave** contribution is **mandatory**.
- SRS (Supplementary Retirement Scheme) is available to all — residents, PRs and foreigners. Foreigners: SGD 35,700/year cap. Contributions are tax-deductible.

(EY pp.1373–1374)

## Social Security — MediSave (Self-Employed)

Self-employed individuals carrying on a trade, business, profession or vocation in Singapore with **net trade income above SGD 6,000/year** must make compulsory MediSave contributions. This applies regardless of nationality (including foreigners) if carrying on trade in Singapore.

| Contribution | Employee (salaried) | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPF (citizens/PRs only) | 20% (age ≤55) | 17% (age ≤55) | Voluntary only |
| MediSave (mandatory component) | Included in CPF | Included in CPF | ~10.5% of net trade income (age ≤55, income >SGD 18,000) |
| CPF for foreigners | **0%** | **0%** | **0% CPF; MediSave mandatory if trade income** |

**MediSave contribution rates for self-employed (SEPs):**

| Annual net trade income (SGD) | Contribution rate (age ≤55) |
|---|---|
| Up to 6,000 | Nil |
| 6,001 – 18,000 | Graduated (stepped, ~4%–8.5%) |
| Above 18,000 | ~10.5% of net trade income |

Contributions are capped once the MediSave account reaches the Basic Healthcare Sum (BHS = **SGD 79,000** in 2026). Once BHS is reached, no further MediSave contributions are required.

(Sources: cpf.gov.sg; iras.gov.sg; EY p.1374)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Individual Trade

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a trade, business, profession or vocation in Singapore. No incorporation required.
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue less allowable business expenses and capital allowances), prepared on generally accepted accounting principles with tax adjustments. Aggregated with other income and taxed at progressive PIT rates 0%–24%. (EY p.1368)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT 0%–24% on net assessable income.
- **Social security:** Mandatory MediSave ~10.5% of net trade income (above SGD 6,000; for under-55). Voluntary CPF contributions eligible for tax relief up to SGD 37,740.
- **VAT (GST) obligations:** GST registration mandatory if taxable turnover exceeds **SGD 1,000,000/year** (standard rate 9% from 1 January 2024). Voluntary registration possible below threshold.
- **Key limits:** No revenue cap. Capital allowances granted on qualifying assets. Sole traders with annual turnover > SGD 500,000 must attach certified financial statements to tax returns.

### Option B — Private Limited Company (Pte. Ltd.)

- **Who qualifies:** Individual who incorporates a Singapore Pte. Ltd. and contracts through the company.
- **Tax base:** Company pays Corporate Income Tax (CIT) at **17%** flat rate on chargeable income. Director-shareholder draws salary (PIT applies) or dividends (tax-exempt under one-tier system).
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - **Start-up Tax Exemption (SUTE):** First 3 years — 75% exemption on first SGD 100,000 chargeable income; 50% on next SGD 100,000.
  - **Partial Tax Exemption (PTE):** Established companies — 75% on first SGD 10,000; 50% on next SGD 190,000.
  - Effective CIT on first SGD 100,000 under SUTE: 17% × 25% = ~4.25%.
- **Social security:** Company must contribute CPF for Singapore citizen/PR employees. Foreigners working through own Pte. Ltd. are not subject to CPF.
- **Key limits:** Must have at least one locally ordinarily resident director. Higher compliance costs than sole proprietorship.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Employment Pass (EP)
- **Eligibility:** Fixed monthly salary ≥ SGD 5,600 (most sectors; higher thresholds for financial services and older applicants; e.g., SGD 10,700 for age 45+). Acceptable degree/professional qualifications. Must pass COMPASS points-based assessment.
- **Benefit:** Work authorization; dependant passes available. Standard progressive PIT applies — no special tax rate.
- **Duration:** Up to 2 years (first issuance); renewable up to 3 years per renewal.
- **Caveats:** Subject to Fair Consideration Framework (FCF) — employer must post job for 14 days unless exempted. (EY pp.1376–1377)

### S Pass
- **Eligibility:** Mid-level skilled workers; fixed monthly salary ≥ SGD 3,300 (general), ≥ SGD 3,800 (financial services).
- **Benefit:** Work authorization. Subject to sector quota and employer monthly levy.
- **Duration:** Up to 2 years; renewable. (EY p.1376)

### EntrePass (for Entrepreneurs)
- **Eligibility:** Foreigner starting a new business in Singapore. Must register a Pte. Ltd., hold ≥30% shareholding. Company must be venture-backed OR hold innovative technology AND meet at least one further qualifying criterion (raised funding, government-incubator backed, founded/sold a tech business, holds IP, has research collaboration in Singapore).
- **Benefit:** Right to operate own business. Subsequent renewals up to 2 years.
- **Duration:** 1 year initial; up to 2 years subsequent renewals.
- **Caveats:** Requires Pte. Ltd. (not sole proprietorship). (EY p.1378)

### Tech.Pass
- **Eligibility:** Established tech entrepreneurs, leaders or technical experts. Last drawn fixed monthly salary ≥ SGD 22,500 in past 12 months AND ≥5 years' cumulative experience in leading role at a tech company with market cap/valuation ≥ USD 500M or ≥ USD 30M funding raised, or at VC firm with ≥ USD 500M AUM.
- **Benefit:** Flexibility to concurrently start companies, be employee, investor, director, consultant/mentor.
- **Duration:** 2 years; renewable. (EY pp.1377–1378)

### Personalized Employment Pass (PEP)
- **Eligibility:** EP holders or overseas professionals with last drawn fixed monthly salary ≥ SGD 22,500.
- **Benefit:** Not tied to a specific employer; can switch jobs without new pass application. Can be unemployed up to 6 months.
- **Duration:** Non-renewable; valid 3 years only; issued once. Must earn ≥ SGD 270,000/year while holding PEP.
- **Caveats:** Cannot do freelance or sole proprietor work. (EY p.1377)

### ONE Pass (Overseas Networks & Expertise Pass)
- **Eligibility:** Fixed monthly salary ≥ SGD 30,000 OR outstanding achievements in arts, culture, sports, academia or research.
- **Benefit:** 5-year personalized pass; work for multiple companies concurrently; exempt from COMPASS and FCF advertising.
- **Duration:** 5 years; renewable. Annual notification of earnings/activities to MOM required. (EY p.1378)

### No Special Income Tax Regime for New Residents
Singapore does not offer a remittance-basis, territorial, or reduced-rate PIT regime specifically for new tax residents. All residents pay the standard progressive rates (0%–24%). The territorial exemption for foreign-sourced income applies to all residents equally.

## 2026 Changes

- **CPF Ordinary Wage ceiling raised to SGD 8,000/month** (from SGD 7,400) effective 1 January 2026 — higher-earning Singapore citizens/PRs will contribute more. (EY p.1373)
- **CPF contribution rate increases (1 January 2026):** Age 55–60: employer 16% (was 15.5%), employee 18% (was 17%); Age 60–65: employer 12.5% (was 12%), employee 12.5% (was 11.5%). Rates for age ≤55 unchanged at 17%/20%. (EY p.1373)
- **MediSave Basic Healthcare Sum (BHS):** Raised to SGD 79,000 for 2026 (from SGD 75,500 in 2025).
- **Working Mother's Child Relief:** Restructured to fixed-dollar amounts for Singapore citizen children born/adopted on or after 1 January 2024 (SGD 8,000 first child, SGD 10,000 second, SGD 12,000 third+). (EY p.1370)
- **CPF top-up relief change (YA 2027+):** CPF cash top-ups to MediSave that attract the Matched MediSave Scheme (MMSS) will not qualify for income tax relief from YA 2027. CPF top-ups that attract Matched Retirement Savings Scheme (MRSS) grants will not qualify from YA 2026. (EY p.1371)
- **GST rate:** 9% (effective since 1 January 2024); no change for 2026.
- **EP salary thresholds:** Increased in September 2023 to SGD 5,600 (general) and SGD 6,200 (financial services) for new applications; age-progressive increases apply.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/SGD = 1.45 (2025 average)
- Single person, no dependants; only earned income relief (SGD 1,000, age under 55) applied; all other reliefs ignored
- Employment scenario: foreigner on EP — zero CPF obligation for both employee and employer
- Sole Proprietor: foreigner — no CPF; only mandatory MediSave at ~10.5% of net trade income (age under 55, income above SGD 18,000; BHS cap not yet reached for new residents)
- Net trade income = gross revenue (no business expenses deducted) — real burden is lower if expenses exist
- MediSave deductibility / CPF relief (up to SGD 37,740) not applied as offset in this simplified model
- No GST modeled — revenue below SGD 1,000,000 threshold
- Foreign-sourced income not included — exempt under territorial system

**Key insight:** Singapore's progressive rates and zero CPF for foreigners produce very low employment effective rates (1.7% at €30k, 10.2% at €60k, 12.6% at €100k). The mandatory MediSave (~10.5%) adds a significant flat cost for sole proprietors, making self-employment materially more expensive than employment at all income levels for foreigners. For Singapore citizens/PRs, add employee CPF 20% (on ordinary wages up to SGD 8,000/month) to both scenarios — the employment rate at €30k becomes approximately 14–18%.

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Singapore citizens / PRs employment scenario:** Employee CPF 20% (ordinary wages up to SGD 8,000/month) not modeled; employer CPF 17% is an additional employer cost
- [ ] **MediSave graduation table SGD 6,000–18,000:** Exact stepped rates for low-income band not extracted; use CPF calculator for precision
- [ ] **MediSave deductibility offset:** The sole proprietor model does not net out the MediSave deductibility and CPF relief (up to SGD 37,740) — real net PIT burden is lower
- [ ] **Company (Pte. Ltd.) scenario:** SUTE/PTE-adjusted corporate effective rate not quantified in summary table; Pte. Ltd. via SUTE is likely lower total burden than sole proprietorship above ~SGD 80k revenue
- [ ] **Non-resident withholding rates:** EY table (p.1372) covers 15%/24% non-resident rates; not detailed here
- [ ] **SRS tax savings for foreigners:** Not modeled — foreigners contributing up to SGD 35,700/year to SRS can reduce PIT significantly at higher income levels
- [ ] **GST registration threshold:** SGD 1,000,000 — sole traders near this threshold should model GST costs
