## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (IR — Impôt sur le Revenu) for residents taxed on worldwide income. Tax year = calendar year. Filing deadline: mid-May or beginning of June of the following year (Form 2042). Withholding at source (prélèvement à la source) in force since 1 January 2019 for employment and pension income; self-employment income settled by monthly/quarterly direct debits. (EY p.447)

Self-employment income is divided into three categories: commercial (BIC — Bénéfices Industriels et Commerciaux), professional (BNC — Bénéfices Non Commerciaux, for liberal professions), and agricultural (BA). (EY p.433)

**IR brackets — 2024 income** (EY p.440; note: 2025 brackets not yet adopted as of EY content date)

| Annual taxable income (EUR) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 11,497 | 0% |
| 11,497 – 29,315 | 11% |
| 29,315 – 83,823 | 30% |
| 83,823 – 180,294 | 41% |
| Over 180,294 | 45% |

**Family coefficient (quotient familial):** France uses joint taxation for married couples and PACS partners. Taxable income is divided by the number of household allowances (parts), tax computed on one part, then multiplied by the number of parts. (EY p.439–440)

| Family composition | Allowances (parts) |
|---|---|
| Single individual | 1 |
| Married/PACS, no children | 2 |
| Married/PACS, one child | 2.5 |
| Married/PACS, two children | 3 |
| Each additional dependent child | +1 |

Tax savings from additional parts are capped (EUR 1,791 per additional half-part for 2024). (EY p.440)

**Exceptional contribution (high-income taxpayers):** (EY p.440)
- Single taxpayer: 3% on gross reference income EUR 250,000–500,000; 4% above EUR 500,000
- Married: 3% on EUR 500,000–1,000,000; 4% above EUR 1,000,000

**20% minimum income tax (CDHR — Contribution Différentielle sur les Hauts Revenus):** French residents with high income must pay minimum 20% IR; calculated on worldwide income; due mid-December 2025 on 2025 income. (EY p.440)

**CSG/CRDS (Contribution Sociale Généralisée / Contribution au Remboursement de la Dette Sociale):** (EY p.440–441)
- Rate on employment income: 9.7% on 98.25% of gross salary (up to EUR 188,400/year ceiling; 100% of portion above EUR 188,400)
- Combined CSG/CRDS on passive income and capital gains: 17.2% (= 9.7% CSG + 0.5% CRDS + 7% additional social surtax)
- 6.8% of CSG is deductible for French income tax purposes (EY p.441)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- 10% standard deduction on net taxable employment income, limited to EUR 14,426 (2024 income); or actual expenses elected (EY p.438–439)
- Self-employed professional income (BNC, accrual or cash method): actual expenses deductible (EY p.433, 439)

**Investment income:** 30% flat rate (12.8% IR + 17.2% CSG/CRDS); taxpayer may elect progressive IR rates if more favorable. (EY p.434)

**Capital gains (investments):** 30% flat rate (12.8% + 17.2% social charges); listed shares can opt for progressive rates. (EY p.438)

**Real estate capital gains:** 19% IR + 17.2% CSG/CRDS = 36.2% total; reduced by abatements for each year held beyond 5 years (22-year exemption for IR; 30-year for social charges); supplementary tax 2%–6% on gains >EUR 50,000. (EY p.438)

**Notes:**
- No option to file separately for married/PACS couples (EY p.439)
- Loss carryforward: generally 6 years; losses in one category may offset profits in other categories with limitations (EY p.441–442)

## Social Security

French social security contributions are due on compensation including bonuses and benefits in kind. Total charge for 2025 approximately 15%–24% for employees and 35%–47% for employers (depending on remuneration level and fund contributions). (EY p.445)

**Employment — Employee contributions (approximate 2025):**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Health insurance (maladie) | 0.75% | 13.30% |
| Basic state pension (CNAV) | 6.90% | 8.55% |
| Complementary pension (AGIRC-ARRCO) | ~3.93% | ~5.86% |
| Unemployment (chômage) | 0% | 4.05% |
| Family allowances (CAF) | 0% | 3.45–5.25% |
| Housing aid (FNAL) | 0% | 0.10–0.50% |
| Work accident (AT/MP) | 0% | ~2.23% |
| CSG/CRDS | 9.70% | 0% |
| **Total (approximate)** | **~21.3%** | **~40–47%** |

Contribution ceilings (plafond de la Sécurité Sociale — PASS):
- PASS 2025: EUR 47,100/year (EUR 3,925/month)
- Basic pension contributions capped at 1× PASS for employee; pension complementary (AGIRC-ARRCO) has different ceilings
- Sickness, family, unemployment: no ceiling (EY p.445)
- CSG/CRDS ceiling for 98.25% base: EUR 188,400/year (EY p.440)

**Self-employed — micro-entrepreneur (auto-entrepreneur) regime:**

For liberal professions (BNC category) affiliated with CIPAV or Sécurité Sociale des Indépendants (SSI):
- Flat social contribution rate: **~25.6%** of gross revenue (professions libérales relevant de la CIPAV: ~21.2%; professions libérales relevant du régime général SSI: ~25.6%). (URSSAF 2025)
- Includes health insurance, pension, disability, maternity/paternity, family contributions
- No floor/ceiling on contributions (proportional to revenue, no minimum for low earners)
- Note: no unemployment benefit coverage for micro-entrepreneurs

**Self-employed — régime réel (actual income basis, BNC or BIC):**
- SSI contributions approximately 40–45% of net profit for liberal professions (BNC)
- Includes health, maternity, retirement (base + complementary), disability, family
- Minimum contribution applies even with zero/negative income
- Contributions partially deductible from taxable income

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Micro-Entrepreneur (Auto-Entrepreneur) / Micro-BNC

- **Who qualifies:** Individual liberal professionals (BNC) with annual revenue below EUR 77,700 (2025 threshold for BNC/services). Commercial activities (BIC) threshold: EUR 188,700 for sales, EUR 77,700 for services. (URSSAF/impots.gouv.fr)
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue minus a standard abattement (flat deduction):
  - **BNC (liberal professions, services):** 34% abattement → taxable income = 66% of revenue
  - BIC services: 50% abattement → taxable income = 50% of revenue
  - BIC sales/accommodation: 71% abattement → taxable income = 29% of revenue
  - Taxable income is then added to other household income and taxed at progressive IR rates
- **Rate / mechanism (option versement libératoire):** Taxpayer may elect to pay a flat IR at source on each quarterly/monthly declaration:
  - BNC liberal professions: **2.2%** of gross revenue (versement libératoire)
  - BIC services: 1.7% of revenue
  - BIC sales: 1.0% of revenue
  - Condition: prior-year reference taxable income must not exceed the 11% bracket ceiling (EUR 29,315 for 2024 bracket; ~EUR 27,478 per part for 2025)
- **Social security (cotisations sociales):** Flat rate on gross revenue:
  - Liberal professions (BNC, CIPAV): **~21.2%** of revenue
  - Liberal professions (BNC, SSI — former régime général): **~25.6%** of revenue
  - SSI rate applies to most "new" liberal professions since 2018; CIPAV retained for specific regulated professions (architects, lawyers, accountants, etc.)
- **VAT obligations:** Franchise en base de TVA (VAT exemption) if revenue below EUR 36,800 (services/BNC) or EUR 91,900 (sales) — 2025 thresholds. Must charge VAT once threshold exceeded mid-year.
- **Key limits:** Revenue ceiling EUR 77,700 (BNC/services); regime ceases to apply if exceeded for two consecutive years. Cannot deduct actual expenses — abattement is fixed. Regime is simple (no bookkeeping, quarterly declarations to URSSAF).

### Option B — Entreprise Individuelle (EI) / Régime Réel — BNC Actual Expenses

- **Who qualifies:** Any liberal professional (BNC); mandatory if revenue exceeds EUR 77,700 or if actual expenses exceed the abattement.
- **Tax base:** Net profit = revenue minus all actual deductible professional expenses (rent, equipment, subscriptions, professional fees, etc.).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net profit added to taxable income and taxed at progressive IR rates (0–45%).
- **Social security:** SSI or CIPAV contributions ~40–45% of net profit; includes base and complementary retirement, health, disability, maternity, family. Contributions are deductible from the BNC taxable base (Madelin law limits apply for retirement contributions).
- **VAT:** Standard VAT obligations from first euro of revenue (or franchise if below thresholds).
- **Key limits:** Requires annual accounting declaration (Form 2035 for BNC); more complex than micro-entrepreneur but allows full expense deductions and access to Madelin deduction for retirement savings.

### Option C — EURL / SASU (Single-Member Company)

- **EURL (Entreprise Unipersonnelle à Responsabilité Limitée):** Single-member LLC taxed as transparent (IR) by default or corporate tax (IS) on election. Manager-associate subject to SSI contributions as self-employed (~40–45% of remuneration + company profit).
- **SASU (Société par Actions Simplifiée Unipersonnelle):** Single-member simplified joint-stock company; always subject to corporate tax (IS) at 25% (15% on first EUR 42,500 for small companies). Sole shareholder acting as president (dirigeant) is treated as employee for social security (full employer + employee contributions apply). Dividends distributed above 10% of share capital are subject to 30% flat tax (PFU) or progressive IR + 17.2% social charges.
- **Key difference:** SASU allows access to unemployment insurance (France Travail) for the president if salaried; EURL manager-associate has no unemployment coverage.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Impatriation Tax Regime (Régime Fiscal des Impatriés — Article 155 B)

- **Eligibility:** Employees seconded to France or individuals directly hired abroad by a French company (extended since November 2018 also to intra-company transfers). Must not have been French tax resident in any of the five tax years preceding arrival. (EY p.431–432)
- **Benefit:**
  - Exemption from French IR on the impatriation premium (expatriate supplement) up to the level of equivalent French reference salary
  - 30% of net remuneration may be treated as exempt impatriation premium (if directly hired abroad by French company)
  - Up to 20% of remaining taxable compensation may be excluded for services performed outside France on non-French workdays
  - 50% exemption on foreign-source dividends, interest, royalties and capital gains for 5 years (subject to conditions) (EY p.432)
  - Social surtax of 17.2% remains payable on full income
- **Duration:** Until 31 December of the 8th year following the year of transfer (for arrivals on or after 6 July 2016). (EY p.432)
- **Application deadline:** Must be exercised upon arrival; election must be made between impatriation regime and HQ rules (see below).
- **Caveats:** Cannot be combined with expatriate HQ rules for the same assignee; 17.2% CSG/CRDS remains due on full income; benefits may not be combined with French headquarters/distribution center employee regime.

### Expatriate HQ Rules (Headquarters and Distribution Center Employees)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign expatriates assigned to French HQ of a multinational company.
- **Benefit:** Tax relief for up to 6 years; eliminates tax-on-tax if employer reimburses excess foreign tax liability; employer reimbursement taxed at corporate rates only.
- **Duration:** Up to 6 years from assignment date. (EY p.432)

## 2026 Changes

- **2025 IR brackets:** EY states (p.440) that 2025 brackets had not yet been adopted as of the content date (2024 brackets apply in the referenced table); 2025 brackets typically indexed annually to inflation (~1–2%). Check impots.gouv.fr for confirmed 2025/2026 brackets.
- **CDHR minimum 20% tax:** New "Contribution Différentielle sur les Hauts Revenus" applies to 2025 income; payable mid-December 2025. Replaces the older 3%/4% exceptional contribution for high earners. (EY p.440)
- **Micro-entrepreneur thresholds:** Annual adjustment for inflation expected; BNC threshold EUR 77,700 in 2025 subject to triennial revision.
- **PASS 2025:** EUR 47,100 (updated annually; affects social contribution ceilings).
- **Impatriation regime:** No announced changes; extended to intra-company transfers since 2018 remains in force.
- **Cross-border telework:** France signed the Framework Agreement on cross-border telework in the EU/EEA/Switzerland, allowing employees living in one country and employed in another to continue under employment-state social security for up to 49.9% of working time spent in home country. (EY p.447)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person (1 part), no dependants, standard deductions only. All figures in EUR (Eurozone).
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee side only; employer ~40–47% not included).
> "Micro-BNC" = gross revenue invoiced; BNC liberal professions, SSI rate 25.6% SS; 34% abattement for IR.
> CSG/CRDS included: for employment 9.7% on 98.25% of gross; for micro-entrepreneur included in 25.6% flat.
> 10% standard deduction on employment income applies (capped at EUR 14,426).

### EUR 30,000 — Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| CSG/CRDS (9.7% × 98.25%) | 9.534% × 30,000 | EUR 2,860 |
| CSG deductible (6.8% × 98.25%) | 6.681% × 30,000 = 2,004 | EUR 2,004 |
| 10% standard deduction | 10% × (30,000 − 2,004) = 2,800; capped at 14,426 | EUR 2,800 |
| IR taxable income | 30,000 − 2,004 − 2,800 | EUR 25,196 |
| IR (brackets) | 0–11,497: 0; 11,497–25,196 @ 11%: 1,507 | EUR 1,507 |
| **Total burden** | **2,860 (CSG/CRDS) + 1,507 (IR)** | **EUR 4,367** |
| **Effective rate** | **4,367 / 30,000** | **14.6%** |

Note: Employee social security contributions (~21.3%) are additional but are the main burden; shown separately:
- Employee SS: ~21.3% × 30,000 = EUR 6,390
- **Total including employee SS:** EUR 10,757 → **35.9%** of gross

## Known Gaps

- [ ] 2025 IR brackets not confirmed in EY text (stated "not yet adopted" as of content date); use impots.gouv.fr for official 2025 and 2026 brackets
- [ ] Micro-entrepreneur social contribution rates: EY does not detail these; URSSAF 2025 rates used (CIPAV ~21.2% vs SSI ~25.6%); exact applicable rate depends on profession and registration date
- [ ] Employee social security rate used is a simplified composite (~21.3%); actual rate varies significantly by salary bracket, ceiling, and complementary pension tier
- [ ] Employer social security rate (~40–47%) is a range from EY p.445; exact rate depends on salary level, sector, and applicable collective agreement
- [ ] CIPAV vs SSI classification for liberal professions: affects which SS rate applies; architects, lawyers, healthcare professionals → CIPAV; IT consultants, management consultants → SSI since 2018
- [ ] Versement libératoire (flat 2.2% IR) for micro-BNC not modeled in examples; would substantially reduce tax at lower income levels for eligible taxpayers (income condition applies)

- [ ] 2026 PASS value not confirmed (expected ~EUR 47,600–48,000; check URSSAF)
