## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (Impuesto Único de Segunda Categoría for employees; Impuesto Global Complementario for annual reconciliation). Residents taxed on worldwide income; foreign nationals in first 3 years of residence taxed on Chilean-source income only. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return filed in April. Spouses taxed separately. (EY p.253–256)

**Reference units:**
- MTU = Monthly Taxable Unit (Unidad Tributaria Mensual, UTM) — inflation-indexed monthly; May 2026 ≈ CLP 70,588
- ATU = Annual Taxable Unit (Unidad Tributaria Anual, UTA) = 12 × MTU; May 2026 ≈ CLP 847,056
- UF (Unidad de Fomento) = inflation-indexed daily unit; May 2026 ≈ CLP 38,590

**Income tax brackets — Employment and Self-Employment (MTU-based)** (EY p.256)

| Taxable income — Exceeding (MTU) | Not exceeding (MTU) | Rate (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 13.5 | 0 |
| 13.5 | 30 | 4 |
| 30 | 50 | 8 |
| 50 | 70 | 13.5 |
| 70 | 90 | 23 |
| 90 | 120 | 30.4 |
| 120 | 310 | 35 |
| 310 | — | 40 |

- Employment income: tax assessed monthly using MTU; optional annual return to recalculate
- Self-employment and business income: assessed annually using ATU (1 ATU = 12 MTUs for the month of December); same rate brackets apply (EY p.256)
- Progressive scale applies to Global Complementario (annual total income tax, aggregating all income)

**Nonresident withholding (Additional Tax):** (EY p.256–257)
- Technical/professional services: 15% (or 20% if payments to related party or tax haven resident)
- Scientific, cultural, sports fees: 20%
- Other services: 35% (general rate)
- Royalties: 30%
- Dividends: 35% (with credit for corporate First Category Tax)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.255)
- Social security contributions (AFP pension, health): deductible from taxable income
- Actual or deemed business expenses (self-employed): actual documented expenses, OR 30% of gross income (standard deduction), limited to 15 ATUs/year (≈ CLP 12,705,840/year at May 2026 rates)
- Mortgage interest: deductible (limits apply)
- Child education expenses: tax credit if family income ≤ UF 792 (≈ CLP 30,563,000/year); max credit UF 4.4 per child per year (≈ CLP 169,796) (EY p.255)
- Voluntary pension and insurance contributions: deductible within limits

**Notes:**
- Capital gains from personal property not used in business: exempt from tax (EY p.255)
- Capital gains from sale of local real estate: taxable; non-taxable amount up to UF 8,000 (≈ CLP 308,720,000) if sale meets criteria (EY p.255)
- Capital gains from shares on Chilean stock exchange: flat 10% (EY p.255)
- Dividends from Chilean corporations: credited against Global Complementario; First Category Tax (27%) credits against personal tax (EY p.254)
- Directors' fees: progressive PIT, 10% provisional withholding without deduction of expenses (EY p.254)

## Social Security

**Employee contributions (borne by employee; withheld by employer):** (EY p.257–258)

| Contribution | Employee rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| AFP pension (individual account) | 10.0% | Plus AFP commission 0.46%–1.45% of taxable income |
| Health insurance (salud — FONASA or ISAPRE) | 7.0% | FONASA (public) or ISAPRE (private top-up) |
| Unemployment insurance (AFC) — indefinite term | 0.6% | (Employer pays 2.4%; employee 0.6%) |
| SIS (Seguro de Invalidez y Sobrevivencia) | — | Paid by employer for employees; employees do not contribute |
| SANNA fund (serious childhood illness) | — | Employer 0.03% |
| **Standard employee total** | **~17.6–18.45%** | Pension 10% + AFP commission + health 7% + AFC 0.6% |

**Maximum wage ceiling for SS contributions (2025/2026):** UF 87.8/month (≈ CLP 3,388,242/month at May 2026 UF; ≈ USD 3,750). Adjusted annually. (EY p.258)

**Employer contributions:** (EY p.257–258)
- Work accident insurance (Mutual de Seguridad): 0.9% base + up to 3.4% depending on risk activity
- SANNA fund: 0.03%
- Unemployment insurance (AFC): 2.4% (indefinite); 3% (fixed-term — fully employer)
- SIS (disability/survivor insurance): shared via AFP; from August 2025–August 2026, employers additionally pay 0.1% (old age pension reform), 0.9% (life expectancy), 1.49% (disability/survival) under 2025 pension reform (Law 21,735), gradually increasing to 8.5% total over 9–11 years
- TOTAL employer contributions (pre-reform baseline): ≈ 3.33–7.33% + progressive pension reform additions

**EY summary for employee:** max base rate ≈ 18.4% (7% health + 10% pension + 0.49–1.44% AFP commission + 0.6% UFC) on salaries up to UF 87.8/month. (EY p.258)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Boletas de Honorarios (Professional Fee Invoices)

- **Who qualifies:** Independent workers, liberal professionals, and individuals providing services not under an employment contract. This is the primary mechanism for freelance/independent professional work in Chile. (EY p.254, 256, 259)
- **Tax base:** Gross fees received. Provisional retention applied to each invoice; annual Global Complementario return adjusts final PIT liability.
- **Rate / mechanism — Provisional retention (retención provisional):**
  - **2026 rate: 15.25%** — withheld by the payer (client) when the client is a legal entity or business, OR self-paid monthly by the worker if client is an individual
  - Official SII announcement: retention increased from 14.5% (2025) to 15.25% (2026), continuing gradual increase towards 17% (2028) per Law 21,133 (SII, 26 December 2025)
  - The 15.25% is a provisional payment credited against the annual Global Complementario (progressive 0%–40%)
  - Annual return in April reconciles: if annual PIT < total retained → refund; if annual PIT > total retained → additional payment due

- **Standard expense deduction for annual return:** 30% of gross fees, limited to 15 ATUs/year (≈ CLP 12,705,840 at May 2026 rates). (EY p.255)

- **Mandatory social security contributions (independent workers — boleta de honorarios):**
  Distribution of the 15.25% retention covers mandatory contributions in priority order:
  1. SIS (Seguro de Invalidez y Sobrevivencia): **1.49%** (2026 rate for independent workers via tax withholding channel, per Superintendencia de Pensiones)
  2. ATEP (work accident/occupational disease): variable (~0.93% base)
  3. SANNA (serious childhood illness fund): small portion
  4. Health (Salud — FONASA): **7%** of taxable base
  5. AFP pension: **10%** of taxable base (plus AFP commission ~0.46–1.45%)
  
  Note: The 15.25% retention may not be sufficient to cover all contributions at higher income levels; any deficit is settled at annual tax return. The taxable base for SS is 80% of gross fees (a deemed expense factor applied before calculating SS obligations). (SII, chileatiende.gob.cl)

- **VAT (IVA):** Liberal professions rendering personal services are generally NOT subject to IVA in Chile. Fee invoices (boletas de honorarios) do not carry IVA. (General rule under DL 825)
- **Key limits:** Self-employed individuals aged men > 55 or women > 50 as of 1 January 2018, pensioners, or those already contributing to non-AFP systems may be exempt from mandatory independent SS contributions. (EY p.258)

### Option B — Primera Categoría (First Category / Company)

- **Who qualifies:** Independent professionals who incorporate a company (SPA — Sociedad por Acciones, or EIRL); suitable for those with higher revenues and significant deductible expenses.
- **Tax base:** Net profit (revenues minus expenses) at corporate level.
- **Rate / mechanism:** First Category Tax (corporate) at 27% (large companies) or 25% (small/medium entities meeting requirements) on net profit. Corporate tax is fully credited against shareholder's Global Complementario when profits are distributed. (EY p.254)
- **Social security:** Director/owner pays as independent worker (same AFP/health as boleta regime).
- **Key limits:** More complex administration; accounting required; requires notary deed for incorporation; optimal when expenses are high or when deferring distribution of profits.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Three-Year Foreign Income Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals who establish tax residence or domicile in Chile.
- **Benefit:** Taxed only on Chilean-source income (not worldwide income) during the first 3 years of residence. Can be extended at the tax authority's discretion.
- **Duration:** Up to 3 years; after which worldwide income taxation applies.
- **Caveats:** Domicile is established from arrival with intention to stay; key indicator is where main economic activity is located. (EY p.253)

### Foreign Specialist Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Foreign technical or professional employees covered under a similar social security system in their home jurisdiction.
- **Benefit:** Exempt from Chilean pension, health, and certain SS contributions; no double SS liability.
- **Caveats:** Does not apply to unemployment insurance (AFC), work accident insurance (ATEP), and SANNA fund. (EY p.258)

## 2026 Changes

- **Boleta de honorarios retention: 15.25% from 1 January 2026** (increased from 14.5% in 2025). Gradual schedule per Law 21,133: 15.25% (2026) → 16% (2027) → 17% (2028). (SII announcement 26 December 2025)
- **Pension reform — Law 21,735 (2025):** Employers begin gradual additional contributions from August 2025. Between August 2025 and August 2026, employers pay: 0.1% (old age pensions) + 0.9% (life expectancy) + 1.49% (SIS/disability). This increases gradually over 9–11 years to reach 8.5% employer contribution to a new collective savings fund. (EY p.257–258)
- **UTM adjustments:** UTM updated monthly for CPI inflation; January 2026 = CLP 69,751, growing monthly. All tax brackets in MTU terms are automatically adjusted.
- **UF cap for SS contributions:** UF 87.8/month wage ceiling adjusted annually; at May 2026 UF ≈ CLP 38,590, the ceiling ≈ CLP 3,388,242/month.
- **Self-employed pension reform bill submitted March 2026:** The executive submitted a bill addressing increased mandatory contributions for self-employed workers (beyond the boleta retention mechanism). No enacted changes as of May 2026 — the gradual retention increase remains the operative mechanism.

**Key insight:** At all three income levels, the boleta B2B effective rate (16.8%–19.3%) is lower than employment (20.3%–23.2%). The differential narrows significantly at €100k due to the SS ceiling — both employment and boleta workers hit the UF 87.8/month cap, after which the SS burden grows slower while PIT continues rising. The standard 30% expense deduction (capped at 15 ATUs = ~€12.8k) provides meaningful reduction at lower/mid incomes but is irrelevant as a percentage at high incomes.

**Important note on employment SS at €100k:** Employment SS drops in effective rate at €100k because the SS wage ceiling (UF 87.8/month ≈ CLP 40.7M/year) represents only ~41% of gross salary, capping total SS contributions. This is why employment effective rate is lower at €100k than at €60k.

- EUR/CLP = 990 (approximate 2025 average; CLP is moderately volatile)

- SS ceiling: UF 87.8/month = CLP 3,388,222/month = CLP 40,658,664/year
- AFC ceiling (unemployment): UF 131.9/month ≈ CLP 5,090,621/month (separate, higher ceiling)
- Boleta SS base: 80% of gross fees per SII rules (20% deemed expense factor)

- Standard 30% deduction for self-employed applied and capped at 15 ATUs
- No voluntary contributions, dependant deductions, or mortgage interest modeled
- Child education tax credit not applicable (income above UF 792/year threshold)

- [ ] Self-employed pension reform bill (submitted March 2026) could change mandatory contribution structure when enacted
- [ ] ATEP (work accident) and SANNA contributions for independent workers: small amounts (~0.93% ATEP, ~0.03% SANNA on SS base) not separately itemized in the effective rate calculations — these are paid from the 15.25% retention before AFP/health
- [ ] The 30% standard deduction cap of 15 ATUs is roughly €12.8k — for the €100k boleta scenario, the cap limits the deduction to 12.8% of gross rather than 30%; actual deductible business expenses (computer, software, office) could exceed the 15 ATU cap if documented
- [ ] Global Complementario calculation uses annual ATU — monthly bracket calculations shown are for employment; the annual reconciliation for boleta workers is the operative final calculation
- [ ] Health ISAPRE (private insurance) top-up above 7% not modeled; many professionals pay ISAPRE premiums of 7%+ CLP amounts
