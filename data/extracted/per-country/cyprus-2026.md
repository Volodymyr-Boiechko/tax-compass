## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PIT on employment, self-employment, rental, and other income. Tax year = calendar year. Annual returns due **31 July** of the following year (for 2025 tax year: 31 July 2026; exception: self-employed with audited accounts by 31 March 2027). (EY pp. 328, 330)

### 2025 PIT Brackets (pre-reform):

| Taxable income (EUR) | Tax rate | Tax due (EUR) | Cumulative tax due (EUR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 19,500 | **0%** | 0 | 0 |
| 19,500 – 28,000 | **20%** | 1,700 | 1,700 |
| 28,000 – 36,300 | **25%** | 2,075 | 3,775 |
| 36,300 – 60,000 | **30%** | 7,110 | 10,885 |
| Above 60,000 | **35%** | — | — |

(EY p. 328)

### 2026 PIT Brackets (post-reform, effective 1 January 2026):

| Taxable income (EUR) | Tax rate | Tax due (EUR) | Cumulative tax due (EUR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 22,000 | **0%** | 0 | 0 |
| 22,001 – 32,000 | **20%** | 2,000 | 2,000 |
| 32,001 – 42,000 | **25%** | 2,500 | 4,500 |
| 42,001 – 72,000 | **30%** | 9,000 | 13,500 |
| Above 72,000 | **35%** | — | — |

(Sources: ibccs.tax; pgeconomides.eu; cyprustaxlife.com — 2026 tax reform)

**Investment / capital income:**
- Dividends: **exempt** from income tax (EY p. 325)
- Interest: **exempt** from income tax (EY p. 325)
- Capital gains: **exempt** (except on Cyprus immovable property and related shares — 20% CGT) (EY p. 326)
- Profits from sale of listed securities: **exempt** (EY p. 326)
- Pension from employment abroad (> EUR 3,420/year): 5% special rate (EY p. 326)

**Special Defence Contribution (SDC):**
- Dividends (domiciled residents): **17%** on dividends received (before 2026 reform)
- Interest (domiciled residents): **30%** on interest received
- Rental income (domiciled residents): 3% on 75% of gross rent → **effective 2.25%**
- **Non-Dom residents: 0% SDC** on all of the above (see Non-Dom regime below)
- **From 1 January 2026:** SDC on dividends reduced to **5%** for domiciled residents; SDC on rental income **abolished** (pgeconomides.eu; easycorporate.com.cy)

**Standard deductions / allowances (2025):**
- Personal allowance: embedded in the 0% band (EUR 19,500 in 2025; EUR 22,000 from 2026)
- Social insurance and GHS contributions: deductible from taxable income
- Life insurance premiums: deductible (certain limits apply)
- Contributions to pension, provident, and medical funds: deductible up to 1/5 of taxable income

**New 2026 deductions (tax reform):**
- Child allowances: EUR 1,000 (1st child), EUR 1,250 (2nd child), EUR 1,500 (3rd+ child)
- Primary residence: up to EUR 2,000 for loan interest or rent payments
- Energy efficiency / electric vehicle upgrade: up to EUR 1,000
- Disaster insurance: up to EUR 500 for home insurance against natural disasters
(pgeconomides.eu 2026)

**Notes:**
- Employees: 20%/EUR 8,550 exemption for first employment in Cyprus (Article 8(21A)) — applies for 5 years from January of year following start; see Special Regimes below (EY pp. 322–323)
- 50% exemption for employment income > EUR 55,000: up to 10 years (Article 8(23)) (EY p. 323)
- 50% exemption for first employment by non-resident for ≥ 15 consecutive years (Article 8(23A)): up to 17 years (EY pp. 323–325)
- "90-day rule": foreign-source employment income exempt if individual was abroad >90 days for non-Cyprus employer (EY p. 322)
- Spouses taxed separately on all income (EY p. 328)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Registered Self-Employed Individual

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered as self-employed with the Tax Department and Social Insurance.
- **Tax base:** Net taxable income (revenue minus allowable business expenses). Fully deductible: all expenses incurred wholly and exclusively for business. Depreciation allowances: plant/machinery 10%/year; industrial buildings 4%/year.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard PIT brackets (2026: 0%/20%/25%/30%/35%). Annual PIT return by 31 July (self-employed without audited statements) or 31 March of second following year (with audited statements). (EY p. 330)
- **Social security:** 15.6% social insurance + 4.0% GHS = **19.6%** on net income. Subject to profession-specific minimum annual base and maximum base (linked to occupation type). (EY p. 328)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual taxable turnover > EUR 15,600 (Cyprus threshold). Standard VAT rate: 19%.
- **Key limits:** No income ceiling. Audited financial statements required if annual turnover > EUR 70,000.

### Option B — Cyprus Ltd (Private Limited Company)

- **Rate:** Corporate income tax **12.5%** on profits. Dividends to Non-Dom shareholder: 0% SDC + 0% income tax + 2.65% GHS. Most efficient structure for profit retention.
- **Typical use:** Highly tax-efficient when combined with Non-Dom status; dividends entirely exempt from income tax and SDC for Non-Doms.
- **Owner's salary:** Standard employment PIT + social contributions on salary portion.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Non-Dom Regime (Special Defence Contribution Exemption)

The headline attraction of Cyprus for relocating professionals and investors. (EY pp. 321, 325–326; legarithm.io; luma.cy)

- **Eligibility:** Be a Cyprus tax resident who is NOT domiciled in Cyprus. Two pathways:
  1. **Domicile of origin outside Cyprus** — automatically treated as non-domiciled when becoming Cyprus tax resident
  2. **Deemed domicile rule:** Becomes domiciled only after being Cyprus tax resident for 17 out of the preceding 20 tax years (so Non-Dom exemption lasts up to **17 years**)
- **Tax residency qualification (60-day rule):** An individual can become a Cyprus tax resident without the standard 183-day stay if ALL of the following are met: (EY p. 321)
  - Stay in Cyprus for at least **60 days** in the tax year
  - Not be a tax resident in any other country (no single country for >183 days)
  - Maintain a permanent residence in Cyprus (owned or rented)
  - Exercise business, employment, or hold directorship in a Cyprus company
- **Benefit:** Complete **exemption from SDC** on:
  - **Dividends** (0% — saving 17% for domiciled residents; from 2026: saving 5%)
  - **Interest** (0% — saving 30% for domiciled residents)
  - Rental income SDC abolished from 2026 (so saving is now 0% for all)
  - **No income tax** on dividends and interest (these are income-tax exempt for everyone)
- **Effective result:** Worldwide dividend and interest income = **0% Cyprus tax** (only 2.65% GHS applies, capped at EUR 180,000 combined income)
- **Duration:** Up to **17 years** from becoming Cyprus tax resident (until "deemed domicile" threshold of 17/20 years is crossed)
- **Extension option:** After 17 years, Non-Dom SDC exemption can be extended by paying EUR 250,000 lump sum (5-year extension); second extension: further EUR 250,000 for another 5 years. Total possible: 27 years of Non-Dom benefits. (easycorporate.com.cy; atca.com.cy)
- **Caveats:**
  - 2.65% GHS still applies on dividend/interest income (up to EUR 180,000 cap)
  - Employment and business income subject to standard PIT
  - Must genuinely not be domiciled in another country OR demonstrate domicile of origin outside Cyprus

### Employment Exemptions for New Employees

**Article 8(21A) — 20% / EUR 8,550 exemption:**
- Eligibility: First employment in Cyprus commenced 27 July 2022 – 31 December 2027 (inclusive); must not have been Cyprus tax resident in the preceding 15 consecutive tax years
- Benefit: Lower of 20% of remuneration or EUR 8,550 exempt from PIT
- Duration: 7 tax years from year following commencement of employment
(EY pp. 322–323)

**Article 8(23) — 50% exemption for high earners (income > EUR 55,000):**
- Eligibility: First employment in Cyprus after 1 January 2012; individual was resident outside Cyprus prior to employment; annual remuneration > EUR 55,000; employment on or before 26 July 2022
- Benefit: 50% of employment income exempt from PIT
- Duration: 10 years (original provision); covered until end of 10-year period
(EY pp. 322–323)

**Article 8(23A) — 50% exemption (expanded, income > EUR 55,000):**
- Eligibility: Non-resident outside Cyprus for at least 15 consecutive tax years immediately before first employment; first employment in Cyprus from 1 January 2022; annual remuneration > EUR 55,000
- Benefit: 50% of employment income exempt from PIT
- Duration: Up to **17 tax years** from first employment year
(EY pp. 323–325)

### Digital Nomad Visa

- **Eligibility:** Non-EU and non-EEA nationals; work remotely using ICT for employers/clients outside Cyprus; minimum monthly net income EUR 3,500 (after taxes and contributions). (EY pp. 337–338)
- **Benefit:** Legal right to reside in Cyprus; tax residency if >183 days (or 60-day rule met). Quota: 500 permits (as of March 2025 ~300 balance remaining).
- **Duration:** 1 year; renewable for 2 years (total 3 years)
- **Family:** Dependent family members can reside in Cyprus but cannot be employed or economically active
- **Tax:** Standard Cyprus PIT applies if tax resident. Non-Dom benefits available if qualifying.
- **Application:** Within 3 months of arrival, submit to Migration Department in Nicosia.

## 2026 Changes (Tax Reform — Enacted)

Major tax reform enacted and effective from **1 January 2026**:

- **Tax-free threshold increased:** EUR 19,500 → **EUR 22,000** (EY p. 328 shows 2025 brackets; 2026 reform confirmed by pgeconomides.eu, cyprustaxlife.com)
- **New PIT brackets:** 0%/20%/25%/30%/35% at €22k/€32k/€42k/€72k thresholds (vs. 0%/20%/25%/30%/35% at €19.5k/€28k/€36.3k/€60k in 2025)
- **SDC on dividends reduced** from 17% to **5%** for domiciled residents (Non-Doms still 0%)
- **SDC on rental income abolished** for all taxpayers
- **New family deductions:** Child allowances (€1,000–€1,500/child), primary residence deduction (€2,000), energy efficiency (€1,000), disaster insurance (€500)
- **Stamp Duty Law abolished** from 1 January 2026
- **Mandatory e-filing:** All individuals with taxable income in Cyprus must file electronically by 31 July of the following year (from 2025 tax year onwards; previously EUR 19,500 threshold for non-filing)
- **GHS contribution cap** remains EUR 180,000 annual income
- **EU Blue Card officially launched** 7 July 2025 — minimum gross salary EUR 43,632/year for ICT and specified sectors (EY p. 333)

(Sources: EY p. 317 — EY notes; pgeconomides.eu; cyprustaxlife.com; ibccs.tax; taxnews.ey.com)
