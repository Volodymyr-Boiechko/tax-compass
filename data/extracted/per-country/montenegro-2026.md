## Personal Income Tax

Montenegro uses a **progressive low-rate PIT** with a generous non-taxable threshold. Tax year = calendar year. Annual tax return due by **end of April** of the following year. (EY p. 1033)

### Employment income (monthly brackets):

| Monthly gross salary (EUR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to €700 | **0%** |
| €700.01 – €1,000 | **9%** |
| Above €1,000.01 | **15%** |

### Self-employment income (annual brackets):

| Annual taxable income (EUR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to €8,400 | **0%** |
| €8,400.01 – €12,000 | **9%** |
| Above €12,000.01 | **15%** |

Note: The annual self-employment brackets (€8,400 / €12,000) correspond directly to 12× the monthly employment brackets (€700 / €1,000). (EY p. 1033)

### Investment income (flat rates):

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Interest | 15% |
| Dividends and profit participations | 15% |
| Property leasing (net of 30% deduction; 50–70% deduction for tourism) | 15% |
| Capital gains | 15% |
| Internet income, video game income, games of chance | 15% |

(EY pp. 1033–1034)

**Municipal surtax:** (EY p. 1034)
- Podgorica and Cetinje: **15%** on PIT assessed
- Budva: **10%** on PIT assessed
- All other municipalities: **13%** on PIT assessed

The surtax is a percentage of the PIT amount itself (not of income). Example: 15% PIT × 15% surtax (Podgorica) = effective rate 15% × 1.15 = **17.25%** on the top marginal income.

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- No personal allowance for employees (the non-taxable band of €700/month serves this function)
- For self-employment not as principal activity: standard 30% expense deduction (or actual documented expenses if higher)
- Lump-sum self-employed: expense deduction based on activity category

**Notes:**
- Non-taxable employment threshold (€700/month = €8,400/year) is one of the highest in Europe
- Losses from self-employment may be carried forward up to 5 years (EY p. 1034)
- Annual tax return required for: self-employment income (unless lump-sum), property income, capital gains, foreign-source income

## Social Security

Employment income subject to social security contributions. Montenegro's structure places nearly all pension burden on the employee. (EY p. 1035)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Pension and disability insurance | **10.0%** | — |
| Unemployment insurance | **0.5%** | **0.5%** |
| **Total** | **10.5%** | **0.5%** |

**Annual cap (2024):** EUR 68,765 on pension and disability contributions (2025 cap not yet published as of EY data date). (EY p. 1035)

**Health insurance:** Montenegro provides state health insurance coverage; the contributions above fund pension, disability, and unemployment. Healthcare coverage is part of the social insurance system (no separate "health contribution" line — funded from state budget and pension fund).

**Contractors (royalties, services, agency work — no other insurance):**
- Pension and disability contribution: **10%** payable by the income payer (EY p. 1035)

**Self-employed contribution base (from secondary sources):**
- Annual revenue up to EUR 30,000: fixed lump-sum monthly contributions
- EUR 30,000–60,000: contributions on 100% of average gross salary (~EUR 200/month total est.)
- Above EUR 60,000: contributions on 150% of average gross salary (~EUR 240/month est.)
- Rates: pension & disability 10% + unemployment 1% = ~11% of contribution base

(Sources: EY p. 1035; digitalnomadtax.eu; adriacom.me)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Preduzetnik (Self-Employed / Sole Trader) — Regular Accounting

The primary regime for IT freelancers and professional service providers, since lump-sum is not available to them.

- **Who qualifies:** All registered sole traders and independent professionals. IT, consulting, marketing, and similar intellectual/online activities **must** use this regime (cannot use lump-sum). (adriacom.me; taxsummaries.pwc.com)
- **Tax base:** Annual accounting profit (revenue minus documented expenses), adjusted per PIT law. If self-employment is not the principal activity: standard 30% expense deduction allowed (EY p. 1033).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT on annual net income: 0% (≤ €8,400), 9% (€8,400–€12,000), 15% (above €12,000). Plus municipal surtax (13–15% on PIT).
- **Social security:** ~11% of contribution base (pension 10% + unemployment 1%), based on annual revenue bracket. Minimum and maximum bases apply.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds **EUR 30,000**. VAT rate: 21%.
- **Filing:** Annual tax return by end of April.

---

### Option B — Lump-Sum (Paušalno) Taxation

**Not available to IT, consulting, marketing, or other professional/intellectual services.** Available for craft/trade activities and other activities specified by classification.

- **Who qualifies:** Entrepreneurs with previous-year turnover not exceeding **EUR 30,000** (threshold increased from EUR 18,000 to EUR 30,000 from 1 January 2025). Excludes lawyers, auditors, and professional service providers. (EY p. 1033; taxsummaries.pwc.com)
- **Tax base:** Fixed monthly lump-sum determined by Tax Administration based on activity type and turnover. Four expense-deduction groups (from 30% to 50%).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed monthly payments (1/12 of annual assessment); simpler bookkeeping.
- **Social security:** Fixed lump-sum social contributions included in monthly payment.
- **Key limits:** EUR 30,000 annual turnover; limited activity types.

(EY p. 1033 — "Lump-sum tax is levied if self-employed individuals realize or plan to realize turnover below EUR 18,000 (starting from 1 January 2025, the threshold will be EUR 30,000)")

---

### Option C — d.o.o. (Private Limited Company)

- **Rate:** CIT 9% (profit ≤ €100,000), 12% (€100,001–€1,500,000), 15% (above €1,500,000). Dividends: 15% withholding.
- **Owner salary:** Standard employment PIT on salary + social contributions.
- **Typical use:** Higher-income earners; provides liability protection.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Digital Nomad Visa

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals wishing to stay and work remotely in Montenegro for a foreign employer or their own foreign entity.
- **Benefit:** Legal right to reside and work in Montenegro. Income tax subject to standard Montenegro PIT (no special exemption mentioned in EY).
- **Duration:** Up to **2 years** once granted. (EY p. 1037)
- **Application:** Visa D for up to 180 days; Single/Unified Permit for longer stays (up to 1 year, extendable). Processing: officially 15 days; in practice ~40 days. (EY p. 1037)
- **Caveats:**
  - No PIT exemption in Montenegro law for digital nomads (unlike Croatia) — standard tax applies
  - With low rates (0% up to €8,400, 9% up to €12,000, 15% above), Montenegro's tax burden is inherently low regardless
  - EU/Schengen/US/UK/Australia/Canada nationals can stay 90 days visa-free per 6-month period

### No Established Newcomer Preferential Regime

Montenegro does not have a formal "non-dom" or newcomer tax incentive program comparable to Malta, Cyprus, or Greece. The low statutory rates function as the main attraction for IT workers and digital nomads.

## 2026 Changes

- **Lump-sum turnover threshold raised to EUR 30,000** (from EUR 18,000) effective 1 January 2025. (EY p. 1033)
- **No changes to PIT brackets** (0%/9%/15%) announced for 2026.
- **Social security cap:** Annual cap for 2024 was EUR 68,765; 2025 cap not yet published at time of EY data (EY p. 1035).
- Montenegro is an EU candidate; tax harmonisation changes possible in coming years.
- Corporate income tax restructured to progressive 9%/12%/15% in 2022 (already in effect).

**Key insight:** Montenegro's self-employed rates (17–19%) are among the lowest in Europe for IT professionals, even without any special regime. The employer social cost is minimal (0.5%), making employment also relatively affordable. The self-employed advantage comes from low effective PIT (generous 0% band) and low social contributions.

**Assumptions:**
- Podgorica municipality (15% surtax — highest); other municipalities 13% surtax
- Employment: employee social contributions 10% pension + 0.5% unemployment = 10.5%; cap applied at ~€68,765 for €100k scenario
- Self-employed: social contributions estimated at ~11% of contribution base; base tiers from digitalnomadtax.eu (estimated; actual per Tax Administration decision)
- No personal allowances beyond the non-taxable band (€700/month employment; €8,400/year self-employment)
- Lump-sum not available for IT/professional services — standard regime used throughout

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Social contribution exact base for self-employed:** EY provides annual cap (€68,765 for 2024) for employees but not the precise base tiers for self-employed. The digitalnomadtax.eu tiers (€0–30k/€30–60k/€60k+) need confirmation from poreskauprava.gov.me
- [ ] **Health insurance / health contribution:** EY shows pension 10% and unemployment 0.5% as the employee social contributions; Montenegro's health coverage is part of the social system but the funding mechanism (whether a separate health contribution exists) is not fully clear from available sources
- [ ] **Annual cap for 2025:** EY explicitly states the 2025 cap was not yet published at data date; effective burden at €100k may change once updated
- [ ] **Lump-sum activity list exclusions:** The exclusion of "IT, consulting, marketing, intellectual services" from lump-sum is from secondary sources; the official classification list (at Tax Administration) is the authoritative reference
- [ ] **Employer health contribution:** Some secondary sources (immigrantinvest.com) cite 5.5% employer pension; EY shows 0% employer pension. EY is the primary source used here — secondary source discrepancy flagged
- [ ] **Digital nomad visa tax status:** Montenegro's digital nomad visa does not appear to provide any PIT exemption (unlike Croatia); this should be confirmed with a local advisor
