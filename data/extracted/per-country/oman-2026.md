## Personal Income Tax

**Oman has NO personal income tax.** There is no PIT on employment income, self-employment income, or investment income for individuals — whether Omani nationals or expatriates. This has been the case historically and remains unchanged. (EY p.1159)

Individuals are **not required to file any personal income tax return** in Oman.

**Other taxes relevant to individuals:**
- No capital gains tax on individuals
- No inheritance tax
- No gift tax
- No wealth tax
- No municipal income tax
- **Corporate Income Tax (CIT):** A new CIT was introduced and applies to companies (15% standard rate effective from tax year 2022). This applies to legal entities, not individuals directly.
- **Value Added Tax (VAT):** 5% VAT introduced in April 2021. Applies to most goods and services. Businesses with turnover exceeding OMR 38,500/year must register for VAT.

**Important update — Tax on Companies:** Under Royal Decree No. 18/2019 (Income Tax Law), a 15% CIT applies to companies and permanent establishments. Small Omani enterprises (revenue ≤ OMR 100,000) were previously exempt; the current status of this exemption should be verified. (EY p.1159–1160)

## Social Security

Oman has a mandatory social insurance system administered by the Social Protection Fund (formerly PASI — Public Authority for Social Insurance). (EY p.1160)

| Contribution | Omani Employee | Employer (for Omani) | Expat Employee | Employer (for Expat) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Social insurance (pension) | 7% of basic salary | 10.5% of basic salary + allowances | Not covered* | 1% (expat protection fund) |
| Work injury / occupational hazard | — | 1% | — | — |
| **Total** | **7%** | **11.5%** | **0%** | **1%** |

*Expatriates are not covered by the Omani social insurance/pension system. The 1% employer contribution for expatriates goes to the Social Protection Fund for expat end-of-service benefits. (EY p.1160)

**Income ceiling:** Basic salary cap applies for pension calculation — the contribution base for pension is basic salary (not total package including housing/transport allowances) for the employee 7%; employer contributions computed on a broader base. (EY p.1160)

**End-of-service gratuity (Expatriates):**
- Expatriate employees not covered by Omani social insurance are entitled to an end-of-service gratuity under the Labour Law
- Minimum: 15 days' basic salary per year for the first 3 years; 1 month's basic salary per year thereafter (varies by contract)
- Gratuity is typically paid by the employer at termination; not a deduction from employee pay

**Notes:**
- Omani nationals: mandatory PASI contributions; covered for pension, disability, death, work injury
- Expatriates: no PIT, no personal SS contribution; employer pays 1% expat protection levy
- No unemployment insurance system

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Individual as a Sole Proprietor / Freelancer

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals operating a business in Oman as a sole proprietor.
- **Tax base:** No personal income tax on sole proprietor income. If the business is structured as a sole proprietorship (individual enterprise), the income is not subject to PIT.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 0% personal income tax. However, if the business exceeds certain thresholds, the income may be subject to CIT as if it were a company (depending on legal structure and whether treated as a permanent establishment).
- **Social security:** Omani nationals: 7% employee contribution + 11.5% employer contribution on basic salary. Expatriate sole traders: no personal SS; 1% employer levy may apply if employing others.
- **VAT obligations:** Register for VAT (5%) if taxable supplies exceed OMR 38,500/year. Standard rate 5%.
- **Key limits:** Oman's commercial law restricts certain business activities to Omani nationals or requires Omani partnership (minimum 30% local ownership in many sectors).

### Company Structure (LLC / SAOC)

- **Who qualifies:** Any investor/entrepreneur establishing a limited liability company.
- **Tax base:** Company profits subject to 15% CIT. Dividends distributed to shareholders (individuals) are NOT subject to personal income tax.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 15% CIT on company net profit; 0% on individual dividends received.
- **Social security:** Company as employer pays SS contributions for Omani employees; 1% for expatriate employees.
- **VAT obligations:** 5% VAT if turnover > OMR 38,500.
- **Key limits:** Minimum 30% Omani ownership for most private companies (LLCs); exceptions in free zones. Free zones (SEZAD, Port of Salalah, etc.) offer CIT holidays for qualifying activities.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Personal Income Tax — Inherently Favorable for All

Since there is no PIT, Oman is inherently attractive for high-earning expatriates. No registration, no filing, no withholding on personal income.

### Free Zones

- **Special Economic Zone at Duqm (SEZAD), Salalah Free Zone, others:** Companies established in free zones enjoy CIT exemptions for 5–30 years depending on the zone and investment size. Benefits apply at company level.
- **Eligibility:** Companies incorporated in the free zone and conducting qualifying activities.
- **Individual impact:** Expatriate employees in free zones still pay no personal income tax (same as rest of Oman).

### Digital Nomad / Remote Worker

- Oman has not (as of EY content date October 2025) launched a formal digital nomad visa or remote worker residence program. Tourist/visit visa stays are generally 30–90 days. Individuals seeking longer-term residence would need to establish employment, investment, or other qualifying basis.
- The absence of PIT makes Oman extremely attractive in theory for location-independent workers, but the visa framework for non-employees remains limited.

## 2026 Changes

- **No PIT introduction announced.** Oman has no plans to introduce a personal income tax as of EY content date. The government has stated that no personal income tax is planned.
- **CIT refinements:** The CIT introduced in 2022 continues; any regulatory amendments for 2026 should be monitored at the Oman Tax Authority (OTA).
- **VAT:** 5% rate remains; no rate increase announced for 2026. Potential expansion of VAT scope to more financial services.
- **Social Protection Fund:** The SPF (which replaced PASI) continues to manage the social insurance system; contribution rates as above.
- **Global Minimum Tax (Pillar Two):** Oman has been evaluating implementation of the 15% global minimum CIT for large multinationals; potential impact on MNC branch/subsidiary structures in 2026+.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Oman has NO personal income tax. Effective income tax rate = 0% for all income levels.
> The only deductions from individual take-home pay are the Omani social insurance contribution (for Omani nationals only; 7% of basic salary).
> Expatriates: 0% PIT, 0% personal SS contribution.

| Gross income | EUR equiv | Scenario | Income tax | SS (personal) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | €30,000 | Employment (Expat) | €0 | €0 | €0 | **0%** |
| €30,000 | €30,000 | Employment (Omani national) | €0 | €2,100 (7% of basic)* | €2,100 | **~7%** |
| €60,000 | €60,000 | Employment (Expat) | €0 | €0 | €0 | **0%** |
| €60,000 | €60,000 | Employment (Omani national) | €0 | €4,200 (7% of basic)* | €4,200 | **~7%** |
| €100,000 | €100,000 | Employment (Expat) | €0 | €0 | €0 | **0%** |
| €100,000 | €100,000 | Employment (Omani national) | €0 | €7,000 (7% of basic)* | €7,000 | **~7%** |

*Assumes basic salary = 100% of gross for simplicity; in practice basic salary is often 30–50% of total package (with housing, transport allowances); the actual SS contribution would be lower.

**Self-employed (sole proprietor, expatriate):** 0% PIT, 0% SS = 0% effective rate.
**Company structure:** 15% CIT at company level; 0% on dividends received by individual shareholders.

**Key insight for digital nomads and high earners:** Oman is among the most tax-efficient jurisdictions globally for individuals. A high-earning expatriate professional or digital nomad residing in Oman legally pays 0% on all personal income. Combined with a 5% VAT (low by global standards) and no capital gains tax, Oman represents one of the most attractive tax environments in the world — subject to visa/residence constraints.

**Assumptions:**
- OMR/EUR: 1 OMR ≈ 2.40 EUR (OMR pegged to USD at 1 OMR = USD 2.597)
- Basic salary assumed 100% of gross for SS calculation simplicity
