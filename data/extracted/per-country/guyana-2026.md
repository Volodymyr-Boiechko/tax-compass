## Personal Income Tax

Residents ordinarily resident or domiciled in Guyana subject to tax on worldwide income. Non-ordinarily-resident individuals: taxed on income accruing in or derived from Guyana, including employment income exercised in Guyana regardless of where received. Additional rule: earned income (including employment income) arising outside Guyana or not ordinarily resident/domiciled individuals is taxable only when received in Guyana. Tax year = calendar year. Returns filed by 30 April of the following year. (EY p.560)

**Residency:** Resident if reside in Guyana for >183 days in the tax year, OR reside or intend to permanently reside in Guyana. Domicile = jurisdiction the individual regards as their permanent home. (EY p.560)

**Employment income:** Salaries, bonuses and all other allowances, including value of employer-provided benefits (accommodation, transportation). Emoluments deposited to an approved Savings Scheme may be exempt. (EY p.560–561)

**Progressive PIT brackets — Annual** (EY p.563)

| Taxable income (GYD) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| Up to GYD 3,120,000 | 25% |
| Above GYD 3,120,000 | 35% |

**Personal allowance:** The greater of GYD 1,560,000/year or one-third of employee's total income from all sources (excluding income subject to withholding taxes). (EY p.562)

**Other income types:**
- Dividends from Guyana resident companies: NOT subject to income tax for residents. (EY p.561)
- Interest, rental income, dividend income from non-resident companies, royalties, income from property: aggregated with other income and taxed at progressive rates. (EY p.561)
- Savings account interest: exempt if individual is ≥60 years old or incapacitated AND income does not exceed GYD 1,200,000/year (if income exceeds GYD 1,200,000, interest earned on savings accounts >GYD 10,000 becomes taxable). (EY p.561)
- Dividends from Guyana mortgage finance companies (approved): exempt. (EY p.561)
- Capital gains: 20% on change of ownership of property in Guyana AND in other specific cases if net taxable capital gain for the year exceeds GYD 500,000. Capital gains accruing outside Guyana: taxable only when received in Guyana (for non-residents/non-domiciled). (EY p.561)
  - Exempt capital gains: shares or stock of public companies; private residence sale if proceeds reinvested in equivalent/greater property within 60 days after year end; assets held >12 months (taxed as ordinary income if sold within 12 months); property received as gift/inheritance using open market value.
- Diamond withholding: 2% on value declared to Guyana Geology and Mines Commission (prepayment of tax, creditable). (EY p.563)
- Gold withholding: sliding scale — 2% of gross proceeds (gold price <USD 1,100/oz); 2.5% (price >USD 1,100/oz). Final tax for gold declared to Guyana Gold Board. (EY p.563–564)
- Small business (manufacturing/construction): registered with Small Business Bureau — taxed at 25% on chargeable income from manufacturing/construction. (EY p.563)
- Nonresidents: 20% final withholding on dividends, interest, royalties, management fees. (EY p.561)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.562)
- Personal allowance: greater of GYD 1,560,000/year or 1/3 of total income from all sources (excl. withholding-subject income).
- Employee NIS contributions: 5.6% of maximum insurable earnings (GYD 280,000/month → annual: ~GYD 188,160).
- Life and medical insurance premiums: up to 10% of income or GYD 600,000 annually (whichever is lower).
- Child allowance: GYD 10,000/month per child under 18, subject to conditions.
- Overtime allowance: first GYD 50,000 of overtime income (deductible; if overtime < GYD 50,000 only actual amount).
- Second job allowance: first GYD 50,000 of income from second job.
- Non-taxable allowances: traveling (if for employment), station, entertainment, subsistence, expense, medical/dental expenses, severance, vacation (up to 1 month gross salary).
- Business deductions: all expenses wholly and exclusively for producing income. Capital allowances 2%–50% depending on asset.

**Property Tax:**
- Net property value up to GYD 40,000,000: nil.
- Next GYD 20,000,000: 0.5%.
- Remainder: 0.75%.
- Filed and paid by 30 April following tax year. (EY p.564)

**Notes:**
- Losses: carried forward against future taxable income (amount may not exceed half the tax payable had the offset not occurred); no carryback. (EY p.564)
- No estate or inheritance taxes in Guyana; "forced heirship" rules apply to asset distribution. (EY p.564)
- Tax treaties: CARICOM Member States (Antigua and Barbuda, Barbados, Belize, Dominica, Grenada, Jamaica, St. Kitts and Nevis, St. Lucia, St. Vincent and the Grenadines, Trinidad and Tobago); Canada; United Kingdom; United Arab Emirates (signed, not yet ratified). (EY p.566)

## Social Security (NIS — National Insurance Scheme)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| NIS (ages 16–59) | 5.6% | 8.4% | 12.5% |
| NIS (age 60+, employed) | 0% | 1.5% | — |
| **Total (ages 16–59)** | **5.6%** | **8.4%** | **12.5%** |

**Maximum monthly insurable earnings:** GYD 280,000/month.
**Annual maximum employee contribution:** 5.6% × GYD 280,000 × 12 = GYD 188,160/year.
**Annual maximum self-employed contribution:** 12.5% × GYD 280,000 × 12 = GYD 420,000/year.

**Notes:**
- Employee contributions are deductible personal expenses. (EY p.562)
- Totalization agreements: CARICOM (Caribbean Community and Common Market). (EY p.565)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment
- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals carrying on a profession, vocation or business in Guyana.
- **Tax base:** Net taxable profits from financial statements, subject to income tax adjustments. All expenses wholly and exclusively for producing income deductible. Capital allowances 2%–50% (straight-line or reducing balance). (EY p.561, p.563)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual rates: 25% on income up to GYD 3,120,000; 35% on excess.
- **Social security:** 12.5% of self-employment income up to GYD 280,000/month (GYD 3,360,000/year max base).
- **VAT obligations:** VAT applies; standard rate 14%.
- **Key limits:** Cannot carry forward losses more than half the otherwise-applicable tax; no carryback. Quarterly advance installments required. (EY p.563, p.565)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No specific preferential tax regime for new residents or expats identified in EY source. Standard territorial + worldwide rules apply (residents taxed on worldwide income; non-ordinarily resident taxed on source income + income received in Guyana).

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> EUR/GYD rate used: 1 EUR ≈ GYD 225 (approximate 2025; GYD/USD ≈ 209, EUR/USD ≈ 1.08 → 1 EUR ≈ 225 GYD).
> "Employment" = gross annual salary; employee NIS 5.6% (capped at GYD 188,160/year); personal allowance (greater of GYD 1,560,000 or 1/3 gross); overtime deductions not modeled.
> "B2B" = self-employment net profit; NIS 12.5% on self-employment income (capped at GYD 420,000/year); personal allowance applied.

**EUR to GYD conversions:**
- €30,000 = GYD 6,750,000
- €60,000 = GYD 13,500,000
- €100,000 = GYD 22,500,000

### Working: GYD 6,750,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee NIS (5.6%, capped GYD 188,160) | 5.6% × 280,000/month × 12 | GYD 188,160 |
| Personal allowance (greater of GYD 1,560,000 or 1/3 × 6,750,000 = 2,250,000) | 1/3 × 6,750,000 | GYD 2,250,000 |
| Less NIS deduction | GYD 188,160 | |
| PIT base | 6,750,000 − 2,250,000 − 188,160 | GYD 4,311,840 |
| Band 1: first GYD 3,120,000 at 25% | 25% × 3,120,000 | GYD 780,000 |
| Band 2: GYD 3,120,000+ at 35% | 35% × (4,311,840 − 3,120,000) | GYD 416,644 |
| Total PIT | 780,000 + 416,644 | GYD 1,196,644 |
| **Total burden** | **188,160 + 1,196,644** | **GYD 1,384,804** |
| **Effective rate** | **1,384,804 / 6,750,000** | **~20.5%** |

### Summary Table

| Gross income | EUR equiv | Scenario | Income tax | NIS / SS | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| GYD 6,750,000 | €30,000 | Employment | GYD 1,196,644 | GYD 188,160 | GYD 1,384,804 | **~20.5%** |
| GYD 6,750,000 | €30,000 | B2B self-employment | GYD 1,264,644 | GYD 420,000 | GYD 1,684,644 | **~24.9%** |
| GYD 13,500,000 | €60,000 | Employment | GYD 3,446,644 | GYD 188,160 | GYD 3,634,804 | **~26.9%** |
| GYD 13,500,000 | €60,000 | B2B self-employment | GYD 3,549,644 | GYD 420,000 | GYD 3,969,644 | **~29.4%** |
| GYD 22,500,000 | €100,000 | Employment | GYD 6,296,644 | GYD 188,160 | GYD 6,484,804 | **~28.8%** |
| GYD 22,500,000 | €100,000 | B2B self-employment | GYD 6,399,644 | GYD 420,000 | GYD 6,819,644 | **~30.3%** |

**Working — GYD 13,500,000 Employment:**
- NIS: capped at GYD 188,160
- Personal allowance: 1/3 × 13,500,000 = 4,500,000
- PIT base: 13,500,000 − 4,500,000 − 188,160 = 8,811,840
- Tax: 25% × 3,120,000 + 35% × (8,811,840 − 3,120,000) = 780,000 + 35% × 5,691,840 = 780,000 + 1,992,144 = GYD 2,772,144

**Working — GYD 22,500,000 Employment:**
- Personal allowance: 1/3 × 22,500,000 = 7,500,000
- PIT base: 22,500,000 − 7,500,000 − 188,160 = 14,811,840
- Tax: 780,000 + 35% × (14,811,840 − 3,120,000) = 780,000 + 35% × 11,691,840 = 780,000 + 4,092,144 = GYD 4,872,144

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/GYD ≈ 225 (approximate; floating rate — GYD/USD officially managed)
- NIS contribution deducted; personal allowance = 1/3 of gross (as GYD 6,750,000 × 1/3 > GYD 1,560,000 fixed amount at all income levels shown)
- NIS annual cap: GYD 188,160 (employment) / GYD 420,000 (self-employed) applied
- No child or other allowances modeled
- B2B: NIS 12.5% on self-employment income (capped at GYD 420,000/year); same progressive rates
