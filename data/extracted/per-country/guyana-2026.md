## Personal Income Tax

Worldwide system for residents. Individuals ordinarily resident or domiciled in Guyana taxed on worldwide income. Non-residents taxed on Guyana-source income (including employment exercised in Guyana, regardless of where income is received). Residency: > 183 days in tax year, OR intending to permanently reside in Guyana. Tax year: calendar year. Individual returns due 30 April of following year. (EY pp.560–561)

**Note on earned income from outside Guyana:** Earned income (including employment income) arising outside Guyana that accrues to a person not ordinarily resident or domiciled in Guyana is taxable in Guyana **only when it is received in Guyana**. (EY p.560)

**Annual PIT rates:** (EY p.563)

| Taxable income | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| Up to GYD 3,120,000 | 25% |
| Over GYD 3,120,000 | 35% |

**Personal allowance:** The greater of GYD 1,560,000/year **or** one-third of the employee's total income from all sources (excluding income subject to withholding taxes). (EY p.562)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.562)
- Personal allowance: GYD 1,560,000/year or 1/3 of total income (whichever is greater)
- NIS employee contributions (5.6% on max monthly insurable earnings of GYD 280,000): deductible
- Life and medical insurance premiums: up to 10% of income or GYD 600,000/year (whichever is lower)
- Child allowance: GYD 10,000/month per child under 18
- Overtime allowance: first GYD 50,000 of overtime income exempt (if OT < GYD 50,000, limited to actual amount)
- Second job allowance: first GYD 50,000 of second job income exempt
- Vacation allowance: up to one month's gross salary
- Travel, station, entertainment, expense, subsistence allowances: deductible if proven to be for employment purposes
- Medical and dental expenses
- Severance pay

**Notes:**
- Dividends from Guyana resident companies: NOT subject to income tax for residents (EY p.561)
- Dividends from non-resident companies: taxable, aggregated with other income
- Interest, rental, royalties, and "other income from property": aggregated and taxed at progressive rates
- Interest in approved Savings Scheme: exempt if individual ≥ 60 or incapacitated AND income ≤ GYD 1,200,000/year
- Non-residents: flat 20% final withholding on dividends, interest, royalties and management fees (EY p.561)
- Capital gains: 20% on change of ownership of property (if net taxable capital gain > GYD 500,000 for the tax year). Capital gains outside Guyana only taxable when received in Guyana for non-residents/non-domiciled. (EY p.561)
- Capital gains exemption: shares/stock of public companies; sale of private residence if proceeds used to purchase equal/greater value property within 60 days of year-end
- Diamond/gold withholding: separate provisional withholding systems for mineral extraction income (EY p.563)
- Small business manufacturing/construction: 25% flat on chargeable income if registered with Small Business Bureau (EY p.563)
- Tax authority: GRA (Guyana Revenue Authority)

## Social Security (NIS — National Insurance Scheme)

Contributions at rates based on maximum monthly insurable earnings of GYD 280,000. (EY p.565)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| NIS (age 16–59) | 5.6% | 8.4% | 12.5% |
| NIS (age 60+) | 0% | 1.5% | N/A |
| **Total (age 16–59)** | **5.6%** | **8.4%** | **12.5%** |

**Income ceiling:** Maximum monthly insurable earnings = GYD 280,000/month (GYD 3,360,000/year).

**Notes:**
- No employee NIS contribution required above age 60
- Employer contribution reduced to 1.5% for employees above age 60
- Self-employed: 12.5% of declared income up to GYD 280,000/month ceiling
- Totalization agreement: CARICOM (Caribbean Community and Common Market) — provides relief from double SS taxes with CARICOM member states (Antigua and Barbuda, Barbados, Belize, Dominica, Grenada, Jamaica, St. Kitts and Nevis, St. Lucia, St. Vincent and the Grenadines, Trinidad and Tobago) (EY p.565)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All individuals with self-employment income, trade, business, or professional activity.
- **Tax base:** Net business income as disclosed in the business's financial statements, subject to tax adjustments. Capital allowances (depreciation) at 2%–50% on cost (straight-line basis limited to 90% of cost).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT rates (25% / 35%) on annual chargeable income. Quarterly advance tax payments on 1 April, 1 July, 1 October, 31 December (each = 1/4 of prior year's tax); balance due 30 April.
- **Social security:** NIS 12.5% on declared income up to GYD 280,000/month ceiling.
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to VAT if revenue exceeds threshold.
- **Key limits:** Losses may be carried forward indefinitely until fully recouped (offset cannot exceed 50% of the tax otherwise payable each year); no carryback.

### Option B — Small Business Bureau Registration (Manufacturing/Construction)

- **Who qualifies:** Small businesses registered with the Small Business Bureau engaged in manufacturing and construction services.
- **Tax base:** Chargeable income from manufacturing/construction activities.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat 25% on chargeable income from qualifying activities.
- **Caveats:** Limited to manufacturing and construction — not available to service professionals.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Specific Expat / Digital Nomad Regime
- Guyana does not have a formal preferential regime for new residents or digital nomads as described in EY source.
- However, the residency-based worldwide taxation system means that non-residents working remotely for foreign clients are taxed only on Guyana-source income.

### Foreign-Earned Income Timing Rule
- **Benefit:** Earned income arising outside Guyana for persons not ordinarily resident or domiciled accrues taxability only **when received in Guyana**. Effectively, foreign earnings not remitted to Guyana are not taxable.
- **Caveats:** For ordinary residents and domiciled individuals, worldwide income is immediately taxable. (EY p.560)

## 2026 Changes

- **Personal allowance:** GYD 1,560,000/year — may be updated by budget; verify with GRA.
- **NIS ceiling:** GYD 280,000/month — may be updated annually by decree.
- **Oil boom context:** Guyana has become one of the world's fastest-growing economies due to major offshore oil discoveries (ExxonMobil-led consortium). Government revenues from oil have grown substantially; this may lead to future tax reform but no specific personal income tax changes announced for 2026.
- No major structural PIT changes announced for 2026.
