## Personal Income Tax

Resident individuals subject to tax on El Salvador-source income AND foreign-source investment income (interest from cash deposits in financial institutions abroad, gains from sales of foreign securities, financial instruments and derivative contracts). Foreign income tax paid abroad may be credited against Salvadoran tax liability. Tax year = calendar year. Returns and tax due within first four months of the following year. (EY p.386, p.390)

**Residency:** Resident if stay in El Salvador >200 consecutive days during a tax year. Individual staying ≤200 consecutive days = nonresident. Individuals deemed residents for more than one calendar year may remain outside the country up to 165 days without losing resident status. Individuals whose principal place of trade or business is in El Salvador are also considered residents. (EY p.386)

**Nonresidents:** Taxed only on El Salvador-source income: assets located in El Salvador; activities carried out or capital invested in El Salvador; services rendered or used in El Salvador (even if received or paid outside El Salvador). Flat 30% rate. (EY p.386, p.389)

**Employment income:** Salary, remuneration, fees, and other compensation for services rendered or used in El Salvador. (EY p.387)

**Progressive PIT brackets — Annual (resident individuals)** (EY p.389)

| Annual taxable income (USD) | Tax on lower amount | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|
| Up to 6,600 | Exempt | 0% |
| 6,600.01 – 9,142.86 | USD 0 | 10% (+ USD 212.12) |
| 9,142.87 – 22,857.14 | USD 212.12 + (10% × [income − 6,600]) | 20% |
| Above 22,857.14 | USD 212.12 + 720 + (20% × [22,857.14 − 9,142.86]) | 30% |

**Simplified bracket table:**

| Annual taxable income (USD) | Rate | Cumulative formula |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 6,600 | 0% | — |
| 6,600.01 – 9,142.86 | 10% | 10% × (income − 6,600) |
| 9,142.87 – 22,857.14 | 20% | 212.12 + 20% × (income − 9,142.86) |
| Above 22,857.14 | 30% | 212.12 + 720 + 30% × (income − 22,857.14) per EY = USD 3,462.86 + 30% × (income − 22,857.14) |

**Other income types:**
- Interest income (from savings/time deposits in Salvadoran financial institutions domiciled in El Salvador): 10% flat if monthly average deposits ≥ USD 25,000. (EY p.387)
- Interest from deposits abroad: exempt for Salvadoran-domiciled individuals. (EY p.387)
- Dividends (paid/registered by resident entities to resident or nonresident individuals): 5% withholding (definitive final tax). If distributing company doesn't withhold, individual must report and pay. Nonresidents in tax-haven jurisdictions: 25% withholding. (EY p.387)
- Directors' fees (residents, not employees): 10% withholding. Directors' fees (nonresidents, not employees): 20% withholding (final). If director is employee: progressive rates apply. (EY p.388)
- Capital gains: flat 10% rate. Capital losses from sale of movable/immovable assets: may offset future capital gains for up to 5 years. Capital losses from securities/financial instruments abroad: offset against same-type capital gains for up to 5 years. (EY p.388–389)
- Payments to tax-haven jurisdictions: 25% final withholding (with certain exemptions). (EY p.388)
- Nonresidents: 30% flat rate on all El Salvador-source income. 20% withholding on remuneration, pensions, commissions, directors' fees (final). (EY p.389)

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.388)
- Personal deduction: USD 1,600 for employed individuals with annual income ≤ USD 9,100.
- For annual income > USD 9,100: may deduct up to USD 800 for medical expenses and up to USD 800 for education expenses (subject to confirmation by tax authorities).
- Business deductions: all costs and expenses necessary to generate taxable income (if not excessive/unreasonable, pertain to same fiscal year, supported by documentation, applicable withholding taxes have been imposed). Expenses ≥ USD 9,125 for goods/services deductible only if paid by check, bank wire, credit/debit card, or documented by written contract. (EY p.388)

**Notes:**
- No separate estate or gift taxes. However, estates taxed as ordinary taxpayers if they derive income before asset distribution to beneficiaries. (EY p.389)
- Losses: no carryforward or carryback for regular losses. Capital losses from movable/immovable asset sales: offset against future capital gains up to 5 years. (EY p.389)
- Employees must file annual income tax returns; not required if annual earnings < USD 60,000 and income was fully subject to withholding per the progressive scale. (EY p.390)
- Tax treaty with Spain only. El Salvador does not generally provide relief for foreign taxes except for investment income described in Section A. (EY p.390–391)

## Social Security

Social security contributions levied monthly on salaries. (EY p.389)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (health/sickness) | 3% | 7.5% | — |
| Pension fund (AFP) | 7.25% | 8.75% | — |
| Business Foundation for Educational Development (FEPADE) | — | 1% (employers with ≥10 employees) | — |
| **Total** | **~10.25%** | **~17.25%+** | — |

**Ceilings:** (EY p.389)
- Social security (health): monthly salary ceiling USD 1,000.
- Pension fund (AFP): NO monthly salary ceiling.

**Notes:**
- Death and pension funds covered by private institutions (AFP), funded through 8.75% employer + 7.25% employee of salaries (no ceiling). (EY p.389)
- Salary in kind NOT subject to Pension Fund contributions (only cash compensation for ordinary services). (EY p.389–390)
- Foreign individuals: Pension Fund Administration has established a refund mechanism; refund available after leaving El Salvador. (EY p.389)
- 1% FEPADE contribution: required for employers with 10 or more employees. (EY p.390)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment
- **Who qualifies:** All individuals deriving income from self-employment services rendered or used in El Salvador, or from a trade/business.
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus deductible business expenses (all costs necessary to generate taxable income, meeting documentation requirements). (EY p.388)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual rates 0%–30% (same scale as employment).
- **Social security:** Not specified for self-employed in EY source.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT applies to services; standard rate 13%.
- **Key limits:** Expenses ≥ USD 9,125 must be paid by check/wire/card or documented by written contract. No loss carryforward/carryback for ordinary losses.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No specific preferential tax regime for newcomers or expats identified in EY source. Nonresidents subject to flat 30% on El Salvador-source income (or 20% withholding on most income types). No special NHR-type regime exists.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> El Salvador uses USD — no currency conversion needed.
> "Employment" = gross annual salary; employee SS ~10.25% (3% health up to USD 12,000/year cap + 7.25% pension no cap); personal deduction USD 1,600 (for income ≤ USD 9,100) or USD 800 medical + USD 800 education (for income > USD 9,100).
> "B2B" = self-employment income at same progressive rates; SS not specified.

### Working: USD 30,000 Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee health SS (3%, capped at USD 1,000/month = USD 12,000/year) | 3% × 12,000 | USD 360 |
| Employee pension SS (7.25%, no cap) | 7.25% × 30,000 | USD 2,175 |
| Total employee SS | 360 + 2,175 | USD 2,535 |
| PIT deductions (income > USD 9,100: USD 800 medical + USD 800 education) | | USD 1,600 |
| PIT base | 30,000 − 1,600 | USD 28,400 |
| Band 1: 0–6,600 at 0% | 0 | USD 0 |
| Band 2: 6,600–9,142.86 at 10% | 10% × 2,542.86 | USD 254 |
| Band 3: 9,142.86–22,857.14 at 20% | 20% × 13,714.28 | USD 2,743 |
| Band 4: above 22,857.14 at 30% | 30% × (28,400 − 22,857.14) | USD 1,663 |
| Total PIT | 254 + 2,743 + 1,663 | USD 4,660 |
| **Total burden** | **2,535 + 4,660** | **USD 7,195** |
| **Effective rate** | **7,195 / 30,000** | **~24.0%** |

**Working — USD 60,000 Employment:**
- Health SS: 3% × 12,000 = USD 360; Pension SS: 7.25% × 60,000 = USD 4,350; Total SS = USD 4,710
- PIT base: 60,000 − 1,600 = 58,400
- Tax: 0 + 254 + 2,743 + 30% × (58,400 − 22,857.14) = 254 + 2,743 + 10,663 = USD 13,660

**Working — USD 100,000 Employment:**
- Health SS: USD 360; Pension SS: 7.25% × 100,000 = USD 7,250; Total SS = USD 7,610
- PIT base: 100,000 − 1,600 = 98,400
- Tax: 0 + 254 + 2,743 + 30% × (98,400 − 22,857.14) = 254 + 2,743 + 22,663 = USD 25,660

**B2B working (USD 30,000):**
- No SS contribution (self-employed regime not defined)
- No personal deduction (USD 1,600 deduction only for employees); assume USD 800+800 = USD 1,600 still available
- Tax: same PIT base calculation → USD 4,660 (same as employment if same deductions apply)
- However, without SS deduction, PIT base = 30,000 − 1,600 = same → USD 4,660
- Effective: 4,660 / 30,000 = 15.5% (revised estimate)

**Assumptions:**
- El Salvador uses USD; no exchange rate conversion needed
- Employee health SS: 3% capped at USD 1,000/month salary = USD 12,000/year
- Employee pension SS: 7.25% with no ceiling
- Personal deductions: USD 800 medical + USD 800 education (for income > USD 9,100); subject to tax authority confirmation
- B2B: self-employed SS rates not specified; assumed 0 for illustration
- Top rate 30% applicable above USD 22,857.14/year — relatively low threshold (≈€21,700)
