## BITCOIN NOTE

> El Salvador made Bitcoin (BTC) legal tender in September 2021 under the Bitcoin Law. As of early 2024, Bitcoin was removed from mandatory acceptance but remains legal tender alongside the USD. Tax obligations must still be paid in USD. Bitcoin income is subject to income tax as capital gains at 10%. The practical impact on employment tax is minimal; wages are still primarily paid in USD.

## Personal Income Tax

Resident individuals taxed on El Salvador-source income AND foreign-source investment income (interest from deposits in financial institutions abroad, gains from sale of foreign securities, financial instruments, and derivative contracts). Nonresidents taxed on El Salvador-source income only. Residency: > 200 consecutive days in a tax year; OR ≤ 200 consecutive days but main place of trade/business is in El Salvador. (EY pp.386–387)

Tax year: calendar year. Returns filed within first 4 months of following year (by April 30). No extensions available.

**Annual PIT brackets (resident individuals — employment and self-employment):** (EY p.389)

| Annual taxable income | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| Up to USD 6,600 | Exempt (0%) |
| USD 6,600.01 to USD 9,142.86 | 10% on excess over USD 6,600 + USD 212.12 |
| USD 9,142.87 to USD 22,857.14 | 20% on excess over USD 9,142.86 + USD 720 |
| Over USD 22,857.14 | 30% on excess over USD 22,857.14 + USD 3,462.86 |

**Simplified bracket view:**

| Exceeding (USD) | Not exceeding (USD) | Tax on lower amount (USD) | Rate on excess |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 6,600 | 0 | 0% |
| 6,600 | 9,142.86 | 212.12 | 10% |
| 9,142.86 | 22,857.14 | 720 | 20% |
| 22,857.14 | — | 3,462.86 | 30% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p.388)
- Personal deduction: USD 1,600/year for employed individuals with annual income ≤ USD 9,100
- Individuals with income > USD 9,100: may deduct up to USD 800 for medical expenses and up to USD 800 for education expenses (subject to tax authority confirmation)
- Business deductions: all costs and expenses necessary to generate or maintain taxable income; must be necessary, same fiscal year, documented, and not excessive or unreasonable

**Notes:**
- Nonresidents: flat 30% on El Salvador-source income (EY p.389)
- Nonresidents (withholding): 20% on remuneration, pensions, commissions, directors' fees (final withholding) (EY p.389)
- Tax-haven jurisdiction payments: 25% final withholding if payment has tax effect in El Salvador (EY p.388)
- Dividends / profits to residents and nonresidents: 5% final withholding (EY p.387)
- Directors' fees (resident non-employees): 10% withholding; (nonresidents): 20% withholding (EY p.388)
- Capital gains: flat 10% tax rate (EY p.388)
- Interest income (savings/time deposits ≥ USD 25,000 average monthly): 10% flat tax (EY p.387)
- Business/investment income from securities, FI, derivatives: 10% flat if conditions met (EY p.387)
- Loss carryforward: capital losses on movable/immovable assets may offset capital gains for up to 5 years; capital losses from foreign securities may offset gains from same assets up to 5 years; no carryback (EY p.389)
- Employees filing not required if annual earnings < USD 60,000 and properly withheld

## Social Security

Two separate systems: ISSS (Instituto Salvadoreño del Seguro Social) for health/work accidents, and the Pension Fund system (AFP) for retirement. (EY p.389)

| Contribution | Employee % | Employer % | Cap |
|---|---|---|---|
| ISSS (health / sickness) | 3.0% | 7.5% | USD 1,000/month salary ceiling |
| AFP (pension fund) | 7.25% | 8.75% | No monthly ceiling |
| INFOTEP (business foundation) | — | 1.0% | Employers with ≥ 10 employees |
| **Total (ISSS + AFP)** | **10.25%** | **16.25%** | — |

**Notes:**
- ISSS covers death, pension, work disability: private institutions fund these at 8.75% employer / 7.25% employee with no monthly ceiling for pension funds (AFP)
- ISSS health/sickness: capped at USD 1,000/month salary
- AFP pension: Pension Fund Administration (AFP) covers compensation received in cash in ordinary services — salary in kind not subject to AFP
- Foreign individuals: AFP refund mechanism available after departure from El Salvador
- No totalization agreement mentioned (EY source); only one tax treaty noted (with Spain — income tax and net worth tax) (EY p.391)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All individuals with self-employment or business income from activities rendered or used in El Salvador.
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses). Documentation requirements: expenses ≥ USD 9,125 must be paid by check, bank transfer, credit/debit card or under written contract.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT rates (0%–30%) on annual net taxable income. Annual return.
- **Social security:** AFP pension contributions apply; ISSS health applies if formally employed.
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IVA (El Salvador VAT) at 13%; monthly filing.
- **Key limits:** Full expense deductibility with documentation; capital gains at 10% flat rate.

### Option B — Investment Income (Flat Rates)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals deriving income from investments in securities, financial instruments, derivatives under specific conditions.
- **Rate:** Flat 10% on income where issuer/capital/risk is Salvadoran or capital employed in El Salvador.
- **Key note:** Interest from financial institutions abroad (not El Salvador) is subject to tax at regular rates in El Salvador for residents — unlike some countries, foreign investment income is taxable for Salvadoran residents.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Territorial Treatment for Certain Foreign Income
- **Benefit:** Income derived from deposits in financial institutions **abroad** (foreign bank interest) is explicitly **NOT subject to El Salvador income tax** for residents. (EY p.387 — "Income derived from deposits in financial institutions abroad is not subject to tax.")
- **Caveats:** This is a specific carve-out — other foreign-source income (except the listed categories of foreign investment income) may be subject to tax for residents.

### No Formal Digital Nomad Visa Confirmed
- As of EY data date, El Salvador does not have a specific digital nomad visa law equivalent to Costa Rica's Law 10008. The government has shown openness to Bitcoin-focused remote workers but no formal digital nomad tax regime is described in EY source.

## 2026 Changes

- **Bitcoin legal tender status:** Bitcoin Law (2021) made BTC legal tender; as of January 2024 the government removed mandatory acceptance obligations for businesses but BTC remains legal tender. Tax obligations still settled in USD. Bitcoin gains: 10% capital gains tax.
- **No major PIT structural changes announced:** USD-denominated brackets remain as shown.
- **ISSS / AFP rates:** No changes announced; ISSS cap at USD 1,000/month, AFP uncapped.
- **Tax treaty with Spain:** Active — avoids double taxation on income and net worth.
- **Employees earning < USD 60,000 not required to file** annual returns if properly withheld.

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **SS deductibility from PIT base:** EY source does not explicitly state whether ISSS/AFP contributions are deductible from employment PIT base. If deductible, effective rates would be modestly lower.
- [ ] **AFP ceiling for self-employed:** EY states no monthly ceiling for AFP; confirm applicability to self-employed.
- [ ] **No digital nomad visa confirmed:** El Salvador has shown interest in attracting Bitcoin/remote workers but no formal digital nomad tax regime described in EY source.
- [ ] **Bitcoin taxation:** Capital gains on BTC held and sold are subject to 10% capital gains tax; daily use of BTC as currency for personal transactions may be treated differently — not fully specified.
- [ ] **Self-employed ISSS:** ISSS health contributions for self-employed not specified; AFP applies to all compensation in ordinary services under employment relationships; self-employed AFP treatment unclear — verify with AFP administrators (Confía, Crecer).
- [ ] **IVA 13%:** Applies to self-employed providing services; not modeled above.
- [ ] **Foreign income taxation:** El Salvador taxes foreign investment income (interest from foreign deposits ≥ USD 25,000 average/month at 10%); foreign interest below threshold and foreign bank deposits explicitly NOT subject to tax for residents.
