## Personal Income Tax

There is NO personal income tax in Qatar for individuals. Employer-paid allowances provided as part of employment income to local and expatriate employees are not taxable for income tax purposes. (EY p.1314)

Partnerships consisting of individuals, as well as individuals carrying on business as professionals or sole traders, are taxed on net business income under the standard corporate income tax rate of 10%. Qatar operates under a territorial tax system — income tax is imposed only on income sourced or generated in Qatar. (EY p.1314)

Capital gains on disposal of real estate and securities derived by individuals are exempt from tax if the assets are not part of the individual's taxable activity. Dividends and capital gains from shares of companies listed on the Qatar Stock Exchange are also exempt from local tax. (EY p.1314)

**Tax year:** Calendar year.

**Tax residency:** Individuals are considered tax residents in Qatar if:
- They have a permanent home in QFC Qatar, or
- They are physically present in Qatar for 183 days or more during a calendar year, or
- They are citizens of Qatar

(EY p.1314)

### PIT Rate Table

| Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All employment income (individuals) | 0% |
| All investment income (individuals) | 0% |
| Business/professional income (sole traders/partnerships) | 10% corporate rate on net income |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Not applicable — no individual PIT

**Notes:**
- No wealth tax, inheritance/estate tax, or gift tax in Qatar (EY p.1315)
- No capital gains tax on real estate and securities not part of business activity (EY p.1315)
- Capital gains from listed QSE shares: exempt

## Social Security

### Qatari Nationals (Private Sector)

Qatari nationals working in the private sector are subject to the Qatari social security system. Monthly contributions are calculated on remuneration up to QAR100,000/month. (EY p.1315)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (Qatari nationals only) | 7% | 14% | N/A — Qataris only |
| **Total** | **7%** | **14%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** QAR100,000/month.

### Non-Qatari / Expat Nationals

- **GCC (non-Qatari) nationals:** Subject to their home country social security system while working in Qatar, under the GCC Unified Law of Insurance Protection Extension. Home country rates and caps apply. (EY p.1315)
- **Non-GCC nationals (expats):** Qatar does NOT impose social security taxes on foreign nationals. However, employees are entitled to an end-of-service benefit upon termination. (EY p.1315)

### End-of-Service Benefit (Non-GCC nationals)

Non-GCC national employees in the private sector are entitled to an end-of-service benefit after completing more than one year of continuous employment. The minimum entitlement is three weeks' wages for each year of service, calculated on the employee's last basic wage, prorated for fractional years. (EY p.1315)

**Notes:**
- Qatari nationals: 7% employee + 14% employer on salary up to QAR100,000/month
- Non-Qatari expats: 0% social security contributions; end-of-service gratuity applies instead
- No SS contributions for self-employed expats

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader / Professional (Individual)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals carrying on business as professionals or sole traders in Qatar
- **Tax base:** Net business income
- **Rate / mechanism:** 10% standard corporate income tax rate on net business income (territorial basis — Qatar-sourced income only)
- **Social security:** 0% for non-Qatari expats; Qatari nationals subject to social security (rate structure for self-employed not specified by EY)
- **VAT obligations:** Qatar has no VAT as of EY data date (October 2025)
- **Key limits:** Territorial system — only Qatar-sourced income taxable; foreign-source income not taxed

### Option B — Qatar Financial Centre (QFC) Entity

- **Who qualifies:** Businesses and professionals operating through QFC-licensed entities
- **Tax base / rate:** QFC has its own separate tax regime; QFC entities are subject to 10% corporate tax on QFC-sourced profits; certain exemptions available
- **Social security:** Not applicable to expats
- **Key limits:** Requires QFC licensing; provides enhanced immigration services and lower documentation requirements

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Personal Income Tax — Universal for All Residents

- **Eligibility:** All individuals working or residing in Qatar (employees, freelancers, investors)
- **Benefit:** 0% personal income tax on all employment income and investment income
- **Duration:** Indefinite (no sunset clause)
- **Caveats:** Business/professional sole traders subject to 10% corporate income tax on net business income; no individual PIT on employment income regardless of nationality or residency status

### Mustaqel Visa (Entrepreneurs and Skilled Professionals)

- **Eligibility:** Entrepreneurs and skilled/talented professionals
- **Benefit:** Work and residence visa for an initial duration of 5 years, renewable at discretion of authorities
- **Duration:** Initial 5 years, renewable
- **Application:** Through Ministry of Interior (EY p.1319)

### QFC Freezone

- **Eligibility:** Businesses operating within the Qatar Financial Centre
- **Benefit:** Dedicated immigration services, lower documentation requirements, dedicated MOI office, specialized QFC entity types
- **Note:** QFC levies service charges for immigration and administrative processing slightly higher than the State (EY p.1320)

## 2026 Changes

- No VAT introduced as of EY data date; GCC-wide VAT implementation discussions ongoing but Qatar has not announced a timeline
- End-of-service benefit rules unchanged
- Qatari national social security rates (7% employee / 14% employer) unchanged

**Employment effective rate: 0% at all income levels for non-Qatari expats.**

**Sole trader working (illustrative at €60,000 gross):**
- Gross revenue: ~QAR237,000 (at 3.95 QAR/EUR)
- Assume 30% deductible expenses → net profit = QAR165,900
- Corporate tax: 10% × 165,900 = QAR16,590 (~€4,200)
- Social security: 0% (non-Qatari)
- Effective rate on gross: ~7%

**Qatari national employment (illustrative at €60k):**
- Employment income tax: 0%
- Social security employee: 7% × QAR237,000 = QAR16,590 (~€4,200) — subject to QAR100,000/month cap
- Effective rate: ~7% (SS only, no income tax)

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/QAR = 3.95 (approximate)
- Non-Qatari expat; no SS contributions
- Sole trader: 30% assumed deductible business expenses for net profit calculation
- No VAT in Qatar
