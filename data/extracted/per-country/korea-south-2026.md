## Personal Income Tax

Korea taxes residents on worldwide income and nonresidents on Korea-source income only. A resident is defined as a person maintaining a domicile or residence in Korea for 183 days or longer. A foreign national who has been a tax resident in Korea for 5 years or less during the preceding 10 years (as of the end of the tax year) is treated as a **non-permanent resident** — taxed only on Korea-source income unless foreign-source income is paid in Korea or remitted to Korea. Tax year = calendar year. PAYE withholding; Composite Income return 1 May – 31 May of following year. Married persons taxed separately; no joint returns. (EY pp. 797–813)

### Local Income Tax (Surtax on National Income Tax)

Local income tax is a **surtax** on national income tax at approximately **10% of the national income tax rate** at each bracket:

| Tax base (KRW) | Local income tax rate |
|---|---|
| First 14,000,000 | 0.6% |
| Next 36,000,000 (14M–50M) | 1.5% |
| Next 38,000,000 (50M–88M) | 2.4% |
| Next 62,000,000 (88M–150M) | 3.5% |
| Next 150,000,000 (150M–300M) | 3.8% |
| Next 200,000,000 (300M–500M) | 4.0% |

**Combined effective top marginal rate:** 45% (national) + 4.5% (local) = **49.5%** on income above KRW2 billion.
**Combined top rate at KRW500M–1,000M:** 42% + 4.2% = **46.2%**

(EY p. 805)

### Flat Tax Rate Option for Foreign Employees (20.9%)

Foreign employees and executive officers who do not have a special relationship with the company per TILL and who began working in Korea between 1 January 2014 and 31 December 2026 may elect a **flat 20.9%** rate (including local income tax) on employment income — **without any exemptions, deductions, or credits**. This is beneficial if the flat rate produces a lower tax than the progressive rate system. Available for 20 years from the first day of work in Korea; elected per tax year. (EY pp. 805–806)

### Earned Income Deduction

A statutory deduction is applied to gross employment income before applying progressive rates: (EY p. 802)

| Gross employment income (KRW) | Earned income deduction |
|---|---|
| 0 – 5,000,000 | 70% of gross |
| 5,000,001 – 15,000,000 | KRW3,500,000 + 40% of amount exceeding KRW5,000,000 |
| 15,000,001 – 45,000,000 | KRW7,500,000 + 15% of amount exceeding KRW15,000,000 |
| 45,000,001 – 100,000,000 | KRW12,000,000 + 5% of amount exceeding KRW45,000,000 |
| Above 100,000,000 | KRW14,750,000 + 2% of amount exceeding KRW100,000,000 |

**Cap:** Maximum earned income deduction is **KRW20,000,000**.

### Personal Deductions

| Deduction | Amount (KRW) |
|---|---|
| Basic deduction — taxpayer, spouse, each qualified dependent | KRW1,500,000 each |
| Additional — aged 70+ | KRW1,000,000 |
| Additional — disabled | KRW2,000,000 |

### Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

- Tentative tax on employment income may be reduced by 55% up to tentative tax of KRW1,300,000, and 30% on any excess
- Credit capped at KRW740,000 if taxable employment income ≤ KRW33M
- Reduces progressively to KRW200,000 at KRW120M–KRW200M taxable income; zero above KRW200M (EY pp. 806–807)

**Standard tax credit (no special credits claimed):** KRW130,000 (employment) or KRW70,000 (no employment income). (EY p. 808)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Business Income (Composite Income)

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals, sole proprietors, professionals (lawyers, accountants, architects, doctors, IT professionals, etc.)
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross receipts minus deductible business expenses including depreciation); combined with other Composite Income and taxed at progressive national rates (6%–45%) + 10% local income tax
- **Rate / mechanism:** No earned income deduction applies (employment income deduction is for employment only); business income deduction rules differ
- **Social security:** Self-employed enroll in national pension (individually; 9% total, both sides) and health insurance as a "local subscriber" (not employee rate); rates differ from employment
- **VAT obligations:** Korean VAT (10%); registration mandatory if annual revenue > KRW8,000,000; simplified tax invoice system for revenue KRW8M–KRW480M
- **Filing:** Composite Income return 1 May – 31 May; quarterly estimated payments

### Option B — No separate presumptive regime

Korea has no flat-rate or presumptive regime for self-employed professionals comparable to India's Section 44ADA. Small and medium enterprises may qualify for **income tax exemptions** (10%–30% reduction on income up to KRW100M from qualifying businesses) until December 2025. (EY pp. 804–805)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Non-Permanent Resident (First 5 Years)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals who have been tax residents for ≤5 years during the preceding 10 years
- **Benefit:** Taxed only on Korea-source income; foreign-source income (paid/remitted outside Korea) is generally exempt
- **Duration:** Until the individual has been resident for more than 5 out of 10 preceding years (EY p. 797)

### Flat 20.9% Tax Rate for Foreign Employees

- **Eligibility:** Foreign employees/executive officers without special company relationship; began working in Korea between 1 January 2014 and 31 December 2026
- **Benefit:** Flat 20.9% rate (incl. 10% local tax) on employment income — no deductions, no credits, but no progressive rate above 20.9%
- **Duration:** Up to 20 years from first day of work in Korea; elect annually
- **Best for:** High earners where the progressive rate (effective above ~KRW88M) exceeds 20.9%
- **Regional HQ workers:** May apply flat rate for 20 years from start of HQ or employment assignment date regardless of the 2026 sunset (EY pp. 805–806)

### Foreign Technician 50% Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Foreign technicians rendering employment services in Korea for the first time on or before 31 December 2026; must be in qualifying technology transfer, research, or specialized materials/equipment sectors
- **Benefit:** 50% exemption from income tax on relevant employment income for **10 years** from the date services began (reduced to 50%; certain sectors get 70% for first 3 years + 50% for 2 more years)
- **Application:** Must file within 10 days of first month of service (EY p. 799)

### Digital Nomad Visa (F1-D)

- **Eligibility:** Individuals employed in their home jurisdiction receiving salary and benefits from that home jurisdiction but working remotely from Korea
- **Tax implication:** If present ≥183 days → Korean tax resident; Korean-source analysis applies; home country employment income paid by foreign employer not in Korea may still be taxable in Korea as resident (EY p. 817)

## 2026 Changes

- **Foreign employee flat tax rate sunset extended to 31 December 2026:** Foreign employees beginning work by 31 December 2026 remain eligible for the 20.9% flat rate election (EY pp. 805–806)
- **Foreign technician 50% exemption sunset:** Applies to technicians rendering services for the first time on or before 31 December 2026 (EY p. 799)
- **Small/medium enterprise income tax reduction:** Tax exemptions (10%–30% on income up to KRW100M) extended through the tax year ending 31 December 2025 (EY pp. 804–805)
- **National pension cap updated from 1 July 2025:** KRW286,650/month per employer/employee, based on average monthly salary KRW6,370,000 (EY p. 810)
- **NHI long-term care rate:** 12.95% of NHI premium (as surtax on health insurance) (EY p. 811)
- **Housing fund savings deduction sunset:** 40% of housing fund deposits (up to KRW2,400,000/year) for head of household with ≤KRW70M employment income who does not own a house — deadline 31 December 2025 (EY p. 803)
- **Married persons remain separately assessed** — no joint filing (EY p. 812)
- **Korea's DTT network:** 97 jurisdictions as of October 2025; social security agreements with 42 jurisdictions

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Flat tax rate sunset December 2026:** Foreign employees who begin working in Korea by 31 December 2026 can elect the 20.9% flat rate. After 2026, this option closes for new arrivals. At income levels above ~KRW100M, the flat rate saves significant tax vs. progressive rates — critical planning window.
- [ ] **Non-permanent resident planning:** During the first 5 years, foreign-source income not remitted to Korea is exempt. High earners with foreign assets should carefully manage remittances to Korea during this period.
- [ ] **Earned income deduction cap:** The KRW20M earned income deduction cap is reached at gross employment income of approximately KRW127M. Above this level, the effective earned income deduction as a % of gross declines further, increasing the effective tax rate.
- [ ] **NHI exemption for foreigners:** Foreigners covered by adequate home-country health insurance or company-provided equivalent may be exempt from Korean NHI contributions. This reduces the effective SS rate by ~4% and is worth pursuing for high-earning assignees.
- [ ] **Business income vs employment income:** Self-employed individuals cannot claim the earned income deduction — this applies only to employment income. At equivalent gross income, self-employed face higher effective income tax than employees (particularly at middle income levels).
- [ ] **Local income tax rate on B2B income:** Local income tax for self-employed business income is also assessed as a surtax at 10% of national income tax. The rate structure is slightly different from the employment table shown — verify exact rates for self-employment income with local counsel.
- [ ] **Inheritance and gift tax:** Korea has a progressive inheritance and gift tax (10%–50%) with significant exemptions (basic KRW200M, spouse KRW500M–KRW3B). Not modeled here but relevant for wealth transfer planning.
