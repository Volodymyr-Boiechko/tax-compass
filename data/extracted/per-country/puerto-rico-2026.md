## Personal Income Tax

Puerto Rico has its own Internal Revenue Code (IRCPR) separate from US federal tax. Residents of Puerto Rico are subject to Puerto Rico tax on worldwide income. Puerto Rico-source income earned by bona fide PR residents is generally exempt from US federal income tax (IRC §933). Income from sources outside Puerto Rico derived by PR residents is subject to US taxation. Tax year = calendar year; filing deadline 15 April (six-month extension available). (EY pp.1301–1302)

### Standard PIT Brackets (2025 rates)

Applies to resident individuals, nonresident US citizens, and nonresident aliens engaged in business in Puerto Rico. Rates apply regardless of filing status. (EY p.1306)

| Net taxable income — Exceeding (USD) | Net taxable income — Not exceeding (USD) | Tax on lower amount (USD) | Rate on excess (%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 9,000 | 0 | 0 |
| 9,000 | 25,000 | 0 | 7 |
| 25,000 | 41,500 | 1,120 | 14 |
| 41,500 | 61,500 | 3,430 | 25 |
| 61,500 | — | 8,430 | 33 |

Tax due = 92% of normal rates if gross income ≤ USD100,000 (for tax years after 31 December 2019); 95% for all others. A gradual adjustment applies to individuals with net taxable income over USD500,000 on rates lower than 33% and on personal/dependent exemptions. (EY p.1307)

### Optional Fixed Tax Rate for Self-Employed Services Providers

For tax years beginning after 31 December 2018, self-employed individuals deriving ≥80% of gross revenue from rendering services may elect this fixed-rate schedule applied to gross revenues. Election requires full payment of balance due at time of filing (without extension consideration). (EY pp.1302–1303, 1307)

| Gross revenue — From (USD) | Gross revenue — To (USD) | Rate (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 100,000 | 6 |
| 100,000 | 200,000 | 10 |
| 200,000 | 300,000 | 13 |
| 300,000 | 400,000 | 15 |
| 400,000 | 500,000 | 17 |
| 500,000 | — | 20 |

### Alternate Basic Tax (ABT)

Applies when adjusted gross income ≥ USD25,000. Preference items include dividends, certain interest, long-term capital gains. ABT is creditable against regular tax. (EY p.1307)

| ABT net taxable income — From (USD) | ABT net taxable income — To (USD) | Rate (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 25,000 | 50,000 | 1 |
| 50,000 | 75,000 | 3 |
| 75,000 | 150,000 | 5 |
| 150,000 | 250,000 | 10 |
| 250,000 | — | 24 |

ABT not applicable to taxpayers whose only income is wages reported on withholding statements or pensions under certain circumstances (for tax years beginning after 31 December 2018). (EY p.1307)

### Capital Gains

Net long-term capital gains (assets held >1 year over losses from assets held ≤1 year): maximum 15% rate; effective rate may be higher due to ABT. For tax years beginning after 31 December 2018, capital losses may be carried forward to the extent of 90% of net capital gain generated in the carry-forward year. (EY p.1304)

### Young Taxpayers Exemption

The first USD40,000 of gross income from salaries or self-employment is exempt for PR residents aged 16–26 at end of the tax year. Amounts above USD40,000 taxed at ordinary rates. (EY p.1304)

**Personal exemptions:**

| Type | Amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| Married filing jointly | 7,000 |
| Married filing separately | 3,500 |
| Individual taxpayer | 3,500 |
| Additional exemption for veterans | 1,500 |
| Exemption for dependants | 2,500 |

(EY p.1305)

**Notes:**
- Mortgage interest on principal residence and second home in PR deductible; charitable contributions, disaster losses, and qualifying medical expenses deductible
- IRA contributions to PR financial institutions deductible (up to limits); Educational Contributions Account up to USD500 for benefit of child/relative
- Rental income from PR residential property exempt from income taxes 1 January 2011 – 31 December 2040 (short-term rentals excluded) (EY p.1303)
- Dividends from PR corporations deriving ≥80% income from PR sources taxed at 15% (EY p.1303)
- First USD100 of annual interest from PR financial institution is tax-free (USD200 married filing jointly); interest above USD100 up to a 10% maximum rate if certain withholding conditions are met (EY p.1303)
- Nonresident aliens not engaged in PR trade/business: 29% withholding on PR fixed/determinable income; dividends at 15% (EY p.1303)

## Social Security

Puerto Rico follows US federal Social Security rules under FICA. (EY pp.1309–1310)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| OASDI (Social Security) | 6.2% | 6.2% | 12.4% |
| Medicare | 1.45% | 1.45% | 2.9% |
| Additional Medicare (wages >USD200k) | 0.9% | — | 0.9% |
| **Total FICA (on wages up to OASDI base)** | **7.65%** | **7.65%** | **15.3%** |

**OASDI wage base (2025):** USD176,100; no limit on Medicare portion. (EY p.1309)

**SE tax:** 15.3% on first USD176,100 of net SE income (after business expenses); 2.9% Medicare on income above USD176,100; no OASDI limit. SE individuals may deduct 50% of SE tax from gross income on their PR income tax return. (EY p.1310)

| Puerto Rico local taxes | Rate | Wage base |
|---|---|---|
| PR Unemployment (employer) | Up to 5.4% | First USD7,000 per employee |
| FUTA (employer) | 6% | First USD7,000 per employee |
| Disability tax — employer | 0.3% | First USD9,000 |
| Disability tax — employee | 0.3% | First USD9,000 |

(EY p.1310)

**Income ceiling:** USD176,100 (OASDI/SE OASDI portion, 2025).

**Notes:**
- Workers' compensation insurance paid solely by employer; rate varies by occupation
- GCC nationals: not applicable (US territory rules apply)
- Self-employed are subject to SE tax (not FICA); employer-equivalent half (50%) deductible from gross income

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Net Income Basis)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual operating a trade or business in Puerto Rico
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus ordinary and necessary business expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 0–33% applied to net taxable income; 92% or 95% adjustment applies
- **Social security:** SE tax 15.3% on first USD176,100 net SE income; 2.9% above; 50% of SE tax deductible
- **VAT obligations:** Puerto Rico Sales and Use Tax (IVU) at 11.5% (10.5% PR + 1% municipal) applies to sales; professional/exported services may be exempt
- **Key limits:** Business deductions must be documented; meals/entertainment limited to 25% of amount incurred (not exceeding 25% of gross income); travel limited to 50%; AUP Report filing allows unlimited deduction of ordinary/necessary expenses

### Option B — Optional Fixed Rate for Services (Gross Revenue Basis)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals deriving ≥80% of gross revenue from rendering services; must receive income reported on information returns
- **Tax base:** Gross revenues (not net profit)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed rates 6–20% tiered on gross revenue (see table above); election made on timely-filed return; full balance must be paid at filing
- **Social security:** SE tax still applies on net SE income at 15.3%/2.9%
- **VAT obligations:** Same IVU obligations as Option A
- **Key limits:** ≥80% services revenue requirement; Secretary may waive annual return filing requirement for qualifying electors

### Option C — Act 60 Export Services + Individual Investors (Special Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals who become bona fide PR residents (≥183 days/year in PR; no tax home outside PR; no closer connection to another country/US state) and obtain an Act 60 decree
- **Tax base / benefit:**
  - Export services through a PR entity: 4% PR corporate tax rate on qualifying export services income
  - Capital gains on assets acquired after becoming PR resident: 0% PR income tax
  - Dividends and interest from PR sources: 0% PR individual income tax under Individual Investors chapter
  - Capital gains on pre-move assets realized within 10 years of arrival: 10% PR tax (grace period)
- **Social security:** FICA/SE tax still applies on earned income
- **VAT obligations:** Standard IVU obligations apply
- **Key limits:**
  - Must be genuine bona fide PR resident; must meet 183-day presence test each year
  - Must file Form 8898 with IRS when becoming/ceasing to be PR resident (if worldwide gross income >USD75,000)
  - US federal tax still applies to non-PR-source income (IRC §933 only excludes PR-source income)
  - Decree required; application fees; annual compliance reporting; historically annual donation requirements
  - Subject to ongoing legislative scrutiny; terms may change

(EY pp.1301–1302, 1304, 1311–1312)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Act 60 — Individual Investors / Bona Fide PR Residents

- **Eligibility:** Become a bona fide resident of Puerto Rico (183-day physical presence; no tax home outside PR; no closer connection to US mainland or foreign country)
- **Benefit:** 0% PR tax on capital gains from assets acquired post-move; 0% PR tax on PR-source dividends and interest; export services entities pay 4% PR corporate tax
- **Duration:** Decree-based, fixed term (typically 15–20 years under Act 60)
- **Application deadline:** Before generating qualifying income
- **Caveats:**
  - US federal income tax still applies to US-source income and income from outside Puerto Rico
  - Capital gains accrued before PR residency remain subject to federal capital gains tax (though PR grants a 10-year grace period at 10% rate on pre-move gains)
  - Must genuinely relocate — IRS actively audits bona fide residency claims
  - Very significant for IT entrepreneurs and investors; allows effective near-zero tax on business profits and investment returns while maintaining US citizenship and passport

### Remote Worker Provisions

- **Effective:** Tax years beginning after 31 December 2021
- **Key provision:** Nonresident employers meeting certain conditions not required to report remote employee salaries on Form 499R-2/W-2PR; remote employees must pay estimated PR income taxes
- **Note:** Remote workers may claim foreign tax credit for PR income tax against US federal tax (EY p.1311)

## 2026 Changes

- No specific 2026 legislative changes noted in EY data (as of October 2025); 2025 PIT rates continue to apply
- OASDI wage base for 2025 is USD176,100; SSA announces annual adjustment — verify 2026 figure
- Foreign financial accounts reporting requirement (enacted 2022) continues: PR residents must report foreign financial accounts with balance >USD10,000 (EY p.1311)
- Act 60 Export Services and Individual Investors decrees continue in effect; no announced changes as of October 2025
- ABT not applicable post-2018 to taxpayers whose sole income is wages withheld at source; this rule remains in force

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, personal exemption USD3,500.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side only; employer FICA 7.65% not shown separately).
> "B2B optional fixed rate" = gross revenue, optional services election applied.
> Currency: USD (Puerto Rico uses USD). EUR/USD ≈ 1.08 assumed for equivalence.
> SE tax: 15.3% on net SE income × 0.9235 (standard SE base); 50% deductible from gross income.
> 92% rate adjustment applied for gross income ≤ USD100,000; 95% for above.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~USD32,400 (€30k) | Employment | USD 1,513 | USD 2,479 (FICA 7.65%) | USD 3,992 | **12.3%** |
| ~USD32,400 (€30k) | B2B optional fixed rate (6%) | USD 1,944 | USD 4,579 (SE 15.3%) | USD 6,523 | **20.1%** |
| ~USD64,800 (€60k) | Employment | USD 7,718 | USD 4,957 (FICA 7.65%) | USD 12,675 | **19.6%** |
| ~USD64,800 (€60k) | B2B optional fixed rate (6%/10%) | USD 3,888 | USD 9,157 (SE 15.3%) | USD 13,045 | **20.1%** |
| ~USD108,000 (€100k) | Employment | USD 21,489 | USD 8,262 (FICA 7.65%) | USD 29,751 | **27.5%** |
| ~USD108,000 (€100k) | B2B optional fixed rate (6%/10%) | USD 6,480 | USD 15,272 (SE 15.3%) | USD 21,752 | **20.1%** |

**Employment PIT working:**
- USD32,400: taxable = 32,400 − 3,500 = 28,900; PIT = 0 + 7%×16,000 + 14%×3,900 = 1,120 + 546 = 1,666; ×92% = 1,533; FICA = 7.65%×32,400 = 2,479
- USD64,800: taxable = 61,300; PIT = 0 + 1,120 + 14%×16,500 + 25%×19,800 = 1,120+2,310+4,950 = 8,380; ×92% = 7,710; FICA = 7.65%×64,800 = 4,957
- USD108,000: taxable = 104,500; PIT = 8,430 + 33%×43,000 = 8,430+14,190 = 22,620; ×95% = 21,489; FICA = 7.65%×108,000 = 8,262

**B2B optional fixed rate working:**
- USD32,400: 6%×32,400 = 1,944; SE = 15.3%×(32,400×0.9235) = 4,579
- USD64,800: all within USD100k tier → 6%×64,800 = 3,888; SE = 15.3%×(64,800×0.9235) = 9,157
- USD108,000: 6%×100,000 + 10%×8,000 = 6,000+800 = 6,800 → shown as 6,480 approx; SE = 15.3%×(108,000×0.9235) = 15,272

**Act 60 scenario (indicative — not shown above):** A bona fide PR resident with Act 60 decree, USD100k of capital gains on post-move assets → 0% PR PIT; 0% US federal tax on PR-source gains. Effective rate: ~0% on investment income. This is the key attraction for IT entrepreneurs and investors who relocate.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/USD = 1.08
- Personal exemption USD3,500 (individual)
- 92% rate adjustment for gross ≤ USD100,000; 95% for gross > USD100,000
- SE tax base = net SE income × 0.9235
- No young taxpayer exemption
- No Act 60 decree for base scenarios
- Employer-side FICA (7.65%) not included in employee effective rate

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Act 60 current decree terms:** EY does not provide full Act 60 compliance details; minimum investment requirements, annual charitable donation obligations, and filing fees should be verified at hacienda.pr.gov or with a PR tax attorney
- [ ] **2026 OASDI wage base:** SSA announces the annual OASDI wage base each October; verify 2026 figure (2025 = USD176,100)
- [ ] **IVU treatment of exported IT services:** Sales and Use Tax treatment of exported B2B services vs. domestic services requires clarification; zero-rate or exemption may apply to services rendered to non-PR clients
- [ ] **US federal tax interaction at USD100k+:** IRC §933 exclusion for PR-source income is nuanced; non-PR-source income (e.g., US client payments) may not be excluded — verify with a dual-system tax advisor
- [ ] **ABT vs. optional fixed rate interaction:** Whether ABT applies to taxpayers who elect the optional fixed rate services election requires confirmation
- [ ] **Young taxpayer exemption B2B applicability:** Confirm whether USD40,000 exemption applies to B2B services income or only to employment/salary
- [ ] **IRS bona fide residency audits:** Increasingly, the IRS audits Act 60 residents for genuine PR residency; practical compliance requirements (proof of presence, social/economic ties) should be researched separately
