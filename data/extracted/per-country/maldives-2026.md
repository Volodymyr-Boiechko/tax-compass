## Personal Income Tax

The Maldives introduced the **Income Tax Act** effective from 1 January 2020. Prior to this, the Maldives had no personal income tax. With the implementation, the Business Profits Tax and the remittance tax (and certain provisions of the land sales tax) were repealed, and individual income tax and employee withholding tax were introduced. (EY p. 947)

Tax is levied on individuals' **business income and all other forms of income** received by them. Individuals earning **only employment income** are required to pay only employee withholding tax and are **not required to file an annual individual income tax return**. (EY p. 947)

### Individual income tax brackets (annual taxable income in MVR)

| Annual taxable income (MVR) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| MVR 720,000 or less | **0%** |
| MVR 720,001 – MVR 1,200,000 | **5.5%** |
| MVR 1,200,001 – MVR 1,800,000 | **8%** |
| MVR 1,800,001 – MVR 2,400,000 | **12%** |
| Over MVR 2,400,000 | **15%** |

Maximum marginal rate: **15%** (EY p. 947)

**Key point:** The MVR 720,000/year tax-free threshold = MVR 60,000/month. This covers the vast majority of Maldivian workers — most individuals earning in MVR will pay no income tax. (EY p. 947)

**Employee withholding tax brackets (monthly remuneration subject to withholding in MVR):**

| Monthly remuneration (MVR) | Withholding tax rate |
|---|---|
| MVR 60,000 or less | **0%** |
| MVR 60,001 – MVR 100,000 | **5.5%** |
| MVR 100,001 – MVR 150,000 | **8%** |
| MVR 150,001 – MVR 200,000 | **12%** |
| Over MVR 200,000 | **15%** |

- Employers withhold and pay tax from remuneration paid to employees, effective from 1 April 2020
- "Remuneration subject to withholding tax" = total remuneration received in a month **after deducting** the employee's contribution to the **Maldives Retirement Pension Scheme**
- Remuneration includes allowances and benefits received in cash or in kind (EY pp. 947–948)

**Business income tax:**
- Imposed at **15%** on profits for all persons (excluding individuals accruing profits from businesses, who are subject to individual income tax rates above)
- First **MVR 500,000** of taxable profits is **exempt** (equivalent to approximately USD 32,425) (EY p. 948)
- Nonresident withholding tax (NWHT) at **10%** on management fees, technical services fees, royalties, and rent for immovable property paid to nonresidents (EY p. 948)
- Business losses: may be carried forward and deducted from profits of subsequent 5 tax years (EY p. 948)

**Capital gains:** No explicit capital gains tax regime mentioned; capital gains of businesses may be included in business income for Complementary Tax purposes.

**Goods and Services Tax (GST):**
- General rate: **8%** on goods and services supplied in the Maldives
- Tourism sector rate: **17%** (effective 1 July 2025) (EY p. 949)
- Green tax: USD 6/occupying guest (revised: USD 12/day for resort hotels and tourist vessels from 1 January 2025) (EY p. 949)

**Functional currency rule (from 2024 tax year):** If a taxpayer's functional currency is not MVR, all taxes under the Income Tax Act must be paid in **USD**. This applies to employee withholding tax, nonresident withholding tax, and capital gains withholding tax for payments relating to periods ending on or after 31 October 2024. (EY pp. 948–949)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Business expenses incurred in respect of business activities are deductible
- Receipts subject to withholding tax are deductible for business income tax purposes
- Private expenses are not deductible (EY p. 948)

## Social Security

**Maldives Retirement Pension Scheme:**

| Contributor | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee | **7%** of gross salary | Deducted from gross; reduces remuneration subject to withholding tax |
| Employer | **7%** of gross salary | Paid by employer on top of salary |

- Pension contributions are deducted before calculating employee withholding tax base (EY p. 947)
- No other explicit social security contributions mentioned in EY guide for individuals

**Income ceiling:** Not specified — appears to apply to all salary levels.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Business Income (Income Tax Act)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual receiving business income in the Maldives
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses); first MVR 500,000 exempt
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive individual income tax brackets (0%–15%); first MVR 720,000 of total income at 0%; same brackets as employment income
- **Social security:** Pension scheme contributions may apply if applicable
- **VAT/GST obligations:** GST registration required if turnover exceeds MVR 1,000,000/year; general rate 8%; tourism 17%
- **Business losses:** Carryforward up to 5 years
- **Filing:** Annual income tax return required (unlike pure employment income earners)
- **Nonresident withholding:** 10% NWHT on specified payments to nonresidents

### Option B — Company (Business Profit Tax / Income Tax)

- **Who qualifies:** Legal entities and persons with business income
- **Tax base:** Taxable profits after deductible expenses; MVR 500,000 exemption
- **Rate:** 15% on profits above MVR 500,000 (EY p. 948)
- **Filing:** Interim returns (31 July: half of prior year tax; 31 January: second half); final return 30 June of following year

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Work Permits

- Foreigners going to the Maldives for employment must obtain a **work permit** from the employer; Maldives Immigration requires employers to deposit a sum of money as a work permit deposit per employee
- Monthly visa fee: **MVR 350** for foreign workers
- **Business visa:** Up to 90 days for MVR 750 from a Maldivian sponsor; extension of additional 90 days possible if total days in calendar year do not exceed 180 (EY p. 949)

### No Territorial Exemption for Residents

- The Maldives taxes residents on their worldwide income (business income and all other forms received); territorial basis does not apply to residents

## 2026 Changes

- **Tourism GST rate increase (EY p. 949):** Tourism sector GST rate increased to **17%** effective 1 July 2025 (was lower previously)
- **Green tax increase (EY p. 949):** Green tax revised to USD 12/day at resort hotels, tourist vessels, and hotels in uninhabited islands from 1 January 2025; USD 6/day for hotels/guesthouses in inhabited islands with ≤50 rooms; USD 12/day for those with >50 rooms
- **Functional currency USD payments (EY pp. 948–949):** From October 2024, taxpayers with non-MVR functional currency must pay taxes in USD; applicable for periods ending on/after 31 October 2024 — now fully in effect
- **No changes to individual income tax rates or brackets** announced for 2026

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Maldives uses MVR (Maldivian Rufiyaa). **1 EUR ≈ MVR 16.5** (approximate; MVR is pegged to USD; USD/MVR ≈ 15.4; verify at mma.gov.mv).
> Employee pension contribution: 7% deducted before applying withholding tax brackets.
> Income tax is on remuneration AFTER 7% pension deduction.

### €30,000 Employment
- Gross: EUR 30,000 × 16.5 = MVR 495,000/year (MVR 41,250/month)
- Monthly ≤ MVR 60,000 → withholding tax rate: **0%**
- Annual income ≤ MVR 720,000 → individual income tax: **0%**
- Pension (employee 7%): MVR 495,000 × 7% = MVR 34,650
- Total burden: MVR 34,650 (pension only)
- Effective rate: 34,650 / 495,000 = **7.0%** (entirely pension)

### €60,000 Employment
- Gross: EUR 60,000 × 16.5 = MVR 990,000/year (MVR 82,500/month)
- After 7% pension: MVR 82,500 × 93% = MVR 76,725/month net remuneration
- Annual net remuneration: MVR 76,725 × 12 = MVR 920,700
- Annual taxable (for individual income tax): MVR 920,700
- 0% on MVR 720,000 = MVR 0
- 5.5% on MVR 200,700 = MVR 11,039
- Total income tax: MVR 11,039
- Pension (employee): MVR 990,000 × 7% = MVR 69,300
- Total burden: MVR 11,039 + MVR 69,300 = MVR 80,339
- Effective rate: 80,339 / 990,000 = **8.1%**

### €100,000 Employment
- Gross: EUR 100,000 × 16.5 = MVR 1,650,000/year
- After 7% pension: MVR 1,650,000 × 93% = MVR 1,534,500 net annual remuneration
- Annual income tax:
  - 0% on MVR 720,000 = MVR 0
  - 5.5% on MVR 480,000 (720k–1.2M) = MVR 26,400
  - 8% on MVR 334,500 (1.2M–1,534,500) = MVR 26,760
  - Total income tax: MVR 53,160
- Pension (employee): MVR 1,650,000 × 7% = MVR 115,500
- Total burden: MVR 53,160 + MVR 115,500 = MVR 168,660
- Effective rate: 168,660 / 1,650,000 = **10.2%**

### Summary table

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (MVR) | Pension 7% (MVR) | Total (MVR) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (MVR 495,000) | Employment | MVR 0 | MVR 34,650 | MVR 34,650 | **7.0%** |
| €30,000 | Self-employed | MVR 0 | N/A (voluntary) | MVR 0 | **0.0%** |

| €60,000 | Self-employed | ~MVR 11,039 | N/A | ~MVR 11,039 | **1.1%** |

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/MVR = 16.5 (approximate; MVR pegged to USD at ~15.4; verify at mma.gov.mv)
- Employee pension: 7% deducted from gross before withholding tax calculation
- Self-employed pension: voluntary (assumed not applicable for B2B scenario); MVR 500,000 business income exemption not applied at these gross income levels (exemption is from business profits, not total income)
- Self-employed scenario applies individual income tax brackets to total income (MVR 720,000 first bracket at 0%)
- At €30,000 gross, self-employed income ≈ MVR 495,000 which is below MVR 720,000 threshold → 0% PIT
- No GST included in scenarios (affects pricing to clients, not personal income tax directly)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Pension scheme applicability to self-employed:** EY describes pension deductions only in the context of employees; whether self-employed individuals participate in the Maldives Retirement Pension Scheme needs confirmation with Maldives Pension Administration Office.
- [ ] **EUR/MVR exchange rate:** MVR is pegged to USD (USD 1 = MVR 15.42 fixed peg); EUR/MVR fluctuates with EUR/USD; verify at Maldives Monetary Authority (mma.gov.mv).
- [ ] **Business income MVR 500,000 exemption:** This exemption applies to business profits tax; the EY text is ambiguous about whether it interacts with the individual progressive brackets or only applies to the corporate-style 15% rate.
- [ ] **Social security beyond pension:** No other social security mentioned; confirm no additional health or unemployment contributions exist.
- [ ] **Remittance basis / worldwide income for residents:** Whether Maldivian residents pay tax on worldwide income or only Maldivian-source income is not fully clarified in EY; assumed worldwide for residents based on text.
- [ ] **No capital gains tax details:** EY confirms no capital gains tax levy; whether foreign-source capital gains of residents are taxable is not addressed.
