## Personal Income Tax

> Finland has a schedular income tax system: earned income (employment and business) is subject to progressive national income tax plus a flat municipal tax (4.7–10.9%), church tax (optional), and YLE broadcasting tax. Capital income is taxed at 30% (34% above €30,000). Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income.

**National income tax rates (2025):**

| Taxable earned income (EUR) | Tax on lower amount (EUR) | Rate on excess % |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 21,200 | 0.00 | 12.64 |
| 21,200 – 31,500 | 2,679.68 | 19.00 |
| 31,500 – 52,100 | 4,636.68 | 30.25 |
| 52,100 – 88,200 | 10,868.18 | 34.00 |
| 88,200 – 150,000 | 23,142.18 | 41.75 |
| 150,000+ | 48,943.68 | 44.25 |

*(EY p. 421)*

**Municipal tax:** Flat rate varying by municipality, 4.7–10.9% of taxable income (average approximately 7.5%)

**Church tax:** 1.0–2.25% for members of Finnish congregations (optional — only applies to members)

**YLE tax:** 2.5% on income exceeding €15,150; capped at €160/year (EY p. 421)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Standard deduction from salary income: up to €750
- Travel expenses between home and workplace: excess above €900, up to €7,000
- Labour union fees: deductible
- Expenses incurred in earning income above €750 (actual costs)
- Employee pension insurance and unemployment insurance contributions are deductible
- Capital income deductions: interest on home loans (30% of excess capital expenses, up to €1,400 single/€2,800 couple); deductibility of pension insurance premiums (ceases 2027)

**Notes:**
- Capital gains taxed at 30% (34% on capital income exceeding €30,000/year)
- Nonresidents: flat 35% withholding on salary; may apply for progressive taxation instead
- 6-month rule: Finnish residents working abroad continuously for ≥6 months may be exempt from Finnish tax on foreign salary if taxed in the source country

## Social Security

> Social security in Finland covers pension insurance, health insurance, unemployment insurance, and accident insurance. Employees pay contributions on top of employer contributions; self-employed pay YEL (entrepreneur pension) contributions.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed (YEL) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension insurance (TyEL) | 7.15% | 17.38% (avg) | 24.1% of confirmed income |
| Health insurance — per diem | 1.45% | 1.87% | 1.04% |
| Health insurance — Medicare | 1.06% | included | included |
| Unemployment insurance | 0.59% (salary) | 0.20% | not applicable |
| Compulsory pension (TyEL) | 8.65% (age 53–62) | — | 25.6% (age 53–62) |
| **Total (approx.)** | **~8.84%** | **~20.65%** | **~24.1–25.6%** |

**Income ceiling:** Employer pension contributions ceiling EUR 2,455,000 (EY p. 423–424)
**Notes:**
- YEL (Yrittäjän eläkevakuutus): Mandatory for self-employed earning >€9,208/year; contributions are 24.1% of "confirmed annual income" (not necessarily actual income)
- Starting entrepreneurs: reduced YEL rate of 18.8% (age <53) or 19.97% (age 53–62) for first 48 months
- Employee pension premium 8.65% applies to those aged 53–62; otherwise 7.15%
- Employee social security contributions are deductible from earned income for tax purposes

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed persons in Finland operate as sole traders (toiminimi / enskild firma) or through a limited company (Oy). The mandatory YEL pension contribution is a key cost driver.

### Option A — Sole Trader (Toiminimi / Yksityinen elinkeinonharjoittaja)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying out business activity in their own name
- **Tax base:** Net business income apportioned between capital income (30% notional return on business assets, taxed at 30/34%) and earned income (taxed at progressive rates)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Capital portion: 30% (or 34% above €30,000); earned income portion: progressive national + municipal tax (~35–55% marginal for higher earners)
- **Social security:** YEL pension contributions mandatory (24.1% of confirmed income); health insurance contributions
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above €15,000 annual turnover; standard VAT rate 25.5% (raised from 24% in 2024)
- **Key limits:** Business losses can be offset against capital income; actual business expenses fully deductible; YEL confirmed income ≠ actual income — can be set independently

### Option B — Entrepreneur's Deduction (Yrittäjävähennys)
- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals (sole traders, partnerships) — applies to business income portion of earned income
- **Tax base:** 5% deduction from net business income taxed as earned income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Reduces the earned income portion by 5% before applying progressive tax rates
- **Social security:** Normal YEL applies
- **VAT obligations:** Standard rules
- **Key limits:** Applies only to earned-income portion; 5% deduction is automatic

### Option C — Limited Company (Osakeyhtiö / Oy)
- **Who qualifies:** Entrepreneurs wanting income-splitting between salary and dividends
- **Tax base:** Company profits at 20% CIT; salary to owner taxed as earned income; dividends partially tax-exempt (85% taxable if from listed company)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Dividend from private company: 75% taxable as capital income (30/34%), 25% tax-exempt if dividend ≤8% of shares' mathematical value and total ≤€150,000; excess fully taxable
- **Social security:** Owner must pay YEL on salary/professional income
- **VAT obligations:** Standard 25.5% VAT
- **Key limits:** Must comply with dividend calculation rules; thin capitalisation rules apply

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Tax Regime (Ulkomaalaisten avainhenkilölaki)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign key employees coming to work in Finland; must not have been Finnish tax resident in 5 years preceding employment; must earn at least €5,800/month
- **Benefit:** Flat 32% final tax rate on Finnish-source salary (instead of progressive rates which can reach ~55%); applies for up to 84 months (extended from 48 months under 2024 legislation for new cases from 1 January 2024 — EY p. 419)
- **Duration:** Maximum 84 months (7 years) for employees beginning after 1 January 2024; 48 months for others
- **Application deadline:** Application must be filed before first day of work in Finland
- **Caveats:** Flat 32% covers only salary income; capital income and other income taxed at normal rates; not available for Finnish citizens; must remain employed throughout

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 418–430)

- Pension insurance deductibility from capital income will no longer be available from 2027 — accelerating use of existing allowances in 2025–2026
- VAT standard rate increased to 25.5% in September 2024 — fully in effect for 2026
- Municipal tax rates are municipality-specific and updated annually
- Expatriate regime extended to 84 months for new cases from 2024 — first beneficiaries reaching original 48-month limit can now be extended

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, resident in Helsinki (municipal rate ~9.3%). Standard deductions only.
> "Employment" = gross salary. "B2B" = sole trader revenue after YEL contributions and minimal business costs.

**Employment at €30,000:**
1. Employee social security: ~€30,000 × 8.84% ≈ €2,652 (deductible)
2. Deductible SS reduces taxable income: €30,000 − €2,652 − €750 (standard deduction) = €26,598
3. National tax: €2,679.68 + (€26,598 − €21,200) × 19% = €2,679.68 + €1,025.62 = €3,705
4. Municipal tax (Helsinki ~9.3%): €26,598 × 9.3% = €2,474
5. YLE tax: (€26,598 − €15,150) × 2.5% = €286 (capped at €160)
6. Total income tax = €3,705 + €2,474 + €160 = €6,339; total burden = €6,339 + €2,652 = €8,991
7. Effective rate = €8,991 / €30,000 = **30.0%**

**Employment at €60,000:**
1. Employee SS: €60,000 × 8.84% = €5,304 (deductible)
2. Taxable: €60,000 − €5,304 − €750 = €53,946
3. National tax: €10,868.18 + (€53,946 − €52,100) × 34% = €10,868 + €628 = €11,496
4. Municipal: €53,946 × 9.3% = €5,017
5. YLE: capped at €160
6. Total tax = €11,496 + €5,017 + €160 = €16,673; total burden = €16,673 + €5,304 = €21,977
7. Effective rate = €21,977 / €60,000 = **36.6%**

**Employment at €100,000:**
1. Employee SS: €100,000 × 8.84% = €8,840 (deductible)
2. Taxable: €100,000 − €8,840 − €750 = €90,410
3. National tax: €23,142.18 + (€90,410 − €88,200) × 41.75% = €23,142 + €923 = €24,065
4. Municipal: €90,410 × 9.3% = €8,408
5. YLE: capped at €160
6. Total tax = €24,065 + €8,408 + €160 = €32,633; total burden = €32,633 + €8,840 = €41,473
7. Effective rate = €41,473 / €100,000 = **41.5%**

**B2B (sole trader) at €60,000 revenue:**
1. YEL pension: assuming confirmed income €40,000 → YEL = €40,000 × 24.1% = €9,640
2. Business costs ~10% = €6,000; net income = €54,000 − €9,640 = €44,360
3. Yrittäjävähennys 5%: €44,360 × 5% = €2,218 deduction → taxable = €42,142
4. National tax: €4,636.68 + (€42,142 − €31,500) × 30.25% = €4,637 + €3,219 = €7,856
5. Municipal: €42,142 × 9.3% = €3,919; YLE capped €160
6. Total tax = €7,856 + €3,919 + €160 = €11,935; total burden = €11,935 + €9,640 = €21,575
7. Effective rate = €21,575 / €60,000 = **36.0%**

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €6,339 | €2,652 | €8,991 | **30.0%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€4,500 | ~€4,823 | ~€9,323 | **~31.1%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €16,673 | €5,304 | €21,977 | **36.6%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | €11,935 | €9,640 | €21,575 | **36.0%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €32,633 | €8,840 | €41,473 | **41.5%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€26,000 | ~€16,000 | ~€42,000 | **~42.0%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, resident Helsinki (municipal rate 9.3%), no dependants
- Employee SS rate ~8.84% (pension 7.15% + health 1.45% + unemployment contribution); fully deductible
- Standard salary deduction €750
- YEL confirmed income set at 2/3 of actual revenue for sole trader estimate
- Yrittäjävähennys (entrepreneur deduction) 5% applied
- YLE tax capped at €160
- Church tax excluded
