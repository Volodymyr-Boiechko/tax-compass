## Personal Income Tax

Latvia taxes residents on worldwide income; nonresidents on Latvia-source income only. Resident if: (a) registered place of residence is in Latvia, (b) present ≥183 days in a 12-month period beginning or ending in the tax year, or (c) Latvian citizen employed abroad by Latvian government. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return filed 1 March – 1 June of following year (1 April – 1 July if income > EUR105,300). (EY pp. 844–854)

### Employment Income Tax Rates

| Annual taxable income (EUR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to 105,300 | **25.5%** |
| Above 105,300 | **33%** |

**Additional 3% on income exceeding EUR200,000** (annual income tax return; includes investment income and capital gains in the EUR200,000 threshold). (EY p. 849)

**Note on 25.5% rate:** Includes progressive structure — the Latvian system nominally has 20%, 23%, and 31% brackets, but the effective combined rates (after the differentiated non-taxable minimum) are commonly summarized as 25.5% up to EUR105,300 and 33% above. (EY pp. 844–845, 849)

### Digital Nomad Visa — Preferential 15% Rate

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals holding a Digital Nomad Visa performing remote work for an employer registered in an OECD member state or self-employed persons registered in an OECD member state
- **Rate:** **15% flat personal income tax** on income received in connection with remote work
- **Duration:** Applied within 365 days from the day the foreigner became a Latvian resident
- **Note:** Only available during the first year of Latvian residency (EY p. 845)

### Differentiated Non-Taxable Minimum (NTM)

- **2025 NTM:** EUR510/month (EUR6,120/year) — applied to all resident individuals as a reduction of taxable income
- **2026 NTM (already set):** EUR550/month (EUR6,600/year)
- **2027 NTM:** EUR570/month (EUR6,840/year)
- Subject to taper: the NTM is reduced at higher income levels (EY p. 848)

**Additional allowances:**
- Per child per month: EUR250 (for parents)
- Private pension contributions: up to 10% of annual taxable income, max EUR4,000/year (sum of pension fund + life insurance)
- Health/accident insurance: up to 10% of taxable income, max EUR750/year
- Medical expenses + professional education + charitable donations: up to 50% of annual taxable income, max EUR600/year

### Capital Gains

- **Rate:** 25.5% on capital gains (same as income from capital)
- Capital gains = disposal price minus acquisition price of capital assets
- Capital gains on sale of principal residence are **exempt** if: owned >60 months and was declared residence for ≥12 months before sale; OR registered in Land Register >60 months and was the only real estate; OR was sole real estate and sale proceeds invested in similar property within 12 months before/after sale (EY p. 847)

### Investment Income

- Dividends: **exempt** if distributed from profit taxed at corporate level after 1 January 2018, or from foreign profit subject to CIT equivalent (EY p. 845)
- Non-qualifying dividends, interest income, investment account income: **25.5%**
- Capital gains: **25.5%**

### Rental Income

- Registered rent agreement: **10%** final tax if registered with tax authorities within 5 business days and no substantial business expenses
- Otherwise: taxed at progressive rates (25.5%/33%)

## Social Security

Mandatory social security contributions apply to employment income. Minimum base: minimum monthly salary **EUR740/month** (2025). Income ceiling for standard SS: **EUR105,300/year**. Above EUR105,300: **solidarity tax** (25%) applies instead of SS. (EY pp. 850–851)

### Standard Social Security (up to EUR105,300/year)

| Contribution | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employer | **23.59%** | Of gross employment income |
| Employee | **10.5%** | Of gross employment income |
| **Combined** | **34.09%** | |

**Foreign employees without Latvian SS (non-EU employer, present >183 days):** Pay quarterly at **31.83%** combined rate.

### Solidarity Tax (above EUR105,300/year ceiling)

On income exceeding EUR105,300, instead of standard SS, a **solidarity tax of 25%** is levied, allocated as follows:
- 1% health insurance (0.5% employee + 0.5% employer)
- 10% PIT contribution (employee side, credited against 33% PIT liability)
- 14% state pension insurance (employer side)

**Net effect for employees above EUR105,300:** The employee's solidarity tax contribution (10.5%) above the ceiling is credited against their income tax; employer pays 23.59% standard SS up to the ceiling, then 14.5% solidarity tax above it. The employer receives a refund of the difference between 23.59% and 14.5% (= 9.09%) by 1 September of the following year. (EY p. 850)

### Microenterprise Tax (alternative)

- An individual performing qualifying business activities may elect the **microenterprise tax** at a flat **25% of revenue** — covers both income tax and SS contributions
- Only income from the individual's business activities covered; employees of microenterprises taxed at standard rates (EY p. 850)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment

- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals, sole traders, and persons with registered economic activity in Latvia
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus eligible business expenses; same rates as employment (25.5%/33%); additional 3% above EUR200,000
- **Social security:** Employee side (10.5%) AND employer side (23.59%) paid by self-employed — effectively 34.09% combined SS on income up to EUR105,300; solidarity tax above
- **VAT obligations:** Latvian VAT 21%; registration mandatory if turnover > EUR50,000/year
- **Key limits:** Tax losses may be carried forward 3 years; loss carryforward permitted

### Option B — Microenterprise Tax

- **Who qualifies:** Individual self-employed persons with qualifying business activities in specified areas (determined by law); not available for all professions
- **Rate:** 25% of gross revenue (replaces both income tax and SS)
- **No expense deductions:** Tax based on gross revenue
- **Cap:** Available at the individual level; employees (if any) remain subject to standard payroll rules
- **Key limits:** Check qualifying activities list; income of employees of the microenterprise is taxed as standard employment income

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Digital Nomad Visa (Category D — Long-Term)

- **Eligibility:** Citizens of third countries employed by employer registered in an OECD member state, or self-employed persons registered in an OECD member state, performing duties remotely while staying in Latvia
- **Benefit:** **15% flat PIT** on remote work income during the first year (365 days) of Latvian residency
- **Duration:** One year only; after 365 days, standard rates (25.5%/33%) apply (EY pp. 845, 853)
- **Visa:** Long-term visa (Category D) for remote work; valid for one year with extension possible; employer must be in OECD member state

### EU Citizens

- EU/EEA/Swiss nationals do not require a residence permit for stays ≤90 days in a 180-day period; longer stays require registration and residence card
- Registration is straightforward; standard PIT rates apply

## 2026 Changes

- **Non-taxable minimum for 2026 already set:** EUR550/month (EUR6,600/year) — increased from EUR510/month in 2025 (EY p. 848)
- **Non-taxable minimum for 2027 pre-set:** EUR570/month (EUR6,840/year)
- **No structural PIT rate changes for 2026** announced — 25.5%/33% rates remain; 3% surcharge on income > EUR200,000 continues
- **Minimum social security base:** EUR740/month (2025); verify 2026 amount
- **Latvia-Russia DTT terminated from 1 January 2024** per Article 31 (EY p. 852)
- **DTT with Andorra and Pakistan signed** but not yet in force (EY p. 852)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants; NTM EUR550/month = EUR6,600/year (2026 rate) applied.
> Currency: EUR (Latvia uses EUR as Eurozone member).
> Employment: 25.5% PIT (income ≤ EUR105,300) + employee SS 10.5%.
> B2B (self-employed): 25.5%/33% PIT on net profit + employee SS 10.5% + employer SS 23.59% (both sides).
> No pension, insurance, or medical deductions modeled.

### €30,000 Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | | €30,000 |
| Non-taxable minimum (2026) | EUR550/month × 12 | €6,600 |
| Taxable income | 30,000 − 6,600 | €23,400 |
| Income tax: 25.5% × 23,400 | | €5,967 |
| Employee SS: 10.5% × 30,000 | | €3,150 |
| **Total burden** | 5,967 + 3,150 | **€9,117** |
| **Effective rate** | 9,117 / 30,000 | **30.4%** |

### €30,000 B2B (self-employed)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue | | €30,000 |
| Assumed business costs (20%) | | €6,000 |
| Net profit | | €24,000 |
| Non-taxable minimum | | €6,600 |
| Taxable income | 24,000 − 6,600 | €17,400 |
| Income tax: 25.5% × 17,400 | | €4,437 |
| SS (both employee + employer sides): 34.09% × 30,000 | | €10,227 |
| **Total burden** | 4,437 + 10,227 | **€14,664** |
| **Effective rate on gross revenue** | 14,664 / 30,000 | **48.9%** |

**Note:** Self-employment SS is very high in Latvia because both employer (23.59%) and employee (10.5%) sides are paid by the self-employed person. The microenterprise tax option (25% of gross revenue) is significantly cheaper at this level: 25% × 30,000 = €7,500 — effective rate on gross = 25%.

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **NTM taper:** The differentiated non-taxable minimum is reduced at higher income levels (taper rules). At €60k and €100k, the full EUR6,600 NTM may not apply. Exact taper schedule not extracted from EY — verify at vid.gov.lv.
- [ ] **Microenterprise qualifying activities:** The microenterprise tax (25% of gross revenue) is only available for specific business activities. Not all self-employed professionals qualify. Verify eligible activities with local accountant.
- [ ] **SS for self-employed above EUR105,300:** Above the EUR105,300 SS ceiling, solidarity tax (25%) applies instead of SS. The effective total rate above the ceiling is different — the solidarity tax breakdown (1% health, 10% PIT contribution, 14% pension) changes the calculation. At €100k, this is not yet relevant.
- [ ] **Digital nomad 15% rate duration:** The 15% rate applies only for the first 365 days of Latvian residency. After that, the standard 25.5%/33% rates apply. This is a meaningful benefit for the first year but requires a rate change in planning from Year 2 onwards.
- [ ] **3% solidarity surcharge:** The additional 3% on annual income > EUR200,000 is not modeled (all examples below EUR200k). Relevant for very high earners.
- [ ] **Dividend exemption planning:** Dividends from qualifying Latvian companies (CIT-paid profits post-2018) are fully exempt from personal income tax. For owner-managed businesses, retaining profits and distributing as dividends is potentially tax-free at the personal level — though the 20/0% CIT regime and distribution rules must be verified.
- [ ] **Capital gains on principal residence:** Exempt under specific conditions (owned >5 years, declared as primary residence). Relevant for expats purchasing property in Latvia for long-term residence.
