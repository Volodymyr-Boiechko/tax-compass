## Personal Income Tax

> Latvia has a progressive personal income tax system. The tax year is the calendar year. Residents are taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Latvian-source income. A solidarity tax applies to income above the social security ceiling (EUR 105,300). A differentiated non-taxable minimum applies for residents.

**PIT rates (employment and self-employment income):**

| Taxable income (EUR/year) | Rate % |
|---|---|
| Up to 105,300 | 25.5 |
| Above 105,300 | 33 |

**Additional solidarity tax:** 3% payable via annual tax return on annual income exceeding EUR 200,000 (includes investment income and capital gains) (EY p. 849)

**Differentiated non-taxable minimum:**
- 2025: EUR 510/month (EUR 6,120/year)
- 2026: EUR 550/month (EUR 6,600/year) — already set by legislation (EY p. 848)
- 2027: EUR 570/month (EUR 6,840/year)
- The non-taxable minimum is applied proportionally; partially phased out at higher incomes

**Child deduction:** EUR 250/child/month (deducted from taxable income)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Employees' portion of social security contributions (10.5%): deductible from gross income
- Private pension fund contributions + life insurance (with accumulation): up to 10% of annual taxable income, max EUR 4,000/year
- Medical expenses and professional education: up to 50% of annual taxable income, max EUR 600/year
- Charitable donations: deductible within limits

**Notes:**
- Digital Nomad visa holders: flat 15% PIT rate on remote work income from OECD-member country employers/clients; available within first 365 days of becoming Latvian resident (EY p. 845)
- Capital gains: 25.5% flat rate; real estate exemptions apply (owned >60 months + declared residence for ≥12 months)
- Rental income: optional 10% flat rate if rent agreement registered within 5 business days of conclusion (EY p. 846)
- Property tax: 0.2%–0.6% of cadastral value on residential properties not used for commercial purposes

## Social Security

> Latvia has mandatory state social security contributions (VSAOI). Employer rate 23.59%; employee rate 10.5%. Income ceiling EUR 105,300/year. Above the ceiling, solidarity tax applies at 25% total (structured as continued contributions but credited partly to PIT). Minimum base: EUR 740/month (2025).

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (up to EUR 105,300) | 10.5% | 23.59% | 31.83% (non-EU employer) |
| Solidarity tax (above EUR 105,300) | 10% (credited to PIT at 33%) | 14.5% | — |
| **Total (up to ceiling)** | **10.5%** | **23.59%** | **31.83%** |

**Income ceiling:** EUR 105,300/year (for standard 10.5%/23.59% rates) (EY p. 850)
**Notes:**
- Employee 10.5%: deductible from gross income for PIT purposes
- Employer 23.59%: not an employee cost but an employer burden above gross salary
- Self-employed: 31.83% on quarterly basis if employed by non-EU employer without permanent establishment in Latvia (EY p. 850)
- Solidarity tax (income above EUR 105,300): total 25% split: 1% health (0.5% employee + 0.5% employer); 10% employee PIT supplement; 14% employer pension — employee's 10% credited to 33% income tax due
- Minimum monthly social security base: EUR 740/month (2025); employer covers difference if employee earns less
- Microenterprise tax: 25% flat (replaces PIT + SS for certain self-employed); available to eligible sole traders (EY p. 850)

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals pay PIT at the same progressive rates as employed persons. Taxable income = gross income minus eligible expenses. Solidarity tax applies above EUR 105,300.

### Option A — Self-Employed (Pašnodarbinātā persona)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered with the State Revenue Service (VID) as self-employed
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus eligible business expenses; same PIT rates (25.5%/33%)
- **Rate / mechanism:** 25.5% up to EUR 105,300; 33% above; solidarity tax above EUR 105,300
- **Social security:** Self-employed pay contributions quarterly; rate depends on registration: if client is Latvian entity, contributions may be split; otherwise 31.83% self-employed rate
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above EUR 40,000 annual turnover; standard VAT rate 21%
- **Key limits:** Losses may be carried forward 3 years; actual business expenses deductible (materials, salaries, rent, depreciation)

### Option B — Microenterprise Tax Regime
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals performing business activities in specific areas determined by law (not generally available to all freelancers); includes some florists, clothing/footwear repair workers, etc.
- **Tax base:** 25% flat on total revenue — replaces both PIT and social security
- **Rate / mechanism:** 25% total tax burden; significantly simplified administration
- **Social security:** Included in the 25% microenterprise tax
- **VAT obligations:** Standard rules apply
- **Key limits:** Applies only to income from the individual's business activities; employment income of employees is still taxed under standard rules

### Option C — Digital Nomad (15% Flat Rate)
- **Who qualifies:** Foreign nationals who have become Latvian tax residents and work remotely for an employer/client registered in an OECD member state; must apply within 365 days of becoming Latvian resident
- **Tax base:** Income from remote work for the OECD-registered employer/client
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat 15% PIT
- **Social security:** Standard social security rules apply separately
- **VAT obligations:** Standard rules
- **Key limits:** 365-day window from first day of Latvian tax residency; only applies to remote work income from OECD employers (EY p. 845)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Digital Nomad Visa
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals employed by or performing services for an employer/client registered in an OECD member state; work performed remotely from Latvia
- **Benefit:** Flat 15% PIT rate on remote work income (vs standard 25.5%/33%); valid for up to 1 year (extendable)
- **Duration:** 1 year, extendable
- **Application deadline:** Must apply within 365 days of becoming Latvian tax resident
- **Caveats:** Standard social security contributions still apply; only OECD-source income qualifies; must maintain actual remote work activity in Latvia

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 844–856)

- **Non-taxable minimum increases to EUR 550/month (EUR 6,600/year)** from 1 January 2026 (already set by legislation; 2025: EUR 510/month) (EY p. 848)
- **2027 non-taxable minimum: EUR 570/month** — already legislated
- Solidarity tax structure unchanged for 2026
- Russia–Latvia DTT: convention with Russian Federation lost validity from 1 January 2024 (EY p. 852)
- Minimum social security base expected to be updated for 2026 (2025: EUR 740/month)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, Latvian tax resident, standard deductions only.
> Latvia uses EUR. Non-taxable minimum EUR 550/month (EUR 6,600/year) used for 2026.

**Employment at €30,000:**
1. Employee SS (10.5%) = €30,000 × 10.5% = €3,150 (deductible)
2. Taxable income = €30,000 − €3,150 − non-taxable minimum €6,600 = €20,250
3. PIT: €20,250 × 25.5% = €5,164
4. Total burden = €5,164 + €3,150 = €8,314
5. Effective rate = €8,314 / €30,000 = **27.7%**

**Employment at €60,000:**
1. Employee SS = €60,000 × 10.5% = €6,300 (deductible)
2. Taxable = €60,000 − €6,300 − €6,600 = €47,100
3. PIT: €47,100 × 25.5% = €12,011
4. Total = €12,011 + €6,300 = €18,311
5. Effective rate = €18,311 / €60,000 = **30.5%**

**Employment at €100,000:**
1. Employee SS = €100,000 × 10.5% = €10,500 (deductible; below EUR 105,300 ceiling)
2. Taxable = €100,000 − €10,500 − €6,600 = €82,900
3. PIT: €82,900 × 25.5% = €21,140
4. Total = €21,140 + €10,500 = €31,640
5. Effective rate = €31,640 / €100,000 = **31.6%**

**B2B (self-employed) at €60,000 revenue:**
1. Business costs ~10% = €6,000; gross net = €54,000
2. Self-employed SS (~31.83% or simplified): assume standard 34.09% total (23.59% + 10.5%): €54,000 × 34.09% = €18,409 (deductible)
3. Taxable = €54,000 − €18,409 − €6,600 = €28,991
4. PIT: €28,991 × 25.5% = €7,393
5. Total = €7,393 + €18,409 = €25,802
6. Effective rate = €25,802 / €60,000 = **43.0%**

*Note: B2B rate is high because self-employed bear both employee (10.5%) and employer (23.59%) social security. Using the Microenterprise Tax (25% flat) could lower the overall rate significantly for eligible activities.*

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €5,164 | €3,150 | €8,314 | **27.7%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€4,000 | ~€10,200 | ~€14,200 | **~47.3%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €12,011 | €6,300 | €18,311 | **30.5%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | €7,393 | €18,409 | €25,802 | **43.0%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €21,140 | €10,500 | €31,640 | **31.6%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€18,000 | ~€34,090 | ~€52,090 | **~52.1%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants, Latvian resident
- Non-taxable minimum EUR 550/month (EUR 6,600/year) for 2026
- Employment SS: employee 10.5% (deductible); employer 23.59% (not in employee burden above)
- B2B: combined SS rate 34.09% (employer + employee equivalent) borne by self-employed; 10% business costs assumed
- EUR currency — no conversion needed
- Microenterprise Tax (25% flat) not modelled above — much lower for eligible sole traders
