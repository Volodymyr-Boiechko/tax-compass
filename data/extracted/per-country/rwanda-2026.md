## Personal Income Tax

Residents are subject to tax on worldwide income; nonresidents are subject to tax on Rwandan-source income only. Tax year = 1 January to 31 December. Tax withheld from employees under PAYE. Self-employed and businesses must file three provisional returns with payments due 30 June, 30 September, and 31 December; final return within three months of accounting period end. (EY pp.1334, 1337)

### Employment and Business Income Brackets

Applicable from 1 November 2023, as provided in Law No. 027/2022 of 20 October 2022. Applies to employment income and taxable business income of individuals and unincorporated entities. (EY p.1336)

### Investment and Other Income

- Dividends: final withholding tax at 15% (EY p.1334)
- Financial interest (except certain exempt categories): 15% (EY p.1334)
- Royalties: 15% (EY p.1335)
- Technical, management, and other service fees: 15% (EY p.1335)
- Lottery and gambling proceeds: 15% (EY p.1335)
- Capital gains on sale of business assets / asset sharing: 30% (EY p.1335)
- Capital gains on direct/indirect sale or transfer of shares: 10% (EY p.1335)
- Sitting allowances: final withholding at 30% (EY p.1335)

### Exempt interest income:
- Interest on deposits in financial institutions for at least one year
- Interest on loans from foreign development financial institutions exempt from income tax in their country of origin
- Interest paid by banks operating in Rwanda to other banks

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Business expenses deductible if incurred wholly and exclusively for direct purpose of the business, real and substantiated, lead to decrease in net assets, and relate to the tax period incurred
- Fixed assets qualify for annual capital allowance deduction (straight-line or declining-balance, 5%–50% depending on asset type)
- Losses may be carried forward five years (more if approved by tax authority)

**Notes:**
- Profits repatriated from Rwanda: subject to 15% withholding (EY p.1335)
- Agricultural income benefits from a 50% exemption after deduction of losses (EY p.1347)
- Small enterprises: lump sum tax of 3% of annual turnover; may opt for normal regime
- Micro enterprises: flat amount of tax based on annual turnover (RWF60,000–300,000 for turnover RWF2M–12M); exempt from filing if turnover <RWF2M (EY p.1336)

## Social Security

The Rwanda Social Security Board (RSSB) administers retirement and other benefits. (EY p.1336)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension / retirement (RSSB) | 6% | 6% | N/A specified |
| Occupational hazard | — | 2% | — |
| Maternity leave | 0.3% | 0.3% | — |
| Community-Based Health Insurance | 0.5% of net salary | — | — |
| **Total** | **~6.8%** | **~8.3%** | — |

Note: Both employers and employees contribute 0.3% of total salary (excluding transport allowance) for maternity leave benefits. Employees contribute 0.5% of net salary toward Community-Based Health Insurance. (EY p.1336)

**Income ceiling:** Not specified by EY; contributions calculated on total salary excluding transport allowance for maternity leave component.

**2027 transition:** Starting January 2027, total contribution rate will increase by 2% annually for both employers and employees, reaching 20% total by 2030. (EY p.1336)

**Notes:**
- Current total pension contributions: 6% employee + 6% employer + 2% occupational hazard (employer only) = 14% total
- Self-employed social security treatment not detailed in EY; likely voluntary or limited coverage
- Community-Based Health Insurance (0.5% of net salary) paid by employees only

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Net Income Basis)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals carrying on business or professional activities in Rwanda
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 0/10/20/30% applied to annual taxable income
- **Social security:** Not detailed by EY for self-employed; Community-Based Health Insurance likely applies
- **VAT obligations:** Rwanda VAT standard rate 18%; registration threshold not specified in EY individual section
- **Key limits:** Business expenses must meet all four deductibility conditions; losses carry-forward 5 years

### Option B — Small Enterprise (Turnover Tax)

- **Who qualifies:** Small enterprises (annual turnover threshold not precisely defined by EY but inferred from context)
- **Tax base:** Annual turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:** 3% of annual turnover (lump sum); may opt for normal PIT regime instead
- **Social security:** Not specified
- **Key limits:** Can opt into normal regime; micro enterprises (turnover <RWF12M) use flat-amount table

### Option C — Micro Enterprise (Flat Amount Tax)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with annual turnover below RWF12,000,000 (~USD10,700)
- **Tax base:** Annual turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:** Flat amount (RWF60,000–300,000); exempt from filing if turnover <RWF2M (EY p.1336)
- **Social security:** Not specified
- **Key limits:** Very low threshold; primarily covers informal small traders

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Dedicated Newcomer PIT Regime

Rwanda does not have a remittance-basis or new-resident preferential PIT regime. Foreign nationals establishing residency in Rwanda become subject to standard Rwandan PIT on worldwide income.

### East African Community (EAC) Free Movement

- Citizens of EAC member states (Burundi, DRC, Kenya, Rwanda, South Sudan, Tanzania, Uganda) are entitled to pass/entry visa-free for up to six months upon arrival. (EY p.1337)

- RSSB contribution rate increase scheduled from January 2027 (+2% annually toward 20% total by 2030); no 2026 change to current rates
- No new special regimes or PIT rate changes announced

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side only; employer 8.3% SS not shown).
> "B2B" = net business income; assumes 30% deductible expenses on gross.
> Currency: RWF (1 EUR ≈ 1,270 RWF approximate).

### Working calculations:

**€30,000 Employment (≈ RWF38,100,000):**
- Annual taxable income = RWF38,100,000 (no personal exemption in EY brackets)
- PIT: 0 + 10%×(1,200,000−720,000) + 20%×(2,400,000−1,200,000) + 30%×(38,100,000−2,400,000)
- = 0 + 48,000 + 240,000 + 30%×35,700,000 = 48,000 + 240,000 + 10,710,000 = RWF10,998,000
- Employee SS: 6.8% × 38,100,000 = RWF2,590,800
- Total: RWF13,588,800; effective rate = 35.7%

**€60,000 Employment (≈ RWF76,200,000):**
- PIT: 48,000 + 240,000 + 30%×(76,200,000−2,400,000) = 288,000 + 30%×73,800,000 = 288,000 + 22,140,000 = RWF22,428,000
- Employee SS: 6.8% × 76,200,000 = RWF5,181,600
- Total: RWF27,609,600; effective rate = 36.2%

**€100,000 Employment (≈ RWF127,000,000):**
- PIT: 288,000 + 30%×(127,000,000−2,400,000) = 288,000 + 30%×124,600,000 = 288,000 + 37,380,000 = RWF37,668,000
- Employee SS: 6.8% × 127,000,000 = RWF8,636,000
- Total: RWF46,304,000; effective rate = 36.5%
