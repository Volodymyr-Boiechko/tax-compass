## Personal Income Tax

> Progressive income tax under Law No. 027/2022 of 20 October 2022. Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Rwanda-source income. Tax year is the calendar year. PAYE system for employment income. Annual returns due within three months after year-end. Administered by RRA (Rwanda Revenue Authority).

### Individual Rates (effective from 1 November 2023)

| Annual taxable income — Exceeding (RWF) | Not exceeding (RWF) | Rate % |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 720,000 | 0 |
| 720,000 | 1,200,000 | 10 |
| 1,200,000 | 2,400,000 | 20 |
| 2,400,000 | — | 30 |

### Rental Income Tax (individual property owners)

| Rental income — Exceeding (RWF) | Not exceeding (RWF) | Rate % |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 180,000 | 0 |
| 180,000 | 1,000,000 | 20 |
| 1,000,000 | — | 30 |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Business expenses deductible if: incurred wholly and exclusively for direct purpose of business; correspond to real expenses with proper invoices/receipts; lead to decrease in net assets; used for activities related to the tax period incurred
- Fixed assets qualify for annual capital allowance deduction (straight-line or declining-balance; rates 5%–50% depending on asset type)
- Sitting allowances: subject to final 30% WHT

**Notes:**
- Employment income includes: wages, salaries, vacation pay, sick pay, medical allowance, payments instead of vacation, directors' fees, commissions, bonuses, gratuities, entertainment allowances and all benefits in kind (employer cars, housing, below-market loans, relative benefits)
- Investment income: 15% final WHT on dividends (except shares/units in collective investment schemes), financial interest (with exceptions: deposits ≥ 1 year at Rwandan banks, development bank loans, inter-bank interest), royalties, technical/management/service fees (except transport), craftsperson/artist/athlete payments, lottery/gambling, retained earnings converted to shares, payments by resident persons on behalf of nonresident contracted persons, profits repatriated, goods sold in Rwanda
- Capital gains: business asset disposals and asset sharing proceeds taxed at 30%; shares (direct/indirect sale or transfer): 10%
- Losses: carry forward up to 5 years; requests to carry forward longer may be made to RRA
- PAYE: employer withholds; if employer cannot withhold, obligation reverts to employee
- Provisional returns: 3 payments due 30 June, 30 September, 31 December (based on prior year)

## Social Security

> Rwanda Social Security Board (RSSB) administers mandatory contributions. (EY p.1336)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| RSSB pension | 6% of monthly salary | 6% of monthly salary | — |
| Occupational hazard | — | 2% of total salary (excl. transport allowance) | — |
| Maternity leave | 0.3% of salary (excl. transport) | 0.3% of salary (excl. transport) | — |
| Community-Based Health Insurance (CBHI) | 0.5% of net salary | — | — |
| **Total** | **~6.8%** | **~8.3%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** None specified in EY source
**Notes:**
- Total contribution rate will increase by 2% annually from January 2027 for both employers and employees, reaching 20% by 2030 (EY p.1336)
- Pension contributions: 6% employee + 6% employer = 12% total; occupational hazard 2% employer only (on salary excluding transport)
- Both employer and employee contribute 0.3% each for maternity leave benefits
- CBHI: employee contributes 0.5% of net salary

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Business / Self-Employed (Graduated Rates)
- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals and businesses with annual turnover > RWF 2,000,000
- **Tax base:** Net business profits (gross income less deductible business expenses and capital allowances)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates (0–30%) as employment income; annual return within 3 months of year-end; provisional payments quarterly
- **Social security:** RSSB voluntary for self-employed; CBHI applies
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above RWF 20,000,000 annual turnover (standard rate 18%)
- **Key limits:** Losses carry forward 5 years

### Option B — Small Enterprise (Lump Sum Tax)
- **Who qualifies:** Small enterprises
- **Tax base:** Annual turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:** 3% of annual turnover (lump sum); can opt into normal tax regime
- **Social security:** As above
- **VAT obligations:** Below VAT threshold
- **Key limits:** Option to switch to normal graduated-rate regime

### Option C — Micro Enterprise (Fixed Amount)
- **Who qualifies:** Micro enterprises (annual turnover RWF 2,000,000–12,000,000)
- **Tax base:** Annual turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed flat tax amounts: RWF 60,000 (RWF 2–4M turnover); RWF 120,000 (RWF 4–7M); RWF 210,000 (RWF 7–10M); RWF 300,000 (RWF 10–12M)
- **Social security:** As above
- **VAT obligations:** Below VAT threshold
- **Key limits:** Persons with annual turnover < RWF 2,000,000 exempt from filing tax declarations

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

> No specific preferential tax regime for new tax residents identified in EY source.

### EAC Citizens — Visa-Free Access
- **Eligibility:** Nationals of East African Community (EAC) Member States
- **Benefit:** Pass/entry visa-free for up to six months
- **Duration:** Per visit/stay up to 6 months
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Work permit still required for employment; standard tax rules apply

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 1,450 RWF (approximate mid-2025).
> Employment: 6% RSSB pension + 0.5% CBHI + 0.3% maternity = ~6.8% employee SS. B2B: CBHI 0.5% only (pension voluntary for self-employed).

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (RWF) | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden (RWF) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈RWF 43,500,000) | Employment | 12,828,000 | 2,958,000 (~6.8%) | 15,786,000 | ~36.3% |
| €30,000 (≈RWF 43,500,000) | B2B | 12,828,000 | 217,500 (0.5% CBHI) | 13,045,500 | ~30.0% |
| €60,000 (≈RWF 87,000,000) | Employment | 25,908,000 | 5,916,000 (~6.8%) | 31,824,000 | ~36.6% |
| €60,000 (≈RWF 87,000,000) | B2B | 25,908,000 | 435,000 (0.5% CBHI) | 26,343,000 | ~30.3% |
| €100,000 (≈RWF 145,000,000) | Employment | 43,308,000 | 9,860,000 (~6.8%) | 53,168,000 | ~36.7% |
| €100,000 (≈RWF 145,000,000) | B2B | 43,308,000 | 725,000 (0.5% CBHI) | 44,033,000 | ~30.4% |

**Working (income tax):**
Band boundaries: 0 on first 720,000; 10% on 720,001–1,200,000; 20% on 1,200,001–2,400,000; 30% above 2,400,000
- RWF 43,500,000: 0+48,000+240,000+30%×(43,500,000−2,400,000) = 288,000+12,330,000 = **12,618,000** (~29.0%)
  *(cumulative through 2,400,000: 0+48,000+240,000=288,000; then 30%×41,100,000=12,330,000; total=12,618,000)*
- RWF 87,000,000: 288,000+30%×84,600,000 = 288,000+25,380,000 = **25,668,000** (~29.5%)
- RWF 145,000,000: 288,000+30%×142,600,000 = 288,000+42,780,000 = **43,068,000** (~29.7%)

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 1,450 RWF (approximate)
- Single, no dependants
- Employment SS: RSSB pension 6% + maternity 0.3% + CBHI 0.5% = 6.8%
- B2B: CBHI 0.5% only
