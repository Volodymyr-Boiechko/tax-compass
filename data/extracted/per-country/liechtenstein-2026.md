## Personal Income Tax

> Liechtenstein levies national income tax plus a communal surcharge (150–180% of the national tax, applied per commune). The effective maximum rate for 2024 is 22.4% (state tax + maximum 180% communal surcharge). Net worth (wealth) tax is integrated into the income tax through a standardized return concept (4% of net assets added to taxable income). The tax year is the calendar year.

**National income tax rates (2024 — progressive, 0% to 22.4% maximum including communal surcharge at 180%):**

| Taxable income (CHF) | National income tax rate % | With max communal surcharge (180%) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 15,000 | 0 | 0% |
| 15,001 – 20,000 | ~1% | ~3% |
| 20,001 – 30,000 | ~2% | ~6% |
| 30,001 – 50,000 | ~4% | ~11% |
| 50,001 – 100,000 | ~6% | ~17% |
| 100,001 – 200,000 | ~7% | ~20% |
| 200,001+ | ~8% | ~22.4% |

*Exact national rate table not reproduced in full in EY source; maximum rate 22.4% (national ~8% × 2.8 multiplier at 180% surcharge). (EY p. 876)*

**Standardized return on net worth (net worth tax integrated into income tax):**
- 4% standardized return rate applied to net market value of all movable and immovable assets in 2025 (EY p. 877)
- This deemed income is added to ordinary taxable income and taxed at the progressive rate
- Liabilities and asset increases during the year may be deducted

**Capital gains:**
- Movable personal/business assets: generally exempt from income tax
- Business participations (shares): anti-avoidance rules from 2019: gains from foreign participations not exempt if value derives mainly from passive sources and profits are low-taxed (EY p. 875)
- Immovable assets: capital gains on real estate taxed as income at the same progressive rates (max 24%); recapture of depreciation taxed at ordinary rates

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- All necessary employment expenses (travel, meals, education) deductible
- Old-age and survivors' insurance, disability, and unemployment premiums: fully deductible
- Pension fund contributions (company and individual): up to 18% of taxable income
- Life insurance, accident and health premiums: limited deduction
- Medical and dental treatment costs: limited deduction
- Children's education costs: limited deduction
- Business expenses (self-employed): all business costs deductible + 4% standardized return on business working capital (anti-avoidance rules from 2019)

**Notes:**
- No personal tax-free allowance mentioned for ordinary residents
- Lump-sum taxation available for non-Liechtenstein citizens who reside in Liechtenstein, do not engage in gainful activity, and live on foreign income/assets — taxed at 25% flat on living costs (EY p. 876)
- Inheritance and gift taxes: abolished since 1 January 2011 (EY p. 877)
- Married individuals: taxed jointly on all income
- No separate wealth tax (integrated into income tax via 4% standardized return)

## Social Security

> Liechtenstein has compulsory contributions for old-age and survivors' insurance (AHV), disability insurance (IV), unemployment insurance, and pension fund (BVG). From 2026, family pension fund contribution rates increase.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| AHV/IV/ALV (old-age, disability, unemployment) — 2025 | 4.70% | 7.38% | 12.1% (of income) |
| Unemployment fund (on up to CHF 126,000/year) | 0.50% (shared) | 0.50% (shared) | included |
| Family pension fund (2025) | 1.9%* | 1.9% | 1.9% |
| Family pension fund (2026) | **2.1%** | 1.9% (unchanged) | **2.1%** |
| Pension plan (BVG minimum) | 4% | 4% | voluntary |
| **Total approx. (employee, 2026)** | **~4.9%** | **~7.38%** | **~12.1%** |

*\* Family pension fund employee share increases from 1.9% to 2.1% from 2026 (EY p. 877). Employer rate stays at 1.9%.*

**Income ceiling:** Unemployment insurance ceiling CHF 126,000/year (EY p. 877)
**Notes:**
- From 2026: employee total SS contribution rate increases to ~4.9% (from ~4.7%); combined employee + employer = 12.28% (EY p. 877)
- Self-employed: 12.1% rate in 2025; 2026 rate not explicitly stated but expected to mirror increases
- Administrative cost contribution (5% of total insurance contributions = 0.58% of contributory salary from 2026) (EY p. 877)
- Company pension plan (BVG): employer must contribute at least equal to employee's 4% mandatory contribution (minimum 8% total per employee)
- EU Regulation 883/04 applies — coordination with EU/EEA countries (EY p. 877)

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals in Liechtenstein pay income tax at progressive rates (max 22.4%) on net business income, with net worth tax (4% standardized return on business assets) integrated. Social security contributions at 12.1%+.

### Option A — Self-Employed (Selbständigerwerbende)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual operating a business or profession in Liechtenstein
- **Tax base:** Net income from business (revenue minus all business expenses); 4% standardized return rate applied to business working capital (anti-avoidance); total deemed income subject to progressive tax
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates up to 22.4% (including communal surcharge at maximum 180%)
- **Social security:** 12.1% AHV/IV/ALV on self-employment income; plus pension fund contributions if applicable
- **VAT obligations:** Liechtenstein applies the Swiss VAT system; standard rate 8.1% (reduced rates 2.6% and 3.8%); registration threshold CHF 100,000 annual turnover
- **Key limits:** Business losses may be carried forward indefinitely (offset limited to 70% of positive taxable income; minimum 30% of positive income always taxable); no carryback (EY p. 876)

### Option B — Lump-Sum Taxation (for non-citizens)
- **Who qualifies:** Non-Liechtenstein citizens domiciled or resident in Liechtenstein; must not engage in gainful activity in Liechtenstein; must live on income from foreign assets/payments
- **Tax base:** Overall living costs of the taxpayer multiplied by 25% flat tax rate; must be "substantial" by tax administration standards
- **Rate / mechanism:** 25% flat on assessed living costs; no other income/wealth taxes
- **Social security:** Normal social security rules apply
- **VAT obligations:** N/A (no gainful activity)
- **Key limits:** Must be "substantial" amount per tax administration practice; not available to Liechtenstein citizens; excellent for wealthy retirees living off foreign assets (EY p. 876)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Lump-Sum Taxation
- **Eligibility:** Non-Liechtenstein citizen; resident in Liechtenstein; no gainful activity in Liechtenstein; lives on income from foreign sources
- **Benefit:** Tax based on 25% of living costs (not actual income/assets); can be highly advantageous for wealthy individuals with low living costs relative to income
- **Duration:** Ongoing while conditions met
- **Application deadline:** Must be arranged with tax authorities upon taking up residence
- **Caveats:** Liechtenstein has very restricted immigration — residence permits limited; EU/EEA citizens and Swiss citizens have easier access; strict limits on available permits (EY p. 878)

### Relocation Key Employees / Business Developers
- **Eligibility:** Liechtenstein government limits immigration significantly; exceptions for EU/EEA/Swiss nationals
- **Benefit:** Standard Liechtenstein tax rates apply (relatively moderate at max 22.4%)
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Application deadline:** Residence visa required before relocation
- **Caveats:** Immigration very restricted; not a typical expat-attraction regime

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 874–878)

- **Family pension fund employee contribution increases to 2.1%** (from 1.9%) from 1 January 2026 (EY p. 877)
- **Total employee SS contribution rate increases to ~4.9%** for 2026
- **Combined employee + employer rate = 12.28%** from 2026
- **Administrative cost contribution = 0.58%** of contributory salary from 2026 (5% of total insurance contributions)
- Croatia DTT: treaty applies as of 2026 (EY p. 878)
- Standardized return rate (net worth component): 4% in 2025 — 2026 rate not yet published

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, resident in Vaduz (communal surcharge ~150%, one of the lower communes).
> Currency: CHF. 1 CHF ≈ 1.05 EUR (approximate 2025 rate). EUR amounts are approximate.
> Note: Exact bracket thresholds not fully published; effective rates estimated from max 22.4% at 180% surcharge.

**Employment at €30,000 (≈ CHF 28,571):**
1. Standardized return on personal assets (assume modest assets ~CHF 50,000): CHF 50,000 × 4% = CHF 2,000 added to income → total taxable ~CHF 30,571
2. Progressive PIT at ~150% communal surcharge (~7% × 2.5 = ~17.5% effective at this income level): CHF 30,571 × ~10% = CHF 3,057 ≈ €3,210
3. Employee SS (4.9% from 2026): CHF 28,571 × 4.9% = CHF 1,400 ≈ €1,470
4. Total = €3,210 + €1,470 = €4,680
5. Effective rate = €4,680 / €30,000 = **~15.6%**

**Employment at €60,000 (≈ CHF 57,143):**
1. Standardized return on assets ~CHF 100,000: CHF 4,000 added → total ~CHF 61,143
2. PIT at ~15% effective: CHF 61,143 × ~15% = CHF 9,171 ≈ €9,630
3. Employee SS: CHF 57,143 × 4.9% = CHF 2,800 ≈ €2,940
4. Total = €9,630 + €2,940 = €12,570
5. Effective rate = €12,570 / €60,000 = **~21.0%**

**Employment at €100,000 (≈ CHF 95,238):**
1. Standardized return ~CHF 150,000 assets: CHF 6,000 → total ~CHF 101,238
2. PIT at ~19% effective (approaching max): CHF 101,238 × ~19% = CHF 19,235 ≈ €20,197
3. Employee SS: CHF 95,238 × 4.9% = CHF 4,667 ≈ €4,900
4. Total = €20,197 + €4,900 = €25,097
5. Effective rate = €25,097 / €100,000 = **~25.1%**

**B2B (self-employed) at €60,000 (≈ CHF 57,143 revenue):**
1. Business costs ~15%: CHF 8,571; net = CHF 48,572; + 4% standardized return on working capital CHF 48,572: CHF 1,943 → taxable ~CHF 50,515
2. Self-employed SS (12.1%): CHF 48,572 × 12.1% = CHF 5,877 ≈ €6,171
3. PIT at ~15%: CHF 50,515 × ~15% = CHF 7,577 ≈ €7,956
4. Total = €6,171 + €7,956 = €14,127
5. Effective rate = €14,127 / €60,000 = **~23.5%**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | ~€3,210 | ~€1,470 | ~€4,680 | **~15.6%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€3,500 | ~€3,600 | ~€7,100 | **~23.7%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | ~€9,630 | ~€2,940 | ~€12,570 | **~21.0%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | ~€7,956 | ~€6,171 | ~€14,127 | **~23.5%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | ~€20,197 | ~€4,900 | ~€25,097 | **~25.1%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€17,000 | ~€10,000 | ~€27,000 | **~27.0%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants, resident in Vaduz (~150% communal surcharge assumed — lower than maximum)
- Standardized net worth return: modest personal assets assumed (EUR equivalent ~1–1.5× annual income)
- Employee SS: 4.9% (2026 rate); employer pays additional ~7.38% separately
- B2B: 12.1% self-employed SS; 15% business costs; 4% standardized return on working capital
- CHF/EUR ≈ 1.05 (approximate; CHF not EUR-pegged)
- Income tax effective rates are approximations — exact bracket thresholds not fully published in EY source
