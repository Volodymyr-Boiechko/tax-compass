## Personal Income Tax

Progressive income tax levied by the national government; regional communities levy surcharges on the state tax. Tax year = calendar year. Filing: self-assessment; returns filed in April of the following year. Since the 2011 tax reform (in force 1 January 2011), net worth tax is integrated into income tax via a standardised return rate rather than being calculated separately. (EY pp. 874–876)

### Income tax rates

| Rate component | Detail |
|---|---|
| National progressive rate | 0% up to maximum ~8% (state level) |
| Communal surcharge | 150%–180% of state tax |
| **Maximum combined rate (2024, 180% surcharge)** | **22.4%** |

- Communities impose surcharges of 150%–180% of state tax; maximum combined rate = **22.4%** (EY p. 876)
- The marginal rate of 22.4% applies to income exceeding CHF 234,870 (Tax Class 1 / single individuals) and CHF 469,740 (Tax Class 2 / married couples jointly taxed) — amounts consistent with Luxembourg comparison in EY
- Married individuals are taxed jointly on all income (EY p. 878)

**Net worth / standardised return integration:**
- Net worth tax is integrated into income tax via a **4% standardised return rate** applied to the net market value of all movable and immovable assets (rate for 2025; annually determined) (EY p. 877)
- This standardised income is added to other taxable income and taxed at the progressive rates
- Real estate and business premises abroad are not subject to this taxation
- Liabilities and any increase in assets during the year may be deducted

**Investment income (dividends, interest, royalties, licences):**
- NOT taxed on actual income amounts
- Taxed via the 4% standardised return on net market value of assets (EY p. 875)

**Capital gains:**
- *Movable assets:* Generally exempt; anti-avoidance rules from 2019 for low-taxed foreign participations (EY p. 875)
- *Immovable assets:* Taxed at the same progressive income tax rates (maximum 24% for immovable assets) (EY p. 875)

**Directors' fees:**
- Resident directors: taxed with other income at progressive rates
- Nonresident directors: 12% withholding tax on fees from Liechtenstein companies (EY p. 875)

**Lump-sum taxation (non-Liechtenstein-citizens only):**
- Available to non-citizens who are resident, do not engage in gainful activity, and live on income from foreign assets/payments
- Tax base = overall living costs × 25% flat rate; must be a substantial amount (EY p. 876)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Employment expenses (travel, meals, education) deductible
- Old-age/survivors' insurance, disability insurance, unemployment insurance premiums: fully deductible
- Pension fund contributions: deductible, up to 18% of taxable income
- Life/accident/health insurance premiums, medical/dental, children's education: limited amounts deductible
- Business working capital: 4% standardised return deductible on business working capital
- No specific personal allowances granted to individual taxpayers (EY p. 876)

**Loss relief:**
- Business losses of self-employed: unlimited carryforward; but offset limited to 70% of taxable income per year (minimum 30% always taxed); no carryback (EY p. 876)

## Social Security

**2025 contribution rates (updated for 2026):** (EY pp. 877–878)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Old-age & survivors' / disability / earnings-replacement insurance (AHV/IV/EO) | 4.70% | 7.38% | 12.10% |
| Unemployment insurance (on salary up to CHF 126,000) | 0.50% | 0.50% | 0.50% |
| Family pension fund (2025 rate) | 1.90% | 1.90% | 1.90% |
| Family pension fund (from **2026**) | **2.10%** | **1.90%** | **2.10%** |
| Administrative cost contribution (~5% of total insurance) | ~0.36% | ~0.57% | ~0.58% |
| **Total approx. (2026, employee side)** | **~7.56%** | **~10.35%** | **~14.68%** |

**2026 change (EY p. 877):** Starting 2026, family pension fund contribution rate increases to 2.1% for employees, self-employed and non-employed persons (from 1.9%). Employers remain at 1.9%. Overall total combined rate (employer + employee) rises to approximately **12.28%**.

**Pension plan:**
- All employees paying into Liechtenstein social security must contribute to a pension plan
- Employer contribution must equal at least the employee's mandatory 4%
- Total minimum pension contributions: at least 8% per employee (EY p. 877)

**Income ceiling:** CHF 126,000 annual salary for unemployment insurance; no ceiling stated for AHV/IV/EO (EY p. 877)

**Totalization agreements:** EU Regulation 883/04 applies (all EU and EFTA countries) (EY p. 877)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed Individual (Sole Trader)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on business or profession in Liechtenstein
- **Tax base:** Net business income after all business expenses and 4% standardised return on working capital; net worth of business assets added via standardised return method
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive income tax rates as employment (maximum 22.4% with maximum communal surcharge); net worth integration via 4% standardised return on all assets
- **Social security:** AHV/IV/EO 12.1% of income + family pension fund 2.1% (2026) + unemployment 0.5% = ~14.7%; not required to be pension plan member
- **VAT obligations:** Liechtenstein applies Swiss VAT law; standard rate 8.1%; threshold CHF 100,000 annual turnover
- **Key limits:** Losses offset up to 70% of taxable income per year; unlimited carryforward; no carryback

### Option B — Lump-Sum Taxation (Non-citizens)

- **Who qualifies:** Non-Liechtenstein-citizen residents who do not work gainfully in Liechtenstein and live on foreign asset income/payments
- **Tax base:** Overall living costs × 25% flat rate (must be substantial)
- **Rate / mechanism:** 25% applied to living costs; no deductions for actual income structure
- **Social security:** Standard contributions apply where applicable
- **Key limits:** No gainful activity in Liechtenstein permitted; immigration restrictions apply (very limited non-EU/EEA access)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Limited Immigration Policy

- Liechtenstein has **limited immigration**; very difficult for non-Swiss/non-EU/EEA nationals to immigrate
- Limited exceptions: citizens of Switzerland and EU/EEA member countries (EY p. 878)
- No formal tax incentive regime for inbound workers comparable to other jurisdictions

### Double Tax Treaties

As of 2026, treaties in force with: Andorra, Austria, Croatia (new — applicable 2026), Czech Republic, Georgia, Germany, Guernsey, Hong Kong SAR, Hungary, Iceland, Jersey, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, Netherlands, Romania, San Marino, Singapore, Switzerland, United Arab Emirates, United Kingdom, Uruguay. Treaties initiated with Bahrain, Belgium, Latvia, Montenegro; negotiations ongoing with several others. (EY p. 878)

## 2026 Changes

- **Family pension fund contribution rate (EY p. 877):** Employee and self-employed contribution to family pension fund increases from 1.9% to **2.1%** as of 1 January 2026. Employer rate remains 1.9%. Combined employer + employee rate rises to ~12.28%.
- **Croatia DTT:** Double tax treaty with Croatia becomes applicable as of 2026 (EY p. 878)
- **No changes to income tax rates or brackets** announced
- **Standardised return rate (net worth integration):** 4% confirmed for 2025; 2026 rate to be set annually
