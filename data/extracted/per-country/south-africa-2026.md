## Personal Income Tax

> Individual residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on South African-source income. Tax year runs 1 March to end of February. Administered by SARS (South African Revenue Service). PAYE system for employees. Married persons taxed separately.

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Pension, provident and retirement annuity fund contributions: deductible up to the lesser of ZAR 350,000 or 27.5% of the higher of "remuneration" or "taxable income" per year
- Medical scheme tax credits: ZAR 364/month for fund member and first dependent; ZAR 246/month for each additional dependent (2025-26)
- Additional medical expense relief: available for excess medical contributions and qualifying medical expenses (subject to age/disability conditions)
- Donations to approved public benefit organisations: deductible up to 10% of taxable income (excluding retirement lump sums and severance benefits); excess carried forward
- Foreign employment income exemption: first ZAR 1,250,000 of remuneration for services rendered outside South Africa exempt (conditions: outside SA 183 full days in 12-month period, including at least 60 consecutive days)
- Capital gains annual exclusion: ZAR 40,000 per individual per year
- Primary residence CGT exclusion: first ZAR 2,000,000 of gain exempt
- Interest exemption: ZAR 23,800/year (general); ZAR 34,500/year (age 65+)

**Notes:**
- Domestic dividends: 20% final WHT (dividends tax); exempt from normal income tax
- Foreign dividends (holdings < 10%): taxable; foreign tax credit available; effective rate capped at 20% via exemption formula
- Royalties to nonresidents: 15% final WHT
- Capital gains tax (CGT): 40% inclusion rate for individuals → effective maximum CGT rate = 18% (40% × 45%)
- Employer contributions to South African pension/provident/retirement annuity funds: included in employee's taxable income as fringe benefit; deemed individual contribution (deductible up to the ZAR 350,000 / 27.5% limit)
- Self-employed professional fees paid to nonresidents: subject to employees' tax withholding if South African source
- Provisional taxpayers: income from non-employment sources ≥ ZAR 30,000/year or exceeding tax threshold; two payments annually (31 August and 28 February); third payment by 30 September
- Loss carry-forward: indefinitely for self-employed if trade continues; no carryback

## Social Security

> South Africa has no formal social security system, but has equivalent mandatory contributions: UIF, Skills Development Levy (employer only) and Compensation Fund. (EY p.1420)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| UIF (Unemployment Insurance Fund) | 1% of remuneration | 1% of remuneration (matching) | None |
| Skills Development Levy (SDL) | None | 1% of employer's total salary bill | None |
| Compensation Fund (COID) | None | Employer contributes (rate determined annually) | None |
| **Total (employee)** | **1%** | **~3%+** | **0%** |

**UIF Income ceiling:** ZAR 17,712/month (effective 1 June 2021)
**Compensation Fund ceiling:** ZAR 633,168 annual remuneration (1 March 2025 – 28 February 2026)
**Notes:**
- UIF: both local and foreign national employees required to contribute; capped at ZAR 177.12/month (employee and employer each)
- SDL: employer-only; 1% of total payroll; funds Skills Development training
- Compensation Fund: employer-only; insures employees against industrial accidents/illnesses; amount varies by industry risk rating
- Self-employed individuals: no UIF or SDL obligation

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Self-Employed (Standard Rates)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a trade, profession or business in South Africa
- **Tax base:** Taxable income (gross income less exempt income, less deductible expenses of a non-capital nature; capital allowances instead of accounting depreciation)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates (18–45%); provisional tax (two payments August/February; third by 30 September); annual return; retirement annuity fund contributions deductible up to ZAR 350,000 / 27.5%
- **Social security:** No UIF/SDL; no mandatory contributions
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above ZAR 1,000,000 annual turnover (standard rate 15%)
- **Key limits:** Loss carry-forward indefinitely if trade continues; no loss carryback; ring-fencing of losses from certain trades applies

### Option B — Turnover Tax (Micro Business)
- **Who qualifies:** Qualifying micro businesses with annual turnover ≤ ZAR 1,000,000; not a company, trust, or personal service provider
- **Tax base:** Taxable turnover (gross receipts less certain exclusions)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Graduated: 0% on first ZAR 335,000; 1% on ZAR 335,001–500,000; 2% on ZAR 500,001–750,000; 3% on ZAR 750,001–1,000,000
- **Social security:** No mandatory contributions
- **VAT obligations:** Must register for VAT if turnover exceeds ZAR 1,000,000
- **Key limits:** Election is biennial; cannot claim deductions; suitable for small tradespeople

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Foreign Employment Income Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Tax-resident individuals rendering services outside South Africa
- **Benefit:** First ZAR 1,250,000 of remuneration exempt from South African normal income tax; foreign tax credit available on excess
- **Duration:** Per year, while conditions met (183+ days outside SA in 12-month period, including 60+ consecutive days)
- **Application deadline:** N/A (applied on tax return)
- **Caveats:** Does not apply to pension/annuity from South African retirement fund (services must be rendered outside SA and pension from foreign fund)

### Remote Work Visa
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals who will work solely for a foreign-based employer from South Africa without providing services to a South African-based entity or client
- **Benefit:** Legal work authorisation; standard tax residency rules apply
- **Duration:** Per visa term
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** If resident (183+ days or ordinarily resident), worldwide income taxable in South Africa

## 2026 Changes

- 2025/26 budget rates confirmed: progressive scale 18%–45%; primary rebate ZAR 17,235; tax thresholds as shown (EY p.1418)
- Medical scheme tax credits: ZAR 364/month (member + first dependent), ZAR 246/month (additional dependants) — confirmed for 2025-26 (EY p.1418)
- Compensation Fund ceiling: ZAR 633,168 annual remuneration (1 March 2025–28 February 2026) (EY p.1421)
- Estate duty: 20% on net assets at death (up to ZAR 30 million); 25% above ZAR 30 million — unchanged (EY p.1419)
- Donations tax: 20% on cumulative donations up to ZAR 30 million lifetime; 25% above — unchanged (EY p.1419-1420)
