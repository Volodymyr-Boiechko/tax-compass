## Personal Income Tax

Individual residents in South Africa are subject to tax on their worldwide income. Nonresidents are subject to tax on income from a South African source. Tax year = 1 March to 28/29 February.

**Residency:** Ordinarily resident rule (South Africa is the individual's true home to which they naturally return) OR physical presence rule (>91 days in current year, >91 days in each of 5 prior years, >915 days in aggregate over prior 5 years, and >91 days in each of those years). An individual absent from SA for ≥330 consecutive full days is not resident for that period. (EY pp.1415–1416)

**Foreign employment income exemption:** Only first ZAR1.25 million of foreign employment remuneration is exempt from South African tax if employee is outside SA for >183 days in any 12-month period and for at least one continuous period of >60 days during the same 12-month period. (EY p.1416)

### Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

Capital gains are taxable through inclusion in taxable income. Only 40% of the net capital gain (after ZAR40,000 annual exclusion) is included in taxable income. Effective CGT rate at 45% marginal rate = 40% × 45% = 18%. For assets held by individuals (not corporates): ZAR40,000 annual exclusion; gain from sale of primary residence excluded up to ZAR2 million. (EY p.1419)

### Other Tax Rates / Investment Income

| Income type | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Domestic dividends | 20% final withholding (dividends tax) |
| Foreign dividends (holdings <10%) | Taxable at progressive rates; portion exempt (effective ~20%) |
| Interest (SA source, under ZAR23,800 cumulative) | Exempt (under 65) |
| Interest (SA source, above exemption) | Taxed at progressive PIT rates |
| Tax-Free Savings Account interest & capital gains | Exempt |
| Medical scheme tax credits (2025/26) | ZAR364/month (member + first dependant); ZAR246/month per additional dependant |

**Rental income:** General deductions apply; interest expense deductible if incurred to produce rental income.

**Deductions:**
- Retirement fund contributions: deductible up to lesser of ZAR350,000 or 27.5% of the higher of "remuneration" or "taxable income" (EY p.1418)
- Medical scheme tax credits (above) deductible directly from tax payable
- Charitable donations: up to 10% of taxable income (excluding retirement and severance)
- Business expenses: deductible if incurred wholly and exclusively for business purposes

**Notes:**
- Estate duty: 20% on net estates; 25% on amounts above ZAR30 million; abatement ZAR3.5 million (ZAR7 million for married couples) (EY p.1419–1420)
- Donations tax: 20% flat on capital transfers; exemption ZAR100,000/year; 25% on donations exceeding ZAR30 million
- Transfer duty on fixed property: 0% up to ZAR1.21 million; up to 13% on ZAR13.31 million+

## Social Security

South Africa does not have a comprehensive social security system. The main payroll-based contributions are: (EY p.1420)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unemployment Insurance Fund (UIF) | 1% | 1% | 0% (not applicable) |
| Skills Development Levy (SDL) | 0% | 1% of payroll | 0% |
| Compensation Fund (COIDA) | 0% | Assessed rate | 0% |
| **Total (UIF only)** | **1%** | **1% + SDL + COIDA** | **0%** |

**UIF income ceiling:** ZAR17,712/month (= ZAR212,544/year) — effective 1 June 2021. (EY p.1420)

**Compensation Fund:** Employer contributions assessed annually on remuneration up to ZAR633,168/year (period 1 March 2025 – 28 February 2026). Rate determined by the Compensation Commissioner based on occupation. (EY p.1421)

**Notes:**
- UIF provides limited unemployment, illness, and death benefits
- Self-employed individuals are NOT subject to UIF or SDL
- SDL (1% of payroll): employer only; funds used for skills development
- COIDA: employer only; insures employees against work-related injuries/illness

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment / Provisional Taxpayer

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals deriving income (excluding exempt income) of ZAR30,000 or more from sources other than remuneration, OR having taxable income above the threshold
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 18–45%; primary rebate ZAR17,235 deductible from tax
- **Social security:** 0% UIF, 0% SDL; no employer contributions required for sole traders
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory VAT registration at ZAR1 million annual taxable turnover; optional below ZAR50,000; standard VAT rate 15%
- **Filing:** Three provisional tax payments: 31 August, 28 February, and 30 September; second payment must be ≥80% (or 90% if taxable income <ZAR1M) of actual annual liability to avoid penalties (EY p.1421)
- **Key limits:** Losses may be carried forward indefinitely for continuing trades; no loss carryback; losses from some activities ("ring-fenced" losses from hobbies or non-commercial ventures) may be ring-fenced

### Option B — Turnover Tax (Micro Business)

- **Who qualifies:** Businesses with annual qualifying turnover ≤ ZAR1,000,000 (ZAR1 million)
- **Tax base:** Gross turnover (not net profit)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Sliding scale on turnover:

| Annual turnover — Exceeding (ZAR) | Annual turnover — Not exceeding (ZAR) | Tax (ZAR) | Rate on excess (%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 335,000 | 0 | 0 |
| 335,000 | 500,000 | 0 | 1 |
| 500,000 | 750,000 | 1,650 | 2 |
| 750,000 | 1,000,000 | 6,650 | 3 |

- **Social security:** 0% UIF/SDL for self-employed
- **VAT obligations:** Businesses registered for turnover tax are automatically registered for VAT but may elect to be excluded from VAT
- **Key limits:** Turnover ≤ ZAR1 million; must register; certain business types excluded (financial services, professionals like doctors/lawyers/accountants under certain conditions, mining, etc.)
- **Key benefit:** Zero tax on first ZAR335,000 of turnover; very low rates vs. standard PIT

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Remote Work Visa

- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals employed by a foreign employer who will work solely for the foreign employer from South Africa and not provide any services to a South African-based entity or client
- **Duration:** Not specified; typically valid for the employment duration
- **Tax implication:** As a SA tax resident (if physically present under 91-day/915-day rules), worldwide income taxable in SA; however, the foreign employment income exemption (first ZAR1.25M exempt) may apply (EY p.1426)

### Business Visa (Self-Employment/Investment)

- **Eligibility:** Foreigners intending to establish or invest in a business in South Africa; minimum capital investment ZAR5 million from abroad invested and retained in book value; business must employ ≥60% South African citizens/permanent residents permanently
- **Duration:** Up to 3 years; biannual compliance confirmation
- **Tax implication:** Business income taxable in South Africa at standard rates (EY p.1425)

### No Dedicated New-Resident Tax Holiday

South Africa does not have a remittance-basis or new-resident PIT holiday. All residents are taxed on worldwide income from date of tax residency. However, the foreign employment income exemption (ZAR1.25M) benefits employees working abroad.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, no retirement contributions, primary rebate ZAR17,235 only.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side; employer UIF 1%+SDL+COIDA not shown separately).
> "B2B standard" = gross revenue, 30% assumed business expenses, provisional taxpayer.
> "Turnover tax" = gross revenue at micro business rates (≤ZAR1M).
> Currency: ZAR (South African Rand). 1 EUR ≈ 19.50 ZAR (approximate).

### Working calculations:

**€30,000 Employment (≈ ZAR585,000):**
- UIF (employee 1%, capped at ZAR17,712/month): 1% × 212,544 = ZAR2,125 (capped annually)
- Taxable income: ZAR585,000
- PIT: 121,475 + 36% × (585,000 − 512,800) = 121,475 + 36% × 72,200 = 121,475 + 25,992 = ZAR147,467
- Less primary rebate: − ZAR17,235 → PIT = ZAR130,232
- Total: 130,232 + 2,125 = ZAR132,357; effective rate = 22.6%

**€30,000 B2B standard (30% expenses → net ZAR409,500):**
- Taxable income: ZAR409,500
- PIT: 77,362 + 31% × (409,500 − 370,500) = 77,362 + 31% × 39,000 = 77,362 + 12,090 = ZAR89,452
- Less primary rebate: − ZAR17,235 → PIT = ZAR72,217
- UIF: 0% (self-employed)
- Effective rate on gross: 72,217 / 585,000 = **12.3%**

**€30,000 Turnover tax (ZAR585,000 > ZAR500,000 threshold):**
- Tax: 1,650 + 2% × (585,000 − 500,000) = 1,650 + 1,700 = ZAR3,350
- Effective rate: 3,350 / 585,000 = **0.6%** (but no expense deductions; very low)

**€60,000 Employment (≈ ZAR1,170,000):**
- UIF: ZAR2,125 (capped)
- PIT: 251,258 + 41% × (1,170,000 − 857,900) = 251,258 + 41% × 312,100 = 251,258 + 127,961 = ZAR379,219
- Less rebate: − 17,235 → PIT = ZAR361,984
- Total: 361,984 + 2,125 = ZAR364,109; effective = 31.1%

**€60,000 B2B standard (30% expenses → net ZAR819,000):**
- PIT: 179,147 + 39% × (819,000 − 673,000) = 179,147 + 39% × 146,000 = 179,147 + 56,940 = ZAR236,087
- Less rebate: − 17,235 → PIT = ZAR218,852
- Effective on gross: 218,852 / 1,170,000 = **18.7%**

**€100,000 Employment (≈ ZAR1,950,000):**
- UIF: ZAR2,125 (capped)
- PIT: 644,489 + 45% × (1,950,000 − 1,817,000) = 644,489 + 45% × 133,000 = 644,489 + 59,850 = ZAR704,339
- Less rebate: − 17,235 → PIT = ZAR687,104
- Total: 687,104 + 2,125 = ZAR689,229; effective = 35.3%

**€100,000 B2B standard (30% expenses → net ZAR1,365,000):**
- PIT: 251,258 + 41% × (1,365,000 − 857,900) = 251,258 + 41% × 507,100 = 251,258 + 207,911 = ZAR459,169
- Less rebate: − 17,235 → PIT = ZAR441,934
- Effective on gross: 441,934 / 1,950,000 = **22.7%**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ZAR585,000 (€30k) | Employment | ZAR130,232 | ZAR2,125 (UIF 1%, capped) | ZAR132,357 | **22.6%** |
| ZAR585,000 (€30k) | B2B standard (30% exp.) | ZAR72,217 | 0 | ZAR72,217 | **12.3%** |
| ZAR585,000 (€30k) | Turnover tax micro | ZAR3,350 | 0 | ZAR3,350 | **0.6%** |
| ZAR1,170,000 (€60k) | Employment | ZAR361,984 | ZAR2,125 (capped) | ZAR364,109 | **31.1%** |
| ZAR1,170,000 (€60k) | B2B standard (30% exp.) | ZAR218,852 | 0 | ZAR218,852 | **18.7%** |
| ZAR1,950,000 (€100k) | Employment | ZAR687,104 | ZAR2,125 (capped) | ZAR689,229 | **35.3%** |
| ZAR1,950,000 (€100k) | B2B standard (30% exp.) | ZAR441,934 | 0 | ZAR441,934 | **22.7%** |

**Turnover tax note:** At €30k gross (ZAR585,000 < ZAR1M threshold), the turnover tax is effectively 0.6% — extremely favorable. However, turnover tax is designed for micro businesses; at €60k gross (ZAR1.17M > ZAR1M), the taxpayer exceeds the turnover tax threshold and must use the standard PIT regime.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/ZAR = 19.50 (approximate)
- Primary rebate ZAR17,235 applied; no other rebates or credits
- No retirement fund contributions assumed
- UIF: 1% on first ZAR17,712/month = ZAR212,544/year → max ZAR2,125/year (employee)
- Self-employed: no UIF; no employer-side SDL/COIDA
- B2B: 30% assumed deductible expenses
- Tax year: 1 March 2025 – 28 February 2026 rates used
- No foreign employment income exemption applied

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Retirement annuity deduction impact:** The ZAR350,000 / 27.5% retirement fund deduction can significantly reduce taxable income for high earners; effective rates shown above do not include this deduction — real effective rates for those making maximum retirement contributions could be 5–10 percentage points lower
- [ ] **Medical scheme credits:** ZAR364/month + ZAR246/month per additional dependant directly reduces tax payable; not included in base case calculations above
- [ ] **Turnover tax excluded business types:** Certain professions (e.g., accountants, attorneys under specific structures) may be excluded from turnover tax; verify eligibility at sars.gov.za
- [ ] **CGT on disposal of business assets:** The 40% inclusion rate and ZAR40,000 annual exclusion significantly affect effective CGT rates for entrepreneurs selling businesses; not modeled above
- [ ] **UIF for self-employed:** Self-employed do not contribute to UIF and therefore do not qualify for UIF benefits (unemployment, illness, maternity); relevant for planning purposes
- [ ] **Provisional tax mechanics:** Third provisional payment (30 September) required to bring total to 100% of actual tax; interest and penalties apply for underpayment; verify SARS deadlines annually
- [ ] **Remote work visa tax treatment:** As a SA tax resident under the physical presence rule, a remote worker's worldwide income is fully taxable; foreign employment income exemption (ZAR1.25M) requires careful eligibility assessment
