## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PIT on employment, pension, business/freelancer, and agricultural income. Tax year = calendar year. Annual returns filed **15 March – 15 July** of the following year. A 4% discount applies if paid in lump sum by 31 July and return filed by 30 April. (EY pp. 521, 529)

**Progressive PIT scale — employment, pension, freelancer, business, agricultural income:** (EY p. 521)

| Taxable income (EUR) | Tax rate | Tax due (EUR) | Cumulative tax due (EUR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 10,000 | **9%** | 900 | 900 |
| 10,001 – 20,000 | **22%** | 2,200 | 3,100 |
| 20,001 – 30,000 | **28%** | 2,800 | 5,900 |
| 30,001 – 40,000 | **36%** | 3,600 | 9,500 |
| 40,001 and above | **44%** | — | — |

**Investment / capital income (flat rates):** (EY pp. 523–524)

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends | 5% (final withholding) |
| Interest | 15% (final withholding) |
| Royalties | 20% (final withholding) |
| Capital gains (securities, real estate) | 15% |
| Internet / video games / games of chance | 15% |

**Real estate rental income (progressive):** (EY p. 524)

| Annual rental income (EUR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to €12,000 | 15% |
| €12,001 – €35,000 | 35% |
| Above €35,000 | 45% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY pp. 521–522)
- Tax allowance based on number of dependent children and income level (exact amount varies; phased out at higher income)
- Mandatory electronic payment requirement: taxpayers with salary/pension/freelancer/rental income must pay **30% of their actual income per year** via electronic means (card, transfer). Maximum limit: EUR 20,000/year. Failure results in additional 22% tax on the shortfall.
- Foreign tax credit available for foreign-source income declared in Greece
- 4% early payment discount (return by 30 April, lump-sum payment by 31 July)

**Notes:**
- Special solidarity contribution: suspended/abolished as of recent legislation (EY does not show it in 2025 rate table; confirm current status)
- Severance pay: first EUR 60,000 exempt; 10% on next EUR 40,000; 20% on next EUR 50,000; 30% above EUR 150,000 (EY p. 522)
- "Deemed income" rule: Tax Administration compares declared income vs. evidence of assets/spending; unjustified wealth increase taxed at 33% (EY p. 523)

## Social Security

**Employment:** Administered by e-EFKA. (EY p. 529)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| All social security (pension, health, unemployment) | **13.37%** | **21.79%** | **35.16%** |

- Maximum monthly base (employees, from 1 Jan 2026): **EUR 7,761.94** (14 payroll periods)
- Employer also pays approximately EUR 20/year additional levy

**Self-employed / freelancers — EFKA insurance categories (2025):**

Self-employed individuals choose one of **6 insurance categories** annually by 31 January. Contributions are fixed monthly amounts (not % of income). (xolo.io; tovima.com; greekcitytimes.com)

| Category | Approx. monthly contribution (2025) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| New entrant (< 5 years insured) | **€156.43/month** | Special reduced rate; first 5 insurance years |
| Category 1 (lowest) | **~€254.65/month** | Available to all after 5 years |
| Category 2 | **~€286/month** | — |
| Category 3 | **~€340/month** | — |
| Category 4 | **~€430/month** | — |
| Category 5 | **~€540/month** | — |
| Category 6 (highest) | **~€650/month** | Maximum pension accrual |

Annual total for Category 1: ~EUR 3,056/year. New entrant: ~EUR 1,877/year.

Contributions are deductible as business expenses for PIT purposes. Choose higher category for higher pension; lower category for lower costs.

**Notes:**
- Contributions increased 2.7% from 2024 to 2025
- 8 out of 10 Greek self-employed choose Category 1 (lowest)
- Maximum annual EFKA contribution cap on pension-related portion: EUR 20,000/year for self-employed (Law 5078/2023, EY p. 521)
- The minimum (imputed) income rule applies: freelancers presumed to earn at least the minimum wage plus 10% of labour costs (capped at EUR 50,000/year) — this affects the taxable base, not contributions (EY pp. 522–523)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Freelancer / Sole Proprietor (Standard Regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered with the Tax Administration (AADE) and EFKA as self-employed. Covers IT consultants, professionals, freelancers.
- **Tax base:** Annual net profit (revenue minus documented expenses). Subject to **minimum (imputed) income rule**: tax is levied on the higher of actual or imputed income (imputed = employee minimum wage + 10% of annual labour costs, capped at EUR 50,000). (EY pp. 522–523)
  - Imputed income rule does not apply during first 3 years; reduced (discount) for years 4 and 5
  - 50% reduction of minimum income for rural locations (< 500 inhabitants, < 1,500 in municipal communities outside Attica, islands < 3,100 inhabitants) (EY p. 523 / Law 5162/2024)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT (9%/22%/28%/36%/44%)
- **Social security:** EFKA insurance category (see above): Category 1 ~EUR 3,056/year; new entrants ~EUR 1,877/year
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT registration; VAT rate 24% (reduced 13% and 6% apply to certain goods/services). Mandatory e-invoicing for B2B from July 2025.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling. Annual PIT return required.

### Option B — Article 5C Regime (50% Exemption — See Special Regimes below)

The 50% income exemption under Article 5C reduces the PIT base by half for qualifying new residents. Not a separate filing regime but an overlay on the standard freelancer/employment regime.

### Option C — Single-Member IKE / EPE (Company)

- CIT: 22% on profits. Dividend: 5% withholding. Owner salary: standard PIT + social contributions.
- Generally attractive above EUR 60k for liability protection and retained earnings.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Article 5C — 50% Income Exemption for New Tax Residents (Employees and Freelancers)

The flagship incentive for relocating professionals. (EY pp. 519–520; taxlaw.gr; taxsummaries.pwc.com)

- **Eligibility:**
  1. Have NOT been a Greek tax resident for **5 out of the 6 years** preceding the transfer of tax residency to Greece
  2. Transfer tax residency to Greece from a country with which Greece has a **double tax treaty or administrative cooperation agreement** on tax matters
  3. Provide employment services in Greece under an employment contract with a Greek legal entity or PE of a foreign company in Greece, OR conduct freelance/business activity in Greece
  4. Declare intention to remain in Greece for at least **2 years**
  5. The employment position no longer needs to be "newly created" (amended as of 28 July 2025)
- **Benefit:** **50% exemption** from PIT and special solidarity contribution on employment or business income earned in Greece. The exempt 50% is not included in the tax base.
- **Duration:** Maximum **7 consecutive tax years**; automatically ceases after year 7 (cannot be extended)
- **Application:** Apply to the Greek Tax Administration (AADE) within 30 days of starting employment/business activity in Greece
- **Caveats:**
  - Exemption applies only to Greek-source employment/business income — not to investment income (dividends, interest, rental income)
  - EFKA social security contributions still apply in full (no reduction in social contributions)
  - Standard PIT applies to the non-exempt 50%
  - After 7 years: fully standard PIT applies; plan accordingly

### Article 5A — Lump-Sum Tax for High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNW)

- **Eligibility:** Not Greek tax resident for 7 of previous 8 years + investment ≥ EUR 500,000 in Greek real estate, businesses, or securities within 3 years (EY pp. 518–519)
- **Benefit:** Fixed EUR 100,000 annual tax (regardless of foreign income amount). Greek-source income taxed normally. Relatives: EUR 20,000/year each.
- **Duration:** Maximum 15 tax years

### Article 5B — Flat 7% Tax for Foreign Pensioners

- **Eligibility:** Not Greek tax resident for 5 of previous 6 years; pension from abroad; relocation from country with Greek administrative cooperation (EY p. 519)
- **Benefit:** 7% flat tax on all foreign-source income (pension and others); no further liability
- **Duration:** Maximum 15 tax years

### Digital Nomad Visa

- **Eligibility:** Third-country nationals employed by or self-employed for foreign entities; must work remotely; financial resources ≥ EUR 3,500/month (EY p. 530)
- **Benefit:** Legal right to reside and work remotely in Greece for up to 12 months (renewable)
- **Tax:** Standard Greek PIT applies if tax resident (≥183 days). No tax exemption specific to the visa.
- **Application:** Apply at Greek consular authority abroad (10-day response target; single window process)
- **Caveats:** Dependent family members cannot work for Greek entities during the stay

## 2026 Changes

- **EY note:** "At the time of updating this chapter, changes to the tax law were expected as of 2026. Readers should obtain updated information before engaging in transactions." (EY p. 517)
- **Article 5C amendment (July 2025):** Requirement that the employment position be "newly created" was abolished; applications pending at amendment date also benefit. Regime is now more accessible.
- **EFKA cap increase from 1 January 2026:** Maximum monthly social security base for employees raised to EUR 7,761.94 (14 periods) — up from EUR 7,572.62 (EY p. 529 / PwC)
- **Inheritance law amendments** expected in 2025 or 2026 (EY p. 533 — "important amendments to the Greek inheritance law were expected to be introduced in 2025 or 2026")
- **Short-term rental restrictions (Athens from January 2025 – December 2025):** New registrations in 3 central Athens districts prohibited; from October 2025, enhanced property standards required for short-term rentals (EY pp. 524–525)
- **36-month rental income PIT exemption:** For residential leases ≥ 3 years signed 8 September 2024 – 31 December 2025 on properties up to 120m²; exemption applies for 36 months from signing (EY p. 524)
- **Mandatory e-invoicing (B2B):** From 1 July 2025 for self-employed

**Key insight — Article 5C is transformative:** At €60k, 5C reduces the effective employment rate from 38.0% to 21.3% — a saving of EUR 10,000/year. At €100k, from 43.4% to 24.3% — saving EUR 19,000/year. The 7-year window makes Greece genuinely competitive for relocating professionals.

**Self-employed advantage:** EFKA Category 1 fixed contributions (~€3,048/year) are far lower than 13.37% of salary at high incomes, making the self-employed route significantly cheaper for social security at €60k+ (€3,048 vs. €8,022). However, PIT is similar or higher due to no deduction from the imputed income rule.

**Assumptions:**
- Standard PIT brackets (9%/22%/28%/36%/44%); no tax allowance for children
- Employee EFKA: 13.37% of gross; not capped at income levels shown (cap ~€108,668 annual threshold — above all scenarios)
- Self-employed EFKA: Category 1 ~€254/month = €3,048/year (2025 estimate); deductible from PIT base
- Article 5C: 50% of (gross minus EFKA) excluded from PIT base; EFKA paid in full
- Employer EFKA (21.79%) NOT included — additional employer cost
- No electronic payment requirement surcharge assumed (taxpayer meets 30% e-payment threshold)
- Minimum imputed income rule may increase PIT base for self-employed at low revenue — not modelled
