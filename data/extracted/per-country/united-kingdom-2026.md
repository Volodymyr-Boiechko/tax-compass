## Personal Income Tax

UK tax year runs 6 April to 5 April. Income tax assessed on worldwide income for residents. PAYE system for employment income. Self-assessment returns due 31 January following the tax year end (31 October for paper). Married persons taxed as separate individuals. (EY p.1633)

**Income tax rates — 2025-26 tax year, England/Wales/Northern Ireland** (EY p.1619)

| Taxable income (GBP) | Tax rate | Band name |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 12,570 | 0% | Personal allowance |
| 12,571 – 50,270 | 20% | Basic rate |
| 50,271 – 125,140 | 40% | Higher rate |
| Over 125,140 | 45% | Additional rate |

*Personal allowance GBP 12,570 (2025-26). Tapered: reduced by GBP1 for every GBP2 of adjusted net income over GBP100,000; fully withdrawn at GBP125,140. (EY p.1618)*

**Dividend income rates — 2025-26 (before 6 April 2026)** (EY p.1609):
- Dividend allowance: GBP 500/year (tax-free)
- Basic rate taxpayer: 8.75%
- Higher rate taxpayer: 33.75%
- Additional rate taxpayer: 39.35%

**Dividend rates from 6 April 2026** (EY p.1609):
- Basic rate: 10.75%
- Higher rate: 35.75%
- Additional rate: 39.35%

**Capital gains tax (CGT) rates — 2025-26** (EY p.1616):
- Annual exempt amount: GBP 3,000
- Gains within basic rate band: 18%
- Gains above basic rate band: 24%
- Business Asset Disposal Relief (up to GBP 1M lifetime): 14% (increasing to 18% from 6 April 2026)
- Residential property (non-PRR): 18% / 24% (same as general from April 2024)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal allowance: GBP 12,570 (EY p.1618)
- Personal savings allowance: GBP 1,000 (basic rate); GBP 500 (higher rate); GBP 0 (additional rate) (EY p.1609)
- GBP 1,000 trading allowance for self-employed (EY p.1609)
- GBP 1,000 property income allowance (EY p.1610)
- Employee business expense deductions (actual, if wholly and exclusively for employment) (EY p.1618)
- Qualifying relocation expenses up to GBP 8,000 (EY p.1618)

**Pension annual allowance 2025-26:** GBP 60,000 (tapered for adjusted income >GBP 260,000; minimum GBP 10,000). Pension contributions reduce taxable income. (EY p.1613)

**Notes:**
- Domicile concept abolished from 6 April 2025; replaced by Qualifying New Resident (QNR) 4-year FIG regime (EY p.1602–1605)
- Remittance basis abolished from 6 April 2025 (EY p.1602)
- Self-employed mandated to keep digital records and report quarterly from 6 April 2026 if income >GBP 50,000 (EY p.1619)

| Weekly earnings (GBP) | Employer rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 96.00 | 0% |
| Over 96.01 | 15% |

**Employment allowance:** Up to GBP 10,500/year per employer to offset employer NIC liability (from April 2025, increased from GBP 5,000). (EY p.1628)

**NIC thresholds frozen until 2031:** November 2025 Budget confirmed freezing of all NIC thresholds until 2031. (EY p.1628)

**Class 4 — Self-employed (2025-26):**

- Note: EY p.1626 states "from 2024-25, Class 2 contributions for the self-employed will be abolished and the main rate of Class 4 National Insurance contributions will be reduced to 9% to 8%."

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Trader (Self-Employment, Schedule D / Self-Assessment)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a trade, profession, or vocation in the UK. Determined by facts and circumstances; HMRC "Employment Status" tests apply. (EY p.1608–1609)
- **Tax base:** Net profit = revenue minus allowable business expenses (wholly and exclusively for business purposes). Cash or accrual basis permissible.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net profit taxed at progressive income tax rates (0–45%, personal allowance GBP 12,570 applies). Class 4 NIC also due (8%/2%).
- **NIC:** Class 4 NIC: 8% on profits GBP 12,570–50,270; 2% above GBP 50,270. No employer NIC due.
- **VAT:** Registration mandatory if taxable turnover exceeds GBP 90,000/year (2025). Standard rate 20%; reduced rates 5% / 0% for specified goods/services.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; no special flat-rate; all expenses must be wholly and exclusively for business. From 6 April 2026: digital record-keeping and quarterly reporting to HMRC if income >GBP 50,000.

### Option B — Limited Company (Ltd) Outside IR35

- **Who qualifies:** IT contractors, consultants, professionals who form their own Personal Service Company (PSC). Working "outside IR35" means the contractor is genuinely self-employed (not a disguised employee) for the specific engagement. IR35 determination (Status Determination Statement) required from medium/large-client companies since April 2021.
- **Tax base / mechanism:**
  1. Ltd Co earns revenue from contracts
  2. Director/owner pays themselves a salary to the NIC Primary Threshold (~GBP 12,570/year) — minimal income tax, minimal NIC
  3. Remaining profit distributed as dividends (after corporation tax)
  4. **Corporation tax:** 25% on profits above GBP 250,000; marginal relief GBP 50,000–250,000; 19% small profits rate (up to GBP 50,000). (EY p.1609 reference; HMRC confirmed)
  5. **Dividends to director-shareholder:** taxed at 8.75% (basic), 33.75% (higher), 39.35% (additional) — before 6 April 2026. Dividend allowance GBP 500 tax-free.
- **NIC for Ltd Co director:**
  - Salary up to primary threshold (GBP 12,570): no employee NIC, no employer NIC
  - Employer NIC on salary above GBP 5,000/year (secondary threshold from April 2025): 15%
  - No NIC on dividends
- **VAT:** Same as sole trader (threshold GBP 90,000); Flat Rate Scheme option for small businesses.
- **Key advantage:** Total tax rate substantially lower than employment + NIC at higher income levels due to: (1) corporation tax at 25% < higher rate IT 40%; (2) no NIC on dividends; (3) salary at minimum NIC threshold.
- **Key risk:** IR35 misclassification — if found to be inside IR35, all income deemed employment income, full PAYE + NIC applies. Substantial back-tax, interest, penalties exposure.

### Option C — Inside IR35 (Deemed Employment via PSC or Umbrella)

- **Mechanism:** If inside IR35, the PSC pays "deemed employment income" to the worker after applying a deemed employment payment calculation; employer NIC applies at 15% on the deemed income. No benefit from the Ltd Co structure.
- **Alternative:** Umbrella company employment — contractor employed by an umbrella, which operates PAYE + full NIC; umbrella charges a margin (~GBP 20–30/week).
- **Tax:** Same as employment (20%/40%/45% + employee NIC 8%/2% + employer NIC 15%).

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Qualifying New Resident (QNR) — 4-Year FIG Regime (from 6 April 2025)

- **Eligibility:** UK resident taxpayers who were previously non-resident in the UK (under UK domestic SRT) for 10 consecutive UK tax years. (EY p.1605)
- **Benefit:** Tax relief on foreign income and gains (FIG) for the first 4 UK tax years of residence (the year of first residence + 3 following years). Relief available on:
  - Foreign income (investment, rental, etc.)
  - Foreign gains (capital gains on non-UK assets)
  - Foreign employment income: relief limited to lower of 30% of qualifying employment earnings or GBP 300,000/year (Overseas Workday Relief — OWR) (EY p.1605, 1608)
- **Downside:** QNRs who claim FIG relief lose their personal allowance (GBP 12,570) and annual CGT exemption (GBP 3,000) for that tax year. (EY p.1605–1606)
- **Duration:** 4 UK tax years only. No extension.
- **Claim:** Annual claim required on self-assessment return. (EY p.1605)
- **Caveats:** This regime replaced the old remittance basis (abolished 6 April 2025). Transitional provisions apply for those previously on remittance basis (Temporary Repatriation Facility — 12% rate in 2025-26/2026-27 for qualifying overseas capital). (EY p.1622)

### Overseas Workday Relief (OWR)

- **Eligibility:** QNR individuals who perform employment duties partly outside the UK.
- **Benefit:** Tax relief on the proportion of employment income attributable to non-UK workdays; limited to the lower of 30% of qualifying employment earnings or GBP 300,000/year. (EY p.1605, 1608)
- **Duration:** Available for 4 years while QNR status applies.

## 2026 Changes

- **Domicile / remittance basis abolished (6 April 2025):** Replaced by 4-year FIG (QNR) regime. Major structural change affecting non-UK domiciled individuals. (EY p.1602)
- **Employer NIC secondary threshold reduced (April 2025):** From GBP 9,100/year to GBP 5,000/year; rate increased from 13.8% to 15%. Employment allowance raised to GBP 10,500. Significant increase in employer NIC costs for medium/large businesses. (EY p.1628)
- **Dividend rates from 6 April 2026:** Basic rate 10.75% (from 8.75%); higher rate 35.75% (from 33.75%); additional rate unchanged at 39.35%. (EY p.1609)
- **Business Asset Disposal Relief from 6 April 2026:** Rate rises to 18% (from 14%); lifetime limit GBP 1M; Business/Agricultural Relief capped at GBP 1M from April 2026. (EY p.1616, 1624)
- **Self-employment digital records (6 April 2026):** Quarterly digital income/expense reporting to HMRC mandatory for self-employed with income >GBP 50,000 (threshold reduces further in 2027+). (EY p.1609, 1619, 1635)
- **NIC thresholds frozen until 2031:** All NIC thresholds frozen; fiscal drag increases NIC burden as wages rise. (EY p.1628)
- **Class 2 NIC abolished (from April 2024):** Already abolished; Class 4 main rate reduced to 8% (was 9%). (EY p.1626)
- **Benefits in kind via PAYE (6 April 2027):** All benefits in kind (except living accommodation and beneficial loans) will be subject to PAYE from April 2027. (EY p.1634)
- **Pension annual allowance:** GBP 60,000 (2025-26); tapered above GBP 260,000 adjusted income. (EY p.1613)
- **Inheritance tax — LTR basis:** From 6 April 2025, IHT applies based on "Long-Term Residency" (10 of last 20 years UK resident) rather than domicile. Run-off period of 3–10 years applies after leaving UK depending on years of prior residence. (EY p.1623)
- **Skilled Worker visa salary threshold:** General threshold GBP 38,700–GBP 41,700 (increased April 2024); PhD threshold GBP 34,830–GBP 37,500. (EY p.1641)
- **ETA for EU nationals from 2 April 2025:** EU/EEA nationals traveling to UK without a visa required to obtain Electronic Travel Authorisation before travel. (EY p.1639)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, England (not Scotland), no dependants. Figures in GBP; EUR conversion at 1 EUR = GBP 0.84.
> "Employment (PAYE)" = gross salary; employee NIC + income tax; employer NIC 15% not shown.
> "Ltd Co outside IR35" = gross revenue; corporation tax 25% on profit; minimum salary GBP 12,570; remainder as dividends.
> Personal allowance GBP 12,570 applies.

### GBP 25,200 (≈ EUR 30,000) — PAYE Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Income tax | 25,200 − 12,570 = 12,630 @ 20% | GBP 2,526 |
| Employee NIC (Class 1) | (25,200 − 12,570) × 8% = 1,010; above UEL: 0 | GBP 1,010 |
| **Total burden** | **2,526 + 1,010** | **GBP 3,536** |
| **Effective rate** | **3,536 / 25,200** | **14.0%** |
| **EUR equivalent** | 3,536 / 0.84 | **EUR 4,210 on EUR 30,000** |

### GBP 25,200 (≈ EUR 30,000) — Ltd Co outside IR35 (15% expenses assumed)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] Scotland income tax rates (higher top rate 48% vs England 45%) not modeled in effective rate examples — Scottish residents will have higher effective rates, particularly at GBP 75,001–125,140 range
- [ ] IR35 determination complexity not modeled: working "outside IR35" requires proper contractual structure, working practices assessment, and Status Determination Statement from medium/large clients; risk of reclassification is material
- [ ] Employer NIC impact on Ltd Co included (GBP 1,136 salary-based), but total employer NIC under PAYE not modeled — at GBP 84,000 salary, employer NIC ≈ GBP 11,850 (15% × (84,000 − 5,000)) additional employer cost
- [ ] QNR 4-year FIG regime (replacing remittance basis) effective rate not computed; loss of personal allowance if FIG relief claimed — complex trade-off requiring individualised analysis
- [ ] Overseas Workday Relief (OWR) effective rate not computed; up to 30% of qualifying employment income exempt — valuable for international commuters in first 4 years
- [ ] Pension contribution tax relief not modeled; higher-rate taxpayer can reclaim 40%/45% on contributions — effectively reduces income tax significantly for those contributing to pensions
- [ ] Dividend rate increase from 6 April 2026 (+2% basic, +2% higher) not yet reflected in 2026-27 calculations; would add ~GBP 200–700 to Ltd Co dividend tax bill in examples above
- [ ] Corporation tax marginal relief (between GBP 50,000–250,000 profit) approximated; exact effective CT rate in this band requires precise calculation per HMRC formula
- [ ] Sole trader (self-employment, Schedule D) effective rate not computed separately; would sit between PAYE and Ltd Co due to Class 4 NIC (8%/2%) with no employer NIC but no CT optimisation
- [ ] Making Tax Digital (from April 2026): quarterly reporting threshold GBP 50,000 — administrative burden not costed
- [ ] IHT long-term resident exposure (10 of 20 years UK resident → full worldwide estate at 40%) not modeled; critical for high net worth individuals planning UK residency
