## Personal Income Tax

Resident individuals are subject to PIT on worldwide income. Nonresident individuals are subject to PIT on income received in São Tomé and Príncipe (STP). Nonresidents taxed at 15% final withholding on gross income derived in STP. (EY p.1349)

**Residency criteria (any one of):**
- Present in STP for more than 180 days during a calendar year
- As of 31 December, has a local residence with intent to maintain it as permanent residence
- Core business/professional activities in STP
- Member of diplomatic/consular mission of STP abroad
- As of 31 December, crew member of vessel/aircraft of STP-resident entity

(EY p.1345)

Income is divided into four schedular categories:
- **Category A:** Dependent income and pensions (employment)
- **Category B:** Business or professional income
- **Category C:** Investment income (dividends, interest, royalties not from own IP)
- **Category D:** Capital gains

Categories A and B income are aggregated and taxed at progressive rates. Categories C and D: 15% final withholding (gross), except individuals may choose to aggregate Category D income. (EY pp.1345–1349)

**Special annual income tax (IERS):** Created in 2023; 20% levied on disposable income exceeding STN50,000/month (~USD2,250/month). (EY p.1349)

### PIT Brackets (Categories A and B — Aggregated)

Income up to STN11,700,000 (~USD526) is exempt. Formula used: Taxable income for bracket = (Amount from first column × rate from second column) − Amount from third column. (EY p.1348)

| Taxable income (STN) | Tax rate (%) | Amount to deduct (STN) |
|---|---|---|
| Up to 11,700,000 | 0 | — |
| 11,700,001 – 50,000,000 | 10 | 881,250 |
| 50,000,001 – 100,000,000 | 13 | 1,380,000 |
| 100,000,001 – 150,000,000 | 15 | 1,875,000 |
| 150,000,001 – 240,000,000 | 20 | 5,625,000 |
| 240,000,001 – 419,999,000 | 25 | 9,000,000 |
| Over 420,000,000 | 30 | 45,048,000 |

(EY p.1348)

**Note on IERS (Special PIT):** Separate 20% rate on disposable income exceeding STN50,000/month — stacks on top of the progressive PIT for high earners. (EY p.1349)

### Category A Exemptions (Employment Income)

The following items are expressly exempt from PIT Category A:
- Employer contributions to social security regimes (retirement/disability)
- Pensions up to STN24,000,000/year (~USD1,080)
- Benefits from employer's social and leisure facilities
- Scholarships (under certain conditions)
- Training courses in STP (under conditions)
- Severance payments up to legal labor law limits

**Deductible from Category A taxable income:**
- Social security contributions
- Union contributions
- Employer compensation for contract termination (under conditions)
- 50% of pension income (under conditions)

(EY p.1346)

### Category B — Business/Professional Income

Business income of individuals with turnover >STN500,000,000/year (~USD22,500) is classified as Group 1 (full CIT rules apply); those with lower turnover or occasional activities are Group 2. Group 1 taxpayers file by end of April; Group 2 by end of February. (EY p.1347)

Agricultural income: 50% exemption after deduction of losses. (EY p.1347)

Certain non-deductible expenses (even if actual costs):
- Travel and accommodation exceeding 10% of gross income
- Housing expenses exceeding 10% of gross income (if allocable to services provision)

### Category C — Investment Income

- 50% of all Category C income (dividends, interest, royalties) is subject to PIT (i.e., the gross amount is halved before applying 15% final withholding)
- Effective rate on gross investment income: ~7.5% (EY p.1347)

### Category D — Capital Gains

Capital gains = increase in net worth value. Prizes over STN5,000 are taxable. Prizes under STN5,000 exempt. No deductions allowed for Category D income. Individuals may choose to aggregate Category D with other income. (EY pp.1347–1349)

**Deductions allowed under PIT Code:**
- Family deductions
- Double tax relief
- Health deductions
- Education and training deductions
- Property deductions (limited to STN15,000,000 ≈ USD675)

## Social Security

Social security contributions are payable on gross income. (EY p.1349)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (all components) | 4% | 6% | N/A specified |
| **Total** | **4%** | **6%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** Not specified by EY.

**Notes:**
- Employer contributes 6%, employee contributes 4% on gross income
- Self-employed social security treatment not detailed by EY
- No totalization agreements currently in force (EY p.1349)
- National minimum wage (2025): STN2,500/month (~USD112) (EY p.1349)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Group 1 Business Income (Turnover >STN500M)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with annual turnover >STN500,000,000 (~USD22,500) or all public entities, joint-stock companies, and nonresident companies with permanent establishment
- **Tax base:** Net business income computed under CIT Code rules (Group 1)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 0–30% applied to net taxable income
- **Social security:** Employer contribution 6% + employee-equivalent 4% (or similar for sole trader); details unclear
- **VAT obligations:** E-Factura (electronic invoicing system) required since 9 December 2019 (EY p.1350)
- **Key limits:** Full CIT Code compliance required; advance payments of 75% of prior year tax due in June, September, December

### Option B — Group 2 Business Income (Occasional/Lower Turnover)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with annual turnover ≤STN500,000,000 (~USD22,500) or providing occasional/temporary business activities
- **Tax base:** Net income; simpler compliance rules
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 0–30%; file return by end of February
- **Social security:** Not specified
- **Key limits:** Category B losses may be carried forward five years (offset only against same type of income)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Visa-Free Access for CPLP, EU, US, Canada

Currently, nationals of Community of Portuguese Language Countries (CPLP), EU, US, and Canada holding valid passports are exempt from visa requirements for periods not exceeding 15 days. Citizens with a valid Schengen or US visa and valid passport (>3 months) may also enter without a visa. (EY p.1350)

In April 2025, government approved a draft law providing for 30-day visa exemption for foreign nationals intending to stay for up to 30 days. (EY p.1350)

### No Dedicated Newcomer PIT Regime

STP does not have a remittance-basis or new-resident preferential PIT regime. Foreign nationals establishing residency in STP become subject to standard PIT on worldwide income.

### Residence Visas

- Yearly residence visas may be granted to foreign citizens; valid for one year, renewable. (EY p.1351)
- No specific work visa category; foreign citizens must obtain residence visa to work

## 2026 Changes

- IERS (Special PIT at 20% on disposable income >STN50,000/month) introduced in 2023 and remains in force (EY p.1349)
- April 2025 draft law for 30-day visa exemption for all foreigners; pending final enactment (EY p.1350)
- No PIT bracket changes announced for 2026 as of October 2025
- E-Factura electronic invoicing system remains mandatory (Decree Law 14/2019)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants.
> Currency: STN (São Tomé and Príncipe dobra). Exchange rate: STN21,113 = USD1 (as of 7 August 2025, EY p.1345); 1 EUR ≈ STN22,800 (approximate, using EUR/USD ≈ 1.08).
> "Employment" = gross salary; SS employee 4%.
> "B2B" = gross revenue; 30% assumed business expenses; Group 2 self-employed.

### Working calculations:

**€30,000 Employment (≈ STN684,000,000):**
- Taxable income: STN684,000,000 (no universal personal deduction shown; zero bracket covers STN11,700,000)
- PIT bracket: above STN420,000,000 → tax = 30% × 684,000,000 − 45,048,000 = 205,200,000 − 45,048,000 = STN160,152,000
- SS (employee 4%): 4% × 684,000,000 = STN27,360,000
- Total: STN187,512,000; effective rate = 27.4%

**€60,000 Employment (≈ STN1,368,000,000):**
- PIT: 30% × 1,368,000,000 − 45,048,000 = 410,400,000 − 45,048,000 = STN365,352,000
- SS: 4% × 1,368,000,000 = STN54,720,000
- Total: STN420,072,000; effective rate = 30.7%

**€100,000 Employment (≈ STN2,280,000,000):**
- PIT: 30% × 2,280,000,000 − 45,048,000 = STN639,000,000 − 45,048,000 = STN638,952,000

Wait — recalculate: 30% × 2,280,000,000 = 684,000,000 − 45,048,000 = STN638,952,000
- SS: 4% × 2,280,000,000 = STN91,200,000
- Total: STN730,152,000; effective rate = 32.0%

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **IERS interaction:** The Special Annual Income Tax (IERS, 20% on disposable income >STN50,000/month) stacks with progressive PIT; full calculation requires understanding of "disposable income" definition and how it interacts with SS deductions; not fully modeled
- [ ] **Self-employed SS structure:** EY specifies 4% employee / 6% employer on gross income but does not detail self-employed SS; verify at INSS (Instituto Nacional de Segurança Social) STP
- [ ] **VAT details:** E-Factura requirement confirms VAT/sales tax system; rate and threshold not specified in EY individual section
- [ ] **Double tax treaties:** STP has treaties only with Portugal (2017) and Cape Verde (2021) — very limited network; most foreign income may lack relief from double taxation (EY p.1350)
- [ ] **Group 1 vs Group 2 threshold:** STN500,000,000 turnover ≈ USD22,500 — a very low threshold; even modest IT freelancers at €30k+ would be Group 1; clarify compliance requirements
- [ ] **Currency depreciation risk:** STN is a volatile currency; EUR/STN rate can shift significantly; verify current rate before use
- [ ] **Practical tax enforcement:** São Tomé and Príncipe is a very small island economy with limited tax administration capacity; practical enforcement and compliance norms may differ from statutory rules
