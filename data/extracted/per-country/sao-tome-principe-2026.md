## Personal Income Tax

> Schedular PIT system with four categories under the PIT Code. Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on income received in STP. Exchange rate used in EY source: STN 21,113 = USD 1 (7 August 2025). Tax year is the calendar year. Administered by the Direcção dos Impostos. E-invoicing mandatory per Decree Law 14/2019 (from 9 December 2019).

### Income Categories

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| A | Dependent income and pensions (employment) |
| B | Business or professional income (self-employment) |
| C | Investment income (dividends, interest, royalties not held as business assets) |
| D | Capital gains |

### Category A and B Rates (progressive, applied to aggregated A+B income)

| Taxable income (STN) | Tax rate % | Amount to deduct (STN) |
|---|---|---|
| Up to 11,700,000 | 0 | — |
| 11,700,001 to 50,000,000 | 10 | 881,250 |
| 50,000,001 to 100,000,000 | 13 | 1,380,000 |
| 100,000,001 to 150,000,000 | 15 | 1,875,000 |
| 150,000,001 to 240,000,000 | 20 | 5,625,000 |
| 240,000,001 to 419,999,999 | 25 | 9,000,000 |
| Over 420,000,000 | 30 | 45,048,000 |

**Tax formula:** Tax = (Taxable income × Rate) − Amount to deduct

**Income up to STN 11,700,000 (approximately USD 526) is exempt from tax.**

### Special Income Tax (IERS)
- Rate: 20% on disposable income exceeding **STD 50,000** (approximately USD 2,250) **per month** (EY p.1349)
- Created in 2023

### Category C and D rates
- Category C and D: gross amount subject to 15% final WHT (default); individuals may choose to aggregate Category D income with A+B at progressive rates
- Only 50% of Category C income (including dividends) subject to PIT
- Prize awards of less than STN 5,000 from lottery/raffle/gambling: exempt

**Standard deductions / allowances (PIT Code deductions):**
- Family deductions
- Double tax relief
- Health deductions
- Education and training deductions
- Property deductions (limited to STN 15,000,000 ≈ USD 675)
- Social security contributions: deductible from Category A income
- Union contributions: deductible from Category A income
- Compensation paid by employee to employer for contract termination (under certain conditions): deductible
- 50% of pension income (under certain conditions): deductible from Category A
- Agricultural income: 50% exemption (after deduction of losses)
- Pensions up to STN 24,000,000/year (approximately USD 1,080): exempt from PIT
- Employer contributions to social security for retirement/disability benefits: exempt
- Benefits from social/leisure facilities provided by employer: exempt
- Employer scholarships: exempt (under conditions)
- Training courses in STP (under conditions): exempt
- Severance up to Labor Law limits: exempt

**Notes:**
- Category A income from single employer: no annual return required (final WHT by employer)
- Category A from multiple entities: different rate table applies
- Category B Group 1 (turnover > STN 500,000,000 / ~USD 22,500): taxable income computed under CIT Code rules; advance payments 75% of prior year tax (June, September, December); return by end of April
- Category B Group 2 (occasional/temporary activities): return by end of February
- Nonresidents: 15% final WHT on gross STP-source income
- Inheritance and gift tax: 5% (lineal descendant/ascendant/spouse); 10% (sibling); 15% (others)
- Travel/accommodation expenses exceeding 10% of gross income and housing expenses exceeding 10% of gross income: not deductible for Category B

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Category B Group 1 (Established Business)
- **Who qualifies:** Taxpayers with permanent residence in STP and annual turnover > STN 500,000,000 (approx. USD 22,500); all public entities, joint-stock companies, nonresident companies with PE in STP
- **Tax base:** Net income computed under CIT Code rules for Group 1 and Group 2
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive A+B rates; advance payments 75% of prior year tax in June, September, December; return by end of April
- **Social security:** 4% employee / 6% employer equivalent
- **VAT obligations:** VAT applies; standard rate not specified in EY individual section
- **Key limits:** Non-deductible: travel/accommodation > 10% of gross income; housing expenses > 10% of gross income

### Option B — Category B Group 2 (Occasional/Temporary Activities)
- **Who qualifies:** Taxpayers with occasional or temporary business activities (isolated professional acts with no evidence of repeated and permanent activity)
- **Tax base:** Gross income less allowable expenses
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive A+B rates; return by end of February; no advance payments
- **Social security:** As above
- **VAT obligations:** As above
- **Key limits:** Same non-deductible expense rules

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Visa-Free for Portuguese Language Community / EU / US / Canada
- **Eligibility:** Nationals of CPLP (Portuguese Language Community), EU, US, Canada with valid passports
- **Benefit:** Entry and stay up to 15 days without visa; Schengen visa or US visa holders (any country) also qualify for 15-day entry
- **Duration:** Up to 15 days
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Work and residence visa required for employment; no specific tax preference for newcomers identified

### Draft Visa Exemption (2025)
- **Eligibility:** All foreign nationals intending to stay up to 30 days
- **Benefit:** Visa exemption (draft law approved April 2025)
- **Duration:** Up to 30 days
- **Application deadline:** N/A — pending final enactment
- **Caveats:** Standard tax residence rules apply

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 24,600 STN (derived from USD/STN 21,113 and EUR/USD ~1.165 mid-2025). Confidence: Low — STN/EUR rate highly uncertain.
> Employment (Category A) and B2B (Category B) use same progressive scale. Social security 4% employee (employment only).

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (STN) | Social security (employee/self) | Total burden (STN) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈STN 738,000,000) | Employment | 175,152,000 | 29,520,000 (4%) | 204,672,000 | ~27.7% |
| €30,000 (≈STN 738,000,000) | B2B | 175,152,000 | 0 | 175,152,000 | ~23.7% |
| €60,000 (≈STN 1,476,000,000) | Employment | 397,152,000 | 59,040,000 (4%) | 456,192,000 | ~30.9% |
| €60,000 (≈STN 1,476,000,000) | B2B | 397,152,000 | 0 | 397,152,000 | ~26.9% |
| €100,000 (≈STN 2,460,000,000) | Employment | 688,152,000 | 98,400,000 (4%) | 786,552,000 | ~32.0% |
| €100,000 (≈STN 2,460,000,000) | B2B | 688,152,000 | 0 | 688,152,000 | ~28.0% |

**Working (income tax using formula: tax = income × rate − deduction):**
- STN 738,000,000: in 30% band (above 420,000,000). Tax = 738,000,000 × 30% − 45,048,000 = 221,400,000 − 45,048,000 = **176,352,000** (~23.9%)
- STN 1,476,000,000: tax = 1,476,000,000 × 30% − 45,048,000 = 442,800,000 − 45,048,000 = **397,752,000** (~26.9%)
- STN 2,460,000,000: tax = 2,460,000,000 × 30% − 45,048,000 = 738,000,000 − 45,048,000 = **692,952,000** (~28.2%)

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 24,600 STN (derived; highly uncertain — Low confidence)
- Single, no dependants; only zero-rate band and deduction formula applied
- IERS (20% on disposable income > STD 50,000/month ≈ STN 1,200,000/month) may also apply — not included in above (would add ~4–5% to effective rate for higher earners)
- Self-employed social security not confirmed — zero applied for B2B
