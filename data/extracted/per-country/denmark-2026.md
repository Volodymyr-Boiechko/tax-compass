## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for residents on worldwide income; nonresidents on Danish-source income. Tax year = calendar year. Annual income tax return filed by 1 May of the following year. (EY p.354, p.359)

An 8% mandatory **labor market tax (AM-bidrag)** is imposed on all salary income BEFORE the income tax calculation. Income taxes are then calculated on income after labor market tax. (EY p.357)

**Income is divided into personal income and net capital income.** Taxable income = personal income + net capital income − allowable deductions. Dividends taxed separately (not included in progressive tax). (EY p.354)

**Approximate total marginal tax rates — 2025 (income after AM-bidrag deduction)** (EY p.357)

| Taxable income (DKK, after AM-bidrag) | Tax rate | Tax due (DKK) | Cumulative (DKK) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 56,087 | 8% | 4,487 | 4,787 |
| Next 608,913 (56,087–665,000) | 42% | 255,743 | 260,230 |
| Above 665,000 | 56% | — | — |

*These rates are estimates based on average municipality tax rates and do not reflect the voluntary church tax.*

**2026 Change:** From 2026, marginal tax rate will be approximately **61%** (including labor market tax). An extra tax of 5% must be paid on income exceeding **DKK 2.5 million** (before labor market tax). (EY p.355)

**Effective gross-to-net calculation for employment:**
1. Apply AM-bidrag 8% to gross: taxable base = gross × 92%
2. Apply progressive rates to the remaining 92%
3. Overall top rate including AM-bidrag on gross income: ~56% in 2025, ~61% from 2026

**Investment income:**
- Dividends and capital gains on shares: 27% (up to DKK 67,500 in 2025; DKK 135,000 for married couples); 42% on excess. (EY p.356)
- Capital gains on sale of shares: up to 42%. (EY p.356)
- Capital gains on residential property occupied by owner: exempt (if conditions met). (EY p.356)
- Royalties (residents): taxed as personal income. Royalties (nonresidents): 22% withholding. (EY p.356)

**Standard deductions / allowances — 2025:** (EY p.357)
- Personal allowance: DKK 51,600 (2025)
- Supplementary allowance for salaried employees: 12.3% of salary income, up to DKK 55,600 (2025)
- Job deduction for salaried employees: 4.5% of salary income above DKK 224,500, up to DKK 2,900 (2025)
- Annuity pension contributions and discontinued pension schemes: deductible up to DKK 65,500/year (2025)
- Employer-administered life annuity schemes: fully deductible
- Interest on all types of debt: deductible from capital income; if net capital income is negative, ~25%–33% of negative amount may be offset against tax on other income
- Commuting costs to/from work (special rates apply)
- Fees to labor unions and unemployment insurance
- Child support and spouse support/alimony payments
- Single-parent additional allowance available; unused allowance may be transferred to spouse (not for expatriate regime). (EY p.357)

**Notes:**
- Married persons must file separate tax returns; however, certain deductions may be transferred between spouses. (EY p.359)
- Trading losses and interest expenses may be offset against other income and taxable gains; carried forward unlimited years but no carryback. (EY p.357)
- Losses from passive partnership interests (>10 non-working owners): only offset against income from same business. (EY p.357)
- Home-ownership tax (2025): 0.51% up to DKK 9,200,000; 1.4% on excess. (EY p.357)
- Inheritance tax: 15% on estate value exceeding DKK 346,000 (2025) for lineal heirs; additional 25% for other beneficiaries. Gifts from estate tax exempt up to DKK 76,900 (2025) for descendants. (EY p.357–358)

## Social Security

Denmark's social security for employees is minimal compared to most EU countries. (EY p.358)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| ATP (Supplementary Pension) | ~DKK 99/month (2026 est.) | ~2× employee portion | varies |
| Labor market insurance | — | ~DKK 15,000/year (est.) | — |
| Other mandatory contributions | 0% | varies | — |
| **Total employee SS** | **~DKK 1,188/year** | **significant** | — |

**ATP (Danish Supplementary Pension Scheme):**
- 2024 monthly contribution: DKK 297; employee pays 1/3 = ~DKK 99/month (DKK 1,188/year); employer pays 2/3.
- 2026 rate: estimated DKK 99/month (exact 2026 rate not yet published at EY content date). (EY p.358)
- The annual cost of insurance depends on the nature of the business and other factors; for a white-collar employee, approximately DKK 15,000/year. (EY p.358)

**Income ceiling:** Not applicable (ATP is fixed amount per month, not percentage-based).

**Notes:**
- No mandatory percentage-based employee social security contributions (unlike most EU countries).
- AM-bidrag (8% labor market contribution) is a tax, not social security, but functions similarly.
- Other mandatory contributions for coverage in Denmark do not apply (beyond ATP). (EY p.358)
- Totalization agreements with: Australia, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Canada (incl. Quebec), Chile, China Mainland, EU Member States, Iceland, India, Israel, Korea (South), Liechtenstein, Montenegro, Morocco, New Zealand, North Macedonia, Norway, Pakistan, Philippines, Serbia, Switzerland, Türkiye, United Kingdom, United States. (EY p.358–359)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Personal Income)
- **Who qualifies:** All self-employed individuals (sole traders, freelancers, professionals).
- **Tax base:** Business income treated as ordinary income (personal income) for the business owner. Expenses deductible to the extent incurred to obtain, secure or maintain business income. (EY p.355)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates as employment (including 8% AM-bidrag). Top marginal rate ~56% in 2025, ~61% from 2026.
- **Social security:** ATP contributions (employee portion); additional amounts vary.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required (25% standard VAT rate; threshold applies).
- **Key limits:** Same tax scale as employment; no special lower rate.

### Option B — Virksomhedsordningen (VSO — Business Tax Scheme)
- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals with business income who want to use the Business Tax Act rules. (EY p.355)
- **Tax base:** Taxable income from trade and industry assessed in accordance with company taxation principles, including rules for depreciation and write-offs.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Business income taxed under company principles; allows retention of profits within the business at corporate tax rate (22%) rather than top personal rate; retained profits taxed at personal rates when withdrawn.
- **Social security:** Standard rules apply.
- **VAT obligations:** Standard 25% VAT.
- **Key limits:** Complex accounting requirements; beneficial for self-employed with significant business assets and retained profits.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Special Expatriate Tax Regime (Flat 32.84% for 84 Months)
- **Eligibility:** Two alternatives:
  1. Salary ≥ DKK 78,100/month (2025; DKK 65,500/month for 2026) before deduction of ATP contribution (approx. DKK 99/month in 2026). Individual must not have been tax resident or otherwise tax liable to Denmark on salary/similar income or business income within the past 10 years. Individual must not have had controlling influence in the employing company 5 years before or during employment.
  2. Acknowledged as scientist or researcher by the Danish Council for Independent Research (no minimum salary requirement).
- **Benefit:** Salary income taxed at flat 32.84% (includes the 8% AM-bidrag; net effective rate on gross = 32.84%) for up to 84 months (7 years).
- **Duration:** Up to 84 months. After the 84-month period, ordinary progressive income tax rules apply.
- **Caveats:** Income other than salary (fringe benefits earned as A-income) during the regime period is subject to normal progressive tax rates. Does not apply to individuals taxed under ordinary rules (e.g., directors). (EY p.355)

## 2026 Changes

- **Marginal tax rate increase from 2026:** Marginal tax rate increases from approximately 56% to approximately 61% (including labor market tax). (EY p.355)
- **New 5% extra tax from 2026:** Additional 5% tax on income exceeding DKK 2.5 million (before labor market tax) from 2026 onward. (EY p.355)
- **Expatriate regime salary threshold for 2026:** DKK 65,500/month (down from DKK 78,100/month in 2025), before deduction of ATP contribution (~DKK 99/month in 2026). (EY p.355)
- **Underpaid tax from 2025 income year:** Underpaid tax up to DKK 24,000 carried forward to 2027 income year; remainder due in three installments in August, September and October 2026. (EY p.359)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only (personal allowance DKK 51,600; salaried employee deductions applied for employment).
> EUR/DKK rate used: 1 EUR = DKK 7.46 (fixed peg; Denmark in ERM II).
> "Employment" = gross salary; AM-bidrag 8% applied first; then progressive rates on remaining 92%.
> "B2B" = self-employment income; same progressive scale as employment.
> Social security: ATP employee portion ~DKK 1,188/year (negligible).
> 2025 brackets used (2026 changes noted but full 2026 bracket schedule not yet confirmed in EY source).

**Working — DKK 746,000 Employment (estimate):**
- AM-bidrag: 8% × 746,000 = DKK 59,680
- After AM-bidrag: DKK 686,320
- Deductions: 51,600 + 55,600 (capped supplementary) = 107,200
- Taxable: 686,320 − 107,200 = DKK 579,120
- Tax: 4,487 + 42% × (579,120 − 56,087) = 4,487 + 42% × 523,033 = 4,487 + 219,674 = DKK 224,161
- Note: DKK 579,120 < DKK 665,000 top bracket, so 56% bracket not reached
- Total: 59,680 + 224,161 = DKK 283,841 + ATP 1,188 = DKK 285,029 → **~38.2%**
- (Revised: ~38.2% for €100k; top 56% bracket only reached above DKK 665k after deductions, i.e., above ~€100k gross)

**Key note — Denmark vs VSO:** Self-employment (VSO scheme) can defer tax on retained profits to corporate rate (22%), improving cash flow for reinvesting self-employed. However, when profits are withdrawn, full personal rates apply. For most income levels below DKK 665k, there is minimal difference between employment and B2B at these income levels.

**Expatriate regime comparison:** Under the 32.84% flat rate, an expat earning DKK 746,000 (€100k) would pay 32.84% × 746,000 = DKK 245,000 vs ~DKK 285,000 under normal rules — saving approximately DKK 40,000/year.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/DKK = 7.46 (ERM II fixed peg)
- Personal allowance DKK 51,600 and salaried employee deductions applied for employment
- B2B: same progressive scale as employment (no separate self-employment deduction beyond business expenses)
- ATP employee contribution: DKK 1,188/year (fixed amount; negligible)
- Church tax NOT included (voluntary; ~0.7% on average)
- 2025 brackets used; 2026 will see ~61% top rate and DKK 2.5M threshold for extra 5% tax
