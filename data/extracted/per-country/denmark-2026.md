## Personal Income Tax

> Denmark has a progressive income tax system. The tax year is the calendar year. Residents are taxed on worldwide income. An 8% labour market contribution (AM-bidrag / arbejdsmarkedsbidrag) is applied first to gross income, and then ordinary income tax is applied to the post-AM income. The effective top marginal rate for 2025 is approximately 56% (including AM-bidrag). From 2026, a further 5% surcharge on income exceeding DKK 2.5 million will push the marginal rate to approximately 61%.

**Labour market contribution (AM-bidrag):** 8% on gross salary income — applied before income tax brackets.

**Income tax rates (applied to income after AM-bidrag deduction):**

| Taxable income (DKK, post-AM-bidrag) | Tax rate | Tax due DKK | Cumulative tax due DKK |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 56,087 | 8% | 4,487 | 4,787 |
| Next 608,913 (up to 665,000) | 42% | 255,743 | 260,230 |
| Over 665,000 | 56% | — | — |

*Note: These are approximate rates including average municipality tax. Dividends taxed separately at 27% (up to DKK 67,500) / 42% above that threshold. (EY p. 357)*

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal allowance: DKK 51,600 (2025)
- Supplementary allowance for salaried employees: 12.3% of salary, capped at DKK 55,600 (2025)
- Job deduction for salaried employees: 4.5% of salary above DKK 224,500, max DKK 2,900 (2025)
- Pension contributions to annuity schemes: deductible up to DKK 65,500/year (2025)
- Interest expenses: deductible from net capital income; 25–33% of negative net capital income may offset other tax

**Notes:**
- Municipal tax varies by municipality; rates above are averages and include municipality tax
- Church tax: additional ~0.7% for members of the Danish National Church
- AM-bidrag (8%) is deducted first, and the remaining income is then subject to the bracket rates above
- Top marginal rate for 2025: ~56% (including AM-bidrag)
- From 2026: extra 5% surcharge on income above DKK 2.5 million (before AM-bidrag) — top marginal ~61% (EY p. 355)

## Social Security

> Denmark has minimal mandatory employee social security contributions. The main employee levy is the ATP (Supplementary Pension Scheme) and a small unemployment insurance. Employer contributions are also modest by EU standards.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| ATP (Supplementary Pension) | DKK ~99/month (2025) | ~2× employee | Varies |
| Unemployment insurance | ~DKK 15,000/year (voluntary, white-collar estimate) | — | Voluntary |
| Labour market contribution (AM-bidrag) | 8% of gross salary | — | 8% of gross |
| **Total employee (approx.)** | **8% + small fixed amounts** | **~small** | **8% AM-bidrag** |

**Income ceiling:** No general ceiling on AM-bidrag
**Notes:**
- ATP employee contribution for 2024: DKK 297/month (1/3 borne by employee); 2025 rate DKK ~99/month employee share (EY p. 358)
- Unemployment insurance is voluntary in Denmark and administered through A-kasser (unemployment funds); annual cost approximately DKK 15,000 for a white-collar employee
- Denmark has totalization agreements with EU/EEA countries and numerous bilateral agreements (EY p. 359)

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals can use the standard personal income tax rules or elect the Business Tax Scheme (Virksomhedsskatteordning / VSO).

### Option A — Standard Personal Income Tax (Personlig beskatning)
- **Who qualifies:** Any sole trader / freelancer
- **Tax base:** Net business income (revenue minus expenses) taxed as personal income at progressive rates including AM-bidrag (8%) and bracket tax
- **Rate / mechanism:** AM-bidrag 8% + bracket tax (up to 56% total marginal rate for 2025)
- **Social security:** AM-bidrag 8% on gross business income; ATP contributions
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory registration above DKK 50,000 annual turnover; standard VAT rate 25%
- **Key limits:** All business expenses deductible; losses from business activity can offset other personal income

### Option B — Business Tax Scheme (Virksomhedsskatteordning — VSO)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual with business income who elects the scheme
- **Tax base:** Business profits assessed under corporate-style rules; allows interest deduction at business level; retained profits within the scheme can be taxed at the corporate rate of 22% (deferred personal tax)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Profits distributed as salary taxed at progressive personal rates; retained profits taxed at 22% (corporate tax rate); effective top rate when distributed ~56%
- **Social security:** AM-bidrag 8% on salary drawn from the scheme
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT rules apply (25%)
- **Key limits:** Detailed bookkeeping required; separate accounts for business and personal finances mandatory; special rules for capital gains from the business

### Option C — Capital Pension / Business Incorporation
- **Who qualifies:** Higher-income entrepreneurs
- **Tax base:** Operating through a company (ApS/A/S) and drawing salary; company profits taxed at 22% CIT; dividends at 27%/42%
- **Rate / mechanism:** Salary taxed at progressive PIT; dividends at 27% (up to threshold) or 42%
- **Social security:** Normal employee/employer contributions
- **VAT obligations:** Standard 25%
- **Key limits:** Company incorporation costs and administrative burden

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Special Expatriate Tax Regime (Forskerordningen)
- **Eligibility:** Two alternatives:
  - (1) Minimum monthly cash salary DKK 78,100/month (2025) or DKK 65,500/month (2026, before ATP deduction); must not have been Danish tax resident in 10 years prior
  - (2) Researchers/scientists acknowledged by the Danish Council for Independent Research — no minimum salary
- **Benefit:** Flat rate of 32.84% (including 8% AM-bidrag) on salary income for the duration of the regime; fringe benefits taxed separately at normal rates
- **Duration:** Up to 84 months (7 years) — extended from 48 months under 2024 legislation for employees commencing after 1 January 2024
- **Application deadline:** Must apply when regime commences; retrospective applications not permitted
- **Caveats:** Cannot have had a controlling influence in the employing company in the 5 years before/during employment; only salary income is covered — investment income taxed at normal rates; after 84 months, full progressive taxation applies (EY p. 355)

## 2026 Changes

> Several significant changes take effect from 2026. (EY p. 355)

- **New 5% surcharge:** From 2026, an additional 5% tax on income exceeding DKK 2.5 million (before AM-bidrag deduction) — top marginal rate increases to approximately 61%
- **Minimum salary for expatriate regime:** Reduced to DKK 65,500/month (from DKK 78,100) for 2026 to attract more foreign talent
- Annual indexation of personal allowance and deduction thresholds
- ATP contribution rates for 2026 not yet published at time of EY review

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single person, no dependants, Danish resident, standard deductions only, average municipality tax.
> Currency: DKK. 1 EUR ≈ 7.46 DKK (approximate 2025 rate).
> DKK 30,000/month ≈ EUR 48,000/year; using EUR amounts as stated for comparability.

**Employment at €30,000 (≈ DKK 223,800):**
1. AM-bidrag: DKK 223,800 × 8% = DKK 17,904 → post-AM income = DKK 205,896
2. Less personal allowance DKK 51,600 → taxable = DKK 154,296
3. Less supplementary deduction: DKK 223,800 × 12.3% = DKK 27,527 (capped at 55,600) → taxable ≈ DKK 126,769
4. PIT on DKK 126,769: all within 8% bracket (below DKK 56,087... no): DKK 56,087 × 8% = DKK 4,487; remaining DKK 70,682 × 42% = DKK 29,686 → total PIT = DKK 34,173
5. Total tax + AM = DKK 17,904 + DKK 34,173 = DKK 52,077 ≈ €6,980
6. Effective rate = €6,980 / €30,000 = **23.3%**

**Employment at €60,000 (≈ DKK 447,600):**
1. AM-bidrag: DKK 447,600 × 8% = DKK 35,808 → post-AM = DKK 411,792
2. Less personal allowance DKK 51,600 and supplementary DKK 55,600 → taxable ≈ DKK 304,592
3. PIT: DKK 56,087 × 8% = DKK 4,487; DKK 248,505 × 42% = DKK 104,372 → PIT = DKK 108,859
4. Total tax = DKK 35,808 + DKK 108,859 = DKK 144,667 ≈ €19,393
5. Effective rate = €19,393 / €60,000 = **32.3%**

**Employment at €100,000 (≈ DKK 746,000):**
1. AM-bidrag: DKK 746,000 × 8% = DKK 59,680 → post-AM = DKK 686,320
2. Less personal allowance DKK 51,600 and supplementary DKK 55,600 → taxable ≈ DKK 579,120
3. PIT: DKK 56,087 × 8% = DKK 4,487; DKK 608,913 × 42% = DKK 255,743 exceeds income: remaining DKK 523,033 × 42% = DKK 219,674; DKK 579,120 within 42% band → PIT = DKK 4,487 + DKK 219,674 = DKK 224,161; check: all of taxable (DKK 579,120 − DKK 56,087 = DKK 523,033) × 42% = DKK 219,674 + DKK 56,087 × 8% = DKK 4,487 → total PIT = DKK 224,161
4. Total tax = DKK 59,680 + DKK 224,161 = DKK 283,841 ≈ €38,050
5. Effective rate = €38,050 / €100,000 = **38.1%**

**B2B (sole trader via VSO) at €60,000 (≈ DKK 447,600 revenue):**
- Business expenses ~15%: taxable profit ≈ DKK 380,460
- AM-bidrag 8% = DKK 30,437; post-AM = DKK 350,023
- Personal allowance and supplementary deductions ~DKK 100,000 → taxable ≈ DKK 250,023
- PIT ≈ DKK 56,087 × 8% + DKK 193,936 × 42% = DKK 4,487 + DKK 81,453 = DKK 85,940
- Total: DKK 30,437 + DKK 85,940 = DKK 116,377 ≈ €15,600
- Effective rate ≈ €15,600 / €60,000 = **~26%** (before retained earnings optimisation via VSO)
