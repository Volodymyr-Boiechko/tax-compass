## Personal Income Tax

> Progressive PIT for residents (worldwide income) and nonresidents (South Sudan-source income). Resident = domiciled in South Sudan or physically present 183+ days in a tax year. Administered by SSRA (South Sudan Revenue Authority). Significant operational disruption due to ongoing conflict — data reflects legal position, not necessarily enforcement reality.

### Employment and Self-Employment Rates (monthly taxable income)

| Monthly taxable income (SSP) | Tax rate % |
|---|---|
| 0 to 20,000 | 0 |
| 20,001 to 40,000 | 5 |
| 40,001 to 57,000 | 10 |
| 57,001 to 90,000 | 15 |
| 90,001 and above | 20 |

### Sole Proprietor Turnover Tax (no audited financial statements)

| Annual gross turnover (SSP) | Annual tax (SSP) |
|---|---|
| 0 to 2,000,000 | Nil |
| 2,000,001 to 4,000,000 | 200,000 |
| 4,000,001 to 7,000,000 | 400,000 |
| 7,000,001 and above | 800,000 |

### Withholding Tax Rates

| Income category | Rate % | Persons subject |
|---|---|---|
| Dividends, interest and royalties | 10 | Residents and nonresidents |
| Technical or consultancy fees | 20 | Nonresidents only |
| Rent | 20 | Residents and nonresidents |
| Government contract payments | 15 | Residents |
| Government contract payments | 20 | Nonresidents |
| Pension income | 10 | Residents and nonresidents |
| Lottery and game winnings | 20 | Residents and nonresidents |

**Note:** Effective 14 August 2023 (2023-24 Finance Act), WHT on interest, dividends, rent, government contract payments and technical fees paid to residents/nonresidents is treated as an advance tax (creditable against final PIT liability).

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Pension deduction: 8% contribution to a government-recognized, self-funded retirement scheme deductible from taxable income
- Business deductions: non-capital expenses wholly and exclusively incurred to produce taxable income
- Capital allowances (instead of accounting depreciation):
  - Buildings and other structures: 10%/year
  - Vehicles, office equipment and computers: 33%/year
  - Other property: 25%/year
  - Intangible assets: 10%/year

**Exemptions:**
- Wages of foreign diplomatic/consular representatives and foreign personnel of liaison offices
- Wages of foreign representatives, foreign officials and foreign employees of international governmental organisations
- If agreed with government: wages of foreign representatives, foreign officials, foreign employees of donor agencies, contractors/grantees carrying out humanitarian aid, reconstruction, civil administration or technical assistance
- Compensation for damages and proceeds of life insurance policies

**Surtax suspension:**
- 2021-22 Finance Act reintroduced 30% surtax on PIT; however SSRA suspended the surtax via circular dated 13 February 2025 (EY p.1430)

**SSRA administrative circular (1 September 2024):** The following wage categories are suspended from PIT administration:
- Wages of employees of institutions of states and administrative areas
- Wages of employees of community-based organisations in states/administrative areas
- Wages of local employees of national and international NGOs
- Wages of local employees of private companies, partnerships and other legal entities

**Notes:**
- Capital gains: treated as business income; included in gross income subject to PIT
- Tax year is the calendar year
- Losses carry forward 5 years (same source); no carryback
- Self-assessment allowed; assessment by SSRA within 3 years of return filing
- End-of-service benefits, pensions, gratuities and social insurance fund payouts: 10% rate (SSRA circular February 2024); gratuity and terminal benefits taxable at 10% (remain taxable)
- Surtax on PIT: legally 30%, but currently suspended by SSRA circular 13 February 2025

## Social Security

> The only social security tax in South Sudan is a pension contribution. (EY p.1430)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| National Social Insurance Fund (pension) | 8% (5% for government staff) | 17% | — |
| **Total** | **8%** | **17%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** None specified in EY source
**Notes:**
- Only pension contributions exist; no health insurance or unemployment fund identified
- SSRA issued circular clarifying that PIT on National Social Insurance Fund payouts is suspended (EY p.1430)
- Self-employed social security treatment not specified

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor with Audited Accounts (Monthly Rate Scale)
- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals and sole proprietors with audited financial statements
- **Tax base:** Net taxable income (gross income less allowable business deductions); capital allowances apply
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive monthly rates (0–20%) applied to annual income (monthly rates × 12)
- **Social security:** Pension 8% if applicable
- **VAT obligations:** Not detailed in EY individual tax section
- **Key limits:** Losses carry forward 5 years (same source)

### Option B — Turnover Tax (No Audited Accounts)
- **Who qualifies:** Sole proprietors without audited financial statements
- **Tax base:** Annual gross turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed annual amounts: nil (≤ SSP 2M); SSP 200,000 (SSP 2–4M); SSP 400,000 (SSP 4–7M); SSP 800,000 (SSP 7M+)
- **Social security:** Pension 8% if applicable
- **VAT obligations:** Not detailed in EY individual tax section
- **Key limits:** No expense deductions; simpler compliance

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Diplomatic / NGO / Aid Worker Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Foreign diplomats, international organisation staff, foreign government representatives, donor agency staff carrying out humanitarian/reconstruction/civil administration work (if agreed with government)
- **Benefit:** Wages exempt from PIT
- **Duration:** Ongoing while qualifying activities continue
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Agreement with South Sudan government required for donor agency/contractor exemption

## 2026 Changes

- SSRA circular (13 February 2025): 30% surtax on PIT suspended (EY p.1430)
- SSRA circular (1 September 2024): Large categories of employment income suspended from PIT administration (EY p.1429)
- SSRA circular (27 February 2024): 10% rate clarified for end-of-service benefits, pensions, gratuities, social insurance fund payouts (EY p.1430)
- Double tax treaties signed with Morocco (1 February 2017) and UAE (1 January 2019) — not yet ratified; DTT with Kenya under consideration (EY p.1431)
- No other specific 2026 rate changes identified

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants, no pension deduction claimed.
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 1,350 SSP (approximate mid-2025; SSP is highly volatile and subject to black market premium — Low confidence).
> Monthly rate table annualised. No surtax (suspended). Pension deduction not applied (conservative).

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (SSP) | Social security (pension) | Total burden (SSP) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈SSP 40,500,000) | Employment | 7,695,000 | 3,240,000 (8%) | 10,935,000 | ~27.0% |
| €30,000 (≈SSP 40,500,000) | B2B | 7,695,000 | 0 | 7,695,000 | ~19.0% |
| €60,000 (≈SSP 81,000,000) | Employment | 15,795,000 | 6,480,000 (8%) | 22,275,000 | ~27.5% |
| €60,000 (≈SSP 81,000,000) | B2B | 15,795,000 | 0 | 15,795,000 | ~19.5% |
| €100,000 (≈SSP 135,000,000) | Employment | 26,295,000 | 10,800,000 (8%) | 37,095,000 | ~27.5% |
| €100,000 (≈SSP 135,000,000) | B2B | 26,295,000 | 0 | 26,295,000 | ~19.5% |

**Working (income tax, annual — monthly bands × 12):**
Annual band thresholds: 0% first SSP 240,000; 5% on SSP 240,001–480,000; 10% on SSP 480,001–684,000; 15% on SSP 684,001–1,080,000; 20% above SSP 1,080,000

- SSP 40,500,000: all in 20% band. Tax = 0+12,000+20,400+59,400+20%×(40,500,000−1,080,000) = 91,800+20%×39,420,000 = 91,800+7,884,000 = **7,975,800** (~19.7%)
  *(Cumulative through bands: 5%×240,000=12,000; 10%×204,000=20,400; 15%×396,000=59,400 = 91,800)*
- SSP 81,000,000: 91,800+20%×79,920,000 = 91,800+15,984,000 = **16,075,800** (~19.8%)
- SSP 135,000,000: 91,800+20%×133,920,000 = 91,800+26,784,000 = **26,875,800** (~19.9%)

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 1,350 SSP (approximate; highly volatile — Low confidence)
- Single, no dependants; 8% pension deduction NOT applied (to show gross burden)
- 30% surtax excluded (suspended by SSRA circular February 2025)
- SSRA administrative suspension of PIT on various wage categories ignored (worst-case legal position used)
