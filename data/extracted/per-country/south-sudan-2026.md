## Personal Income Tax

Resident individuals (domiciled in South Sudan OR physically present ≥183 days in a tax year) are subject to PIT on their worldwide income. Nonresident individuals are taxed on income sourced in South Sudan. (EY pp.1428–1429)

**Income subject to tax:**
- Wages and employment income
- Income from entrepreneurial activities
- Income from use of movable or immovable property
- Income from use of intangible property
- Capital gains (treated as gross business income, subject to PIT)

**Exempt income:**
- Wages of foreign diplomatic and consular representatives and foreign personnel of liaison offices
- Wages of foreign representatives, foreign officials, and employees of international governmental organizations
- Wages agreed with the government of South Sudan for foreign employees of donor agencies, NGO contractors/grantees carrying out humanitarian aid, reconstruction, civil administration, or technical assistance
- Compensation for damages and proceeds of life insurance policies

**Significant suspension (effective 1 September 2024):** The SSRA (South Sudan Revenue Authority) issued a circular suspending PIT administration and calculation on:
- Wages of employees of state/administrative institutions
- Wages of employees of community-based organizations registered in states/administrative areas
- Wages of local employees of national and international NGOs
- Wages of local employees of private companies, partnerships, and other legal entities

(EY p.1429) **This suspension has very significant practical implications — as of October 2025, PIT on private sector wages appears to be suspended.**

**PIT surtax (30%):** The 2021-22 Finance Act reintroduced a 30% surtax on PIT. However, the SSRA has suspended the surtax through a circular dated 13 February 2025. (EY p.1430)

### Employment / Self-Employment Income Tax Brackets (Monthly)

PIT on wages and entrepreneurial activities (sole proprietors) is based on these monthly tax rates. (EY p.1429)

| Monthly taxable income — Exceeding (SSP) | Monthly taxable income — Not exceeding (SSP) | Rate (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 20,000 | 0 |
| 20,000 | 40,000 | 5 |
| 40,000 | 57,000 | 10 |
| 57,000 | 90,000 | 15 |
| 90,001 | — | 20 |

### Entrepreneurial Activities — Sole Proprietors Without Audited Accounts (Annual Turnover Tax)

Sole proprietors without audited financial statements pay tax based on annual gross turnover levels: (EY p.1429)

| Annual gross turnover (SSP) | Annual tax (SSP) |
|---|---|
| 0 – 2,000,000 | Nil |
| 2,000,001 – 4,000,000 | 200,000 |
| 4,000,001 – 7,000,000 | 400,000 |
| 7,000,001 and above | 800,000 |

### Withholding Taxes (2023-24 Finance Act — advance tax treatment from 14 August 2023)

| Income category | Tax rate (%) | Persons subject to tax |
|---|---|---|
| Dividends, interest, and royalties | 10 | Residents and nonresidents |
| Technical or consultancy fees | 20 | Nonresidents only |
| Rent | 20 | Residents and nonresidents |
| Government contract payments | 15 | Residents |
| Government contract payments | 20 | Nonresidents |
| Pension income | 10 | Residents and nonresidents |
| Lottery and game winnings | 20 | Residents and nonresidents |

(EY p.1430)

**Deductions:**
- Pension contribution: 8% contribution to a government-recognized, self-funded retirement scheme is deductible in determining taxable income (EY p.1429)
- Business expenses: deductible only if incurred wholly and exclusively to produce taxable income; accounting depreciation not deductible (capital allowances used instead)
- Capital allowances: straight-line or reducing-balance over useful life; buildings/structures 10%; vehicles/office equipment/computers 33%; other property 25%; intangible assets 10%
- Business losses: carried forward up to 5 years to offset future profits from same source; no carryback

**Notes:**
- No property tax, net worth tax, inheritance tax, or gift tax (EY p.1430)
- End-of-service benefits, pensions, gratuities taxed at 10% rate (SSRA circular, 27 February 2024)
- SSRA circular of 13 February 2025 confirms PIT on National Social Insurance Fund payouts suspended; gratuity/terminal benefits remain taxable at 10%

## Social Security

The only social security tax in South Sudan is a pension contribution based on employment income. (EY p.1430)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension contribution | 8% | 17% | N/A (employees only) |
| (Government staff rate) | 5% | 17% | — |
| **Total** | **8%** | **17%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** Not specified by EY.

**Notes:**
- Employee rate: 8% for private sector employees; 5% for government staff
- Employer rate: 17% for all employers
- No social security system for self-employed
- The 8% pension contribution is deductible from taxable income (per deductions above)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor with Audited Accounts

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals carrying on entrepreneurial activities with proper audited financial records
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Monthly progressive PIT brackets 0–20% (annualized equivalent)
- **Social security:** No social security for self-employed
- **VAT obligations:** Not specified in EY section; South Sudan applies a Business Profit Tax for larger enterprises; VAT details unclear for individuals
- **Key limits:** Business losses carried forward 5 years; capital allowances in place of accounting depreciation

### Option B — Sole Proprietor Without Audited Accounts (Turnover Tax)

- **Who qualifies:** Sole proprietors without audited financial statements
- **Tax base:** Annual gross turnover (flat tax amounts — see table above)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed annual tax amounts by turnover tier (nil below SSP2M; SSP200,000 for SSP2M–4M; SSP400,000 for SSP4M–7M; SSP800,000 for above SSP7M)
- **Social security:** No social security for self-employed
- **Key limits:** Does not require proper books; very simple compliance; no deductions for expenses

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Tax Exemptions for Humanitarian/NGO Workers

- Foreign employees of NGOs, donor agencies, contractors/grantees carrying out humanitarian aid, reconstruction, civil administration, or technical assistance — if wages are agreed with the Government of South Sudan — are exempt from PIT (EY p.1428)

### Visa and Residency

- All visitors must obtain a visa except citizens of countries with visa exemption agreements (Egypt, Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda — visa-free for short-term visits typically up to 3 months)
- Work permits: issued for 1 year, annually renewable
- Residence permits: special (min. 3 years continuous residence; or 5 years continuous + scientific/technical/commercial activities); ordinary (5 years); temporary (12 months, renewable up to 12 months at a time)
- Note: Visitors must have yellow fever vaccination certificate and negative COVID-19 test

(EY pp.1431–1432)

## 2026 Changes

- **PIT suspension (September 2024):** SSRA circular effective 1 September 2024 suspended PIT administration on private company employees, NGO local staff, and government employees — no announced reversal as of October 2025; practical enforcement uncertain
- **Surtax suspension (February 2025):** 30% PIT surtax suspended by SSRA circular 13 February 2025; formally still in statute (2021-22 Finance Act); practically not being applied
- **Advance tax treatment (August 2023):** Withholding on dividends, interest, rent, government contract payments treated as advance (not final) tax; file return to reconcile
- No structural PIT rate changes announced for 2026

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants.
> Extreme caution: PIT suspended on private sector wages since 1 September 2024; effective rates below are statutory only.
> Currency: SSP (South Sudanese Pound). Exchange rate extremely volatile; 1 EUR ≈ 5,000–6,000 SSP (approximate 2025 market rate; official rate differs significantly). This is a Low-confidence data environment.
> Employee SS: 8% on gross salary (no ceiling specified); employer SS 17% not shown.

### Statutory rates (for reference, recognizing PIT suspension context):

| Gross income | Scenario | Statutory income tax | Social security (employee) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~SSP150,000,000 (€30k at 5,000 SSP/EUR) | Employment — statutory | ~SSP30,000,000 (20%) | SSP12,000,000 (8%) | ~SSP42,000,000 | **~28%** |
| ~SSP150,000,000 (€30k) | Employment — post-suspension (practical) | ~SSP0 | SSP12,000,000 (8%) | ~SSP12,000,000 | **~8%** |
| ~SSP150,000,000 (€30k) | Turnover tax (gross > SSP7M) | SSP800,000 (flat) | 0 | SSP800,000 | **~0.5%** |
| ~SSP300,000,000 (€60k) | Employment — statutory | ~SSP60,000,000 (20%) | SSP24,000,000 (8%) | ~SSP84,000,000 | **~28%** |

**Note:** At income levels equivalent to €30k+ in SSP terms, almost all income falls in the top 20% bracket. The practical effective rate as of late 2024 is likely much lower due to the SSRA PIT suspension. The turnover tax is applicable to sole proprietors without audited accounts.

**Pension deduction impact:** If 8% employee pension contribution is deductible, statutory PIT base is reduced; not modeled above.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/SSP ≈ 5,000 (indicative only; extremely volatile; official rates differ from market rates)
- Monthly brackets applied on monthly income; 20% bracket reached quickly at European-equivalent incomes
- PIT surtax (30%) excluded as suspended since February 2025
- No pension deduction applied in base case
- Employer-side SS (17%) not shown

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **SSRA PIT suspension practical status:** The September 2024 circular suspending PIT on most employment income is highly unusual; verify current status (whether reinstated, modified) directly with SSRA or a Juba-based tax advisor before any reliance on statutory rates
- [ ] **PIT surtax reinstatement risk:** The 30% surtax was suspended by SSRA circular (February 2025); it remains on the statute books from the 2021-22 Finance Act; SSRA could reinstate it at any time
- [ ] **Exchange rate:** South Sudan has multiple exchange rates (official, parallel market, Central Bank of South Sudan official rate); practical USD/SSP and EUR/SSP rates differ dramatically; any ZAR/SSP or EUR/SSP calculations require current market verification
- [ ] **VAT system:** EY does not describe a VAT system for individuals in South Sudan; verify whether VAT applies to services provided by freelancers/consultants
- [ ] **Social security ceiling:** EY specifies 8% employee / 17% employer but does not specify a maximum contribution base; verify with National Social Insurance Fund of South Sudan
- [ ] **Double tax treaties:** Treaties with Morocco and UAE signed but not yet ratified as of October 2025 (EY p.1431); treaty with Kenya under consideration; effective treaty relief for most countries not available
- [ ] **Practical enforcement environment:** South Sudan is in a complex peace/conflict context; tax administration capacity is limited; actual compliance and enforcement may differ substantially from statutory law
