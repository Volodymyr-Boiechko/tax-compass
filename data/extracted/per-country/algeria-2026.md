## Personal Income Tax

Progressive IRG (Impôt sur le Revenu Global) on worldwide income for residents; Algerian-source income for non-residents. Tax year = calendar year. Annual filing deadline: 30 April (form G1). (EY p.27)

Monthly income below DZD 30,000 is fully exempt from IRG since the 2020 Complementary Finance Act. Monthly income DZD 30,000–35,000 benefits from a transitional tax relief. (EY p.27)

**Monthly taxable income brackets (IRG — employment income):**

| Monthly taxable income (DZD) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 20,000 | 0% |
| 20,001 – 40,000 | 23% |
| 40,001 – 80,000 | 27% |
| 80,001 – 160,000 | 30% |
| 160,001 – 320,000 | 33% |
| 320,001+ | 35% |

The monthly taxable base is subject to a 40% reduction (first tax relief), with a minimum of DZD 1,000/month and a maximum of DZD 1,500/month. (EY p.27)

Monthly income DZD 30,000–35,000: additional second relief applies:
IRG = (IRG after first tax relief) × (137/51) − (27,925/8) (EY p.27)

**Non-monthly bonuses:** Taxed at a reduced flat rate of 10%. (EY p.27)

**Flat Rate Tax (IFU — Impôt Forfaitaire Unique):** Self-employment and business income ≤ DZD 8 million is subject to IFU (a single tax covering IRG, VAT, and corporate tax):
- Production activities, sales of goods, and distribution via digital platforms / direct network sales: **5%**
- Other activities (services): **12%**

If net income exceeds DZD 8 million, taxed under the "real regime" at the standard IRG employment rates. (EY p.26)

**Investment income:**
- Dividends: 15% final withholding tax (EY p.26)
- Interest on loans/deposits: 10% advance withholding; 1% final WHT for interest ≤ DZD 50,000; 10% tax credit for interest > DZD 50,000 (EY p.26)
- Directors' fees: 15% final withholding (EY p.26)

**Capital gains:**
- Long-term assets (held > 3 years): 35% of gain included in taxable income
- Short-term assets: 70% of gain included in taxable income
- Reduced 5% rate when capital gain is reinvested (EY p.26)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- 40% reduction on monthly taxable base (min DZD 1,000, max DZD 1,500/month) for employment
- Social insurance contributions paid by employee (deductible)
- Support payments (alimony)
- Insurance premiums paid as owner or lessee
- Interest on loans for business or principal home acquisition/construction
- Contributions to retirement pension or social insurance

**Notes:**
- Resident = owner/beneficial owner of a home, tenant ≥ 1 year, principal residence, centre of main interests, working in Algeria, or Algerian government agent abroad without foreign personal income tax liability (EY p.25)
- Non-residents taxed on Algerian-source income only (EY p.28)

## Social Security

Two regimes: one for employees, one for non-employees (self-employed). (EY p.29)

**Employees (CNAS — Caisse Nationale des Assurances Sociales):**

| Contribution | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Social security (all branches) | **9%** | **25%** |
| Social action fund | — | **1%** |
| CACOBATPH (construction/public works/water sectors only) | — | **12.96%** |

- Employer contributions are paid monthly; employee contributions withheld monthly (EY p.29)

**Non-employees (CASNOS — Caisse Nationale de Sécurité Sociale des Non-salariés):**

| Contribution | Self-employed |
|---|---|
| Social security | **15%** of annual revenue |

- Minimum contribution: DZD 32,400/year
- Maximum contribution: DZD 648,000/year
- Paid annually (EY p.30)

**Income ceiling:** No explicit cap for employees; CASNOS maximum is DZD 648,000/year (EY p.30)

**Totalization agreements:** Algeria has agreements with Belgium, France, and Tunisia. Foreign workers from these countries with detachment certificates may remain in their home country's system and be exempt from Algerian contributions. (EY p.30)

**Notes:**
- Employee contributions are deductible from IRG taxable income (EY p.27)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — IFU (Impôt Forfaitaire Unique) — Flat Regime for Small Business

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals and businesses with annual revenue ≤ DZD 8 million (~€55,000 at DZD 145/EUR)
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue (IFU is a single combined tax replacing IRG + VAT + corporate tax)
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - 5% on revenue from production, sales of goods, or digital platform/network sales
  - 12% on revenue from other activities (services)
- **Social security:** CASNOS at 15% of annual revenue (min DZD 32,400, max DZD 648,000/year)
- **VAT obligations:** IFU replaces VAT; no separate VAT filing below the DZD 8 million threshold
- **Key limits:** DZD 8 million annual revenue ceiling (~€55,000). Above this, switches to "real regime" with standard IRG rates.

(EY p.26–27)

### Option B — Real Regime (Régime Réel)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals with annual revenue > DZD 8 million, or those electing the real regime
- **Tax base:** Net profit = gross revenue minus allowable business expenses (depreciation, general expenses)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive IRG monthly bracket rates (0%–35%) applied to annual net profit
- **Social security:** CASNOS at 15% (capped at DZD 648,000/year)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT applies; monthly G50 filing required by 20th of each month
- **Key limits:** No upper revenue limit; mandatory for businesses > DZD 8 million revenue

(EY p.26–27)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

No specific preferential tax regime for newcomers, expats, or digital nomads is described in EY. Standard IRG applies to all residents on worldwide income. (EY pp.25–32)

## 2026 Changes

- No specific 2026 tax changes announced in the EY 2025-26 guide for Algeria.
- The DZD 30,000/month full IRG exemption (introduced by 2020 Complementary Finance Act) remains in force.
- IFU regime rates and thresholds appear unchanged from prior year.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> **1 EUR ≈ DZD 145** (approximate mid-2025 market rate; verify at bank-of-algeria.dz).
> Employment scenario: monthly salary, 40% reduction applied (capped DZD 1,500/month), 9% employee social security deductible before IRG.
> B2B scenario: IFU 12% (services) for revenue ≤ DZD 8M; CASNOS 15% capped at DZD 648,000/year.

### €30,000 Employment (≈ DZD 4,350,000/year = DZD 362,500/month)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross monthly salary | DZD 362,500 | |
| Employee SS (9%) | 9% × 362,500 | DZD 32,625 |
| After SS deduction | 362,500 − 32,625 | DZD 329,875 |
| 40% reduction (capped DZD 1,500) | cap applied | DZD 1,500 |
| Monthly taxable base | 329,875 − 1,500 | DZD 328,375 |
| IRG: 0–20,000 @ 0% | DZD 0 | |
| IRG: 20,001–40,000 @ 23% | 23% × 20,000 | DZD 4,600 |
| IRG: 40,001–80,000 @ 27% | 27% × 40,000 | DZD 10,800 |
| IRG: 80,001–160,000 @ 30% | 30% × 80,000 | DZD 24,000 |
| IRG: 160,001–320,000 @ 33% | 33% × 160,000 | DZD 52,800 |
| IRG: 320,001–328,375 @ 35% | 35% × 8,375 | DZD 2,931 |
| Monthly IRG | | DZD 95,131 |
| Annual IRG | × 12 | DZD 1,141,572 |
| Annual employee SS | 32,625 × 12 | DZD 391,500 |
| **Total annual burden** | 1,141,572 + 391,500 | **DZD 1,533,072** |
| **Effective rate** | 1,533,072 / 4,350,000 | **35.2%** |

### €30,000 B2B — IFU 12% (DZD 4,350,000/year, within DZD 8M limit)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
