## Personal Income Tax

An individual is liable to income tax on their taxable income for the year of assessment commencing 1 April and ending 31 March. Taxable income = total assessable income from employment, business, investment and other sources, after deducting qualifying payments and reliefs.

**Residency:** A person is a tax resident if they:
- Reside (generally live) in Sri Lanka, OR
- Are present in Sri Lanka for periods aggregating 183 days or more in a 12-month period commencing or ending during the year of assessment, OR
- Are an employee or official of the Government of Sri Lanka with spouse posted abroad

Nonresidents are taxed on income arising in or derived from a source in Sri Lanka only.

Exchange rate: LKR309.38 = USD1 (as of 9 February 2026). (EY p.1450)

**Important exemptions:**
- From 1 January 2020 to 31 March 2025: foreign-source income derived in foreign currency remitted to Sri Lanka through a bank was exempt from income tax
- From 1 January 2020 to 31 March 2025: income from services rendered in or outside Sri Lanka to a non-resident person to be utilized outside Sri Lanka, if payment made in foreign currency through a bank, was exempt
- **These exemptions expired 31 March 2025.** (EY p.1453)

### PIT Brackets — Year of Assessment 2025/26 (commencing 1 April 2025)

| Taxable income — Amount (LKR) | Tax rate (%) | Tax due (LKR) | Cumulative tax (LKR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 1,000,000 | 6 | 60,000 | 60,000 |
| Next 500,000 | 18 | 90,000 | 150,000 |
| Next 500,000 | 24 | 120,000 | 270,000 |
| Next 500,000 | 30 | 150,000 | 420,000 |
| Above 2,500,000 | 36 | — | — |

(EY p.1457)

**Effective structure:**
- 0–LKR1,000,000: 6%
- LKR1,000,001–1,500,000: 18%
- LKR1,500,001–2,000,000: 24%
- LKR2,000,001–2,500,000: 30%
- Above LKR2,500,000: 36%

### Personal Relief

| Period | Annual personal relief |
|---|---|
| Years of assessment ending before 31 March 2025 | LKR1,200,000 |
| Years of assessment commencing on or after 1 April 2025 | LKR1,800,000 |

The personal relief of LKR1,800,000 effectively means no tax is owed on taxable income up to LKR1,800,000 (since LKR1,800,000 falls in the first two brackets, and the relief is deducted from taxable income before applying the brackets). (EY pp.1454–1455)

### Investment Income

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Capital gains (gains from realization of investment assets — included in taxable income) | 10% |
| Dividends | Generally included in taxable income at progressive rates |
| Terminal benefits — up to LKR10M | 0% |
| Terminal benefits — LKR10M–20M | 6% of excess |
| Terminal benefits — above LKR20M | 12% of excess + LKR600,000 |

Investment gains: LKR50,000 annual exemption; annual total gains ≤ LKR600,000 exempt from income tax (subject to certain conditions). (EY p.1454)

Gains from listed shares (SEC-listed) are exempt. Principal residence exempt if owned continuously for 3 years and lived in for at least 2 of those 3 years. (EY p.1454)

### Withholding Taxes on Payments to Nonresidents

| Type of payment | Rate (%) |
|---|---|
| Dividends | 15 |
| Interest (excluding exempt interest) | 10 |
| Payments for land, sea, air transport or telecoms services | 2 |
| Other payments | 14 |

(EY p.1456)

**Qualifying payments and reliefs (deductible from taxable income):**
- Personal relief: LKR1,800,000/year (from 1 April 2025)
- Donations to approved charitable institutions: up to one-third of taxable income or LKR75,000 (whichever is less)
- Rental income relief: 25% of total rental income (for repairs, maintenance, depreciation on investment property)
- Solar panel deduction: LKR600,000/year for roof-mounted solar panels connected to national grid

**Other deductions:**
- Business expenses deductible if incurred in production of income from the business
- Losses from business: carried forward to set off against business income (or investment income) in succeeding 6 years

**Notes:**
- VAT standard rate: 18%; VAT registration threshold: LKR60 million/year (LKR15 million/quarter); proposed reduction to LKR36M/year / LKR9M/quarter (EY p.1457)
- APIT (Advance Personal Income Tax) mandatory from 1 January 2023: deducted by employer from employees earning > LKR150,000/month; employees must file annual return if earning > LKR150,000/month
- Tax year: 1 April to 31 March; returns due 30 November following end of year of assessment

## Social Security

Most employees are covered by the Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) Act of 1958. (EY p.1458)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| EPF (Employees' Provident Fund) | 8% | 12% | Not applicable |
| ETF (Employees' Trust Fund) | 0% | 3% | Not applicable |
| **Total** | **8%** | **15%** | **0%** |

**Income ceiling:** Not specified by EY; contributions calculated on total earnings.

**End-of-service (gratuity):** Under the Payment of Gratuity Act 1983, employees who have worked for >5 years are entitled to gratuity = half a month's salary for each year of service. This is payable by the employer on termination.

**Notes:**
- Self-employed individuals are NOT subject to EPF or ETF contributions
- ETF is fully employer-paid; not deducted from employee earnings
- Provident fund benefits received from approved funds are exempt from income tax (EY p.1451)
- Pension/gratuity/Employees' Trust Fund income received from such funds representing income from investments made after 1 April 1987 is exempt

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Net Income)

- **Who qualifies:** Any resident individual carrying on a trade, business, or profession in Sri Lanka
- **Tax base:** Net business income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses incurred in production of income)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PIT brackets 6–36% applied to net taxable income after personal relief (LKR1,800,000)
- **Social security:** No EPF/ETF for self-employed; no mandatory contributions
- **VAT obligations:** VAT at 18% if turnover ≥ LKR60 million/year; quarterly estimated tax payments under self-assessment (SET)
- **Key limits:** Business losses carried forward 6 years (deductible against business or investment income); capital allowances in lieu of accounting depreciation

### Option B — Partnership (Taxable above LKR1M at 6%)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals conducting business through partnerships
- **Rate:** Partnerships with taxable income >LKR1 million taxed at 6%; capital gains at 10% (EY p.1452)
- **Key limits:** Relevant for multiple-partner structures; individual partners also taxed on partnership distributions

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Terminated Foreign Currency Income Exemptions (Previously Very Significant)

- From 1 January 2020 to **31 March 2025**: Foreign-source income in foreign currency remitted through a bank was entirely exempt. This made Sri Lanka very attractive for digital nomads and remote workers during this period.
- **Expired 31 March 2025.** Foreign-source income of residents is now fully taxable at progressive rates from 1 April 2025. (EY p.1453)

### No Current Dedicated Newcomer Regime

As of April 2025, Sri Lanka does not have a remittance-basis or foreign-income-exemption regime for new residents. Standard worldwide income taxation applies to all residents.

---

## 2026 Changes

- **Personal relief increased to LKR1,800,000** from 1 April 2025 (was LKR1,200,000); this significantly reduces PIT for lower and middle earners (EY p.1454)
- **APIT mandatory from 1 January 2023**: applies to employees earning >LKR150,000/month; no employee consent needed (EY p.1455)
- **Foreign income exemptions expired 31 March 2025**: from 1 April 2025, all resident worldwide income taxable (EY p.1453)
- **2025/26 PIT brackets effective** (commencing 1 April 2025): 6% on first LKR1M, 18% on next LKR500k, 24% on next LKR500k, 30% on next LKR500k, 36% on excess above LKR2.5M
- **VAT threshold proposed reduction**: from LKR60M to LKR36M annually (pending enactment) (EY p.1457)
- IRA(A) No. 2 of 2025 most recent amendment to Inland Revenue Act

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, personal relief LKR1,800,000 applied.
> "Employment" = gross salary (employee-side; employer 15% EPF+ETF not shown).
> "B2B" = gross revenue; 30% assumed business expenses; self-employed (no EPF/ETF).
> Currency: LKR (Sri Lanka Rupee). 1 EUR ≈ LKR334 (at LKR309/USD × 1.08 EUR/USD).
> Employee SS: EPF 8% on gross salary.

### Working calculations:

**€30,000 Employment (≈ LKR10,020,000):**
- EPF (employee 8%): 8% × 10,020,000 = LKR801,600
- Taxable income = 10,020,000 − 1,800,000 = LKR8,220,000
- PIT: 60,000 + 90,000 + 120,000 + 150,000 + 36% × (8,220,000 − 2,500,000) = 420,000 + 36% × 5,720,000 = 420,000 + 2,059,200 = LKR2,479,200
- Total: 2,479,200 + 801,600 = LKR3,280,800; effective rate = 32.7%

**€30,000 B2B (30% expenses → net LKR7,014,000):**
- Taxable: 7,014,000 − 1,800,000 = LKR5,214,000
- PIT: 420,000 + 36% × (5,214,000 − 2,500,000) = 420,000 + 36% × 2,714,000 = 420,000 + 977,040 = LKR1,397,040
- EPF: 0 (self-employed)
- Effective on gross: 1,397,040 / 10,020,000 = **13.9%**

**€60,000 Employment (≈ LKR20,040,000):**
- EPF: 8% × 20,040,000 = LKR1,603,200
- Taxable: 20,040,000 − 1,800,000 = LKR18,240,000
- PIT: 420,000 + 36% × (18,240,000 − 2,500,000) = 420,000 + 36% × 15,740,000 = 420,000 + 5,666,400 = LKR6,086,400
- Total: 6,086,400 + 1,603,200 = LKR7,689,600; effective = 38.4%

**€60,000 B2B (30% expenses → net LKR14,028,000):**
- Taxable: 14,028,000 − 1,800,000 = LKR12,228,000
- PIT: 420,000 + 36% × (12,228,000 − 2,500,000) = 420,000 + 36% × 9,728,000 = 420,000 + 3,502,080 = LKR3,922,080
- Effective on gross: 3,922,080 / 20,040,000 = **19.6%**

**€100,000 Employment (≈ LKR33,400,000):**
- EPF: 8% × 33,400,000 = LKR2,672,000
- Taxable: 33,400,000 − 1,800,000 = LKR31,600,000
- PIT: 420,000 + 36% × (31,600,000 − 2,500,000) = 420,000 + 36% × 29,100,000 = 420,000 + 10,476,000 = LKR10,896,000
- Total: 10,896,000 + 2,672,000 = LKR13,568,000; effective = 40.6%

**€100,000 B2B (30% expenses → net LKR23,380,000):**
- Taxable: 23,380,000 − 1,800,000 = LKR21,580,000
- PIT: 420,000 + 36% × (21,580,000 − 2,500,000) = 420,000 + 36% × 19,080,000 = 420,000 + 6,868,800 = LKR7,288,800
- Effective on gross: 7,288,800 / 33,400,000 = **21.8%**

This made Sri Lanka very attractive for digital nomads. This exemption has now expired; effective rates shown above apply from 1 April 2025.

- EUR/LKR = 334 (LKR309/USD × EUR/USD 1.08)

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Foreign income exemption replacement regime:** The 1 January 2020 – 31 March 2025 foreign currency income exemption expired; any replacement or new incentive for export-of-services income or digital nomads should be monitored through the Inland Revenue Department (ird.gov.lk)
- [ ] **EPF contribution ceiling:** EY does not specify a ceiling on EPF contributions; verify whether an annual or monthly earnings cap applies
- [ ] **VAT registration threshold reduction:** Proposed reduction from LKR60M to LKR36M/year pending enactment; if enacted, more self-employed professionals would need to register for VAT; verify current status
- [ ] **APIT quarterly payment mechanics:** Self-employed file quarterly estimated tax under the Statement of Estimated Tax (SET) system; penalties for underpayment; verify current quarterly due dates for 2025/26
- [ ] **Economic crisis context:** Sri Lanka's economic conditions in 2022-2025 led to significant policy changes; tax rates and thresholds may be adjusted further; monitor for additional IRA amendments
- [ ] **Capital gains on listed shares exemption:** Gains from shares listed on the Colombo Stock Exchange are currently exempt; verify whether this remains in effect for 2025/26 given the economic reform context
- [ ] **EPF for professionals providing services:** Some professional service providers may be classified differently for EPF purposes; verify applicability of EPF to IT freelancers billing through sole proprietorship vs. company
