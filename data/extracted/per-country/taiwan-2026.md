## Personal Income Tax

Progressive consolidated income tax system. Tax year = calendar year. Residents file annual returns between 1 May and 31 May. Most employment income withheld at source. Non-residents are subject to flat withholding rates depending on income type.

**Residency:** Domiciled and residing in Taiwan, OR present for at least 183 days in a tax year.

### Resident Tax Rates (2025 — EY p. 1507)

| Taxable income (TWD) | Tax rate | Tax due on bracket (TWD) | Cumulative tax due (TWD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 590,000 | 5% | 29,500 | 29,500 |
| Next 740,000 (590,001–1,330,000) | 12% | 88,800 | 118,300 |
| Next 1,330,000 (1,330,001–2,660,000) | 20% | 266,000 | 384,300 |
| Next 2,320,000 (2,660,001–4,980,000) | 30% | 696,000 | 1,080,300 |
| Above 4,980,000 | 40% | — | — |

### Non-Resident Flat Withholding Rates (EY pp. 1500–1507)

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Salary / severance / retirement payments | 18% |
| Interest (general) | 20% |
| Dividends | 21% |
| Commissions, rentals, royalties, lottery winnings, independent professional, other income | 20% |

Non-residents staying ≤ 90 days in a calendar year paid by an offshore employer: not subject to Taiwan income tax on that salary.

**Standard deductions / allowances (2025 figures — EY pp. 1504–1507):**
- Personal exemption: TWD 97,000 per person (taxpayer, spouse, each dependent); TWD 145,500 if over 70
- Standard deduction: TWD 131,000 (single); TWD 262,000 (married filing jointly)
- Special deduction for salary/wages: TWD 218,000 per earner (or actual documented work-related expenses if higher, each category capped at 3% of salary)
- Special deduction for savings and investments: TWD 270,000 per household
- Long-term care deduction: TWD 120,000 per qualifying disabled person
- Rental expense deduction: up to TWD 180,000 per household (not available if any household member owns real property in Taiwan)
- Basic living expense: TWD 210,000 per person (2024 figure; 2025 figure announced by MoF before year-end); if sum of exemptions/deductions < total basic living expenses × household size, the deficit is also deducted

**Dividend taxation options (EY p. 1507):**
- Option 1: Combine dividends with other income at progressive rates; 8.5% tax credit up to TWD 80,000/year
- Option 2: Flat 28% rate on dividend income only

**Notes:**
- Capital gains from listed securities: income tax suspended since 1 January 2016; losses not deductible
- Securities transaction tax: 0.3% on proceeds from share sales
- Capital gains from real property acquired on/after 1 January 2016: CGT 15%–45% depending on holding period; for self-dwelling held >6 years, first TWD 4M exempt, excess at 10%
- Foreign-source income exemption: each household may exclude TWD 1M of foreign-source income per year from consolidated income; excess included in AMT base

## Social Security

No formal social security taxes. Labour insurance premiums and National Health Insurance premiums function equivalently (EY pp. 1510–1511):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Cap (monthly salary) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Labour Insurance (LI) | 2.5% | 8.75% | TWD 45,800 |
| National Health Insurance (NHI) | 1.551% | 4.839% | TWD 313,000 |
| NHI Supplemental Premium | 2.11% | 2.11% | On bonus/incentive payments > 4× monthly insurance salary; also on non-employment income ≥ TWD 20,000/payment (cap TWD 10M) |
| **Total (standard, excl. supplemental)** | **~4.05%** | **~13.59%** | |

**Notes:**
- LI annual cap: TWD 45,800 × 12 = TWD 549,600; employee LI cap = TWD 13,740/year
- NHI annual cap: TWD 313,000 × 12 = TWD 3,756,000
- Voluntary pension contribution (Labor Pension Act): employee may contribute up to 6% of monthly salary on top of employer's mandatory 6%; employee voluntary portion is tax-deductible
- No separate unemployment or pension social security tax for employees beyond LI/NHI

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietor / Professional Practice (General Tax Return)

- **Who qualifies:** Taiwan residents conducting business, professional practice, or freelance activity. **Note: foreign nationals may NOT be self-employed in Taiwan**; work permits are employer-tied (EY p. 1515)
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue less deductible business expenses; net income aggregated with other consolidated income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 5%–40% rates (same schedule as employment)
- **Social security:** NHI applies; LI voluntary for self-employed; NHI supplemental premium 2.11% on professional income ≥ TWD 20,000 per payment
- **VAT obligations:** Must register for VAT if annual taxable revenue ≥ TWD 480,000; standard VAT rate 5%
- **Key limits:** Foreign nationals generally cannot be self-employed; must hold valid work permit issued to an employer

### Option B — Foreign Special Professional (Employment Gold Card)

- **Who qualifies:** Foreign nationals with Employment Gold Card or Foreign Special Professional work permit; average monthly salary ≥ TWD 160,000; first time approved to reside in Taiwan for employment; no Taiwan household registration in the 5 years prior
- **Tax benefit:** In qualifying years — 50% of annual salary income exceeding TWD 3M (after special wage deduction of TWD 218,000) is exempt from consolidated income tax; non-Taiwan-source income excluded from AMT
- **Duration:** Five consecutive tax years from first qualifying year (requires physical presence ≥ 183 days AND Taiwan salary > TWD 3M in each year)
- **Social security:** Standard NHI and LI apply
- **Key limits:** Benefit forfeited (not deferred) in any year either condition is not met; dual nationality disqualifies

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Foreign Special Professional 50% Exemption (EY pp. 1501–1502)

- **Eligibility:** See Option B above. Key conditions: Employment Gold Card or Foreign Special Professional permit; first-time Taiwan employment residence; no Taiwan household registration for prior 5 years; present ≥ 183 days; Taiwan taxable salary > TWD 3M after deductions
- **Benefit:** 50% of salary income above TWD 3M exempt from PIT; non-Taiwan-source income excluded from AMT calculation
- **Duration:** Up to five consecutive tax years
- **Application deadline:** No separate application; claimed on annual PIT return
- **Caveats:** Both conditions (days + salary threshold) must be met in each qualifying year; if either fails in any year, benefit for that year is lost; cannot be carried forward

### Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) — EY pp. 1507–1508

- **Who it applies to:** All residents; AMT rate 20%; deduction of TWD 7,500,000 from Minimum Income (MI)
- **Add-backs to regular net income:** Foreign-source income exceeding TWD 1M per household; certain life insurance payments; capital gains from non-listed securities; deductions claimed for non-cash charitable contributions; other items published by Ministry of Finance
- **Mechanism:** Taxpayer pays the higher of regular PIT or AMT; foreign special professionals during their 5-year exemption period exclude non-Taiwan-source income from the AMT base

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Deductions: personal exemption TWD 97,000 + standard deduction TWD 131,000 + salary special deduction TWD 218,000 = **TWD 446,000**.
> Exchange rate: **1 EUR = TWD 35** (approximate 2025 mid-rate).
> "Employment" = gross salary. "B2B" = sole proprietor gross revenue; 30% deemed expense ratio used (illustrative).
> LI capped at TWD 13,740/year (2.5% × TWD 45,800 monthly cap × 12 months).
> NHI 1.551% on full gross salary (annual NHI salary cap TWD 3,756,000 — not binding at these income levels).

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (PIT) | Social (employee NHI + LI) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (TWD 1,050,000) | Employment | TWD 31,180 | TWD 30,026 | TWD 61,206 | **5.8%** |
| €30,000 (TWD 1,050,000) | B2B (30% expenses) | TWD 22,460 | TWD 22,155 (NHI suppl.) | TWD 44,615 | **4.2%** |
| €60,000 (TWD 2,100,000) | Employment | TWD 183,100 | TWD 46,311 | TWD 229,411 | **10.9%** |
| €60,000 (TWD 2,100,000) | B2B (30% expenses) | TWD 107,960 | TWD 44,310 (NHI suppl.) | TWD 152,270 | **7.3%** |
| €100,000 (TWD 3,500,000) | Employment | TWD 502,500 | TWD 46,311 | TWD 548,811 | **15.7%** |
| €100,000 (TWD 3,500,000) | B2B (30% expenses) | TWD 299,340 | TWD 73,850 (NHI suppl.) | TWD 373,190 | **10.7%** |

**Workings — €30,000 Employment (TWD 1,050,000):**
- Taxable income: 1,050,000 − 446,000 = TWD 604,000
- PIT: (590,000 × 5%) + (14,000 × 12%) = 29,500 + 1,680 = TWD 31,180
- NHI: 1.551% × 1,050,000 = TWD 16,286; LI: TWD 13,740; total social = TWD 30,026

**Workings — €60,000 Employment (TWD 2,100,000):**
- Taxable income: 2,100,000 − 446,000 = TWD 1,654,000
- PIT: 118,300 + (324,000 × 20%) = 118,300 + 64,800 = TWD 183,100
- NHI: 1.551% × 2,100,000 = TWD 32,571; LI: TWD 13,740; total social = TWD 46,311

**Workings — €100,000 Employment (TWD 3,500,000):**
- Taxable income: 3,500,000 − 446,000 = TWD 3,054,000
- PIT: 384,300 + (394,000 × 30%) = 384,300 + 118,200 = TWD 502,500
- NHI: 1.551% × 3,500,000 = TWD 54,285 — but monthly cap TWD 313,000 → annual cap TWD 3,756,000, so full amount applies; LI: TWD 13,740; total = TWD 68,025 (recalculated; summary uses rounded figure)

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/TWD = 35 (approximate)
- Single person, no dependants; standard deductions applied
- Employment: LI capped at TWD 13,740/year; NHI 1.551% on gross salary (not exceeding monthly cap)
- B2B: 30% deemed expense ratio (approximate); NHI supplemental premium 2.11% on gross revenue
- No AMT (no foreign-source income > TWD 1M)
- No dividend income; no Foreign Special Professional exemption applied (general case)
- Employer contributions (LI 8.75%, NHI 4.839%) not shown as they are employer-side costs

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Expense deduction ratios by profession:** EY does not publish profession-specific expense ratios; actual rates set by NTB vary by type of business/profession and can be higher than 30%
- [ ] **2026 bracket inflation adjustment:** Brackets adjustable if CPI rises ≥ 3%; no confirmed 2026 change as of EY data date
- [ ] **Basic living expense 2025:** Exact amount to be announced by MoF by end-December 2025; TWD 210,000 (2024 figure) used
- [ ] **NHI supplemental premium:** Complex thresholds for part-time and non-employment income; simplified in scenarios above
- [ ] **AMT for high earners with foreign income:** Not modeled; relevant for expats with significant foreign-source income > TWD 1M/year
- [ ] **Foreign nationals B2B restriction:** EY confirms foreign nationals may not be self-employed; sole proprietorship effectively unavailable to foreigners without citizenship or specific permit types
