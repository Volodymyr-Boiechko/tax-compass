## Personal Income Tax

Paraguay has a **territorial tax system** with a **low flat rate** for capital/investment income and a progressive (but low) rate for personal service income. The personal income tax is called IRP (Impuesto a la Renta Personal). Residents taxed on Paraguay-source income; non-residents taxed on Paraguay-source income. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Returns filed by March of the following year. (EY p.1212)

**IRP structure — Two bases:**

**1. Capital / investment income (IDU — Impuesto a los Dividendos y Utilidades + other capital income):**
- 8% flat rate on income and capital gains derived from capital (dividends, interest, capital gains from property sales, etc.)
- Note: Dividends/profits paid to resident Paraguayan individuals: 8%; to non-residents: 15% (EY p.1213)

**2. Personal service income (income from labor — independent and dependent):**

| Net annual income (PYG) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to PYG 50,000,000 | 8% |
| PYG 50,000,001 – 150,000,000 | 9% |
| PYG 150,000,001+ | 10% |

**Tax-free threshold:** Gross income from personal services must exceed PYG 80,000,000/year before IRP applies (triggers at the point total gross income exceeds PYG 80 million since 1 January of that year). (EY p.1214)

Note: PYG 80,000,000 ≈ EUR 10,000–11,000 at current exchange rates (1 EUR ≈ PYG 7,700–8,000). **This means low-income individuals do not pay IRP at all.**

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Individuals may deduct actual expenses related to personal service income (food, clothing, rent, furniture, entertainment, personal expenses — notably broad list) (EY p.1215)
- Expenses must be supported by documentation complying with tax rules
- No itemized cap in the law for most personal expenses; practical deductibility is the key feature
- Capital gains from disposal of movable property: exempt if total does not exceed PYG 20,000,000/year (EY p.1214)

**Notes:**
- No taxation on the 13th salary (legally required bonus month in December): EXEMPT (EY p.1215)
- No taxation on exchange differences from holding foreign currency/deposits
- Interest from national public debt securities and municipalities: EXEMPT
- Stock exchange bond profits: EXEMPT
- Returns from investment funds (Law No. 5452/2015): EXEMPT
- Interest from banking deposits and credit cooperatives (Law 861/1996): EXEMPT
- Compensation for dismissal up to legal minimum: EXEMPT
- Scholarships under national programs: EXEMPT

## Social Security

Paraguay's social security is managed by the IPS (Instituto de Previsión Social). (EY p.1216)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security (IPS) | 9% (11% banking) | 16.5% (17% banking) | Voluntary |
| **Total** | **9%** | **16.5%** | **Voluntary** |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling specified; contributions apply on full compensation. (EY p.1216)

**Notes:**
- Banking sector employees: 11% (employee), 17% (employer)
- IPS provides health care, disability, maternity, and pension benefits
- Paraguay has totalization agreements with Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Ecuador, Spain, and Uruguay (EY p.1216)
- Self-employed: no mandatory IPS; may voluntarily affiliate
- Work permits are not required for residents holding a residence permit (EY p.1218)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Independent Professional / Self-Employed (IRP — Renta Personal)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual providing independent professional services in Paraguay where gross income from personal services exceeds PYG 80,000,000/year.
- **Tax base:** Gross income from personal services minus documented personal expenses (broad allowance; food, clothing, rent, household goods, entertainment, etc. all deductible with documentation).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive IRP: 8% up to PYG 50M net income; 9% on PYG 50–150M; 10% above PYG 150M. The effective net rate is very low given broad expense deductibility.
- **Social security:** Voluntary IPS; no mandatory SS for self-employed.
- **VAT obligations:** Register for IVA (10% standard rate; 5% for basic foodstuffs and pharmaceuticals) if providing taxable services. Monthly filing with the Subsecretaría de Estado de Tributación (SET).
- **Key limits:** Must maintain invoice/documentation system; if income below PYG 80M/year, no IRP obligation.

### Option B — Small Business (Micro/Small Enterprise)

- **Who qualifies:** Small businesses below the IRP threshold (PYG 80M/year) or structured as a SIMPLE or RESIMPLE (simplified tax regimes for micro-enterprises).
- **Tax base:** Simplified fixed-rate payments replacing IVA and/or IRP obligations.
- **Rate / mechanism:** RESIMPLE: small fixed monthly payment based on activity/income range, replacing IRP and IVA; for annual revenue below PYG 80M.
- **Social security:** Same voluntary IPS.
- **Key limits:** Revenue cap below PYG 80M for RESIMPLE; useful for micro-entrepreneurs and informal sector entrants.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Temporary and Permanent Residence — Very Accessible

- **Eligibility:** Any foreign national wishing to reside in Paraguay.
- **Process:** Temporary residence valid 2 years (renewable); then permanent residence application. Documents: passport, birth certificate, police records, marriage/divorce certificate, criminal records, consular visa (some nationalities), proof of economic solvency. (EY p.1217)
- **Tax impact:** Residents taxed only on Paraguay-source income (territorial). Foreign income NOT taxed.
- **Work permits:** Not required separately — residence permit is sufficient for self-employment and work. (EY p.1218)
- **Duration:** Permanent residence card valid 10 years (renewable indefinitely).

### The "Paraguay Option" for Digital Nomads and Entrepreneurs

Paraguay's combination of features makes it highly attractive:
1. **Flat 8–10% IRP** (among the lowest personal income tax rates in Latin America)
2. **Territorial taxation**: foreign-source income = 0% Paraguayan tax
3. **No mandatory social security for self-employed**
4. **Easy residence process**: straightforward documentation, no minimum investment required (unlike Panama/Uruguay)
5. **Low cost of living**
6. **IVA 10%** (VAT — moderate rate)

## 2026 Changes

- **No IRP rate changes announced** for 2026; the 8%/9%/10% structure (progressive based on net income) remains. (EY p.1215)
- **PYG 80M threshold:** Adjust for inflation — the threshold may be periodically revised by decree; verify at SET.
- **IPS:** No contribution rate changes announced; 9%/16.5% employee/employer rates remain.
- **Totalization agreements:** Existing agreements with Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Ecuador, Spain, Uruguay continue.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants.
> PYG/EUR rate: 1 EUR = PYG 7,800 (approximate 2025; PYG ~7,200–8,000/USD × 1.08).
> Employment: IRP (progressive 8%–10%) + IPS employee 9%.
> Self-employed B2B: IRP only; IPS voluntary (not included); assume 30% of gross as documented expenses (conservative).
> Tax-free threshold: PYG 80,000,000 applies at gross income level; if gross exceeds PYG 80M, all income from that point is taxable (or only excess? — EY states "from the point it reaches PYG 80M since 1 January").

**Employment PIT working — PYG 468,000,000:**
- IPS: 9% × 468M = 42,120,000
- Net income: 468M − 42.12M = 425,880,000
- IRP: 50M @ 8% = 4,000,000; 50M–150M @ 9% = 9,000,000; 150M–425.88M @ 10% = 27,588,000 → Total = 40,588,000
- (Corrected: 40,588,000 IRP, not 43,974,000 — revision: 10% × 275,880,000 = 27,588,000 → 4M+9M+27.588M = 40,588,000)

**SE B2B working — PYG 468,000,000 (30% deduction):**
- Net = 468M × 70% = 327,600,000
- IRP: 50M @ 8% = 4M; 50M–150M @ 9% = 9M; 150M–327.6M @ 10% = 17,760,000 → Total = 30,760,000

**Key insight:** Paraguay is arguably the most tax-efficient country in South America for self-employed professionals and entrepreneurs. The combination of a territorial system (foreign income exempt) + broad expense deductibility + low 8–10% IRP rates + no mandatory social security for the self-employed creates effective rates of 6–7% even at €30k–€100k gross income. For a digital nomad or location-independent professional, this is exceptional. The easy residence process (no minimum investment requirement, unlike Panama) adds to the appeal.

**Assumptions:**
- PYG/EUR = 7,800 (approximate 2025)
- PYG 80M gross income threshold exceeded at all modeled levels
- Employment: IPS 9% deducted from PIT base (treated as before-tax deduction); IRP on net
- SE B2B: 30% documented expense deduction (conservative; actual may be higher)
- 13th month salary (Christmas bonus): EXEMPT — not included in taxable calculation
- Capital gains from movable property < PYG 20M: EXEMPT — not modeled
