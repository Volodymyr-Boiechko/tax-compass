## SYSTEM NOTE — Paraguay's Simple, Low-Burden Tax System

> Paraguay has one of the simplest and lowest personal income tax burdens in Latin America:
>
> - **Personal income tax (IRP):** Progressive at only 3 rates: **8% / 9% / 10%** on net income from personal services (employment and self-employment). Maximum rate is 10%.
> - **Capital gains / other income (excluding personal services):** Flat **8%** (non-deductible).
> - **Dividends and profit distributions (IDU):** 8% (resident) or 15% (nonresident) — final withholding.
> - **Territorial system:** Paraguay taxes only Paraguayan-source income. Foreign-source income is completely exempt.
> - **Social security:** IPS (Instituto de Previsión Social) at 9% employee / 16.5% employer.
> - **No work permit required:** Temporary and permanent residents do not need a work permit — residence permit is sufficient for gainful activity.

## Personal Income Tax (IRP — Impuesto a la Renta Personal)

Paraguay residents (national or foreign individuals domiciled in Paraguay) are subject to IRP. The IRP applies to two types of income that are taxed separately: (EY pp.1212–1216)

### Type 1 — Income from Personal Services (Employment & Self-Employment)

Taxable income includes:
- Salaries, wages, bonuses, per diems (not subject to accounting), commissions, and other remuneration in any form from private or public entities, binational entities
- Remuneration from independent personal services
- Board members' fees and other management remuneration

**Non-taxable threshold for personal services:** The IRP on personal services is triggered only once total gross income taxed under this heading **exceeds PYG 80 million** annually (computed from 1 January of the tax year). Once this threshold is reached, IRP is levied on all gross income received **minus deductible expenses incurred in the first fiscal year following the day on which the amount is reached**. (EY p.1214)

**Progressive IRP rates on net income from personal services:** (EY p.1215–1216)

| Net income — Up to (PYG) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to 50,000,000 | 8% |
| 50,000,001 to 150,000,000 | 9% |
| 150,000,001 and above | 10% |

### Type 2 — Other Income and Capital Gains (Excluding Personal Services and IDU)

Flat rate: **8%** on gross income (no deductions allowed for this category). (EY p.1215)

Includes:
- Capital gains from disposal of rights, shares, or interests in resident or nonresident entities giving rights to movable or immovable property in Paraguay
- Capital gains from sale or assignment of real estate (including leasing rights)
- Capital gains from disposal of movable property
- Lottery, raffle, bingo prizes > PYG 500,000
- Interest and investment income from money deposits, financial instruments, credit operations, factoring, leasing, asset securitization
- Royalties received in Paraguay or from rights used in Paraguay
- Income from transparent legal structures (trusts, investment funds)

**Exempt from IRP:** (EY pp.1214–1215)
- Interest on public debt securities (government bonds)
- Capital gains from sale of property by expropriation
- Capital gains from movable property if total annual disposal ≤ PYG 20 million
- Exchange differences from holding foreign currency or deposits in foreign currency
- 13th salary (mandated by Labor Code)
- Dismissal compensation up to legal minimum
- Diplomatic/consular remuneration (reciprocity)
- Ex gratia or non-contributory pensions
- Stock market securities gains (listed bonds and shares regulated by National Securities Commission)
- Bank and financial institution interest (deposits/savings governed by Law No. 861/1996 and cooperatives/savings mutual funds)
- Prizes ≤ PYG 500,000 in lotteries/raffles

### Dividends and Profit Tax (IDU — Impuesto a Dividendos y Utilidades)

- Rate: **8%** if recipient is a Paraguayan resident; **15%** if recipient is a nonresident.
- Applies to: dividends, profits, surpluses and any benefits derived by shareholders/partners of sole proprietorships, partnerships, cooperatives, and entities resident or incorporated in Paraguay.
- Excludes income taxed by IRP. (EY p.1213)

### Deductions for Personal Services Income

Individuals may deduct expenses from income derived from independent personal services and employment. Fully deductible if documented per tax rules. Deductible items include: (EY p.1215)
- Food
- Clothing
- Rent of house and maintenance (whether owned or rented)
- Purchase of furniture, household appliances and household goods
- Entertainment allowances
- Other personal expenses

**Note:** For other income and capital gains (Type 2), **no deductions are allowed.**

## Social Security (IPS — Instituto de Previsión Social)

| Contribution | Employee % | Employer % |
|---|---|---|
| IPS (general) | 9% | 16.5% |
| IPS (banking employees) | 11% | 17% |
| **Total (general)** | **9%** | **16.5%** |

**Income ceiling:** Not specified in EY source — contributions levied on employees' compensation.

**Notes:**
- Totalization agreements: Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Ecuador, Spain, Uruguay (EY p.1216)
- Paraguayan SS Institution (IPS) is formal in documentation requirements for foreign individuals (Certificates of Coverage)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — IRP on Personal Services (Standard)

- **Who qualifies:** All resident individuals deriving income from independent personal services.
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus deductible personal expenses (documented as per tax rules). Triggered once cumulative annual gross income from personal services exceeds PYG 80 million.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive 8%/9%/10% on net income. Annual return by March.
- **Social security:** IPS 9% employee contribution applies; employer portion may be required if engaging workers.
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IVA (Paraguay VAT) at 10% standard rate (5% for food, medicine, agricultural inputs); monthly filing.
- **Key limits:** Below PYG 80 million annual gross income → no IRP liability (complete exemption). Very generous threshold.

### Option B — IRACIS / IRE Business Regime (for formal business entities)

- **For companies and formal business:** Standard corporate tax regime (IRE) at 10% on net profit. This is not available to individual natural persons under IRP — the IRP regime applies to individuals.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Territorial Taxation (Permanent)
- **Eligibility:** All individuals.
- **Benefit:** Only Paraguay-source income taxed. Foreign-source income completely exempt.
- **Duration:** Permanent feature.

### Simple Residency (No Work Permit Required)
- **Eligibility:** All foreigners obtaining residence permits.
- **Benefit:** No separate work permit needed. Temporary and permanent residents may conduct gainful activities with residence permit only.
- **Path to residency:**
  - Tourist entry: 90 days renewable
  - Spontaneous/occasional residence: 90 days (renewable once in same year)
  - Temporary residence: 2 years (renewable); path to permanent after 2 years
  - Permanent residence: 10 years (renewable); requires proof of economic solvency
- (EY pp.1216–1218)

### Very Low Tax Rate (Top Rate 10%)
- Paraguay's maximum personal income tax rate of 10% on net income from personal services, combined with no tax on foreign income, makes it one of the most attractive jurisdictions in South America for digital nomads and high-income earners.

### IRP Non-Taxable Threshold (PYG 80 million)
- Annual gross income from personal services below PYG 80 million (≈ USD 10,600 / ≈ €9,600 at 2026 rates) is completely exempt from IRP. (EY p.1214)

## 2026 Changes

- **IRP rates:** 8%/9%/10% progressive on net income from personal services — no changes announced.
- **IDU rates:** 8% (residents) / 15% (nonresidents) — no changes announced.
- **IPS rates:** 9% employee / 16.5% employer — no changes announced.
- **PYG 80 million threshold:** Verify annual update with SET (Subsecretaría de Estado de Tributación).
- **No major structural tax changes announced for 2026.**
- **Double tax treaties:** Chile, Qatar, Spain, Taiwan, UAE, Uruguay.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single person, no dependants, income from personal services only.  
> "Employment" = gross salary; IPS 9% employee (no ceiling specified — applied to full gross); IRP on net income.  
> "B2B" = self-employment (independent personal services); IPS 9% on gross; 30% deductible expenses assumed; IRP on net.  
> 1 EUR ≈ PYG 8,300 (approximate May 2026; 1 USD ≈ PYG 7,540, 1 EUR ≈ 1.10 USD).

### Working: PYG 249,000,000/year (≈ €30,000) — Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | — | PYG 249,000,000 |
| IPS employee (9%) | 9% × 249,000,000 | PYG 22,410,000 |
| Deductible expenses (30% of gross) | 30% × 249,000,000 | PYG 74,700,000 |
| Net income | 249,000,000 − 22,410,000 − 74,700,000 | PYG 151,890,000 |
| IRP: 8% on 50,000,000 = 4,000,000; 9% on (150,000,000−50,000,000)=100,000,000 → 9,000,000; 10% on (151,890,000−150,000,000)=1,890,000 → 189,000 | 4,000,000 + 9,000,000 + 189,000 | PYG 13,189,000 |
| **Total burden** | **13,189,000 + 22,410,000** | **PYG ~35,599,000** |
| **Effective rate** | **35,599,000 / 249,000,000** | **~14.3%** |

### Working: PYG 249,000,000/year (≈ €30,000) — B2B

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue | — | PYG 249,000,000 |
| IPS (9%) | PYG 22,410,000 | — |
| Expenses (30%) | PYG 74,700,000 | — |
| Net income | 249,000,000 − 22,410,000 − 74,700,000 | PYG 151,890,000 |
| IRP: same as above | PYG 13,189,000 | — |
| **Total burden** | **13,189,000 + 22,410,000** | **PYG ~35,599,000** |
| **Effective rate** | **35,599,000 / 249,000,000** | **~14.3%** |

Note: Employment and B2B are identical in structure for IRP — both use progressive rates on net income from personal services.

**Key insight:** Paraguay's effective tax rate is remarkably stable at approximately **14–15% total** (IRP + IPS) across all income levels modeled. This stability results from the near-flat structure (8%–10% rates are close) combined with the 9% IPS. The IRP maximum rate of 10% means the income tax component alone never exceeds 10% of net income. At the PYG 80 million threshold (≈ €9,600), income below that level pays zero IRP.

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR ≈ PYG 8,300 (approximate May 2026)
- IPS: 9% on full gross income (no ceiling specified)
- Deductible personal expenses: 30% of gross income (documented; illustrative)
- IRP on net income (gross − IPS − expenses): progressive 8%/9%/10%
- PYG 80 million exemption threshold: all three income scenarios exceed this threshold
- IVA (10%/5%) not modeled for B2B
- No IDU (dividends tax) scenario modeled — applies to profit distributions, not personal service income

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **IPS income ceiling:** EY source does not specify a salary ceiling for IPS contributions. If uncapped, the 9% applies to full gross income. Verify at ips.gov.py.
- [ ] **Deductible expense list:** The list of deductible personal expenses (food, clothing, rent, furniture, entertainment) is broad and subjective. The 30% used above is illustrative — actual amount depends on documented expenses.
- [ ] **PYG 80 million threshold 2026:** May be updated by SET decree — verify at set.gov.py.
- [ ] **IRP rates 2026:** 8%/9%/10% confirmed for current period; verify 2026 rates with SET.
- [ ] **IVA (10%):** Applies to self-employed providing services; not modeled. IVA is a significant additional obligation.
- [ ] **No digital nomad visa explicitly confirmed:** Paraguay's straightforward residency regime (no work permit needed) and 10% maximum tax rate effectively serve the same purpose, but there is no law specifically designated as a "digital nomad visa."
- [ ] **Exchange rate:** PYG rate against EUR/USD managed; verify at bcp.gov.py.
- [ ] **IDU for business structures:** If a freelancer operates through a company and distributes profits, IDU (8% for residents) applies in addition to corporate tax — total combined rate would be higher than the IRP personal services rate.
