## Personal Income Tax

Three-level system: federal (Bundessteuer / impôt fédéral direct), cantonal (Kantonssteuer), and municipal (Gemeindesteuer) taxes — each levied separately. Tax year = calendar year (current-year basis). Returns generally due 31 March of the following year. Married persons taxed jointly on all types of income. (EY p.1488)

No single "national" tax table exists — cantons set their own progressive rates; municipalities apply a multiplier (Steuerfuss) to the cantonal rate. Rates vary dramatically by canton of residence. (EY p.1481, 1485)

**Federal income tax — maximum overall rate: 11.5%** (EY p.1485)

Federal tax is progressive; the combined federal + cantonal + municipal rate for high earners varies from approximately 22% (Zug, Schwyz — lowest-tax cantons) to ~45%+ (Geneva, Vaud) of taxable income. (EY p.1481)

**Cantonal and municipal taxes** (EY p.1481, 1485):
- Cantonal rate: progressive, 0–34% depending on canton and income
- Municipal multiplier (Steuerfuss): varies; adds 50%–170% of the cantonal tax
- Combined cantonal + municipal top rates: approximately 8.05%–33.63% of taxable income
- Church tax: cantonal percentage of income tax for registered members of recognized churches; varies by canton

**Zurich canton reference rates (federal + cantonal + municipal):**

| Income level (CHF) | Approx. combined effective rate (Zurich city) |
|---|---|
| CHF 50,000 | ~17–19% |
| CHF 100,000 | ~25–27% |
| CHF 150,000 | ~30–32% |
| CHF 200,000+ | ~33–36% |
| Top marginal (approx.) | ~40–42% |

(Source: Zurich canton tax calculator; these are approximations; individual circumstances vary)

**Key reference cantons (combined top effective rates for CHF 300k+ single):**
- Zug: ~22–24% (one of the lowest in Switzerland)
- Schwyz: ~23–25%
- Nidwalden: ~24%
- Zurich: ~34–37%
- Geneva: ~44–46%
- Vaud: ~41–43%
- Basel-City: ~38–40%

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Necessary employment expenses (Berufskosten): commuting costs, meals, professional expenses (actual or flat rate)
- Social security contributions (AHV/IV/ALV/BVG employee portions): fully deductible from taxable income (EY p.1484)
- Qualified pension plan contributions (pillar 2 and pillar 3a): deductible within limits (EY p.1484)
- Expatriate flat deduction: CHF 18,000/year for housing and other expatriate expenses; additional actual expenses deductible if proven (EY p.1484)
- Health insurance premiums: standardized deductions allowed

**Investment income:**
- Dividends: 35% Swiss withholding tax (Verrechnungssteuer) at source; fully creditable for Swiss tax residents against income tax (EY p.1482–1483)
- Interest >CHF 200/year from bank accounts: same 35% withholding; creditable against income tax
- Capital gains from movable assets (shares, securities): **exempt from federal and cantonal income tax** for private investors (EY p.1483). Exception: classified as professional securities dealer — taxed as ordinary income.
- Capital gains from real estate: taxable in all cantons (special cantonal real estate gains tax) (EY p.1483)

**Net wealth tax:**
- Federal level: no net wealth tax
- Cantonal and municipal: all cantons levy annual net wealth tax on worldwide assets (except real estate abroad and permanent establishments abroad); rates approximately 0.10%–0.88% of net assets (EY p.1485)
- Significant for high net worth individuals; not common in other EU countries

**Notes:**
- Married couples taxed jointly; Einheitstarif (joint tariff) with splitting mechanisms in many cantons; can create a "marriage penalty" compared to unmarried couples filing separately
- Home ownership reform: Switzerland voted 28 September 2025 to abolish imputed rental income (Eigenmietwert) taxation; reform effective from 2028 at the earliest (EY p.1484)
- No general capital gains tax on private movable assets — one of Switzerland's most attractive features

## Social Security

Three-pillar system: (1) AHV/IV/EO (state pension/disability/maternity); (2) BVG (occupational pension); (3) individual savings. (EY p.1486)

**Pillar 1 — AHV/IV/ALV contributions (2025)** (EY p.1486):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| AHV (old-age & survivors) | 5.30% | 5.30% | 7.80%* |
| IV (disability) | — | — | included in AHV rate |
| EO (maternity/service) | included | included | included |
| ALV (unemployment) | 1.10% | 1.10% | not applicable |
| ALV supplementary (>CHF 148,200) | 0.50% | 0.50% | not applicable |
| **Total AHV/IV/EO** | **5.30%** | **5.30%** | **~10.00%** |
| **Total AHV+ALV (up to CHF 148,200)** | **6.40%** | **6.40%** | **~10.00%** |

*Self-employed: maximum 10% AHV rate on income from business/profession (EY p.1487). No ceiling on AHV contributions for self-employed.

**ALV (unemployment) ceiling:** CHF 148,200/year; contributions of 2.2% total (split equally) only on this portion. Supplementary ALV 0.5% each on income above CHF 148,200. (EY p.1486)

**AHV — no ceiling:** Unlike ALV, AHV contributions have no income ceiling. Self-employed pay up to 10% on entire net income. (EY p.1486–1487)

**Pillar 2 — BVG (occupational pension):**
- Mandatory for employees earning >CHF 22,680/year (2025 coordination deduction threshold)
- Employee + employer each contribute at least 50% of total mandatory contribution
- Contribution rates: age-graded (typically 7–18% total, split equally)
- Self-employed: not required to join a pension fund (not in EY p.1487)

**Health insurance (Krankenversicherung):**
- Individual mandatory private health insurance (not employer-based)
- Premium: CHF 300–700+/month depending on insurer, region (Prämienregion), deductible choice (franchise)
- Employer does NOT pay health insurance for employees (unlike most EU countries)
- Cantonal premium subsidies available for low-income earners

**Self-employed AHV contributions:** (EY p.1487)
- Maximum 10% of net income from business/profession
- Minimum CHF 503/year if income below CHF 9,800
- Reduced scale for income CHF 9,800–57,400 (minimum 5.371%)
- On income above CHF 57,400: full 10% applies
- Additional contributions to family compensation fund and administrative costs
- Not required to join pension fund (Pillar 2); recommended but optional

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Selbstständigerwerbende / Indépendant (Sole Trader / Self-Employed Professional)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals operating a business or practicing an independent profession (Freie Berufe: doctors, lawyers, architects, IT consultants, designers, etc.) on their own account and at their own risk. Key test: commercial independence, own client base, own tools/infrastructure, risk of loss. (EY p.1482)
- **Tax base:** Net profit = revenue minus all necessary business expenses. All reasonable professional expenses deductible (office rent or home office, equipment, software, professional development, insurance, travel, professional fees). Cash-basis or accrual accounting.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net profit included in overall taxable income → taxed at federal + cantonal + municipal progressive rates. No special flat rate regime (unlike Italy's Forfettario or Portugal's IFICI). No distinction between employment income and self-employment income for tax rate purposes.
- **Social security:** AHV up to 10% of net income (no ceiling); ALV not applicable; private health insurance; optional BVG.
- **VAT (MWST):** Registration mandatory once annual revenue exceeds CHF 100,000 (threshold for small businesses). Standard rate 8.1%; reduced rate 2.6% for essential goods; special rate 3.8% for accommodation. Below CHF 100,000: voluntary registration possible (beneficial for B2B with input tax recovery). (ESTV)
- **Key benefits:** No Gewerbesteuer-equivalent at cantonal level for professionals; no distinction between "liberal profession" and "trade" for income tax rate purposes (unlike Germany); capital gains on business-asset sale included in taxable income; private capital gains exempt.
- **Key limits:** Choice of canton of residence has massive impact on total tax rate; relocation to Zug/Schwyz can reduce combined rate from ~40% to ~22–24% compared to Zurich or Geneva.

### Option B — GmbH or AG (Corporate Structures)

- **GmbH (Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung):** Minimum capital CHF 20,000. Corporate income tax: federal 8.5% on net profit; cantonal/municipal varies widely (approx. 12–21% combined federal + cantonal for most cantons). Effective combined rate in Zug: ~11.8%; in Zurich: ~19.7%; in Geneva: ~24.2% (approximate 2025 effective rates).
- **AG (Aktiengesellschaft):** Minimum capital CHF 100,000. Same corporate tax rates as GmbH.
- **Owner-manager salary:** Deductible for corporate tax; taxed as employment income at personal progressive rates + full SS.
- **Dividends:** Subject to 35% withholding tax (Verrechnungssteuer); fully refundable to Swiss residents through tax return (creditable against income tax). Dividends to shareholders with ≥10% participation: only 70% included in federal taxable income (10% exclusion at cantonal level varies); effective relief method. (EY p.1483)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Lump-Sum Taxation (Pauschalbesteuerung / Forfait Fiscal)

- **Eligibility:** Resident aliens who were resident or domiciled abroad for the past 10 years; must not engage in any employment or carry on a business in Switzerland. (EY p.1485)
- **Benefit:** Tax imposed on income imputed from living expenses (rather than actual worldwide income). Lump sum may not be less than the tax that would be payable on: Swiss real estate income + Swiss investment income + Swiss patent/royalty income + pensions from Swiss sources + any foreign income where treaty exemption is claimed. (EY p.1485)
- **Duration:** Ongoing as long as conditions are met; some cantons grant for limited period only.
- **Caveats:** Cannot engage in ANY employment or carry on any Swiss business. Amount negotiated with cantonal tax authorities in many cantons. Available in most cantons (not Zurich — abolished 2010, not Berne, not Basel-City). Very attractive for very high net worth retirees/passive investors. Not available for working professionals.

### Expatriate Flat Deduction

- **Eligibility:** Expatriates (as defined by Swiss law: foreign employees on assignment to Switzerland). (EY p.1484)
- **Benefit:** Annual flat deduction of CHF 18,000 for housing and expatriate-related expenses; additional actual expenses deductible if documented. (EY p.1484)
- **Duration:** For the period of assignment in Switzerland.
- **Caveats:** No equivalent to France's impatriation regime or Netherlands' 30% ruling — the CHF 18,000 flat deduction is the primary expat benefit; modest by comparison.

### No NHR-Equivalent

Switzerland has no non-habitual resident flat-rate regime comparable to Portugal's IFICI (20% flat) or Italy's Regime Forfettario. All income is taxed progressively at federal + cantonal + municipal rates regardless of residency duration. The primary planning tool for newcomers is **canton selection** (Zug, Schwyz, Nidwalden for lowest rates) and **lump-sum taxation** if not working.

## 2026 Changes

- **France-Switzerland double tax treaty amendment:** New treaty in force 24 July 2025, applicable from 1 January 2026; includes updated telework provisions. Cross-border workers can telework up to 40% from France without Swiss tax implications. From January 2026, automatic salary data exchange between Swiss and French tax authorities begins. (EY p.1489–1491)
- **Italy-Switzerland cross-border commuter agreement:** In force from 17 July 2023; Switzerland retains 80% of withholding tax on new cross-border commuters; old transitional regime (exclusive Swiss taxation) continues until 2033 for pre-July 2023 commuters. (EY p.1490)
- **Telework law (federal):** Swiss Federal Council set 1 January 2025 as effective date for federal law on taxing telework internationally; creates legal basis for telework taxation rules under Swiss treaties. (EY p.1491)
- **Home ownership reform (Eigenmietwert):** Referendum passed 28 September 2025 to abolish imputed rental income on owner-occupied properties and restrict mortgage interest deductions. Reform expected in force 2028 at earliest; no impact for 2026. (EY p.1484)
- **Croatian nationals:** Swiss Federal Council reviewing Croatian national quotas near end of 2025; announcement expected for 2026 quota numbers for B and L permits. (EY p.1494)
- **AHV/ALV contribution rates:** 2025 rates confirmed (AHV 10.6% total employee + employer; ALV 2.2% up to CHF 148,200); subject to annual review by Swiss compensation offices (SVA).
- **VAT rates (from 1 January 2024):** 8.1% standard, 2.6% reduced, 3.8% accommodation — in force; no announced change for 2026.
