## Personal Income Tax

**There is no personal income tax in Bahrain.** Employer-paid allowances provided as part of employment income are not taxable for income tax purposes. There is no definition of a tax resident in domestic legislation. (EY p.112)

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Employment income | **0%** |
| Business / self-employment income | **0%** |
| Capital gains | **0%** |
| Dividends | **0%** |
| Interest | **0%** |
| Inheritance / estate / gift | **0%** (no such taxes) |
| Wealth tax | **0%** (no such tax) |

A tax residency certificate can be obtained for the purposes of applying a double tax treaty concluded with Bahrain. (EY p.112)

## Social Security / Social Insurance

Bahrain's social security system has **three different contribution structures** depending on nationality. All calculated on total remuneration up to **BHD 4,000/month** ceiling.

### Bahraini Nationals (private sector):

| Component | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Unemployment contribution | 1% | 1% |
| Employment injuries | — | 3% |
| Old age, non-employment disability and death | 7% | 13% |
| **Total** | **8%** | **17%** |

- Employer contribution of 17% is scheduled to increase by 1% each January until it reaches 20% in 2028 (EY p.112)
- Annual health insurance fee: BHD 22.5 per Bahraini employee (paid by employer to Ministry of Health via SIO; not required if employer provides equivalent medical/health insurance) (EY p.113)

### GCC Non-Bahraini Nationals (private sector):

- Subject to their **home country social security system** under the GCC Unified Law of Insurance Protection Extension
- Home country social security rates and caps apply
- Not subject to Bahraini social insurance

(EY p.113)

### Non-GCC Nationals (private sector):

| Component | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Unemployment contribution | 1% | — |
| Employment injuries | — | 3% |
| **Total** | **1%** | **3%** |

- Total combined contribution: **4%** (EY p.113)
- Employees also entitled to end-of-service benefit (see below)

**Income ceiling (all nationalities):** BHD 4,000/month total remuneration

**End-of-service benefits for non-Bahraini national employees (effective 1 March 2024):**
- Employers must pay monthly end-of-service contributions electronically through the SIO portal
- Years 0–3: 4.2% per month (= 0.5 months' base salary per year)
- Year 4+: 8.4% per month (= 1 month's base salary per year)
- Benefits funded by contributions paid to SIO; paid to employee at end of employment

(EY p.113)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employed / Sole Proprietor / Free Zone Business

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual operating a business in Bahrain
- **Tax base:** N/A — no income tax on individuals
- **Rate / mechanism:** **0%** income tax on all business income
- **Social security:** Social insurance applies if employing Bahraini nationals; self-employed individuals' own contributions depend on nationality and registration
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual taxable supplies ≥ BHD 37,500; VAT rate 10% (standard); zero-rated and exempt categories exist
- **Key limits:** No income tax regardless of income level or structure

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Golden Visa (introduced 2022)

- **Eligibility:** Various categories:
  - Retired individuals (not residing in Bahrain) earning ≥ BHD 4,000/month basic salary
  - Property owners in Bahrain with total value ≥ BHD 200,000 (at time of purchase)
  - Talented individuals nominated by a government agency
  - Employees in Bahrain ≥ 5 years with average salary ≥ BHD 2,000/month in past 5 years
  - Retirees who worked in government/private sector in Bahrain ≥ 15 years with average pension > BHD 2,000/month in last 5 years
- **Benefit:** 10-year residency; allows sponsoring dependants
- **Tax benefit:** Since there is no income tax, the main benefit is long-term residency security (EY p.115)

### Self-Sponsored Residency (Long-Term Visa)

- **Eligibility:** Various categories:
  - Foreign investors owning a company in Bahrain, receiving monthly income ≥ BHD 500 and fixed deposit ≥ BHD 15,000
  - Retirees from GCC government/private sector (≥ 15 years service), monthly income ≥ BHD 500, fixed deposit ≥ BHD 5,000
  - Property owners in Bahrain worth ≥ BHD 50,000 with regular monthly income ≥ BHD 500
- **Benefit:** 2-, 5-, or 10-year residency; allows sponsoring dependants (EY p.115)

## 2026 Changes

- **Employer social insurance contribution increase (Bahraini nationals):** Employer contribution (currently 17%) increases by 1% each January until reaching 20% in 2028. (EY p.112)
- **End-of-service benefit system (from 1 March 2024):** Monthly SIO portal payments replaced accrual system for non-Bahraini national employees. (EY p.113)
- No personal income tax changes — Bahrain does not levy personal income tax.

## Effective Rate Examples

> Bahrain has NO personal income tax. The only mandatory individual burden is social insurance.
> **1 EUR ≈ BHD 0.41** (BHD pegged at BHD 0.376/USD; using EUR/USD ~1.08 → BHD 0.406/EUR approximately).
> Non-GCC expatriate scenario: employee pays 1% social insurance; capped at BHD 4,000/month base.
> Bahraini national scenario: employee pays 8% social insurance; capped at BHD 4,000/month.

| Gross income (BHD) | EUR equiv | Scenario | Income tax | Social insurance (employee) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| BHD 12,180 | €30,000 | Employment — non-GCC expat (1%) | BHD 0 | BHD 122 (1% × 12,180) | BHD 122 | **1.0%** |
| BHD 12,180 | €30,000 | Employment — Bahraini national (8%) | BHD 0 | BHD 974 (8% × 12,180) | BHD 974 | **8.0%** |
| BHD 12,180 | €30,000 | B2B (self-employed) | BHD 0 | BHD 0–122 (varies) | BHD 0–122 | **0%–1.0%** |
| BHD 24,360 | €60,000 | Employment — non-GCC expat (1%) | BHD 0 | BHD 480 (capped at BHD 4,000/month base) | BHD 480 | **2.0%** |
| BHD 24,360 | €60,000 | Employment — Bahraini national (8%) | BHD 0 | BHD 3,840 (capped) | BHD 3,840 | **15.8%** |
| BHD 40,600 | €100,000 | Employment — non-GCC expat (1%) | BHD 0 | BHD 480 (capped) | BHD 480 | **1.2%** |
| BHD 40,600 | €100,000 | Employment — Bahraini national (8%) | BHD 0 | BHD 3,840 (capped) | BHD 3,840 | **9.5%** |

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/BHD = 0.406 (BHD pegged at 0.376/USD; EUR/USD ~1.08)
- Non-GCC expatriate: 1% employee unemployment contribution; capped at BHD 4,000/month (BHD 48,000/year)
- Bahraini national: 8% employee contribution (1% unemployment + 7% pension/disability/death); capped at BHD 4,000/month (BHD 48,000/year → max employee contribution BHD 3,840/year)
- B2B: self-employed social insurance obligations vary by nationality and registration; employer end-of-service contributions not included in employee burden
- GCC non-Bahraini nationals subject to home country SS — not modelled here
- End-of-service benefit contributions (4.2%/8.4%) are paid by employer to SIO, not deducted from employee salary
