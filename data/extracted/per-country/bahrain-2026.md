## Personal Income Tax

> Bahrain imposes no personal income tax on individuals. There is no income tax, capital gains tax, or wealth tax for individuals. The only direct levy on individuals is the social security contribution (GOSI/SIO).

| Taxable income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All income | 0% |

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- None applicable — no PIT exists

**Notes:**
- Bahraini and GCC nationals: GOSI contributions apply (7% employee, 12% employer)
- Expatriates (non-GCC): SIO levy 1% employee + 3% employer (unemployment scheme)
- No personal tax return filing obligation for individuals (EY p. 112)

## Social Security

> Bahrain operates the General Organisation for Social Insurance (GOSI) for Bahraini/GCC nationals and the Social Insurance Organisation (SIO) for expatriates.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| GOSI — Bahraini/GCC nationals | 7% | 12% | N/A |
| SIO — expatriates | 1% | 3% | N/A |
| **Total (Bahraini/GCC)** | **7%** | **12%** | **—** |
| **Total (expatriates)** | **1%** | **3%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** Not specified in EY source (p. 113)
**Notes:**
- Expatriate SIO levy funds an involuntary unemployment insurance programme
- Self-employed Bahraini nationals may register voluntarily with GOSI

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Bahrain has no personal income tax, so self-employment income bears no income tax. Key considerations are VAT registration and Commercial Registration.

### Option A — Sole Proprietorship (Commercial Registration)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual conducting business activity in Bahrain
- **Tax base:** No income tax; VAT applies above BHD 37,500 annual turnover
- **Rate / mechanism:** 0% income tax on all profits
- **Social security:** GOSI if Bahraini/GCC national; SIO levy not applicable to self-employed expatriates
- **VAT obligations:** Mandatory VAT registration threshold BHD 37,500; standard VAT rate 10%
- **Key limits:** Must obtain Commercial Registration (CR) from Ministry of Industry, Commerce and Tourism

### Option B — Free Zone Company
- **Who qualifies:** Businesses in designated free zones (e.g., Bahrain International Investment Park)
- **Tax base:** 0% income tax; customs duty exemptions
- **Rate / mechanism:** Full tax exemption with no time limit
- **Social security:** GOSI rules still apply to Bahraini/GCC employees
- **VAT obligations:** Standard VAT rules apply
- **Key limits:** Must meet free zone licensing requirements

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Zero Personal Income Tax — Universal
- **Eligibility:** All residents and workers in Bahrain regardless of nationality
- **Benefit:** Zero PIT on all employment and business income
- **Duration:** Indefinite (no PIT law exists)
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Home-country tax obligations may apply; Bahrain has DTTs with ~40 countries (EY p. 115)

### Golden Residency Visa
- **Eligibility:** Investors, retirees, and skilled professionals meeting financial thresholds
- **Benefit:** 10-year renewable residency without employer sponsorship
- **Duration:** 10 years, renewable
- **Application deadline:** Ongoing
- **Caveats:** Does not create additional tax; Bahrain has no PIT regardless

## 2026 Changes

> No changes to personal income tax for 2026 — Bahrain does not levy PIT. (EY pp. 112–117)

- No changes to GOSI/SIO contribution rates announced for 2026
- VAT rate remains 10% (introduced January 2022)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single person, no dependants.
> Bahrain uses BHD (Bahraini Dinar); 1 BHD ≈ 2.65 EUR (approximate). EUR amounts are approximate conversions.
> Because Bahrain has zero PIT, the only individual burden is the social security contribution.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security (employee) | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment (expatriate) | €0 | €300 (1% SIO) | €300 | **1.0%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | €0 | €0 | €0 | **0.0%** |
| €60,000 | Employment (expatriate) | €0 | €600 (1% SIO) | €600 | **1.0%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | €0 | €0 | €0 | **0.0%** |
| €100,000 | Employment (expatriate) | €0 | €1,000 (1% SIO) | €1,000 | **1.0%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | €0 | €0 | €0 | **0.0%** |

**Step-by-step (€30,000 expatriate employment):**
1. PIT = 0 (no PIT law)
2. SIO employee contribution = €30,000 × 1% = €300
3. Total burden = €300; effective rate = €300 / €30,000 = 1.0%

**Step-by-step (€30,000 Bahraini national employment):**
1. PIT = 0
2. GOSI employee contribution = €30,000 × 7% = €2,100
3. Total burden = €2,100; effective rate = 7.0%

**Assumptions:**
- Expatriate (non-GCC) employee pays 1% SIO; employer pays additional 3%
- Bahraini national employee pays 7% GOSI; effective rate 7.0% at all income levels
- Self-employed non-GCC individuals bear no GOSI/SIO unless voluntarily enrolled
- No income tax, no capital gains tax, no wealth tax
- EUR/BHD ≈ 2.65 (approximate; BHD pegged to USD at 0.376 BHD/USD)
