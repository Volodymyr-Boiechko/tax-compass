## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PAYE system. Tax year = 1 January to 31 December. Employment income withheld monthly by employers under PAYE or the Final Deduction System (FDS). Residents taxed on all Zimbabwean-source income; non-residents also taxed on Zimbabwean-source income. Administered by the Zimbabwe Revenue Authority (ZIMRA).

**Currency context (EY p. 1731):**
- Zimbabwe operates a **dual-currency system** (multi-currency system extended to 31 December 2030)
- **ZWG (Zimbabwe Gold)** = new local currency introduced 5 April 2024; replaced ZWL (Zimbabwe Dollar)
- ZWL → ZWG conversion: ZWG 1 = ZWL 2,498.72 (official conversion rate, 5 April 2024)
- ZWG is now an ISO 4217-registered currency (registered 25 June 2024)
- Tax payable in the currency in which income is received; if foreign currency income exceeds 50% of total income, tax paid equally in USD and ZWG (50/50 split)
- Exchange rate used: RBZ average interbank rate (as of 5 April 2024 when RBZ auction system was discontinued)

**Residency:** No statutory definition in the Income Tax Act; based on facts and circumstances indicating degree of presence. Living and working temporarily = resident but not ordinarily resident; transient visitor = neither resident nor ordinarily resident.

**Non-residents:** Subject to the same progressive rates as residents on Zimbabwean-source employment income; same tax tables apply (EY p. 1732)

### Tax Rates — USD (unchanged from 2024 to 2025 — EY p. 1738)

| Annual taxable income (USD) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 1,200 | 0% |
| 1,201 – 3,600 | 20% |
| 3,601 – 12,000 | 25% |
| 12,001 – 24,000 | 30% |
| 24,001 – 36,000 | 35% |
| Above 36,000 | 40% |

**3% AIDS Levy:** Applied on the cumulative tax due (not on income); effectively adds 3% to the computed income tax. Combined top rate: **40% × 1.03 = 41.2%** — often stated as **"25% + 3% AIDS levy"** or **"40% + 3% AIDS levy"** at the relevant bracket. (EY pp. 1737–1738)

### Tax Rates — ZWG (1 January 2025 – 31 December 2025 — EY p. 1738)

| Annual taxable income (ZWG) | Tax rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 33,600 | 0% |
| 33,601 – 100,800 | 20% |
| 100,801 – 336,000 | 25% |
| 336,001 – 672,000 | 30% |
| 672,001 – 1,008,000 | 35% |
| Above 1,008,000 | 40% |

Plus 3% AIDS Levy on cumulative income tax.

**Standard deductions / allowances (EY pp. 1735–1736):**
- Pension contributions: up to USD 5,400/year (or ZWG equivalent) per employee — tax deductible
- Employee deductible expenses: wholly, exclusively, and necessarily incurred in performance of duties
- Bonuses/performance awards: exempt up to USD 700/year (or ZWG equivalent)
- School fees waived by employer: 50% exempt, up to 3 children
- Severance pay: first USD 3,200 or one-third exempt (from 1 January 2024; prior to 2024: USD 10,000 or ZWL 5M)
- Pension commutation on retrenchment: first USD 1,500 or one-third exempt (max USD 10,000)
- Medical expenses/medical aid contributions paid by employer: exempt
- Age-55+ taxpayer credits (EY p. 1736): USD 900/year tax credit; also first USD 3,000 of bank interest, treasury bill income, or rental income exempt for those aged 55+

**Tax credits (EY p. 1736):**
- Taxpayers over 55 years: USD 900/year (or ZWG equivalent)
- Blind or disabled persons: USD 900/year (or ZWG equivalent)
- Medical aid contributions and medical expenses: 50% tax credit

## Social Security

National Social Security Authority (NSSA) contributions (EY p. 1739):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| NSSA | **4.5%** | **4.5%** | On insurable earnings |
| **Total** | **4.5%** | **4.5%** | |

**NSSA income ceiling (EY p. 1739):**
- Maximum insurable earnings = **USD 700/month** (or ZWG equivalent) — based on previous 3 months' average Total Consumption Poverty Line (TCPL) for a family of five, multiplied by 3.5; effective from 1 June 2024
- Maximum monthly NSSA contribution: 4.5% × USD 700 = **USD 31.50/month** per employee

**Notes:**
- NSSA provides pension, accident/disability benefits
- No separate health insurance or unemployment insurance social contributions for employees
- Wealth tax: 1% on residential properties with value ≥ USD 250,000 (from 1 January 2024); max USD 50,000/year per property; principal private residence exempt (EY p. 1738)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Trade and Investment Income (General Self-Assessment)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual earning income from trade or investment activities (business owners, self-employed professionals, investors)
- **Tax base:** Total income received or accrued less allowable business deductions; capital allowances on fixed assets at 25% over 4 years; wear-and-tear on commercial buildings at 2.5% over 40 years
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive rates 0%–40% + 3% AIDS levy; quarterly provisional payments (10% by March, 35% by June, 65% by September, 100% by December); annual ITF 12C return by 30 April
- **Social security:** NSSA if employees engaged; sole traders without employees may not be enrolled
- **VAT obligations:** Register for VAT if annual taxable turnover exceeds USD 40,000; standard rate 15%
- **Key limits:** Trade and investment losses carried forward up to 6 years; mining losses indefinitely; income from trade/investment not deductible against employment income

### Option B — Presumptive Tax (Informal Sector)

- **Who qualifies (EY p. 1738–1739):** Informal traders, cross-border traders, hairdressers, transport operators (taxis, omnibuses, certain vehicles), driving schools, cottage industry operators, restaurants; **from 1 January 2025, self-employed professionals (architects, engineers, legal practitioners, health practitioners, real estate agents) must file annual self-assessment returns** (EY p. 1739)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed amounts per sector (not detailed in EY personal tax chapter); presumptive tax chargeable if annual return was not submitted for the prior year; credited against final tax on assessment
- **Key limits:** From 1 January 2025, the named professional categories are no longer covered by presumptive tax — they must self-assess

### Option C — Partnership Income

- Partners in a partnership are NOT subject to employee tax; individually taxed on their respective shares of partnership profits at **25% + 3% AIDS levy** = 25.75% (EY p. 1732)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No General Newcomer Exemption

Zimbabwe does not offer a territorial or newcomer-exemption regime. All individuals (residents and non-residents) are taxed on Zimbabwean-source income from the moment they derive such income.

### Non-Resident Treatment

- Non-residents: same progressive rates as residents on Zimbabwean-source employment income
- Technical/managerial/administrative/consulting service fees and royalties to non-residents: **15% withholding** (EY p. 1735); may be reduced under applicable tax treaty
- Non-executive directors: **20% withholding** on gross director fees (final tax — EY p. 1735)
- Freelance insurance agents, brokers, property negotiators: **20% withholding** (credited against annual tax)
- Non-resident artists/entertainers: **15% withholding** (EY p. 1735)

## 2026 Changes

- **ZWG currency now in permanent use** through 31 December 2030 (multi-currency extension announced EY p. 1731)
- **2025 ZWG tax tables** in force (EY p. 1738); 2026 tables expected to be updated for inflation via Finance Act
- **USD tax tables unchanged** from 2024 to 2025 (EY p. 1738); may be updated for 2026
- **Self-employed professionals self-assessment from 1 January 2025:** Architects, engineers, lawyers, doctors, health practitioners, real estate agents — must file annual ITF1 returns (EY p. 1739)
- **NSSA ceiling:** USD 700/month (from 1 June 2024) — in force; may be updated annually based on TCPL
- **Capital gains 4% CGT on ZSE-listed securities:** Legislative situation unclear — 28 June 2024 amendment attempted to repeal but referenced a non-existent provision; "uncertain tax position" as of EY data date (EY p. 1733)
