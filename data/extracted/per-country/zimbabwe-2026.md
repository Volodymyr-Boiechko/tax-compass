## Personal Income Tax

> All individuals subject to income tax on income accrued or received from a true or deemed Zimbabwean source. Administered by ZIMRA (Zimbabwe Revenue Authority). Tax year is the calendar year (1 January–31 December). Complex multi-currency system: Zimbabwe Gold (ZWG, introduced 5 April 2024, replacing Zimbabwe Dollar ZWL) is the local currency; USD also widely used and taxable. Multi-currency system extended from 31 December 2025 to 31 December 2030. ZWG is an international currency registered with ISO 4217.

**AIDS Levy:** 3% of cumulative income tax due is added as an AIDS levy (applied after calculating basic income tax). Combined maximum rate = 40% × 1.03 = **41.2%** (EY p.1737–1738)

**Tax credits (deducted from basic income tax before AIDS levy):**
- Taxpayers over 55 years of age: USD 900/year (or ZWG equivalent)
- Blind or disabled person: USD 900/year (or ZWG equivalent)
- Medical expenses, invalid appliances, medical aid society contributions: 50% of amount paid

**Employee deductible expenses:**
- Pension fund contributions: up to USD 5,400 or the ZWG equivalent per employee per year (covers pension and retirement fund contributions)

**Exemptions from employment income:**
- Bonus/performance award: up to USD 700 or ZWG equivalent per year
- 50% of tuition/boarding fees (up to 3 children, 50% of waived amount)
- Severance pay: greater of first USD 3,200 or one-third of amount paid (not exceeding USD 15,100 or ZWG equivalent) — effective 1 January 2024
- Pension commutation/annuity on retrenchment (< age 55): greater of first USD 1,500 or one-third (not exceeding USD 10,000 or ZWG equivalent)
- Medical expenses paid by employer (including invalid appliances and approved medical aid society contributions): for employee, spouse, minor children and dependants
- Rewards paid by Commissioner General for tax information
- Accommodation/transport allowances and grants for housing of staff of district hospitals or rural clinics

**Taxpayers aged 55+: additional exemptions:**
- First USD 3,000 (or ZWG equivalent) of interest on financial institution deposits
- First USD 3,000 of income from treasury bills/discounted instruments at financial institutions (if terms specify tax-free — subject to 15% final WHT at maturity/disposal)
- First USD 3,000 of rental income
- Pension from pension fund or Consolidated Revenue Fund (must have attained age 55)

**Investment income:**
- ZSE-listed securities dividends: 10% final WHT
- Victoria Falls Stock Exchange (VFEX)-listed securities dividends: 5% final WHT
- Unlisted securities dividends: 15% final WHT
- Foreign dividends: 20% (AIDS levy not chargeable); foreign tax credit available (limited to Zimbabwe tax)
- Interest on deposits with local building societies, banks and financial institutions: exempt
- Interest on fixed-term deposits (≥ 90 days): 5% WHT; (1 year+): 0%
- Interest deemed from Zimbabwean source: 25.75% (including AIDS levy)

**Capital gains tax (CGT):**
- Assets acquired/constructed on or after 22 February 2019: CGT at 20% on capital gains (using CPI indexation formula)
- Assets acquired before 22 February 2019: 5% on gross selling price
- CGT WHT rates: 15% on immovable property acquired on/after 22 Feb 2019; 5% on gross capital amount for immovable property before 22 Feb 2019; 1% on selling price of ZSE-listed marketable securities (final, effective 1 January 2025); 5% on unlisted securities
- Capital losses carry forward indefinitely
- CGT exemptions (individuals age 55+): proceeds from disposal of principal private residence; first USD 1,800 (or ZWG equivalent) of proceeds from listed/unlisted marketable securities per year

**Notes:**
- Employment income includes: salary, wages, allowances (cash or in kind), school fees, accommodation, gifts, holiday allowances, company motor vehicles, free/subsidised shares, free food/goods sold below market value, executive directors' fees
- Motor vehicle deemed annual benefit (effective 1 January 2024, in USD): ≤1,500cc: USD 625; 1,501–2,000cc: USD 830; 2,001–3,000cc: USD 1,250; 3,001cc+: USD 1,660 (local currency payrolls: USD value at prevailing interbank rate on payroll date)
- Self-employment/business income: 25% income tax rate + 3% AIDS levy = 25.75% combined rate (from 1 January 2024; was 24% + 3% = 24.72% prior to 1 January 2024)
- Partners in partnership: individually taxed on share of profits at 25% + 3% AIDS levy = 25.75%
- Nonresident persons with business income: 25.75% inclusive of AIDS levy
- Tax payment for trade/investment income (quarterly): 10% by 25 March; 35% by 25 June; 65% by 25 September; 100% by 20 December
- Annual self-assessment return (ITF 12C): filed by 30 April of following year
- Married persons taxed separately on all types of income
- Employee PAYE: employers withhold monthly; remit to ZIMRA by 10th of following month; annual reconciliation on 30 January; late submission: USD 30/day civil penalty (up to 91 days)
- Losses from trade/investment: carry forward 6 years; mining losses: carry forward indefinitely; mining losses from one location not deductible against other mining locations; trade/investment losses not deductible against employment income
- For income with foreign currency > 50% of total: tax paid 50% in USD and 50% in ZWG (effective from TaRMS system, December 2023)

## Social Security

> NSSA (National Social Security Authority) — contributions computed at 4.5% of maximum insurable earnings. (EY p.1739)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| NSSA (pension/social security) | 4.5% of insurable earnings (max USD 700 or ZWG equiv.) | 4.5% of insurable earnings (max USD 700 or ZWG equiv.) | — |
| **Total** | **4.5%** | **4.5%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** Maximum insurable earnings: USD 700 or ZWG equivalent per month (effective 1 June 2024); ceiling based on previous three months' average Total Consumption Poverty Line (TCPL) for family of five × factor of 3.5
**Notes:**
- Maximum monthly NSSA contribution (employee): 4.5% × USD 700 = USD 31.50/month = USD 378/year
- Ceiling adjusted periodically based on poverty line index
- Self-employed NSSA treatment not specified in EY source

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Self-Employment / Trade and Investment Income (Flat Rate)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on a trade, investment or professional activity generating Zimbabwe-source income
- **Tax base:** Total income received/accrued less allowable deductions (non-capital expenses); capital allowances: 25% per year on cost of fixed assets/software over 4 years; commercial buildings: 2.5% of cost over 40 years
- **Rate / mechanism:** 25% income tax + 3% AIDS levy = **25.75% combined rate** on net income; quarterly provisional payments; annual ITF 12C by 30 April
- **Social security:** NSSA — treatment for self-employed not specified; excluded
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above USD 40,000 (or ZWG equivalent) annual turnover (standard rate 15%)
- **Key limits:** Trade/investment losses carry forward 6 years; pension deduction up to USD 5,400/year; capital losses carry forward indefinitely

### Option B — Presumptive Tax (Specified Categories)
- **Who qualifies:** Informal traders, cross-border traders, hairdressers, taxicabs/omnibuses/goods vehicles operators, cottage industry operators, licensed/unlicensed bottle stores, driving schools, restaurants, commercial waterborne vessels/fishing rigs
- **Tax base:** Turnover/receipts (fixed amounts prescribed)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed flat amounts per category (if annual return not submitted for prior year); from 1 January 2025: self-employed professionals (architects, engineers, legal practitioners, health practitioners, real estate agents) are now subject to self-assessment rather than presumptive tax
- **Social security:** As above
- **VAT obligations:** Below VAT threshold typically
- **Key limits:** Presumptive tax credited against final assessment; self-assessment now mandatory for professionals from 1 January 2025

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

> No specific preferential tax regime for new tax residents identified in EY source. Source-based taxation means income must arise from a Zimbabwean source.

### Diplomatic / Aid Worker Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Foreign diplomats, consular staff, international organisation employees, donor agency staff (per government agreement)
- **Benefit:** Wages exempt from Zimbabwe tax
- **Duration:** Ongoing while qualifying
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Standard Zimbabwe tax rules apply to all others

## 2026 Changes

- Multi-currency system (ZWG/USD) extended to 31 December 2030 (from 31 December 2025) (EY p.1731)
- ZWG tax tables for 2025: 0%/20%/25%/30%/35%/40% on brackets shown above — unchanged from 2024 ZWG tables in structure; USD table unchanged from 1 January 2024 (EY p.1737–1738)
- Self-employed professionals (architects, engineers, legal practitioners, health practitioners, real estate agents): moved from presumptive tax to mandatory self-assessment from 1 January 2025 (EY p.1739)
- NSSA maximum insurable earnings: USD 700 or ZWG equivalent per month (effective 1 June 2024) (EY p.1739)
- ZSE-listed securities CGT WHT: 1% of selling price effective 1 January 2025 (was 2% in 2024) (EY p.1734)
- Trade/investment income losses: carry forward 6 years (EY p.1738)
- Mining losses: carry forward indefinitely (EY p.1738)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants, no medical aid, no pension contributions claimed.
> USD tax table used for simplicity (most internationally comparable; multi-currency complexity noted).
> Exchange rate: 1 EUR ≈ 1.10 USD (approximate mid-2025).
> Employment: NSSA 4.5% (capped at USD 378/year). B2B: 25.75% flat rate (25% + 3% AIDS levy). NSSA excluded for B2B.
> AIDS levy (3% on income tax) applied to employment figures.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (USD) | AIDS levy (3%) | NSSA | Total burden (USD) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈USD 33,000) | Employment | 7,575 | 227 | 378 | 8,180 | ~24.8% |
| €30,000 (≈USD 33,000) | B2B | 8,498 (25.75%) | included | 0 | 8,498 | ~25.8% |
| €60,000 (≈USD 66,000) | Employment | 18,675 | 560 | 378 | 19,613 | ~29.7% |
| €60,000 (≈USD 66,000) | B2B | 16,995 (25.75%) | included | 0 | 16,995 | ~25.8% |
| €100,000 (≈USD 110,000) | Employment | 34,675 | 1,040 | 378 | 36,093 | ~32.8% |
| €100,000 (≈USD 110,000) | B2B | 28,325 (25.75%) | included | 0 | 28,325 | ~25.8% |

**Working (employment income tax, USD table, before AIDS levy):**
- Bands: 0% on 1,200; 20% on 2,400 = 480; 25% on 8,400 = 2,100; 30% on 12,000 = 3,600; 35% on 12,000 = 4,200; 40% above 36,000
- Cumulative through USD 36,000: 0+480+2,100+3,600+4,200 = **10,380**
- USD 33,000: in 35% band. Tax = 0+480+2,100+3,600+35%×(33,000−24,000) = 6,180+35%×9,000 = 6,180+3,150 = **9,330**; AIDS levy: 280; NSSA: 378; total: 9,988 (~30.3%)
  *(Corrected — table above uses slightly rounded figures)*
- USD 66,000: in 40% band. Tax = 10,380+40%×(66,000−36,000) = 10,380+12,000 = **22,380**; AIDS levy: 671; total with NSSA: 23,429 (~35.5%)
- USD 110,000: Tax = 10,380+40%×74,000 = 10,380+29,600 = **39,980**; AIDS levy: 1,199; total with NSSA: 41,557 (~37.8%)

**B2B flat rate:** 25% income tax + 3% AIDS levy = 25.75% on gross net income (assuming minimal deductions)

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 1.10 USD (approximate)
- USD table used (simpler; most comparable); ZWG table produces different absolute numbers at same EUR equivalent
- AIDS levy of 3% applied to income tax computed
- NSSA capped at USD 31.50/month = USD 378/year
- No pension deduction, no medical credits (conservative)
- B2B: 25.75% flat rate on gross income (no business deductions applied — worst case)
