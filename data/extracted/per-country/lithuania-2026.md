## Personal Income Tax

> Lithuania has a progressive PIT system effective from 1 January 2026, based on aggregate annual income. AMS (Average Monthly Salary) is the key threshold reference: AMS for 2026 = EUR 2,312.15/month. Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Lithuanian-source income.

**PIT rates from 1 January 2026 (aggregate annual income basis):**

| Taxable income (EUR/year) | Rate % |
|---|---|
| Up to 36 AMS (≈ EUR 83,237) | 20 |
| 36 AMS – 60 AMS (≈ EUR 83,237 – 138,729) | 25 |
| Above 60 AMS (above ≈ EUR 138,729) | 32 |

*AMS for 2026 = EUR 2,312.15/month (not yet adopted at time of EY review); AMS for 2025 = EUR 2,108.88/month. (EY p. 880, 884)*

**From 1 January 2026, the following income types are taxed on aggregate annual income basis:**
- Employment income
- Self-employment income
- Directors' fees / management board remuneration
- Royalties
- Management fees (non-member partners)
- Certain disposals of waste (above 12 AMS)
- Annual self-employment income under business certificate exceeding EUR 50,000
- Non-employment income exceeding 12 AMS (excluding income taxed at flat 15%)

**Other income tax rates (2026):**
- Dividends: 15% flat (excluded from aggregate annual income)
- Non-employment income up to 12 AMS: 15%
- Capital gains on shares held >5 years (not via investment account): 15% flat (excluded from aggregate)
- Capital gains on shares acquired under employer option and sold ≥3 years after right to acquire: 15% flat
- Investment account: withdrawals taxed at 15% up to 12 AMS; above 12 AMS subject to aggregate rates from 2026
- Waste disposal income up to 12 AMS: 5%
- Self-employment income up to EUR 20,000: 5%
- Self-employment income EUR 20,001–EUR 42,500 (2026 threshold): progressive rate formula up to 20%
- Self-employment income above EUR 42,500: aggregate annual income rates apply (from 2026)

**Non-taxable minimum:**
- 2025: up to EUR 8,964/year if annual income ≤ 12 monthly minimum wages; phased out at higher incomes
- 2026: formula-based, determined by Lithuanian legislation (EY p. 883)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- General non-taxable minimum (income-dependent)
- Life insurance premiums and pension contributions: max EUR 1,500/year (transitional 10-year period from January 2025 — only for contracts concluded before 31 December 2024)
- Vocational training/education expenses: deductible (including spouse/children); tuition only if loan obtained, amount = loan repayment in year
- Maximum total deductions: 25% of taxable income

**Notes:**
- Russia–Lithuania DTT denounced as of 1 January 2026 (EY p. 889)
- Inheritance tax: 5% if taxable value <EUR 150,000; 10% if ≥EUR 150,000; close relatives (children, parents, spouses) exempt
- Real estate tax: progressive for non-primary residences from 1 January 2026; 0.2–1% on value above EUR 50,000; additional 0.2% for commercial properties from 2026 (National Defense Fund)

## Social Security

> Lithuania has a split social insurance system. The employee pays the majority. From 1 January 2024: employee pays 12.52% (or 15.52% with pension accumulation); employer pays 1.45% (regular contract) to 2.17% (fixed-term). Health insurance: 6.98% of gross salary (employee). Ceiling: 60 AMS/year.

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| State social insurance (SoDra) | 12.52% (or 15.52% with pension accumulation) | 1.45%–2.17% | 12.52% on 90% of income (capped at 43 AMS) |
| Mandatory health insurance (PSD) | 6.98% | — | 6.98% on minimum monthly salary base |
| Guarantee Fund | — | 0.16% | — |
| Long-term Employee Benefit Fund | — | 0.16% | — |
| **Total employee** | **~19.5%** | **~1.77%** | **~12.52% + 6.98% = ~19.5%** |

**Income ceiling for social security:** 60 AMS/year ≈ EUR 138,729 (2026); above this, no social insurance contributions (EY p. 886)
**Notes:**
- Employee 12.52%: comprises pension insurance (8.72%), sickness (3.48%), maternity (0.32%); if pension accumulation elected: 15.52% (extra 3% to 2nd pillar pension fund)
- Health insurance 6.98%: deducted from gross salary by employer; not deductible from income for PIT purposes
- Minimum monthly salary for 2025: EUR 1,038; for 2026: EUR 1,153 (EY p. 887)
- Self-employed: 12.52% SS on 90% of income subject to PIT, capped at 43 AMS/year (≈ EUR 99,422/year for 2026); health insurance paid on minimum monthly salary base or full income (6.98%)
- Employer social security (1.45%) ceiling: 60 AMS/year (above ceiling, no employer contribution either)

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals in Lithuania can operate under individual activity (standard PIT rules), business certificate (fixed fee), or via a company. From 2026, self-employment income above EUR 50,000 is taxed on aggregate annual income basis.

### Option A — Individual Activity (Individuali veikla)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registered with the tax authority for self-employment
- **Tax base:** Gross income minus eligible expenses (30% lump-sum deduction of gross income available as alternative to actual costs) — wait, Lithuania does not have a standard 30% expense deduction; costs are deductible if documented. EY states: actual expenses deductible (EY p. 881)
- **Rate / mechanism (2026):** Up to EUR 20,000: 5%; EUR 20,001–EUR 42,500: progressive formula (up to 20%); above EUR 42,500: aggregate annual income rates (20/25/32%)
- **Social security:** 12.52% on 90% of self-employment income subject to PIT; capped at 43 AMS/year; health insurance 6.98%
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above EUR 45,000 annual turnover; standard VAT rate 21%
- **Key limits:** Losses may not be offset against employment income; may carry forward 5 years (EY not specified — general Lithuanian rules)

### Option B — Business Certificate (Verslo liudijimas)
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals engaged in specific activities listed by municipalities; activities include trade, services, crafts
- **Tax base:** Fixed annual or semi-annual fee set by municipality (not based on actual income)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed fee replaces PIT; from 2026, self-employment income above EUR 50,000 under business certificate is taxed on aggregate annual income basis (EY p. 881)
- **Social security:** Minimum social insurance contributions based on minimum monthly salary
- **VAT obligations:** Generally exempt (small activity scale)
- **Key limits:** Only specific activities eligible; income threshold EUR 50,000 above which regular PIT applies from 2026

### Option C — Private Limited Company (UAB)
- **Who qualifies:** Any entrepreneur
- **Tax base:** Company profits at 15% CIT (reduced 0% for micro-companies with ≤10 employees and income ≤EUR 300,000 for 2024); director salary taxed as employment income
- **Rate / mechanism:** Director draws salary (progressive PIT 20/25/32%) + dividends (15% flat)
- **Social security:** Normal employee/employer contributions on salary
- **VAT obligations:** Registration above EUR 45,000 annual turnover
- **Key limits:** Annual filing; dividends at 15%

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Investment Account Regime (from 1 January 2025)
- **Eligibility:** Any Lithuanian tax resident; account designated solely for investments and reported to tax authorities
- **Benefit:** Taxation of investment income deferred until withdrawal from the account; at withdrawal, 15% on amounts up to 12 AMS (~EUR 27,746/year); above 12 AMS: aggregate annual income rates from 2026
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Application deadline:** N/A — applied by designating account as investment account
- **Caveats:** Dividends excluded from investment account (taxed at 15% as received); principal contributions (not gains) may be withdrawn tax-free

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 879–894)

- **Aggregate annual income basis for PIT introduced from 1 January 2026** — employment, self-employment, royalties, directors' fees, and other income consolidated into one progressive scale (20/25/32%)
- **AMS for 2026: EUR 2,312.15/month** (key threshold; not yet adopted at EY review date)
- **New PIT brackets (2026):** 20% up to 36 AMS; 25% on 36–60 AMS; 32% above 60 AMS
- **Self-employment income thresholds updated:** EUR 42,500 (was EUR 35,000) as boundary between progressive formula and aggregate income rates
- **Business certificate threshold: EUR 50,000** — income above this subject to aggregate annual rates
- **New real estate tax schedule from 1 January 2026** for non-primary residences: progressive 0.2%–1% on value above EUR 50,000; additional 0.2% for commercial properties (National Defense Fund)
- **Minimum monthly salary increases to EUR 1,153** for 2026 (from EUR 1,038 in 2025) — affects SS minimum base
- **Russia DTT denounced** — no treaty protection for Russia-sourced income from 1 January 2026
- Capital gains on shares held >5 years: taxed at flat 15% (excluded from aggregate annual income)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, Lithuanian tax resident.
> Lithuania uses EUR. AMS 2026 = EUR 2,312.15/month; 36 AMS = EUR 83,237/year; 12 AMS = EUR 27,746.
> Non-taxable minimum estimated at ~EUR 6,000/year for 2026 (based on 2025 rules and formula).

**Employment at €30,000:**
1. Health insurance 6.98%: €30,000 × 6.98% = €2,094 (not deductible for PIT)
2. SoDra 12.52%: €30,000 × 12.52% = €3,756 (deductible from PIT base? — in Lithuania SS is withheld but not separately deductible from PIT base; income tax is on gross salary less non-taxable minimum)
3. Non-taxable minimum ~€6,000 → PIT base = €30,000 − €6,000 = €24,000
4. PIT: €24,000 × 20% = €4,800
5. Total burden = €4,800 (PIT) + €3,756 (SoDra) + €2,094 (health) = €10,650
6. Effective rate = €10,650 / €30,000 = **35.5%**

**Employment at €60,000 (below 36 AMS = €83,237):**
1. Health insurance: €60,000 × 6.98% = €4,188
2. SoDra: €60,000 × 12.52% = €7,512
3. Non-taxable minimum phased out at higher income; assume ~€3,000 → PIT base = €57,000
4. PIT: €57,000 × 20% = €11,400
5. Total burden = €11,400 + €7,512 + €4,188 = €23,100
6. Effective rate = €23,100 / €60,000 = **38.5%**

**Employment at €100,000 (above 36 AMS = €83,237):**
1. Health insurance: €100,000 × 6.98% = €6,980 (capped at 43 AMS for SS, but health insurance ceiling at 60 AMS)
2. SoDra: capped at 60 AMS = €138,729; €100,000 × 12.52% = €12,520
3. PIT (no non-taxable minimum at this income): €83,237 × 20% = €16,647; (€100,000 − €83,237) × 25% = €4,190 → PIT = €20,837
4. Total burden = €20,837 + €12,520 + €6,980 = €40,337
5. Effective rate = €40,337 / €100,000 = **40.3%**

**B2B (self-employed, individual activity) at €60,000 revenue:**
1. Business costs ~10% = €6,000; net = €54,000
2. Social security: 12.52% on 90% of €54,000 = €48,600 × 12.52% = €6,085; capped at 43 AMS/year (€99,422) — not exceeded
3. Health insurance: 6.98% on self-employment income (minimum base); actual may be lower — assume 6.98% on income: €54,000 × 6.98% = €3,769
4. Self-employment income €42,500+ of €54,000 taxed at aggregate rates: up to €42,500 at progressive formula (effective ~15%); €54,000 − €42,500 = €11,500 × 20% = €2,300; formula portion €42,500 → effective ~12.5% = ~€5,313 → total PIT ~€7,613
5. Total burden = €7,613 + €6,085 + €3,769 = €17,467
6. Effective rate = €17,467 / €60,000 = **~29.1%**

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €4,800 | €5,850 | €10,650 | **35.5%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€3,000 | ~€5,600 | ~€8,600 | **~28.7%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €11,400 | €11,700 | €23,100 | **38.5%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | ~€7,613 | ~€9,854 | ~€17,467 | **~29.1%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €20,837 | €19,500 | €40,337 | **40.3%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€18,000 | ~€16,000 | ~€34,000 | **~34.0%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants, Lithuanian resident
- 2026 rates and AMS (EUR 2,312.15/month) applied; aggregate annual income basis from 2026
- Employee SS: 12.52% SoDra + 6.98% health insurance
- B2B: individual activity; 10% business costs; SS on 90% of taxable income; self-employment thresholds: 5% up to EUR 20,000; formula up to EUR 42,500; 20%+ above
- Non-taxable minimum: ~EUR 6,000 at EUR 30,000 income; phased out at higher incomes
- EUR currency — no conversion needed
