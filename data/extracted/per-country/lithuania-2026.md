## Personal Income Tax

Lithuania levies personal income tax (GPM — Gyventojų pajamų mokestis) on worldwide income for residents. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return due by 1 May of the following year. From 1 January 2026, employment income, self-employment income, directors' fees, royalties (in part) and capital gains (in part) are taxed on an **aggregate annual basis** at the progressive rates below. (EY pp. 879–884)

### Employment income tax rates (from 1 January 2026)

| Taxable income (aggregate) | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to 36 AMS | **20%** |
| 36 AMS – 60 AMS | **25%** |
| Over 60 AMS | **32%** |

- **AMS (Average Monthly Salary)** = average monthly salary set by Lithuanian legislation
- AMS for **2025** = EUR 2,108.88/month (EUR 25,306.56/year) — used for 2025 calculations
- AMS for **2026** = EUR 2,312.15/month (EUR 27,745.80/year) — not yet adopted as of EY data date (EY p. 880)
- 36 AMS (2026) = EUR 83,234; 60 AMS (2026) = EUR 138,729
- Prior to 2026: 20% on income up to 60 AMS; 32% on income exceeding 60 AMS (no 25% band)

**Key 2026 aggregate income change:** From 1 January 2026, the following income streams are taxed on aggregate basis (combined with employment income for progressive rate purposes): employment income, self-employment income, directors' fees/management board remuneration, royalties (exceeding 12 AMS threshold), capital gains (exceeding 12 AMS threshold), and income from small partnerships (non-members). (EY p. 880)

### Other income tax rates

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (from 1 Jan 2026) | **15%** flat (excluded from aggregate) |
| Interest (up to 12 AMS) | **15%** |
| Interest (over 12 AMS, from 2026) | Added to aggregate annual income |
| Royalties (up to 12 AMS) | **15%** |
| Royalties (over 12 AMS, from 2026) | Added to aggregate annual income |
| Capital gains (up to 12 AMS) | **15%** |
| Capital gains (over 12 AMS, from 2026) | Added to aggregate annual income |
| Capital gains from shares held >5 years (not via investment account, from 2026) | **15%** flat (excluded from aggregate) |
| Waste disposal income | **5%** |
| Illness/maternity/paternity allowances | **15%** |

**Self-employment income (from 1 January 2026):** (EY p. 881)
- Up to EUR 20,000: **5%**
- EUR 20,000–EUR 42,500: progressively increasing rate (income tax credit formula, reaching 20%)
- Over EUR 42,500: added to aggregate annual income and taxed at aggregate progressive rates (20%/25%/32%)
- Business certificate holders: income over EUR 50,000 added to aggregate from 2026

**Nontaxable minimum (personal allowance):**
- Annual nontaxable minimum amount: depends on income; maximum EUR 8,964 for 2025 (if annual income ≤ 12 monthly minimum wages)
- Tapers to zero as income increases above 12 monthly minimum wages (EY p. 883)
- Nonresidents may deduct nontaxable minimum from Lithuanian-source income at year-end

**Standard deductions / allowances:** (EY p. 883–884)
- Cumulative life insurance premiums (paid on own and spouse's/minor children's behalf)
- Pension contributions to pension funds (own, spouse's, minor children's)
- Pension contributions to EEA/OECD country pension funds (with conditions)
- Vocational training/study expenses (including tuition for spouse and children, if on graduation basis)
- **Total deductible expenses cap:** 25% of taxable income (taking deductions into account)
- Life insurance and pension contribution deductions: additionally capped at EUR 1,500 per year (transitional rules apply — deductions phased out for contracts entered after 31 December 2024 over 10-year period ending 2034)

**Exempt income (key items):** (EY p. 882)
- Death allowances to spouse, children, parents up to five monthly minimum wages
- State/municipal budget allowances
- Life insurance payments (in certain cases)
- Gains from property sales not requiring legal registration (up to EUR 2,500)
- Income from sale of certain movable/immovable property in Lithuania (conditions apply)
- Securities capital gains up to EUR 500/year (additional criteria apply)
- Stock options exercised ≥ 3 years after grant (if agreement after 1 February 2020)

## Social Security

**Social insurance contributions (from 1 January 2024):** (EY pp. 886–888)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social insurance (pension/sickness/maternity) | **12.52%** | **1.45%** (regular); **2.17%** (fixed-term) | **12.52%** |
| Pension accumulation scheme (optional +) | **+3.0%** if participating | — | **+3.0%** if participating |
| Health insurance | **6.98%** | — (paid by employee) | **6.98%** |
| Guarantee Fund | — | **0.16%** | — |
| Long-term Employee Benefit Fund | — | **0.16%** | — |
| **Total (employee, standard)** | **~19.50%** | **~1.77%** | **~19.50%** |
| **Total (employee, with pension accumulation)** | **~22.50%** | **~1.77%** | **~22.50%** |

**Notes:**
- Employee social insurance: 12.52% (or 15.52% if in pension accumulation scheme) withheld by employer from gross salary (EY p. 886)
- Social insurance contributions are **NOT deductible** in computing employee income tax base (EY p. 886)
- Health insurance 6.98%: withheld by employer from gross salary; also NOT deductible for income tax
- Employer social insurance: 1.45% of gross salary (regular contracts); 2.17% (fixed-term); ceiling: 60 AMS per year for most contributions (excluding employee health insurance and employer's social insurance) (EY p. 886)
- Self-employed: 12.52% levied on 90% of income subject to PIT, but capped at 43 AMS per year (EY p. 887)
- Self-employed health insurance: 6.98% based on minimum monthly salary (not on actual income)
- Minimum monthly salary (2025): EUR 1,038; (2026): EUR 1,153 (EY p. 887)

**Income ceiling:** 60 AMS per year for most employer social insurance contributions (EY p. 886)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Activity (Self-Employment)

- **Who qualifies:** Any resident individual earning self-employment or business income; most common regime for freelancers
- **Tax base:** Gross self-employment income minus documented business expenses (or deemed expenses via standard deduction where permitted)
- **Rate / mechanism (from 2026):**
  - Up to EUR 20,000: **5%** PIT
  - EUR 20,000–EUR 42,500: progressive increase (tax credit formula) reaching 20%
  - Over EUR 42,500: added to aggregate income and taxed at 20%/25%/32% brackets
- **Social security:** 12.52% social insurance on 90% of PIT base (capped at 43 AMS); 6.98% health insurance on minimum monthly salary base
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual turnover exceeds EUR 45,000; standard rate 21%
- **Key limits:** Business losses may offset other income; expenses must be documented

### Option B — Business Certificate (Verslo liudijimas)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals engaged in listed activities (retail trade, certain services) — limited scope; not all activities eligible
- **Tax base / Rate:** Fixed annual fee per municipality (regardless of actual revenue)
- **Social security:** Reduced social insurance based on minimum monthly salary
- **Key limits (from 2026):** Annual income over EUR 50,000 from business certificate activity is added to aggregate income; income over EUR 45,000 during a tax period was previously exempt from standard PIT — now subject to aggregate rules (EY pp. 881, 884)
- **VAT:** Generally exempt; VAT registration required at EUR 45,000 threshold

### Option C — Individual Enterprise (Individuali įmonė)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registering a separate legal entity (individual enterprise)
- **Tax base:** Business profit (after deductible expenses); taxed at corporate income tax rate (15%) at entity level
- **Social security:** Owner pays as self-employed
- **Key limits:** More complex accounting; full legal entity registration required

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Electronic Resident (E-Resident) Status

- Foreign nationals may apply for e-resident status to use Lithuanian administrative and commercial services electronically (EY p. 892)
- E-residency allows access to Lithuanian digital infrastructure but does not automatically confer tax residency
- Status granted for 3 years; renewable

### Inbound Worker / Expat Provisions

- Nonresidents employed by Lithuanian companies: taxed on Lithuanian-source employment income only
- Residents of Lithuania employed by foreign companies (or nonresidents employed by foreign companies to work in Lithuania): subject to Lithuanian income tax on employment income
- EU social security Regulation 883/2004 applies; bilateral agreements with Belarus, Canada, Moldova, Russian Federation, Ukraine, United States (EY pp. 887–888)

### Residence Permits

- **Temporary residence permit** for investors: up to 3 years, if investments meet Law on Investments of the Republic of Lithuania requirements (EY p. 892)
- **EU Blue Card** (highly skilled workers): grants right to work and reside

## 2026 Changes

- **Three-bracket progressive rate (EY p. 884):** From 1 January 2026, employment income taxed at 20% (up to 36 AMS) / 25% (36–60 AMS) / 32% (over 60 AMS). Previously: 20% up to 60 AMS, 32% over 60 AMS.
- **Aggregate income taxation (EY p. 880):** From 1 January 2026, employment income, self-employment income (over EUR 42,500), directors' fees, royalties (over 12 AMS), capital gains (over 12 AMS) and other specified income are taxed on aggregate annual basis using progressive brackets.
- **Self-employment thresholds updated (EY p. 881):** EUR 42,500 threshold for aggregate inclusion (up from EUR 35,000 previously); business certificate aggregate threshold EUR 50,000 (up from EUR 45,000).
- **Dividends (EY p. 881):** From 1 January 2026, dividends taxed at flat 15% and excluded from aggregate annual income calculation.
- **Capital gains — shares held >5 years (EY p. 883):** From 1 January 2026, capital gains from sale of shares not acquired through an investment account, held for more than 5 years, taxed at flat 15% and excluded from aggregate income.
- **AMS for 2026 (EY p. 880):** EUR 2,312.15/month (not yet adopted as of EY data date); key thresholds: 36 AMS ≈ EUR 83,234; 60 AMS ≈ EUR 138,729.
- **Minimum monthly salary 2026 (EY p. 887):** EUR 1,153 (up from EUR 1,038 in 2025).
- **Real estate tax (EY p. 885–886):** From 1 January 2026, real estate owned by individuals (excluding primary residence) taxed at new progressive rates: 0.2% (EUR 50k–200k), 0.4% (EUR 200k–400k), 0.6% (EUR 400k–600k), 0.8% (EUR 600k–1M), 1% (over EUR 1M). Additional 0.2% commercial property tax for National Defense Fund.
- **Investment account concept (EY p. 883):** From 1 January 2025 (already in force), investment accounts allow deferral of tax on qualifying investment income until withdrawal.
- **Life insurance / pension deduction phase-out (EY p. 884):** 10-year transitional period; contracts entered after 31 December 2024 no longer generate deductible expenses.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Lithuania uses EUR. 2026 rates and thresholds applied.
> AMS 2026 = EUR 2,312.15/month; 36 AMS = EUR 83,234; 60 AMS = EUR 138,729.
> Nontaxable minimum assumed EUR 0 (tapers to zero at higher incomes; at €30k it partially applies).
> Employee social security: social insurance 12.52% + health insurance 6.98% = **19.50%** (withheld from gross).
> Self-employed social security: social insurance 12.52% on 90% of income (capped at 43 AMS ≈ EUR 99,423) + health insurance 6.98% on minimum monthly salary basis.

### €30,000 Employment (2026)
36 AMS = EUR 83,234 → entire €30,000 falls in 20% band.
Nontaxable minimum partially applies; conservatively assume EUR 3,000 nontaxable at this income level.

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Nontaxable minimum (est.) | EUR 3,000 | EUR 3,000 |
| Taxable income | 30,000 − 3,000 | EUR 27,000 |
| PIT (20% band) | 20% × 27,000 | EUR 5,400 |
| Social insurance (employee) | 12.52% × 30,000 | EUR 3,756 |
| Health insurance (employee) | 6.98% × 30,000 | EUR 2,094 |
| **Total burden** | 5,400 + 3,756 + 2,094 | **EUR 11,250** |
| **Effective rate** | 11,250 / 30,000 | **37.5%** |

### €30,000 Self-employed (individual activity, 2026)
EUR 20,000 × 5% + EUR 10,000 progressive (avg ~12%) = EUR 1,000 + EUR 1,200 = EUR 2,200 PIT est.
Social insurance: 12.52% × 90% × 30,000 = EUR 3,381; health: 6.98% × EUR 1,153 × 12 = ~EUR 965.

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| PIT (5% on first €20k; ~12% avg on next €10k) | EUR 1,000 + EUR 1,200 | EUR 2,200 |
| Social insurance (12.52% × 90% × 30,000) | EUR 3,381 | EUR 3,381 |
| Health insurance (6.98% × min. salary base EUR 13,836/yr) | EUR 966 | EUR 966 |
| **Total burden** | 2,200 + 3,381 + 966 | **EUR 6,547** |
| **Effective rate** | 6,547 / 30,000 | **21.8%** |

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Nontaxable minimum formula:** Exact calculation for 2026 income levels requires the full Lithuanian formula for the general nontaxable minimum amount; EUR 3,000 at €30k is an approximation.
- [ ] **Self-employment PIT credit formula (EUR 20k–42.5k band):** EY describes a "tax credit formula" for progressively increasing rates — exact formula not fully specified in the guide.
- [ ] **Social insurance ceiling at 60 AMS:** Applies only to certain employer contributions; employee health insurance has no ceiling — needs confirmation for €100k scenario.
- [ ] **AMS 2026 official confirmation:** EUR 2,312.15 stated as not yet adopted as of EY data date; verify at Lithuanian State Social Insurance Fund (Sodra) / VMI (Tax Inspectorate).
- [ ] **Business certificate eligible activities:** Not all self-employment activities qualify; specific list maintained by VMI.
- [ ] **Investment account details:** Full list of qualifying investments for tax deferral not specified in EY guide.
