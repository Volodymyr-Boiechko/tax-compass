## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (Einkommensteuer) for residents taxed on worldwide income. Tax year = calendar year. Filing deadline: end of April (paper); end of June (electronic); extension possible if filed by tax advisor. Married persons taxed separately on all types of income. (EY p.97)

Austria distinguishes income categories: agriculture/forestry, dependent employment, self-employment (including directors' fees), business, investment, rental, and other. (EY p.91)

**Einkommensteuer brackets — 2026** (EY p.94, explicitly stated as rates for 2026)

| Taxable income (EUR) | Rate | Tax due (EUR) | Cumulative tax due (EUR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 13,539 | 0% | 0.00 | 0.00 |
| Next 8,453 (13,539–21,992) | 20% | 1,690.60 | 1,690.60 |
| Next 14,466 (21,992–36,458) | 30% | 4,339.80 | 6,030.40 |
| Next 33,907 (36,458–70,365) | 40% | 13,562.80 | 19,593.20 |
| Next 34,494 (70,365–104,859) | 48% | 16,557.12 | 36,150.32 |
| Next 895,141 (104,859–1,000,000) | 50% | 447,570.50 | 483,720.82 |
| Above 1,000,000 | 55%* | — | — |

*55% rate applies for 2016 through 2029. (EY p.94)

**Non-regular payments (13th/14th month salary, vacation/Christmas bonus):** Taxed at special reduced rates (Sonderzahlungen), provided they do not exceed 1/6 of regular payments: (EY p.94)

| Amount of non-regular payment | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to EUR 620 | 0% |
| Next EUR 24,380 | 6% |
| Next EUR 25,000 | 27% |
| Next EUR 33,333 | 35.75% |
| Over EUR 33,333 | Progressive ordinary rate |

If 1/6 of regular payments ≤ EUR 2,570, non-regular payments are fully tax-free. This is a significant benefit for Austrian employees receiving 14-salary packages.

**Investment income:** Final withholding tax 27.5% on dividends, capital gains from securities (from 1 April 2012); 25% on savings deposits and current account interest from Austrian sources. (EY p.91)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Standard employment deduction (Werbungskostenpauschale): EUR 132/year (EY p.94)
- Employee social security contributions are deductible if required by law (EY p.94)
- Special expenses: church taxes, tax consulting fees, donations to qualifying organizations (EY p.94)
- Business deductions: all actual professional expenses for self-employed / business income

**Capital gains:**
- Sale of businesses, partnership interests: taxed as ordinary income; may be distributed over 3 years if held ≥7 years; half-rate if sold due to owner retirement after ≥7 years; gains >EUR 7,300 fully taxed in current year (EY p.93)
- Sale of shares in corporation: 27.5% (EY p.93)
- Sale of real estate: 30% (tax base may differ from market value depending on acquisition date) (EY p.93)
- Other privately held assets held >1 year: not taxable (EY p.93)

**Notes:**
- Married persons file separately (no joint return option) (EY p.97)
- Loss from one source may generally offset profit from another; unlimited carryforward for commercial/business/self-employment activities (EY p.94–95)

## Social Security

Five pillars: old-age pension, accident, health, unemployment, and insolvency guarantee. Contributions for 2026 explicitly stated in EY. (EY p.95–96)

**Employee and employer contributions — 2026** (EY p.96)

| Contribution | Employee % | Employer % | Total % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension insurance | 10.25% | 12.55% | 22.80% |
| Accident insurance | 0.00% | 1.10% | 1.10% |
| Health insurance | 3.87% | 3.78% | 7.65% |
| Unemployment insurance | 2.95% | 2.95% | 5.90% |
| Accommodation promotion | 0.50% | 0.50% | 1.00% |
| Chamber contribution | 0.50% | 0.00% | 0.50% |
| Insolvency guarantee fund | 0.00% | 0.10% | 0.10% |
| **Total** | **18.07%** | **20.98%** | **39.05%** |

**Additional employer-only contributions** (EY p.96):
- Severance pay fund (Mitarbeitervorsorge — "Abfertigung Neu"): 1.53% of salary, no ceiling
- Family burden equalization fund (FLAF): 3.0%, no ceiling
- Community tax (Kommunalsteuer): 3.0%, no ceiling
- Chamber of commerce contribution: 0.31%–0.40%, depending on federal state

**Wage base ceiling (Höchstbeitragsgrundlage) — 2026:** (EY p.96)
- Regular monthly salary: EUR 6,930/month
- 13th and 14th month (non-regular) payments: EUR 13,860 per payment
- Maximum annual employee SS contribution: EUR 17,392.91
- Maximum annual employer SS contribution: EUR 20,285.50

**Self-employed — SVS (Sozialversicherungsanstalt der Selbständigen):**
The SVS (formerly SVA) covers all self-employed persons under the Gewerbliche Sozialversicherungsgesetz (GSVG). Contributions approximately **26.83% of net income** (income from tax assessment, reduced by SS contributions themselves):
- Pension: ~18.50% of contribution base
- Health: ~7.65% of contribution base
- Accident: ~1.53% (flat, included)
- Unemployment: not covered for self-employed
- Contribution base = net income from tax assessment (after business expenses, before SS deduction); SS is deductible from taxable income

**SVS minimum and maximum contribution base (2025/2026 approximate):**
- Minimum contribution base (Mindestbeitragsgrundlage): ~EUR 6,221/year (2025); contributions ~EUR 1,668/year minimum
- Maximum contribution base: EUR 83,160/year (2025)
- New self-employed in first 3 years: provisional contributions at minimum base; adjustment after tax assessment

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Neue Selbständige (New Self-Employed — § 2 Abs. 1 Z. 4 GSVG)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals who earn income from self-employment (freelance work, knowledge-based services, IT, consulting, journalism, arts) WITHOUT a trade license (Gewerbeschein). Typically applies to creative, scientific, intellectual, or personal service activities. Taxed under § 22 EStG (income from self-employment). (bmf.gv.at; svs.at)
- **Tax base:** Net profit = revenue minus all deductible professional expenses. Cash-basis (Einnahmen-Ausgaben-Rechnung) permitted if annual revenue ≤ EUR 700,000. All reasonable business expenses deductible.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Net profit taxed at progressive Einkommensteuer rates (0–55%). Tax-free threshold EUR 13,539 applies.
- **Social security (SVS/GSVG):** ~26.83% of contribution base (health ~7.65% + pension ~18.50% + accident ~0.68%). Contribution base = net income from tax assessment. SS contributions deductible from taxable income. No unemployment coverage.
- **VAT obligations:** Kleinunternehmer exemption (§ 6 Abs. 1 Z. 27 UStG): VAT-exempt if annual revenue ≤ EUR 35,000 net (2025). Above EUR 35,000: must charge VAT at 20% (standard) or 10% (reduced). Can voluntarily register for VAT from EUR 1 (beneficial for B2B with input tax recovery).
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; complex year-end adjustment of SS contributions after tax assessment; professional indemnity insurance recommended but not mandatory.

### Option B — Gewerbetreibende (Trade Business — § 23 EStG)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals performing commercial/trade activities requiring a trade license (Gewerbeschein). Taxed under § 23 EStG (business income).
- **Tax base:** Same as Neue Selbständige (net profit).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive Einkommensteuer rates. Plus Gewerbesteuer: Austria abolished the federal trade tax in 1994; only municipal taxes apply (Kommunalsteuer 3% on payroll — relevant only for employees). No trade tax equivalent for sole trader income in Austria.
- **Social security:** Same SVS contributions as Neue Selbständige (~26.83%).
- **VAT:** Same Kleinunternehmer threshold EUR 35,000.
- **Key limits:** Gewerbeschein (trade license) required; slightly more administrative overhead. Key difference from Neue Selbständige: activity classification, not tax burden.

### Option C — GmbH (Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung)

- **Who qualifies:** Any person wishing to incorporate; minimum capital EUR 35,000 (EUR 17,500 paid-in at formation); simplified "GmbH light" option (EUR 10,000 capital) available.
- **Tax base:** Corporate income tax (Körperschaftsteuer — KöSt): 23% flat rate (reduced from 25% in 2024). Municipal business tax (Kommunalsteuer 3%) on salaries.
- **Self-employed managing director:** If sole shareholder and managing director, treated as self-employed for SVS (GSVG) purposes — no statutory employee SS. Managing director salary drawn from GmbH is deductible for GmbH, taxable for director at progressive rates. Dividends: 27.5% KESt (capital gains/dividend withholding tax).
- **Key benefit:** KöSt 23% on retained profits is lower than top progressive rate 48–55%; useful for income smoothing.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Expatriate Regime (Zuzugsbegünstigung)

- **Eligibility:** Employee assigned by foreign employer to Austrian employer (subsidiary or permanent establishment); must not have had Austrian residence in the preceding 10 years; Austrian assignment ≤5 years (contractual extension option after 2016 reform for up to additional 5 years). (EY p.95)
- **Benefit:** Lump-sum deduction of 20% of tax base (up to EUR 10,000/year per employee) from the monthly payroll tax calculation, in lieu of deductions for double housing, home leave, and extraordinary expenses. (EY p.95)
- **Duration:** Up to 5 years (contractual option for prolongation).
- **Conditions:** Employee must maintain primary residence abroad; assignment must not exceed 5 years. (EY p.95)
- **Caveats:** Optional — if employer deducts expenses as lump sum, employee may still file individual tax return voluntarily if actual deductible expenses exceed the lump sum. (EY p.95)

### Zuzugsfreibetrag (Immigration Tax Credit)

Austria has no NHR-style flat-rate regime. The main benefit for inbound workers is the expatriate lump-sum deduction above. No separate income exemption for foreign-source income exists (unlike NHR or impatriation regimes in other EU countries).

## 2026 Changes

- **PIT brackets for 2026 confirmed:** EY explicitly states the bracket table is "for 2026" (EY p.94). Tax-free threshold raised to EUR 13,539 (from prior-year levels); key change reflects indexation.
- **Social security rates 2026:** Explicitly stated in EY p.96: employee total 18.07%, employer total 20.98%. Wage base ceiling EUR 6,930/month.
- **Accommodation promotion contribution:** EY notes (p.96 footnote) "A change in the amount of the accommodation promotion contribution may be introduced from 2026" — monitor svs.at for updates.
- **KöSt reduction:** Corporate income tax reduced to 23% (from 25%) effective 2024; remains in force for 2026.
- **Kleinunternehmer VAT threshold:** EUR 35,000 for Austria (above EU minimum EUR 25,000 set by Directive); no announced change for 2026.
- **55% top rate:** Explicitly noted as applicable "for 2016 through 2029" — no change expected before 2030. (EY p.94)
- **SVS contribution rates:** ~26.83% for Neue Selbständige remains in force; annual indexation of minimum/maximum bases (check svs.at for 2026 values).

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only. All figures in EUR (Eurozone).
> "Employment" = gross salary; employee SS 18.07% included; employer 20.98% not shown.
> "Neue Selbständige (NS)" = gross revenue; SVS ~26.83%; 15% business expenses assumed.
> 13th/14th month salary benefit NOT modeled (employment effective rate would be lower if it applies).

### EUR 30,000 — Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (18.07%, capped at EUR 6,930/month → not triggered at 30k) | 18.07% × 30,000 | EUR 5,421 |
| PIT base | 30,000 − 5,421 − 132 (Werbungspauschale) | EUR 24,447 |
| PIT | 0–13,539: 0; 13,539–21,992: 20% × 8,453 = 1,691; 21,992–24,447: 30% × 2,455 = 737 | EUR 2,428 |
