## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system (ISR — Impuesto Sobre la Renta) for resident individuals taxed on worldwide income. Tax year = calendar year. Annual return filed in April; deadline 30 April of the following year. Married persons taxed separately. (EY p.998–1006)

**Standard deductions / allowances (personal deductions for residents):** (EY p.1003)
- Medical, dental, hospital and psychology fees (paid to licensed professionals, by check/card/electronic transfer)
- Funeral expenses up to approximately MXN 41,273 (= 1 annual UMA)
- Charitable donations up to 7% of prior-year taxable income
- Real interest on mortgage for primary residence (principal ≤ 750,000 UDIs ≈ MXN 6,255,682)
- Voluntary retirement account contributions: up to 5 × annual UMA ≈ MXN 206,367; not exceeding 10% of annual income
- Health insurance premiums paid to Mexican insurers
- School transportation and tuition fees (private schools up to MXN 14,200–24,500 per level)
- Total personal deductions capped at lower of 5 × annual UMA (≈ MXN 206,367) or 15% of total income

**Employment subsidy (subsidio al empleo):** Tax credit for employees earning ≤ MXN 10,171/month; maximum MXN 475/month. (EY p.1004)

**Notes:**
- No general zero-rate personal allowance (progressive starts at 1.92% from first peso)
- Dividend income from Mexican companies: additional 10% withholding on top of corporate tax (final) (EY p.1000)
- Capital gains from shares on Mexican stock exchanges: 10% final tax (EY p.1002)
- Primary residence sale: exempt up to 700,000 UDIs (≈ MXN 5,838,636) if certain conditions met (EY p.1002)
- No federal estate or inheritance tax; local real estate property tax only (EY p.1005)

## Social Security

**IMSS (Instituto Mexicano del Seguro Social)** contributions calculated on Salary Base of Contribution (SBC). SBC capped at 25 × daily UMA (2026 daily UMA = MXN 113.14 → annual SBC cap ≈ MXN 1,032,528). (EY p.1005–1006)

| Contribution branch | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Enfermedades y Maternidad — fixed quota (up to 3 UMAs daily) | — | ~20.40% on amount up to 3 UMAs |
| Enfermedades y Maternidad — excess above 3 UMAs | 0.40% | 1.10% |
| Prestaciones en dinero (cash sickness benefits) | 0.25% | 0.70% |
| Gastos médicos pensionados (pensioner medical) | 0.375% | 1.05% |
| Invalidez y Vida (disability and life) | 0.625% | 1.75% |
| Cesantía en Edad Avanzada y Vejez — CEAV (old age/retirement) | 1.125% | 3.150% |
| Riesgos de Trabajo (work injury — variable by sector) | — | 0.50–15.00% |
| INFONAVIT (housing fund) | — | 5.00% |
| Retiro (mandatory pension savings to AFORE) | — | 2.00% |
| **Approximate employee total (above 3 UMAs)** | **~2.775%** | **~30–40%** |

**Notes:**
- EY states: employee maximum annual contribution ≈ MXN 28,154; employer maximum ≈ MXN 209,057 (at SBC cap) (EY p.1005–1006)
- Total employer rate is complex (variable risk + fixed quota + percentage tiers); the 2.775% for employees is the standard figure for formal workers above 3 UMAs daily threshold
- IMSS is mandatory only for employed workers (patrones must register employees); self-employed have no mandatory IMSS obligation
- Profit sharing (PTU): employers must distribute 10% of pre-tax profits to employees annually (Labor Law obligation)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — RESICO-PF (Régimen Simplificado de Confianza — Personas Físicas)

- **Who qualifies:** Resident individuals with actividades empresariales or servicios profesionales (honorarios) income; annual revenue ≤ MXN 3,500,000 (~€175,000). Cannot be used if taxpayer is also a shareholder-employee of the paying entity. (Art. 113-E LISR; SAT)
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue actually collected in the period — NO expense deductions.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Fixed rate applied to gross revenue by bracket. Annual brackets confirmed in RMF 2026 Annex 8 (DOF 28/12/2025):

  | Annual income — Exceeding (MXN) | Not exceeding (MXN) | Rate (%) |
  |---|---|---|
  | 0 | 300,000 | 1.00 |
  | 300,000 | 600,000 | 1.10 |
  | 600,000 | 1,000,000 | 1.50 |
  | 1,000,000 | 2,500,000 | 2.00 |
  | 2,500,000 | 3,500,000 | 2.50 |

  Monthly provisional payments based on the month's income bracket; annual return reconciles total. If annual income crosses a bracket, the higher rate applies to amounts in that bracket (not retroactively to all income).

- **Social security:** IMSS not mandatory. Voluntary enrollment available ("Seguro Voluntario"). No mandatory social contribution; this is the largest cost advantage vs. employment.
- **VAT obligations:** Must collect and remit IVA (16% standard rate) on services; issue electronic CFDIs. Separate from ISR; neutral if invoicing IVA-registered clients.
- **Key limits:** MXN 3,500,000 annual revenue ceiling; if exceeded, must exit RESICO-PF and switch to standard regime.

---

### Option B — Régimen de Actividades Empresariales y Servicios Profesionales (Standard Self-Employment)

- **Who qualifies:** All resident individuals with business or professional income; no revenue ceiling.
- **Tax base:** Net income = gross revenue minus deductible business expenses (supported by CFDIs). (EY p.999)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive ISR brackets as employment income (1.92%–35%); monthly provisional payments by the 17th of the following month.
- **ISR withholding on honorarios:** When a legal entity pays honorarios to an individual, it withholds 10% as a provisional payment; credited against annual ISR. (EY p.999)
- **Social security:** IMSS voluntary (same as Option A).
- **VAT:** IVA 16% on most services; monthly returns required.
- **Key limits:** No ceiling; full expense deductibility makes this better only when expenses are very high (reducing the ISR base significantly — at 2% RESICO rate, expenses would need to be ≥ 98% of revenue to make standard regime favorable, which is nearly impossible).

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No General "New Resident" Tax Incentive

Mexico does not have a formal preferential regime for newly arrived tax residents. Residents are subject to worldwide income tax from day 1 of residency establishment. (EY p.998)

### Short-Stay Nonresident Exemption

- **Eligibility:** Individuals providing services in Mexico for fewer than 183 days (non-consecutive) in any 12-month period, paid by a nonresident employer with no PE in Mexico.
- **Benefit:** Salary income exempt from Mexican ISR. (EY p.999)
- **Duration:** Applies as long as the 183-day threshold is not exceeded.
- **Caveats:** Once 183 days is crossed, ISR applies from the first day of service; the 183 days include Saturdays, Sundays, and holidays.

### Platform Economy Simplified Withholding

- Income via digital platforms: operators withhold ISR at 2.1% (transport/delivery), 4% (accommodation), or 1% (goods sales); these can be treated as final payments if conditions are met. (EY p.999)

## 2026 Changes

- **RMF 2026 published 28 December 2025:** Annual ISR bracket peso amounts updated for inflation; rates 1.92%–35% unchanged. RESICO-PF rates 1%–2.5% unchanged.
- **UMA 2026 = MXN 113.14/day** (annual = MXN 41,273): used for social security caps, deduction ceilings, and personal allowance calculations.
- **RESICO-PF ceiling MXN 3,500,000 unchanged:** No announced increase for 2026.
- **MLI fully operative since 2024:** OECD Multilateral Instrument modifies Mexico's tax treaties; potential impact on digital nomads and cross-border remote workers with treaty claims. (EY p.1007)
- **No structural PIT changes** announced for 2026 as of October 2025 (EY content date).

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, personal deductions not claimed (simplified baseline).
> EUR/MXN rate: 1 EUR = MXN 20 (approximate 2025 average; note: MXN is volatile — range was MXN 17–21/EUR in 2025; rates in EUR terms shift proportionally).
> "Employment" = gross salary, employee-side ISR + IMSS (~2.775%); employer contributions not shown.
> "RESICO-PF B2B" = gross revenue; RESICO rate applied to full gross; no IMSS (not mandatory).
> Annual income used throughout.

**Key insight — RESICO-PF is extraordinarily low burden:** For professional services freelancers earning under MXN 3,500,000/year (~€175k), RESICO-PF at 1%–2.5% on gross revenue with no mandatory social contributions yields effective rates of 1%–2.5% — among the globally lowest self-employment tax regimes. The delta vs. employment at €60k is dramatic: 26% vs. 2%. The trade-off is loss of IMSS healthcare (voluntary enrollment available) and the MXN 3.5M ceiling.

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/MXN = 20 (approximate 2025 average; peso is volatile — actual rate shifts will change EUR-equivalent figures proportionally)
- IMSS employee contribution: 2.775% of gross; capped at annual max ~MXN 28,154 (employee portion at SBC cap)
- Employment subsidy not applicable at these income levels (phase-out below ~MXN 122k/year gross)
- Personal deductions not claimed
- RESICO-PF: applicable bracket rate applied to full annual revenue at the bracket containing total income
- IVA (16%) not shown — collected from clients and remitted to SAT; cost-neutral for B2B services
- PTU (profit sharing) and state payroll tax (ISN, 1–3% of payroll, employer side) not included
- No AFORE (mandatory pension savings for employees via employer 2% + employee 1.125%) shown in effective rate — AFORE contributions are asset accumulation, not a pure tax, though they reduce take-home pay
