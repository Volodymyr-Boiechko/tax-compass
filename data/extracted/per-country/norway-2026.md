## Personal Income Tax

> Norway has a two-component income tax system: (1) ordinary income tax at a flat 22% on all net taxable income (after deductions), and (2) a progressive step tax (trinnskatt) levied on gross personal income from employment and pensions. The effective top marginal rate for 2025 is approximately 47.4% (22% ordinary + 17.7% step + 7.7% employee social security). Tax year = calendar year. Residents taxed on worldwide income.

**Ordinary income tax:** 22% flat rate on all taxable net income (after standard deductions)
- Personal deduction reduces ordinary income tax base by NOK 108,550 (2025)

**Step tax (trinnskatt) rates on gross personal income (employment/pension) — 2025:**

| Gross income (NOK) | Step tax rate % |
|---|---|
| 0 – 217,400 | 0 |
| 217,400 – 306,050 | 1.7 |
| 306,050 – 697,150 | 4.0 |
| 697,150 – 942,400 | 13.7 |
| 942,400 – 1,410,750 | 16.7 |
| Above 1,410,750 | 17.7 |

*(EY p. 1152)*

**Personal allowance for ordinary income tax:** Standard minimum allowance = 46% of gross employment income, maximum NOK 92,000 (2025); reduces taxable income for ordinary tax

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Standard minimum allowance (minstefradrag): 46% of employment income, capped at NOK 92,000 — reduces ordinary income base
- Personal deduction (personfradrag): NOK 108,550 (2025) — further reduces ordinary income base
- Interest expenses on loans: fully deductible from ordinary income (at 22% effective relief)
- Business deductions (self-employed): all expenses wholly and exclusively for earning income are deductible

**Notes:**
- Top marginal rate 2025: 22% + 17.7% (step) + 7.7% (employee SS) = 47.4%
- Dividends: shareholders' model — adjusted by factor 1.72 for 2025; effective rate on dividends = 37.84%
- Capital gains on shares: adjusted by 1.72 factor; effective rate 37.84%
- Wealth tax: 0.525% on taxable net assets above NOK 1,760,000 (municipal 0.475% + national 0.05% on assets above NOK 20.7 million: 0.575%) (EY p. 1153)
- Inheritance/gift taxes: abolished from 1 January 2014 (EY p. 1153)
- Exit tax: applies on unrealized profits on shares >NOK 3 million upon emigration; 12-year installment option

## Social Security

> Employees and employers, as well as self-employed, must pay social security contributions. Employee rate: 7.7% (2025); self-employed: 11%; employer: 14.1% (2025, general rate).

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (trygdeavgift / arbeidsgiveravgift) | 7.7% | 14.1% | 11.0% |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling — contributions payable on all income (EY p. 1153)
**Notes:**
- Employee 7.7%: not deductible from income for PIT purposes; payable on gross employment income
- Self-employed 11.0%: on all personal income from business
- Employer 14.1% (general rate): lower in certain municipalities (differentiated regional rates)
- Additional employer surcharge (5% on salary >NOK 85,000/employee) introduced 2023 — abolished from 1 January 2025 (EY p. 1153)
- Ordinary income tax base is gross income minus standard allowance minus personal deduction; employer contributions deductible as business expenses for CIT purposes

## Self-Employment Regimes

> Self-employed individuals pay ordinary income tax (22% on net profit after deductions), step tax on personal income drawn, and 11% social security contributions. The partnership model may apply if operating through a partnership.

### Option A — Sole Trader (Enkeltpersonforetak)
- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on independent business activity in Norway
- **Tax base:** Net business profit (revenue minus all business expenses); all business profits exceeding a risk-free return on invested capital are taxed as personal income at standard rates
- **Rate / mechanism:** 22% ordinary income tax on net profit (less personal deduction NOK 108,550 and standard allowance where applicable); step tax on gross personal income drawn; 11% SS on business profits
- **Social security:** 11% trygdeavgift on all self-employment income (no ceiling)
- **VAT obligations:** Registration required above NOK 50,000 annual turnover; standard VAT rate 25%; reduced rates 15% (food) and 12% (passenger transport)
- **Key limits:** Business losses: carry forward 10 years; no carryback (general rule); depreciation rules for assets used in business; capital gains on business assets taxed as ordinary income

### Option B — Partnership (Ansvarlig Selskap / ANS / KS)
- **Who qualifies:** Two or more persons operating a business together
- **Tax base:** "Net assessment" — partnership income computed at partnership level; allocated to partners as personal income; multiplied by 1.72 factor for distributed profits (2025)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Effective rate on distributed income 51.5% (2025) to ensure same taxation as limited companies; ordinary income at 22%; step tax on partner income; SS at 11%
- **Social security:** Same 11% SS as self-employed
- **VAT obligations:** Standard NOK 50,000 threshold; 25% standard rate
- **Key limits:** Complex calculation; partnership income allocated based on ownership percentage

### Option C — Optional Simplified PAYE (25% flat for foreign workers)
- **Who qualifies:** Nonresidents or first-year residents working in Norway; not applicable to offshore workers or foreign seafarers; annual employment income must be below a certain threshold
- **Tax base:** Gross employment income (no deductions allowed)
- **Rate / mechanism:** 25% flat final tax (including employee SS of 7.7%); if A1/Certificate of Coverage provided by home country, exempt from Norwegian SS → 17.3% flat rate
- **Social security:** Included in 25% (7.7%); exempt if A1 certificate provided
- **VAT obligations:** Not applicable
- **Key limits:** Optional — individual may choose regular progressive taxation instead; no annual tax return required (EY p. 1150)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Optional Simplified PAYE (PAYE for Foreign Workers)
- **Eligibility:** Nonresidents or individuals in their first year of Norwegian tax residence; employed in Norway; annual income below a specified threshold; not offshore workers or foreign seafarers
- **Benefit:** Flat 25% tax (includes 7.7% SS) on gross employment income — no deductions; if home-country SS continues (A1/Certificate of Coverage), only 17.3% flat rate applies; no annual tax return obligation
- **Duration:** Available in first-year and nonresident years; case-by-case evaluation required for extended use
- **Application deadline:** Election made via employer at commencement of employment
- **Caveats:** No deductions allowed in simplified regime; not available for self-employed; may be less beneficial than regular taxation for lower-income individuals who would benefit from standard deductions; cannot use if self-employed

## 2026 Changes

> (EY pp. 1148–1158)

- Additional employer surcharge (5% on salary >NOK 85,000/employee, introduced 2023) **abolished from 1 January 2025** — no surcharge in 2026 (EY p. 1153)
- Step tax rates and thresholds indexed annually — 2026 thresholds not yet confirmed at time of EY review (2025 rates apply)
- Ordinary income tax rate: 22% (unchanged)
- Dividend/capital gain adjustment factor: 1.72 for 2025; 2026 factor not yet published
- Wealth tax: 0.525% on assets >NOK 1,760,000 (no change announced); national element 0.05% on assets >NOK 20.7 million

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants, Norwegian tax resident.
> Currency: NOK. 1 EUR ≈ 11.5 NOK (approximate 2025 rate). EUR amounts are approximate conversions.
> Employment: ordinary income tax (22%) + step tax + employee SS (7.7%).

**Employment at €30,000 (≈ NOK 345,000):**
1. Employee SS: NOK 345,000 × 7.7% = NOK 26,565 ≈ €2,310
2. Standard allowance: 46% of NOK 345,000 = NOK 158,700 (capped at NOK 92,000) → use cap: NOK 92,000
3. Ordinary income base: NOK 345,000 − NOK 92,000 − NOK 108,550 (personal deduction) = NOK 144,450
4. Ordinary income tax: NOK 144,450 × 22% = NOK 31,779
5. Step tax: NOK 217,400 × 0% = 0; NOK 127,600 × 1.7% = NOK 2,169 → step = NOK 2,169
6. Total tax = NOK 31,779 + NOK 2,169 = NOK 33,948 ≈ €2,952
7. Total burden = €2,952 + €2,310 = €5,262
8. Effective rate = €5,262 / €30,000 = **17.5%**

**Employment at €60,000 (≈ NOK 690,000):**
1. Employee SS: NOK 690,000 × 7.7% = NOK 53,130 ≈ €4,620
2. Ordinary income base: NOK 690,000 − NOK 92,000 − NOK 108,550 = NOK 489,450
3. Ordinary income tax: NOK 489,450 × 22% = NOK 107,679
4. Step tax: 0% up to NOK 217,400; NOK 88,650 × 1.7% = NOK 1,507; NOK 384,950 × 4.0% = NOK 15,398; above NOK 306,050 to NOK 690,000 = NOK 383,950 × 4.0% = NOK 15,358 → check: NOK 217,400–306,050 = NOK 88,650 × 1.7% = NOK 1,507; NOK 306,050–690,000 = NOK 383,950 × 4.0% = NOK 15,358 → step = NOK 16,865
5. Total tax = NOK 107,679 + NOK 16,865 = NOK 124,544 ≈ €10,830
6. Total burden = €10,830 + €4,620 = €15,450
7. Effective rate = €15,450 / €60,000 = **25.8%**

**Employment at €100,000 (≈ NOK 1,150,000):**
1. Employee SS: NOK 1,150,000 × 7.7% = NOK 88,550 ≈ €7,700
2. Ordinary income base: NOK 1,150,000 − NOK 92,000 − NOK 108,550 = NOK 949,450
3. Ordinary income tax: NOK 949,450 × 22% = NOK 208,879
4. Step tax: NOK 0–217,400: 0%; NOK 217,400–306,050 (NOK 88,650) × 1.7% = NOK 1,507; NOK 306,050–697,150 (NOK 391,100) × 4.0% = NOK 15,644; NOK 697,150–942,400 (NOK 245,250) × 13.7% = NOK 33,599; NOK 942,400–1,150,000 (NOK 207,600) × 16.7% = NOK 34,669 → step = NOK 85,419
5. Total tax = NOK 208,879 + NOK 85,419 = NOK 294,298 ≈ €25,591
6. Total burden = €25,591 + €7,700 = €33,291
7. Effective rate = €33,291 / €100,000 = **33.3%**

**B2B (sole trader) at €60,000 (≈ NOK 690,000 revenue):**
1. Business costs ~15%: NOK 103,500; net profit = NOK 586,500
2. Self-employed SS: NOK 586,500 × 11% = NOK 64,515 ≈ €5,610
3. Ordinary income base: NOK 586,500 − NOK 108,550 (personal deduction) = NOK 477,950 (no minstefradrag for self-employed)
4. Ordinary income tax: NOK 477,950 × 22% = NOK 105,149
5. Step tax (on NOK 586,500): NOK 88,650 × 1.7% + NOK 280,450 × 4.0% = NOK 1,507 + NOK 11,218 = NOK 12,725
6. Total tax = NOK 105,149 + NOK 12,725 = NOK 117,874 ≈ €10,250
7. Total burden = €10,250 + €5,610 = €15,860
8. Effective rate = €15,860 / €60,000 = **26.4%**

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax | Social security | Total burden | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | €2,952 | €2,310 | €5,262 | **17.5%** |
| €30,000 | B2B | ~€3,500 | ~€2,500 | ~€6,000 | **~20.0%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | €10,830 | €4,620 | €15,450 | **25.8%** |
| €60,000 | B2B | €10,250 | €5,610 | €15,860 | **26.4%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | €25,591 | €7,700 | €33,291 | **33.3%** |
| €100,000 | B2B | ~€23,000 | ~€9,350 | ~€32,350 | **~32.4%** |

**Assumptions:**
- Single person, no dependants, Norwegian resident
- Employment: standard allowance 46% capped at NOK 92,000; personal deduction NOK 108,550; employee SS 7.7%
- B2B: sole trader; 15% business costs; personal deduction NOK 108,550; no standard allowance; self-employed SS 11%
- Step tax 2025 rates applied; ordinary tax 22%
- EUR/NOK ≈ 11.5 (approximate; NOK not EUR-pegged)
- Wealth tax not included in calculations above (applies on net assets >NOK 1,760,000)
