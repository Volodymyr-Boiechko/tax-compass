## Personal Income Tax

Norway operates a dual-income tax system. Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents taxed on Norwegian-source income. Tax year = calendar year. Employers deduct tax at source. Self-employed file annual returns. The Norwegian Tax Administration (Skatteetaten) issues pre-filled returns (skattemeldingen) by April; deadline to amend/approve: 30 April. (EY p.1148)

**Income tax structure:**
1. **Flat tax on "ordinary income" (alminnelig inntekt):** 22% on taxable ordinary income (gross income minus deductions minus personal allowance)
2. **Bracket tax (trinnskatt) on "personal income" (personinntekt):** Progressive surtax on gross income (no deductions)
3. **Trygdeavgift (national insurance / social security member fee):** Levied on personal income

**Flat tax on ordinary income — 22%** (EY p.1148)
- Applied on net income after standard deductions
- Ordinary income includes: salary, business income, dividends (with shield deduction), interest, rental income, capital gains

**Bracket tax (trinnskatt) — 2025 rates** (EY p.1148)

| Personal income (NOK) | Bracket tax rate | Cumulative |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 217,400 | 0% | — |
| 217,401 – 306,050 | 1.7% | — |
| 306,051 – 697,150 | 4.0% | — |
| 697,151 – 942,400 | 13.6% | — |
| 942,401 – 1,410,750 | 16.6% | — |
| Over 1,410,750 | 17.6% | — |

**Trygdeavgift (national insurance contribution)** (EY p.1149):
- Employees: **7.9%** on personal income (no ceiling)
- Self-employed: **11.1%** on net self-employment income up to NOK 12G; above 12G: 7.9%
  (G = Grunnbeløp = NOK 124,028 for 2025; 12G = NOK 1,488,336)
- Pensioners: 5.1%

**Combined top marginal rate:** 22% (flat) + 17.6% (top bracket tax) + 7.9% (trygdeavgift) = **47.5%** effective marginal rate above NOK 1,410,750 for employees. (EY p.1149)

**For self-employed above the G-threshold:** 22% + 17.6% + 11.1% = ~50.7% (simplified; actual computation more complex due to different bases).

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Personal allowance (personfradrag): NOK 108,550 (2025) — deducted from ordinary income (22% tax base)
- Minimum standard deduction (minstefradrag): 46% of gross income, min NOK 68,200, max NOK 104,450 (for employment/pension income)
- Standard deduction replaces actual employment expenses in most cases
- Interest on loans: deductible from ordinary income (22% saving)
- Pension savings: contributions to IPS (individual pension savings) deductible up to NOK 15,000/year
- Union dues, work tools: limited deductions available

**Notes:**
- Dividends from Norwegian shares: taxed after a risk-free return shield (skjermingsfradrag); excess dividends multiplied by 1.72 and taxed at 22% (effective 35.2% on dividends for shareholders) — for personal shareholders in AS companies
- Capital gains on shares: same 35.2% effective rate (multiplier 1.72)
- Interest income: included in ordinary income at 22%
- No separate payroll tax for employees; employers pay employer's NI (arbeidsgiveravgift) at 14.1% (regional variation 0%–14.1%)
- PAYE for foreign workers: Norway has a simplified PAYE scheme for foreign workers allowing flat 25% withholding (no deductions)

## Social Security

Norway's trygdeavgift is the main employee "social security" contribution; employer's NI (arbeidsgiveravgift) is the employer side. (EY p.1149)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trygdeavgift (NI member fee) | 7.9% | — | 11.1% (up to 12G), 7.9% above |
| Arbeidsgiveravgift (employer NI) | — | 14.1% (south; varies 0–14.1% by zone) | — |
| **Total employee/self-emp** | **7.9%** | **14.1%** | **11.1%** |

**Income ceiling (Trygdeavgift):** No ceiling for employees (7.9% on all income). For self-employed: 11.1% up to 12G (NOK 1,488,336), 7.9% above.

**Notes:**
- Trygdeavgift is deductible from ordinary income (reduces 22% flat tax base)
- Employer's arbeidsgiveravgift: base rate 14.1% in Oslo; reduced rates in northern/peripheral zones (0% in zone 5 — very northern Norway). Not deducted from employee's pay.
- AFP (early retirement supplement): additional employer contribution in private sector (~2.5% of salary); sector-specific

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Sole Proprietorship (Enkeltpersonforetak — ENK)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual registering a sole proprietorship in the Brønnøysund Register. No revenue ceiling for this regime. Must register if annual turnover > NOK 50,000.
- **Tax base:** Net business income (revenue minus business expenses). The "personal income" base for trygdeavgift (11.1%) is gross profit before certain deductions.
- **Rate / mechanism:** 22% flat tax on net ordinary income (after deductions + personal allowance) + bracket tax (trinnskatt) on personal income + trygdeavgift 11.1% (up to 12G), 7.9% above.
- **Social security:** Trygdeavgift 11.1% (vs. 7.9% for employees); difference ~3.2% extra vs. employment. This is the main disadvantage of ENK vs. employment.
- **VAT obligations:** Register for MVA (Merverdiavgift, 25% standard; 15% food; 12% passenger transport/hotel) when turnover exceeds NOK 50,000/year. File MVA returns (2-monthly or annual for small businesses).
- **Key limits:** Unlimited personal liability. Can opt for AS (limited company) for liability protection once revenue justifies extra admin.

### Option B — Limited Company (Aksjeselskap — AS) + Salary/Dividend

- **Who qualifies:** Freelancers/consultants who have incorporated an AS (minimum share capital NOK 30,000). Income extracted as salary or dividends.
- **Tax base:** Company pays 22% corporate tax on profits; individual taxed on salary (same progressive rates) or dividends (35.2% effective). Dividends benefit from shield deduction (risk-free return on invested capital is tax-free).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Optimal salary = approximately the G amount (NOK 124,028) to maximize pension/trygdeavgift rights; remaining profit extracted as (lower-taxed) dividends.
- **Social security:** Company pays employer NI (14.1%) on salary; dividends have no SS contribution.
- **VAT obligations:** Same MVA rules as ENK.
- **Key limits:** More complex administration; minimum share capital NOK 30,000; annual accounts filing required.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### PAYE Scheme for Foreign Workers (Kildeskatt på lønn)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign workers in Norway who are not tax resident for the full year; workers on short-term assignments (typically first 1–2 years); applicable to citizens of EEA and most other countries.
- **Benefit:** Flat 25% withholding on gross wage income (no deductions, no filing of annual return). Simple and predictable. Employer withholds and remits; employee has no filing obligation.
- **Duration:** Available for the duration of non-resident or newly resident status; once tax resident for the full year with significant ties, must switch to standard regime.
- **Application deadline:** Elect via employer; employer registers with Skatteetaten.
- **Caveats:** 25% flat may be higher or lower than actual liability under standard regime; worth calculating both for individual circumstances. Does not build up standard pension rights in Norway (trygdeavgift not paid under PAYE scheme).

### Expatriate Deductions (for full tax residents)

- **Foreign commuter deduction:** Non-Norwegian residents working in Norway may deduct commuting costs to home country
- **Standard expat deduction:** Up to 10% of gross salary (max NOK 40,000) for "standard deduction for foreign employees" — available for first 2 years of employment in Norway (EY p.1151)

## 2026 Changes

- **Bracket tax thresholds:** Reset annually with indexation; 2025 thresholds as above. 2026 thresholds will be announced in the state budget (typically October 2025) — not yet published at EY content date.
- **Trygdeavgift rates:** 7.9% (employee), 11.1% (SE) remain unchanged from 2023 reform that reduced from 8.0%/11.4%. No changes announced for 2026.
- **Dividend multiplier:** 1.72 remains for 2025/2026; no announced change.
- **AS minimum capital:** Reduced to NOK 30,000 in prior years; no further reduction announced.
- **Global minimum tax (Pillar Two):** Norway enacted Pillar Two rules effective 2024; affects large multinationals, not individual PIT.

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants.
> NOK/EUR rate: 1 EUR = NOK 11.70 (approximate 2025 average).
> Employment: 22% flat + trinnskatt + trygdeavgift 7.9%.
> Self-employed ENK: 22% flat + trinnskatt + trygdeavgift 11.1%.
> Standard deductions: minstefradrag (46% of gross, capped at NOK 104,450) and personfradrag (NOK 108,550) applied for employment.

### Working: NOK 351,000 (≈ €30,000) Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |

| Minstefradrag (46%, capped) | 46% × 351,000 = 161,460 → capped at 104,450 | NOK 104,450 |
| Ordinary income (taxable) | 351,000 − 104,450 − 27,729 − 108,550 | NOK 110,271 |
| Flat tax 22% | 22% × 110,271 | NOK 24,260 |
| Bracket tax (trinnskatt): 0–217,400 @ 0% | 0 | NOK 0 |
| Bracket tax: 217,401–306,050 @ 1.7% | 1.7% × 88,650 | NOK 1,507 |
| Bracket tax: 306,051–351,000 @ 4.0% | 4.0% × 44,950 | NOK 1,798 |

**Key insight:** Employment in Norway has a lower effective rate (16–27%) than self-employment (25–32%) primarily due to the higher trygdeavgift for self-employed (11.1% vs. 7.9%) and the absence of the minstefradrag deduction for ENK. At €60k+ employment, the high bracket taxes (13.6%+) kick in and the gap narrows. Norway's top marginal rate (47.5% for employees, ~50% for high-earning SE) is among the highest in OECD. The AS company structure is preferred by high earners to optimize dividend vs. salary split.

**Assumptions:**
- NOK/EUR = 11.70 (approximate 2025)
- Employment: minstefradrag 46% (capped at NOK 104,450); personfradrag NOK 108,550
- ENK: no expense deductions (pure service income); personfradrag applied; no minstefradrag for SE
- Trygdeavgift deductible from ordinary income base (22% tax)
- Employer arbeidsgiveravgift (14.1%) not included in employee burden
- No AS dividend optimization modeled
