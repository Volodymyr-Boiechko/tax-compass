## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PAYE system. Tax year = 1 July to 30 June. Employment income and business income are withheld or assessed under PAYE. Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents taxed on Uganda-source income only. Administered by the Uganda Revenue Authority (URA).

**EY caution:** "Because of the possible changes to the tax law, readers should obtain updated information before engaging in transactions." (EY p. 1570)

**Residency:** At least one of: permanent home in Uganda; present for 183+ days in the tax year; present for average of 122+ days in tax year and two preceding years; government employee posted abroad.

**Foreign non-citizens:** Taxed on worldwide income only after being resident for a total of 2 years during lifetime (same rule as Tanzania). Prior to that, only Uganda-source income taxed.

**Notes on income categories (EY pp. 1570–1572):**
- Employment income includes wages, salaries, vacation pay, sick pay, payment in lieu of vacation, directors' fees, commissions, bonuses, gratuities, and most benefits in kind (employer-provided cars, housing, stock options); travel allowances taxable if "excessive"
- Self-employment/business income: trading profits, gains from business asset disposals, professional/management fees, insurance compensation for loss of profits
- Investment income: dividends subject to 15% final withholding; interest income at 15% final withholding; rental income for those earning > UGX 2,820,000/year at 12% on gross rental; rental ≤ UGX 2,820,000/year: 0%
- Capital gains: 30% on disposal of business assets
- Short-term residents (non-citizens present ≤ 2 years): exempt from Uganda tax on foreign-source employment income if they have paid foreign income tax on that income
- Motor vehicle benefit: depreciated at 35% reducing balance annually when computing benefit value
- Digital Services Tax (DST): 5% on non-resident persons providing digital services to customers in Uganda (not associates) — quarterly returns (EY p. 1571)

**Deductions:**
- No personal deductions allowed (EY p. 1571)
- Business deductions: expenses incurred wholly and exclusively in production of income; identified bad debts; wear-and-tear (Class 1: computers 40%; Class 2: plant/machinery in farming/manufacturing/mining 30%; Class 3: all other depreciable assets 20%)

## Social Security

The National Social Security Fund (NSSF) is a statutory savings program (EY p. 1575):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| NSSF | **5%** | **10%** | On total monetary remuneration |
| **Total** | **5%** | **10%** | |

**Income ceiling:** No ceiling stated; contributions based on total monetary remuneration

**Notes:**
- NSSF mandatory registration for employers regardless of number of employees (amended 7 January 2022 — EY p. 1575)
- Members aged 45+ who have contributed for ≥ 10 years may access up to 20% of accrued benefits mid-term
- No compulsory separate health or unemployment insurance contributions for employees beyond NSSF
- Estate and gift tax: not levied in Uganda; net worth tax: not levied in Uganda (EY p. 1575)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Business Income (General Assessment)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual earning business or professional income in Uganda
- **Tax base:** Net income = gross revenue less allowable business expenses (ordinary and necessary expenses incurred wholly and exclusively in production of income)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive PAYE rates; same schedule as employment income (0%–40%); quarterly provisional returns required
- **Social security:** NSSF: if the self-employed individual employs staff, NSSF mandatory; sole traders without employees not compulsorily enrolled as individuals
- **VAT obligations:** Register for VAT if annual taxable supplies ≥ UGX 150,000,000; standard rate 18%
- **Key limits:** Provisional tax returns quarterly; final return within 6 months of tax year-end (31 December); losses carried forward 7 years (from 1 July 2023) but limited to 50% of loss in first year of carry-forward; then full amount in subsequent years (EY p. 1573)

### Option B — Rental Income (Simplified)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with rental income from immovable property
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  - Gross rental ≤ UGX 2,820,000/year: 0% (EY p. 1571)
  - Gross rental > UGX 2,820,000/year: **12% of gross rental income** (final tax for individuals; different from general income tax)
- **Deductions:** Mortgage interest on rented premises NOT deductible for rental income
- **Filing:** Separate from employment/business income assessment

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Short-Term Resident Exemption (EY p. 1571)

- **Eligibility:** Non-citizen individuals who are not "resident" in Uganda for income tax purposes (i.e., presence ≤ 2 cumulative years during lifetime, OR short-term resident defined as non-citizen present ≤ 2 years continuously)
- **Benefit:** Foreign-source employment income exempt from Uganda tax if the individual has paid foreign income tax on that income
- **Duration:** Per year; ceases once individual has been Uganda resident for 2 cumulative years during lifetime

### No General Newcomer / Expat Preferential Regime

Uganda does not offer a general territorial or participation-exemption regime for new residents beyond the two-year foreign-income exemption for short-term residents.

## 2026 Changes

- No specific 2026 rate changes announced in EY guide; rates as stated above reflect law as of 1 October 2025
- **Wear-and-tear reclassification (effective 1 July 2021):** Class 1 (computers, data handling: 40%); Class 2 (farming/manufacturing/mining plant: 30%); Class 3 (all other: 20%) — this reclassification is current law (EY p. 1572)
- **NSSF mandatory registration amendment (7 January 2022):** Applies to all employers regardless of employee count — current law
- **Loss carry-forward amendment (1 July 2023):** 7-year carry-forward, limited to 50% in first year — current law (EY p. 1573)
- **VAT refund threshold (1 July 2024):** UGX 5M → UGX 10M minimum for refund claims — current law (EY p. 1574)
- **DST (Digital Services Tax) 5%:** In force for non-resident digital services providers — current law (EY p. 1571)
- Uganda budget typically announced mid-year; any post-October 2025 changes not captured

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **NSSF applicability to self-employed sole traders:** EY describes NSSF as employer obligation; mandatory for employers; sole traders without employees may not be compulsorily covered — needs URA confirmation
- [ ] **Business expense deductibility:** 15% deemed expense ratio is illustrative; actual deductible amounts depend on specific documented expenses
- [ ] **VAT registration threshold:** UGX 150,000,000 stated; confirm current threshold for 2026 with URA
- [ ] **Tax year mismatch:** Uganda tax year is 1 July – 30 June (not calendar year); effective rate calculations use annual bracket amounts; actual PAYE is monthly based
- [ ] **2026/27 budget:** Uganda budget typically announced June for tax year starting July; post-October 2025 changes not captured
- [ ] **DST for digital service providers:** 5% on gross receipts from Ugandan customers; no deductions — relevant for non-resident tech companies, not individual employees
