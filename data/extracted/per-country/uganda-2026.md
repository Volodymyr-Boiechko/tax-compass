## Personal Income Tax

> Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents on Uganda-source income only. Tax year runs 1 July to 30 June. PAYE system for employees. Individuals file quarterly provisional returns; final return within six months of year-end. Administered by URA (Uganda Revenue Authority). Note: EY cautions that tax law may change — readers should obtain updated information before transacting.

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- No personal deductions allowed (EY p.1571)
- Business deductions: expenses deductible to the extent incurred in production of income; bad debts incurred in production of taxable income also deductible
- Capital allowances (depreciation) — declining-balance rates:
  - Class 1 (computers and data handling equipment): 40%
  - Class 2 (plant and machinery — farming, manufacturing, mining): 30%
  - Class 3 (automobiles, vehicles, office furniture, all other depreciable assets): 20%
  - Industrial buildings / certain commercial buildings: straight-line over 20 years

**Exemptions:**
- Foreign-source employment income exempt if foreign income tax has been paid on that income
- Education cash allowances by employer: taxable but exempt from social security if employer pays school directly or reimburses actual fees
- Short-term residents (non-Ugandan citizens present ≤ 2 years): income from foreign sources exempt

**Notes:**
- Employment income: wages, salaries, vacation pay, sick pay, payment in lieu of vacation, directors' fees, commissions, bonuses, gratuities, entertainment allowances, benefits in kind (cars, housing, stock options); travel allowances taxable if excessive
- Motor vehicle benefit: depreciated at 35% annual reducing-balance rate when computing the benefit
- Self-employment and business income: same rate scale applies; capital gains from disposal of business assets: 30%
- Dividends: 15% final WHT (residents and nonresidents)
- Interest: 15% final WHT (residents); royalties aggregated with other income at graduated rates (residents)
- Rental income: taxed separately; 0% for individuals with gross rental income ≤ UGX 2,820,000/year; 12% for gross rental income > UGX 2,820,000/year; partnership rental income: 0% up to UGX 2,820,000/year
- Nonresidents: 15% WHT on investment income, rental of immovable property, management fees, royalties, consultancy fees, any payments for services in Uganda
- Digital Services Tax (DST): 5% on nonresident persons providing digital services to Uganda customers (not associates); quarterly returns by 15th day after each quarter-end
- Losses: carry forward 7 years (from 1 July 2023); 50% deduction limit in years 2–7 (50% of the loss carried forward deductible at beginning of following year); no carryback (except long-term contracts in final year)
- WHT local: 6% on supplies of goods/services > UGX 1,000,000 if payer is designated; professional/management fees: 6% local, 15% international

## Social Security

> NSSF (National Social Security Fund) — statutory savings program for retirement benefits. (EY p.1575)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| NSSF | 5% of total monetary remuneration | 10% of total monetary remuneration | Voluntary |
| **Total** | **5%** | **10%** | **—** |

**Income ceiling:** None specified (all monetary remuneration)
**Notes:**
- NSSF Act Cap. 222 (amended 7 January 2022): mandatory registration for all employers regardless of size; mandatory regular contributions
- Members aged 45+ who have contributed for 10+ years: eligible for midterm access up to 20% of accrued benefits
- Voluntary contributions allowed above standard rates
- Self-employed may contribute voluntarily

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Individual Business / Self-Employed (Graduated Rates)
- **Who qualifies:** Resident individuals with self-employment, business or professional income from Uganda sources
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross income less deductible expenses; capital allowances apply)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Same progressive rates (0–40%) as employment income; quarterly provisional returns; final return within 6 months of year-end
- **Social security:** NSSF voluntary; 5% if voluntarily contributing
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required above UGX 150,000,000 annual turnover (standard rate 18%)
- **Key limits:** No personal deductions; losses carry forward 7 years (50% limitation from year 2); capital gains from business assets: 30%

### Option B — Rental Income (Separate Tax)
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals with rental income from immovable property
- **Tax base:** Gross rental income
- **Rate / mechanism:** 0% if ≤ UGX 2,820,000/year; 12% on gross rental income if > UGX 2,820,000/year
- **Social security:** As above
- **VAT obligations:** As above
- **Key limits:** Taxed separately from other income; mortgage interest not deductible against rental income

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Short-Term Resident Exemption
- **Eligibility:** Non-Ugandan citizens present in Uganda for ≤ 2 years
- **Benefit:** Foreign-source income exempt from Uganda income tax
- **Duration:** Up to 2 years from arrival
- **Application deadline:** N/A
- **Caveats:** Uganda-source income remains taxable; once 2-year threshold passed, worldwide income becomes taxable

## 2026 Changes

- Loss carry-forward rule (7 years, 50% limitation) effective from 1 July 2023 — ongoing (EY p.1573)
- Initial allowance on plant, machinery and industrial buildings repealed from 1 July 2023 (EY p.1572)
- VAT refund claim threshold increased to UGX 10 million (from UGX 5 million) effective 1 July 2024 (EY p.1573)
- No specific 2026 rate changes identified; note EY cautions that law may change rapidly

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Assumes single resident person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Exchange rate used: 1 EUR ≈ 4,000 UGX (approximate mid-2025).
> Employment: NSSF 5% employee. B2B: NSSF voluntary (excluded).
> No personal deductions.

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (UGX) | Social security (NSSF) | Total burden (UGX) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 (≈UGX 120,000,000) | Employment | 34,824,000 | 6,000,000 (5%) | 40,824,000 | ~34.0% |
| €30,000 (≈UGX 120,000,000) | B2B | 34,824,000 | 0 | 34,824,000 | ~29.0% |
| €60,000 (≈UGX 240,000,000) | Employment | 82,824,000 | 12,000,000 (5%) | 94,824,000 | ~39.5% |
| €60,000 (≈UGX 240,000,000) | B2B | 82,824,000 | 0 | 82,824,000 | ~34.5% |
| €100,000 (≈UGX 400,000,000) | Employment | 138,824,000 | 20,000,000 (5%) | 158,824,000 | ~39.7% |
| €100,000 (≈UGX 400,000,000) | B2B | 138,824,000 | 0 | 138,824,000 | ~34.7% |

**Working (income tax):**
- UGX 120,000,000: exactly at top of 30% band. Tax = **34,824,000** (~29.0%)
- UGX 240,000,000: in 40% band. Tax = 34,824,000 + 40% × (240,000,000 − 120,000,000) = 34,824,000 + 48,000,000 = **82,824,000** (~34.5%)
- UGX 400,000,000: Tax = 34,824,000 + 40% × (400,000,000 − 120,000,000) = 34,824,000 + 112,000,000 = **146,824,000** (~36.7%)

**Assumptions:**
- Exchange rate: 1 EUR = 4,000 UGX (approximate)
- Single, no dependants; no personal deductions (none allowed)
- NSSF 5% on full gross income (employment); nil for B2B
- No rental income separately taxed
