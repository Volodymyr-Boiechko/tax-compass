## Personal Income Tax

Worldwide system for residents. Residents taxed on worldwide income; nonresidents taxed on Ecuador-source income only (flat 25% withholding). Tax year: calendar year (1 January–31 December). (EY pp.370–373)

**Residency:** > 183 days (consecutive or non-consecutive) in a calendar year; OR > 183 days in a 12-month period across two consecutive calendar years (if main economic interests are in Ecuador); OR main economic interests in Ecuador (presumed if individual owns assets > USD 1 million in Ecuador and has been a tax resident).

**Temporary fiscal residence (from 20 December 2023):** 5-year term for individuals who have not acquired tax residence before; must make investment in real property or productive activities ≥ USD 150,000, OR receive monthly foreign income of ≥ USD 2,510. During temporary residence, only Ecuadorian-source income is taxed. (EY p.370)

**Additional deductions:**
- Third-age deduction (≥ 65 years old): USD 12,081 additional deduction (2025)
- Disability deduction (30%–49%): USD 14,497.20; 50%–74%: USD 16,913.40; 75%–84%: USD 19,329.60; 85%–100%: USD 24,162.00
- Rare/catastrophic disease (18% of 100 × basic basket = USD 14,369.58 max deduction)

**Exempt income:** (EY p.371)
- 13th salary (Christmas bonus = 1/12 annual compensation): exempt
- 14th salary (education bonus = 1 Unified Basic Remuneration = USD 470 for 2025): exempt
- Retirement fund contributions (8.33%/year from 2nd year of employment): exempt
- Industrial accident/death compensation, severance payments: exempt

**Notes:**
- Dividends from Ecuadorian companies: 12% withholding; must be added to global income for progressive rate application (0%–37%) (EY p.371)
- Capital gains on shares: 10% withholding (final) (EY p.371)
- Interest to residents: 2% provisional withholding (credited against annual tax)
- Nonresidents: flat 25% withholding on Ecuador-source income (EY p.373)
- Tax authority: SRI (Servicio de Rentas Internas)

## Social Security (IESS — Instituto Ecuatoriano de Seguridad Social)

All private, public and foreign employees and self-employed professionals covered. (EY p.374)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| IESS (pension + health + other) | 9.45% | ~11.15% (approx.) | 20.50% (approx.) |
| **Total** | **9.45%** | **~11.15%** | **~20.50%** |

**Notes:**
- Employee IESS (9.45%) is deductible for income tax purposes
- Expatriates in a labor relationship must register with IESS from first day of work; foreign residents contributing to IESS may not continue home-country coverage
- Totalization agreements: Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Dominican Republic, El Salvador, Paraguay, Peru, Portugal, Spain, Uruguay and Venezuela. Ecuador and Canada signed a totalization agreement on 24 June 2024, approved by National Assembly 1 May 2025. (EY p.374)
- **RIMPE (simplified regime for small businesses):** self-employed under RIMPE may have different SS obligations

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Régimen General)

- **Who qualifies:** All individuals with self-employment, professional, or business income in Ecuador.
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses). Deductible: directly incurred costs, interest on business debts (limited to Central Bank rate), relevant taxes, insurance premiums, travel/lodging, depreciation, wages paid to employees (including SS), business losses.
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive PIT rates (0%–37%) on annual net taxable income. Monthly provisional withholding at 8% on fees paid by legal entities.
- **Social security:** IESS ~20.50% of income (self-employed rate).
- **VAT obligations:** Subject to IVA (Ecuador VAT) at 15% standard rate on most services.
- **Key limits:** No revenue ceiling; full expense deductibility. Business income taxed at 29.5% on net profit (alternative for business income per EY p.371).

### Option B — RIMPE (Régimen MIPYMES / Simplified Regime for Small Businesses)

- **Who qualifies:** Micro, small and medium-sized enterprises (MIPYMEs) meeting size thresholds set by the government. Popular regime for small self-employed professionals.
- **Tax base:** Gross revenue (no expense deductions).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Simplified tax on gross revenue:
  - Micro-enterprises (emprendedores): very low revenue → fixed annual tax (table-based, starts near 0.5% on revenue)
  - Small businesses: 1%–2% on annual gross revenue
- **Social security:** IESS applies.
- **VAT obligations:** IVA obligations may be simplified.
- **Key limits:** Revenue ceiling applies (threshold set by SRI; verify current limit with SRI).

### Option C — Remote Work Visa (Visa para Trabajo Remoto)

- **Who qualifies:** Foreign nationals who perform work activities remotely for foreign companies.
- **Requirement:** Stable labor relationship with a foreign company AND remuneration ≥ 3 × Unified Basic Remuneration = USD 1,380/month (USD 16,920/year) for 2025.
- **Tax treatment:** Under territorial temporary fiscal residence rules, only Ecuador-source income is taxed — foreign income from remote work not subject to Ecuador PIT.
- **Caveats:** Ministry of Foreign Affairs may establish additional requirements. (EY p.375)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Temporary Fiscal Residence (from 20 December 2023)
- **Eligibility:** Individuals who have not acquired Ecuador tax residence and have not maintained such residence at any time prior to this legislation entering into force.
- **Benefit:** 5-year temporary fiscal residence — only Ecuadorian-source income is taxed (foreign income exempt).
- **Conditions:** Investment in real property or productive activities in Ecuador ≥ USD 150,000 OR monthly foreign income ≥ USD 2,510 (within 120-day period from first day of arriving in Ecuador).
- **Duration:** 5 years (if all conditions met within 120 days of arrival).
- **Caveats:** After 5-year term, if the individual remains in Ecuador and meets normal residency criteria, worldwide income becomes taxable. (EY p.370)

### Remote Work Visa (Temporary Residence for Remote Work)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals performing remote work activities for foreign companies.
- **Benefit:** Territorial taxation — only Ecuador-source income taxed; foreign remote-work income exempt.
- **Income requirement:** ≥ USD 1,380/month from foreign employer (3 × minimum wage).
- **Caveats:** Must demonstrate stable labor relationship with foreign company. Temporary residence visa valid for 2 years; renewable. (EY p.375)

## 2026 Changes

- **PIT brackets:** Ecuador uses USD-denominated brackets; updated annually. The 2025 brackets shown reflect the most recent EY data — verify 2026 updated amounts with SRI.
- **Basic basket unit:** USD 798.31 for 2025; updated annually.
- **14th salary:** USD 470 for 2025 (= Unified Basic Remuneration / minimum wage); updated by government decree.
- **Temporary fiscal residence:** Fully operative from 20 December 2023; continues to apply in 2026.
- **Remote work visa:** Operative; requirement of USD 1,380/month may be updated with minimum wage changes.
- **Ecuador–Canada totalization agreement:** Signed 24 June 2024; approved by National Assembly 1 May 2025 — operative in 2026.
- **No major PIT structural changes announced for 2026.**
