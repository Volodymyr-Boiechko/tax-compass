## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system for residents on worldwide income; nonresidents on Ecuadorian-source income. Tax year = 1 January – 31 December. Married persons taxed separately. Ecuador uses USD as its currency. (EY p.370, p.374)

**Residency:** Resident if stay exceeds 183 days (consecutive or non-consecutive) within a calendar year, OR if stay exceeds 183 days within a 12-month period in two consecutive calendar years (unless main activities/interests are elsewhere). Also considered resident if main core of activities/economic interests is in Ecuador (where individual is direct or indirect owner of assets in Ecuador >USD 1 million and has been a tax resident). Individual also considered resident if not in any other country for >183 days in the tax year. (EY p.370)

**Temporary fiscal residence (from 20 December 2023):** Applicable to individuals who have not acquired tax residence previously. Five-year term; requires: investment in real property or productive activities of at least USD 150,000 OR receipt of monthly foreign income of at least USD 2,510. Taxed only on Ecuadorian income. (EY p.370)

**Employment income exempt bonuses (annual, for all employees):** (EY p.371)
- Christmas bonus (13th salary): ≈ 1/12 of annual compensation
- Education bonus (14th salary): one Unified Basic Remuneration (USD 470 for 2025)
- Retirement funds: equal to 1/12 of year's remuneration (8.33%; payable from 2nd year with same employer)
- Compensation for industrial accidents/death, dismissal without legal cause, severance, pension/retirement fund payments: all exempt.

**Other income types:**
- Dividends (Ecuadorian companies to individuals): 12% withholding; included in global income for progressive rate purposes. (EY p.371)
- Interest paid to residents: added to taxable income at progressive rates; 2% withholding (credited against annual tax). (EY p.371)
- Capital gains from sales of shares: 10% withholding. (EY p.371)
- Royalties: treated as ordinary income. (EY p.371)
- Nonresidents staying ≤6 months and receiving Ecuadorian-source income: 25% withholding (final). (EY p.373)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- Social security contributions paid by employees: deductible. (EY p.372)
- Personal expenses discount: 18% discount on annual income tax based on number of family dependants and basic basket units. Maximum discount varies by number of dependants. (EY p.372)

## Social Security

The Social Security Institute (IESS) manages the social security system covering health benefits, pensions and certain social payments. All private, public, foreign employees and self-employed are covered. (EY p.374)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social security (IESS) | 9.45% | 12.15% | varies |
| **Total** | **9.45%** | **12.15%** | — |

**Notes:**
- Employee SS contributions ARE deductible for income tax purposes. (EY p.372)
- Expatriates in a labor relationship must register with IESS from their first day of work. (EY p.374)
- Foreign residents contributing to IESS may not continue receiving coverage from their home country system. (EY p.374)
- Totalization agreements with: Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Dominican Republic, El Salvador, Paraguay, Peru, Portugal, Spain, Uruguay, Venezuela; Canada totalization agreement signed 24 June 2024, approved by National Assembly 1 May 2025. (EY p.374)

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Standard Self-Employment (Individual Business Activity)
- **Who qualifies:** Sole proprietors, active members of partnerships, independent professionals conducting business activities in Ecuador.
- **Tax base:** Net income from business activities; all deductible business expenses: directly incurred costs, business debt interest (up to Central Bank rate), taxes levied on the business (excluding income tax), SS contributions, pension/savings fund contributions, depreciation, amortization of losses, salaries/wages and fringe benefits, profit-sharing (15%), severance, insurance premiums, provisions for uncollectible receivables, income tax and SS if assumed by employer, expenses at contract/invoice completion. (EY p.372–373)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Progressive annual rates 0%–37% (same scale as employment).
- **Social security:** Self-employed register with IESS; contribution rates not specified in EY source.
- **VAT obligations:** VAT (IVA) applies; standard rate 15% (reduced 5% for certain goods). Must register and file.
- **Key limits:** 5-year loss carryforward. Filing between 10 March and 28 March (depending on 9th digit of tax ID). (EY p.374)

### Option B — Temporary Fiscal Residence (New Residents)
- **Who qualifies:** Individuals who have not previously acquired tax residence and invest ≥USD 150,000 in Ecuador OR receive ≥USD 2,510/month foreign income.
- **Tax base:** Ecuadorian-source income only (foreign income exempt).
- **Rate / mechanism:** Standard progressive rates 0%–37% on Ecuador-source income.
- **Social security:** Standard rules apply.
- **Key limits:** 5-year term; conditions must be met within 120-day period from first day arriving in Ecuador. (EY p.370)

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Temporary Fiscal Residence
- **Eligibility:** All individuals who have not previously acquired Ecuadorian tax residence; must invest ≥USD 150,000 in real property or productive activities, OR receive monthly foreign income of ≥USD 2,510.
- **Benefit:** Taxed only on Ecuadorian-source income for 5 years; foreign-source income not taxed.
- **Duration:** 5 years (if all conditions met within 120-day period from first arrival).
- **Caveats:** Conditions must be continuously met; legislation entered into force 20 December 2023. (EY p.370)

### Remote Work Visa
- **Eligibility:** Foreign nationals performing work activities remotely for foreign companies; must demonstrate stable labor relationship with a foreign company and remuneration over 3× Ecuadorian minimum wage (≥USD 1,380/month or USD 16,920/year).
- **Benefit:** Legal work authorization in Ecuador; can perform professional services remotely.
- **Duration:** Temporary residence visa valid 2 years; renewable.
- **Caveats:** Ministry of Foreign Affairs may establish additional requirements; norm is still being finalized. (EY p.375)

## Effective Rate Examples

> Single person, no dependants, standard deductions only.
> Ecuador uses USD — no currency conversion needed.
> "Employment" = gross annual salary; employee SS 9.45% deducted and deductible from PIT base; zero-rate band USD 12,081 applied.
> "B2B" = self-employment net profit; progressive rates; SS not specified for self-employed (estimated at 9.45% for comparison).
> No personal expense discount modeled (requires documented receipts).
> Exempt bonuses (13th/14th salary) NOT included in gross — standard salary only.

### Working: USD 30,000 Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Employee SS (9.45%) | 9.45% × 30,000 | USD 2,835 |
| PIT base | 30,000 − 2,835 | USD 27,165 |
| Band 1: USD 0–12,081 at 0% | 0% | USD 0 |
| Band 2: USD 12,081–15,387 at 5% | 5% × 3,306 | USD 165 |
| Band 3: USD 15,387–19,978 at 10% | 10% × 4,591 | USD 459 |
| Band 4: USD 19,978–26,422 at 12% | 12% × 6,444 | USD 773 |
| Band 5: USD 26,422–27,165 at 15% | 15% × 743 | USD 111 |
| Total PIT | 165 + 459 + 773 + 111 | USD 1,508 |
| **Total burden** | **2,835 + 1,508** | **USD 4,343** |
| **Effective rate** | **4,343 / 30,000** | **~14.5%** |
