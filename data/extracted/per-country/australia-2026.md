## Personal Income Tax

Progressive system. Tax year = 1 July – 30 June (financial year). Filing deadline: 31 October (self-lodged) or extended dates if using a registered tax agent. (EY p.81)
Australian residents taxed on worldwide income. Nonresidents taxed on Australian-source income only.
Spouses taxed separately; joint filing not permitted. (EY p.81)
Tax is collected via PAYG (Pay As You Go) withholding for employees; quarterly PAYG instalments or annual return for self-employed.

### Resident individual income tax rates — 2025-26 (1 July 2025 – 30 June 2026)

The **Stage 3 tax cuts** took effect from 1 July 2024 and remain in force for 2025-26. These cuts restructured the brackets, reducing the 32.5% rate to 30%, eliminating the 37% bracket starting point at $120,000, and raising the threshold for the 45% bracket. (EY p.79)

| Taxable income (AUD) | Rate | Tax on lower amount (AUD) |
|---|---|---|
| $0 – $18,200 | 0% | $0 |
| $18,201 – $45,000 | 16% | $0 |
| $45,001 – $135,000 | 30% | $4,288 |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | 37% | $31,288 |
| Over $190,000 | 45% | $51,638 |

> Source: EY p.79 (confirmed: rates effective 1 July 2025, 2025-26 tax year). The tax-free threshold of AUD $18,200 applies to Australian residents.

**Medicare Levy:** Additional 2% on taxable income for resident individuals who are eligible for Medicare services. (EY p.80)
- Low-income earners: Medicare levy reduction/exemption applies below ~$26,000 (single; threshold indexed annually)
- Medicare Levy Surcharge (MLS): Additional 1%–1.5% for high-income earners without adequate private hospital cover (income >$97,000 single, 2025-26)
- Nonresidents: Not liable for Medicare levy (EY p.79)

**Low Income Tax Offset (LITO):** (ATO 2025-26)
- Maximum offset: AUD $700 (for taxable income ≤ $37,500)
- Phases out: $700 − 5 cents per $1 above $37,500 (i.e., zero at $45,000); then $325 − 1.5 cents per $1 above $45,001 (zero at $66,667)
- Applied as a tax offset (credit against tax payable); not refundable if tax is zero

**Low Income Superannuation Tax Offset (LISTO):** AUD $500 maximum; available to low-income earners with concessional superannuation contributions. Income ≤ $37,000. Paid directly into the super fund.

**Note — LMITO (Low and Middle Income Tax Offset):** The LMITO ended 30 June 2022. It is **not available** for 2025-26. (ATO)

### Nonresident individual income tax rates — 2025-26

| Taxable income (AUD) | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 – $135,000 | 30% |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | 37% |
| Over $190,000 | 45% |

Nonresidents: no tax-free threshold; no Medicare levy; no LITO. (EY p.79)

**Capital gains and losses:**
- Assets held ≥ 12 months: 50% CGT discount applied (only 50% of the capital gain included in taxable income). (EY p.77)
- Assets held < 12 months: Full capital gain included at marginal rates.
- Main residence (principal home): generally exempt from CGT.
- Assets acquired before 19 September 1985: generally exempt. (EY p.77)
- Temporary residents: not taxable on gains from non-TAP assets. (EY p.78)

**Standard deductions / allowances:**
- No equivalent broad "standard deduction." Deductions must be substantiated.
- Work-related expenses: deductible if incurred in producing assessable income (receipts required for >$300 combined). (EY p.78)
- Home office expenses: deductible (fixed rate 67 cents/hour from 2022-23, or actual costs method)
- Vehicle expenses: deductible (logbook method or cents-per-km up to 5,000 km)
- Self-education expenses: deductible if related to current employment/business
- Charitable donations: deductible if to a Deductible Gift Recipient (DGR)
- Personal superannuation contributions: deductible if a "notice of intent to claim" is filed with the fund (since 2017; income test abolished)
- Investment expenses (interest on loans to purchase income-producing assets): deductible
(EY p.78–79)

**Notes:**
- Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT): Most non-cash employment benefits taxable to the employer at 47% on grossed-up value; generally not income of the employee. (EY p.75)
- Working holiday makers (WHM): special flat rates apply (15% on first $45,000; then resident rates).
- Tax File Number (TFN): Must be provided to employer and super fund. Withholding at highest rate (47%) if not provided. (EY p.77)

## Social Security

Australia's social security for workers operates through **compulsory superannuation (pension)** rather than a European-style social insurance system. There are no separate contribution-based unemployment or sickness insurance premiums deducted from wages. (EY p.80)

### Superannuation Guarantee (SG) — for employees

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Self-employed (ABN sole trader) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superannuation Guarantee | 0% (voluntary) | **12%** of OTE | 0% (voluntary) |
| Medicare Levy | 2% (income tax) | — | 2% (income tax) |

**Ordinary Time Earnings (OTE) base:** Super is paid on OTE — salary, wages, and most cash compensation for ordinary hours. Excludes overtime earnings from SG calculation (though can be included). (EY p.80)

**Maximum OTE base (2025-26):** AUD $62,500 per quarter = AUD $250,000/year. No obligation to pay SG on OTE above this cap. (EY p.80)

**SG rate 2025-26:** 12.0% (increased from 11.5% on 1 July 2025). Scheduled to remain at 12% permanently.

**Super for self-employed ABN sole traders:** Self-employed individuals are **not required** to pay super guarantee contributions. They can make voluntary personal superannuation contributions:
- Concessional (pre-tax / deductible): up to $30,000/year (concessional cap; taxed at 15% within the fund)
- Non-concessional (post-tax): up to $120,000/year (non-concessional cap; tax-free within fund)
- Deductible personal contributions: sole traders can claim a deduction for personal super contributions by lodging a "notice of intent to claim deduction" with their fund (Section 290-180)
(ATO; EY p.80)

**Division 293 tax:** An additional 15% super contributions tax on concessional contributions for individuals with income + super contributions > AUD $250,000/year. (EY p.79–80)

**Income ceiling:** Super cap applies at OTE $250,000/year for employer SG obligations. No Medicare levy ceiling.

**Notes:**
- No payroll tax equivalent at federal level (payroll tax is a state/territory levy on wages above threshold, e.g., NSW: 5.45% above AUD $1.2M threshold)
- No federal unemployment insurance premium for employees
- Workers compensation: compulsory state-based insurance paid by employer; not deducted from wages

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — ABN Sole Trader (Individual)

- **Who qualifies:** Any individual carrying on business or providing services under an Australian Business Number (ABN). The most common structure for freelancers and contractors in Australia. Income reported as business income on individual tax return (Form 1 / myTax).
- **Tax base:** Gross business revenue minus allowable deductions (business expenses, depreciation, home office, vehicle, professional development, equipment). Net profit taxed at individual progressive rates.
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  1. Net business income assessed at standard resident marginal rates (same table as employment income).
  2. Tax-free threshold ($18,200) applies.
  3. LITO applies (up to $700 offset).
  4. Medicare levy: 2% on taxable income.
  5. **No automatic super** — must voluntarily contribute. Concessional contributions up to $30,000/year are tax-deductible and reduce taxable income.
  6. **No PAYG withholding** (payer does not withhold tax from ABN invoices unless ABN not provided). Sole trader must make quarterly PAYG installments or pay full tax on assessment.
- **Social security:** No compulsory super or social insurance payments. Voluntary super at up to $30,000/year concessional cap.
- **VAT obligations (GST):** Must register for GST once turnover exceeds AUD $75,000/year. Rate: 10% on most goods and services. File BAS (Business Activity Statement) quarterly or annually. Input tax credits available on business purchases.
- **Key limits:** No liability protection (personal assets at risk). At scale, a company structure (Pty Ltd) may provide tax deferral and liability protection.

### Option B — Proprietary Limited Company (Pty Ltd)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed person who incorporates. Provides limited liability and potential tax efficiency at higher income levels.
- **Tax base:** Company income taxed at corporate rate; individual extracts salary and/or dividends.
- **Rate / mechanism:**
  1. Base company tax rate (2025-26): 30% on taxable income.
  2. Small business entity (SBE) rate: 25% for companies with aggregated annual turnover < AUD $50 million (base rate entity passive income rules apply). Most contractor Pty Ltds qualify for 25%.
  3. Salary extracted: deductible to the company; subject to individual income tax + super (employer must pay 12% SG on the employee's salary).
  4. Dividends (franked): carry franking credits equal to tax paid at company level; individual includes grossed-up dividend in income but receives a franking credit offset — prevents double taxation.
  5. Retained earnings: taxed at 25% company rate; deferral of personal tax until distribution.
- **Social security:** Must pay 12% SG on any salary paid (even to director/sole shareholder).
- **VAT obligations:** Same GST registration requirements as sole trader.
- **Key limits:** Additional compliance costs (ASIC registration, annual review fees, separate tax return, accounting costs). ATO scrutiny on "personal services income" rules: if 80%+ of company revenue comes from one client (or income is "personal services income"), company tax concessions are reduced and income attributed to the individual.

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Temporary Resident Regime

- **Eligibility:** Individuals who hold a temporary resident visa (e.g., subclass 400, 482 TSS) and whose spouse/de facto is not an Australian citizen/permanent resident/NZ citizen.
- **Benefit:** Exempt from Australian tax on most **foreign-source income** (foreign investment income, foreign capital gains on non-TAP assets). Only Australian-source income and foreign employment income earned while in Australia is taxable. (EY p.74)
- **Duration:** Applies for the period of temporary residency.
- **Caveats:** Does not apply to foreign employment income earned in Australia. Capital gains on Australian property (TAP) remain taxable. Super accumulated while a temporary resident can be withdrawn (as a Departing Australia Superannuation Payment, DASP) after leaving Australia — subject to 35% withholding tax (65% for WHM).

### Working Holiday Maker (WHM) Tax Rates

- **Eligibility:** Holders of subclass 417 (Working Holiday) or 462 (Work and Holiday) visas.
- **Benefit / Rate:** Flat 15% on first AUD $45,000 of taxable income; then resident marginal rates above $45,000. No tax-free threshold.
- **Duration:** Applies while on WHM visa.
- **Caveats:** No LITO available; Medicare levy still applies.

### New Zealand Citizens (Subclass 444)

- **Eligibility:** NZ citizens on subclass 444 special category visa.
- **Benefit:** Indefinite right to remain and work in Australia; treated as residents for tax purposes from arrival.
- **Duration:** Ongoing.
- **Caveats:** Not classified as permanent residents for super access purposes (cannot access super until permanent residency or citizenship granted).

## 2026 Changes

- **Stage 3 tax cuts remain in effect (from 1 July 2024):** The revised Stage 3 cuts (legislated under the Albanese government in February 2024) reduced the 32.5% rate to 30%, lifted the 37% bracket threshold from $120,000 to $135,000, and raised the 45% threshold from $180,000 to $190,000. These cuts are now fully in force for 2025-26. (EY p.79)
- **Super rate at 12.0% from 1 July 2025:** The Superannuation Guarantee rate increased from 11.5% to 12.0% on 1 July 2025 — the final step in the scheduled increases. Remains at 12% permanently going forward. (EY p.80)
- **Maximum OTE base 2025-26:** AUD $62,500/quarter (AUD $250,000/year). Indexed annually. (EY p.80)
- **Concessional super cap:** AUD $30,000/year (2024-25 and 2025-26). Up from $27,500 in 2023-24.
- **LITO unchanged:** $700 maximum offset; phase-out unchanged (zero at $66,667).
- **LMITO ended:** No Low and Middle Income Tax Offset for 2022-23 onwards. Not reinstated.
- **Temporary residents:** DASP withholding remains at 35% (65% for WHM funds).
- **Division 293 threshold:** $250,000 income + super contributions; unchanged.

- $0–$18,200: $0
- $18,201–$45,000: 16% × $26,800 = $4,288

**Key insights — Employment vs. ABN Sole Trader:**
- In Australia, the income tax calculation is **identical** for employees and ABN sole traders at the same income level. The progressive rates apply equally to both. This is different from many countries where self-employed pay additional social insurance on top.
- The critical difference is **superannuation:** An employee at AUD $99,000 receives 12% super ($11,880/year) on top of their salary — a total employer cost of $110,880. The ABN sole trader receives no automatic super; they must voluntarily fund their retirement.
- For cross-country comparison purposes: at €60,000 (AUD $99,000), Australia's effective income tax + Medicare rate of 25.0% is significantly lower than the US (25.0–26.2% in CA), Canada (25.1–27.1% in Ontario), or Poland (23–38% depending on regime).
- If the ABN sole trader makes voluntary concessional super contributions ($30,000/year, taxed at 15% in fund), this reduces their taxable income and effective rate. E.g., at AUD $99,000 with $10,000 super contribution: taxable income = $89,000; tax ≈ $18,838; effective rate ≈ 19.0%.

**Pty Ltd (small business, 25% rate) comparison at AUD $165,000:**
- Company pays 25% × $165,000 = $41,250 corporate tax
- Remaining $123,750 distributable as franked dividend
- Individual includes $165,000 grossed up; offsets $41,250 franking credit
- Top personal tax deferred until distribution; effective combined rate ~37–42% depending on extraction timing
- Tax deferral benefit: significant if company retains profits for investment

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/AUD = 1.65 (approximate 2025 average)
- Standard resident individual rates (not WHM, not temporary resident)
- No deductions other than LITO; no super contributions
- Medicare levy at 2% (no MLS assumed — income below $97,000 single threshold at lower brackets; no MLS shown)
- No state payroll tax (these are employer-side costs not borne by individuals)
- Employment: employer super (12%) shown separately as an additional employer cost, not deducted from gross salary shown
