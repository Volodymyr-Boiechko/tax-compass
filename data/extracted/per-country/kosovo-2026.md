## Personal Income Tax

Kosovo taxes residents on worldwide income; nonresidents on Kosovo-source income only. Resident if: (a) principal residence in Kosovo, (b) physically present ≥183 consecutive or non-consecutive days in any 12-month period, or (c) personal business enterprise established in Kosovo or has place of effective management in Kosovo. Tax year = calendar year (1 January – 31 December). Annual return due 31 March of following year; no filing required if income is only wages, interest, rent (if quarterly tax was paid), lottery winnings, intangible property income, or certain special categories. Kosovo uses the **euro (EUR)** as its currency. (EY pp. 820–826)

### Income Tax Brackets

Kosovo's PIT system is remarkably simple — three bands with a very low effective rate: (EY p. 825)

| Annual taxable income (EUR) | Tax rate | Tax due (EUR) | Cumulative tax (EUR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 3,000 | **0%** | 0 | 0 |
| Next 2,400 (3,001–5,400) | **8%** | 192 | 192 |
| Above 5,400 | **10%** | — | — |

**Effective top rate: 10%** — one of the lowest in Europe.

### Key Exempt Income

- Wages of foreign diplomatic/consular representatives and UN/IGO staff
- Dividends received by resident and nonresident taxpayers (fully exempt)
- Pensions and social welfare payments by the Kosovo government
- Inheritance (if heir is spouse, biological/adopted child, or parent) when value ≤ EUR5,000; larger inheritances within these categories also exempt
- Educational expenses paid by employer (if paid to accredited institution and employee remains employed for ≥24 months after education)
- Training expenses paid by employer for job-related training
- Benefits in kind granted by employer up to EUR65 (above EUR65 is taxable)
- Wages of persons with disabilities
- Interest on Kosovo government-guaranteed or issued instruments

**No capital gains tax per se:** Capital gains from sale of capital assets are recognized as business income and taxed at business income rates (0%/8%/10%). (EY p. 823–824)

**Gifts:** Monetary gifts or gifts in kind exceeding EUR5,000 in a tax period are taxable income; gifts between spouses, parent-to-child, child-to-parent exempt regardless of amount (EY p. 825)

### Withholding Taxes

| Income type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Intangible property income (quarterly) | 10% of quarterly taxable income |
| Lottery winnings | 10% (withheld at source) |

## Social Security (Pension Contributions)

Kosovo's only mandatory social security levy is a **pension contribution** administered by the Kosovo Pension Savings Trust. There is **no health insurance contribution** or unemployment insurance levy in Kosovo's mandatory payroll system. (EY pp. 825–826)

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pension contribution | **5%** of gross monthly salary | **5%** of gross monthly salary | Combined 10% |
| Voluntary top-up | Up to 10% total (both sides) | Up to 10% total | Max combined 15% |

**Minimum base:** Gross salary cannot be lower than the minimum salary set by the Social Council of Kosovo (minimum wage **EUR350/month** in 2025).

**Maximum base:** No stated income ceiling on pension contributions.

**Self-employed:** Must contribute 10%–30% of net income (gross income minus allowable deductions). Must file quarterly statements and make quarterly payments within 15 days after the end of each calendar quarter.

**Deductibility:** Pension contributions paid by employers are deductible as business expenses (subject to limits set by applicable law). Personal pension contributions are deductible from taxable income.

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Small Business (Annual Gross ≤ EUR30,000, no books)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals with annual gross income from business activities ≤ EUR30,000 who are not required and do not opt to keep books and records
- **Tax base / Rate:** Quarterly payments based on gross revenue per quarter:
  - **3% of each quarter's gross income** from trade, agriculture, and similar activities (minimum EUR37.50/quarter)
  - **9% of each quarter's gross income** from professional, vocational, entertainment services, and similar activities (minimum EUR37.50/quarter)
- **Social security:** Pension contributions 10%–30% of net income (quarterly)
- **VAT obligations:** VAT registration required if annual taxable turnover > EUR50,000; standard VAT rate 18%
- **Key limits:** Cannot keep books under this regime; above EUR30,000 annual gross must keep books and use Option B

### Option B — Standard Self-Employment (Annual Gross > EUR30,000, or books maintained)

- **Who qualifies:** Self-employed individuals with annual gross income > EUR30,000, or those who elect to keep books and records regardless of size
- **Tax base:** Net income (gross revenue minus deductible business expenses); same brackets as employment income (0%/8%/10%)
- **Rate / mechanism:** Quarterly advance payments: first quarter = 25% of prior-year total tax liability; subsequent quarters = 110% × prior quarter liability / 4; reduced by withheld taxes
- **Business deductions available:**
  - All expenses wholly, exclusively, and directly related to generating income
  - Health insurance premiums for employees
  - Pension contributions (within legal limits)
  - Bad debts (meeting specified criteria)
  - Depreciation of business assets
  - Representation costs up to 1% of annual gross income
  - Advertising and promotional costs
  - Travel, meals, lodging within Ministry of Finance norms
  - Charitable contributions up to 10% of taxable income
- **Social security:** Pension 10%–30% of net income (quarterly)
- **VAT:** 18% standard rate; registration mandatory if turnover > EUR50,000

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### No Dedicated Newcomer Tax Regime

Kosovo does not have a remittance basis, territorial tax, or new-resident PIT exemption. Foreign nationals who become Kosovo tax residents are subject to standard rates on worldwide income from the date of residency.

### EU/Schengen Visa Exemption

- EU Member State citizens and Schengen area nationals, plus Albania, Andorra, Monaco, Montenegro, San Marino, Serbia, and Vatican City citizens may enter and stay in Kosovo for up to 90 days per 6 months by presenting a valid biometric ID card — no visa required
- This facilitates initial exploration for potential new residents

## 2026 Changes

- **No structural PIT changes announced** as of EY data date (October 2025)
- **Minimum wage:** EUR350/month confirmed as minimum pension contribution base
- **VAT threshold:** EUR50,000/year turnover
- **No inheritance or gift tax** in Kosovo (gifts above EUR5,000 from non-family treated as income)
- **OECD Model Tax Convention:** Kosovo has introduced temporary international measures to supplement domestic tax laws in line with OECD MTC principles for situations not addressed by existing law (EY p. 827)

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants; EUR-denominated (Kosovo uses EUR).
> Employment: progressive PIT (0%/8%/10%) + pension contribution 5% employee.
> B2B: small business quarterly regime at 9% on gross (professional services ≤ EUR30,000) or standard 10% on net above EUR5,400.
> No income ceiling on pension contributions.

### €30,000 Employment

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | | €30,000 |
| Tax on first €3,000 @ 0% | | €0 |
| Tax on next €2,400 @ 8% | 8% × 2,400 | €192 |
| Tax on remaining €24,600 @ 10% | 10% × 24,600 | €2,460 |
| **Total income tax** | | **€2,652** |
| Pension employee: 5% × 30,000 | | **€1,500** |
| **Total burden** | 2,652 + 1,500 | **€4,152** |
| **Effective rate** | 4,152 / 30,000 | **13.8%** |

### €30,000 B2B (self-employed professional — gross > EUR30,000 → standard regime)

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue | | €30,000 |
| Assumed business expenses (20%) | | €6,000 |
| Net income | | €24,000 |
| Tax on first €3,000 @ 0% | | €0 |
| Tax on next €2,400 @ 8% | | €192 |
| Tax on remaining €18,600 @ 10% | 10% × 18,600 | €1,860 |
| **Total income tax** | | **€2,052** |
| Pension (10%–30% of net; using 10%) | 10% × 24,000 | **€2,400** |
| **Total burden** | 2,052 + 2,400 | **€4,452** |
| **Effective rate on gross revenue** | 4,452 / 30,000 | **14.8%** |

### Summary Table

| Gross income | Scenario | Income tax (€) | Pension/SS (€) | Total (€) | **Effective rate** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| €30,000 | Employment | 2,652 | 1,500 | **4,152** | **13.8%** |
| €30,000 | B2B (standard, 20% costs) | 2,052 | 2,400 | **4,452** | **14.8%** |
| €60,000 | Employment | 5,652 | 3,000 | **8,652** | **14.4%** |
| €60,000 | B2B (standard, 20% costs) | 4,452 | 4,800 | **9,252** | **15.4%** |
| €100,000 | Employment | 9,652 | 5,000 | **14,652** | **14.7%** |
| €100,000 | B2B (standard, 20% costs) | 7,452 | 8,000 | **15,452** | **15.5%** |

**Working for €60,000 employment:**
- Tax: 0 + 192 + 10% × (60,000 − 5,400) = 192 + 5,460 = **5,652**
- Pension: 5% × 60,000 = 3,000; total = **8,652; effective 14.4%**

**Working for €100,000 employment:**
- Tax: 0 + 192 + 10% × (100,000 − 5,400) = 192 + 9,460 = **9,652**
- Pension: 5% × 100,000 = 5,000; total = **14,652; effective 14.7%**

**Key insight:** Kosovo has the lowest effective income tax rate in Europe — essentially a flat ~9–10% income tax above EUR5,400. The 5% employer pension contribution is the main additional labor cost. Total burden hovers around 14–15% even at high incomes, making Kosovo extremely attractive from a tax perspective. There is no health insurance levy, no housing levy, and no VAT on services below EUR50,000 turnover.

**Assumptions:**
- Currency: EUR (Kosovo uses EUR as official currency)
- Single person, no dependants; no pension deductions from taxable income assumed (employer portion not shown)
- Pension: employee 5% (no ceiling); self-employed 10% of net income
- B2B: 20% cost ratio; net income used as tax base; standard regime (not small business quarterly regime)
- No dividend income (dividends exempt in Kosovo)
- Employer pension contribution (5%) not shown

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **Small business quarterly regime at €30k:** If gross revenue is exactly EUR30,000 (not above), the small business 9% quarterly regime applies (professional services). Tax = 9% × 30,000 = EUR2,700 vs standard regime EUR2,052 on net income. Small business regime is less efficient for professional services above ~EUR22,800 gross if costs are 20%+. Verify which regime is more beneficial based on actual costs.
- [ ] **Self-employed pension (10%–30%):** EY states the self-employed must contribute between 10% and 30% of net income to the Kosovo Pension Savings Trust. The actual rate within this range is unclear — minimum 10% assumed above. Verify applicable rate.
- [ ] **VAT threshold EUR50,000:** Self-employed with gross > EUR50,000 must register for VAT at 18%. At €60k and €100k gross, VAT registration is mandatory. For B2B clients who can reclaim VAT, this is compliance-neutral; for B2C, it adds 18% cost.
- [ ] **No health insurance levy:** Kosovo's social security system currently only covers pension contributions. There is no mandatory health insurance contribution from employees/employers. Public health services are funded through general taxation.
- [ ] **Business income capital gains:** Capital gains are taxed as business income at the standard PIT rates (0%/8%/10%). There is no separate, potentially lower capital gains rate — but the 10% top rate is already very low.
- [ ] **Dividends fully exempt:** Dividends received by both resident and nonresident shareholders are exempt from Kosovo PIT. Combined with the low 10% CIT rate, this makes Kosovo-based owner-managed companies potentially very tax-efficient.
