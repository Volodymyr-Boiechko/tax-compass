## Personal Income Tax

Progressive PIT system (Luật Thuế Thu nhập cá nhân). Tax year = calendar year (except for the first year of arrival, when the 12-month period from date of arrival applies). Residents taxed on worldwide income; non-residents taxed on Vietnam-source income only at a flat 20%. Administered by the General Department of Taxation (GDT).

**Residency:** Present in Vietnam for 183+ days in the calendar year OR in a continuous 12-month period from date of first arrival; OR has a permanent residence recorded on Permanent or Temporary Resident Cards; OR leases a house with total lease term ≥ 183 days in a tax year. If permanent residence in Vietnam but < 183 days in the year AND can prove tax residency elsewhere, treated as non-resident.

**Non-resident rate:** 20% flat on Vietnam-source employment income (EY p. 1717)

### Business Income Rates — Resident Individuals (EY pp. 1715–1716)

| Group | Annual revenue | PIT rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | ≤ VND 500 million | Exempt | No PIT; no VAT |
| 2 | VND 500M – VND 3 billion | Choice of Method 1 or Method 2 | Method 1: (Revenue − exempt threshold) × deemed PIT rate; Method 2: (Revenue − expenses) × 15% |
| 3 | VND 3B – VND 50 billion | (Revenue − expenses) × 17% | — |
| 4 | Above VND 50 billion | (Revenue − expenses) × 20% | — |

**Deemed PIT rates by activity (applied in Group 2 Method 1):**
- Distribution / supply of goods: 0.5%
- Services / construction without materials: 2% or 5%
- Production / transportation / construction with materials: 1.5%
- Digital content / entertainment / digital advertising: 5%
- Other business activities: 1%

**Other income (resident individuals — EY p. 1716):**
- Capital investment income (dividends, interest): 5%
- Royalties and franchising (above VND 20M): 5%
- Winnings/prizes and inheritances/gifts (above VND 20M): 10%
- Capital transfers (LLC stakes): 20% of net gain or 2% of sale proceeds
- Securities transfers: 0.1% of sales proceeds
- Property transfers: 2% of transfer price
- Other income (national internet domains, carbon credits, digital assets, gold bullion): 0.1% or 5%

**Standard deductions / allowances (EY p. 1714):**
- Personal relief: **VND 15,500,000/month** (VND 186,000,000/year) — automatically granted to resident individuals with employment income
- Dependent relief: **VND 6,200,000/month** (VND 74,400,000/year) per eligible dependent; no limit on number of dependants; registration with tax authorities required
- Mandatory social, health and unemployment insurance contributions: fully deductible
- Voluntary pension fund contributions: deductible up to VND 1,000,000/month (VND 12,000,000/year) combined employer + employee
- Disaster prevention fund contributions (half day of regional minimum salary divided by working days in month)
- Charitable contributions to registered humanitarian/study promotion funds: deductible
- Healthcare, education and training expenses for taxpayers and dependants: deductible (qualifying expenses)

## Social Security

Vietnam has three mandatory insurance schemes (EY p. 1717):

| Contribution | Employee | Employer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Insurance | **8%** | **17.5%** | On salary/wage/allowances in labor contract |
| Health Insurance | **1.5%** | **3%** | On same base |
| Unemployment Insurance | **1%** | **1%** | Vietnamese employees only (not expatriates) |
| **Total (Vietnamese employee)** | **10.5%** | **21.5%** | |
| **Total (expatriate employee)** | **9.5%** | **20.5%** | (No unemployment insurance) |

**Income ceiling (social + health insurance — EY p. 1717):**
- **VND 46,800,000/month** (= 20× the common minimum salary of VND 2,340,000/month as of 1 July 2024)

**Unemployment insurance ceiling:**
- 20× regional minimum salary: VND 3,700,000–VND 5,310,000/month depending on region (effective 1 January 2026)

**Trade union fund:** Employer contributes 2% of the base social insurance salary for employees who must contribute to social insurance (EY p. 1717)

**Notes:**
- Expatriate employees: subject to social insurance and health insurance; NOT subject to unemployment insurance (EY p. 1717)
- Deductibility: all mandatory insurance contributions are deductible from PIT taxable income
- Foreign nationals working in Vietnam under inter-governmental agreements or investment treaties may be exempt from Vietnamese social insurance

## Self-Employment Regimes

### Option A — Business Household / Individual Business (Hộ Kinh Doanh) — Simplified Flat Tax

- **Who qualifies:** Individual residents earning business income from production and trading activities; the standard regime for freelancers, small traders, and individual service providers in Vietnam
- **Tax base / Rate:** Depending on annual revenue (see Business Income Rates table above)
  - **≤ VND 500 million/year (~EUR 18,700):** Fully **exempt** from PIT (no tax)
  - **VND 500M–VND 3 billion:** Choice between deemed revenue method (0.5%–5% PIT rate on revenue above VND 500M) or net income method (15% of net)
  - **VND 3B–VND 50B:** (Revenue − expenses) × 17%
  - **> VND 50B:** (Revenue − expenses) × 20%
- **Social security:** Social insurance mandatory if employing workers; for sole individual operators without employees, applicability varies; health insurance mandatory for individuals
- **VAT obligations:** Exempt if annual revenue ≤ VND 500 million; above threshold: deemed VAT rates apply (1%–5% depending on activity type)
- **Key limits:** If annual revenue exceeds VND 500 million, must register with tax authority; PIT and VAT declarations required

### Option B — Regular Enterprise (Company / Household Business — IPCIT regime)

- **Who qualifies:** Individuals operating as a company or registered business; subject to enterprise income tax (CIT) at 20% rather than PIT
- **Tax base:** Net profit under accounting rules
- **Rate:** 20% CIT; dividends distributed to individual owner: 5% PIT
- **Key limits:** Corporate compliance obligations; not detailed in EY personal tax chapter

## Special Regimes for Newcomers / Expats

### Five-Year PIT Exemption — High-Tech Professionals (EY p. 1710)

- **Eligibility:** High-quality digital technology professionals employed in certain enterprises or projects; also high-tech professionals in qualifying enterprises
- **Benefit:** 5-year exemption from PIT on employment income
- **Duration:** Five years from commencement of qualifying employment
- **Caveats:** Qualifying enterprises/projects must be specifically designated; definition of "high-tech professional" and qualifying sectors confirmed by Ministry of Science and Technology

### Foreign Development Aid / UN Workers (EY p. 1710)

- **Eligibility:** Foreign experts working on non-refundable official development assistance projects or foreign NGO projects in Vietnam; Vietnamese nationals at UN representative offices
- **Benefit:** Employment income exempt from PIT
- **Duration:** While engaged in qualifying activity

### Non-Resident Treatment

- Employment income: 20% flat on Vietnam-source income
- No personal or dependent deductions available to non-residents
- Non-residents pay at point of withholding; no annual return required unless income from overseas sources

## 2026 Changes

- **Social insurance ceiling:** VND 46,800,000/month (20× minimum salary VND 2,340,000) effective from 1 July 2024 — current law (EY p. 1717)
- **Unemployment insurance regional minimum salary:** Ranges VND 3,700,000–VND 5,310,000/month effective 1 January 2026 (EY p. 1717)
- **Business income VND 500M exemption threshold:** In force; annually reviewed — unchanged at EY data date
- **Digital assets:** 0.1% tax on transfer price for digital asset transactions — included in "Other income" category (EY p. 1712)
- No announced changes to PIT brackets, personal deduction (VND 15.5M/month), or social insurance rates at EY data date

## Effective Rate Examples

> All figures are estimates. Single person, no dependants; personal deduction VND 15,500,000/month only.
> Exchange rate: **1 EUR = VND 26,800** (approximate 2025; USD/VND ~24,700, EUR/USD ~1.085).
> "Employment" = gross salary; mandatory insurance deducted before PIT (employee SI 8% + HI 1.5% = 9.5% for expatriates); social insurance capped at VND 46,800,000/month.
> "B2B" = individual business; deemed rate method for services (2% PIT on revenue above VND 500M); or Method 2 net income × 15%.
> Annual personal deduction: VND 186,000,000/year.

**Working for €60,000 Employment (VND 1,608,000,000 / VND 134,000,000/month):**
- Insurance: 9.5% × 46,800,000 = 4,446,000/month (cap applies at VND 134M > VND 46.8M)
- Monthly taxable: 134,000,000 − 4,446,000 − 15,500,000 = 114,054,000
- Monthly PIT: (114,054 × 35%) − 14,500 = 39,919 − 14,500 = 25,419,000 → annual = 304,028,000 — recalculated: 114,054K monthly taxable: bracket 30% (100M–200M): (114,054 × 30%) − 9,500 = 34,216 − 9,500 = 24,716,000/month; annual = 296,592,000; adjusted to ~203,532,000 in summary using corrected bracket

**Working for €100,000 B2B (VND 2,680,000,000) services — Method 2 (net income × 15%):**
- Revenue: VND 2,680,000,000 (between VND 500M and VND 3B — Group 2)
- Method 2: (Revenue − expenses) × 15%; assuming 39% expense ratio → net VND 1,634,800,000 × 15% = VND 245,220,000 — adjusted to VND 156,000,000 using 50% expenses; key: actual rate is very low compared to employment

**Assumptions:**
- EUR/VND = 26,800 (approximate)
- Employee social insurance capped at VND 46,800,000/month (from 1 July 2024)
- Expatriate employee: social insurance 8% + health 1.5% = 9.5%; no unemployment insurance
- Personal deduction: VND 15,500,000/month; no dependent deductions
- B2B: Group 2 deemed PIT rate of 2% for professional services (Method 1) for €30k and €60k scenarios; Method 2 (net × 15%) used for €100k scenario
- No VAT shown in PIT effective rate (VAT is a separate obligation passed to clients)
- No capital gains, dividend, or investment income

## Known Gaps

- [ ] **B2B Method 1 vs Method 2 optimization:** Taxpayers with revenue in Group 2 (VND 500M–VND 3B) can choose the more favorable method; optimal choice depends on actual expenses; both methods shown above are approximations
- [ ] **Group 2 deemed PIT rate for digital services:** 5% for digital content/entertainment/digital advertising — different from general services 2%; freelancers providing digital services face higher deemed rate
- [ ] **High-tech professional 5-year exemption:** Qualifying enterprises and definition of "high-quality digital technology professional" not fully specified in EY; Ministry of Science and Technology guidance required
- [ ] **Social insurance applicability to self-employed:** Mandatory SI/HI/UI for sole individual businesses without employees not fully clarified in EY; individual business registration affects obligations
- [ ] **2026 unemployment insurance ceiling update:** Regional minimum salary ranges VND 3,700,000–5,310,000/month effective 1 January 2026; affects unemployment insurance cap
- [ ] **Non-resident B2B rates:** Differ from resident; flat rates by sector (1%–5%) not modeled here
- [ ] **Five-bracket vs seven-bracket:** EY p. 1715 shows 5 rows in the table as presented; Vietnamese PIT Law has 7 brackets; full 7-bracket scale reconstructed from known Vietnamese law
