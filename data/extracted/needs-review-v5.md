## Countries Where PwC Page Did Not Provide Full Data

PwC's "taxes-on-personal-income" pages focus on PIT brackets and do not always
detail social security rates or special regimes. For these items, EY data was
accepted as correct where PwC did not contradict it:

- Israel: SS rates (employee 12.7%, self-employed 19.99%) not on PwC PIT page
- Italy: INPS Gestione Separata (26.07%), Regime Forfettario details not on PwC PIT page
- Jamaica: NIS/NHT/Education Tax rates not on PwC PIT page
- Japan: SS employee rates not on PwC PIT page
- Jersey: SS Class 1/2 rates not on PwC PIT page
- Jordan: SS employee 7.5% not on PwC PIT page
- Kazakhstan: Pension 10%, health 2% not on PwC PIT page
- Kenya: NSSF/SHIF/AHL rates not on PwC PIT page
- Kosovo: Pension 5% employee rate not on PwC PIT page
- Kuwait: Kuwaiti national SS 10.5% not on PwC PIT page
- Laos: SS 5.5% not on PwC PIT page
- Latvia: SS 10.5% employee not on PwC PIT page
- Lebanon: NSSF 3% not on PwC PIT page

For full SS verification, see the "other-taxes" or "taxes-on-personal-income"
subpages of each PwC country summary.
